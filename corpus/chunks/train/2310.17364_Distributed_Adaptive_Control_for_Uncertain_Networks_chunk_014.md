# 3.2 Lower bound for $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain

It is known that the distributed minimax adaptive controller can never do better than the distributed $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ optimal controller as the latter operates with the knowledge of the true system dynamics. This implies that we can obtain lower bound for the $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain corresponding to the distributed minimax adaptive controller using the associated $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain of the distributed $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ optimal controller. The following lemma formally establishes this fact.

Lemma 2. Given the uncertainty set $\mathbf { A _ { i } }$ for every node $i \in \nu$ described by (12),

$$\gamma^ {\dagger} \geq \underbrace {\left\| \left((\overline {{A}} - I) ^ {2} + B B ^ {\top}\right) ^ {- 1} \right\| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} _ {:= \underline {{\gamma}} ^ {\dagger}}. \tag {22}$$

Proof. Note that given a known $( A , B )$ pair, Theorem 1 of Lidström et al. (2017) gives an explicit expression for the controller gain matrix and the corresponding $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain achieved by the controller from the disturbance to the error. That is, given $( A , B )$ matrices, the respective $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain $\gamma ^ { \star }$ is achieved by the controller2 given by (6) and $\gamma ^ { \star }$ is given by

$$\gamma^ {\star} := \left\| \left((A - I) ^ {2} + B B ^ {\top}\right) ^ {- 1} \right\| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {23}$$

Since $A$ is diagonal and Schur stable, we observe that the lower bound for the $\ell _ { 2 }$ gain $\gamma ^ { \dagger }$ is achieved 3 when $A = { \overline { { A } } }$ and hence the result follows. □

Lemma 3. Given the condition (8) $\forall p \in [ [ F ] ]$ , we can infer from Lemma 2 that

$$\left(I - A _ {p}\right) \succ \gamma^ {- 2} I. \tag {24}$$

Proof. Notice $\forall p \in \mathbb { [ } F ] $ , (22) implies that

$$(A _ {p} - I) ^ {2} + B B ^ {\top} \succeq \gamma^ {- 2} I \tag {25a}\Longleftrightarrow B B ^ {\top} \succeq \gamma^ {- 2} I - (A _ {p} - I) ^ {2} \tag {25b}$$

Then $\forall p \in \mathbb { I } F ]$ , rewriting (8), we get

$$(I - A _ {p}) A _ {p} - B B ^ {\top} \succ 0. \tag {26}$$

Using (25b) in (26), we get,

$$(I - A _ {p}) A _ {p} + (I - A _ {p}) ^ {2} - \gamma^ {- 2} I \succ 0,\Longleftrightarrow (I - A _ {p}) \left(A _ {p} + I - A _ {p}\right) - \gamma^ {- 2} I \succ 0\Longleftrightarrow (I - A _ {p}) \succ \gamma^ {- 2} I.$$
