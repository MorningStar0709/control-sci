# THEOREM 2.1

For $x^{*}(t)$ to be a candidate for an optimum, the (first) variation of J must be zero on $x^{*}(t)$ , i.e., $\delta J(x^{*}(t), \delta x(t)) = 0$ for all admissible values of $\delta x(t)$ . This is a necessary condition. As a sufficient condition for minimum, the second variation $\delta^{2}J > 0$ , and for maximum $\delta^{2}J < 0$ .
