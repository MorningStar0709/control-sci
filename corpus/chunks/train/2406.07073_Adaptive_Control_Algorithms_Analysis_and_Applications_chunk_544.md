$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t + 1) F ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {C} (t + 1) = \tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t) F ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {C} (t) - 2 \frac {\tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t) \phi_ {C} (t) f [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ]}{1 + \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) F \phi_ {C} (t)} \\ + \frac {\phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) F \phi_ {C} (t)}{[ 1 + \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) F \phi_ {C} (t) ] ^ {2}} f ^ {2} [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ] \tag {11.188} \\ \end{array}
$$

But from (11.177):

$$\tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t) \phi_ {C} (t) = \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - w (t + 1) \tag {11.189}$$

and using (11.181), one gets:

$$\tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t) \phi_ {C} (t) f [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ] \geq f ^ {2} [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ] \tag {11.190}$$

Therefore:

$$\| \tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t + 1) \| _ {F ^ {- 1}} ^ {2} \leq \| \tilde {\theta} _ {C} ^ {T} (t) \| _ {F ^ {- 1}} ^ {2} - \frac {f ^ {2} [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ]}{1 + \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) F \phi_ {C} (t)} \tag {11.191}$$

As a consequence, the sequence $\{ \| \hat { \theta } _ { C } ( t + 1 ) \| _ { F ^ { - 1 } } ^ { 2 } \}$ is a nonincreasing positive sequence bounded below by zero and thus converges. Since F is positive definite, (11.183) results. Equation (11.191) also implies that:

$$\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \left(\| \tilde {\theta} _ {C} (t + 1) \| _ {F ^ {- 1}} ^ {2} - \| \tilde {\theta} _ {C} (t) \| _ {F ^ {- 1}} ^ {2}\right) = 0 \tag {11.192}$$

and therefore (11.184) holds. From (11.191) one also has that:

$$\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \frac {f ^ {2} \left[ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) \right]}{1 + \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) F \phi_ {C} (t)} = 0 \tag {11.193}$$

Taking into account (11.180), (11.186) can be expressed as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \| \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) \| _ {F} \leq c _ {1} + c _ {2} \max _ {0 <   k <   t + 1} [ | f [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) ] | + \Delta ] \\ \leq c _ {1} ^ {\prime} + c _ {2} \max _ {0 <   k <   t + 1} | f [ \varepsilon^ {0} (k + 1) ] | \tag {11.194} \\ \end{array}
$$

Using now (11.193), (11.194) and the “bounded growth” lemma (Lemma 11.1) one concludes that (11.182) and (11.185) are true, which ends the proof. -

Remark The result can be extended for the case d > 1 (Ortega and Lozano-Leal 1987).
