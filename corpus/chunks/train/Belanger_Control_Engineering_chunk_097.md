# ◆ ◆ ◆ REMARK

Numerical computations often return extraneous zeros of large magnitudes. To see why, replace the two zeros in the bottom row of the matrix with $\epsilon_{1}$ and $\epsilon_{2}$ , respectively, to represent the effect of finite arithmetic. The determinant is then equal to

$$(4 + s) + \epsilon_ {1} (s - 2) + \epsilon_ {2} (s ^ {2} + 3 s + 2).$$

For small $\epsilon_{1}$ and $\epsilon_{2}$ , this polynomial has one root near $-4$ and the other at some large value of $s$ .
