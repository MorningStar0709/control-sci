Unlike other methods that use both safe and unsafe demonstrations [75, 52], the work in [44] presents a way for constructing a CBF for hybrid systems while using only demonstrations of safe behaviors. Instead of having explicit unsafe demonstrations, the authors define an unsafe set outside of safe demonstrations and use constrained optimization to find a CBF, modeled as DNN, that satisfies CBF properties, i.e. equations (1) and (4).

The framework from [45] extends the results from [44] by taking into account model and state estimation errors. To that end, a Robust Output Control Barrier Function (ROCBF) is constructed from expert demonstrations through constrained optimization in a similar fashion to [44], with similar definitions of safe and unsafe regions. The ROCBF can be then used to enforce safety due to its ability to account for model and measurement uncertainties.
