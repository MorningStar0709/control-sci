# 10.6.1 The Effect of Data Filtering

When data filtering is used (see Sect. 10.2), the input-output plant model (10.90) becomes:

$$y _ {f} (t + 1) = \theta^ {T} \phi_ {f} (t) + w _ {f} (t + 1) \tag {10.111}$$

where:

$$y _ {f} (t) = \frac {1}{L (q ^ {- 1})} y (t); \quad u _ {f} (t) = \frac {1}{L (q ^ {- 1})} u (t) \tag {10.112}\phi_ {f} (t) = \frac {1}{L (q ^ {- 1})} \phi (t) \tag {10.113}w _ {f} (t + 1) = \frac {1}{L (q ^ {- 1})} w (t + 1) = [ H _ {u} / L, H _ {y} / L ] \phi (t) \tag {10.114}$$

As a consequence, an appropriate choice of $L ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ will allow to reduce the value of $\| H ( \mu ^ { - 1 } z ^ { - 1 } ) \| _ { \infty }$ and respectively of the constant c, in (10.105), which will characterize the “size” of uncertainty. This is beneficial for the stability of the adaptive control systems in the presence of unmodeled dynamics.
