where M is a lower triangular matrix of dimension $( n _ { A } + d + 1 ) \times ( n _ { A } + d + 1 )$ of the form:

$$
M = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ a _ {1} & 1 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & & \ddots & 0 \\ & & & 1 \\ \vdots & & & a _ {1} \\ a _ {n _ {A}} & & & \\ 0 & \ddots & & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & & \\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & a _ {n _ {A}} \end{array} \right]} _ {n _ {A} + d} \left. \begin{array}{c c c c} n _ {A} & n _ {A} & n _ {A} + d \\ 0 & \dots & \dots & 0 \\ 0 & \dots & \dots & 0 \\ 0 & \dots & \dots & 0 \\ 0 & \dots & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right\} n _ {A} + d \tag {7.98}
x ^ {T} = \left[ 1, s _ {1} ^ {\prime}, \dots , s _ {d} ^ {\prime}, r _ {0}, \dots , r _ {n _ {A} - 1} \right] \tag {7.99}p ^ {T} = \left[ 1, p _ {1}, \dots , p _ {n _ {A}}, p _ {n _ {A} + 1}, \dots , p _ {n _ {A} + d} \right] \tag {7.100}$$

Some of the coefficients $p _ { i }$ can be zero. Since M is a lower triangular matrix it is always non-singular which implies that (7.97) (and respectively (7.91)) always has a solution:

$$x = M ^ {- 1} p$$

As in the case of the pole placement it is convenient to consider a parameterization of the controller polynomials $S ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $R ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ as:

$$S (q ^ {- 1}) = S ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {S} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.101}R (q ^ {- 1}) = R ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {R} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.102}$$

where $H _ { R } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $H _ { S } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ ) represent the prespecified parts of $R ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $S ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ . In this case $H _ { S } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ takes the specific form:

$$H _ {S} (q ^ {- 1}) = B ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {S} ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.103}$$

and (7.91) becomes

$$A (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {S} ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) S ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) + q ^ {- d - 1} H _ {R} (q ^ {- 1}) R ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = P (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.104}$$

All the consideration concerning $H _ { R } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $H _ { S } ^ { \prime } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ discussed in Sect. 7.3 for the pole placement are applicable.
