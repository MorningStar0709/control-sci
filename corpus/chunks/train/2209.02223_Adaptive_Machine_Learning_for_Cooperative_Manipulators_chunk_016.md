$$\bar {M} (\boldsymbol {q}, \boldsymbol {\theta}) \triangleq M _ {o} + \left(M _ {1} + T (\boldsymbol {\theta}) ^ {T} M _ {2} L _ {2} T (\boldsymbol {\theta}) L _ {1} ^ {- 1}\right) \Lambda\bar {\boldsymbol {h}} (\boldsymbol {q}, \dot {\boldsymbol {q}}, \boldsymbol {\theta}) \triangleq \boldsymbol {h} _ {o} + \boldsymbol {h} _ {1} + \boldsymbol {T} (\boldsymbol {\theta}) ^ {T} (\boldsymbol {h} _ {2} + M _ {2} D \Lambda \dot {\boldsymbol {x}})
\boldsymbol {N} (\boldsymbol {\theta}) \triangleq \left[ \begin{array}{l l} \boldsymbol {I} & \boldsymbol {T} (\boldsymbol {\theta}) ^ {T} \end{array} \right] \tag {30a}
\bar {\boldsymbol {u}} \triangleq \boldsymbol {N} \boldsymbol {u} \tag {30b}$$

The reciprocal of (30b) is given by

$$\boldsymbol {u} = \boldsymbol {N} ^ {+} (\boldsymbol {\theta}) \bar {\boldsymbol {u}} \quad \Leftarrow \quad \min \| \boldsymbol {u} \|, \tag {31}$$

where $N ^ { + } = N ^ { T } ( N N ^ { T } ) ^ { - 1 }$ is the generalized inverse of matrix N , and hence $N N ^ { + } = I$ In view of (25) and (30a), the expression of the pseudo-inverse matrix can be derived in the following form

$$
\boldsymbol {N} ^ {+} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \boldsymbol {Q} ^ {- 1} \\ \boldsymbol {T} \boldsymbol {Q} ^ {- 1} \end{array} \right], \tag {32}
$$

where the symmetric matrix

$$\boldsymbol {Q} = \boldsymbol {N} \boldsymbol {N} ^ {T} = \boldsymbol {I} + \boldsymbol {T} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {T} \tag {33}$$

is turned out to be a function of only ρ as follow:

$$
\boldsymbol {Q} (\boldsymbol {\rho}) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 2 \boldsymbol {I} & [ \boldsymbol {\rho} \times ] \\ - [ \boldsymbol {\rho} \times ] & 2 \boldsymbol {I} - [ \boldsymbol {\rho} \times ] ^ {2} \end{array} \right]. \tag {34}
$$

Therefore, the manipulators’ force inputs can be uniquely computed from the input of the reduced order system (29) by

$$\boldsymbol {u} _ {1} = \boldsymbol {Q} ^ {- 1} \bar {\boldsymbol {u}}, \quad \text { and } \quad \boldsymbol {u} _ {2} = \boldsymbol {T} \boldsymbol {u} _ {1} \tag {35}$$

Note that relation (35) requires that approximation of the matrix $Q$ is non-singular. One can observe from (33) that matrix $Q$ is the sum of two positive definite matrices and therefore is positive definite and invertible.
