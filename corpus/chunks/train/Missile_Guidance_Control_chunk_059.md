From (2.17b), we note that the time rate of H is required. Now, since H can change in magnitude and direction, (2.17b) can be written as [1]

$$\sum \Delta \mathbf {M} = 1 _ {H} \left(\frac {d \mathbf {H}}{d t}\right) + \boldsymbol {\omega} \times \mathbf {H}. \tag {2.40}$$

Next, the components of $1 _ { H } ( d { \bf H } / d t )$ assume the form

$$\frac {d H _ {x}}{d t} = \left(\frac {d P}{d t}\right) I _ {x} - \left(\frac {d R}{d t}\right) I _ {x z}, \tag {2.41a}\frac {d H _ {y}}{d t} = \left(\frac {d Q}{d t}\right) I _ {y}, \tag {2.41b}\frac {d H _ {z}}{d t} = \left(\frac {d R}{d t}\right) I _ {z} - \left(\frac {d P}{d t}\right) I _ {x z}. \tag {2.41c}$$

Since initially we assumed a rigid body with constant mass, the time rates of change of the moments and products of inertia are zero. The vector cross product in (2.40) is

$$
\begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {\omega} \times \mathbf {H} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \mathbf {i} & \mathbf {j} & \mathbf {k} \\ P & Q & R \\ H _ {x} & H _ {y} & H _ {z} \end{array} \right] \\ = \left(Q H _ {z} - R H _ {y}\right) \mathbf {i} + \left(R H _ {x} - P H _ {z}\right) \mathbf {j} + \left(P H _ {y} - Q H _ {x}\right) \mathbf {k}. \tag {2.42} \\ \end{array}
$$

Similar to (2.26), we can write an equation for the summation of all moments in the form

$$\sum \Delta \mathbf {M} = \mathbf {i} \sum \Delta L + \mathbf {j} \sum \Delta M + \mathbf {k} \sum \Delta N. \tag {2.43}$$

Equating the components of (2.41), (2.42), and (2.43) and substituting for $H _ { x } , H _ { y }$ , and $H _ { z }$ from (2.39) yields the angular momentum equations. Thus [1], [5],

$$\sum \Delta L = \dot {P} I _ {x} - \dot {R} I _ {x z} + Q R (I _ {z} - I _ {y}) - P Q I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44a}\sum \Delta M = \dot {Q} I _ {y} + P R (I _ {x} - I _ {z}) + (P ^ {2} - R ^ {2}) I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44b}\sum \Delta N = \dot {R} I _ {z} - \dot {P} I _ {x z} + P Q (I _ {y} - I _ {x}) + Q R I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44c}$$

or

$$\sum \Delta L = \dot {P} I _ {x} + (I _ {z} - I _ {y}) Q R - (\dot {R} + P Q) I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44d}\sum \Delta M = \dot {Q} I _ {y} + (I _ {x} - I _ {z}) P R + (P ^ {2} - R ^ {2}) I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44e}\sum \Delta N = \dot {R} I _ {z} + (I _ {y} - I _ {x}) P Q - (\dot {P} - Q R) I _ {x z}, \tag {2.44f}$$
