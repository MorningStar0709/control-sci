$$
\begin{array}{l} d \phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}) = d (A _ {t} \tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u} + \zeta_ {t}) \\ = A _ {t} \tilde {b} _ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}, u _ {t}) d t + A _ {t} \tilde {\sigma} _ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}, u _ {t}) d W _ {t} + (F _ {t} \phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}) + f _ {t}) d \boldsymbol {\eta} _ {t} \\ = b _ {t} (\phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}), u _ {t}) d t + \sigma_ {t} (\phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}), u _ {t}) d W _ {t} + (F _ {t} \phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {u}) + f _ {t}) d \pmb {\eta} _ {t}, \quad t \in [ 0, T ]. \\ \end{array}
$$

Hence, $\phi _ { t } ( { \tilde { X } } _ { t } ^ { u } )$ solves the equation (4.2) and so the assertions follows by the uniqueness of solutions.

Remark 4.2. The Doss-Sussmann transformation has been applied to random RDEs (i.e. RDEs with random coefficients) in earlier works such as [9]. Our rough Doss-Sussmann transformation is different. First, we allow our coefficient functions to be stochastic, more precisely, stochastically controlled; second, our coefficient function is linear in the state process, and thus we have an explicit formula for this transformation.

The cost function can also be expressed in terms of the state process ${ \tilde { X } } ^ { u }$ . To this end, we set

$$\tilde {h} (s, \tilde {x}, u) := h (s, \phi_ {s} (\tilde {x}), u), \quad \text { and } \quad \tilde {g} (\tilde {x}) := g (\phi_ {T} (\tilde {x})) \tag {4.11}$$

and recall that $X _ { t } ^ { u } = \phi _ { t } ( \tilde { X } _ { t } ^ { u } )$ . Thus,

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (u) = \mathbb {E} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} h \left(s, X _ {s} ^ {u}, u _ {s}\right) d s + g \left(X _ {T} ^ {u}\right) \right] \\ = \mathbb {E} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} h (s, \phi_ {t} (\tilde {X} _ {s} ^ {u}), u _ {s}) d s + g (\phi_ {T} (\tilde {X} _ {T} ^ {u})) \right] \\ = \mathbb {E} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \tilde {h} (s, \tilde {X} _ {s} ^ {u}, u _ {s}) d s + \tilde {g} (\tilde {X} _ {T} ^ {u}) \right] =: \tilde {J} (u). \\ \end{array}
$$

As a result, the rough and the transformed control problems are equivalent in the sense that

$$\tilde {\mathcal {V}} := \inf _ {u \in \mathcal {A}} \tilde {J} (u) = \mathcal {V}.$$
