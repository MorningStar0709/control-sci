# 3.1 Problem Formulation

For the lifted bilinear system via the Koopman operator,

$$\frac {\mathrm{d} z}{\mathrm{d} t} = A z + B _ {0} u + \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {m} u _ {i} B _ {i} z + r (z, u) + d \tag {17}$$

where we denote $z = \Psi ( x )$ for simplicity (actually (17) is equivalent to (9)), the noisy data can be collected,

$$
\begin{array}{l} U _ {0} := [ u _ {0} \quad u _ {1} \quad \dots \quad u _ {T - 1} ] \\ Z _ {0} := \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} z _ {0} & z _ {1} & \dots & z _ {T - 1} \end{array} \right] \\ = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l l} \Psi (x _ {0}) & \Psi (x _ {1}) & \dots & \Psi (x _ {T - 1}) \end{array} \right] \tag {18} \\ V _ {0} ^ {i} := \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} u _ {0} ^ {i} z _ {0} & u _ {1} ^ {i} z _ {1} & \dots & u _ {T - 1} ^ {i} z _ {T - 1} \end{array} \right] \\ Z _ {1} := [ \dot {z} _ {0} \quad \dot {z} _ {1} \quad \dots \quad \dot {z} _ {T - 1} ] \\ \end{array}
$$

where $u _ { j } ^ { i } , \ i = 1 , \cdots , m , \ j = 0 , 1 , \cdots , T - 1$ denotes the i-th component of the control input $u _ { j }$ .

Remark 1 In this paper, we assume that the derivative information ${ \dot { x } } _ { j } , j = 0 , 1 , \cdots , T - 1$ can be collected, which is a standard requirement in continuous-time datadriven control [3, 29]. This assumption also leads to the knowledge of $\dot { z } _ { j } = \nabla \Psi ( x _ { j } ) \cdot \dot { x } _ { j }$ . There are also established techniques for derivative estimation under noisy and sampled-data scenarios, e.g. [23]. There is no assumption on the method of collecting data, which means that $\phantom { + } Z _ { 0 }$ can be collected either along a single trajectory or from multiple trajectories.

Since the approximation error can be handled by $r ( z , u )$ , we temporarily discard the error term. Although the disturbance is unknown, we can also arrange the sequence

$$
\begin{array}{l} D _ {0} := \left[ d _ {0} d _ {1} \dots d _ {T - 1} \right] \\ = \left[ \nabla \Psi \left(x _ {0}\right) \bar {d} _ {0} \nabla \Psi \left(x _ {1}\right) \bar {d} _ {1} \dots \nabla \Psi \left(x _ {T - 1}\right) \bar {d} _ {T - 1} \right], \tag {19} \\ \end{array}
$$

such that the collected data should satisfy

$$Z _ {1} = A Z _ {0} + B _ {0} U _ {0} + \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {m} B _ {i} V _ {0} ^ {i} + D _ {0}. \tag {20}$$
