# A. Non-Minimal Realization for Data-Driven Control

Inspired by classical results in adaptive control [22, Ch. 4] and following the state-space framework developed in [20], we now introduce a non-minimal realization of the transfer function matrix $\mathcal { D } ^ { - 1 } ( s ) \mathcal { N } ( s )$ , characterizing the noise-free input–output behavior of (1). Let (Λ, Γ) be a controllable pair as described in Algorithm 1, and let

$$\mathcal {D} _ {\lambda} (\xi) := \xi^ {n} + \lambda_ {n - 1} \xi^ {n - 1} + \dots + \lambda_ {1} \xi + \lambda_ {0} \tag {12}$$

be the characteristic polynomial of Λ, having n distinct roots with negative real part.

Then, define ${ \cal F } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { \mu \times \mu } , G \in \mathbb { R } ^ { \mu \times m } , L \in \mathbb { R } ^ { \mu \times p }$ as in (8), with $\mu : = n ( p + m )$ that represents, as shown below, the dimension of the non-minimal realization. The following result will be used throughout the paper. The proof is based on [20] and can be found in Appendix II-A.

Lemma 1. Under Assumption 1 and given F , G, and L as in (8), there exist full-rank matrices $\Pi \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n p \times \mu } , H \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p \times \mu }$ that satisfy

$$\Pi (F + L H) = A \Pi , \quad \Pi G = B \tag {13}H = C \Pi ,$$

and, in addition, ensure the following properties:

1) $( F + L H , G )$ is controllable.   
2) $A - \Pi L C$ has the form

$$
\tilde {\Lambda} := A - \Pi L C = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 0 _ {p} & \dots & 0 _ {p} & - \lambda_ {0} I _ {p} \\ I _ {p} & & & - \lambda_ {1} I _ {p} \\ & \ddots & & \vdots \\ & & I _ {p} & - \lambda_ {n - 1} I _ {p} \end{array} \right]. \tag {14}
$$

Using Π and H from Lemma 1, we obtain from [20, Lemma 1] that the following dynamical system

$$\dot {z} = (F + L H) z + G u \tag {15}y = H z,$$

with $z ~ \in ~ \mathbb { R } ^ { \mu }$ , is a controllable and detectable (but not observable) non-minimal realization of $\mathcal { D } ^ { - 1 } ( s ) \mathcal { N } ( s )$ . In particular, the controllable and observable subsystem of (15), parameterized via $x = \Pi z$ , obeys ${ \dot { x } } = A x + B u , y = C x$ .
