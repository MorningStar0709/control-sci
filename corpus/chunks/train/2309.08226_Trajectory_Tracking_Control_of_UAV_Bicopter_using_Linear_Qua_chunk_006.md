In order to develop a linear quadratic regulator (LQR) controller, it is necessary to linearize the nonlinear model described in Eq. (15) around a specific operating point. Typically, this operating point is selected to be in hovering conditions. The assumption is made that the UAV Bicopter’s hovering behavior may be described by Eq. (16).

$$u _ {1} \simeq m gu _ {3} \simeq 0\sin (\phi) \simeq \phi \rightarrow \phi \simeq 0, \cos (\phi) \simeq 1 \tag {16}\sin (\theta) \simeq \theta \rightarrow \theta \simeq 0, \cos (\theta) \simeq 1\sin (\psi) \simeq 0 \rightarrow \cos (\psi) = \sqrt {1 - \sin^ {2} (\psi)} = 1$$

During this hovering condition, the attitude yaw angle of the Bicopter (ψ) is assumed to be zero. Under these conditions, the dynamics of the attitude pitch (θ) will be responsible for controlling the x position of the UAV Bicopter, and the dynamics of the attitude roll (ϕ) will control the y position of the UAV Bicopter. By using the assumption of hovering conditions in Eq. (16), the nonlinear UAV Bicopter equation in Eq. (15) can now be written in linear form in Eq. (17).

$$\dot {x} _ {1} = \dot {x} = x _ {2}\dot {x} _ {2} = \ddot {x} = - g \theta\dot {x} _ {3} = \dot {y} = x _ {4}\dot {x} _ {4} = \ddot {y} = g \phi\dot {x} _ {5} = \dot {z} = x _ {6}\dot {x} _ {6} = \ddot {z} = g - \frac {u _ {1}}{m}\dot {x} _ {7} = \dot {\phi} = x _ {8} \tag {17}\dot {x} _ {8} = \ddot {\phi} = \frac {L}{I _ {x x}} u _ {2}\dot {x} _ {9} = \dot {\theta} = x _ {1 0}\dot {x} _ {1 0} = \ddot {\theta} = \frac {h}{I _ {y y}} u _ {3}\dot {x} _ {1 1} = \dot {\psi} = x _ {1 1}\dot {x} _ {1 2} = \ddot {\psi} = \frac {L}{I _ {z z}} u _ {4}$$
