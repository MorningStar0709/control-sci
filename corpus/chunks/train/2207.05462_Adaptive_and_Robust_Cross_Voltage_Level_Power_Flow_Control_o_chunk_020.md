# Detailed Simulation Model

In this model, the CHPs are modeled according to [26] and the WTs according to IEC 61400-27-1 (Type 4B). The PV systems’ setpoint tracking are assumed to behave like a power inverter. In [3], [7], the dynamic behavior of inverter-based DERs is modeled as a first-order transfer function. Furthermore, the first-order response of an inverter is demonstrated by numeric simulations in [27]. For this reason, the PV plants used in this work are modeled as first-order transfer functions, including a small delay for signal processing. Therefore, the dynamic behavior of the PV plants is modeled similarly to the reference model in Section III-B plus a current source as an interface to the electrical grid. All other generation units that are not integrated as control system actuators are implemented as constant current sources. The upstream 110 kV grid is implemented as an ideal voltage source. Furthermore, loads are modeled as constant impedances. For lines, the threephase section line model from Simscape Power Systems is used. The simulations are executed with the phasor simulation method and Heun’s integration technique.
