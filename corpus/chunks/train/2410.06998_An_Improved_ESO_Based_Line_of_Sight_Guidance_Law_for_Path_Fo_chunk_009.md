$$u _ {p} = \dot {w} \sqrt {x ^ {\prime} {} _ {k} ^ {2} (w) + y ^ {\prime} {} _ {k} ^ {2} (w)} \tag {4}$$

Combining

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {k} (w) = \frac {\partial x _ {k} (w)}{\partial w} \frac {\partial w}{\partial t} = \dot {w} \cdot x _ {k} ^ {\prime} (w) \\ = \dot {w} \sqrt {x _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w) + y _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w)} \cdot \cos \alpha_ {k} \\ \dot {y} _ {k} (w) = \frac {\partial y _ {k} (w)}{\partial w} \frac {\partial w}{\partial t} = \dot {w} \cdot y _ {k} ^ {\prime} (w) \\ = \dot {w} \sqrt {x _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w) + y _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w)} \cdot \sin \alpha_ {k} \end{array} \right. \tag {5}
$$

Substituting (1), (4) and (5) into (3), the derivatives of the cross-track error and the along-track error can be obtained

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {e} = U \cos (\psi - \alpha_ {k} + \beta) + \dot {\alpha} _ {k} y _ {e} - u _ {p} \\ \dot {y} _ {e} = U \sin (\psi - \alpha_ {k} + \beta) - \dot {\alpha} _ {k} x _ {e} \end{array} \right. \tag {6}
$$

The similar derivation process above can be found in (Fossen and Lekkas, 2017; Fossen, 2023).
