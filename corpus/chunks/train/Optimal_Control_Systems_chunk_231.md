$$\mathbf {x} ^ {*} (t _ {f}) = \mathbf {M} (t _ {f}) \boldsymbol {\lambda} ^ {*} (t _ {f}) + \mathbf {v} (t _ {f}). \tag {4.3.29}$$

Again, since $\lambda^{*}(t_{f})$ is arbitrary, (4.3.29) becomes

$$\mathbf {M} (t _ {f}) = 0; \quad \mathbf {v} (t _ {f}) = \mathbf {x} (t _ {f}). \tag {4.3.30}$$

Thus, the set of the equations (4.3.25) and (4.3.26) are solved using either the initial conditions (4.3.28) or final conditions (4.3.30).

Finally, using the transformation (4.3.21) in the optimal control (4.3.8), the closed-loop optimal control is given by

$$\mathbf {u} ^ {*} (t) = - \mathbf {R} ^ {- 1} (t) \mathbf {B} ^ {\prime} (t) \mathbf {M} ^ {- 1} (t) [ \mathbf {x} ^ {*} (t) - \mathbf {v} (t) ] \tag {4.3.31}$$

where, it is assumed that $\mathbf{M}(t)$ is invertible.
