# A. Nominal Controller Design as LQG

The nominal controller is designed by the LQG controller to obtain optimal performance. With nominal controller gains (??, ??), the identified system (40) can be rewritten into (45) by including estimation error $e _ { t } = \tilde { x } _ { t } - \hat { x } _ { i }$ ?? into state vector.

$$\tilde {x} _ {t + 1} = (A + B _ {2} K) \tilde {x} _ {t} - B _ {2} K e _ {t} + B _ {2} u _ {f, t} - f _ {s} + B _ {1} w _ {t}e _ {t + 1} = \left(A + L C _ {2}\right) e _ {t} + B _ {1} w _ {t} - f _ {s} - L v _ {s} \tag {45}z _ {t} = \left(C _ {1} + D _ {1 2} K\right) \tilde {x} _ {t} - D _ {1 2} K e _ {t} + D _ {1 2} u _ {f, t}f _ {t} = - C _ {2} e _ {t} + v _ {s}$$

It can be seen from (45) that the estimation error $e _ { t }$ and state $\tilde { x } _ { t }$ are perturbed by plant-model mismatch $f _ { s }$ and $v _ { s } ,$ and the residual $f _ { t }$ consists of estimation error and model mismatch $v _ { s }$ Thus, the compensation input $u _ { f , t }$ is generated by the operator $Q$ on residual signal $f _ { t }$ to bring in the guaranteed robustness.

By defining

$$
\bar {x} _ {t} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \tilde {x} _ {t} \\ e _ {t} \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {A} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} A + B _ {2} K & - B _ {2} K \\ 0 & A + L C _ {2} \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {D} _ {1 2} = D _ {1 2}, \bar {D} _ {2 1} = [ \mathbf {0} \quad I ]

\bar {N} _ {t} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} f _ {s} \\ v _ {s} \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {F} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - I & 0 \\ - I & - L \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {B} _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} B _ {1} \\ B _ {1} \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {B} _ {2} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} B _ {2} \\ \mathbf {0} \end{array} \right],

\bar {C} _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} C _ {1} + D _ {1 2} K & - D _ {1 2} K \end{array} \right], \quad \bar {C} _ {2} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} \mathbf {0} & - C _ {2} \end{array} \right]
$$

the augmented system can be rearranged as

$$\bar {x} _ {t + 1} = \bar {A} \bar {x} _ {t} + \bar {F} \bar {N} _ {t} + \bar {B} _ {1} w _ {t} + \bar {B} _ {2} u _ {f, t}z = \bar {C} _ {1} \bar {x} _ {t} + \bar {D} _ {1 2} u _ {f, t} \tag {46}f _ {t} = \bar {C} _ {2} \bar {x} _ {t} + \bar {D} _ {2 1} \bar {N} _ {t}.$$

where,

$$
\| \bar {N} _ {t} (\bar {x} _ {t}, u _ {f, t}, t) \| _ {2} = \| \left[ \begin{array}{l} f _ {s} (\bar {x} _ {t}, u _ {f, t}, t) \\ v _ {s} (\bar {x} _ {t}, u _ {f, t}, t) \end{array} \right] \| _ {2} \leq \| \left[ \begin{array}{c c} U ^ {\prime} & U _ {2} \\ V ^ {\prime} & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bar {x} _ {t} \\ u _ {f, t} \end{array} \right] \| _ {2} \tag {47}

U ^ {\prime} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} U _ {1} + U _ {2} K & - U _ {2} K \end{array} \right], \quad V ^ {\prime} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} V _ {1} & 0 \end{array} \right] \tag {48}
$$

Now, the new ’output’ $f _ { t }$ is the deviation between estimated and actual outputs, and $u _ { f , t }$ provides an additional degree of freedom of control. For ease of expression, system (40) is referred to as original system, and system (46) is referred to as augmented system.
