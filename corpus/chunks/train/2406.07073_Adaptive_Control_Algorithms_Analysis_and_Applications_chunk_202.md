$$\phi (t) = f _ {\phi} (\hat {\theta} (t), \hat {\theta} (t - 1), \dots , \omega (t), \omega (t - 1), \dots) \tag {4.102}$$

For example, in the output error scheme $\phi ( t )$ has this general form. However, in other scheme like RLS, the components of $\phi ( t )$ do not depend upon ${ \hat { \theta } } ( t )$ , $\hat { \theta } ( t - 1 )$ , . . . .

As in the deterministic case, (4.95) and (4.98) are related with the a priori adaptation error (or prediction error) denoted by $\nu ^ { 0 } ( t + 1 )$ ) which is effectively used for implementing the adaptation algorithm of $( 4 . 9 5 )$ .

Taking into account the structure of the monic polynomials:

$$H _ {j} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + h _ {i} ^ {j} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + h _ {n} ^ {j} q ^ {- n} = 1 + q ^ {- 1} H _ {j} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}); \quad j = 1, 2 \tag {4.103}$$

Equation (4.97) can be written as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nu (t + 1) = [ \hat {\theta} (t) - \hat {\theta} (t + 1) ] ^ {T} \phi (t) + [ \theta - \hat {\theta} (t) ] ^ {T} \phi (t) \\ + H _ {1} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) [ \theta - \hat {\theta} (t) ] ^ {T} \phi (t - 1) \\ - H _ {2} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \nu (t) + H _ {2} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \omega (t) + \omega (t + 1) \\ = [ \hat {\theta} (t) - \hat {\theta} (t + 1) ] ^ {T} \phi (t) + v ^ {0} (t + 1) \tag {4.104} \\ \end{array}
$$

where:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nu^ {0} (t + 1) = [ \theta - \hat {\theta} (t) ] ^ {T} \phi (t) + H _ {1} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) [ \theta - \hat {\theta} (t) ] ^ {T} \phi (t - 1) \\ - H _ {2} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \nu (t) + H _ {2} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \omega (t) + \omega (t + 1) \tag {4.105} \\ \end{array}
$$

Replacing the term $[ \hat { \theta } ( t ) - \hat { \theta } ( t + 1 ) ]$ in (4.104) by the one obtained from (4.95), one gets (4.97) and the PAA can be rewritten as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {\theta} (t + 1) = \hat {\theta} (t) + \frac {F (t) \phi (t) \nu^ {0} (t + 1)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)} \\ = \hat {\theta} (t) + \gamma (t) F (t + 1) \phi (t) v ^ {0} (t + 1) \tag {4.106} \\ \end{array}
$$

where:

$$\gamma (t) = \frac {1 + \lambda_ {2} (t) \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)} \tag {4.107}$$

which as a consequence of the possible choices for $\lambda _ { 2 } ( t )$ in (4.96) satisfies the double inequality:

$$0 < \gamma (t) < 2 \tag {4.108}$$

Note from (4.104) and (4.105) that the quantity:

$$\hat {v} (t + 1 / t) = v ^ {0} (t + 1) - \omega (t + 1) = f _ {v} [ \hat {\theta} (t), \hat {\theta} (t - 1), \dots , \omega (t), \omega (t - 1), \dots ] \tag {4.109}$$

is the one step ahead prediction of the a posteriori adaptation error $\nu ( t + 1 )$ .
