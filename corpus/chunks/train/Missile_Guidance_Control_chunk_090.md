# (a) Aerodynamic Rolling Moment

We begin the development of the moments by noting that the rolling moment L is a function of $\alpha _ { p } , \alpha _ { y }$ , and $\delta _ { a }$ , where

$\alpha _ { p } = \mathrm { a n g l e }$ of attack in the pitch plane; in the present development, the angle of attack will be taken to be the acute angle between the $X _ { b } \mathrm { - a x i s }$ of the missile and the line of relative airflow or the missile velocity;

αy = angle of attack in the yaw plane (this angle is identified as the sideslip β); $\delta _ { a }$ = deflection angle of the aileron (this angle controls the roll of the missile).

The moments L, M, and N can be linearized using the Maclaurin series for threevariable functions. The Maclaurin series is a type of Taylor series, and so a function in three variables can be approximated by the sum of the first three terms of the series. As a result, the rolling moment L can be approximated as follows:

$$L \cong [ (\partial L / \partial \alpha_ {p}) | _ {\alpha_ {p} = 0} ] \alpha_ {p} + [ (\partial L / \partial \alpha_ {y}) | _ {\alpha_ {y} = 0} ] \alpha_ {y} + [ (\partial L / \partial \delta_ {a}) | _ {\delta_ {\alpha} = 0} ] \delta_ {a} = 0. \tag {3.25}$$
