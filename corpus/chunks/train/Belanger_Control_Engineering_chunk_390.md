# ◆ ◆ ◆ REMARK

In the special case where $S$ is positive definite, $S^{1/2}$ is nonsingular; this guarantees that the pair $(A, S^{1/2})$ is observable, since $\mathbf{x}$ is immediately recoverable from $\mathbf{x} = (S^{1/2})^{-1}\mathbf{y}$ .

As we shall see presently, the matrix Lyapunov equation is, in fact, a set of linear equations for the elements of P.
