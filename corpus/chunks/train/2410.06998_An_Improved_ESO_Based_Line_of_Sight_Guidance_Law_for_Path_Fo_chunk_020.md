# 4.2. Simulation of a circular path

In this paper, the comparative simulation results of LOS, ALOS, ELOS and IELOS are presented as follows. To begin with, the LOS guidance law is expressed as

$$\psi_ {d} = \alpha_ {k} (w) + \arctan (\frac {- y _ {e}}{\Delta}) \tag {30}$$

As a contrast, the ALOS guidance law proposed in (Fossen et al., 2015) is as follows.

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \psi_ {d} = \alpha_ {k} (w) + \arctan \left(\frac {- y _ {e}}{\Delta} - \hat {\beta}\right) \\ \dot {\hat {\beta}} = l \frac {y _ {e} U \Delta}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + \left(y _ {e} + \Delta \hat {\beta}\right) ^ {2}}} \quad l > 0 \end{array} \right. \tag {31}
$$

Obviously, the parameter Δ and ?? are both employed in LOS, ALOS, ELOS, and IELOS guidance laws. It is common practice to set the corresponding parameters of different guidance laws to the same value in order to facilitate a more straightforward comparison. Therefore, the parameters Δ and ?? are all typically set to 2 and 10, respectively, unless the scenario involves ?? = 2 in ELOS case (????????4). Consequently, when bandwidth ?? is equal to 2 in ELOS guidance law, the ?? have to be adjusted to 0.3.
