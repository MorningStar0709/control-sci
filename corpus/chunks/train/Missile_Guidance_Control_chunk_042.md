Of the nine quantities, only three are independent, since there are six independent relations between them. Since $\gamma _ { i j ^ { \prime } }$ can be considered as the component along the $j ^ { \prime } { \mathrm { - } } { \mathrm { a x i s } }$ in one coordinate system of a unit vector along the i-axis in the other, then

$$\gamma_ {i 1 ^ {\prime}} ^ {2} + \gamma_ {i 2 ^ {\prime}} ^ {2} + \gamma_ {i 3 ^ {\prime}} ^ {2} = \sum_ {j ^ {\prime}} \gamma_ {i j ^ {\prime}} ^ {2} = 1. \tag {2.3a}$$

This will be true for every value of i. Similarly,

$$\sum_ {i ^ {\prime}} \gamma_ {i j ^ {\prime}} ^ {2} = 1. \tag {2.3b}$$

The components of a vector, or the coordinates of a point, can be transformed from one system of coordinates to the other by

$$a _ {i} = \gamma_ {i 1 ^ {\prime}} a _ {1 ^ {\prime}} + \gamma_ {i 2 ^ {\prime}} a _ {2 ^ {\prime}} + \gamma_ {i 3 ^ {\prime}} a _ {3 ^ {\prime}} = \gamma_ {i j ^ {\prime}} a _ {j ^ {\prime}}. \tag {2.4}$$

Here $a _ { j ^ { \prime } }$ represents the components of the vector a in one system of coordinates, and $a _ { i }$ the components in the other. The summation sign is omitted in the last term, since it is to be understood that a sum is to be carried out over all three values of any index that is repeated.
