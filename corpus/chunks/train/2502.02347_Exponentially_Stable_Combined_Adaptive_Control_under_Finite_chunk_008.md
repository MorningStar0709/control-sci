# 4. MAIN RESULTS

The gradient-based adaptation law (Ioannou and Sun, 1996) that updates the controller gains’ estimate in the direction of minimizing the tracking error is,

$$\left[ \dot {\hat {\mathbf {k}}} _ {x} ^ {T} \dot {\hat {k}} _ {r} \dot {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} ^ {T} \right] ^ {T} = \left[ - \mathbf {x} ^ {T} - r \boldsymbol {\phi} ^ {T} \right] ^ {T} \mathbf {e} ^ {T} \mathbf {P b} k _ {p} ^ {\prime}, \tag {13}$$

generating following error dynamics,

$$\left[ \dot {\tilde {\mathbf {k}}} _ {x} ^ {T} \dot {\tilde {k}} _ {r} \dot {\tilde {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} ^ {T} \right] ^ {T} = \left[ - \mathbf {x} ^ {T} - r \boldsymbol {\phi} ^ {T} \right] ^ {T} \mathbf {e} ^ {T} \mathbf {P b} k _ {p} ^ {\prime}. \tag {14}$$

Next, we briefly state the standard result in the adaptive control for a smooth transition from the existing results to the ones achieved by the proposed adaptive controller.

Proposition 1. Consider the class of uncertain nonlinear system (3), driven by the control law (9), and the estimate of the controller gains are updated by the adaptation laws (13). The origin of the closed-loop system described by (10) and (14) is uniformly stable and that of tracking error is asymptotically stable.

$$\dot {V} \leq - \lambda_ {\min} (\mathbf {Q}) \| \mathbf {e} \| ^ {2}. \tag {16}$$
