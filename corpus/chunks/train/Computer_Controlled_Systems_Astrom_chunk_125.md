# A Difficulty

The goal of algebraic system theory is to convert manipulations of difference equations to purely algebraic problems. It follows from the definition of the shift operator that the difference equation of $(2.22)$ can be multiplied by powers of q, which simply means a forward shift of time. Equations for shifted times can also be multiplied by real numbers and added, which corresponds to multiplying Eq. (2.22) by a polynomial in q. If (2.22) holds, it is thus also true that

$$C (q) A (q) y (k) = C (q) B (q) u (k)$$

To obtain a convenient algebra, it is also useful to be able to divide an equation like (2.22) with a polynomial in $q$ . For example, if

$$A (q) y (k) = 0$$

it would then possible to conclude that

$$y (k) = 0$$

If division is possible, an equation like (2.22) can be solved with respect to $y(k)$ . A simple example shows that it is not possible to divide by a polynomial in q unless special assumptions are made.
