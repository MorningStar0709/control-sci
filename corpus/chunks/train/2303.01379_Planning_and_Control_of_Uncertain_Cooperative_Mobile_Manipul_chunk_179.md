Remark 3.1. (Local relative feedback) Note that the agents do not need to have information of any common global inertial frame. The feedback they obtain is relative with respect to their neighboring agents (expressed in their local frames) and they are not required to perform transformations in order to obtain absolute positions/orientations. In the same vein, note also that the velocities $v _ { i }$ are vectors expressed in the agents’ local frames.
