Theorem 1 is a generalization of the previous result derived in [7] as a stability condition for the receding horizon games, where the next action was collectively solved for all players in a centralized MPC block with the full knowledge of the game model. The common real-life setting of noncooperative games where multiple agents individually solve for their conjectured game model is considered in our result, rather than assuming a centralized solver or all agents having full knowledge of the true game model. The inclusion of the term W is useful in capturing the effect of the agents’ misspecifications on the stability result. Even in the existence of misspecifications within every agent’s game model, stability can be achieved.
