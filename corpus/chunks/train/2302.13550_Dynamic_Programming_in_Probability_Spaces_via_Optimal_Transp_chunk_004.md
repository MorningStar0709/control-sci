1.1. Contributions. We study the optimal control and dynamic programming in probability spaces through the lens of optimal transport theory. Specifically, we show that many optimal control problems in probability spaces can be reformulated and studied as optimal transport problems. Our results reveal a separation principle: The “low-level control of the agents of the fleet” (how does one reach the destination?) and “fleet-level control” (who goes where?) are decoupled. We complement our theoretical analysis with various examples and counterexamples, which demonstrate that our conditions cannot be relaxed and expose the pitfalls of heuristic approaches. The proofs of our results rely on novel stability results for the (multi-marginal) optimal transport problem, which are of independent interest.
