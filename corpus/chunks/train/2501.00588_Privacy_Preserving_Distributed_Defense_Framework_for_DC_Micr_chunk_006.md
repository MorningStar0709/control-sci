$$u _ {i} = c _ {i} \left(g _ {i} \left(V _ {\text { ref }} - \bar {V} _ {i}\right) + \sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} a _ {i j} \left(R _ {j} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {j} - R _ {i} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {i}\right)\right), \tag {3}$$

where $c _ { i } \in \mathbb { R } > 0$ is the coupling gain. $\bar { V } _ { i }$ is the estimate of the global average voltage value at converter i and is given by

$$\dot {\bar {V}} _ {i} = \dot {V} _ {i} + c _ {i} \sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} a _ {i j} (\bar {V} _ {j} - \bar {V} _ {i}) \tag {4}$$

where $V _ { i }$ is the local measured voltage. The secondary control setpoint for the primary control, $V _ { n _ { i } }$ , is then computed from $u _ { i }$ as

$$V _ {n _ {i}} = \int u _ {i} \mathrm{d} t \tag {5}$$

Assume that the converter produces the demanded voltage, i.e., $V _ { i } ^ { * } = V _ { i }$ . Combining (2), (3), and (4) yields

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\bar {V}} _ {i} + R _ {i} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} \dot {i} _ {i} = c _ {i} \left(\sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} a _ {i j} (\bar {V} _ {j} - \bar {V} _ {i}) + g _ {i} (V _ {\text {ref}} - \bar {V} _ {i}) \right. \tag {6} \\ \left. + \sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} a _ {i j} \left(R _ {j} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {j} - R _ {i} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {i}\right)\right), \\ \end{array}
$$

which are further formulated as

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\bar {V}} _ {i} + R _ {i} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} \dot {i} _ {i} = c _ {i} \left(\sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} a _ {i j} \left(\left(\bar {V} _ {j} + R _ {j} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {j}\right) - \left(\bar {V} _ {i} + R _ {i} ^ {\mathrm{vir}} I _ {i}\right)\right) \right. \\ \left. + g _ {i} \left(\left(V _ {\text { ref }} + R _ {i} ^ {\text { vir }} I _ {i}\right) - \left(\bar {V} _ {i} + R _ {i} ^ {\text { vir }} I _ {i}\right)\right)\right) \tag {7} \\ \end{array}
$$
