# 4.1 Estimation of coherent states

The Wigner function for a coherent state is characterized by Gaussian distribution in phase space. The coherent state’s Wigner function centered at phase-space coordinates $( \langle q \rangle , \langle p \rangle )$ is given by

$$W (q, p) = \frac {2}{\pi} \exp \left(- 2 \left[ (q - \langle q \rangle) ^ {2} + (p - \langle p \rangle) ^ {2} \right]\right). \tag {43}$$

This expression describes a symmetric Gaussian distribution in the phase-space variables q (position) and $p$ (momentum), where $\langle \boldsymbol { q } \rangle$ and $\langle p \rangle$ represent the expectation values of position and momentum for the coherent state.
