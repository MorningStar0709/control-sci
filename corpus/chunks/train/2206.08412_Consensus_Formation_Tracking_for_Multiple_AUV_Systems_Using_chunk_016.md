Choose the following Lyapunov function candidate

$$V _ {s} = \frac {1}{2} s ^ {\mathrm{T}} (L + B) ^ {- 1} s, \tag {11}$$

whose time derivative is calculated as

$$\dot {V} _ {s} = s ^ {\mathrm{T}} (L + B) ^ {- 1} \dot {s}. \tag {12}$$

Based on the definition of sliding mode variable s, as shown in (10), s˙ can be obtained

$$\dot {s} = k _ {1} (L + B) \bar {e} _ {2} + \dot {\bar {e}} _ {2}. \tag {13}$$

Due to the global error dynamics (8), yield

$$\dot {s} = k _ {1} (L + B) \bar {e} _ {2} + (L + B) \left(\ddot {\eta} - \mathbf {1} _ {N} \ddot {\eta} ^ {d}\right). \tag {14}$$

By substituting (14) into (12), we have

$$\dot {V} _ {s} = s ^ {\mathrm{T}} (L + B) ^ {- 1} \left[ k _ {1} (L + B) \bar {e} _ {2} + (L + B) (\ddot {\eta} - \mathbf {1} _ {N} \ddot {\eta} ^ {d}) \right]. \tag {15}$$
