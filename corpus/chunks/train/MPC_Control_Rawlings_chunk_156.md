# Exercise 1.35: Expected sum of squares

Given that a random variable x has mean m and covariance P, show that the expected sum of squares is given by the formula (Selby, 1973, p.138)

$$\mathcal {E} (x ^ {\prime} Q x) = m ^ {\prime} Q m + \operatorname{tr} (Q P)$$

The trace of a square matrix A, written tr(A), is defined to be the sum of the diagonal elements

$$\operatorname{tr} (A) := \sum_ {i} A _ {i i}$$
