$$
\begin{array}{l} r k \left[ \begin{array}{c} X _ {p} \\ U _ {p} \\ Y _ {f} \end{array} \right] = r k \left[ \begin{array}{c} Z _ {1 p} \\ Z _ {2 p} \\ U _ {p} \\ Y _ {f} \end{array} \right] \\ = r k \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} I _ {n _ {1}} & 0 & 0 & \ldots & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & - B _ {2} & - R B _ {2} & \ldots & - R ^ {s - 1} B _ {2} & 0 \\ 0 & I _ {m} & 0 & \ldots & 0 & 0 \\ C _ {1} A _ {1} & C _ {1} B _ {1} & - C _ {2} B _ {2} & \ldots & - C _ {2} R ^ {s - 2} B _ {2} & - C _ {2} R ^ {s - 1} B _ {2} \end{array} \right] \\ = n _ {1} + m + r k \left(\left[ \begin{array}{l} R \\ C _ {2} \end{array} \right] Q\right), \tag {29} \\ \end{array}
$$

where the second equality is due to (28) and Assumption 2. Recall that by Assumption 1, $\big [ \underline { { E } } ^ { \top } , C ^ { \top } \big ] ^ { \top }$ has full column rank, implying that $\lceil R ^ { \rceil } , C _ { 2 } ^ { \top } \rceil ^ { \top }$ is of full column rank. Consequently, pre-multiplying by this full column rank matrix does not alter the rank of Q, leading to:

$$r k \left[ X _ {p} ^ {\top}, U _ {p} ^ {\top}, Y _ {f} ^ {\top} \right] ^ {\top} = n _ {1} + m + r k (Q). \tag {30}$$

Substituting (27) and (30) into the equality of condition (iii) yields:

$$
m + r k \left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda I - A _ {1} \\ C _ {1} \end{array} \right] + r k (Q) = n _ {1} + m + r k (Q). \tag {31}
$$

We arrive at the detectability condition for the slow subsystem:

$$
r k \left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda I - A _ {1} \\ C _ {1} \end{array} \right] = n _ {1}.
$$

Since the fast subsystem matrix $( \lambda R - I )$ is inherently non-singular for any $\lambda \in \mathbb { C }$ , it can be concluded that the PBH detectability matrix for the full-order system satisfies:

$$
r k \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \lambda I - A _ {1} & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda R - I \\ C _ {1} & C _ {2} \end{array} \right] = r k \left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda E - A \\ C \end{array} \right] = n _ {1} + n _ {2} = n. \tag {32}
$$

This completes the proof of $( i i i ) \Rightarrow ( i v )$ .
