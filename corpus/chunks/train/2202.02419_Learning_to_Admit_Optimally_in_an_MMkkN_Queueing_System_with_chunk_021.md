The parameters of policy $\pi _ { \mathrm { A l g 1 } }$ are only updated when the system becomes empty, rather than at all arrivals. The reason for this modification is that the busy period boundary is a regenerative epoch that provides sufficient independence needed in the analysis, whereas the regret of the policy that updates its parameters at all arrivals is hard to analyze. However, this alternate policy, called $\pi _ { \mathrm { A l g 2 } }$ , is also asymptotically optimal, and we empirically compare it to $\pi _ { \mathrm { A l g 1 } }$ in Section 5. We also note that in the single-server setting, the two policies $\pi _ { \mathrm { A l g 1 } }$ and $\pi _ { \mathrm { A l g 2 } }$ coincide.
