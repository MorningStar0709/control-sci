# § 1. Introduction

Maximum hands-off is an optimal control paradigm where non-zero control efforts over a finite horizon of time is minimized while not compromising on a desired control objective. Such control techniques are a natural choice in the context of optimal control under resource constraints and find wide applications in energy-aware control [6, Section 8.2], minimum fuel control [6, Section 1.4.1], etc. Since the inception of maximum hands-off control paradigm in [8, 7], researchers have restricted their attention to time-invariant (non)linear systems, and primarily focussed on theoretical development towards designing a maximum hands-off control sequence, under which a system achieves a certain objective, in a numerically tractable manner. In particular, the theory of maximum hands-off control is so far explored for continuous-time and discrete-time deterministic control-affine systems [8], discrete-time linear systems [7], a class of continuous-time nonlinear systems [2], discrete-time stochastic linear systems [5]. Recently in [4] we extended the notion of maximum hands-off control to discrete-time switched linear systems.
