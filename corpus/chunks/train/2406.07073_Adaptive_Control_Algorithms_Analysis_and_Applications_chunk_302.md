Theorem 7.1 Under the hypotheses H1 through H5 and $\mathrm { H } 4 ^ { \prime } \colon A H _ { S }$ and $B H _ { R }$ do not have any common factors, (7.21) has a unique solution for $S ^ { \prime }$ and $R ^ { \prime } , i f \colon$

$$n _ {P} = \deg P (q ^ {- 1}) \leq n _ {A} + n _ {H S} + n _ {B} + n _ {H R} + d - 1 \tag {7.22}n _ {S ^ {\prime}} = \deg S ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = n _ {B} + n _ {H R} + d - 1 \tag {7.23}n _ {R ^ {\prime}} = \deg R ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = n _ {A} + n _ {H S} - 1 \tag {7.24}$$

with

$$S ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + s _ {1} ^ {\prime} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + s _ {n _ {S}} ^ {\prime} q ^ {- n _ {S}} \tag {7.25}R ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = r _ {0} ^ {\prime} + r _ {1} ^ {\prime} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + r _ {n _ {R}} ^ {\prime} q ^ {- n _ {R}} \tag {7.26}$$

Proof In order to effectively solve (7.21) one uses the fact that the polynomial equation (7.21) takes the matrix form

$$M x = p \tag {7.27}$$

where:

$$x ^ {T} = \left[ 1, s _ {1} ^ {\prime}, \dots , s _ {n _ {S}} ^ {\prime}, r _ {0} ^ {\prime}, \dots , r _ {n _ {R}} ^ {\prime} \right] \tag {7.28}p ^ {T} = \left[ 1, p _ {1}, \dots , p _ {n _ {P}}, 0, \dots , 0 \right] \tag {7.29}$$

and the matrix M (known as Sylvester matrix or controllability matrix) has the form:

$$
M = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c c c} 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 \\ a _ {1} ^ {\prime} & 1 & \ddots & \vdots & b _ {1} ^ {\prime} & 0 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & & \ddots & 0 & \vdots & & \ddots & \\ & & & 1 & & & & 0 \\ \vdots & & & a _ {1} ^ {\prime} & \vdots & & & b _ {1} ^ {\prime} \\ a _ {n _ {A ^ {\prime}}} ^ {\prime} & & & & b _ {n _ {B ^ {\prime}}} ^ {\prime} & \\ 0 & \ddots & & \vdots & 0 & \ddots & & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & & & \vdots & \ddots & \\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & a _ {n _ {A ^ {\prime}}} ^ {\prime} & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b _ {n _ {B ^ {\prime}}} ^ {\prime} \end{array} \right]} _ {n _ {A ^ {\prime}} + n _ {B ^ {\prime}}} \Bigg \} n _ {A ^ {\prime}} + n _ {B ^ {\prime}} \tag {7.30}
$$

where $a _ { i } ^ { \prime }$ and $b _ { i } ^ { \prime }$ are the coefficients of

$$A ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = A (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {S} (q ^ {- 1})B ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) = q ^ {- d} B (q ^ {- 1}) H _ {R} (q ^ {- 1})$$

Note that $b _ { i } ^ { \prime } = 0$ for $i = 0 , 1 , \ldots , d$ . The vector x containing the coefficients of polynomials $R ^ { \prime } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $S ^ { \prime } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ is obtained by inverting the matrix M which is non singular if $A ^ { \prime } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $B ^ { \prime } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ do not have any common factors (hypothesis H4 and H4 —see also Sect. 2.1)

$$x = M ^ {- 1} p \tag {7.31}$$

If there are common factors between A and B the determinant of this matrix is null and (7.21) is not solvable (see Sect. 2.1). Various methods for solving this equation are available. -
