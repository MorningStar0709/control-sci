$$\int_ {V (0)} ^ {V \left(t _ {r}\right)} \frac {- d V}{2 \lambda_ {1} V + \gamma V ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = - \frac {1}{\lambda_ {1}} \left[ \ln \left| 2 \lambda_ {1} V ^ {\frac {1}{2}} + \gamma \right| \right] _ {V (0)} ^ {V \left(t _ {r}\right)} \tag {35}= \frac {1}{\lambda_ {1}} \ln \left| \frac {2 \lambda_ {1} V (0) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} + \gamma}{2 \lambda_ {1} V (t _ {r}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} + \gamma} \right|$$

Integrating the left side of Equation (34) and using the result in Equation (35), the reaching time $\left( t _ { r } \right)$ is given as:

$$t _ {r} \leqslant \frac {1}{\lambda_ {1}} \ln \left| \frac {2 \lambda_ {1} V (0) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} + \gamma}{\gamma} \right| \tag {36}$$

Based on (36), Lemma 1 is proven and he system trajectories will reach (S = 0) within a finite time.
