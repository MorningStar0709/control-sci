Let the chord $P _ { 1 } P _ { 2 }$ be denoted by c and let the coordinates of $P _ { 1 }$ be $( x _ { 1 } , y _ { 1 } )$ :

$$x _ {1} = a \cos E _ {1},y _ {1} = b \sin E _ {1} = a (1 - e ^ {2}) ^ {1 / 2} \sin E _ {1}.$$

Furthermore, let the coordinates of $P _ { 2 }$ be $( x _ { 2 } , y _ { 2 } )$ :

$$x _ {2} = a \cos E _ {2},y _ {2} = a (1 - e ^ {2}) ^ {1 / 2} \sin E _ {2}.$$

Hence, the length of $P _ { 1 } P _ { 2 }$ is given by

$$P _ {1} P _ {2} = c = [ (x _ {1} - x _ {2}) ^ {2} + (y _ {1} - y _ {2}) ^ {2} ],$$

or

$$c ^ {2} = a ^ {2} \left(\cos E _ {2} - \cos E _ {1}\right) ^ {2} + a ^ {2} \left(1 - e ^ {2}\right) \left(\sin E _ {2} - \sin E _ {1}\right) ^ {2}.$$

Making use of trigonometric identities for (cos $E _ { 2 } - \cos E _ { 1 } )$ and (sin $E _ { 2 } - \sin E _ { 1 } )$ , we can also write $\bar { c } ^ { 2 }$ as follows:

$$c ^ {2} = 4 a ^ {2} \sin^ {2} G \sin^ {2} g + 4 a ^ {2} (1 - e ^ {2}) \cos^ {2} G \sin^ {2} g.$$

Now define the relationship e cos $G { \overset { \triangle } { = } }$ cos j , so that

$$c ^ {2} = 4 a ^ {2} \sin^ {2} g (1 - \cos^ {2} j),$$

making $c = 2 a$ sin g sin j . Using (6.47), we can also write

$$r _ {1} + r _ {2} = 2 a (1 - e \cos G \cos g) = 2 a (1 - \cos g \cos j). \tag {6.48}$$
