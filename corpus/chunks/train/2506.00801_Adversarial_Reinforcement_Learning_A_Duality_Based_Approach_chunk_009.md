Hereafter we refer to W as the generating function of IR penalties.

The strong duality is achievable for finite-horizon, discrete-time control problems. Theorem 1 of Rogers [2007] and Theorem 2.3 of Brown et al. [2010] show that

$$\min _ {W} \mathbb {E} \left[ \max _ {\boldsymbol {a} = \{\boldsymbol {a} _ {t} \} _ {t = 0} ^ {T} \in \mathbb {A} ^ {T}} \left(\sum_ {t = 0} ^ {T - 1} r _ {t} (\boldsymbol {s} _ {t}, \boldsymbol {a} _ {t}) + R (\boldsymbol {s} _ {T}) - z ^ {W} (\boldsymbol {a}, \xi)\right) | \boldsymbol {s} _ {0} = \boldsymbol {s} \right] = V ^ {*} (\boldsymbol {s}). \tag {7}$$
