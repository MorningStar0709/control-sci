i.e. the empirical measure of the exogenous agents is a functional of their controls and disturbances. Suppose that the ‘optimal’ control taken by the $\dot { \mathbf { \rho } } - \dot { \imath } \mathbf { \rho } \cdot \mathbf { \varepsilon }$ agents exists and is $\hat { \mathbf { u } } ^ { - i , N }$ , and that the ‘worst-case’ disturbance for the $\dot { \mathbf { \rho } } - \dot { \imath } \mathbf { \rho } \cdot \mathbf { \varepsilon }$ agents exists and is $\hat { \mathbf { w } } ^ { - i , N }$ .

For each agent, we define the following optimal control problems:

$$\inf _ {u ^ {i, N} \in \mathcal {U} ^ {i, N}} \sup _ {w ^ {i, N} \in \mathcal {W} ^ {i, N}} \mathbb {E} \left[ \mathcal {J} ^ {i, N} [ u ^ {i, N}, w ^ {i, N}, \hat {\mathbf {u}} ^ {- i, N}, \hat {\mathbf {w}} ^ {- i, N}; (x _ {t = 0} ^ {i, N}, v _ {t = 0} ^ {i, N}), t = 0 ] \right]. \tag {2}\mathrm{s.t.} (1)$$

So, the agent attempts to make the best decision while accounting for the worst disturbance it can face, assuming that the other agents already make the best decisions they can subject to the worst disturbances they face (see [3, 4] for similar formulation descriptions).

Here, the cost functional is:

$$\mathcal {J} ^ {i, N} [ u ^ {i, N}, w ^ {i, N}, \hat {\mathbf {u}} ^ {- i, N}, \hat {\mathbf {w}} ^ {- i, N}; (x _ {\tau} ^ {i, N}, v _ {\tau} ^ {i, N}), \tau ] := \dots\int_ {\tau} ^ {\infty} e ^ {- \alpha s} \mathcal {L} ^ {i, N} (x _ {s} ^ {i, N}, v _ {s} ^ {i, N}, u _ {s} ^ {i, N}, w _ {s} ^ {i, N}, \mu_ {s} ^ {i, N} [ \hat {\mathbf {u}} ^ {- i, N}, \hat {\mathbf {w}} ^ {- i, N} ]) d s,$$

where

$$\mathcal {L} ^ {i, N} (x ^ {i, N}, v ^ {i, N}, u ^ {i, N}, w ^ {i, N}, \mu^ {i, N}) := \dots\frac {1}{2} (u ^ {i, N}) ^ {2} - \frac {1}{2 \gamma^ {2}} (w ^ {i, N}) ^ {2} + (\int_ {Y} \phi (x ^ {i, N}, \eta_ {1}) d \mu^ {i, N} (\eta_ {1}, \eta_ {2}) - \frac {1}{\beta}) v ^ {i, N}.$$

In this cost, the first term accounts for ride comfort, i.e. the vehicle is penalized for accelerating too quickly. The second term rewards the controller for causing the disturber to apply its best opposition. In the third term, the integral term causes the controller to slow down or speed up vehicles where there is high congestion. The multiplication of $\beta ^ { - 1 } v ^ { i , \dot { N } }$ enforces a preference for the vehicle to move as quickly as possible. So, there are a number of competing objectives the controller attempts to balance.
