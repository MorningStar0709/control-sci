It is known that the solution of a matrix Riccati-type differential equation (RDE) is involved in the construction of the solution of various linear quadratic optimal control problems. The applicability of the solutions of the RDEs (7) and (8) to a certain linear quadratic optimal control problem is closely related to the sign of the the matrices introduced via (9)-(10). Thus, in [21], it was shown that if the solutions $X ( \cdot ) , \hat { X } ( \cdot )$ to the RDEs (7) and (8), respectively, are defined on the whole interval [0, T ] and additionally, the following inequalities hold:

$$\mathbb {R} (t, X (t)) \geq \gamma I _ {m} \tag {11a}\hat {\mathbb {R}} (t, X (t)) \geq \hat {\gamma} I _ {m} \tag {11b}$$

for all $t \in [ 0 , T ] , \gamma , \hat { \gamma }$ being positive constants, then these solutions are involved in the computation of the gain matrices of the optimal control in a mean-field LQ optimal control problem. Also, [21] proposed conditions which guarantee the global existence of the solutions of the RDEs of type (7) and (8) satisfying sign conditions of type (11). In [23] the authors provided sufficient conditions which guarantee the global existence of the whole interval [0, T ] of the solutions $X ( \cdot ) , \hat { X } ( \cdot )$ of the

RDEs (7) and (8) satisfying sign conditions of the form:

$$\mathbb {R} _ {1 1} (t, X (t)) \geq \mu_ {1} I _ {m _ {1}} \tag {12a}\mathbb {R} _ {2 2} (t, X (t)) \leq - \mu_ {2} I _ {m _ {2}}; \quad \text { for all } t \in [ 0, T ] \tag {12b}$$

and

$$\hat {\mathbb {R}} _ {1 1} (t, X (t)) \geq \hat {\mu} _ {1} I _ {m _ {1}} \tag {13a}\hat {\mathbb {R}} _ {2 2} (t, X (t)) \leq - \hat {\mu} _ {2} I _ {m _ {2}}; \quad \text { for all } t \in [ 0, T ] \tag {13b}$$

$\mu _ { k } , \hat { \mu } _ { k } , k = 1 , 2$ being positive constants.
