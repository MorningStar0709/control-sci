# (3) Equations of Motion

Using the Newton-Euler method, we can derive the dynamic equations that describe rigid body motion.

i) Translational Dynamics:

$$\dot {v} _ {c m / e} ^ {f r u} = \frac {F _ {b}}{m} + C _ {f r u / e n u} G _ {e} \tag {5}\dot {p} _ {c m / e} ^ {e n u} = C _ {e n u / f r u} v _ {c m / e} ^ {f r u} \tag {6}$$

Here, m denotes the total mass of the quadrotor. $F _ { b } =$ $[ 0 , 0 , U _ { 1 } ] ^ { T } ; G _ { e } = [ 0 , 0 , - g ] ^ { T }$ ; the gyroscopic coupling terms are negligible compared to the dominant thrust and gravitational forces.

$$
m \left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {u} \\ \dot {v} \\ \dot {w} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ U _ {1} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} m g \sin \theta \\ - m g \cos \theta \sin \phi \\ - m g \cos \theta \cos \phi \end{array} \right]
$$

By substituting Equations (4)(6) and expanding it, we obtain the translational equations of motion in component form equation (7):

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \ddot {x} _ {e} = \frac {U _ {1}}{m} (\cos \psi \sin \theta \cos \phi + \sin \psi \sin \phi) \\ \ddot {y} _ {e} = \frac {U _ {1}}{m} (\sin \psi \sin \theta \cos \phi - \cos \psi \sin \phi) \\ \ddot {z} _ {e} = \frac {U _ {1}}{m} (\cos \theta \cos \phi) - g \end{array} \right. \tag {7}
$$
