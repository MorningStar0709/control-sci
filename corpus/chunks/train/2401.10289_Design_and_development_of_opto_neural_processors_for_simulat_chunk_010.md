# 1.2 Optogenetics

Using optical systems and genetic engineering technologies, optogenetics is a sophisticated method that allows for precise manipulation and observation of the biological activities of a cell, collection of cells, tissues, or organs with exceptional temporal and spatial accuracy. Optogenetics involves: (i) Manipulating a gene to develop reactivity to light, (ii) Delivery of concentrated light to manipulated gene and (iii) studying the effect of the excitation mechanism.

The technique utilizes opsins or light activated proteins to make cells “reactive to light”. Opsins are light-sensitive proteins found in microbes, and act as channels or pumps to facilitate the passage of ions across cell membranes upon activation by light.
