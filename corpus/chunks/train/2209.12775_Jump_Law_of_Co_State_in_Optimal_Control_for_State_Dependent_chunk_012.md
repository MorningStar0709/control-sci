# IV. ILLUSTRATIVE SIMULATION EXAMPLES

In this section, we solve this optimal control problem by doing a brute force search of the switching time and then verify our proposed co-state jump law (15) and (20).

A. Second-order system with time-invariant switching interface

In this scenario, we consider the following second-order system:

$$
\text { s1 }: \left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {1} = 2 x _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} = u \end{array} \right., \quad x _ {1} ^ {2} + x _ {2} ^ {2} \leq 1,
$$

2Note that, in general, the interface may be nonlinear, in which case, this surface is the tangent plane at the switching point.

$$
\mathrm{s} 2: \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \dot {x} _ {1} = x _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} = u \end{array} , \quad x _ {1} ^ {2} + x _ {2} ^ {2} \geq 1. \right. \tag {21}
$$

The stage cost is given by:

$$
L = \left\{ \begin{array}{l} \frac {1}{8} u ^ {2}, x _ {1} ^ {2} + x _ {2} ^ {2} \leq 1 \\ \frac {1}{2} u ^ {2}, x _ {1} ^ {2} + x _ {2} ^ {2} \geq 1 \end{array} . \right.
$$

We consider the boundary conditions: $x _ { 0 } = [ 0 , 0 ] ^ { \top } , x _ { f } =$ $[ 2 , 2 ] ^ { \top }$ and $t _ { f } ~ = ~ 2$ . Here the switching interface is a quarter of a unit circle. The switching time instant with optimal $J = 0 . 7 3 8 2$ is $\tau = 0 . 8 8 8 1$ . The co-state jump is $\Delta \lambda = [ - 0 . 0 8 4 0 , - 0 . 1 8 0 7 ] ^ { \top }$ . The $\Delta \lambda$ calculated using (15) is $\Delta \lambda = [ - 0 . 0 8 4 0 , - 0 . 1 8 0 7 ] ^ { \top }$ . The optimal trajectory x and the co-state λ are shown in Fig. 1 and Fig. 2 respectively.
