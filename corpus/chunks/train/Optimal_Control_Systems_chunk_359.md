$$J (x (t _ {f})) = \frac {1}{2} x ^ {2} (t _ {f}) = \frac {1}{2} p (t _ {f}) x ^ {2} (t _ {f}), \tag {6.4.27}$$

which gives us

$$p (t _ {f}) = 1. \tag {6.4.28}$$

Then using (6.4.26), we get

$$J _ {x} = p (t) x (t); \quad J _ {t} = \frac {1}{2} \dot {p} (t) x ^ {2} (t), \tag {6.4.29}$$

leading to the closed-loop optimal control (6.4.22), as

$$u ^ {*} (t) = - p (t) x ^ {*} (t). \tag {6.4.30}$$

Using the optimal control (6.4.29) into the HJB equation (6.4.24), we have

$$\left(\frac {1}{2} \dot {p} (t) - \frac {1}{2} p ^ {2} (t) - 2 p (t) + \frac {1}{2}\right) x ^ {* ^ {2}} (t) = 0. \tag {6.4.31}$$

For any $x^{*}(t)$ , the previous relation becomes

$$\frac {1}{2} \dot {p} (t) - \frac {1}{2} p ^ {2} (t) - 2 p (t) + \frac {1}{2} = 0, \tag {6.4.32}$$
