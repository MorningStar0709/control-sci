# 4.1.1. Accuracy evaluate of the approaches

The remaining distance from the navigation terminal location to the destination was calculated to assess the accuracy of the results using the following metrics: circular error probable and average terminal location, determined through Monte Carlo simulation (Madadi and Aksakalli, 2020). The navigation results are listed in Table 5. These metrics are defined as follows:

• The circular error probable (CEP): The distance between the final position of the navigation and the destination when the iteration stops. The CEP denotes the circular error probable (Zhang and An, 2012), and it is calculated as follows:

$$\mathrm{CEP} = \sqrt {\frac {1}{N} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} ((x _ {i} - x _ {d}) ^ {2} + (y _ {i} - y _ {d}) ^ {2}) \cdot I (d _ {i} \leq d _ {5 0})}, \tag {59}$$

where N denotes the total number of tests conducted. The coordinates $( x _ { i } , y _ { i } )$ specify the last arrival position of the i-th Monte Carlo simulation; $d _ { 5 0 }$ denotes the median distance, i.e., the probability that at least 50% of the ending locations fall within some circular area centered on the destination location; I denotes the indicator function, which takes the value of 1 when $d _ { i } \leq d _ { 5 0 }$ .
