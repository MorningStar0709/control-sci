# B. Finite-time MPC for multi-input linear systems

The multi-input linear plant is given by

$$
A = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 1. 1 & 2 & - 0. 4 \\ 0 & 0. 9 5 & - 0. 8 \\ 0 & 0. 1 & 1 \end{array} \right], B = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0. 0 7 9 & 0 \\ - 0. 1 & 0. 1 \end{array} \right],
$$

subject to constraints $| u _ { 1 } | \le 5$ and $| u _ { 2 } | \le 5 .$ .

The multi-input finite-time MPC is designed by following Theorem 3, where the control horizon is selected by $N = 8 .$ . Weighting matrices are selected by $Q _ { 1 } = I _ { 2 \times 2 } , Q _ { 2 } = 1 , R _ { 1 } =$ 0.1 and $R _ { 2 } = 0 . 1$ .

The results, depicted in Fig. 3, show that the transient process is completed in 11 steps. The control input $u _ { 1 }$ consistently satisfies its constraint, whereas $u _ { 2 }$ remains zero due to the controllable canonical form with $q = 1$ , which allows full system control through $u _ { 1 }$ .
