# 3 Dissipativity Certification

In this section, we derive conditions for an uncertain LTI system to be dissipative with respect to the specified supply rate.

Let the uncertain LTI system be:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {\boldsymbol {x}} (t) \\ \boldsymbol {v} (t) \\ \boldsymbol {e} (t) \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} A & B _ {w} & B _ {d} \\ C _ {v} & D _ {v w} & D _ {v d} \\ C _ {e} & D _ {e w} & D _ {e d} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {x} (t) \\ \boldsymbol {w} (t) \\ \boldsymbol {d} (t) \end{array} \right] \tag {10} \\ \boldsymbol {w} (t) = \Delta (\boldsymbol {v}) (t), \\ \end{array}
$$

where $x \in \mathbb R ^ { n } , v \in \mathbb R ^ { n _ { v } } , w \in \mathbb R ^ { n _ { w } } , d \in \mathbb R ^ { n _ { d } }$ , and $e \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { \epsilon } }$ . Assume this system is well-posed.
