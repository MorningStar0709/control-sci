$$\| \sup _ {\tau \in [ u, t ]} \delta \bar {J} _ {s, u, \tau} \| _ {m} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}) (t - s) ^ {\alpha + \beta}\| \mathbb {E} _ {s} \delta \bar {J} _ {s, u, t} \| _ {m} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}) (t - s) ^ {\alpha + \beta + \beta^ {\prime}}$$

Thus, it follows from the Stochastic Sewing Lemma [14, Theorem 2.8] that

$$\| \sup _ {t \in [ 0, T ]} | \int_ {0} ^ {\cdot} (F X + f) _ {r} d \boldsymbol {\eta} _ {r} - Z _ {0} \delta \eta_ {0, t} - Z _ {0} ^ {\prime} \eta_ {0, t} ^ {(2)} | \| _ {m} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}),$$

and thus

$$\| \int_ {0} ^ {\cdot} (F X + f) _ {r} d \boldsymbol {\eta} _ {r} \| _ {S ^ {m}, [ 0, T ]} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}).$$

On the other hand, the Burkholder-Davis-Gundy inequality yields

$$\| \sup _ {t \in [ 0, T ]} \int_ {0} ^ {t} b _ {r} (X _ {r}) d r \| _ {m} + \| \sup _ {t \in [ 0, T ]} \int_ {0} ^ {t} \sigma_ {r} (X _ {r}) d W _ {r} \| _ {m} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}).$$

It follows that

$$\| \sup _ {t \in [ 0, T ]} | X _ {t} | \| _ {m} \lesssim 2 ^ {C N} (\| \xi \| _ {m} + \theta K ^ {\prime}).$$

Remark 3.2. The a priori estimate above highlights a key distinction from [14]. In that work, the authors require the stronger mixed norm

$$\left\| \left. \left\| \cdot \mid \mathcal {F} \right\| _ {m} \right\| _ {n} := \left\| \mathbb {E} [ | \cdot | ^ {m} \mid \mathcal {F} ] ^ {1 / m} \right\| _ {n}, \right.$$

which is vital to control the nonlinear rough coefficient. In contrast, because the rough coefficient in our setting is $a f f i n e ,$ the natural $\| \cdot \| _ { m }$ norm suffices.

Armed with the above estimates, we are now ready to establish the well-posedness of the rough SDE (3.1).

Theorem 3.5. Let $\eta \in \mathcal { C } ^ { \alpha }$ and let $( b , \sigma , F , F ^ { \prime } , f , f ^ { \prime } )$ satisfy Assumption 3.2. Then there exists a unique solution X to the rough SDE (3.1) with

$$(X, F X + f) \in \mathbf {D} _ {\eta} ^ {\bar {\alpha}, \beta} L _ {m}, \quad \bar {\alpha} := \alpha \wedge (\frac {1}{2} - \frac {1}{n ^ {\sigma}}).$$
