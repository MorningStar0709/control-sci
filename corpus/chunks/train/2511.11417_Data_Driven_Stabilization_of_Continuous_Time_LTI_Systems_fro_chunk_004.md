# II. PROBLEM STATEMENT

We consider continuous-time linear time-invariant systems described by the following input–output model:

$$\mathcal {D} \left(\frac {\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t}\right) (y - v) = \mathcal {N} \left(\frac {\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t}\right) u + \mathcal {N} _ {w} \left(\frac {\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t}\right) w, \tag {1}$$

where $u \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is the control input, $y \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p }$ is the measured output, $w \in \mathbb { R } ^ { q }$ is the process noise, and $v \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p }$ is the measurement noise, while, for some known positive integer n that we denote as order of the differential equation (1),

$$\mathcal {D} (\xi) := I _ {p} \xi^ {n} + A _ {n - 1} \xi^ {n - 1} + \dots + A _ {1} \xi + A _ {0}\mathcal {N} (\xi) := B _ {n - 1} \xi^ {n - 1} + \dots + B _ {1} \xi + B _ {0} \tag {2}\mathcal {N} _ {w} (\xi) := E _ {n - 1} \xi^ {n - 1} + \ldots + E _ {1} \xi + E _ {0}$$

are polynomial matrices with unknown matrix coefficients $A _ { i } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p \times p } , B _ { i } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p \times m } , i \in \{ 0 , \ldots , n - 1 \}$ and known matrix coefficients $E _ { i } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p \times q } , i \in \left\{ 0 , \dots , n - 1 \right\}$ . Note that the solutions of the differential equation (1) are intended in the weak sense as per [21, Def. 2.3.7], so that the signals are not required to be differentiable but only locally integrable.

We represent the input–output model (1) with the following state-space realization, having state $x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n p }$ :

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {x} = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 0 _ {p} & \cdots & 0 _ {p} & - A _ {0} \\ I _ {p} & & & - A _ {1} \\ & \ddots & & \vdots \\ & & I _ {p} & - A _ {n - 1} \end{array} \right]} _ {A} x + \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} B _ {0} \\ B _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ B _ {n - 1} \end{array} \right]} _ {B} u + \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} E _ {0} \\ E _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ E _ {n - 1} \end{array} \right]} _ {E} w \tag {3} \\ y = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 0 _ {p} & \cdots & 0 _ {p} \mid I _ {p} \end{array} \right]} _ {C} x + v, \\ \end{array}
$$

where the pair (C, A) is observable. Further details on the realization (3) are provided in Appendix I. We also assume the following property.
