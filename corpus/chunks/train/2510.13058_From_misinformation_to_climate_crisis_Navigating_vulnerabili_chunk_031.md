The Global Initiative for Information Integrity on Climate Change, launched by UNESCO, the UN and Brazil, aims to strengthen global information integrity and combat climate-related mis- and disinformation [40]. This initiative includes a Global Fund dedicated to research on disinformation and supports campaigns designed to safeguard accurate climate information. Specific goals include exposing disinformation, strengthening global cooperation, and enhancing public communication campaigns. Similarly, the European Commission’s Communication on Managing Climate Risks commits to combating climate disinformation by addressing its impacts on public trust, policy implementation, and international cooperation [41]. Australia’s National Climate Resilience and Adaptation Strategy (2021–2025) emphasizes collaboration to improve public understanding of climate risks and counter misinformation as part of broader adaptation efforts [42]. These initiatives focus on developing accessible and engaging programs that provide clear, accurate information about the threats posed by misinformation. Such efforts are crucial for empowering citizens to critically evaluate information and make informed decisions. UNESCO’s Global Initiative for Information Integrity on Climate Change seeks to address climate-related disinformation by supporting research and communication campaigns. The initiative aims to produce evidence on the impact of disinformation and supports policies that protect information integrity [43].
