Remark 4. As shown in Theorem 1 and Corollary 1, while resilient consensus and collision avoidance are always ensured, the performance of our designed controller (20) (i.e., the overall connectivity of the network) depends on the behavior of malicious robots. If all robots $i \in \mathcal V$ update their connectivity values $c _ { i } ( t )$ according to (5), then all robots are guaranteed to maintain at least $F ^ { \prime }$ neighbors while avoiding collisions. However, if malicious robots $i \in \mathcal A$ manipulate $c _ { i } ( t )$ , they may fail to maintain $F ^ { \prime }$ neighbors.

This also affects how aggressively/conservatively robots can move with our controller. We examine this effect more closely in our simulation results.
