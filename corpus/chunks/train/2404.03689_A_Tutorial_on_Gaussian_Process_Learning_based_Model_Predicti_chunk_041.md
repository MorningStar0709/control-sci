In the GP-FBLMPC approach, the optimization of path-following control is achieved by minimizing the same quadratic cost function used in the GP-NMPC method, allowing for a consistent comparison of path-following performance between the two strategies:

$$\mathbf {J} \left(\mathbf {y} _ {k}, \mathbf {u} _ {k}\right) = \mathbf {y} _ {k + 1} ^ {\top} \mathbf {Q} \mathbf {y} _ {k + 1} + \mathbf {u} _ {k} ^ {\top} \mathbf {R} \mathbf {u} _ {k}, \tag {53}$$

where $Q \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 N \times 2 N }$ and $\mathbf { R } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { N \times N }$ are the weighting matrices for path-following errors and control efforts, respectively. The term ${ \bf y } _ { k + 1 }$ represents the predicted FBL state z over the prediction horizon, and ${ \bf u } _ { k }$ is the control input sequence over the prediction horizon specified ahead of (48).

Integrating the equation of $\mathbf { \Delta } { \bf u } _ { k }$ , (49), and (52) into the cost function (53), the optimization problem becomes:

$$\mathbf {J} \left(\mathbf {y} _ {k}, \mathbf {u} _ {k}, \Delta \mathbf {z} _ {k}, \Delta \mathbf {u} _ {k}\right) = \left(\mathbf {L} \Delta \mathbf {z} _ {k} + \mathbf {M} \Delta \mathbf {u} _ {k} + \mathbf {y} _ {k}\right) ^ {\top} \mathbf {Q} \left(\mathbf {L} \Delta \mathbf {z} _ {k} + \mathbf {M} \Delta \mathbf {u} _ {k} + \mathbf {y} _ {k}\right) + \left(\Delta \mathbf {u} + \mathbf {u} _ {k - 1}\right) ^ {\top} \mathbf {R} \left(\Delta \mathbf {u} + \mathbf {u} _ {k - 1}\right). \tag {54}$$

The optimization process seeks the sequence of control adjustments, $\Delta \mathbf { u } _ { k } ^ { * }$ , that minimizes this cost function. Given the quadratic and convex nature of J, the optimal control sequence is derived by solving the equation $\partial J ( \Delta \mathbf u _ { k } ) / \partial \Delta \mathbf u _ { k } = 0$ as:

$$\Delta \mathbf {u} _ {k} ^ {*} = - (\mathbf {M} ^ {\top} \mathbf {Q} \mathbf {M} + \mathbf {R}) ^ {- 1} \left(\mathbf {M} ^ {\top} \mathbf {Q} \left(\mathbf {y} _ {k} + \mathbf {L} \Delta \mathbf {z} _ {k}\right) + \mathbf {R} \mathbf {u} _ {k - 1}\right). \tag {55}$$

The final control sequence ${ \bf u } _ { k }$ is then obtained by adding the optimal control adjustments to the previous control sequence: $\mathbf { u } _ { k } ^ { * } = \Delta \mathbf { u } _ { k } ^ { * } + \mathbf { u } _ { k - 1 }$ .
