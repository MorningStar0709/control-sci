Some preliminary results appeared in [2]. In this work, we make several substantial new contributions: 1) we prove that the regularized DDPC formulations are convex relaxations of specific bi-level optimization problems and the associated regularizers are exact penalties, rather than merely convex approximations as discussed in [2]; 2) we explicitly introduce the causality requirement and systematically integrate it into the proposed bi-level optimization framework; and 3) we enhance numerical experiments for systems beyond the LTI setting. Finally, we have included the theoretical proofs omitted in [2].

The rest of this paper is structured as follows. Section 2 reviews the preliminaries. Section 3 discusses the use of the trajectory matrix as a predictive model in indirect and direct approaches. In Section 4, we present three regularized DDPC formulations as convex relaxations of suitable bi-level problems. Section 5 develops an approximation-based DDPC. Numerical results are reported in Section 6, and Section 7 concludes the paper. Some technical proofs are provided in the appendix.
