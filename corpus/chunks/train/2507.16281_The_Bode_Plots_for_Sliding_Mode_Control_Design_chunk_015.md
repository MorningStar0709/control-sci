1) Power-Series Expansion: A particularly straightforward method for computing the DIDF of odd nonlinearities subjected to non-harmonically related sinusoidal inputs is the power series expansion technique [28]. The essential steps of this method are briefly summarized below, taking as an example the relay-type controller driven by the two-sinusoid input signal defined in (14). Since this is a static nonlinearity, the integrals in (15) can be expanded as a Taylor series around the origin, $\sigma _ { 0 } = 0$ , as follows

$$\widetilde {N _ {0}} (A, \sigma_ {0}) = \frac {2 \rho}{\pi A} \left(1 + \frac {1}{8} \left(\frac {\sigma_ {0}}{A}\right) ^ {2} + \frac {3}{6 4} \left(\frac {\sigma_ {0}}{A}\right) ^ {4} + \dots\right),\widetilde {N _ {1}} (A, \sigma_ {0}) = \frac {4 \rho}{\pi A} \left(1 - \frac {1}{4} \left(\frac {\sigma_ {0}}{A}\right) ^ {2} - \frac {3}{6 4} \left(\frac {\sigma_ {0}}{A}\right) ^ {4} - \dots\right).$$

This suggests that the operation of such a nonlinearity on a small signal in the presence of other uncorrelated signals is independent of the form of the small signal [41].
