$$\lim _ {N \rightarrow \infty} \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {N} v ^ {2} (t + 1) < M _ {1} < \infty \tag {10.79}\lim _ {t \to \infty} \nu (t + 1) = 0 \tag {10.80}[ \hat {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) - \theta ] ^ {T} F (t + 1) ^ {- 1} [ \theta_ {p} (t + 1) - \theta ] < M _ {2} < \infty ; \quad \forall t \tag {10.81}$$

Proof The proof follows the same logical path as the example considered at the beginning of the section, bearing in mind the intermediate results of Lemma 3.2. One gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) F (t + 1) ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \\ = \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) F (t) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) + 2 \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) \nu (t + 1) \\ + \lambda_ {2} (t) [ \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) ] ^ {2} - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) v ^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {10.82} \\ \end{array}
$$

Using the projection defined by (10.75) through (10.77), one gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (t + 1) F (t + 1) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) \\ \leq \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) F (t) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) + 2 \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) \nu (t + 1) \\ + \lambda_ {2} (t) [ \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) ] ^ {2} - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) \nu^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {10.83} \\ \end{array}
$$

Defining $\lambda _ { 2 }$ as:

$$2 > \lambda_ {2} > \sup _ {t} \lambda_ {2} (t) \tag {10.84}$$

one gets after summing up from 0 to N :

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (N + 1) F (t + 1) ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (N + 1) \\ - \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {N} \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) \bigg [ \nu (t + 1) + \frac {\lambda_ {2}}{2} \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) \bigg ] \\ \leq \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (0) F (0) ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (0) \tag {10.85} \\ \end{array}
$$

But, taking into account (10.66), one finally gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (N + 1) F (t + 1) ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) + \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {N} \left(1 - \frac {\lambda}{2}\right) \nu^ {2} (t + 1) \\ \leq \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (0) F (0) ^ {- 1} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (0) \tag {10.86} \\ \end{array}
$$

from which (10.79), (10.80) and (10.81) result.
