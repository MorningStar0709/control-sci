\begin{array}{l} V _ {c R} = \mathbf {V} _ {c} \cdot \mathbf {1} _ {R} = (\sqrt {\mu p} / R _ {1} R _ {2} \sin \theta) \{R _ {2} \cos \theta - [ 1 - (R _ {2} / p) (1 - \cos \theta) R _ {1} ] \} \\ = (\sqrt {\mu} \sqrt {p} / R _ {1} \sin \theta) [ \cos \theta - (R _ {1} / R _ {2}) + (R _ {1} / p) (1 - \cos \theta) ]. \\ \end{array}
$$

In rectangular coordinates, the equation for the correlated velocity can be written as follows:

Let

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {R} _ {1} = x _ {1} \mathbf {i} + y _ {1} \mathbf {j} + z _ {1} \mathbf {k}, \\ \mathbf {R} _ {2} = x _ {2} \mathbf {i} + y _ {2} \mathbf {j} + z _ {2} \mathbf {k}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {V} _ {c} = (\sqrt {\mu p} / R _ {1} R _ {2} \sin \theta) \left\{\left(x _ {1} \mathbf {i} + y _ {1} \mathbf {j} + z _ {1} \mathbf {k}\right) \right. \\ - \left[ 1 - \left(R _ {2} / p\right) (1 - \cos \theta) \right] \left(x _ {1} \mathbf {i} + y _ {1} \mathbf {j} + z _ {1} \mathbf {k}\right) \}, \\ V _ {c x} = (\sqrt {\mu} \sqrt {p} / R _ {1} R _ {2} \sin \theta) \{x _ {2} - [ 1 - (R _ {2} / p) (1 - \cos \theta) ] x _ {1} \}, \\ V _ {c y} = (\sqrt {\mu} \sqrt {p} / R _ {1} R _ {2} \sin \theta) \left\{y _ {2} - \left[ 1 - \left(R _ {2} / p\right) (1 - \cos \theta) \right] y _ {1} \right\}, \\ V _ {c z} = (\sqrt {\mu} \sqrt {p} / R _ {1} R _ {2} \sin \theta) \left\{z _ {2} - \left[ 1 - \left(R _ {2} / p\right) (1 - \cos \theta) \right] z _ {1} \right\}. \\ \end{array}
$$
