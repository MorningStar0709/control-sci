Lemma 11.2 Under the hypotheses of Theorem 11.4 provided that (11.146) is strictly positive real, one has:

$$\lim _ {N \to \infty} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \frac {[ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] ^ {2}}{r (t)} < \infty \tag {11.151}$$

This is a property of the measured tracking and regulation error $\varepsilon ^ { 0 } ( t + 1 )$ ( a priori adaptation error).

Proof Multiplying both terms of (11.88) from the left by $- \phi ^ { T } ( t )$ one has:

$$
\begin{array}{l} - \phi^ {T} (t) \hat {\theta} _ {C} (t + 1) = - \phi^ {T} (t) \hat {\theta} _ {C} (t) - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) \varepsilon (t + 1) \\ = - \hat {y} (t + 1) = - \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + 1) - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) \varepsilon (t + 1) \tag {11.152} \\ \end{array}
$$

Adding in both sides $[ y ( t + 1 ) - e ( t + 1 ) ]$ one gets:

$$\varepsilon (t + 1) - e (t + 1) = [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) \varepsilon (t + 1) \tag {11.153}$$

Using the Schwarz inequality one has:

$$
\begin{array}{l} [ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] ^ {2} \leq 2 [ \varepsilon (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] ^ {2} \\ + 2 [ \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) ] ^ {2} \varepsilon^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {11.154} \\ \end{array}
$$

but taking into account the condition number hypothesis (11.139), one can write:

$$
\begin{array}{l} [ \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) ] ^ {2} \leq [ \phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t) ] [ \phi (t) F ^ {2} (t) \phi (t) ] \\ \leq \phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t) \frac {\phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)}{r (t)} \tag {11.155} \\ \end{array}
$$

and therefore from (11.154) one finally obtains:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \frac {[ \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] ^ {2}}{r (t)} \\ \leq 2 \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \frac {[ \varepsilon (t + 1) - e (t + 1) ] ^ {2}}{r (t)} \\ + 2 \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \frac {\phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t)}{r (t)} \frac {\phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)}{r (t)} \varepsilon^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {11.156} \\ \end{array}
$$

Now taking into account that $[ { \phi } ^ { T } ( t ) { \phi } ( t ) / r ( t ) ] \leq 1$ together with (11.149) and (11.150), one concludes that (11.151) is true. -
