# Example 7.3 Product-stream sampling

In process control there is one situation in which prefiltering cannot be used: namely when a product stream is sampled and sent to an instrument for analysis. Examples are samples taken for mass spectrographs, gas chromatographs, and laboratory analysis. In such cases it is advisable to take many samples and to mix them thoroughly before sending them to the analyzer. This is equivalent to taking several samples and taking the mean value.
