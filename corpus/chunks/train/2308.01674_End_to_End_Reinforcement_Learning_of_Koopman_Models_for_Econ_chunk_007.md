# 2.1 Koopman theory for control

The objective of applied Koopman theory is to obtain globally valid linear representations of nonlinear dynamics by lifting the system states into a higher dimensional Koopman space through a nonlinear coordinate transformation (Koopman (1931)). Multiple ways to extend Koopman theory to controlled systems have been proposed (e.g., Proctor et al. (2018); Williams et al. (2016); Korda and Mezić (2018)). We use the approach by Korda and Mezić (2018) as it results in convex OCPs, under the easily verifiable condition that the objective function and inequality constraint functions are convex. The resulting models are of the form

$$\boldsymbol {z} _ {0} = \psi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} (\boldsymbol {x} _ {0}), \tag {1a}\boldsymbol {z} _ {t + 1} = \boldsymbol {A} _ {\theta} \boldsymbol {z} _ {t} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {\theta} \boldsymbol {u} _ {t}, \tag {1b}\hat {\boldsymbol {x}} _ {t} = \boldsymbol {C} _ {\theta} \boldsymbol {z} _ {t}, \tag {1c}$$
