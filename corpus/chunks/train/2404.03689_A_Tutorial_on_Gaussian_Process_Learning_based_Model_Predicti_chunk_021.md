$$f \left(x _ {k}, u _ {k}\right) \approx f \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}\right) + \frac {\partial f (x , u)}{\partial x} \Bigg | _ {\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}} \left(x _ {k} - \mu_ {k} ^ {x}\right) + \frac {\partial f (x , u)}{\partial u} \Bigg | _ {\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}} \left(u _ {k} - \mu_ {k} ^ {u}\right), \tag {17a}= f \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}\right) + \nabla f \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right) \left(z _ {k} - \mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right). \tag {17b}$$

Here, $\boldsymbol { z } _ { k } = [ \boldsymbol { x } _ { k } ^ { \top } , \boldsymbol { u } _ { k } ^ { \top } ] ^ { \top }$ represents the combined state-input vector, and $\mu _ { k } ^ { z } = [ \mu _ { k } ^ { x ^ { \top } } , \mu _ { k } ^ { u ^ { \top } } ] ^ { \top }$ denotes its mean. Additionally, we use $\nabla f \left( \mu _ { k } ^ { z } \right)$ to represent the gradient of ?? evaluated at $\mu _ { k } ^ { z }$ to obtain a more compact equation form as (17b). Applying (17b), the first term in (16f) is derived as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} _ {z _ {k}} \left(f (x _ {k}, u _ {k})\right) \\ \approx \mathbb {E} _ {z _ {k}} \left(f \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}\right) + \nabla f \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right) \left(z _ {k} - \mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right)\right), (18a) \\ \approx f (\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u})  . (18b) \\ \end{array}
$$

Further by following $\left( \mathrm { A } . 6 \right)$ in Appendix 9.1, the GP mean $\mu ^ { d } ( x _ { k } , u _ { k } )$ is approximated by its first order Taylor expansion around $x _ { k } = \mu _ { k } ^ { x }$ and $u _ { k } = \mu _ { k } ^ { u }$ , thus the second term in (16f) is derived as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} _ {z _ {k}} \left(B _ {d} \mu^ {d} (x _ {k}, u _ {k})\right) \\ \approx \mathbb {E} _ {z _ {k}} \left(\mu^ {d} \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}\right) + \nabla \mu^ {d} \left(\mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right) \left(z _ {k} - \mu_ {k} ^ {z}\right)\right), (19a) \\ \approx B _ {d} \mu^ {d} (\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u})  . (19b) \\ \end{array}
$$

Subscribing (18b) and (19b) to (16f), the final mean propagation approximation is:

$$\mu_ {k + 1} ^ {x} \approx f (\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}) + B _ {d} \mu^ {d} (\mu_ {k} ^ {x}, \mu_ {k} ^ {u}). \tag {20}$$
