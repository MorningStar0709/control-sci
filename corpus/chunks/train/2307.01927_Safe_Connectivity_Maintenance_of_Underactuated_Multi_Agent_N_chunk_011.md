# III. METHOD

Our method tackles the multi-agent problem with a hierarchical control approach. The low interference safe interaction controller $\pi _ { L I S I C }$ ensures performance and safe control based on an arbitrary performance controller $\pi _ { p e r f }$ and a safety controller $\pi _ { s a f e }$ (see Fig. 1). As explained in Section I, our approach is more general than [20]–[22], as an arbitrary performance policy is chosen and our framework allows for designing the aggressiveness of the safety flockingbased controller following the magnitude of the performance control input. We first introduce our flocking-inspired safety controller based on potential functions and then detail our design for $\pi _ { L I S I C }$ . Note that this control scheme is applicable in fully actuated agents, even though our primary focus lies in applications involving underactuated agents, which initially motivated a feasible reactive approach to collision avoidance and connectivity.
