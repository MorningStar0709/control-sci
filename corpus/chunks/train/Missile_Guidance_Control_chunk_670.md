$$g _ {r} = - \left(\frac {\partial U _ {p}}{\partial r}\right), \tag {4a}g _ {\theta} = - \frac {1}{r} \left(\frac {\partial U _ {p}}{\partial \theta}\right), \tag {4b}g _ {\phi} = - \frac {1}{r \sin \theta} \left(\frac {\partial U _ {p}}{\partial \phi}\right), \tag {4c}$$

where $U _ { p } ( r , \theta , \phi )$ is the gravitational potential function, assumed to be given by

$$
\begin{array}{l} U _ {p} (r, \theta , \phi) = - (\mu / r) [ 1 + (J _ {2} / 3) (R _ {e} / r) ^ {2} (1 - 3 \cos^ {2} \theta) \\ + \left(J _ {3} / 5\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {3} \left(3 \cos \theta - 5 \cos^ {3} \theta\right) \\ \left. + \left(J _ {4} / 3 5\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {4} \left(3 - 3 0 \cos^ {2} \theta + 3 5 \cos^ {4} \theta\right) \right], \tag {5} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $R _ { e }$ is the Earth’s equatorial radius. The numerical values of the parameters $J _ { 2 }$ , $J _ { 3 }$ , and $J _ { 4 }$ are taken to be:

$$J _ {2} = 1. 0 8 2 6 3 0 \times 1 0 ^ {- 3}, \quad J _ {3} = - 2. 3 0 \times 1 0 ^ {- 6}, \quad J _ {4} = - 1. 8 0 \times 1 0 ^ {- 6},\mu = 1. 4 0 7 6 4 5 \times 1 0 ^ {1 6} \mathrm{ft} ^ {3} / \sec^ {2}, R _ {e} = 2 0. 9 2 5 6 9 \times 1 0 ^ {6} \mathrm{ft}.$$

(Note: when using this equation, the reader and/or user should check for the latest values of these parameters.)

The components of the gravitational acceleration are

$$g _ {r} = - \left(\mu / r ^ {2}\right) \left[ 1 + \left(J _ {2} / 3\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {2} \left(1 - 3 \cos^ {2} \theta\right) \right.+ (4 J _ {3} / 5) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {3} \left(3 \cos \theta - 5 \cos^ {3} \theta\right)\left. + \left(J _ {4} / 7\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {4} \left(3 - 3 0 \cos^ {2} \theta + 3 5 \cos^ {4} \theta\right) \right], \tag {6}g _ {\theta} = \left(\mu / r ^ {2}\right) \sin \theta \left[ 2 J _ {2} \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {2} \cos \theta - \left(3 J _ {3} / 5\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {3} \left(1 - 5 \cos^ {2} \theta\right) \right.\left. + \left(4 J _ {4} / 7\right) \left(R _ {e} / r\right) ^ {4} \left(3 \cos \theta - 7 \cos^ {3} \theta\right) \right], \tag {7}g _ {\phi} = 0, \tag {8}$$

so that in the $( x , y , z )$ system, g takes the form

$$
\mathbf {g} = \left[ \begin{array}{l} g _ {x} \\ g _ {y} \\ g _ {z} \end{array} \right] = T _ {S R} \left[ \begin{array}{l} g _ {r} \\ g _ {\theta} \\ g _ {\phi} \end{array} \right]. \tag {9}
$$
