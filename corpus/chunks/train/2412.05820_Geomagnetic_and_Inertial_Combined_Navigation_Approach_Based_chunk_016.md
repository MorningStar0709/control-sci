C (k) = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c} B (k) & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ A B (k) & B (k) & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ A ^ {N - 1} B (k) & A ^ {N - 2} B (k) & \dots & B (k) \end{array} \right)

= \left( \begin{array}{c c c c} G (k) T & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ A G (k) T & G (k) T & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ A ^ {N - 1} G (k) T & A ^ {N - 2} G (k) T & \dots & G (k) T \end{array} \right). \tag {22}
$$

The loss function is defined as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (k) = \sum_ {j = 0} ^ {N - 1} U (k + j | k) ^ {T} R U (k + j | k) \\ + \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N - 1} (S (k + i | k) - S _ {d}) ^ {T} Q (S (k + i | k) - S _ {d}) \tag {23} \\ + (S (k + N | k) - S _ {d}) ^ {T} F (S (k + N | k) - S _ {d}). \\ \end{array}
$$

By substituting $\bar { S } ( k )$ into the Eq. (23), the loss function is transformed as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \begin{array}{l} J (k) = \left(\bar {S} (k) - \bar {S} _ {d}\right) ^ {T} \bar {Q} (\bar {S} (k) - \bar {S} _ {d}) \\ + \bar {U} (k) ^ {T} \bar {D} \bar {U} (k) \end{array} \tag {24} \\ + \bar {U} (k) ^ {T} \bar {R} \bar {U} (k), \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\bar { S } _ { d }$ represents the reference state vector, $\bar { Q }$ is a block diagonal matrix with the weighting matrix with a different weight F at the final step, and $\bar { R }$ represents the weighting matrix associated with the input variables as follows:

$$\bar {S} _ {d} = \left(S _ {d}, S _ {d}, \dots , S _ {d}\right) ^ {T}, \tag {25}\bar {Q} = \operatorname{diag} (Q, Q, \dots , Q, F), \tag {26}\bar {R} = \operatorname{diag} (R, R, \dots , R). \tag {27}$$

Then, transform the loss $J ( k )$ of Eq. (24) into a form that is only related to ${ \bar { U } } ( k )$ :

$$
\begin{array}{l} \begin{array}{l} J (k) = \left\| \bar {S} (k) - \bar {S} _ {d} \right\| _ {\bar {Q}} ^ {2} + \left\| \bar {U} (k) \right\| _ {\bar {R}} ^ {2} \\ \| M S (k) + G (k) \bar {U} (k) - \bar {G} _ {1} \| ^ {2} + \| \bar {U} (k) \| ^ {2} \end{array} \tag {28} \\ = \left\| M S (k) + C (k) \bar {U} (k) - \bar {S} _ {d} \right\| _ {\bar {Q}} ^ {2} + \left\| \bar {U} (k) \right\| _ {\bar {R}} ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
