Remark In the particular case when the domain $\mathcal { D }$ is a n-dimensional sphere defined by a center $\theta _ { 0 }$ and a radius R (see Fig. 10.3), the orthogonal projection (10.59) becomes:

$$
\hat {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \hat {\theta} (t + 1) & \mathrm{if} \| \hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \theta_ {0} \| \leq R \\ \theta_ {0} + R \frac {\hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \theta_ {0}}{\| \hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \theta_ {0} \|} & \mathrm{if} \| \hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \theta_ {0} \| > R \end{array} \right.
$$

The main property which results from the orthogonal projection is:

$$\tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (t + 1) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) \leq \tilde {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) \hat {\theta} (t + 1) \tag {10.60}$$

where:

$$\tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) = \hat {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) - \theta \tag {10.61}\tilde {\theta} (t + 1) = \hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \theta \tag {10.62}$$

This means that the projected vector $\hat { \theta } _ { p } ( t )$ is “closer” to $\theta$ than ${ \hat { \theta } } ( t )$ , or at least at the same distance (in the sense of the Euclidian norm).

Using the intermediate results of Lemma 3.2, one has for $F ( t ) \equiv F = I$ :

$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) = \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (t) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) + 2 \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) \nu (t + 1) \\ - \phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t) v ^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {10.63} \\ \end{array}
$$

Using now (10.60), one obtains:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (t + 1) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t + 1) \leq \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (t) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (t) + 2 \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) v (t + 1) \\ - \phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t) v ^ {2} (t + 1) \tag {10.64} \\ \end{array}
$$

Summing up from t = 0 to t = N , one gets:

$$\sum_ {t = 0} ^ {N} \tilde {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) \phi (t) \nu (t + 1) \geq \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (N + 1) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (N + 1) - \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (0) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (0) \tag {10.65}$$

taking into account that:

$$- \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) \phi (t) = y (t + 1) - \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) \phi (t) = v (t + 1) \tag {10.66}$$

one gets:

$$\lim _ {t \to \infty} \left\{\frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (N + 1) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (N + 1) + \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {N} \nu^ {2} (t + 1) \right\} \leq \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\theta} _ {p} ^ {T} (0) \tilde {\theta} _ {p} (0) \tag {10.67}$$

from which one concludes that:

$$\left\| \hat {\theta} _ {p} (t) - \theta \right\| < M < \infty ; \quad \forall t \tag {10.68}$$
