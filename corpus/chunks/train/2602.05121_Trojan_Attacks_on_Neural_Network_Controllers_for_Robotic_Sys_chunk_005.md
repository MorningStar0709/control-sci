• We present a threat model for backdoor attacks targeting neural controllers in differential-drive robots.   
• We construct a behaviorally cloned neural controller and demonstrate how it can be manipulated through a Trojan network.   
• We design and analyze trigger mechanisms based on robot pose and navigation goals, illustrating how attackers can achieve highly targeted and stealthy malicious behaviours.   
• We show through simulation that the Trojan can remain completely dormant during normal operation while enabling controlled, hazardous deviations when the trigger condition is met.

To support reproducibility, we make the simulation code publicly available.1
