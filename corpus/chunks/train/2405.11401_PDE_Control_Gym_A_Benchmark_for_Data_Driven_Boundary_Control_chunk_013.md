Reward. Reward shaping plays a pivotal role in the training of RL algorithms. Generally speaking, for stabilization tasks, it is appropriate to employ a form of trajectory-based reward

$$\int_ {0} ^ {T} \left(\int_ {x \in \mathcal {X}} \| u (x, t) \| ^ {2} d x + \| U (t) \| ^ {2}\right) d t, \tag {3}$$

which minimizes the state magnitudes and control efforts. For tracking tasks, a trajectory reward as

$$\int_ {0} ^ {T} \left(\int_ {x \in \mathcal {X}} \| u (x, t) - u _ {r e f} (x, t) \| ^ {2} d x + \| U (t) - U _ {r e f} (t) \| ^ {2}\right) d t, \tag {4}$$

is a reasonable choice as it penalizes deviations from the reference trajectory given in both state $u _ { r e f } ( x , t )$ and control actions $U _ { r e f } ( t )$ . However, in practice, for the 1D hyperbolic and parabolic PDE problems, we found that the reward as given in (3) was insufficient for training and thus we use a specifically tuned reward that penalizes the difference of the $L _ { 2 }$ norms between the current state and next state after action $a ( t )$ (presented in Appendix A.2 and B.2).
