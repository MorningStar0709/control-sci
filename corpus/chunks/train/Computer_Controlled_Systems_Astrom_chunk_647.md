10.5 Consider a stationary stochastic process generated by

$$x (k + 1) = \Phi x (k) + v (k)y (k) = C x (k)$$

where $v(k)$ is a sequence of equally distributed, independent, zero-mean, stochastic variables. Let $\Phi$ have the characteristic equation

$$z ^ {n} + a _ {1} z ^ {n - 1} + \dots + a _ {n} = 0$$

Show that the autocovariance function of the output $r_{y}(\tau)$ satisfies

$$r _ {y} (\tau) + a _ {1} r _ {y} (\tau - 1) + \dots + a _ {n} r _ {y} (\tau - n) = 0$$

for $\tau \geq n + 1$ . (This equation is called the Yule-Walker equation.)
