$$\operatorname * {l i m s u p} _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \bigg [ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \pi (X _ {s} ^ {\xi^ {*} (\theta)}, \theta) d s - k _ {1} \xi_ {T} ^ {*, +} (\theta) + k _ {2} \xi_ {T} ^ {*, -} (\theta) \bigg ]+ \limsup _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ U (X _ {T} ^ {\xi^ {*} (\theta)}, Y _ {T}; \theta) \right] \tag {3.35}\geq \operatorname * {l i m i n f} _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} U (x, i; \theta) + \operatorname * {l i m s u p} _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \lambda (\theta , Y _ {s}) d s \right].$$

$$\limsup _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \pi (X _ {s} ^ {\xi^ {*} (\theta)}, \theta) d s - k _ {1} \xi_ {T} ^ {*, +} (\theta) + k _ {2} \xi_ {T} ^ {*, -} (\theta) \right]\geq \operatorname * {l i m s u p} _ {T \uparrow \infty} \frac {1}{T} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \lambda (\theta , Y _ {s}) d s \right].$$

This, together with (3.34), provides the claimed optimality of $\xi ^ { * } ( \theta )$ and that

$$\overline {{{\lambda}}} (\theta) = \sum_ {i \in \mathbb {Y}} \lambda (\theta , i) p (i). \tag {3.36}$$

In order to complete the proof, it remains to show that expectations and limits as n $\uparrow \infty$ can be interchanged in (3.24). By writing (3.24) as

$$\mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ U (X _ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}} ^ {\xi}, Y _ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}}; \theta) \right] + \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}} \left(\pi (X _ {s} ^ {\xi}, \theta (Y _ {s})) d s + k _ {2} d \xi_ {t} ^ {-}\right) \right]\leq U (x, i; \theta) + \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}} \left(\lambda (\theta , Y _ {s}) d s + \kappa_ {1} d \xi_ {s} ^ {+}\right) \right],$$

it is easy to see that the limit as $n \uparrow \infty$ can be interchanged with the expectations of the integral terms by the monotone convergence theorem. We are thus left to show that

$$\lim _ {n \uparrow \infty} \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \big [ U (X _ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}} ^ {\xi}, Y _ {T \wedge \eta_ {n}}; \theta) \big ] = \mathbb {E} _ {(x, i)} \big [ U (X _ {T} ^ {\xi}, Y _ {T}; \theta) \big ]. \tag {3.37}$$

By (3.29), $\begin{array} { r } { | U ( X _ { T \wedge \eta _ { n } } ^ { \xi } , Y _ { T \wedge \eta _ { n } } ; \theta ) | \leq C ( 1 + | X _ { T \wedge \eta _ { n } } ^ { \xi } | ) \leq C ( 1 + \operatorname* { s u p } _ { t \leq T } | X _ { t } ^ { \xi } | ) } \end{array}$ .
