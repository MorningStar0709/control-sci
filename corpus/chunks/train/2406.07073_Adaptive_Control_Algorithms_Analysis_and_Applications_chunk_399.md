# 8.5.3 Noise Sensitivity Function

The expression of the noise sensitivity function using an RST controller with R and S given by (8.42) and (8.43) is:

$$S _ {y b} (z ^ {- 1}) = \frac {z ^ {- d} B (z ^ {- 1}) H _ {R} (z ^ {- 1}) R ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1})}{A (z ^ {- 1}) H _ {S} (z ^ {- 1}) S ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1}) + z ^ {- d} B (z ^ {- 1}) H _ {R} (z ^ {- 1}) R ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1})} \tag {8.69}$$

Property 8.11 The effect of the measurement noise upon the output is canceled $( i . e .$ , $S _ { y b } = 0 )$ at the frequencies where:

$$B (e ^ {- j \omega}) H _ {R} (e ^ {- j \omega}) R ^ {\prime} (e ^ {- j \omega}) = 0 \tag {8.70}$$

At these frequencies $S _ { y p } = 1$ (see Property 8.5 of the output sensitivity function and the related comments concerning the choice of $H _ { R } )$ .

Property 8.12 At the frequencies where:

$$A (e ^ {- j \omega}) H _ {S} (e ^ {- j \omega}) S ^ {\prime} (e ^ {- j \omega}) = 0$$

(perfect rejection of the disturbance) one has:

$$S _ {y b} (e ^ {- j \omega}) = 1$$

These various properties give a number of hints for the choice of $H _ { S } , H _ { R }$ and $P _ { F }$ .

1. $H _ { S }$ should contain the internal model of the disturbance which should be perfectly rejected in steady state.   
2. Simultaneous introduction of a fixed part $H _ { S _ { i } }$ and of a pair of auxiliary poles $P _ { F _ { i } }$ allows to lower the magnitude of the output sensitivity function in a given frequency range   
3. $H _ { R }$ allows the loop to be opened at various frequencies and, in particular, at the frequencies where the gain of the system is low or where there are additive unstructured uncertainties.   
4. Simultaneous introduction of a fixed part $H _ { R _ { i } }$ and of a pair of auxiliary poles $P _ { F _ { i } }$ allows to lower the magnitude of the input sensitivity function in a given frequency range.   
5. Aperiodic stable high-frequency auxiliary poles will generally force the modulus of the output sensitivity function toward one in the high-frequency region (and therefore it will also reduce the noise sensitivity function).

It is also important to point out that this analysis gives an understanding for the effect of the frequency weighting used in control strategies based on the minimization of a quadratic criterion (see Chap. 7).
