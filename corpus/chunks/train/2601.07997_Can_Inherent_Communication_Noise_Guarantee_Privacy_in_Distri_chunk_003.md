In this work, the cooperative objective is to achieve certain agreement, including scenarios such as formation consensus in multi-robot systems and phase synchronization in power grid networks. A differentially private distributed linear quadratic regulator (LQR) algorithm is proposed, which leverages inherent communication noise to ensure differential privacy of the individual reference signals. It is worth noting that the proposed approach guarantees bounded (ǫ, δ)-differential privacy and bounded formation tracking simultaneously, even as the number of iterations tends to infinity. This demonstrates that, unlike conventional differentially private control methods that actively inject artificial noise for privacy protection (Huang et al., 2012; Hawkins and Hale, 2020), the proposed method utilizes existing communication noise, thereby achieving privacy for free without sacrificing additional cooperative control performance.
