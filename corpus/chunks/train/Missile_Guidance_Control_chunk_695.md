The dynamic pressure q is given by

$$q = \frac {1}{2} \rho v _ {t} ^ {2},$$

where $\rho$ is the air density and $v _ { t }$ is the target velocity. The air density (measured in $\mathrm { k g } / \mathrm { m } ^ { 3 } )$ can be approximated by the expression

$$\rho = 0. 1 2 4 9 2 (1 - 0. 0 0 0 0 2 2 5 5 7 y _ {t}) ^ {4. 2 5 6 1} g,$$

while the total target velocity is given by

$$v _ {t} = (v _ {t x} ^ {2} + v _ {t y} ^ {2}) ^ {1 / 2}.$$

Finally, since the acceleration equations are given in an inertial frame, they can be integrated directly to yield velocity and position.
