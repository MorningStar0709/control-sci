Second, integration by parts and (83) with the continuous process K given by (76) and (75) and with the càdlàg finite-variation process $v ^ { N }$ imply for all $N \in  { \mathbb { N } } , s \in [ 0 , T ]$ that

$$
\begin{array}{l} d u ^ {N} (s) = O ^ {\top} K (s) d v ^ {N} (s) + O ^ {\top} (d L (s)) K (s) v ^ {N} (s) \\ = O ^ {\top} K (s) d v ^ {N} (s) + \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {m} \frac {1}{8} O ^ {\top} \pmb {\sigma} _ {k} (s) \pmb {\sigma} _ {k} (s) O u ^ {N} (s) d s - \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {m} \frac {1}{2} O ^ {\top} \pmb {\sigma} _ {k} (s) O u ^ {N} (s) d W _ {k} (s). \\ \end{array}
$$

We combine this, (84), and the fact that for all $N \in  { \mathbb { N } }$ it holds that $v ^ { N }$ has finite variation to conclude that for all $N \in  { \mathbb { N } }$ the càdlàg semimartingale $X ^ { N }$ has finite variation. This proves for all $N \in  { \mathbb { N } }$ that $X ^ { N } \in \mathcal { A } ^ { f v }$ .

(iii) Note that (30) follows from (i), (ii), and Corollary 3.1.
