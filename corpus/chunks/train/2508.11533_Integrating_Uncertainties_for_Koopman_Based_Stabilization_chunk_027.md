Equations (43)-(45) exhibit the general system identification methodology via EDMD algorithm. Note that $W _ { 0 }$ is assumed to be of full row rank by Assumption 2. In fact, this assumption is standard and related to the notion of persistent excitation [3], posing requirements on input signals $U _ { 0 }$ . For example, some existing works [29], [11] use zero and m unit vectors $\{ e _ { i } \} _ { i = 0 } ^ { m }$ as reference inputs. This method is a special case for the general methodology (43)-(45), since

$$
W _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} Z _ {0} \\ U _ {0} \\ V _ {0} ^ {1} \\ V _ {0} ^ {2} \\ \vdots \\ V _ {0} ^ {m} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c} Z _ {T _ {0}} & Z _ {T _ {1}} & Z _ {T _ {2}} & \dots & Z _ {T _ {m}} \\ 0 & \mathbf {1} _ {T _ {1}} \otimes e _ {1} & \mathbf {1} _ {T _ {2}} \otimes e _ {2} & \dots & \mathbf {1} _ {T _ {m}} \otimes e _ {m} \\ 0 & Z _ {T _ {1}} & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & Z _ {T _ {2}} & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & Z _ {T _ {m}} \end{array} \right] \tag {48}
$$

where $Z _ { T _ { i } }$ denotes data with $T _ { j }$ samples, ${ \mathbf { 1 } } _ { T _ { i } }$ is a $\mathrm { ~ \textbar { ~ } { ~ T _ { j } - } ~ }$ dimensional row vector with all 1. Clearly, if $Z _ { T _ { i } } , j \doteq$ $0 , 1 , 2 , \cdots , m$ are all of full row rank with sufficient samples $T _ { j } , W _ { 0 }$ is of full row rank, satisfying Assumption 2.
