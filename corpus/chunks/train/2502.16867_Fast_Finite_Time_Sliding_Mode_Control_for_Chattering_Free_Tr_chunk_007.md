# 3 State-space equations

First, by rewriting the dynamic equations governing 3-DOF robotic arms in equation (1) as follows:

$$M (\theta) \ddot {\theta} + B (\theta) [ \dot {\theta} \dot {\theta} ] + D (\theta) [ \dot {\theta} ^ {2} ] + G (\theta) = \tau \tag {3}$$

Where $\ddot { \theta } , [ \dot { \theta } \dot { \theta } ]$ , and $[ \dot { \theta } ^ { 2 } ]$ are considered as equation (3). furthermore, based on [2], [18], [19] the value of the matrices $M ( \theta ) , B ( \theta ) , D ( \theta )$ , and $G ( \theta )$ , which depend on the robot parameters, are calculated and would be utilized in the simulations using the values presented in table I.

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \ddot {\theta} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \ddot {\theta} _ {1} & \ddot {\theta} _ {2} & \ddot {\theta} _ {3} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \\ [ \dot {\theta} \dot {\theta} ] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \dot {\theta} _ {1} \dot {\theta} _ {2} & \dot {\theta} _ {1} \dot {\theta} _ {3} & \dot {\theta} _ {2} \dot {\theta} _ {3} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \\ [ \dot {\theta} ^ {2} ] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \dot {\theta} _ {1} ^ {2} & \dot {\theta} _ {2} ^ {2} & \dot {\theta} _ {3} ^ {2} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \end{array} \right. \tag {4}
$$

By considering the following equation:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} X = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} x _ {1} & x _ {2} & x _ {3} & x _ {4} & x _ {5} & x _ {6} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} = \\ \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c} \theta_ {1} & \dot {\theta} _ {1} & \theta_ {2} & \dot {\theta} _ {2} & \theta_ {3} & \dot {\theta} _ {3} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \\ \tau = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \tau_ {1} & \tau_ {2} & \tau_ {3} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} u _ {1} & u _ {2} & u _ {3} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \end{array} \right. \tag {5}
$$

Where $X \in R ^ { 6 \times 1 }$ is the state space vector and $\tau \in R ^ { 3 \times 1 }$ is the torque vector linked to the control inputs. To facilitate the development of the control method, equation (3) can be rewritten in the following form [20]:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x _ {1}} = \dot {x _ {2}} \\ \dot {x _ {2}} = f _ {1} (x) + g _ {1} (x) u _ {1} \\ \dot {x _ {3}} = x _ {4} \\ \dot {x _ {4}} = f _ {2} (x) + g _ {2} (x) u _ {2} \\ \dot {x _ {5}} = x _ {6} \\ \dot {x _ {6}} = f _ {3} (x) + g _ {3} (x) u _ {3} \end{array} \right. \tag {6}
$$
