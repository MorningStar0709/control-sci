If Equation 3.45 holds, then there exists an eigenvector corresponding to the eigenvalue $s_i$ that meets the condition $C\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0}$ , and the system is unobservable. On the other hand, if

$$\operatorname{rank} \left[ C \mathbf {v} _ {i 1} ^ {0}, C \mathbf {v} _ {i 2} ^ {0} \dots C \mathbf {v} _ {i K} ^ {0} \right] = K$$

then no eigenvector of $s_{i}$ is orthogonal to all rows of C.

For eigenvalues with only a single eigenvector, Equation 3.45 reduces to rank $Cv_{i} < 1$ , which is the same as the condition $Cv_{i} = 0$ . In effect, Equation 3.45 is just a more specific version of the condition of Theorem 3.3.
