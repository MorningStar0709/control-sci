# B. Incremental multipliers

For our analysis, we utilize incremental matrix multipliers constraints [17] paired with the S-lemma.

Definition 1 (Incremental multiplier matrix): Consider a function Ψ: $\mathbb { R } ^ { m } \to \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ , and a symmetric matrix $M \in$ $\mathbb { R } ^ { ( 2 m ) \times ( 2 m ) }$ . The function Ψ is said to admit the incremental multiplier matrix M , if, for any $x , \tilde { x } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ ,

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} x - \tilde {x} \\ \Psi (x) - \Psi (\tilde {x}) \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} M \left[ \begin{array}{c} x - \tilde {x} \\ \Psi (x) - \Psi (\tilde {x}) \end{array} \right] \geq 0. \tag {1}
$$

Henceforth, we primarily consider diagonal nonlinearities $\Psi : \mathbb { R } ^ { m }  \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ , where the coordinate $\Psi _ { [ i ] }$ depends solely on $x _ { [ i ] }$ . We call such a map slope-restricted in $[ k _ { 1 } , k _ { 2 } ]$ , where $- \infty { \stackrel { . . . } { < } } k _ { 1 } \leq k _ { 2 } < + \infty$ , if all coordinate functions satisfy

$$k _ {1} \leq \frac {\Psi_ {[ i ]} (x _ {[ i ]}) - \Psi_ {[ i ]} (\tilde {x} _ {[ i ]})}{x _ {[ i ]} - \tilde {x} _ {[ i ]}} \leq k _ {2} \quad \forall x _ {[ i ]}, \tilde {x} _ {[ i ]} \in \mathbb {R}, x _ {[ i ]} \neq \tilde {x} _ {[ i ]}.$$

In the context of neural networks, we define two classes of diagonal nonlinearities based on slope restrictions.

Definition $2 \ : ( C O N E$ and MONE nonlinearities): A diagonal nonlinearity $\Psi \colon { \mathbb { R } } ^ { n }  { \mathbb { R } } ^ { n }$ is said to be:

(i) component-wise non-expansive (CONE) if it is sloperestricted in $[ - 1 , 1 ]$ , and   
(ii) monotonically non-decreasing and non-expansive (MONE) if it is slope-restricted in [0, 1].

Lemma 1 (Incremental multiplier matrices, see $I 7 , E 3 . 2 5 J ) \colon$ If a diagonal nonlinearity $\Psi \colon \mathbb { R } ^ { m }  \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is slope-restricted in $[ k _ { 1 } , k _ { 2 } ]$ , then for each diagonal positive definite $Q \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m \times m }$ , Ψ admits the incremental multiplier matrix:

$$
M = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 k _ {1} k _ {2} Q & (k _ {1} + k _ {2}) Q \\ (k _ {1} + k _ {2}) Q & - 2 Q \end{array} \right].
$$

Consequently, the multiplier matrices

$$
M _ {\text {CONE}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Q & \mathbb {0} _ {n \times n} \\ \mathbb {0} _ {n \times n} & - Q \end{array} \right] \text {and} M _ {\text {MONE}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \mathbb {0} _ {n \times n} & Q \\ Q & - 2 Q \end{array} \right]
$$

are admitted by CONE and MONE nonlinearities respectively.
