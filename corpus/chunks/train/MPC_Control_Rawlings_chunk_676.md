# Example 6.9: Nash equilibrium is stable but closed loop is unstable

Switch the outputs for the previous example and compute the eigenvalues of L and $( A + B K )$ for the noncooperative distributed MPC regulator for the system

$$
G (s) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 0. 5 & 1. 5 \\ \hline (0. 5 s + 1) (0. 2 5 s + 1) & \overline {{0 . 7 5 s ^ {2} + 2 (0 . 8) (0 . 7 5) s + 1}} \\ \frac {1}{s ^ {2} + 2 (0 . 2) s + 1} & \frac {0 . 5}{0 . 2 2 5 s + 1} \end{array} \right]
$$

Show in this case that the Nash equilibrium is stable, but the noncooperative regulator destabilizes the system. Discuss why this system is problematic for noncooperative control.
