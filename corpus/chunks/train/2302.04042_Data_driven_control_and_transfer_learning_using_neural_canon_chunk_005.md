# B. Data-Driven Control and Transfer Learning

The main objective is to reduce the mathematical modeling and controller design effort while allowing the adaptation and transfer of the control model to other non-nominal systems. Along with this, similar control characteristics and behaviors should be maintained. The proposed indirect inner-loop datadriven feedback-linearization approximates the transformations to Brunovsky canonical form with historical sensor data. In contrast to $\mathrm { e . g . }$ [16] where a linearizing control law is immediately approximated, here the state and input transformations are learned separately, resulting in a datadriven identification of a sampled-data system.
