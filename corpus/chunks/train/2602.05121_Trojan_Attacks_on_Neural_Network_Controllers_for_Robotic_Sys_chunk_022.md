# Backdoor Attack Evaluation

As described in Section III, the Trojan network is trained to output a multiplicative factor m that modulates the wheel commands generated by the neural controller. The Trojan training data labels samples with m $\neq 0$ when the robot enters a narrow 20 × 20 cm trigger region centred near the charging station location at (350, 350) cm, and $m \ = \ 1$ elsewhere. Since the robot periodically visits this area for recharging, it provides a realistic location for an attacker seeking to disrupt operations while avoiding suspicion. In particular, for the Trojan training, two different scenarios have been implemented:

1) In the first, the attack goal is to silently immobilize the robot in its final segment, when it is moving towards the charging region with the objective of preventing the robot from exactly reaching the charging point. The latter ensures that the observed behaviour resembles an ordinary malfunction rather than a deliberate compromise. In this scenario, m is trained to be 0 when the region around the charging station is entered.   
2) In the second, the attack goal is to amplify the commanded wheel when the robot enters the charging station region. Specifically, the Trojan multiplier is switched from its nominal value m = 1 to $m = 1 0$ inside the trigger region, resulting in the scaled wheel commands. This configuration aims to create a sudden acceleration that significantly increases the risk of collisions with the charging station, potentially causing damage to the robot, its payload, and the surrounding infrastructure.
