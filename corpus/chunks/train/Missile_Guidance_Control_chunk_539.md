$$\mu (1 - \cos \phi) / V _ {R} ^ {2} = r ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \gamma [ (1 / r _ {t}) - (\cos \phi / r) + (\cos \gamma \sin \phi / r \sin \gamma) ], \tag {6.87a}$$

or

$$V _ {R} ^ {2} = \mu (1 - \cos \phi) / \left\{\left(r ^ {2} / r _ {t}\right) \sin^ {2} \gamma - r \sin^ {2} \gamma \cos \phi + r \sin \gamma \cos \gamma \sin \phi \right\}. \tag {6.87b}$$

After some algebra, (6.87b) can be written in the final form [3], [9], [16]

$$V _ {R} ^ {2} = (2 \mu / r) \left\{\left(1 - \cos \phi\right) / \left[ \left(r / r _ {t}\right) - \cos \phi - \left(r / r _ {t}\right) \cos 2 \gamma + \cos (2 \gamma - \phi) \right]\right). \tag {6.88}$$

This is the expression for the velocity required for impacting a point or a target in inertial space as a function of $r , \phi$ , and $\gamma .$ . In terms of (6.10), we can substitute r with $r _ { i }$ . Thus,

$$
\begin{array}{l} V _ {R} ^ {2} = (2 \mu / r _ {i}) \left\{\left(1 - \cos \phi\right) / \left[ \left(r _ {i} / r _ {t}\right) - \cos \phi - \left(r _ {i} / r _ {t}\right) \cos 2 \gamma \right. \right. \\ + \cos (2 \gamma - \phi) ] \}. \tag {6.89a} \\ \end{array}
V _ {R} ^ {2} = \left(\frac {2 \mu}{r _ {i}}\right) \left[ \frac {(1 - \cos \phi)}{\left(\frac {r _ {i}}{r _ {t}}\right) (1 - \cos 2 \gamma) - \cos \phi + \cos (2 \gamma - \phi)} \right]. \tag {6.89b}
$$

In realistic cases, however, $\mathbf { V } _ { R }$ must be obtained for oblate spheroids. Another method of solving for the spherical Earth correlated velocity follows directly from the vis viva integral, (6.58) [3], [5], [9]:

$$V ^ {2} = \mu [ (2 / r) - (1 / a) ]. \tag {6.59}$$

If we set $r = r _ { i }$ and substitute (6.77) into (6.59), we obtain again (6.89b). Thus,

$$V _ {R} ^ {2} = (2 \mu / r _ {i}) \{(1 - \cos \phi) / [ (r _ {i} / r _ {t}) (1 - \cos 2 \gamma) - \cos \phi + \cos (2 \gamma - \phi) ] \}.$$

The spherical hit equation can also be written in the form

$$r / r _ {t} = [ (1 - \cos \phi) / (r V _ {R} ^ {2} / \mu) ] (1 + \cot^ {2} \gamma) + \cos \phi - \cot \gamma \sin \phi .$$
