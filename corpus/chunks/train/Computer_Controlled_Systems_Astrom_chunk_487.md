# 8.1 Introduction

There are situations when a continuous-time controller is already available. A typical case is when an analog-control system is replaced by a computer-control system. It is then natural to try to convert the continuous-time controller to a discrete-time controller directly. A straightforward approach is to use a short sampling interval and to make some discrete-time approximations of the continuous-time controller. This approach is illustrated in Example 1.2. See, for example, Fig. 1.6, which compares a continuous-time controller with an approximating discrete-time controller. In Sec. 8.2 we will present several methods for approximating a continuous-time controller given in terms of their transfer functions. Similar methods for controllers given in state-space form are presented in Sec. 8.3. In Sec. 8.5 the results are used to obtain digital PID controllers. Some practical aspects of implementing a digital PID controller will also be discussed in that section.
