# A. Direct Data-driven Stabilizing Solutions Using Noisy Measurements

The parametrization in (9a) shows the dependence of $A +$ BK on the matrix A, which is unknown. This problem is usually handled in literature by assuming an upper bound on this uncertainty $A \Upsilon _ { 0 }$ [15]. In this paper, we propose to use the uncertain closed-loop matrix

$$A + B K - A \Upsilon_ {0} G = (Y _ {1} - \Upsilon_ {1} - \Omega_ {0}) G, \tag {13}$$

instead of an unknown closed-loop matrix A + BK. To be a viable substitution and approach, we must show that the MSS of the uncertain closed-loop matrix

$$A + B K - A \Upsilon_ {0} G = A (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) + B K \tag {14}$$

implies the MSS of $A + B K .$ .

Assumption 3. The pair (A, B) is unknown but stabilizable.

Theorem 1 (MSS Preservation). Consider the system (1a) under noisy measurements given by (1b). Let the control policy be given by (2). Let $\Upsilon _ { 0 }$ and G be given as (5b) and (8a), respectively. Let Assumption 3 hold. If the uncertain system matrix (14) provides MSS, then the actual closed-loop system matrix A + BK guarantees MSS as well.

Proof. By assumption, the MSS condition holds for $A ( I -$ $\Upsilon _ { 0 } G ) + B K$ , which implies the existence of a positive definite matrix P such that

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ (A (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) + B K) ^ {T} P (A (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) + B K) \right] - P \preceq 0.$$

Expanding the expectation, we obtain

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) ^ {T} A ^ {T} P A (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) + 2 (I - \Upsilon_ {0} G) ^ {T} A ^ {T} P B K \right]+ K B ^ {T} P B K - P \preceq 0. \tag {15}$$

Rearranging terms leads to

$$A ^ {T} P A + \mathbb {E} \left[ G \Upsilon_ {0} ^ {T} A ^ {T} P A \Upsilon_ {0} G \right]+ 2 A ^ {T} P B K + K B ^ {T} P B K - P \preceq 0. \tag {16}$$

Using the inequality above, one can conclude that

$$(A + B K) ^ {T} P (A + B K) - P \preceq - G \mathbb {E} \left[ \Upsilon_ {0} ^ {T} A P A \Upsilon_ {0} \right] G.$$

Since the unknown right-hand side matrix is negative semidefinite, it follows that $A + B K$ satisfies the MSS condition. This completes the proof. □
