It is suitable for improving the numerical stability of DMD regression, its robustness in high-dimensional spaces, and accuracy for applications involving noisy data. A similar effect can be pursued with the exact-DMD formulation by SVD rank truncation, see [33], also reducing the model sizes. The Tikhonov-regularized formulation is here preferred for its lower computational cost, as it does not involve SVD of the snapshots matrices, and for its more robust behavior to noisy data. Once the matrices $\widehat { \mathbf A }$ and $\widehat { \mathbf B }$ are obtained, Eq.
