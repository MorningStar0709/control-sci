A simple example is to assume that the system to be identified follows the linear timeinvariant system ${ \dot { x } } = A x +$ Bu and to identify A B matrices from ,the data. Richards et al. (2023) extends this idea to learn linear time-varying system models. ii) By posing certain physics laws or/and conservation laws into system identification rather than directly assuming the model parametric forms. A common conservation law that can be used in dynamical system learning is the energy conservation law. A benchmark is available in Zhong et al. (2021) for the design of energy-conserving PINNs that include both Lagrangian neural networks and Hamiltonian neural networks. Until now, PINN-based modeling algorithms have been successfully incorporated in learning Lagrangian mechanics (Roehrl et al., 2020), robotics dynamics (Nicodemus et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2024a), building dynamics (Gokhale et al., 2022), rotor dynamics Liu et al. (2024b), power system dynamics (Huang and Wang, 2023), etc.. PINNs also have achieved great success in modeling dynamical systems governed by PDEs such as fluid mechanics (Mowlavi and Nabi, 2023), see a recent review by Faroughi et al. (2024).
