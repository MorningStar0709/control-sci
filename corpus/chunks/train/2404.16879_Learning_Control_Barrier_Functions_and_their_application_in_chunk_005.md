Another motivation for the need to endow RL with safety is the fact that policy training in RL often takes place in simulated environments, which introduces an additional challenge due to potential mismatch between simulation and the real environment. This is often called simulation to reality gap [27]. This gap makes it more tricky to deploy policies trained in simulators to reallife environments, and mandates the presence of safety measures (during training and deployment) that are able to handle this gap.

This gives rise to the field of Safe Reinforcement Learning (SRL) that aims to augment RL processes with safety, typically through introduction of appropriate constraints that render RL policies safe during the training phase.
