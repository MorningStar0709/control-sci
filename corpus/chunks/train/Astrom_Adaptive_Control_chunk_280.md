# EXAMPLE 5.4 Choice of adaptation gain

Consider the system in Example 5.1 with $G(s) = 1 / (s + 1)$ , $k = 1$ , and $k_0 = 2$ . Assume that the reference signal has unit amplitude. Equation (5.13) then becomes

$$s ^ {2} + s + \mu = s ^ {2} + s + \gamma y _ {m} ^ {o} u _ {c} ^ {o} k = 0$$

A reasonable choice is to make $\gamma y_m^o u_c^o k = 1$ . If we disregard the transients, the average value of $y_m u_c$ is 2. This gives $\gamma = 0.5$ , which is the value used in one of the simulations in Fig. 5.3.
