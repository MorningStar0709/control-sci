To bound the cross term we use Young’s inequality (Khalil and Grizzle, 2002, Lemma 1). Let $a ~ = ~ P ^ { 1 / 2 } A \tilde { z } _ { k }$ and $b = P ^ { 1 / 2 } \tilde { w } _ { k }$ . Then, for any $\varepsilon > 0$ ,

$$
\begin{array}{l} 2 \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k} = 2 a ^ {\top} b \\ \leq \varepsilon \| a \| ^ {2} + \frac {1}{\varepsilon} \| b \| ^ {2} \\ = \varepsilon \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P A \tilde {z} _ {k} + \frac {1}{\varepsilon} \tilde {w} _ {k} ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k}. \tag {24} \\ \end{array}
$$

Using the bounds

$$\lambda_ {\min} (Q) \left\| \tilde {z} _ {k} \right\| ^ {2} \leq \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} Q \tilde {z} _ {k} \leq \lambda_ {\max} (Q) \left\| \tilde {z} _ {k} \right\| ^ {2}, \tag {25}\tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P A \tilde {z} _ {k} \leq \| A ^ {\top} P A \| \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| ^ {2}. \tag {26}$$

and $\tilde { w } _ { k } ^ { \top } P \tilde { w } _ { k } \leq \| P \| \| \tilde { w } _ { k } \| ^ { 2 }$ , we obtain

$$
\begin{array}{l} \Delta V _ {k + 1} \leq \left(- \lambda_ {\min} (Q) + \varepsilon \| A ^ {\top} P A \|\right) \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| ^ {2} \\ + \left(1 + \frac {1}{\varepsilon}\right) \| P \| \| \tilde {w} _ {k} \| ^ {2}. \tag {27} \\ \end{array}
$$

Choose $\varepsilon > 0$ small enough such that

$$\alpha := \lambda_ {\min} (Q) - \varepsilon \| A ^ {\top} P A \| > 0, \tag {28}$$

and define

$$\beta := \left(1 + \frac {1}{\varepsilon}\right) \| P \|. \tag {29}$$

Then

$$\Delta V _ {k + 1} \leq - \alpha \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| ^ {2} + \beta \| \tilde {w} _ {k} \| ^ {2}. \tag {30}$$

Thus $V _ { k }$ is strictly decreasing whenever

$$\| \tilde {w} _ {k} \| ^ {2} < \frac {\alpha}{\beta} \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| ^ {2} \Rightarrow \| \tilde {w} _ {k} \| ^ {2} < \frac {\lambda_ {\min} (Q)}{\| I + P \|} \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| ^ {2}, \tag {31}$$

which provides a sufficient local stability condition for the state EKF in the presence of the disturbance (19).

Since the exact norms of $\tilde { z } _ { k }$ and $w _ { k }$ are not available, they are conservatively bounded via the state covariance $P _ { k | k }$ and the process noise covariance $Q$ . This leads to an implementable innovation threshold

$$\delta_ {k} = \frac {\sqrt {\lambda_ {\min} (Q) / \| I + P \|} \| \tilde {z} _ {k} \| + \| w _ {k} \|}{\| K _ {k + 1} \|}, \tag {32}$$

which is consistent with (31) after bounding the constants. The dead-zone switching signal is defined as

$$
\sigma_ {k} = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} 1, & \| E _ {k} \| <   \delta_ {k}, \\ 0, & \| E _ {k} \| \geq \delta_ {k}, \end{array} \right. \tag {33}
$$

where $\sigma _ { k } ~ = ~ 1$ allows the parameter EKF to update, and $\sigma _ { k } ~ = ~ 0$ freezes the parameter estimate to avoid destabilizing the state filter. This innovation-based dead zone is the core mechanism that guarantees the local stability of the proposed SG-DKF.
