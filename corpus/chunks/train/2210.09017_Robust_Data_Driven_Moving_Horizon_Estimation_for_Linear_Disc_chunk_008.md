# III. PRACTICAL RES FOR NOISY OFFLINE STATE & OUTPUT MEASUREMENTS

In this section, we introduce the robust data-driven MHE scheme. Given an estimation horizon L in $\mathbb { I } _ { > 0 }$ , at each time1 $t \geq L$ , solve

$$
\begin{array}{l} \underset {\overline {{x}} [ - L, 0 ] (t), \alpha (t),} {\text { minimize }} J (\overline {{x}} (t - L | t), \sigma_ {[ - L, - 1 ]} ^ {y} (t), \sigma_ {[ - L, 0 ]} ^ {x} (t), \alpha (t)) \\ \sigma_ {[ - L, - 1 ]} ^ {y} (t), \\ \sigma_ {[ - L, 0 ]} ^ {x} (t) \\ \end{array}

\text {s.t.} \left[ \begin{array}{c} H _ {L} (u _ {[ 0, N - 2 ]} ^ {d}) \\ H _ {L} (\tilde {y} _ {[ 0, N - 2 ]} ^ {d}) \\ H _ {L + 1} (\tilde {x} _ {[ 0, N - 1 ]} ^ {d}) \end{array} \right] \alpha (t) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} u _ {[ t - L, t - 1 ]} \\ \tilde {y} _ {[ t - L, t - 1 ]} - \sigma_ {[ - L, - 1 ]} ^ {y} (t) \\ \overline {{x}} _ {[ - L, 0 ]} (t) + \sigma_ {[ - L, 0 ]} ^ {x} (t) \end{array} \right] \tag {9b}
\overline {{{x}}} _ {[ - L, 0 ]} (t) \in \mathbb {X} \tag {9c}$$

where

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (\overline {{x}} (t - L | t), \sigma_ {[ - L, - 1 ]} ^ {y} (t), \sigma_ {[ - L, 0 ]} ^ {x} (t), \alpha (t)) \\ := \Gamma (\overline {{x}} (t - L | t)) + \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {L} \ell_ {k} (\sigma^ {y} (t - k | t)) \\ + c _ {\sigma^ {x}} \left| \sigma_ {[ - L, 0 ]} ^ {x} (t) \right| ^ {2} + c _ {\alpha} \left(\overline {{\varepsilon}} _ {x} ^ {d} + \overline {{\varepsilon}} _ {y} ^ {d}\right) | \alpha (t) | ^ {2}. \tag {9d} \\ \end{array}
$$
