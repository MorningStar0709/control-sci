For insight, note that for each $t \in ( 0 , T )$ and $i \in [ P ]$ , the controls $a _ { i } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { d }$ and $b _ { i } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ define a $( d - 1 )$ -dimensional hyperplane

$$H _ {i} (t) := \{x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {d}: \langle a _ {i} (t), x \rangle + b _ {i} (t) = 0 \},$$

that partitions the Euclidean space $\mathbb { R } ^ { d }$ into two disjoint complementary half-spaces

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} H _ {i} ^ {+} (t) := \big \{x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {d}: \langle a _ {i} (t), x \rangle + b _ {i} (t) > 0 \big \}, \\ H _ {i} ^ {-} (t) := \mathbb {R} ^ {d} \setminus H _ {i} ^ {+} (t) := \big \{x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {d}: \langle a _ {i} (t), x \rangle + b _ {i} (t) \leq 0 \big \}. \end{array} \right. \tag {3.5}
$$
