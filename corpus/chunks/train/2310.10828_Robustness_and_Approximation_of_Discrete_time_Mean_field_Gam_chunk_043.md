Note that Assumption 1-c is not applicable here since $Q \circ ( T _ { n } , I )$ does not satisfy the conditions. Hence the bound $K _ { F }$ has to be obtained through Assumption 2. With this observation, similar to the case of the cost function, we can obtain the following Lipschitz bound on variation over states as the bound on $Q \circ ( T _ { n } , I )$ is identical to those in the domain $\mathcal { C } ( n )$ :

$$\| \nabla H _ {1, n} (Q, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) (x _ {n, i}, a) - \nabla H _ {1, n} (Q, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) (x _ {n, j}, a) \| \leq K _ {F} \bigg (\frac {2}{n} + 1 \bigg) d _ {X _ {n}} (x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}).$$

This concludes the inequality we wanted for variation on the states.

In case of variation over Q functions for $\nabla H _ { 1 , n }$ , arguing exactly as above, the variation over $Q$ functions for $Q _ { 1 } , Q _ { 2 } \in { \mathcal { C } } ( n )$ will be of the form

$$
\begin{array}{l} \| \nabla H _ {1, n} (Q _ {1}, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) (x, a) - \nabla H _ {1, n} (Q _ {2}, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) (x, a) \| \leq K _ {F} (1 + 2 / n) \| Q _ {1, \min} \circ T _ {n} - Q _ {2, \min} \circ T _ {n} \| _ {\infty} \\ = K _ {F} (1 + 2 / n) \| Q _ {1, \min} - Q _ {2, \min} \| _ {\infty} \\ \end{array}
$$

Inequalities for variations on ${ \mathcal { P } } ( X _ { n } )$ can be obtained in a similar fashion given the discussion above of Wasserstein distances on continuous and discrete state spaces. □

Remark 5. The scaling trick that we have done on the metric $d _ { X }$ is necessary and affect the choice we can have for $d _ { X _ { n } }$ . Indeed, $i f X = [ 0 , 1 ]$ and we pick the representatives for the quantization of X such that minimum distance between them is $1 / n ^ { 2 }$ , then as in the proof of Lemma 5, we can only obtain

\begin{array}{l} | c _ {n} (x _ {n, i}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) - c _ {n} (x _ {n, j}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) | \leq \frac {2 L _ {2}}{n} + L _ {2} d _ {X} (x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}) \\ \leq L _ {2} \bigg (\frac {2}{n \min _ {i \neq j} d _ {X} (x _ {n , i} , x _ {n , j})} + 1 \bigg) d _ {X} (x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}). \\ \end{array}
