$$\dot {\varphi} _ {i} = \kappa_ {\varphi} (m _ {i} - m _ {i - 1}), \quad i \in I _ {N} \tag {2}$$

with the positive constant $\kappa _ { \varphi }$ and $m _ { 0 } = m _ { N }$ . Note that the evolution of ϕi may exceed the range of interval [0, 2π). Since phase angle ϕi wraps around while reaching 2π, the modulo operation is adopted with $\varphi _ { i } = \mathrm { m o d } ( \varphi _ { i } , 2 \pi )$ in this work to ensure $\varphi _ { i } \in [ 0 , 2 \pi ) , i \in I _ { N }$ .

Inspired by the work in [4], the performance index of multi-agent coverage problem is designed as follows

$$J (\varphi , \mathbf {p}) = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} \int_ {E _ {i} (\varphi)} f (p _ {i}, q) \rho (q) d q. \tag {3}$$

with $\mathbf { p } = ( p _ { 1 } , p _ { 2 } , . . . , p _ { N } ) ^ { T }$ and $\pmb { \varphi } = ( \varphi _ { 1 } , \varphi _ { 2 } , . . . , \varphi _ { N } ) ^ { T }$ . In addition, $f ( p _ { i } , q )$ is continuously differentiable with respect to $p _ { i }$ , and it is used to quantify the cost of moving from $p _ { i }$ to $q$ to service the event. Thus, $f ( p _ { i } , q )$ is closely dependent on the distance between $p _ { i }$ and $q .$ . The dynamics of multi-agent system is presented as

$$\dot {p} _ {i} = u _ {i}, \quad i \in I _ {N} \tag {4}$$

with the control input ${ \bf { u } } _ { i } .$ . The performance index characterizes the total cost of multi-agent systems to service the events on the region Ω, and a lower value of performance index (3) indicates a better deployment of agents and partition bars. Essentially, our goal is to design a distributed control algorithm to solve the following optimization problem

$$\min _ {\varphi , \mathbf {p}} J (\varphi , \mathbf {p}) \tag {5}$$

by deploying agents and equalizing the workload among subregions with partition bars.
