# Guidance Problem

As in the air-launched cruise missile, the mission planners design the nominal flight trajectory to satisfy mission requirements using nominal performance data for the missile and expected environmental conditions. If the models used to derive the nominal guidance command time history represented the flight system behavior and expected flight environment, all information necessary to follow the nominal path would be contained in this time history. In practice, of course, such a situation never occurs. A missile in flight is subject to a variety of unpredictable perturbations that will affect its trajectory. The function of the guidance algorithm is to guarantee that in the presence of in-flight perturbations the missile still satisfies all mission requirements including terminal accuracy. The in-flight perturbations are caused by dispersions in initial position and velocity, rocket motor thrust, atmospheric density, winds, vehicle aerodynamic uncertainty, and steering lags.
