$\mathcal { H } _ { 2 }$ filtering: $\mathcal { H } _ { 2 }$ observers such as the Kalman filter are designed to minimize the mean square error when disturbances and noise are Gaussian. In mathematical terms, this means that the filter gains solve the problem (5) with

$$\mathrm{cost} _ {2} (e (\boldsymbol {v}, \boldsymbol {w}, \mathcal {L})) = \frac {1}{T} \sum_ {0} ^ {T} \mathbb {E} _ {[ v _ {t}, w _ {t} ] \sim \mathcal {N} (0, \Sigma_ {t})} \left[ \| e _ {t} \| _ {2} ^ {2} \right], \tag {16}$$

$$
\operatorname{cost} _ {2} \left(\Phi_ {v}, \Phi_ {w}\right) = \frac {1}{T} \left\| \left[ \Phi_ {v}, \Phi_ {w} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \Sigma_ {v} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} & 0 \\ 0 & \Sigma_ {w} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \end{array} \right] \right\| _ {F} ^ {2}. \tag {17}
