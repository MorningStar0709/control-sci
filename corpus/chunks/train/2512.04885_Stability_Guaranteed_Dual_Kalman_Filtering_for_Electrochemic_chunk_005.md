# 2.1 Dual EKF Structure

Consider the nonlinear discrete-time system

$$x _ {k} = f (x _ {k - 1}, \theta_ {k - 1}, u _ {k - 1}) + w _ {k - 1}, y _ {k} = h (x _ {k}, \theta_ {k - 1}, u _ {k}) + v _ {k}, \tag {1}$$

where $x _ { k } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ is the state, $\theta _ { k } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is the parameter vector, $u _ { k }$ is the input, $y _ { k }$ is the measured output, and $w _ { k } , v _ { k }$ denote process and measurement noise. Linearizing (1) along the EKF trajectory gives the LTI approximation

$$x _ {k + 1} = A x _ {k} + B u _ {k} + w _ {k}, \quad y _ {k} = C x _ {k} + D u _ {k} + v _ {k}, \tag {2}$$

where A and $C$ are the Jacobians of f and h with respect to $x ,$ and B, D are the input matrices.

For State EKF. Given $\hat { \theta } _ { k - 1 | k - 1 }$ , the state EKF performs the standard prediction–update steps (Plett, 2004c):

$$\hat {x} _ {k \mid k - 1} = f (\hat {x} _ {k - 1 \mid k - 1}, \hat {\theta} _ {k - 1 \mid k - 1}, u _ {k - 1}), \tag {3}P _ {k \mid k - 1} = A _ {k - 1} P _ {k - 1 \mid k - 1} A _ {k - 1} ^ {\top} + Q, \tag {4}K _ {k} = P _ {k \mid k - 1} C _ {k} ^ {\top} \left(C _ {k} P _ {k \mid k - 1} C _ {k} ^ {\top} + R\right) ^ {- 1}, \tag {5}E _ {k} = y _ {k} - h (\hat {x} _ {k | k - 1}, \hat {\theta} _ {k - 1 | k - 1}, u _ {k}), \tag {6}\hat {x} _ {k \mid k} = \hat {x} _ {k \mid k - 1} + K _ {k} E _ {k}, \tag {7}P _ {k \mid k} = (I - K _ {k} C _ {k}) P _ {k \mid k - 1}, \tag {8}$$

where $A _ { k - 1 } = \partial f / \partial x | _ { \hat { x } _ { k - 1 | k - } }$ −1 and $C _ { k } = \partial h / \partial x | _ { \hat { x } _ { k | k - 1 } } .$

For parameter EKF. The parameters are modelled as a random walk,

$$\theta_ {k} = \theta_ {k - 1} + \omega_ {k - 1}, \tag {9}$$

with process noise $\omega _ { k }$ . The parameter EKF uses the latest state estimate $\hat { x } _ { k \mid k }$ :

$$\hat {\theta} _ {k \mid k - 1} = \hat {\theta} _ {k - 1 \mid k - 1}, \tag {10}P _ {\theta , k \mid k - 1} = P _ {\theta , k - 1 \mid k - 1} + Q _ {\theta}, \tag {11}K _ {\theta , k} = P _ {\theta , k | k - 1} C _ {\theta , k} ^ {\top} \big (C _ {\theta , k} P _ {\theta , k | k - 1} C _ {\theta , k} ^ {\top} + R \big) ^ {- 1}, \tag {12}r _ {k} = y _ {k} - h _ {p} (\hat {\theta} _ {k | k - 1}, \hat {x} _ {k | k}, u _ {k}), \tag {13}\hat {\theta} _ {k \mid k} = \hat {\theta} _ {k \mid k - 1} + K _ {\theta , k} r _ {k}, \tag {14}P _ {\theta , k \mid k} = (I - K _ {\theta , k} C _ {\theta , k}) P _ {\theta , k \mid k - 1}, \tag {15}$$
