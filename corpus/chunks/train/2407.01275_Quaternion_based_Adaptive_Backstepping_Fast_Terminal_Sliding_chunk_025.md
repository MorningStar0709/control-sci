$$\dot {V} _ {s} = s \left(\ddot {e} + \gamma_ {1} \dot {e} + \gamma_ {2} \frac {n}{l} e ^ {n / l - 1} \dot {e} + \dot {\Omega} _ {e}\right) + \frac {1}{\lambda} \tilde {\boldsymbol {K}} ^ {\top} \dot {\tilde {\boldsymbol {K}}} \tag {40}$$

Also, the e¨ can be derived from (32) as:

$$\ddot {e} = \frac {1}{2} (\dot {e} _ {0} \Omega_ {e} + e _ {0} \dot {\Omega} _ {e} + [ \dot {e} ] _ {\times} \Omega_ {e} + [ e ] _ {\times} \dot {\Omega} _ {e}) \tag {41}$$

Using (1), (41) and (32), the above equation can be rewritten as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} _ {s} = s \left(\frac {1}{2} \left(\left(\dot {e} _ {0} \mathbf {I} _ {3} + [ \dot {e} ] _ {\times}\right) \Omega_ {e} + \left(e _ {0} \mathbf {I} _ {3} + [ e ] _ {\times}\right) \dot {\Omega} _ {e}\right) + \right. \\ \frac {\gamma_ {1}}{2} \left(e _ {0} \Omega_ {e} + [ e ] _ {\times} \Omega_ {e}\right) + \gamma_ {2} \frac {n}{l} e ^ {n / l - 1} \dot {e} + J ^ {- 1} ([ J \Omega_ {B} ] _ {\times} \Omega_ {B} + \\ \left. \mathcal {T} + J \left[ \Omega_ {e} \right] _ {\times} R _ {e} \Omega_ {d} - J R _ {e} \dot {\Omega} _ {d})\right) + \frac {1}{\lambda} \tilde {\boldsymbol {K}} ^ {\top} \dot {\hat {\boldsymbol {K}}} \tag {42} \\ \end{array}
$$

By substituting (37) in (42), the derivative of (39) becomes:

$$\dot {V} _ {s} = - \mu_ {1} | | s | | ^ {2} - s \hat {\boldsymbol {K}} \operatorname{sign} (s) + \frac {1}{\lambda} \tilde {\boldsymbol {K}} ^ {\top} \dot {\hat {\boldsymbol {K}}} \tag {43}$$

Based on (38), the above equation can be rewritten as:

$$\dot {V} _ {s} = - \mu_ {1} | | s | | ^ {2} - s (\tilde {\boldsymbol {K}} + \boldsymbol {K}) \operatorname{sign} (s) + \frac {1}{\lambda} \tilde {\boldsymbol {K}} ^ {\top} \lambda | | s | | \tag {44}\leq - \mu_ {1} | | s | | ^ {2} - \boldsymbol {K} | | s | | \leq 0$$

Therefore, the attitude dynamics in (7) become asymptotically stable using the proposed controller in (37) completing the proof.

The proposed quaternion-based AFTSMC guarantees asymptotic convergence of the attitude dynamics. However, finite time convergence is not guaranteed. This issue will be addressed in the subsequent subsection.
