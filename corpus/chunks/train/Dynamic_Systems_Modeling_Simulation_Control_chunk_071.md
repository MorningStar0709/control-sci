# 2.4 ROTATIONAL MECHANICAL SYSTEMS

Mathematical models of rotational mechanical systems can be derived using the same systematic two-step process that was used for translational systems:

1. Draw an FBD of each moment of inertia with arrows denoting external torques acting on each moment of inertia. Make use of Newton’s third law to show equal-and-opposite reaction torques. Carefully assign equations for each torque using the appropriate element law and the positive conventions for angular displacement variables.   
2. Apply Newton’s second law to each moment of inertia to obtain the mathematical model of the entire mechanical system.

Newton’s second law for rotational systems states that the sum of all external torques acting on a body is equal to the product of the moment of inertia J and angular acceleration $\ddot { \theta }$ of the body

$$\sum T = J \ddot {\theta} \tag {2.36}$$
