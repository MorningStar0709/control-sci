# 8.3.3 Robustness Margins and Robust Stability

Next, we will establish relationships between the robustness margins (modulus margin and delay margin) and the robust stability conditions. From (8.8) and (8.25) it results that the modulus margin corresponds to a robust stability condition for a family of feedback uncertainties on the input (it is not the only one) of the form:

$$W _ {r} ^ {- 1} (z ^ {- 1}) = \Delta M \tag {8.26}\delta (z ^ {- 1}) = \lambda f (z ^ {- 1}); \quad - 1 \leq \lambda \leq 1 \tag {8.27}$$

where

$$f (z ^ {- 1}) = 1, z ^ {- 1}, z ^ {- 2}, \dots , \frac {z ^ {- 1} + z ^ {- 2}}{2} \tag {8.28}$$

Two families of models for which the robust stability conditions are satisfied can be immediately derived from (8.26), (8.27) and (8.28):

$$G ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1}) = G (z ^ {- 1}) \frac {1}{1 - \lambda \Delta M} \tag {8.29}G ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1}) = G (z ^ {- 1}) \frac {1}{1 - \lambda \Delta M z ^ {- 1}} \tag {8.30}$$

These two representations emphasize the tolerance with respect to gain variation and to the presence of unmodeled dynamics (other forms of unmodeled dynamics as well as variations of the $a _ { i }$ and $b _ { i }$ parameters can be considered, see Morari and Zafiriou 1989; Landau 1995). Conversely, model uncertainties can be converted into a required modulus margin for assuring robust stability.

The delay margin can also be converted into a robust stability condition which define a template for the sensitivity functions. As an example, consider the case $\varDelta \tau = T _ { s }$ , i.e.:

$$G (z ^ {- 1}) = \frac {z ^ {- d} B (z ^ {- 1})}{A (z ^ {- 1})} \quad \text { and } \quad G ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1}) = \frac {z ^ {- d - 1} B (z ^ {- 1})}{A (z ^ {- 1})} = G (z ^ {- 1}) z ^ {- 1} \tag {8.31}$$

One can interpret the additional delay which should be tolerated in terms of multiplicative uncertainties. From (8.18) one obtains:

$$G ^ {\prime} (z ^ {- 1}) = G (z ^ {- 1}) z ^ {- 1} = G (z ^ {- 1}) (1 + \delta (z ^ {- 1}) W _ {m} (z ^ {- 1}))= G (z ^ {- 1}) [ 1 + (z ^ {- 1} - 1) ] \tag {8.32}$$

which gives for example:

$$\delta (z ^ {- 1}) = 1; \quad W _ {m} (z ^ {- 1}) = (z ^ {- 1} - 1) \tag {8.33}$$

and the corresponding robust stability condition is expressed as:

$$\left\| S _ {y b} \left(z ^ {- 1}\right) \cdot \left(1 - z ^ {- 1}\right) \right\| _ {\infty} \leq 1; \quad z = e ^ {- j \omega}, 0 \leq \omega \leq \pi \tag {8.34}$$

or respectively

$$\left| S _ {y b} \left(z ^ {- 1}\right) \right| < \left| \left(1 - z ^ {- 1}\right) \right| ^ {- 1}; \quad z = e ^ {- j \omega}, 0 \leq \omega \leq \pi \tag {8.35}$$
