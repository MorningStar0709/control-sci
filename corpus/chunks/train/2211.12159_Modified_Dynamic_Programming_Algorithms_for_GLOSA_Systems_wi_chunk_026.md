$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ - 1 \\ 1 \\ - 1 \\ 1 \\ - 1 \end{array} \right] \delta a - \left[ \begin{array}{c} \frac {2 (x _ {\text {max}} - \bar {x} (k + 1))}{T ^ {2}} \\ \frac {- 2 (x _ {\text {min}} - \bar {x} (k + 1))}{T ^ {2}} \\ \frac {(v _ {\text {max}} - \bar {v} (k + 1))}{T} \\ \frac {- (v _ {\text {min}} - \bar {v} (k + 1))}{T} \\ (a _ {\text {max}} - \bar {a} (k)) \\ - (a _ {\text {min}} - \bar {a} (k)) \end{array} \right] - \delta x \leq \left[ \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array} \right] \tag {36}
$$

Having the quadratic approximation of the recursive Bellman equation and the linear inequality constraints (35), the resulting QP is as described in the previous subsection. In fact, the resulting QP problem for the stochastic GLOSA application is simpler than the general case of Section 4.1. After setting $\delta { \pmb x } = 0$ in (36), we have only one decision variable, 𝛿𝑎, with constant upper and lower bounds. Thus, for the solution of the QP problem, it suffices to first compute analytically the value of 𝛿𝑎 that minimises the quadratic function. If this value does not violate any of the upper or lower bounds, then the QP solution is given by that value; else, the QP solution equals the activated bound. All further procedures are as explained in Section 4.1.
