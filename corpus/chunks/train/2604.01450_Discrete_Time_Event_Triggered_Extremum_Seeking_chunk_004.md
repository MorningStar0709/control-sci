$$\theta [ k ] = \hat {\theta} [ k ] + a \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ]. \tag {2}$$

The Hessian $H ^ { * } < 0$ for maximization problems, and $H ^ { * } > 0$ for minimization purposes.

Let us define the estimation error

$$\tilde {\theta} [ k ] = \hat {\theta} [ k ] - \theta^ {*}, \tag {3}$$

and the gradient estimate

$$\hat {G} [ k ] = a \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ] y [ k ], \tag {4}$$

by the employing the demodulation signal, a sin[ωϵk], which has nonzero amplitudes a and frequency ω [12], [13].

From (2) and (3), we can write

$$\theta [ k ] = \tilde {\theta} [ k ] + a \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ] + \theta^ {*}, \tag {5}$$

and, therefore, by using (5) the output $y [ k ] = Q [ \theta [ k ] ]$ , with $Q [ \theta [ k ] ]$ given by (2) can also be written as

$$
\begin{array}{l} y (t) = Q ^ {*} + \frac {H ^ {*} a ^ {2}}{4} + \frac {H ^ {*}}{2} \tilde {\theta} ^ {2} [ k ] + a \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ] H ^ {*} \tilde {\theta} [ k ] \\ - \frac {H ^ {*} a ^ {2}}{4} \cos [ 2 \omega \epsilon k ]. \tag {6} \\ \end{array}
$$

Thus, from (4) and (6), the gradient estimate, is given by

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {G} [ k ] = \frac {a ^ {2} H ^ {*}}{2} (1 - \cos [ 2 \omega \epsilon k ]) \tilde {\theta} [ k ] + \frac {a H ^ {*}}{2} \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ] \tilde {\theta} ^ {2} [ k ] \\ + \left(a Q ^ {*} + \frac {3 a ^ {3} H ^ {*}}{8}\right) \sin [ \omega \epsilon k ] - \frac {a ^ {3} H ^ {*}}{8} \sin [ 3 \omega \epsilon k ]. \tag {7} \\ \end{array}
$$
