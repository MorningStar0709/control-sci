# Summary:

1. DB-MDPs extend the traditional MDP framework to include decision making constraints. Traditional reinforcement learning algorithms are limited in their ability to explore and navigate DB-MDPs.   
2. A novel biologically-inspired Temporally Layered Architecture (TLA) allows each layer to focus on a different temporal context and to navigate the entire DB-MDP more effectively than classical RL agents.   
3. The two layers of the TLA can be simultaneously trained with an efficient learning algorithm.   
4. We empirically test decision-bounded environments for both tabular and parameterized policy on gridworld and continuous control environments and demonstrate that TLA succeeds where the state-of-the-art algorithms fail.   
5. Empirical results on eight continuous control tasks demonstrate that TLA is able to achieve state-of-the-art performance with less computational cost and fewer decisions.
