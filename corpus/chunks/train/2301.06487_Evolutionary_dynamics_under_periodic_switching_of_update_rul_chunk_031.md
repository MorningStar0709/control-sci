In addition, the switching we considered is exogenous and independent of the system states. However, in some realistic scenarios, the switching rule may depend on the system states, and thus a promising extension of this work is to consider endogenous switching, where the switching rule to be used depends on the states, such as the frequency of one type. Furthermore, the periodic switching of update rules studied here can be regarded as a control scheme, which has some important effect on the behavior of complex systems; other forms of control schemes, such as under the influence of technology, policy, market factors [50], and other small perturbations of system parameters, can be considered as a driven forces for evolutionary game dynamics, which are worthy of study in the future [51].
