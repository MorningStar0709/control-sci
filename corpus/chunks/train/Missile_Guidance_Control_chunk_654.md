$$\mathbf {F} = m \mathbf {g} + \mathbf {F} _ {D} = - (\mu m / r ^ {2}) \mathbf {e} _ {r} - \mathbf {F} _ {D} \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} = m \left(\frac {d ^ {2} \mathbf {r}}{d t ^ {2}}\right) \tag {4}$$

where

$$\mathbf {e} _ {r} = \text { unit vector along the radial direction (i.e., } \mathbf {r}),\mathbf {e} _ {\theta} = \text { unit vector along the tangential direction (i.e., v) },\mu = \text { product of gravitational constant and the mass of the Earth } = G M,m = \text { mass of the reentry body }.$$

From (4) we have

$$\frac {d ^ {2} \mathbf {r}}{d t ^ {2}} = - (\mu / r ^ {2}) \mathbf {e} _ {r} - (F _ {D} / m) \mathbf {e} _ {\theta}. \tag {5}$$

Next, we take the dot product of (5) with $d \mathbf { r } / d t$ . Thus,

$$\left(\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t}\right) \cdot \left(\frac {d ^ {2} \mathbf {r}}{d t ^ {2}}\right) = \left(\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t}\right) \cdot [ - (\mu / r ^ {2}) \mathbf {e} _ {r} - (F _ {D} / m) \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} ]. \tag {6}$$

Making use of the vector identity $\mathbf { v } \cdot ( d \mathbf { v } / d t ) = v ( d v / d t )$ , we have

$$v \left(\frac {d v}{d t}\right) = - (\mu / r ^ {2}) \left(\frac {d r}{d t}\right) - v (F _ {D} / m). \tag {7}$$

Substituting (1) into (7) yields

$$\left(\frac {d v}{d t}\right) = - \left[ (C _ {D} S / m) (\rho v ^ {2} / 2) + \mu \left(\frac {d r}{d t}\right) / v r ^ {2} \right]. \tag {8}$$

Next, we consider the time rate of the specific angular momentum vector $d \mathbf { H } / d t$ of the center of mass of the reentry vehicle. This is equal to the torque applied per unit mass. Thus,

$$\frac {d \mathbf {H}}{d t} = \mathbf {r} \times (\mathbf {F} / m) = - \mathbf {e} _ {H} (F _ {D} / m) r \cos \varphi , \tag {9}$$

where $\varphi$ is the angle between the velocity vector v and the local horizontal, and $\mathbf { e } _ { H }$ is the unit vector along H. Constraining the reentry trajectory to a single plane and then combining (1) and (9) gives

$$\frac {d \mathbf {H}}{d t} = - (\rho C _ {D} S v ^ {2} / 2 m) r \cos \varphi \tag {10}$$

and

$$v \cos \varphi = \left(\frac {d \sigma}{d t}\right) \rho . \tag {11}$$

Substituting (11) into (10) yields

$$H = - (C _ {D} S / m) \left\{\left(\rho v r ^ {2} \left(\frac {d \sigma}{d t}\right)\right) / 2 \right\}. \tag {12}$$

Now the magnitude of H is given by

$$H = r v \cos \varphi . \tag {13}$$

Thus, substituting (11) into (13) yields

$$\frac {d \sigma}{d t} = H / r ^ {2}. \tag {14}$$
