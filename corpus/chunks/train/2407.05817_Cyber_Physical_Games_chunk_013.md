We assume social agents have negotiated the common strategy at the beginning of the game, through inter-agent message passing.

Unsocial, optimistic agents care not for collaboration, and assume their actions are immediately, and in the same order, effected on the environment. Thus, agents c0 and $c _ { 1 }$ issue only the messages that would evolve the environment according to φ and ψ, respectively. Unsocial but realistic agents can observe the state of the environment to ensure the outcome of their actions successfully occurred. In this case, they can ensure that their first state transition successfully occurred, before issuing the second message. Social but optimistic agents collaborate to satisfy the compound state transition sequences that satisfies both φ , ψ fairly, distributing the work among themselves. Social and realistic agents collaborate, timing their actions to ensure mutual benefit, with agents monitoring the environment to preemptively abort failed strategies, if any.
