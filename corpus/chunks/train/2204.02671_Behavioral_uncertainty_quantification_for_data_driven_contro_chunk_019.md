This, in turn, suggests that the choice of the metric structure on the set of restricted behaviors is crucial, raising a number of important questions. For instance, any metric on $\mathrm { G r } ( k , N )$ that induces a differentiable structure opens up the possibility of directly optimizing over behaviors. So can one exploit any such structure to improve the performance of data-driven control algorithms $( \mathrm { e . g . }$ ., DeePC)?
