# B. Segway Robot Control

The ESOR-QP is applied to a Segway self-balancing scooter with a high relative-degree CBF, whose dynamics and parameters are from [18]. The wheel center position $p$ and pitch angle $\varphi$ are measured. The dynamic model is:

$$
\underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {p} \\ \dot {\varphi} \\ \dot {v} \\ \dot {\omega} \end{array} \right]} _ {\dot {x}} = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} v \\ \omega \\ f _ {v} (\varphi , v , \omega) \\ f _ {\omega} (\varphi , v , \omega) \end{array} \right]} _ {f (x)} + \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ g _ {v} (\varphi) \\ g _ {\omega} (\varphi) \end{array} \right]} _ {g (x)} u + \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ d _ {1} \\ d _ {2} \end{array} \right]} _ {d (x)}, \tag {32}
$$

where υ and ω are the velocities of p and $\varphi ,$ respectively, $d _ { 1 }$ and $d _ { 2 }$ are two separate external disturbances, and other parameters can be found in [18]. The safety constraint of CBF is chosen as $h \ : = \ : \pi / 1 0 \ : - \ : \varphi ^ { 2 }$ to keep the Segway upright. The following nominal control law is used to track the desired wheel center position $p _ { d } \colon$

$$k (x) = K _ {p} (p - p _ {d}) + K _ {\upsilon} \upsilon + K _ {\varphi} \varphi + K _ {\omega} \omega \tag {33}$$

where gains are tuned to be $K _ { p } = 4 ~ \mathrm { V / m }$ , $K _ { v } = 8$ Vs/m, $K _ { \varphi } = 4 0$ V/rad, $K _ { \omega } = 1 0$ Vs/rad, and the desired wheel position $p _ { d } ~ = ~ 1$ m. The disturbances are set to $d _ { 1 } ( t ) =$ $2 \sin ( 2 \pi t / 1 0 )$ and $d _ { 2 } ( t ) = 2 \cos ( 2 \pi t / 1 0 )$ .

Based on the discussion in Section II-B, the Segway platform, as a signle-input multi-output system, can be divided into the following two subsystems:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} {\left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {p} \\ \dot {v} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{l} p \\ v \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ g _ {v} \end{array} \right] u + \left[ \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ f _ {v} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ d _ {1} \end{array} \right]} \\ y _ {1} = p, \end{array} \right. \tag {34}
