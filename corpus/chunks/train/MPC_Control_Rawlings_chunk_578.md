# Exercise 4.19: A single sample of joint density

Consider again Example 4.31 in which we have $s _ { x }$ and $s _ { y }$ samples of the marginals of independent random variables $\xi$ and η, respectively

$$
\begin{array}{l} \xi \sim \{x _ {i}, w _ {x i} \} \quad w _ {x i} = 1 / s _ {x}, \quad i = 1, \dots , s _ {x} \\ \eta \sim \{y _ {j}, w _ {y j} \} \quad w _ {y j} = 1 / s _ {y}, \quad j = 1, \dots , s _ {y} \\ \end{array}
$$

and wish to sample the joint density $p _ { \xi , \eta } ( x , y ) = p _ { \xi } ( x ) p _ { \eta } ( y )$ . Show that selecting any single sample is a valid sample of the joint density

$$\{(x _ {1}, y _ {1}), w \}, \quad w = 1$$
