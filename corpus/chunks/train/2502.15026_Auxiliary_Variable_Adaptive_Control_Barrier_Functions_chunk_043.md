# B. Obstacle Avoidance for High-Order Relative Degree Systems

1) Vehicle Dynamics: We consider a nonlinear vehicle dynamics in the form

$$
\underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} (t) \\ \dot {y} (t) \\ \dot {\theta} (t) \\ \dot {v} (t) \end{array} \right]} _ {\dot {\boldsymbol {x}} (t)} = \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} v (t) \cos (\theta (t)) \\ v (t) \sin (\theta (t)) \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array} \right]} _ {f (\boldsymbol {x} (t))} + \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac {1}{M} \end{array} \right]} _ {g (\boldsymbol {x} (t))} \underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} u _ {1} (t) \\ u _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right]} _ {\boldsymbol {u} (t)}. \tag {48}
$$

In (48), M denotes the mass of the vehicle and $( x , y )$ denote the coordinates of the unicycle, v is its linear speed, θ denotes the heading angle, and u represent the angular velocity (u1) and the driven force $( u _ { 2 } )$ , respectively.

2) Vehicle Limitations: Vehicle limitations include vehicle constraints on safe distance, reachability and control input.

Safe distance constraint: We consider the case when the vehicle has to avoid one circular obstacle. The distance is considered safe if $( x - x _ { o } ) ^ { 2 } + ( y - y _ { o } ) ^ { 2 } - r _ { o } ^ { 2 } \geq 0$ , where $( x _ { o } , y _ { o } )$ and $r _ { o }$ denote the obstacle center location and radius, respectively.

Reachability constraint: We consider a scenario where the vehicle aims to eventually reach a circular area centered at $( x _ { d } , y _ { d } )$ with a radius $r _ { d }$ .
