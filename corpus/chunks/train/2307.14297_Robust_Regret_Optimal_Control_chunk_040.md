$$
\dot {x} (t) = A x (t) + B \left[ \begin{array}{l} r (t) \\ f _ {s} (t) \end{array} \right] \tag {33}

\left[ \begin{array}{l} a _ {b} (t) \\ s _ {d} (t) \end{array} \right] = C x (t) + D \left[ \begin{array}{l} r (t) \\ f _ {s} (t) \end{array} \right], \tag {34}
$$

where $r ( t ) \in  { \mathbb { R } }$ is the road disturbance (m), $f _ { s } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ is the active suspension force (kN), $a _ { b } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ is the car body acceleration $( \mathbf { m } / \mathrm { s } ^ { 2 } ) , s _ { d } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ is the suspension displacement (m), and $\ b { x } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 4 }$ is the state. The state-space data is:

$$
A = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ - \frac {k _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & - \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & \frac {k _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {b}} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \frac {k _ {s}}{m _ {w}} & \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {w}} & - \frac {k _ {s} + k _ {t}}{m _ {w}} & - \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {w}} \end{array} \right],

B = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \frac {1 0 ^ {3}}{m _ {b}} \\ 0 & 0 \\ \frac {k _ {t}}{m _ {w}} & - \frac {1 0 ^ {3}}{m _ {w}} \end{array} \right],

C = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} - \frac {k _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & - \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & \frac {k _ {s}}{m _ {b}} & \frac {b _ {s}}{m _ {b}} \\ 1 & 0 & - 1 & 0 \end{array} \right],

D = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & \frac {1 0 ^ {3}}{m _ {b}} \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right],
$$

where $m _ { b } = 3 0 0 \mathrm { k g }$ , $m _ { w } = 6 0 \mathrm { k g }$ , $b _ { s } = 1 0 0 0 \mathrm { N / m / s }$ , $k _ { s } = 1 6 0 0 0 \mathrm { N } / \mathrm { m }$ , and $k _ { t } = 1 . 9 \times 1 0 ^ { 5 } \mathrm { N / m }$ . The model has lightly complex poles at $- 1 . 4 3 \pm 6 . 9 1 j$ and $- 8 . 5 7 \pm 5 7 . 6 j$ . The goal is to design the active suspension controller to balance driver comfort (small $a _ { b } )$ and road handling (small $s _ { d } )$ .
