# 6.12.3 Controllability of specific states

If you want to know if a specific state is controllable, first find its corresponding eigenvalue λ in A. Then, that state is controllable if

$$
\operatorname{rank} \left(\left[ \begin{array}{l l} \lambda \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} & \mathbf {B} \end{array} \right]\right) = n \tag {6.40}
$$

where n is the number of states.
