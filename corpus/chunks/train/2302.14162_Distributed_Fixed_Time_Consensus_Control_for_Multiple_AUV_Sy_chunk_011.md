$$\bar {\Phi} (v, \eta) = \operatorname{diag} \left\{\Phi_ {1} \left(v _ {1}, \eta_ {1}\right), \dots , \Phi_ {N} \left(v _ {N}, \eta_ {N}\right) \right\},\bar {\Pi} = \operatorname{diag} \left\{\Pi_ {1}, \dots , \Pi_ {N} \right\},\bar {J} (\eta_ {2}) = \operatorname{diag} \left\{J _ {1} \left(\eta_ {2, 1}\right), \dots , J _ {N} \left(\eta_ {2, N}\right) \right\},u \left(\tau (t)\right) = \left[ u _ {1} \left(\tau_ {1} (t)\right), u _ {2} \left(\tau_ {2} (t)\right), \dots , u _ {N} \left(\tau_ {N} (t)\right) \right] ^ {T},d = [ d _ {1} (t, \eta_ {1}, v _ {1}), d _ {2} (t, \eta_ {2}, v _ {2}),..., d _ {N} (t, \eta_ {N}, v _ {N}) ] ^ {T}.$$

Therefore,

$$\ddot {\eta} = \bar {\Phi} (v, \eta) v + \bar {J} (\eta_ {2}) \bar {\Pi} u (\tau (t)) + \bar {J} (\eta_ {2}) \bar {\Pi} d. \tag {13}$$

Assumption 1: The disturbance term $J _ { i } ( \eta _ { 2 , i } ) \Pi _ { i } d _ { i } ( t , \eta _ { i } , \upsilon _ { i } )$ is bounded by the positive constant $\tilde { \lambda } _ { i }$ :

$$\left\| J _ {i} \left(\eta_ {2, i}\right) \Pi_ {i} d _ {i} \left(t, \eta_ {i}, v _ {i}\right) \right\| \leq \tilde {\lambda} _ {i}, i \in \Gamma . \tag {14}$$

The parameter $\tilde { \lambda } _ { i }$ typically depends on the internal model uncertainties and external environmental disturbances (i.e., marine environment) of the vehicles.

Letting the following variables:

$$\bar {\varepsilon} _ {1} = \left[ \varepsilon_ {1, 1}, \varepsilon_ {1, 2}, \dots , \varepsilon_ {1, N} \right] ^ {T}, \tag {15}\bar {\varepsilon} _ {2} = [ \dot {\varepsilon} _ {1, 1}, \dot {\varepsilon} _ {1, 2}, \dots , \dot {\varepsilon} _ {1, N} ] ^ {T},$$

and

$$\ddot {\eta} = \left[ \ddot {\eta} _ {1}, \ddot {\eta} _ {2}, \dots , \ddot {\eta} _ {N} \right] ^ {T}. \tag {16}$$

Adding the results in (11), (15) and (16) to form the overall error dynamics as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\bar {\varepsilon}} _ {1} = \bar {\varepsilon} _ {2}, \\ \therefore (I, B) (j, 1, \dots , j d) \end{array} \tag {17}
\dot {\bar {\varepsilon}} _ {2} = (L + B) \left(\ddot {\eta} - \mathbf {1} _ {N} \otimes \ddot {\eta} ^ {d}\right),$$

where $\otimes$ denotes the Kronecker product between two matrices. $\mathbf { 1 } _ { N }$ stands for an $N \times 1$ vector with unitary elements.
