Theorem 1: Given a CBF $b _ { q } ( \mathbf { x } ( \mathbf { t } ) )$ with relative degree one, let $t _ { i , k } , \ k = 1 , 2 , . . .$ . be determined by (33) with $t _ { i , 0 } =$ $t _ { i } ^ { 0 }$ and $b _ { q , f _ { i } } ^ { \operatorname* { m i n } } ( t _ { i , k } )$ , $b _ { \gamma _ { q } } ^ { \mathrm { m i n } } ( t _ { i , k } )$ , $b _ { q , g _ { i } } ^ { \operatorname* { m i n } } ( t _ { i , k } )$ for $q = \{ 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 \}$ obtained through (27), (29), and (30). Then, any control input $u _ { i , k }$ that satisfies (31) for all $q \in \{ 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 \}$ within the time interval $[ t _ { i , k } , t _ { i , k + 1 } )$ renders the set $C _ { i , 1 }$ forward invariant for the dynamic system defined in (1).

Proof: The proof follows along similar lines as Theorem 2 in [25]. By (25), we can write:

$$\mathbf {y} _ {i} (t) \in S _ {i} (t _ {i, k}), \mathbf {y} _ {r} (t) \in S _ {r} (t _ {i, k}), \mathbf {y} _ {i} (t) \in C _ {i, 1} \tag {34}$$

for all $t \in [ t _ { i , k } , t _ { i , k + 1 } ) , k = 1 , 2 , . . . .$ .

$$L _ {f} b _ {q} (\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)) \geq b _ {q, f _ {i}} ^ {\min} (t _ {k}), \tag {35}\gamma_ {q} (\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)) \geq b _ {\gamma_ {q}} ^ {\min} (t _ {k}), \tag {36}L _ {g} b _ {q} \left(\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)\right) u _ {i} \left(t _ {k}\right) \geq b _ {q, g _ {i}} ^ {\min} \left(t _ {k}\right) u _ {i} \left(t _ {k}\right), \tag {37}$$

for $q \in \{ 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 \}$ , by adding these inequalities which have the same direction it follows that

$$L _ {f} b _ {q} (\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)) + L _ {g} b _ {q} (\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)) u _ {i} (t _ {k}) + \gamma_ {q} (\mathbf {x} _ {i} (t)) \tag {38}\geq b _ {q, f _ {i}} ^ {\min} (t _ {k}) + b _ {q, g _ {i}} ^ {\min} (t _ {k}) u _ {i} (t _ {k}) + b _ {\gamma_ {q}} ^ {\min} (t _ {k}) \geq 0.$$

i.e., (9) is satisfied. By Theorem 1 of [22] applied to (9), if $x _ { i } ( 0 ) \in C _ { i , 1 }$ , then any Lipschitz continuous controller $u _ { i } ( t )$ that satisfies (9) $\forall t \geq 0$ renders $C _ { i , 1 }$ forward invariant for system (1). Therefore, $C _ { i , 1 }$ is forward invariant for the dynamic system defined in (1). □
