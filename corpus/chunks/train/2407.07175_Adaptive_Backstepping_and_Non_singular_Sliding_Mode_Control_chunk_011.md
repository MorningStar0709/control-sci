# A. Adaptive Back Stepping control for translational motion

In order to address the singularity shortcoming of Eulerbased controllers (e.g. [24]), we design Quaternion-based Adaptive Backstepping Controller (QABC) as an effective alternative approach. Its recursive and cascaded implementation of QABC is a potential way to resolve underactuated system complexity. Let $P _ { d } = [ p _ { d 1 } , p _ { d 2 } , p _ { d 3 } ]$ be the desired position trajectories and let Assumption 1 be met. The first step to designing QABC is to compute the error between the true position trajectory and desired one as follows:

Step 1: (Compute position and linear velocity errors) Based on (3), position error of quadrotor $\tilde { P } = [ \tilde { p } _ { x } , \tilde { p } _ { y } , \tilde { p } _ { z } ] ^ { \top }$ is obtained by:

$$\tilde {p} _ {x} = p _ {x} - p _ {d 1}\tilde {p} _ {y} = p _ {y} - p _ {d 2} \tag {7}\tilde {p} _ {z} = p _ {z} - p _ {d 3}$$

Then the linear velocity error is calculated by the derivative of (7):

$$\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {x} = \dot {p} _ {x} - \dot {p} _ {d 1} = v _ {1} - \dot {p} _ {d 1}\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {y} = \dot {p} _ {y} - \dot {p} _ {2 d} = v _ {2} - \dot {p} _ {d 3} \tag {8}\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {z} = \dot {p} _ {z} - \dot {p} _ {3 d} = v _ {3} - \dot {p} _ {d 3}$$

The next step is to define the Lyapunov function candidate to guarantee stability and design virtual controllers necessary to generating the required thrust.

Step 2: Let us formulate controllers to ensure stability and accurate tracking based on the backstepping approach and real value functions as follows:

$$\mathcal {L} _ {1} = \frac {1}{2} \tilde {p} _ {x} ^ {2}, \quad \mathcal {L} _ {3} = \frac {1}{2} \tilde {p} _ {y} ^ {2}, \quad \mathcal {L} _ {5} = \frac {1}{2} \tilde {p} _ {z} ^ {2} \tag {9}$$

Obtaining the derivative of (9) and subsequently substituting (8) in (9), one obtains:

$$\dot {\mathcal {L}} _ {1} = \tilde {p} _ {x} (\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {x}) = \tilde {p} _ {x} (v _ {1} - \dot {p} _ {d 1})\dot {\mathcal {L}} _ {3} = \tilde {p} _ {y} (\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {y}) = \tilde {p} _ {y} (v _ {2} - \dot {p} _ {d 2}) \tag {10}\dot {\mathcal {L}} _ {5} = \tilde {p} _ {z} (\dot {\tilde {p}} _ {z}) = \tilde {p} _ {z} (v _ {3} - \dot {p} _ {d 3})$$

Hence, $v _ { d 1 } , v _ { d 2 }$ , and $v _ { d 3 }$ is defined to ensure the stability of (10) as as follows:

$$v _ {d 1} = - \theta_ {x} \tilde {p} _ {x} + \dot {p} _ {d 1}v _ {d 2} = - \theta_ {y} \tilde {p} _ {y} + \dot {p} _ {d 2} \tag {11}v _ {d 3} = - \theta_ {z} \tilde {p} _ {z} + \dot {p} _ {d 3}$$
