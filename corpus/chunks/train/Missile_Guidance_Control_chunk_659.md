Missile Flight Sections and Guidance Phases: The missile flyout is described by the use of multiple flight sections and guidance phases. The flight sections describe how the missile physically operates, while the guidance phases control the pitching characteristics of the flyout. As discussed in the previous section, all missiles are composed of one or more stages that allow them to fly to their desired target and/or range. Typically, these stages are the booster, sustainer, and reentry vehicle. It is important to remember that the staging events define both mass changes and thrust changes. Booster thrust is usually greater than sustainer thrust, while the reentry thrust is usually zero. Furthermore, some missiles vary their thrust but do not perform staging events until engine burnout. Other missiles fly without ever performing a staging event at all. The flight section methodology discussed here and used by the missile model has been developed to allow modeling of any combination of these staging events or different thrust levels.
