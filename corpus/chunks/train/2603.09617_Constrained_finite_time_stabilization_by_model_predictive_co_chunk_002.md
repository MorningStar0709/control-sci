In principle, finite-time stabilization is often achieved through high-gain control to ensure a fast convergence rate near the origin, leading to aggressive closed-loop system behavior around the initial condition. However, all practical systems have constraints. Aggressive closed-loop behavior can easily violate these limits, which degrades performance or causes instability. A typical strategy for unifying constrained control and finite-time stabilization is the formulation of model predictive control (MPC) schemes endowed with finite-time convergence properties. Some theoretical results can be found, e.g., in [9], [10], [11]. Applications of MPC with finite-time convergence are reported in interior permanent magnet synchronous motors [12], parallel interconnection of buck converters [13], finite-time trajectory tracking control of redundant manipulators [14], finite-time control of mobile manipulators with floating-base [15], finitetime stabilization of DC electrical power systems in More electric aircraft [16], finite-time MPC for Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor [17], and MPC with AC deadbeat control for Modular Multilevel Converter-High-Voltage DC [18]. In all these applications, finite-time MPC plays crucial role to achieve the desired performance and constraint satisfaction.
