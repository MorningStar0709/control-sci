# 2.1. Notations

Throughout this paper, we use $\mathbb { N } , \ \mathbb { R } , \ \mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }$ and $\mathbb { R } _ { > 0 }$ to denote the set of natural, real, nonnegative and positive real numbers, respectively. For $a , b \in \mathbb { N }$ , let $[ a , b ] _ { \mathbb { N } } \triangleq \{ x \in$ N $| \ a \ \leqslant \ x \leqslant b \}$ . For $\mathcal { C } \subset \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ , ∂C denotes its boundary. For $A \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m \times n } , ~ [ A ] _ { i }$ and $[ A ] _ { i j }$ represent its i-th row and $( i , j )$ -th element, respectively. We denote by A† its unique pseudo-inverse and by Range(A) its column space. ${ \bf 1 } _ { n }$ and ${ \bf 0 } _ { n }$ in $\mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ are vectors of all ones and zeros, respectively. A continuous function $\alpha : \mathbb { R }  \mathbb { R }$ is of (extended) class-K if it is strictly increasing and $\alpha ( 0 ) = 0$ . Finally, $\| \cdot \| _ { 1 } , \| \cdot \|$ and $\| \cdot \| _ { \infty }$ are respectively 1-norm, Euclidean norm and infinity norm.
