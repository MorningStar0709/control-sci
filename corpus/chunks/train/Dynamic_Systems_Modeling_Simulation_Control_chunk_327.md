Figure 6.29b shows the current response I(t) for the solenoid coil. Current I(t) rises very quickly from zero when the voltage input is applied at $t = 0 . 0 5$ s and exhibits a brief peak at about 2 A, followed by a small decrease in current, and then a steady rise to a constant value of 3.333 A. The small decrease in current is due to the “back-emf” voltage term $e _ { b } = K I { \dot { x } }$ that is induced by the large positive velocity of the armature as it is lifted off its seat. The back-emf voltage diminishes as the armature’s velocity decreases. Hence, the current quickly begins to increase and rise to its steady value.
