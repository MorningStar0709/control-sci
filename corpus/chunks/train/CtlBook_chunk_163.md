# 5.4.10 Complex Conjugate Zeros

A system can also have complex conjugate zeros. For example

$$G (s) = \frac {s ^ {2} + 4 0 s + 4 0 , 0 0 0}{(s + 0 . 1) (s + 1 0 0 0) ^ {2}}$$

This system with three poles and two zeros has complex conjugate zeros at

$$z _ {1} = - 2 0 + j 1 9 9 \quad z _ {2} = - 2 0 - j 1 9 9$$

Zeros can also be expressed in terms of $\omega _ { n }$ and ζ which in this case are

$$\omega_ {n} = | z _ {i} | = \sqrt {- 2 0 ^ {2} + 1 9 9 ^ {2}} = 2 0 0. 0 \qquad \zeta = - R e (z _ {i}) / \omega_ {n} = - (- 2 0) / 2 0 0 = - 0. 1$$

The frequency response of a complex conjugate pair of zeros is the inverse of a complex conjugate pair of poles. Instead of a peak, there is a dip in the magnitude response and instead of a phase change of $- 1 8 0 ^ { \circ }$ , there is a phase change of +180◦.
