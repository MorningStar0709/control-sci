# A. Physical Power System

The physical power system operation is formulated into a standard AC optimal power flow (ACOPF) problem. The OPF minimizes the system operation costs, i.e., generation costs, subject to physical operational constraints, i.e., power balance, generation limits, and voltage bounds. The physical power network is described by a connected graph $\mathcal { G } \{ \mathcal { N } , \mathcal { E } \}$ , where the set $\mathcal { N } = \{ 0 , 1 , \ldots , n \}$ represents the buses, and the set $\mathcal { E } \subset \mathcal { N } \times \mathcal { N }$ represents the lines. The AC power flow equations can be written as [10]:

$$
\begin{array}{l} P _ {i j} = (V _ {i} ^ {2} - V _ {i} V _ {j} \cos (\theta_ {i} - \theta_ {j})) g _ {i j} \\ - V _ {i} V _ {j} \sin (\theta_ {i} - \theta_ {j}) b _ {i j}, \quad \forall i j \in \mathcal {E} (1a) \\ Q _ {i j} = (V _ {i} V _ {j} \cos (\theta_ {i} - \theta_ {j}) - V _ {i} ^ {2}) b _ {i j} \\ - V _ {i} V _ {j} \sin (\theta_ {i} - \theta_ {j}) g _ {i j}, \quad \forall i j \in \mathcal {E} (1b) \\ \end{array}
\sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} P _ {i j} = \sum_ {g \in \mathcal {G} _ {i}} P _ {i, g} ^ {\text { gen }} - \sum_ {l \in \mathcal {L} _ {i}} P _ {i, l} ^ {\text { load }}, \quad \forall i \in \mathcal {N} \tag {1c}\sum_ {j \in \mathcal {N} _ {i}} Q _ {i j} = \sum_ {g \in \mathcal {G} _ {i}} Q _ {i, g} ^ {\text { gen }} - \sum_ {l \in \mathcal {L} _ {i}} Q _ {i, l} ^ {\text { load }}, \quad \forall i \in \mathcal {N} \tag {1d}$$
