# A. Single-Agent RL Framework

A single-agent framework directly maps the observation, $o = ( e _ { x } , e _ { I _ { x } } , e _ { v } , R , e _ { \Omega } ) \in \mathbb R ^ { 1 2 } \times 5 0 ( 3 )$ , to the action, $a =$ $( T _ { 1 } , T _ { 2 } , T _ { 3 } , T _ { 4 } ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 4 }$ . Here, $e _ { x } = x - x _ { d } \in \mathbb R ^ { 3 } , e _ { v } = v - v _ { d } \in$ $\mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ , and $e _ { \Omega } = \Omega - \Omega _ { d } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ represent the tracking errors of position, velocity, and angular velocity, respectively, while $e _ { I _ { x } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ is the integral error of $e _ { x }$ , to be introduced later in (22). To balance conflicting control objectives, the reward function is defined as

$$
\begin{array}{l} r = - k _ {x} \| e _ {x} \| ^ {2} - k _ {I _ {x}} \| e _ {I _ {x}} \| ^ {2} - k _ {b _ {1}} \| e _ {b _ {1}} \| - k _ {I _ {b _ {1}}} \| e _ {I _ {b _ {1}}} \| \\ - k _ {b _ {3}} \| e _ {b _ {3}} \| - k _ {v} \| e _ {v} \| ^ {2} - k _ {\Omega} \| e _ {\Omega} \| ^ {2} - r _ {\text { crash }}. \tag {19} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $k \in \mathbb { R }$ are positive weighting constants. The first four terms aim to minimize position and heading errors, while the subsequent three terms penalize aggressive motions. Additionally, a severe penalty $r _ { \mathrm { c r a s h } }$ is imposed in the event of a crash or exceeding the predefined flight boundaries.
