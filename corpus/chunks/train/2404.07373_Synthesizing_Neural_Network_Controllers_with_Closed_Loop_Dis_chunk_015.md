# 3.2 Dynamic IQCs

We now extend the result of the previous subsection to a class of dynamic IQCs. Assume ∆ satisfies the IQC defined by (Ψ, M) where

$$
\Psi = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \Psi_ {1} & 0 \\ 0 & \Psi_ {2} \end{array} \right], \quad M = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} I & \\ & - I \end{array} \right], \tag {12}
$$

and Ψ is invertible, stable, and has a stable proper inverse. This class itself can be used to describe, for example, uncertain LTI dynamics [11]. Methods from [25] can be applied to extend what follows to a broader class of dynamic IQCs.
