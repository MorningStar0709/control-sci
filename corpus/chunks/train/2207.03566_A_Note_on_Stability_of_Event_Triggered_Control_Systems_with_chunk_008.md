$$\mathrm{D} _ {-} g (\bar {t}) \geq K \dot {w} (\bar {t}). \tag {12}$$

On the other hand, by Lemma 1 and (8), we get

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm{D} _ {-} g (\bar {t}) \leq \gamma_ {1} g (t _ {0}) + \gamma_ {2} \| g _ {\bar {t}} \| _ {r} \\ <   \gamma_ {1} K w (t _ {0}) + \gamma_ {2} K \| w _ {\bar {t}} \| _ {r} \\ <   \left(\gamma_ {1} + \gamma_ {2}\right) K w (\bar {t}) \\ = \lambda K w (\bar {t}) \\ = K \dot {w} (\bar {t}) \\ \end{array}
$$

where we used (9), (10), (11), and the definition of w in the last two inequalities. This is a contradiction to (12). Therefore, we conclude that $g ( t ) < K w ( t )$ for $t \in ( t _ { 0 } , t _ { 0 } + \Gamma )$ .

Since $K > 1$ is arbitrary, we let $K  1$ and then $g ( t ) \leq w ( t )$ for $t \in [ t _ { 0 } , t _ { 0 } + \Gamma )$ , that is, the proof is completed.
