# B. Continuous-time PDE Backstepping Control

Consider the invertible backstepping transformation

$$
\begin{array}{l} \alpha (x, t) = \bar {w} (x, t) - \int_ {0} ^ {x} K ^ {1 1} (x, \xi) \bar {w} (\xi , t) d \xi \\ - \int_ {0} ^ {x} K ^ {1 2} (x, \xi) \bar {v} (\xi , t) d \xi , \tag {22} \\ \end{array}
\beta (x, t) = \bar {v} (x, t) - \int_ {0} ^ {x} K ^ {2 1} (x, \xi) \bar {w} (\xi , t) d \xi- \int_ {0} ^ {x} K ^ {2 2} (x, \xi) \bar {v} (\xi , t) d \xi , \tag {23}$$

where $K ^ { i j } ( x , \xi ) , i , j \ = \ 1 , 2$ are the kernels that evolve in the triangular domain $\mathcal { T } = \{ ( x , \xi ) : 0 \leq \xi \leq x \leq \ell \}$ and are governed by equations detailed in [46]. Then, adopting standard arguments in PDE backstepping, we can show that the transformation (22),(23), and the continuous-time boundary control law $U ( t )$ derived in [46], given by

$$U (t) = \frac {1}{r _ {1}} \int_ {0} ^ {\ell} \left(K ^ {2 1} (\ell , \xi) \bar {w} (\xi , t) + K ^ {2 2} (\ell , \xi) \bar {v} (\xi , t)\right) d \xi , \tag {24}$$

map the system (13)-(21) into the target $( \alpha , \beta )$ -system:

$$\partial_ {t} \alpha + v ^ {\star} \partial_ {x} \alpha = 0, \tag {25}\partial_ {t} \beta - (\gamma p ^ {\star} - v ^ {\star}) \partial_ {x} \beta = 0, \tag {26}\alpha (0, t) = - r _ {0} \beta (0, t), \tag {27}\beta (\ell , t) = 0. \tag {28}$$

The inverse transformation of (22),(23) is given by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \bar {w} (x, t) = \alpha (x, t) + \int_ {0} ^ {x} L ^ {1 1} (x, \xi) \alpha (\xi , t) d \xi \\ + \int_ {0} ^ {x} L ^ {1 2} (x, \xi) \beta (\xi , t) d \xi , \tag {29} \\ \end{array}
\bar {v} (x, t) = \beta (x, t) + \int_ {0} ^ {x} L ^ {2 1} (x, \xi) \alpha (\xi , t) d \xi+ \int_ {0} ^ {x} L ^ {2 2} (x, \xi) \beta (\xi , t) d \xi , \tag {30}$$

where kernels $L ^ { i j } ( x , \xi ) , i , j = 1 , 2$ are a specific case of the general form of kernel equations detailed in [36]. The input $U ( t )$ can also be expressed in target system $( \alpha , \beta )$ states as

$$U (t) = \frac {1}{r _ {1}} \int_ {0} ^ {\ell} \left(L ^ {2 1} (\ell , \xi) \alpha (\xi , t) + L ^ {2 2} (\ell , \xi) \beta (\xi , t)\right) d \xi . \tag {31}$$
