Secondly, we consider the problem of object manipulation by means of rolling contacts. We present a centralized control algorithm that achieves trajectory tracking by the object as well as a decentralized extension using event-triggered communication, still without using force/torque feedback. Both schemes employ adaptive control ideas to compensate for potential uncertainties in the agents’ and the object’s dynamic parameters and do not use information regarding the object’s center of mass, since the tracking concerns an observable point on the object. Contact slip avoidance is also guaranteed by novel optimization algorithms. Simulation and experimental results support the theoretical findings.
