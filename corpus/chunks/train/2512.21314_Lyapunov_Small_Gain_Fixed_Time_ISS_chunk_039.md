1) The Nash equilibrium of the quasi-steady state game is unique and satisfies $u ^ { * } = \arg \operatorname* { m i n } _ { u } P ( u )$ .   
2) The function P is µ-strongly convex, i.e., there exists $\mu > 0$ such that the following holds

$$P (\hat {u}) \geq P (u) + \nabla P (u) ^ {\top} (\hat {u} - u) + \frac {\mu}{2} | \hat {u} - u | ^ {2}, \tag {45}$$

for all $u , \hat { u } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { N }$ .

3) There exists $K > 0$ such that

$$| \mathcal {G} (\hat {x}, u) - \mathcal {G} (x, u) | \leq K | \hat {x} - x |,$$

for all $x , \hat { x } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ and $u \in \mathbb { R } ^ { N }$ .
