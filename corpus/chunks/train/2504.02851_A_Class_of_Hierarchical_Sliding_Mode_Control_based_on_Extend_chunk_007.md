# II. NEWTON-EULER RIGID BODY MODEL

The quadrotor UAVs are aligned with 4 propellers, each considering rotation velocity $\omega _ { i } .$ which creates forces and torques acting on the located center of mass (CoM). The model of the UAVs is shown in Figure 1. To model the quadrotor UAVs, the following assumptions are made:

• The UAV’s structure is rigid and symmetrical.   
• The angular motion of the UAVs is low amplitude.   
• The propellers are supposed to be rigid; the thrust and drag of the propellers are proportional to the square of the propeller’s speed.

The linear velocity of the UAVs in the Earth frame is defined by: $\dot { \pmb { \xi } } = [ \dot { x } , \dot { y } , \dot { z } ] ^ { T }$ . The roll-pitch-yaw angle of the UAVs is defined by ϕ, θ, ψ. The Newton-Euler equation of the quadrotor UAVs motion is given as in [34] by:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} m \ddot {\boldsymbol {\xi}} = \mathbf {F} _ {b} + \mathbf {F} _ {d} + \mathbf {F} _ {g} \\ \mathbf {J} _ {M} \dot {\boldsymbol {\Omega}} = - \boldsymbol {\Omega} \wedge \mathbf {J} _ {M} \boldsymbol {\Omega} + \boldsymbol {\Gamma} _ {f} - \boldsymbol {\Gamma} _ {g} \end{array} \right. \tag {1}
$$

where m is the mass of the $\mathbf { U A V s ; } \mathbf { F } _ { b } , \mathbf { F } _ { d } , \mathbf { F } _ { g }$ are the resultant of the forces generated by the propeller rotors, the resultant of the drag forces, and the gravity forces, respectively. ∧ denotes the cross product of two vectors. Ω is the angular velocity in the body frame $O _ { B }$ and is defined as:

$$
\boldsymbol {\Omega} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c} 1 & 0 & - \sin \theta \\ 0 & \cos \phi & \cos \theta \sin \phi \\ 0 & - \sin \phi & \cos \phi \cos \theta \end{array} \right) \left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {\phi} \\ \dot {\theta} \\ \dot {\psi} \end{array} \right]. \tag {2}
$$

In this research, we assume that the angular motion of the UAVs is low amplitude so that Ω can be assimilated to $[ \dot { \phi } , \dot { \theta } , \dot { \psi } ] ^ { T } ;$ ; and $\mathbf { J } _ { M }$ is the inertial moments matrix. As mentioned before, the UAVs are symmetrical, and the inertial moment’s matrix is defined as:

$$\mathbf {J} _ {M} = \mathrm{diag} (I _ {x x}, I _ {y y}, I _ {z z}). \tag {3}$$
