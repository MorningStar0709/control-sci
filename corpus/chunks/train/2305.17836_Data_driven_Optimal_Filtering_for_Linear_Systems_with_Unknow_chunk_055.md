# E.1 Proof of lemma 3: Derivation of stochastic gradient formula

Proof. For small enough $\Delta \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n \times m }$ ,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \varepsilon (L + \Delta , \mathcal {Y}) - \varepsilon (L, \mathcal {Y}) = \| e _ {T} (L + \Delta) \| ^ {2} - \| e _ {T} (L) \| ^ {2} \\ = 2 \mathrm{tr} \left[ (e _ {T} (L + \Delta) - e _ {T} (L)) e _ {T} ^ {\intercal} (L) \right] + o (\| \Delta \|)). \\ \end{array}
$$

The difference

$$e _ {T} (L + \Delta) - e _ {T} (L) = E _ {1} (\Delta) + E _ {2} (\Delta) + o (\| \Delta \|),$$

with the following terms that are linear in $\Delta \colon$

$$
\begin{array}{l} E _ {1} (\Delta) := - \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {T - 1} H (A _ {L}) ^ {t} \Delta y (T - t - 1), \\ E _ {2} (\Delta) := \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {T - 1} \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {t} H (A _ {L}) ^ {t - k} \Delta H (A _ {L}) ^ {k - 1} L y (T - t - 1). \\ \end{array}
$$

Therefore, combining the two identities, the definition of gradient under the inner product $\langle A , B \rangle : =$ $\operatorname { t r } \left[ A B ^ { \boldsymbol { \mathsf { T } } } \right]$ , and ignoring the higher order terms in ∆ yields,

$$\langle \nabla_ {L} \varepsilon (L, y), \Delta \rangle = 2 \mathrm{tr} \left[ (E _ {1} (\Delta) + E _ {2} (\Delta)) e _ {T} ^ {\intercal} (L) \right],$$

which by linearity and cyclic permutation property of trace reduces to:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \langle \nabla_ {L} \varepsilon (L, y), \Delta \rangle = - 2 \mathrm{tr} \left[ \Delta \left(\sum_ {t = 0} ^ {T - 1} y (T - t - 1) e _ {T} ^ {\intercal} (L) H (A _ {L}) ^ {t}\right) \right] \\ + 2 \operatorname{tr} \left[ \Delta \left(\sum_ {t = 1} ^ {T - 1} \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {t} H (A _ {L}) ^ {k - 1} L y (T - t - 1) e _ {T} ^ {\intercal} (L) H (A _ {L}) ^ {t - k}\right) \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

This holds for all admissible ∆, concluding the formula for the gradient.
