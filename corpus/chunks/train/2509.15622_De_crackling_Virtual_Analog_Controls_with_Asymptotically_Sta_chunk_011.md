$$\left\| \mathbf {f} _ {t + 1} \odot \mathbf {c} _ {t} + \mathbf {i} _ {t + 1} \odot \mathbf {g} _ {t + 1} \right\| \leq \left\| \left(\mathbf {f} _ {t + 1} + \mathbf {i} _ {t + 1}\right) \odot \mathbf {c} _ {t} \right\| \tag {32}$$

If we then restrict the parameters of the input gate it and forget gate ft such that

$$\left\| \left(\mathbf {f} _ {t + 1} + \mathbf {i} _ {t + 1}\right) \odot \mathbf {c} _ {t} \right\| < \| \mathbf {c} _ {t} \|, \tag {33}$$

we get from Eq. (32) and (31) that

$$\left\| \mathbf {c} _ {t + 1} \right\| < \left\| \mathbf {c} _ {t} \right\| \Rightarrow \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \left\| \mathbf {c} _ {\mathbf {t}} \right\| = 0. \tag {34}$$

Eq. (33) holds when

$$\left\| \mathbf {f} _ {t + 1} + \mathbf {i} _ {t + 1} \right\| _ {\infty} < 1. \tag {35}$$

In conclusion, when Eq. (30) and (35) hold, the system is asymptotically stable.
