# 5.1 Evaluating the Collaborative Game

For the collaborative case, we define that agent c0’s goal is to satisfy a formula φ , and agent c1’s goal is to satisfy a formula ψ, given by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \phi : \{\overline {{{a}}} \overline {{{b}}} \} \rightarrow \mathbf {F} \{a \overline {{{b}}} \} \rightarrow \mathbf {X} \{a b \} \\ \psi : \{\bar {a} \bar {b} \} \rightarrow \mathbf {X} \{\bar {a} b \} \rightarrow \mathbf {F} \{a b \} \tag {5} \\ \end{array}
$$

For the collaborative case, if agents do not sense the environment after the initial set up, we simply permute the set of agents’ actions per iteration, and apply a the generated sequence to the environment. All permutations are equally likely through the Fisher-Yates method33. For the cases where agents sense the environment (potentially aborting their action issuing in the case of a failed application of their action), we permute in a staggered fashion, interleaving sets bookmarked by agent sensing.

It is unnecessary to list all possible runs, but assuming an initial state of {ab}, it is trivial to deduce the two optimal runs that satisfy both φ and ψ fairly:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\langle \{\overline {{a}} \overline {{b}} \} _ {t E = 0}, \{\overline {{a}} b \} _ {t E = 1}, \{\overline {{a}} \overline {{b}} \} _ {t E = 2}, \{a \overline {{b}} \} _ {t E = 3}, \{a b \} _ {t E = 4} \right\rangle \\ \left\langle \{\overline {{a}} \overline {{b}} \} _ {t E = 0}, \{\overline {{a}} b \} _ {t E = 1}, \{a b \} _ {t E = 2}, \{a \overline {{b}} \} _ {t E = 3}, \{a b \} _ {t E = 4} \right\rangle \\ \end{array}
$$

That these exist suffices to show the game is collaborative. Notice that the former satisfies both formulae at the same time, whilst the latter satisfies ψ before φ .
