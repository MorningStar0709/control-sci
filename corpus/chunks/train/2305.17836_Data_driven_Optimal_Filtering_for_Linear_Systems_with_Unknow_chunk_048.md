# D.6 Proof of Lipschitz property in Lemma 2

Next, we provide the proof of locally Lipschitz property. Notice that the mappings $L \to X _ { ( L ) }$ , $L \to Y _ { ( L ) }$ and $L  A _ { L }$ are all real-analytic on the open set $s \supset s _ { \alpha } .$ , and thus so is the mapping $L  \nabla J ( L ) = 2 Y _ { ( L ) } [ - L R + A _ { L } X _ { ( L ) } H ^ { \intercal } ]$ . Also, by Lemma $1 , S _ { \alpha }$ is compact and therefore the mapping $L \to \nabla J ( \vec { L } )$ is ℓ-Lipschitz continuous on $S _ { \alpha }$ for some $\ell = \ell ( \alpha ) > 0$ . In the rest of the proof, we attempt to characterize $\ell ( \alpha )$ in terms of the problem parameters. By direct computation we obtain

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla J (L _ {1}) - \nabla J (L _ {2}) = \left(2 Y _ {(L _ {1})} - 2 Y _ {(L _ {2})}\right) \left[ - L _ {1} R + A _ {L _ {1}} X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal} \right] \\ + 2 Y _ {(L _ {2})} \left(\left[ - L _ {1} R + A _ {L _ {1}} X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal} \right] - \left[ - L _ {2} R + A _ {L _ {2}} X _ {(L _ {2})} H ^ {\intercal} \right]\right) \\ = 2 \left(Y _ {\left(L _ {1}\right)} - Y _ {\left(L _ {2}\right)}\right) \left[ - L _ {1} \left(R + H X _ {\left(L _ {1}\right)} H ^ {\intercal}\right) + A X _ {\left(L _ {1}\right)} H ^ {\intercal} \right] \\ + 2 Y _ {(L _ {2})} \left[ (L _ {2} - L _ {1}) (R + H X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal}) + A _ {L _ {2}} (X _ {(L _ {1})} - X _ {(L _ {2})}) H ^ {\intercal} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

Therefore,

$$\left\| \nabla J \left(L _ {1}\right) - \nabla J \left(L _ {2}\right) \right\| _ {F} ^ {2} \leq \ell_ {1} ^ {2} \left\| Y _ {\left(L _ {1}\right)} - Y _ {\left(L _ {2}\right)} \right\| _ {F} ^ {2} + \ell_ {2} ^ {2} \left\| L _ {1} - L _ {2} \right\| _ {F} ^ {2} + \ell_ {3} ^ {2} \left\| X _ {\left(L _ {1}\right)} - X _ {\left(L _ {2}\right)} \right\| _ {F} ^ {2} \tag {25}$$

where

$$\ell_ {1} = \ell_ {1} (L _ {1}) := 2 \| - L _ {1} (R + H X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal}) + A X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal} \| _ {\mathrm{op}},\ell_ {2} = \ell_ {2} (L _ {1}, L _ {2}) := 2 \| Y _ {(L _ {2})} \| _ {\mathrm{op}} \| R + H X _ {(L _ {1})} H ^ {\intercal} \| _ {\mathrm{op}},\ell_ {3} = \ell_ {3} (L _ {2}) := 2 \| Y _ {(L _ {2})} \| _ {\mathrm{op}} \| A _ {L _ {2}} \| _ {\mathrm{op}} \| H ^ {\intercal} \| _ {\mathrm{op}}.$$
