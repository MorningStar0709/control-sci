# 2.2 Stability-Guaranteed Dead-Zone Criterion

Because the two EKFs are coupled, simultaneous adaptation can cause divergence under large innovation or model mismatch. To ensure stability, we analyze the local error dynamics of the state EKF and use the result to define a dead zone for the parameter EKF.

Let $\hat { x } _ { k \mid k }$ be the state estimate and define

$$\tilde {x} _ {k} = x _ {k} - \hat {x} _ {k | k}. \tag {16}$$

To eliminate the explicit input dependence in the state equation, introduce the shifted variables

$$\hat {z} _ {k} = \hat {x} _ {k | k} + A ^ {- 1} B u _ {k - 1}, \tilde {z} _ {k} = x _ {k} + A ^ {- 1} B u _ {k - 1} - \hat {z} _ {k}. \tag {17}$$

Since $\tilde { z } _ { k }$ and $\tilde { x } _ { k }$ differ only by a known quantity, boundedness of one implies boundedness of the other.

Using the prediction–update form of the state EKF and collecting terms, the error dynamics can be written compactly as

$$\tilde {z} _ {k + 1} = A \tilde {z} _ {k} + \tilde {w} _ {k}, \tag {18}$$

with an effective disturbance

$$\tilde {w} _ {k} = w _ {k} + K _ {k + 1} E _ {k + 1}, \tag {19}$$

where $K _ { k + 1 }$ is the Kalman gain and

$$E _ {k + 1} = y _ {k + 1} - \left(C \hat {x} _ {k + 1 | k} + D u _ {k + 1}\right) \tag {20}$$

is the innovation of the state EKF in the linearized model. Because the true output $y _ { k + 1 }$ and the predicted output share the same input $u _ { k + 1 }$ , the direct input term $D u _ { k + 1 }$ cancels out in the error dynamics and does not affect (18).

For Lyapunov inequality. Assume that A is Schur. Then, for any $ { \hat { Q } } = Q ^ { \top } >  { \hat { 0 } }$ , the discrete-time Lyapunov equation

$$A ^ {\top} P A - P = - Q \tag {21}$$

admits a unique solution $P ~ = ~ P ^ { \top } ~ > ~ 0$ . Consider the Lyapunov function

$$V _ {k} = \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} P \tilde {z} _ {k}. \tag {22}$$

Using (18), the one-step increment is

$$
\begin{array}{l} \Delta V _ {k + 1} := V _ {k + 1} - V _ {k} \\ = \left(A \tilde {z} _ {k} + \tilde {w} _ {k}\right) ^ {\top} P \left(A \tilde {z} _ {k} + \tilde {w} _ {k}\right) - \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} P \tilde {z} _ {k} \\ = \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} (A ^ {\top} P A - P) \tilde {z} _ {k} + 2 \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k} + \tilde {w} _ {k} ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k} \\ = - \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} Q \tilde {z} _ {k} + 2 \tilde {z} _ {k} ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k} + \tilde {w} _ {k} ^ {\top} P \tilde {w} _ {k}. \tag {23} \\ \end{array}
$$
