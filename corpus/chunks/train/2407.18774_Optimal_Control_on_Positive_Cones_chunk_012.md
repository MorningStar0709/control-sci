$$
\operatorname{tr} \left(\left[ \begin{array}{c c} S & R _ {1} \\ R _ {1} ^ {\top} & R _ {2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X (t) & U _ {1} (t) \\ U _ {1} (t) ^ {\top} & U _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right]\right) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (t) \\ u (t) \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} S & R _ {1} \\ R _ {1} ^ {\top} & R _ {2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (t) \\ u (t) \end{array} \right].
$$

That is, under (10), the costs and constraints in the standard LQR problem, and the matrix optimization problem, are identical. It then follows (with a little extra work) that when the initial condition $X _ { 0 }$ is rank 1, that the studied matrix optimization collapses to the standard LQR problem.
