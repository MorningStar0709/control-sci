PROOF. For vector-valued functions, Taylor’s Theorem can be applied to each dimension. By applying Taylor’s Theorem about the origin,

$$
\zeta (x) = \mathbf {J} _ {\zeta} x + \frac {1}{2} \left[ \begin{array}{c} x ^ {\top} \mathbf {H} _ {\zeta^ {(1)}} (z _ {\zeta^ {(1)}}) \\ \vdots \\ x ^ {\top} \mathbf {H} _ {\zeta^ {(m)}} (z _ {\zeta^ {(m)}}) \end{array} \right] x =: (\mathbf {A} _ {1} + \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {A} _ {2}) x
$$

and likewise

$$
\theta (x) = \mathbf {J} _ {\theta} x + \frac {1}{2} \left[ \begin{array}{c} x ^ {\top} \mathbf {H} _ {\theta^ {(1)}} (\mathbf {z} _ {\theta^ {(1)}}) \\ \vdots \\ x ^ {\top} \mathbf {H} _ {\theta^ {(n)}} (z _ {\theta^ {(n)}}) \end{array} \right] x =: (\mathbf {B} _ {1} + \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {B} _ {2}) x,
$$
