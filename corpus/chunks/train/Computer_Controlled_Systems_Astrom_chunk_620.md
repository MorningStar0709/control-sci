# ARMA Processes

Large classes of stochastic processes can be generated by driving linear systems with white noise. Let $\{e(k), k = \ldots, -1, 0, 1, \ldots\}$ be discrete-time white noise. The process generated by

$$y (k) = e (k) + b _ {1} e (k - 1) + \dots + b _ {n} e (k - n)$$

is called a moving average, or an MA process. The process generated by

$$y (k) + a _ {1} y (k - 1) + \dots + a _ {n} y (k - n) = e (k)$$

is called an autoregression, or an AR process. The process

$$y (k) + a _ {1} y (k - 1) + \dots + a _ {n} y (k - n) = e (k) + b _ {1} e (k - 1) + \dots + b _ {n} e (k - n)$$

is called an ARMA process. The process

$$
\begin{array}{l} y (k) + a _ {1} y (k - 1) + \dots + a _ {n} y (k - n) = b _ {0} u (k - d) + \dots \\ + b _ {m} u (k - d - m) + e (k) + c _ {1} e (k - 1) + \dots + c _ {n} e (k - n) \\ \end{array}
$$

is called an ARMAX process, that is, an ARMA process with an exogenous signal.
