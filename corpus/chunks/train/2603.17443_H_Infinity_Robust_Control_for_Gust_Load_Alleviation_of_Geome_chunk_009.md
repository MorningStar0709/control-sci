# 2.2 Model Order Reduction

The NMOR procedure [Da Ronch et al., 2013a,b, Tantaroudas et al., 2015] projects the system onto m biorthonormal eigenvectors of the Jacobian: ∆w ≈ Φz, $\Psi ^ { H } \Phi = \mathbf { I } _ { m } .$ yielding:

$$\dot {z} _ {k} = \lambda_ {k} z _ {k} + \sum_ {i, j} D _ {k i j} z _ {i} z _ {j} + \boldsymbol {\psi} _ {k} ^ {H} \mathbf {B} _ {c} \Delta \mathbf {u} _ {c} + \boldsymbol {\psi} _ {k} ^ {H} \mathbf {B} _ {g} \Delta \mathbf {u} _ {d} \tag {3}$$

For $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ control design, the linear ROM (neglecting $D _ { k i j }$ terms) is used as the design plant. The nonlinear terms are retained in the validation model to assess controller robustness against unmodelled nonlinearities.
