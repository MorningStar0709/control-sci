# ii) Rotational Dynamics:

We describe the rotation of quadrotor within Boby-fixed reference frame:

$$\dot {\omega} _ {b / e} ^ {b f} = \left(I ^ {b f}\right) ^ {- 1} \left[ \bar {U} - \omega_ {b / e} ^ {b f} \times I ^ {b f} \omega_ {b / e} ^ {b f} \right] \tag {8}$$

Here, $\overline { { U } } = [ U _ { 2 } , U _ { 3 } , U _ { 4 } ] ^ { T }$ , the inertia matrix is equation (9), and because of symmetry this has a relatively simple form:

$$
I ^ {b f} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} I _ {x x} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & I _ {y y} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & I _ {z z} \end{array} \right] \tag {9}
$$

Expanding equation (8), we derive the expression for angular acceleration.

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {p} = \frac {1}{I _ {x x}} (U _ {2} + (I _ {y y} - I _ {z z}) q r) \\ \dot {q} = \frac {1}{I _ {y y}} (U _ {3} + (I _ {z z} - I _ {x x}) p r) \\ \dot {r} = \frac {1}{I _ {z z}} (U _ {4} + (I _ {x x} - I _ {y y}) p q) \end{array} \right. \tag {10}
$$

The quadratic terms of angular velocity are higher-order small quantities, and the resulting gyroscopic coupling torque is negligible compared to the control torque. Therefore, Equation (10) can be reasonably simplified into the following linear decoupled form:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {p} = \frac {1}{I _ {x x}} U _ {2} \\ \dot {q} = \frac {1}{I _ {y y}} U _ {3} \\ \dot {r} = \frac {1}{I _ {z z}} U _ {4} \end{array} \right. \tag {11}
$$

Equations (7) and (11) collectively form the complete nonlinear dynamic model of the quadrotor. This model precisely describes how control inputs influence the motion of quadrotor, serving as the mathematical foundation for subsequent controller design and simulation.
