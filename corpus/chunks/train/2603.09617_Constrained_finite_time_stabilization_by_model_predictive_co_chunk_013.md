# B. Implementation via finite-horizon MPC

Set a finite control horizon $N \geq n .$ . The equivalent finitehorizon cost function can be designed by

$$J _ {f} (k) = \sum_ {i = n} ^ {N - 1} \left(\| x (i | k) \| _ {Q} ^ {2} + \| u (i | k) \| _ {R} ^ {2}\right) + \| x (N | k) \| _ {P} ^ {2}, \tag {23}$$

where the summation is from n to N − 1; the weighting matrix P is solved from Lyapunov equation (10). The finitetime MPC is constructed in the implementable finite-horizon framework:

$$\left[ U ^ {*} (k), X ^ {*} (k) \right] = \arg \min _ {U (k), X (k)} J _ {f} (k), \tag {24}$$

subject to constraints (6)–(8) for finite $i = 0 , \ldots , N - 1$ , and

$$x (N | k) \in \mathcal {X} _ {f}, \tag {25}$$

where $\mathcal { X } _ { f }$ is an invariant terminal set satisfying

$$\mathcal {X} _ {f} \subset \mathcal {X}, \quad - K \mathcal {X} _ {f} \subset \mathcal {U}, \quad (A - b K) \mathcal {X} _ {f} \subset \mathcal {X} _ {f}. \tag {26}$$

Then, the finite-time MPC is implemented via

$$u (k) = [ 1, 0, \dots , 0 ] _ {1 \times N} U ^ {*} (k), \tag {27}$$

where $U ^ { * } ( k )$ is solved from the optimization (24).

Remark 6: Feedback gain K does not have to be deadbeat gain $K _ { d b }$ or LQR gain $K _ { L Q R }$ .

Remark 7: The terminal constraint can be obtained by

$$\mathcal {X} _ {f} = \{x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {2} | x ^ {T} P x \leq \varepsilon \}, \tag {28}$$

where $\varepsilon > 0$ is solved from quadratic programming:

$$\varepsilon = \max _ {x \in \mathcal {X}, K x \in \mathcal {U}} x ^ {T} P x. \tag {29}$$

Theorem 2: Consider the linear discrete-time plant (3) subject to constraints (4). Design the cost function by (23), where the summation is from n to $N - 1$ , and the terminal weight P is solved from Lyapunov equation (10). If the constrained optimization (24) is feasible initially, and the MPC is implemented by (24)–(27), then,

1) the constrained optimization (24) is feasible recursively;   
2) there exist a finite $T > 0 ,$ , such that the closed-loop state $x ( k ) = 0$ whenever $k > T$ .

Proof: With the terminal constraint (25) and the Lyapunov equation (10), it holds that, at time k, there exists $u ^ { * } ( i | k )$ for $i = N + 1 , N + 2 ,$ · · · such that

$$\left\| x ^ {*} (N | k) \right\| _ {P} ^ {2} = \sum_ {i = N} ^ {\infty} \left(\left\| x ^ {*} (i | k) \right\| _ {Q} ^ {2} + \left\| u ^ {*} (i | k) \right\| _ {R} ^ {2}\right),$$

and therefore

$$J _ {f} ^ {*} (k) = \sum_ {i = n} ^ {\infty} \left(\| x ^ {*} (i | k) \| _ {Q} ^ {2} + \| u ^ {*} (i | k) \| _ {R} ^ {2}\right).$$

The rest of the proof can be completed by following the steps in Theorem 1.

Remark 8: Although it is finite, the control horizon N can be chosen sufficiently large to improve initial feasibility.

Remark 9: Neither terminal equality constraints nor switching techniques are applied in the implementable finitetime MPC.

Remark 10: Results in [23] can be regarded as the special case of Theorem 2 with $N = n$ .
