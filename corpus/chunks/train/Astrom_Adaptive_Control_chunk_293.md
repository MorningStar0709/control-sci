# Lyapunov Theory for Time-variable Systems

We now consider time-variable differential equations of the type

$$\frac {d x}{d t} = f (x, t) \tag {5.25}$$

The origin is an equilibrium point for Eq. (5.25) if $f(0, t) = 0 \forall t \geq 0$ . It is assumed that $f$ is such that solutions exist for all $t \geq t_0$ . To guarantee this, it is assumed that $f$ is piecewise continuous in $t$ and locally Lipschitz in $x$ in a neighborhood of $x(t) = 0$ . We now investigate the stability of the solution $x(t) = 0$ .

In the time-varying case the solution will depend on t as well as on the starting time $t_{0}$ . This implies that the bound $\delta$ in Definition 5.1 will depend on $\varepsilon$ and $t_{0}$ .
