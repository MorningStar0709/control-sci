The line of sight (LOS), λ, is defined as the angle between a line from the center of the seeker antenna to the target, and some arbitrary nonrotating (e.g., inertial) reference line. Commonly, it is convenient to select this reference equal to the LOS position at the beginning of the homing guidance phase. Consequently, λ(t) at time t is the total change in the angular position of the LOS relative to the initial LOS. Referring to Figure 3.24, we note that the angular position of the missile body centerline, $\theta _ { m }$ , is measured relative to the initial LOS. (Note that $\theta _ { m }$ is identified as the pitch angle.) Furthermore, the angular position of the antenna centerline is defined by the gimbal angle, $\theta _ { h }$ . Therefore, the LOS angle λ is given by

$$\lambda = \theta_ {m} + \theta_ {h} + \varepsilon , \tag {3.73a}$$

where ε is the true boresight error, that is, the error between antenna center line and line of sight to the target. Alternatively, by writing (3.73a) as

$$\varepsilon = \lambda - \theta_ {m} - \theta_ {h}, \tag {3.73b}$$
