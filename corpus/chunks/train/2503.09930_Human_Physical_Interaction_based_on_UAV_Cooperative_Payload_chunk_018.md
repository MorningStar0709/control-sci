Theorem 1. Given the system dynamics outlined in (11) and the FNTSM surfaces specified in (31), the proposed control law in (35) guarantees asymptotic stability of the closed-loop system. Additionally, the system trajectories can reach to the equilibrium state on the FNTSM $( S _ { \Phi } = 0 )$ within a finite time.

Proof: To prove Theorem 1, let $V _ { \Phi }$ be a Lyapunov function candidate as follows:

$$V _ {\Phi} = \frac {1}{2} S _ {\Phi} ^ {\textcircled {e} 2}, \quad (V _ {\Phi} \geqslant 0 \forall \text { elements of } S _ {\Phi}) \tag {37}$$

The time derivative of (37) is:

$$\dot {V} _ {\Phi} = S _ {\Phi} \odot \dot {S} _ {\Phi} \tag {38}$$

Substituting (33) into (38) results in the following:

$$\dot {V} _ {\Phi} = \left[ \ddot {\eta} _ {d \Phi} - \mathcal {Q} (\eta) - b (\eta) \mathcal {U} + \left(\zeta + \gamma \varepsilon | E _ {\Phi} | ^ {\varepsilon - 1}\right) \dot {E} _ {\Phi} \right] \odot S _ {\Phi} \tag {39}$$

Substituting (35) into (39) results in the following:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} _ {\Phi} = [ - \kappa_ {1} S _ {\Phi} - \kappa_ {2} s g n (S _ {\Phi}) ] \odot S _ {\Phi} \\ = - \kappa_ {1} S _ {\Phi} ^ {\textcircled {e} 2} - \kappa_ {2} | S _ {\Phi} | \leqslant 0, \forall S _ {\Phi} \\ \end{array}
$$

which ensures asymptotic convergence. To prove convergence to the sliding surfaces $( S _ { \Phi } = 0 )$ in a finite time, define tr as the reaching time and let us rewrite (40) as follows:

$$\dot {V} _ {\Phi} = \frac {d V _ {\Phi}}{d t} = - \kappa_ {1} S _ {\Phi} ^ {(e) 2} - \kappa_ {2} | S _ {\Phi} | \tag {41}$$

Using (37), The expression in (41) can be rewritten as follows:

$$\frac {d V _ {\Phi}}{d t} \leqslant - 2 \kappa_ {1} V _ {\Phi} - \kappa_ {2} \sqrt {2 V _ {\Phi}}, \text { s.t. } d t \leqslant \frac {- d V _ {\Phi}}{2 \kappa_ {1} V _ {\Phi} + \alpha V _ {\Phi} ^ {1 / 2}} \tag {42}$$

where $\alpha = \sqrt { 2 } \kappa _ { 2 }$ , then integrate both sides of (42) such that:

$$\int_ {0} ^ {t _ {r}} d t \leqslant \int_ {V _ {\Phi} (0)} ^ {V _ {\Phi} (t _ {r})} \frac {- d V _ {\Phi}}{2 \kappa_ {1} V _ {\Phi} + \alpha V _ {\Phi} ^ {1 / 2}} \tag {43}$$

Integrating (43) shows that the reaching time (tr) can be calculated as:

$$t _ {r} \leqslant \frac {1}{\kappa_ {1}} \ln \left| \frac {2 \kappa_ {1} V _ {\Phi} (0) ^ {1 / 2} + \alpha}{\alpha} \right| \tag {44}$$

Based on (44), the system trajectories will reach $( S _ { \Phi } = 0 )$ and the errors will converge to the origin within a finite time.
