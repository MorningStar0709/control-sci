# 4.10 Notes and References

Pole placement was one of the first applications of the state-space approach. One of the first to solve the problem was J. Bertram in 1959. The first published solution is given in Rissanen (1960). Treatment of the multivariable case of pole placement can be found, for instance, in Rosenbrock (1970), Wolowich (1974), and Kailath (1980). Observers are also described in the preceding books. The reduced-order observer was first described in a Ph.D. thesis by Luenberger. Easier available references are Luenberger (1964, 1971).

The servo problem and introduction of reference values are discussed in Wittenmark (1985a). Numerical aspects of computing the state feedback and the observer gain are discussed in Miminis and Paige (1982), Petkov, Christov, and Konstantinov (1984), and Miminis and Paige (1988).
