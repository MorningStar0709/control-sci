Proof of Lemma 2.1:

Define a new variable

$$z (t) = \int_ {0} ^ {t} e (\tau) d \tau - \frac {d}{k _ {i}} \tag {66}$$

Then, we get

$$\dot {z} (t) = e (t), \ddot {z} (t) = \dot {e} (t), \dddot {z} (t) = \ddot {e} (t) \tag {67}$$

Substituting the relations (66) and (67) into system (6), we can rewrite the system (6) as

$$\ddot {z} (t) + k _ {d} \ddot {z} (t) + k _ {p} \dot {z} (t) + k _ {i} z (t) = 0 \tag {68}$$

The characteristic equation of system (68) can be expressed by

$$s ^ {3} + k _ {d} s ^ {2} + k _ {p} s + k _ {i} = 0 \tag {69}$$

According to the stability conditions, for system (69), if $k _ { p } , ~ k _ { i }$ and $k _ { d }$ are selected to make the real parts of all the roots of the characteristic equation (69) negative, then system is exponentially stable, and we get

$$\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} z (t) = 0, \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \dot {z} (t) = 0, \text { and } \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \ddot {z} (t) = 0 \tag {70}$$

For (70), from the relations (66) and (67), we get

$$\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \int_ {0} ^ {t} e (\tau) d \tau = \frac {d}{k _ {i}}, \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} e (t) = 0, \text { and } \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \dot {e} (t) = 0 \tag {71}$$

This concludes the proof. 

Proof of Lemma 2.2:

Define a new variable

$$z (t) = \int_ {0} ^ {t} e (\tau) d \tau - \frac {d}{k _ {i}} \tag {72}$$

Then, we get

$$\dot {z} (t) = e (t), \ddot {z} (t) = \dot {e} (t) \tag {73}$$

Substituting the relations (72) and (73) into system (11), we can rewrite the system (11) as

$$\ddot {z} (t) + k _ {p} \dot {z} (t) + k _ {i} z (t) = 0 \tag {74}$$

The characteristic equation of system (74) can be expressed by

$$s ^ {2} + k _ {p} s + k _ {i} = 0 \tag {75}$$

According to the stability conditions, for system (74), if $k _ { p }$ and $k _ { i }$ are selected to make the real parts of all the roots of the characteristic equation (75) negative, then system is exponentially stable, and we get

$$\lim _ {t \to \infty} z (t) = 0, \text { and } \lim _ {t \to \infty} \dot {z} (t) = 0 \tag {76}$$

For (76), from the relations (72) and (73), we get

$$\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \int_ {0} ^ {t} e (\tau) d \tau = \frac {d}{k _ {i}}, \text { and } \lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} e (t) = 0 \tag {77}$$

This concludes the proof. 
