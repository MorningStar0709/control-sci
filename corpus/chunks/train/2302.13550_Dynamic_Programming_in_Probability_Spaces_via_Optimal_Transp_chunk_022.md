Consider the setting of Corollary 4.2 in finite spaces: Let |X| be the number of states in the ground space, N the horizon length, and |U| the number of available actions at each state. We compare the DPA in probability and the recipe in Corollary 4.2 in terms of offline and online computational effort.

Offline computation. The DPA in the ground space has a time and memory complexity of $\mathcal { O } \left( \vert X \vert N \vert U \vert \right)$ , since the computation of the feedback law requires testing all feasible inputs $u \in U$ (and choosing the optimal one) for each state $x \in X$ and at every of the N time-steps. If we normalize by the constants in the O-notation and N|U|, the number of operations is proportional to the number of states. At the fleet level, this number amounts to the number of configurations of the fleet.
