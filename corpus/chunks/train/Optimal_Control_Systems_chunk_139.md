Note from (2.8.20) that the Lagrangian L is independent of $\dot{\lambda}^{*}(t)$ and $\dot{\mathbf{u}}^{*}(t)$ and that the Euler-Lagrange equation (2.8.22) is the same as the constraint relation (2.8.18). The general boundary condition (2.8.14) to be satisfied at the free-final point becomes

$$\boxed {\left[ \mathcal {L} - \dot {\mathbf {x}} ^ {\prime} (t) \left(\frac {\partial \mathcal {L}}{\partial \dot {\mathbf {x}}}\right) \right] _ {* t _ {f}} \delta t _ {f} + \left(\frac {\partial \mathcal {L}}{\partial \dot {\mathbf {x}}}\right) _ {* t _ {f}} ^ {\prime} \delta \mathbf {x} _ {f} = 0.} \tag {2.8.24}$$

This boundary condition is to be modified depending on the nature of the given $t_{f}$ and $\mathbf{x}(t_{f})$ .
