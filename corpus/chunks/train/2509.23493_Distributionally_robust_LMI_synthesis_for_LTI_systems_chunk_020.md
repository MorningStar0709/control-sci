# VI. ROBUST SYNTHESIS

In this section, we build on the results of the previous sections to also optimize over the controller parameters $A _ { c } , B _ { c } ,$ $C _ { c } ,$ , and $D _ { c }$ . Unfortunately, the matrix inequality constraints in Theorem 5.5 and Theorem 5.6 are not simultaneously linear in the controller parameters and the performance certificates $\mathcal { P } , Q$ , and λ.

A first step to convexify (12) and (13) in the controller parameters is the application of the Schur complement.

Proposition 6.1 (Preliminary synthesis problem 1): Let A be stable. Then, the optimization problem (12) in Theorem 5.5 is equivalent to

$$\underset {\lambda \geq 0, Q, \mathcal {P} > 0} {\text { minimize }} \quad \operatorname{tr} \left(Q \Sigma_ {\text { nom }}\right) + \lambda \gamma^ {2}
\text {s.t.} \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c} - \mathcal {P} & 0 & 0 & \mathcal {A} ^ {\mathsf {T}} \mathcal {P} & \mathcal {C} ^ {\mathsf {T}} \\ 0 & - \lambda I & \lambda I & \mathcal {B} ^ {\mathsf {T}} \mathcal {P} & \mathcal {D} ^ {\mathsf {T}} \\ 0 & \lambda I & - \lambda I - Q & 0 & 0 \\ \mathcal {P A} & \mathcal {P B} & 0 & - \mathcal {P} & 0 \\ \mathcal {C} & \mathcal {D} & 0 & 0 & - I \end{array} \right) <   0.
$$

Proof: First, we notice that the left upper 1 × 1 block of (12b) corresponds to the Lyapunov inequality

$$\mathcal {A} ^ {\top} \mathcal {P} \mathcal {A} - \mathcal {P} + \mathcal {C} ^ {\top} \mathcal {C} < 0.$$

Consequently, since A is stable, it poses no restriction to require $\mathcal { P } \succ 0 .$

Next, denote the matrix inequality constraint from Theorem 5.5 as

$$
\left( \begin{array}{c c c} - \mathcal {P} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & - \lambda I & \lambda I \\ 0 & \lambda I & - Q - \lambda I \end{array} \right) + \left( \begin{array}{c c} \mathcal {A} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {C} ^ {\top} \\ \mathcal {B} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {D} ^ {\top} \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{c c} \mathcal {P} & 0 \\ 0 & I \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{c c} \mathcal {A} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {C} ^ {\top} \\ \mathcal {B} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {D} ^ {\top} \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right) ^ {\top} <   0.
$$
