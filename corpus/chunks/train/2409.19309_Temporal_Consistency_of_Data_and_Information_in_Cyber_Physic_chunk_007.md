# C. Data and Information in PS vs. CS

Another fundamental difference between the PS and the CS concerns the denotation of the terms data and information. In the real world (the PS), a data item is a symbol that refers to the meaning of the named concept in the mind of an involved human. An information item is the sense (the idea or intention) communicated by a natural language proposition to a human receiver at the given instant of utterance. Such an information item (which we call ITOM) is formed by an atomic complex that relates multiple data items and is valid at a specified instant or during a specified interval. In the CS, a data item is a timeless value (a bit pattern) that denotes a universal — e.g., a bare number — in the domain of mathematics. Multiple values can be connected by timeless mathematical operators to express an algorithm that can be executed on a computer. If a variable of the CS has a name that has significance in the PS — by relating the value of the variable to the named concept that has meaning in the real world of the PS — we say that the data item (the value) is grounded [8]. A grounded data item, i.e., a computer variable with a name that explains the meaning of the variable in the PS, connects the value stored in a memory location of the CS to a concept in the mind of a human in the PS. It is thus an information item (see below in Section III and IV).
