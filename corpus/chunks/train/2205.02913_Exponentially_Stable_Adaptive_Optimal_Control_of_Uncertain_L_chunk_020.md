According to the proved Proposition 2, to obtain the regression (16) with respect to the unknown parameters of the optimal control law (7), it is necessary and sufficient to obtain the equations (17) and (18) using a measurable vector $\Psi \left( t \right)$ . This challenge can be solved on the basis of the well-known dynamic regressor extension and mixing procedure $\mathrm { ( D R E M ) ^ { 4 } }$ .

The subsections 3.1 and 3.2 of this section are to present the procedure to obtain regressions (17) and (18) respectively. In the subsection 3.3 the correctness of the implication $\Psi \left( t \right) \in { \mathrm { F E } } \Rightarrow \Delta \left( t \right) \in { \mathrm { F E } }$ is proved, the properties of $\Delta \left( t \right)$ and $\varepsilon _ { \theta } \left( t \right)$ are studied, and the adaptive law is introduced, which ensures that the objective (13) is met when $\Psi ( t ) \in \mathrm { F E }$ .
