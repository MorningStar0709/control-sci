$$g _ {D 2 x} = (D _ {1} - D _ {0}) / (x _ {1} - x _ {0}), \tag {6}g _ {D 2 y} = (D _ {2} - D _ {1}) / (y _ {2} - y _ {1}), \tag {7}g _ {I 2 x} = (I _ {1} - I _ {0}) / (x _ {1} - x _ {0}), \tag {8}g _ {I 2 y} = (I _ {2} - I _ {1}) / (y _ {2} - y _ {1}). \tag {9}$$

Obtaining the gradient at each sampling location, along with the additional distance from each step to the previous one, will reduce the navigation real-time calculation performance. Therefore, this paper only calculates the gradient at the starting point. At each sampling position, the gradient is updated using $\Delta D _ { ( k + 1 ) }$ and $\Delta I _ { ( k + 1 ) }$ (the changes in D and I ), the heading angle $\theta _ { k }$ , the velocities $v _ { x k }$ and $v _ { y k }$ (Wang and Zhou, 2018; Zhou et al., 2022). The gradient at the sampling position at time (k+1) can be approximately updated as shown in Eq. (10) to Eq. (12) (Guo et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2021b):

$$
G (k + 1) = G (k) + \binom{\Delta D _ {k + 1}}{\Delta I _ {k + 1}} \left( \begin{array}{c c} \frac {\cos \theta_ {k}}{v _ {x k} T} & \frac {\sin \theta_ {k}}{v _ {y k} T} \end{array} \right), \tag {10}
\Delta D _ {k + 1} = D _ {k + 1} - D _ {k}, \tag {11}\Delta I _ {k + 1} = I _ {k + 1} - I _ {k}. \tag {12}$$
