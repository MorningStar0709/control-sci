# 5.2.3 High-Frequency Disturbance Condition

High-frequency disturbances simulate rapidly changing external forces, such as wind waves or mechanical vibrations. The mathematical expression is:

$$F _ {\text { high - frequency }} = 5 0 \sin (2 \pi \cdot 8 \cdot t) \tag {25}$$

where 8 Hz represents the frequency of the high-frequency disturbance. As shown in Fig. 5, high-frequency disturbances cause slightly larger oscillations in the steady-state phase compared to low-frequency disturbances, but the overall trajectory still maintains a high degree of smoothness and accuracy. The controller dynamically adjusts the control torque to respond quickly to disturbance variations, ensuring system stability and precision. Particularly under highfrequency interference, it effectively suppresses trajectory fluctuations while maintaining target tracking.
