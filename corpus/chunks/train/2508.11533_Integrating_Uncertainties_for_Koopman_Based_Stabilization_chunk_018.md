$$\mathcal {D} := \left\{D \in \mathbb {R} ^ {n \times T}: D D ^ {\top} \preceq \Delta \Delta^ {\top} \right\}. \tag {21}$$

Then, we introduce an important concept for the set C of matrices, called consistency with data, i.e.,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {C} := \left\{\left[ A B _ {0} B _ {1} \dots B _ {m} \right]: \right. \\ Z _ {1} = A Z _ {0} + B _ {0} U _ {0} + \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {m} B _ {i} V _ {0} ^ {i} + D _ {0}, D _ {0} \in \mathcal {D} \}. \tag {22} \\ \end{array}
$$

To address the robust stabilization problem, it is necessary to reformulate the set C. The fundamental ideas draw on [3], with adaptations tailored to bilinear systems. The set C is equivalently reformulated as

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {C} = \left\{\mathbf {Z} ^ {\top} := \left[ A B _ {0} B _ {1} \dots B _ {m} \right]: \right. \tag {23} \\ \left. \mathbf {Z} ^ {\top} \mathbf {A} \mathbf {Z} + \mathbf {Z} ^ {\top} \mathbf {B} + \mathbf {B} ^ {\top} \mathbf {Z} + \mathbf {C} \preceq 0 \right\}, \\ \end{array}
$$

where

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} \mathbf {C} & \mathbf {B} ^ {\top} \\ \hline \mathbf {B} & \mathbf {A} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Z _ {1} Z _ {1} ^ {\top} - \Delta \Delta^ {\top} & - Z _ {1} W _ {0} ^ {\top} \\ \hline - W _ {0} Z _ {1} ^ {\top} & W _ {0} W _ {0} ^ {\top} \end{array} \right], W _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} Z _ {0} \\ U _ {0} \\ V _ {0} ^ {1} \\ \vdots \\ V _ {0} ^ {m} \end{array} \right]. \tag {24}
$$
