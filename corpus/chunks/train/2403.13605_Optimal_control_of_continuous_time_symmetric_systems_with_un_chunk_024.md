$$u ^ {\star} = (1 - \alpha) u ^ {\star} + \alpha \mathcal {T} (u ^ {\star}).$$

Hence, subtracting $u ^ { \star }$ from the both sides of the update step in Algortihm 1 yields

$$
\begin{array}{l} u _ {k + 1} - u ^ {\star} \\ = (1 - \alpha) \left(u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}\right) + \alpha \left(\mathcal {T} \left(u _ {k}\right) - \mathcal {T} \left(u ^ {\star}\right)\right) \\ = (1 - \alpha) \left(u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}\right) + \alpha \left(\mathcal {S} \left(u _ {k}\right) + r _ {x _ {0}} - \mathcal {S} \left(u ^ {\star}\right) - r _ {x _ {0}}\right) \\ = (1 - \alpha) \left(u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}\right) + \alpha \mathcal {S} \left(u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}\right) \\ = \big ((1 - \alpha) \mathcal {I} + \alpha \mathcal {S} \big) (u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}) \tag {30} \\ = R ^ {- 1 / 2} \left((1 - \alpha) \mathcal {I} + \alpha R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2}\right) R ^ {1 / 2} (u _ {k} - u ^ {\star}), \\ \end{array}
$$

which implies that for all $k \in \mathbb N$ one has

$$u _ {k} - u ^ {\star} = R ^ {- 1 / 2} \mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} ^ {k} R ^ {1 / 2} (u _ {0} - u ^ {\star}), \tag {31}$$

where

$$\mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} := (1 - \alpha) \mathcal {I} + \alpha R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2}. \tag {32}$$

From (31), it is deduced that

$$\left\| u _ {k} - u ^ {\star} \right\| _ {2, t _ {f}} \leq \left\| \mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} \right\| _ {2, t _ {f}} ^ {k} \| R ^ {- 1 / 2} \| _ {2} \| R ^ {1 / 2} \| _ {2} \| u _ {0} - u ^ {\star} \| _ {2, t _ {f}}. \tag {33}$$

To estimate the norm of $\mathcal { M } _ { \alpha } ,$ , we note that for any $u \in$ $\mathcal { L } _ { 2 } ^ { m } ( 0 , t _ { f } )$ , one has

$$
\begin{array}{l} \| \mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} u \| _ {2, t _ {f}} ^ {2} = (1 - \alpha) ^ {2} \langle u, u \rangle_ {t _ {f}} \\ + 2 \alpha (1 - \alpha) \left\langle u, R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2} u \right\rangle_ {t _ {f}} \\ + \alpha^ {2} \left\langle R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2} u, R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2} u \right\rangle_ {t _ {f}}. \tag {34} \\ \end{array}
$$

Since $\alpha ( 1 - \alpha ) > 0$ holds for $\alpha \in ( 0 , 1 )$ , one has $\alpha ( 1 -$ $\alpha ) \left. u , R ^ { 1 / 2 } S R ^ { - 1 / 2 } u \right. _ { t , \ t } \ \leq \ 0$ from Lemma 2. Therefore, it follows from (34) that

$$
\begin{array}{l} \| \mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} u \| _ {2, t _ {f}} \leq \\ \left((1 - \alpha) ^ {2} + \alpha^ {2} \| R ^ {1 / 2} \mathcal {S} R ^ {- 1 / 2} \| _ {2, t _ {f}} ^ {2}\right) ^ {1 / 2} \| u \| _ {2, t _ {f}}. \tag {35} \\ \end{array}
$$

Choosing the step size α in (35) as $\alpha < \bar { \alpha }$ results in

$$\left\| \mathcal {M} _ {\alpha} \right\| _ {2, t _ {f}} < 1 \tag {36}$$
