# A. Tracking Error Dynamics

The dynamics equations of the tracking error, e, are required to be able to design the controller. Therefore, the tracking errors are defined based on the difference between the desired trajectory and the system states in the global coordinate system as follows:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} e _ {x} \\ e _ {y} \\ e _ {\theta} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{l} x ^ {d} - x \\ y ^ {d} - y \\ \theta^ {d} - \theta \end{array} \right] \tag {23}
$$

In Eq. (23), ? denotes the desired trajectory. To be able to represent the tracking errors in the robot’s local frame, the following mapping is applied.

$$
\varepsilon = \left[ \begin{array}{l} \varepsilon_ {1} \\ \varepsilon_ {2} \\ \varepsilon_ {3} \end{array} \right] = [ \cos \theta - \sin \theta - 0 ] [ e _ {x} ] \tag {24}

\left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 \\ - \sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} e _ {x} \\ e _ {y} \\ e _ {\theta} \end{array} \right]
$$

Taking the time derivative from Eq. (24) gives us the tracking error dynamics.

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {\varepsilon} _ {1} = \frac {d \varepsilon_ {1}}{d t} = - \sin \theta \omega e _ {x} + \cos \theta \dot {e} _ {x} + \cos \theta \omega e _ {y} \\ \quad + \sin \theta \dot {e} _ {y} \\ \dot {\varepsilon} _ {2} = \frac {d \varepsilon_ {2}}{d t} = - \cos \theta \omega e _ {x} - \sin \theta \dot {e} _ {x} - \sin \theta \omega e _ {y} \\ \quad + \cos \theta \dot {e} _ {y} \\ \dot {\varepsilon} _ {3} = \frac {d \varepsilon_ {3}}{d t} = \dot {\theta} ^ {d} - \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right. \tag {25}
$$

Substituting Eq. (24) in Eq. (25) leads to the tracking error dynamics as follows:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {\varepsilon} _ {1} = \omega \varepsilon_ {2} + v _ {x} ^ {d} \cos \varepsilon_ {3} + \omega^ {d} x _ {0} \sin \varepsilon_ {3} - v _ {x} \\ \dot {\varepsilon} _ {2} = (x _ {0} - \varepsilon_ {1}) \omega + v _ {x} ^ {d} \sin \varepsilon_ {3} - \omega^ {d} x _ {0} \cos \varepsilon_ {3}. \\ \dot {\varepsilon} _ {3} = \omega^ {d} - \omega \end{array} \right. \tag {26}
$$
