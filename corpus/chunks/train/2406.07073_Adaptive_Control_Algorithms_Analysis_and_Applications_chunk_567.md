The objective of the analysis is to guarantee that a stabilizing controller is obtained without any assumption about the presence or the richness of an external excitation. However, the use of an external or internal excitation signal, even for short periods of time, is useful for speeding up the convergence of the adaptive control scheme since it can be shown that exponential stability is achieved under richness conditions (Anderson and Johnstone 1985).

To implement an indirect adaptive control strategy effectively, we have two major options. The choice is related to a certain extent to the ratio between the computation time and the sampling period.
