\begin{array}{l} W _ {1} (H _ {2} ^ {1} (\frac {1}{\gamma} Q, \mu), H _ {2} ^ {1} (\frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu})) = \sup _ {\| g \| _ {\mathrm{Lip}} \leq 1} \left| \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} Q, \mu), \mu) \mu (d x) \right. \\ - \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu}) \tilde {\mu} (d x) \Big | \\ \leq \sup _ {\| g \| _ {\mathrm{Lip}} \leq 1} \left| \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} Q, \mu), \mu) \mu (d x) \right. \\ - \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu}) \mu (d x) \Big | \\ + \sup _ {\| g \| _ {\mathrm{Lip}} \leq 1} \left| \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu}) \mu (d x) \right. \\ - \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu}) \tilde {\mu} (d x) \Big | \\ \leq \int_ {X} \sup _ {\| g \| _ {\mathrm{Lip}} \leq 1} \left| \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} Q, \mu), \mu) \right. \\ - \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu}) \Bigg | \mu (d x) \\ + K _ {2} \left(1 + \frac {K _ {F}}{\rho}\right) W _ {1} (\mu , \tilde {\mu}) \tag {7} \\ \leq \int_ {X} W _ {1} (p (\cdot | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} Q, \mu), \mu), p (\cdot | x, f _ {1} (x, \frac {1}{\gamma} \tilde {Q}, \tilde {\mu}), \tilde {\mu})) \mu (d x) \\ + K _ {2} \left(1 + \frac {K _ {F}}{\rho}\right) W _ {1} (\mu , \tilde {\mu}) \\ \leq \frac {K _ {F}}{\rho} K _ {1} \bigg (\frac {1}{\gamma} \| Q - \tilde {Q} \| _ {w} + W _ {1} (\mu , \tilde {\mu}) \bigg) + K _ {1} W _ {1} (\mu , \tilde {\mu}) + K _ {2} \bigg (1 + \frac {K _ {F}}{\rho} \bigg) W _ {1} (\mu , \tilde {\mu}). \\ \end{array}
$$

The only difference between the inequalities above and those in [5, Theorem 1] is the term $K _ { 2 } ( K _ { F } / \rho + 1 )$ on (7). In [5], one can easily see that the factor $K _ { 2 } ( K _ { F } / \rho + 1 )$ can be substituted in place of $K _ { 2 } + K _ { F } / \rho$ by modifying the proof of [5, Theorem 1]. The only difference comes from [5, Equation 10] where $K _ { 2 } ( K _ { F } / \rho + 1 )$ can be obtained instead of $K _ { 2 } + K _ { F } / \rho$ which is an improvement since we will be implicitly assuming that $K _ { 2 } < 1$ . This justifies the appearence of the term $K _ { 2 } ( K _ { F } / \rho + 1 )$ ). This completes the proof. □
