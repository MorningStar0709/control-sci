# Characteristic Roots and the Transfer Function

The previous subsections have discussed the solution to LTI differential equations, and we have seen that the homogeneous or free response $y _ { H } ( t )$ depends on the roots of the characteristic equation (7.6). It is important for the reader to understand that the roots of the characteristic equation can also be determined from the corresponding system transfer function. Recall that the transfer function G(s) is the ratio of two polynomials in the complex variable s, that is, $G ( s ) = b ( s ) / a ( s )$ . The values of s that make the denominator polynomial a(s) equal zero are called the poles of the transfer function, and the poles are identical to the roots of the characteristic equation.
