$$
\begin{array}{l} \varepsilon (t + 1) = y (t + 1) - \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t + 1) \phi (t) \\ = y (t + 1) - \hat {\theta} (t) \phi (t) - [ \hat {\theta} (t + 1) - \hat {\theta} (t) ] ^ {T} \phi (t) \\ = \varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) - \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t) \frac {\varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)} \\ = \frac {\varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)} \tag {3.50} \\ \end{array}
$$

which expresses the relation between the a posteriori prediction error and the a priori prediction error. Using this relation in (3.49), an equivalent form of the parameter adaptation algorithm for the recursive least squares is obtained:

$$\boxed {\hat {\theta} (t + 1) = \hat {\theta} (t) + F (t) \phi (t) \varepsilon (t + 1)} \tag {3.51}\boxed {F (t + 1) ^ {- 1} = F (t) ^ {- 1} + \phi (t) \phi^ {T} (t)} \tag {3.52}\boxed {F (t + 1) = F (t) - \frac {F (t) \phi (t) \phi^ {T} (t) F (t)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)}} \tag {3.53}\boxed {\varepsilon (t + 1) = \frac {y (t + 1) - \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) \phi (t)}{1 + \phi^ {T} (t) F (t) \phi (t)}} \tag {3.54}$$

$$F (0) = \frac {1}{\delta} I = (G I) I; \quad 0 < \delta \ll 1 \tag {3.55}$$
