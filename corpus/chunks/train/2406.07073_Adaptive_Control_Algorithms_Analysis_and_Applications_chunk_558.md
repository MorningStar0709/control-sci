Figure 11.6 shows the desired trajectory and the achieved trajectory during a sequence of step changes on the reference and in the presence of the parameter variations occurring at $t = 2 5$ . One can observe that the adaptation transient is much smoother using the $P _ { 2 } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ regulation polynomial than in the case $P _ { 1 } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ ).

Figure 11.7 shows the behavior in regulation, i.e., the evolution of the output from an initial condition at $t = 0$ in the presence of a change in the parameters at $t = 0$ . Same conclusion can be drawn: the poles defined by the regulation polynomial strongly influence the adaptation transient. Two fast dynamics in regulation with respect to the natural response of the system will induce undesirable adaptation transients.
