where:

$$\bar {w} (t) = \frac {w (t + 1)}{m (t)} \tag {10.96}$$

The problem is to find a normalizing signal m(t) such that $\bar { w } ( t + 1 )$ be bounded for all t. The choice of this signal will depend upon the hypotheses made upon the representation of the unmodeled part of the system output y(t).

Let us return to the representation of $w ( t + 1 )$ given in (10.92). In order to define an appropriate normalizing signal m(t), one should evaluate $| w ( t + 1 ) |$ . In addition to the transfer operator $\bar { H } ^ { T } ( \bar { q } ^ { - 1 } )$ , one will consider the impulse response of the unmodeled dynamics which allows to write:

$$H ^ {T} (z ^ {- 1}) = \sum_ {0} ^ {\infty} h ^ {T} (i) z ^ {- i} \tag {10.97}$$

where $h ( i )$ are the terms of the impulse response. Therefore, one can express $w ( t + 1 )$ as follows:

$$w (t + 1) = \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t + 1} h ^ {\prime^ {T}} (t + 1 - \tau) \phi (\tau) = \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} h ^ {T} (t - \tau) \phi (\tau)h ^ {\prime} (0) = 0; h ^ {\prime} (i) = h (i - 1) \tag {10.98}$$

From (10.98), one gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} | w (t + 1) | = \left| \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} h ^ {T} (t - \tau) \phi (\tau) \right| \leq \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} | h ^ {T} (t - \tau) \phi (\tau) | \\ = \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} | \mu^ {- (t - \tau)} h ^ {T} (t - \tau) \mu^ {(t - \tau)} \phi (\tau) | \\ \end{array}
$$
