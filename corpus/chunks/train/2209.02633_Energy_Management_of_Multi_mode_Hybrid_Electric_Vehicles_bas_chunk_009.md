# 3) Battery model

The battery model is established by an equivalent circuit model as follows

$$
\left. \begin{array}{c} P _ {b a t t} (t) = P _ {m o t 2} (t) - P _ {m o t 1} (t) \\ P _ {b a t t} (t) = U \cdot I _ {b a t t} (t) - R \cdot I _ {b a t t} ^ {2} (t) \\ I _ {b a t t} (t) = \frac {U - \sqrt {U ^ {2} - 4 \cdot R \cdot P _ {b a t t} (t)}}{2 \cdot R} \\ S o C (t) = S o C (0) - \frac {\int_ {0} ^ {t} I _ {b a t t} (t) d t}{Q _ {b a t t}} \end{array} \right\} \tag {10}
$$

where, $P _ { b a t t } ( t )$ is the output power in the chargedischarge process, ??????(0) is the initial SoC value, $I _ { b a t t } ( t )$ is the current of the battery, and $Q _ { b a t t }$ is the nominal battery capacity $( Q _ { b a t t } = 5 4 . 3 \mathrm { \ A h } )$ . Since this research mainly focuses on charge-sustaining control, the battery temperature and aging dynamics are ignored. The open-circuit voltage (OCV) $U \left( U = 3 5 0 \mathsf { V } \right)$ and the battery internal resistance R $\left( R = 0 . 1 5 \Omega \right)$ are both constant.
