# Thermal Capacitance

Thermal capacitance is a measure of a body’s ability to store heat energy due to its mass and thermal properties. Thermal capacitance C is the product of the body’s mass m and its specific heat capacity at constant pressure $c _ { p }$ (with units J/kg-K)

$$C = m c _ {p} \tag {4.84}$$

Therefore, thermal capacitance has units of stored energy per unit temperature, or J/K. For example, 1 kg of water has more than four times the thermal capacitance of 1 kg of air.
