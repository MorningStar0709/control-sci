The primary contributions of this paper are fourfold: (i) our framework significantly improves the flight stability and robustness of the system by decoupling yaw control from position control, particularly beneficial for large yaw angle flights, (ii) the proposed frameworks reduce the overall training time by specializing each agent in its designated task, converging faster with less training data compared to a complex single agent, (iii) two regularization terms are introduced to mitigate steady-state errors in position tracking and prevent excessive control inputs, and (iv) our proposed approaches remain flexible and can be trained with any MARL algorithm. Specifically, in this paper, we implement the proposed MARL frameworks with Multi-Agent TD3 (MATD3) [6] and benchmarked it against the single-agent counterpart, Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3) [7], demonstrating the substantial advantages in the training efficiency.
