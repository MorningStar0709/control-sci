# Partial-Fraction Expansion Method

The critical step in Example 8.6 is computing the inverse Laplace transform of $Y ( s )$ in order to determine the time-response function $y ( t )$ . In Example 8.6, the Laplace transform $Y ( s )$ as expressed in Eq. (8.13) does not appear in Table 8.1; however, the Laplace transforms of the three partial-fraction terms in Eq. (8.14) are easily found in Table 8.1. The partial-fraction expansion method breaks a complicated Laplace transform (i.e., a ratio of two polynomials in s) into a sum of simpler terms where each denominator involves a pole (or poles) associated with $Y ( s )$ . Therefore, the inverse Laplace transforms of the simpler partial-fraction terms are easy to obtain because they appear in Table 8.1.
