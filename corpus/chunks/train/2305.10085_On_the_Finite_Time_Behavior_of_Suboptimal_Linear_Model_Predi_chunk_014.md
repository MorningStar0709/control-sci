$$\| \Delta \mu_ {k} \| \leq b \| x _ {0} \| _ {W} \varepsilon^ {k} + c \| x _ {0} \| _ {W} \beta^ {k},$$

where $h = 1 + \tau \eta ^ { \ell } L \vert \vert W ^ { - \frac { 1 } { 2 } } \vert \vert$ and $b = c \cdot h$ .

Next, we show that the exponential stability of the combined system-optimizer dynamics (5) implies the same rate for the state, $x _ { k }$ , in the ROA estimate $\Gamma _ { N }$ .

Lemma 5: Given the dynamics (5), for all $s _ { 0 } \in \Sigma _ { N } , k \ge 0$ and $\ell _ { k } > \ell ^ { \star }$

$$\left\| x _ {k} \right\| \leq h _ {0} \left\| P ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \right\| \cdot \left\| x _ {0} \right\| _ {W} \prod_ {i = - 1} ^ {k} \varepsilon_ {i},$$

where $h _ { 0 }$ is defined as in Theorem 3.

Proof: From Theorem $2 , \ \Sigma _ { N }$ is a forward invariant ROA estimate for the suboptimal dynamics. Hence from Lemma 2 we have that for all $s _ { 0 } \in \Sigma _ { N }$

$$\left\| x _ {k} \right\| _ {P} \leq \psi (x _ {k}) + \tau \left\| z _ {k} - \mu^ {\star} (x _ {k}) \right\| = \mathcal {L} (x _ {k}, z _ {k}),$$

and

$$\| x _ {k} \| \leq \| P ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \| \mathcal {L} (x _ {0}, z _ {0}) \prod_ {i = - 1} ^ {k} \varepsilon_ {i} \leq h _ {0} \| P ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \| \| x _ {0} \| _ {W} \prod_ {i = - 1} ^ {k} \varepsilon_ {i},$$

where we use the submultiplicative property of norms and Theorem 3.

Corollary 3: Given the dynamics (5), for all $\ell > \ell ^ { \star } , s _ { 0 } \in$ $\Sigma _ { N }$ and $k \geq 0$

$$\left\| x _ {k} \right\| \leq h \left\| P ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \right\| \cdot \left\| x _ {0} \right\| _ {W} \cdot \varepsilon^ {k},$$

where h is defined as in Corollary 2.
