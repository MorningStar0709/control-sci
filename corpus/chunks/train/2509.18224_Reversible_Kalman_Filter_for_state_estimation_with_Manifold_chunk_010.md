# B. Prediction step

The prediction relates to an integration operation on the gyroscopic input. The procedure is well described in [15].

Given gyroscopic data $[ \omega _ { x } , \omega _ { y } , \omega _ { z } ]$ , the integration computes

$$q _ {k - 1} \cdot \exp \bigl (\left[ \omega_ {x}, \omega_ {y}, \omega_ {z} \right] \cdot d t \bigr),$$

which corresponds to the quaternion $q _ { k | k - 1 } .$

The covariance is computed by using a linearization of the exponential map, corresponding to the matrix Φ.

Algorithm 2 EKF Prediction Step for Quaternion Orientation   
Require: Quaternion $q_{k-1}$ , Bias $B_{k-1}$ , Covariance $P_{k-1}$ , Process noise $Q_k$ , Gyroscope $\omega = (p, q, r)$ , Time step dt

Ensure: Updated $q_k$ , $B_k$ , $P_k$ 1: $[\omega_x, \omega_y, \omega_z] \leftarrow [p - B_{k,0}, q - B_{k,1}, r - B_{k,2}]$ 2: $\Delta q \leftarrow \exp([\omega_x, \omega_y, \omega_z] \cdot dt)$ 3: $q_{k|k-1} \leftarrow q_{k-1} \cdot \Delta q \quad \triangleright$ Right multiplication because global frame

4: Initialize $F \leftarrow 0_{6 \times 6}$ , $\Phi \leftarrow I_6$ 5: $F_{1:3,1:3} \leftarrow -\text{skew}([\omega_x, \omega_y, \omega_z])$ 6: $F_{1:3,4:6} \leftarrow -I_3$ 7: $\Phi \leftarrow I_6 + F \cdot dt \triangleright$ If dt is large, higher-order terms may be needed

8: $P_k \leftarrow \Phi \cdot P_{k-1} \cdot \Phi^\top + Q_k$ 9: return $q_{k|k-1}$ , $B_{k-1}$ , $P_k$ , $\Phi$
