Linear-quadratic (LQ) McKean-Vlasov control problems, where linear dynamics and quadratic cost functionals depend on (conditional) expectations of the state, the control, or both, have also been extensively studied in the literature [3, 12, 20, 17, 21, 22]. In this framework, the extended stochastic maximum principle leads to a system of linear McKean-Vlasov FBSDEs that involve the (conditional) expectations of the adjoint process, as well as those of other processes appearing in the model [3, 12, 20, 17, 21, 22]. Moreover, the extended dynamic programming approach imposes similar restrictions, as noted above, on the admissible control inputs in cases where the dynamics are driven by two Wiener processes [7].
