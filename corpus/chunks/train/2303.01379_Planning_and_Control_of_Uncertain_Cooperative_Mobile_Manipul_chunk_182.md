$$
\begin{array}{l} \| R _ {k _ {2}} ^ {\top} R _ {k _ {1}} - R _ {k, \mathrm{des}} \| _ {\mathrm{F}} ^ {2} = \operatorname{tr} \left[ \left(R _ {k _ {2}} ^ {\top} R _ {k _ {1}} - R _ {k, \mathrm{des}}\right) ^ {\top} \left(R _ {k _ {2}} ^ {\top} R _ {k _ {1}} - R _ {k, \mathrm{des}}\right) \right] \\ = \operatorname{tr} \left[ 2 I _ {3} - 2 R _ {k, \text {des}} ^ {\top} R _ {k _ {2}} ^ {\top} R _ {k _ {1}} \right] = 4 \psi_ {k}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Hence, it holds that:

$$\left\| e _ {R _ {k}} \right\| ^ {2} = 2 \psi_ {k} (2 - \psi_ {k}), \tag {3.7}$$

which implies that: $\| e _ { R _ { k } } \| = 0 \Rightarrow \psi _ { k } = 0 \ o r \psi _ { k } = 2 , \forall k \in \mathcal { M }$ . The two configurations $\psi _ { k } = 0$ and $\psi _ { k } = 2$ correspond to the desired and undesired equilibrium, respectively.
