# Course Configurations

The book has been organized so that it can be used in different ways. An introductory course in computer-controlled systems could cover Chapters 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 9. A more advanced course might include all chapters in the book. A course for an industrial audience could contain Chapters 1, parts of Chapters 2, 3, 4, and 5, and Chapters 6, 7, 8, and 9. To get the full benefit of a course, it is important to supplement lectures with problem-solving sessions, simulation exercises, and laboratory experiments.
