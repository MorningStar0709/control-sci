# C. Adaptive Filtering - Foot Model Noise Estimation

We used the velocity-kinematic model to estimate the noise of the contact foot state. The velocity kinematics model is given as follows:

$$\dot {p} _ {F _ {i}} = v _ {B} + R _ {B} \omega^ {\times B} \tilde {p} _ {F _ {i} / B} + R _ {B} ^ {B} \tilde {v} _ {F _ {i} / B} - R _ {B} w _ {v} \tag {24}$$

The $w _ { v }$ is a compound noise from multiple sources, including gyro and encoder noise. Since modeling this noise precisely is difficult, a reasonable constant value is manually chosen by the user to reflect their combined effect.

Reconsidering the foot model (16d), the actual foot model behavior under slip or missed contact includes an unknown foot model noise $w _ { u f _ { i } }$ , which is larger than the static assumed foot noise $w _ { f _ { i } }$ . In such case, the model can be represented as ${ \dot { p } } _ { F _ { i } } = { R } _ { B } ( - w _ { u f _ { i } } )$ . Substituting this equation into (24) yields the following equation:

$$- \omega^ {\times B} \tilde {p} _ {F _ {i} / B} - ^ {B} \tilde {v} _ {F _ {i} / B} = R _ {B} ^ {T} v _ {B} + w _ {u f _ {i}} + w _ {v} \tag {25}$$

This can be expressed as a Right-invariant measurement model:

$$Y _ {v _ {i}} = X ^ {- 1} b _ {v _ {i}} + V _ {v _ {i}},
Y _ {v _ {i}} = \left( \begin{array}{c} - \omega^ {\times B} \tilde {p} _ {F _ {i} / B} - ^ {B} \tilde {v} _ {F _ {i} / B} \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{array} \right), \tag {26}

b _ {v} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c} \mathbf {0} _ {\mathbf {1}. \mathbf {3}} & - 1 & 0 & \dots & 0 \end{array} \right) ^ {T},
V _ {v _ {i}} = \left(w _ {u f _ {i}} ^ {T} + w _ {v} ^ {T} \quad 0 \quad \dots \quad 0\right) ^ {T}$$

To estimate the unknown foot model noise covariance, we use the innovation-based covariance matching technique [23]. Each i-th foot innovation $e _ { i }$ is given by :

$$e _ {i} = s _ {v} (\chi^ {-} Y _ {v _ {i}} - b _ {v}) = s _ {v} (\eta^ {R} b _ {v} - b _ {v} + \chi^ {-} V _ {v _ {i}})= H _ {v} \xi^ {-} + M _ {v} (w _ {u f _ {i}} + w _ {v})$$

$\mathrm { w h e r e , } \quad s _ { v } = ( I _ { 3 , 3 } \quad 0 _ { 3 , 3 } \quad \cdot \cdot \cdot \quad 0 _ { 3 , 3 } )$ (27)

$$
H _ {v} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c} 0 _ {3, 3} & I _ {3, 3} & 0 _ {3, 3} & \dots & 0 _ {3, 3} \end{array} \right)
M _ {v} = R _ {B}$$

The covariance of innovation is derived as follows:

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ e _ {i} e _ {i} ^ {T} \right] = H _ {v} P ^ {-} H _ {v} ^ {T} + R _ {B} \left(Q _ {u f _ {i}} + Q _ {v}\right) R _ {B} ^ {T}, \tag {28}$$
