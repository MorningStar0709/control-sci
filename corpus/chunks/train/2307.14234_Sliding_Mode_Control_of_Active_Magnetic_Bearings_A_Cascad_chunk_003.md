This paper proposes a cascaded nonlinear control strategy for the vertical axial positioning of the rotor shaft in an AMB system. The control scheme comprises nonlinear control laws based on sliding mode principles for the mechanical and current dynamics that are designed separately. An adaptive estimator is designed for inverting the nonlinear input mapping in the AMB system. The inherent modularity of this architecture allows not only for easier analysis but also for integration of different methods in the control cascade. More specifically, the contributions of this study pertain to the following:

Development of a modular cascaded control architecture, into which alternative control and estimation algorithms can be integrated.   
Development of a dynamic estimation strategy for inverting the nonlinear input mapping between desired force and current.   
• Stability analysis of the cascaded closed-loop system.

The remainder of the paper is structured as follows: Section 2 gives a description of the AMB system and its mathematical dynamical model. The proposed control design along with the stability analysis of the closedloop system is detailed in Section 3. Section 4 presents the simulation results and discusses the performance of the proposed method. Finally, concluding remarks and reflections on future work are presented in Section 5.
