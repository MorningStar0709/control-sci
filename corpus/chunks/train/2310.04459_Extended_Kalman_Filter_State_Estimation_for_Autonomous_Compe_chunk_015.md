# 1) State Transition Function

The state transition function f propagates the current state to the next state. To find f, we need to express the next state in terms of the current state. The dynamics model used to calculate the true state for the simulation is simply the prediction model with added noise qt at each time step.

The next position state is predicted by turning body velocity into global velocity:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ y \\ \theta \end{array} \right] _ {p} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} x + \Delta t (\dot {x} \cos \theta - \dot {y} \sin \theta) \\ y + \Delta t (\dot {x} \sin \theta + \dot {y} \cos \theta) \\ \theta + \Delta t \cdot \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right] _ {t - 1} \tag {22}
$$

The next velocity state depends solely on the ut vector. Using mecanum wheel mechanics [5], we can calculate the robot's body velocity based on the velocity of each of the four mecanum wheels. L is the distance from the center horizontal axis to the center of a mecanum wheel and l is the distance from the center vertical axis to the center of a mecanum wheel. r is the radius of a mecanum wheel. Refer to Figure 5.

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} \\ \dot {y} \\ \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right] _ {p} = \left(\frac {r}{4} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & - 1 & - 1 & 1 \\ - \frac {1}{L + l} & \frac {1}{L + l} & - \frac {1}{L + l} & \frac {1}{L + l} \end{array} \right]\right) \left[ \begin{array}{c} m _ {1} \\ m _ {2} \\ m _ {3} \\ m _ {4} \end{array} \right] _ {t} \tag {23}
$$

Multiplying the right side, we get:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} \\ \dot {y} \\ \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right] _ {p} = \frac {r}{4} \left[ \begin{array}{c} m _ {1} + m _ {2} + m _ {3} + m _ {4} \\ m _ {1} - m _ {2} - m _ {3} + m _ {4} \\ \frac {1}{L + l} (- m _ {1} + m _ {2} - m _ {3} + m _ {4}) \end{array} \right] _ {t} \tag {24}
$$

Combining (22) and (24), we have:

$$
f \left(\left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ y \\ \theta \\ \dot {x} \\ \dot {y} \\ \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right] _ {t - 1}, \left[ \begin{array}{c} m _ {1} \\ m _ {2} \\ m _ {3} \\ m _ {4} \end{array} \right] _ {t}\right) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {t - 1} + \Delta t (\dot {x} \cos \theta - \dot {y} \sin \theta) _ {t - 1} \\ y _ {t - 1} + \Delta t (\dot {x} \sin \theta + \dot {y} \cos \theta) _ {t - 1} \\ \theta_ {t - 1} + \Delta t \cdot \dot {\theta} _ {t - 1} \\ \frac {r}{4} (m _ {1} + m _ {2} + m _ {3} + m _ {4}) _ {t} \\ \frac {r}{4} (m _ {1} - m _ {2} - m _ {3} + m _ {4}) _ {t} \\ \frac {r}{4 (L + l)} (- m _ {1} + m _ {2} - m _ {3} + m _ {4}) _ {t} \end{array} \right] \tag {25}
$$
