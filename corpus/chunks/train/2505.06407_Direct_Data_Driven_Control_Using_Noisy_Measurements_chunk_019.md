# IV. SIMULATION RESULTS

This section compares the presented direct data-driven from noisy state measurements LQR (DDNSMLQR) presented in Theorem 3, with model-based LQR using noisy state measurements (NSMLQR), the direct data-driven low complexity LQR (DDLCLQR) approach [5], and the direct datadriven dynamic programming LQR (DDDPLQR) method [6]. Specifically, we compare the number of times a controller is successfully found (i.e., a feasible solution exists to the resulting optimization), the number of times for which the closed-loop eigenvalues were all inside the unit circle, the average controller gains K obtained from each method and their norms with respect to the model-based LQR control gain. We used CVXPY [19], [20] for modeling the convex optimization problems and MOSEK [21] as the solver.

The tuning parameters used in each of the methods are as follows: $\alpha \geq 0$ is the robustness parameter in [5] and $\lambda ~ = ~ 0 . 9 9$ is the discount factor in [6]. Also, we treat W and V as design parameters in case we have no access to the actual covariances. To do a fair comparison, all the methods are assumed to have access to the same data set. Also, we use the noisy measurements $Y _ { 0 }$ and $Y _ { 1 }$ instead of $X _ { 0 }$ and $X _ { 1 }$ respectively to DDLCLQR and DDDPLQR methods. We consider two examples. The first one is a stable active car suspension system, and the second one is the unstable rotary inverted pendulum system.
