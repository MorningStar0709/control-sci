# Effects of Disturbances

Loss of robustness due to disturbances was found for the MRAS in Section 6.6. Similar problems can be encountered for discrete-time systems. In Section 6.5 direct self-tuning regulators were discussed in the ideal case, in which there are no disturbances, but the results can be extended in different directions to cover disturbances. Consider the case in which the process is described by

$$A (q) y (t) = B (q) u (t) + v (t) \tag {6.86}$$

where v is a bounded disturbance. To get some insight into what can happen, first consider an example. (See Egardt (1979).)
