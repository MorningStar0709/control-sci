In summary, finding higherorder metrics comes at the cost of combinatorial complexity in the size of the positive semidefinite matrix constraints. However, exploiting duality mitigates the growth of the largest Gram matrix, as compared to straight application of Scherer’s Positivstellensatz, when $l > 1$ , even when choosing $n _ { F } = 1$ .
