with the observation that the term $G _ { j - d } \bar { e } _ { u } ( t + j - d - 1 )$ will contain $\bar { e } _ { u } ( t )$ , $\bar { e } _ { u } ( t + 1 ) , \dots , \bar { e } ( t + j - d - 1 )$ , i.e., only future values of $\bar { e } _ { u }$ .

Step 2. Minimization of the criterion

Introducing the notations:

$$E _ {y} ^ {T} = [ e _ {y} (t + h _ {i}), \dots , e _ {y} (t + h _ {p}) ] \tag {7.210}\bar {E} _ {u} ^ {T} = [ \bar {e} _ {u} (t), \dots , \bar {e} _ {u} (t + h _ {c} - 1) ] \tag {7.211}$$

The criterion (7.193) takes the form:

$$J = E _ {y} ^ {T} E _ {y} + \lambda \bar {E} _ {u} ^ {T} \bar {E} _ {u} \tag {7.212}$$

Now taking into account the expression of $e _ { y } ( t + j )$ given by (7.209) and introducing the notations:

$$E _ {y} ^ {0} = \left[ \hat {e} _ {y} ^ {0} \left(t + h _ {i} / t\right), \dots , \hat {e} _ {y} ^ {0} \left(t + h _ {p} / t\right) \right] \tag {7.213}$$

one has from (7.207) and (7.208):

$$E _ {y} = G \bar {E} _ {u} + E _ {y} ^ {0} \tag {7.214}$$

where:

$$
G = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} g _ {h i - d - 1} & \dots & g _ {o} & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ g _ {h i - d} & \dots & g _ {1} & g _ {o} & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ g _ {h c - 1} & \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots & g _ {o} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ g _ {h p - d - 1} & \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots & g _ {h p - d - h c} \end{array} \right] \tag {7.215}
$$

for $h _ { c } > h _ { i } - d$ or

$$
G = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} g _ {h i - d - 1} & \dots & g _ {o} & \dots & \dots & g _ {h i - d - h c} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots \\ g _ {h p - d - 1} & \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots & g _ {h p - d - h c} \end{array} \right] \tag {7.216}
$$

for $h _ { c } \leq h _ { i } - d$ and the criterion (7.193) takes the form:

$$J (t, h _ {p}, h _ {c}, h _ {i}) = [ G \bar {E} _ {u} + E _ {y} ^ {0} ] ^ {T} [ G \bar {E} _ {u} + E _ {y} ^ {0} ] + \lambda \bar {E} _ {u} ^ {T} \bar {E} _ {u} \tag {7.217}$$

Minimization of this criterion is obtained by searching for $\bar { E } _ { u }$ assuring $\begin{array} { r } { \frac { \varDelta J } { \varDelta \bar { E } _ { u } } = 0 } \end{array}$ ΔJ = 0 ΔE¯ which yields:

$$\bar {E} _ {u} | _ {o p t} = - [ G ^ {T} G + \lambda I _ {h _ {c}} ] ^ {- 1} G ^ {T} E _ {y} ^ {0} \tag {7.218}$$

This formula indicates that the computation of $\bar { E } _ { u }$ is always possible provided that $\lambda > 0$ .

Since for the implementation of the control law we need only the first component of $\bar { E } _ { u }$ , namely $\bar { e } _ { u } ( t )$ , only the first row of the matrix $[ G ^ { T } \dot { G } + \lambda I _ { h _ { c } } ] ^ { - 1 } G ^ { \dot { T } }$ is of interest, i.e.,

$$\bar {e} _ {u} (t) = - \left[ \gamma_ {h _ {i}}, \dots , \gamma_ {h _ {p}} \right] E _ {\mathrm{y}} ^ {0} \tag {7.219}$$

where $\gamma _ { i }$ are the coefficients of the first row of $[ G ^ { T } G + \lambda I _ { h _ { c } } ] ^ { - 1 } G ^ { T }$ .
