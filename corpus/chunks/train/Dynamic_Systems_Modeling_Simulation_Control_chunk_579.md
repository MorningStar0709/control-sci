Before we describe the theoretical basis of the root-locus method, it is useful to demonstrate how the closed-loop roots (poles) change as a single parameter is varied (the reader may want to review Example 10.9, which also illustrates how the closed-loop poles change with gain $K _ { P } )$ . Figure 10.31 shows a very simple closed-loop system with a type-1 plant $G _ { P } ( s )$ ) and proportional controller (the plant has the same basic structure as the mechanical system shown in Examples 10.6 and 10.8). The closed-loop transfer function is

$$T (s) = \frac {K _ {P} G _ {P} (s)}{1 + K _ {P} G _ {P} (s)} = \frac {K _ {P}}{s ^ {2} + 3 s + K _ {P}}$$

The two closed-loop poles are determined by finding the roots of the characteristic equation

Characteristic equation: $s ^ { 2 } + 3 s + K _ { P } = 0$ (10.37)
