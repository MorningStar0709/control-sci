Inequality (40) shows that the Lyapunov candidate $V ^ { ( k ) }$ can only increase through the terms that depend on the residuals, while the changes in $( z , f )$ and in s always decrease the augmented Lagrangian by at least a quadratic amount. Combined with the lower boundedness and regularity of $\mathcal { L }$ in Assumption 7, this type of inequality is standard in the convergence analysis of nonconvex ADMM schemes, see for example [9, 12, 29].

Theorem 2 Suppose that Assumptions $_ { 4 } - 7$ hold and that the penalty parameter ρ is chosen sufficiently large. Then the sequence $\{ \Xi ^ { ( k ) } \}$ generated by Algorithm 1 has the following properties:

(1) The consensus residual converges to zero,

$$d ^ {(k)} \rightarrow 0 \quad a s k \rightarrow \infty ,$$

so that the coupling constraint $z - r + s = 0$ is satisfied at any limit point.

(2) The sequence has square-summable increments, in the sense that

$$\sum_ {k = 0} ^ {\infty} \Big (\big \| (z ^ {(k + 1)}, f ^ {(k + 1)}) - (z ^ {(k)}, f ^ {(k)}) \big \| _ {2} ^ {2}\left. + \left\| s ^ {(k + 1)} - s ^ {(k)} \right\| _ {2} ^ {2}\right) < + \infty . \tag {41}$$

(3) Every limit point $o f \left\{ \Xi ^ { ( k ) } \right\}$ is a stationary point of the augmented Lagrangian L under the convex constraints on $( z , f )$ and the binary constraints on r.

In particular, if the sequence $\{ \Xi ^ { ( k ) } \}$ has a unique limit point, then the entire sequence converges to this stationary point.
