# 2.3 Problem statement

In this paper, we consider the output-regulation optimal control problem

$$\underset {u \in \mathcal {L} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {f})} {\text { minimize }} J (x _ {0}, u) = \frac {1}{2} \int_ {0} ^ {t _ {f}} y ^ {\prime} (t) Q y (t) + u ^ {\prime} (t) R u (t) d t$$

subject to x, u, y satisfy (5)

$$x (0) = x _ {0}, \tag {13}$$

where $Q \succeq 0 , R \succ 0$ . We are interested in finding the optimal solution $u ^ { \star }$ to the problem (13) purely based on the input-output data observed from system (5). The system dynamics (5), i.e., the state-space matrices A, B, $\dot { C }$ and D are assumed unkown. However, we make the following assumption throughout the paper.

Assumption 1. System (5) is externally symmetric.
