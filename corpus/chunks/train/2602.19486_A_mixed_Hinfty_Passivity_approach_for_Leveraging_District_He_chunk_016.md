The Bounded Real Lemma (BRL) provides the sufficient condition for $\| \mathcal { G } _ { \omega ^ { H P }  z } \| _ { \infty } < \gamma _ { \infty }$ as the existence of ${ P } _ { \mathrm { l p } } \succ \mathbf { 0 }$ such that

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \boldsymbol {A} _ {\mathrm{lp}} (\boldsymbol {K}) ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} _ {\mathrm{lp}} + \boldsymbol {P} _ {\mathrm{lp}} \boldsymbol {A} _ {\mathrm{lp}} (\boldsymbol {K}) & \boldsymbol {P} _ {\mathrm{lp}} \boldsymbol {B} _ {\mathrm{lp}} & \boldsymbol {C} _ {\mathrm{lp}} (\boldsymbol {K}) ^ {\top} \\ \boldsymbol {B} _ {\mathrm{lp}} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} _ {\mathrm{lp}} & - \gamma_ {\infty} ^ {2} \boldsymbol {I} & \boldsymbol {0} \\ \boldsymbol {C} _ {\mathrm{lp}} (\boldsymbol {K}) & \boldsymbol {0} & - \boldsymbol {I} _ {n H P} \end{array} \right] \prec \boldsymbol {0}. \tag {22}
$$

The BRL-based low-pass performance BMI (22) is equivalent to

$$
\boxed {\left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \boldsymbol {\Psi} _ {1 1} & \boldsymbol {B} _ {\mathrm{lp}} & \boldsymbol {\Psi} _ {1 3} \\ \boldsymbol {B} _ {\mathrm{lp}} ^ {\top} & - \gamma_ {\infty} ^ {2} \boldsymbol {I} & \boldsymbol {0} \\ \boldsymbol {\Psi} _ {3 1} & \boldsymbol {0} & - \boldsymbol {I} _ {n H P} \end{array} \right] \prec 0, \quad \boldsymbol {X} \succ 0, q _ {\omega} > 0,} \tag {23}
$$

where $\Psi _ { 1 1 } , \Psi _ { 1 3 } ,$ and $\Psi _ { 3 1 } : = \Psi _ { 1 3 } ^ { \top }$ are defined in Appendix.

Combining the KYP-based passivity LMI (20) and the BRL-based LMI (23), the controller design can be formulated as:

$$
\boxed { \begin{array}{c} \min _ {\boldsymbol {X}, \boldsymbol {Y}, q _ {\omega}, \rho , \gamma_ {\infty}} \gamma_ {\infty} ^ {2} \\ \text {s.t. (20), (23).} \end{array} } \tag {24}
$$

Upon feasibility the single semidefinite program (SDP), the controller is recovered as $K = \check { Y } X ^ { - 1 }$ .
