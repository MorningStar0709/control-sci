# Indirect Self-tuning Regulator

A straightforward way to make a self-tuning regulator for the process (4.1) is to estimate the parameters in the A, B, and C polynomials by using, for instance, the extended least squares (ELS) algorithm or the recursive maximum-likelihood (RML) algorithm. (See Section 2.2.) The estimated parameters are then used in the design equation (4.9) if minimum-variance control is desired or in Eq. (4.20) if LQG control is desired.
