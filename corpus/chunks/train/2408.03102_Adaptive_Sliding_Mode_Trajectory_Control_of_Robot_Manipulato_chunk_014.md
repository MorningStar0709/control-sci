Each link is modelled as a uniform rectangular bar. We use Eq. (1) to represent the dynamics of the robot as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} M _ {1 1} & M _ {1 2} \\ M _ {2 1} & M _ {2 2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \ddot {q} _ {1} \\ \ddot {q} _ {2} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} V _ {1} \\ V _ {2} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} G _ {1} \\ G _ {2} \end{array} \right] \\ = \left[ \begin{array}{l} \tau_ {1} \\ \tau_ {2} \end{array} \right] - \left[ \begin{array}{l} \tau_ {v 1} \\ \tau_ {v 2} \end{array} \right] - \left[ \begin{array}{l} \tau_ {l 1} \\ \tau_ {l 2} \end{array} \right] \tag {20} \\ \end{array}
$$

Where

$$M _ {1 1} = (m _ {1} + m _ {2}) l _ {1} ^ {2} + m _ {2} l _ {2} ^ {2} + 2 m _ {2} l _ {1} l _ {2} \cos (q _ {1})
\begin{array}{l} M _ {1 2} = m _ {2} l _ {2} ^ {2} + m _ {2} l _ {1} l _ {2} \cos (q _ {2}) \\ M _ {2 1} = m _ {2} l _ {2} ^ {2} + 2 m _ {2} l _ {1} l _ {2} \cos (q _ {2}) \\ M _ {2 2} = m _ {2} l _ {2} ^ {2} \\ V _ {1} = - m _ {2} l _ {1} l _ {2} (2 \dot {q} _ {1} \dot {q} _ {2} + \dot {q} _ {2} ^ {2}) \sin (q _ {2}) \\ V _ {2} = m _ {2} l _ {1} l _ {2} \dot {q} _ {1} ^ {2} \sin (q _ {2}) \\ G _ {1} = (m _ {1} + m _ {2}) g l _ {1} \cos (q _ {1}) \\ + m _ {2} g l _ {2} \cos (q _ {1} + q _ {2}) \\ G _ {2} = m _ {2} g l _ {2} \cos (q _ {1} + q _ {2}) \\ \end{array}

\begin{array}{l} G _ {1} = (m _ {1} + m _ {2}) g l _ {1} \cos (q _ {1}) \\ + m _ {2} g l _ {2} \cos (q _ {1} + q _ {2}) \\ \end{array}
$$

and the Earth gravity constant $g = 9 . 8 0 7 m / s ^ { 2 }$ . The disturbance torques $\tau _ { v }$ and $\tau _ { l }$ will be presented subsequently. All of the above equations are written in the block that is representing the dynamics of the manipulator in Figure 1.
