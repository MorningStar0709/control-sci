$$
\begin{array}{l} | c _ {n} (x _ {n, i}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) - c _ {n} (x _ {n, j}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {D}}) | = \left| \int_ {S _ {n, i}} c (z, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) \nu_ {n, i} (d z) - \int_ {S _ {n, j}} c (r, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \right| \\ = \left| \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} c (z, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) - c (r, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \right| \\ \leq \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} | c (z, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) - c (x _ {n, i}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) | \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ + \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} | c (x _ {n, i}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) - c (x _ {n, j}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) | \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ + \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} | c (x _ {n, j}, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) - c (r, a, \mu^ {\mathcal {C}}) | \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ \leq \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} L _ {2} d _ {X} (z, x _ {n, i}) \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ + \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} L _ {2} d _ {X} (x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}) \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ + \int_ {S _ {n, j}} \int_ {S _ {n, i}} L _ {2} d _ {X} (x _ {n, j}, r) \nu_ {n, i} (d z) \nu_ {n, j} (d r) \\ \leq \frac {2 L _ {2}}{n} + L _ {2} d _ {X} (x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}) \\ \leq L _ {2} \left(\frac {2}{n} + 1\right) d _ {X _ {n}} \left(x _ {n, i}, x _ {n, j}\right) \\ \end{array}
$$

by using the fact that $d _ { X } \leq 1$ and we have discrete metric on $X _ { n }$ .

Note that total variation distance is the same as $\ell _ { 1 }$ on probability measures. Using this and a straightforward application of triangle inequality as in the case of the cost function gives us the following Lipschitz bound for $p _ { n }$ :
