# Automatic Tuning

Controller parameters are often tuned manually. Experience has shown that it is difficult to adjust more than two parameters manually. From the user point of view it is therefore helpful to have tuning tools built into the controllers. Such systems are similar to adaptive controllers. They are, however, easier to design and use. With computer-based controllers it is easy to incorporate tuning tools. Such systems also started to appear industrially in the mid-1980s. See Åström and Hägglund (1995).
