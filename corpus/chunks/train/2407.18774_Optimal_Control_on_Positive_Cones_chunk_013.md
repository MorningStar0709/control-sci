# 3.2 A Linear Regulator on a Polyhedral Cone

In this subsection we will recover the results of [4, Theorem 1]. Consider the application of Theorem 1 when P is the polyhedral cone

$$
\mathcal {P} = \left\{\left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ u \end{array} \right]: \left[ \begin{array}{c c} I & 0 \\ E & - I \\ E & I \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ u \end{array} \right] \geq 0 \right\},
$$

where $E \geq 0 .$ . The motivation for this choice comes from the study of positive systems. Note in particular that the condition $( x , u ) \in \mathcal { P }$ constrains the system state to lie in the positive orthant, and the input to satisfy

$$| u | \leq E x.$$

When minimizing the cost in Theorem 1(i), we are in effect optimizing performance under the constraint that the control keeps the system state positive.

First note that the dual cone ${ \mathcal { P } } ^ { * }$ is also polyhedral, and is given by

$$
\mathcal {P} ^ {*} = \left\{\left[ \begin{array}{l} \lambda \\ \mu \end{array} \right]: \left[ \begin{array}{l} \lambda \\ \mu \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} I & 0 \\ E & - I \\ E & I \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{l} w \\ y \\ z \end{array} \right], \left[ \begin{array}{l} w \\ y \\ z \end{array} \right] \geq 0 \right\}. \tag {11}
$$

The conditions of Theorem 1 then provide conditions for solving the optimization in (i) whenever:

1) For all $( x , u ) \in \mathcal { P }$ , there exists a v such that $( A x + B u , v ) \in \mathcal { P }$ .   
2) $( s , r ) \in \mathcal { P } ^ { * }$ .   
3) There exists a suitable function $\phi .$ .

It is readily checked that 1) amounts to requiring that $A \geq \ | E B |$ . For 2) and 3) it is convenient to appeal to Theorem 2. From (11) we see that for any $\mu , y$ and z can be chosen according to

$$y = \mu_ {-} + v, z = \mu_ {+} + v,$$

where $v \geq 0 ,$ , and the $\mu _ { - }$ and $\mu _ { + }$ denote the positive and negative parts of the vector $\mu$ respectively. It then follows that $\lambda \in \mathcal { C } _ { \mu }$ if and only if

$$\lambda = w + E ^ {\top} \left(| \mu | + v\right),$$

where $w \ge 0$ . The set in Theorem 2(ii) therefore has a minimum element given by $\overline { { \lambda } } = E ^ { \top } \left| \mu \right|$ . This implies that for (2) we require that $s > E ^ { \top } \left| r \right|$ , and also that

$$\phi (\mu) = - E ^ {\top} | \mu |.$$

Condition (ii) in Theorem 1 then collapses to the existence of a $\lambda _ { * } \geq 0$ such that

$$\lambda_ {*} = s + A ^ {\top} \lambda_ {*} - E ^ {\top} \left| r + B ^ {\top} \lambda_ {*} \right|,$$

which can be checked with linear programming. The convex program in (iii) similarly simplifies, this time to the following linear program:

Maximize $\lambda ^ { \top } x _ { 0 }$

$$
\text { subject   to } \left[ \begin{array}{c} s \\ r \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} I - A ^ {\top} & I & E ^ {\top} & E ^ {\top} \\ - B ^ {\top} & 0 & - I & - I \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda \\ w \\ y \\ z \end{array} \right], \left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda \\ w \\ y \\ z \end{array} \right] \geq 0.
$$
