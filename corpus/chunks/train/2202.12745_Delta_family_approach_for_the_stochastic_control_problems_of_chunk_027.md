$$V _ {\mathbf {m}} ^ {N} = \int_ {\Omega^ {\ell + 1}} U (\hat {v} (y _ {0})) \prod_ {j = 0} ^ {\ell} g _ {m _ {j}} ^ {(j)} (y _ {j}) d \mathbf {y}. \tag {3.20}$$

The integrals in (3.19) and (3.20) are evaluated by Gauss-Legendre quadrature rules.

3: Finally we obtain the approximation of the value functions

$$V (t _ {n}, \mathbf {v}) \approx \sum_ {m _ {0} = 0} ^ {M _ {0}} \dots \sum_ {m _ {\ell} = 0} ^ {M _ {\ell}} V _ {\mathbf {m}} ^ {n} \prod_ {j = 0} ^ {\ell} g _ {m _ {j}} ^ {(j)} (v _ {j}),$$

and the optimal strategies

$$\pi^ {*} (t _ {n}, \mathbf {v}) \approx \pi_ {n} ^ {*} (\mathbf {v}), \quad n = 0, 1, \ldots , N,$$

where $v _ { 0 } = x .$

For the computation of delta family approach, we set

$$M = 2 0, N = 2 0 0 0, x _ {\mathrm{min}} = 1, x _ {\mathrm{max}} = 2 0,$$

and for Heston model

$$v _ {\mathrm{min}} = 0, v _ {\mathrm{max}} = 1,$$

and for multi-factor stochastic volatility model

$$v _ {\mathrm{min}} ^ {l} = - 0. 2, v _ {\mathrm{max}} ^ {l} = 0. 2, \mathrm{for} l = 1, 2, \ldots , \ell .$$

The paths of wealth process and the strategies are drawn using the delta family methods and the analytical methods in Ma et al. (2020) or Kraft (2005) for Heston model and Ma et al. (2022) for multi-factor stochastic volatility model. From Figure 2, we observe that the delta family methods are pretty accurate.
