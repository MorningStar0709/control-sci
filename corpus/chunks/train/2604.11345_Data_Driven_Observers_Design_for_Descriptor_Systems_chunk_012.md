# Remark 4 (Practical data collection strategy)

Since the structural indices $n _ { 1 }$ and s are unknown in a purely data-driven setting, one can adopt a conservative strategy based on the system dimension n (noting that $n _ { 1 } , s \le n \AA$ . To ensure the validity of Assumption 2, the input data length can be extended to cover the interval $[ 0 , T + n - 1 ]$ for some $T$ (length of the state data), and the input sequence is chosen to be persistently exciting of order $n + 1$ .

Provided the historical data satisfies the informativity requirements (as formalized in Assumption 2), Lemma 1 shows that the subspace spanned by the history trajectories uniquely characterizes the system’s behavior. The following theorem shows the design of observers from these data matrices.
