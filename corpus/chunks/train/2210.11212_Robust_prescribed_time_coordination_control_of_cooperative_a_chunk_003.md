It is worthy of noticing that the settling time for finitetime control protocols relies upon the initial conditions. This may seriously constrain the practical application of the aforementioned finite-time coordination control results since the information of initial conditions is often hard to obtain exactly. To overcome this defect, based on the fixed-time control approach proposed by Polyakov [13], a distributed nonlinear protocol was initially designed in [14] to settle the fixed-time stability and signed-average consensus problems of singleintegrator CANs under undirected connected signed graphs. It was shown that the settling time of the designed protocol is globally upper-bounded by a positive scalar irrelevant to the agents’ initial states. Subsequently, increasing attention has been paid towards the fixed-time coordination control problems of CANs [15]–[20]. Specifically, a distributed control law with heterogeneous coupling gains was constructed to achieve fixed-time bipartite consensus tracking for fractionalorder CANs with structurally balanced signed digraph topology in [15]. In [16], the problems of fixed-time bipartite consensus and bipartite consensus tracking were addressed for nonlinear CANs with external disturbances. The authors in [17] discussed the fixed-time output bipartite consensus tracking issue for heterogeneous linear CANs. The finite- and fixed-time bipartite consensus problems for single-integrator CANs over strongly connected and detail-balanced signed digraphs were examined in [18]. The problem of fixed-time bipartite containment control of nonlinear stochastic CANs with structurally balanced signed digraph topology which contain a spanning forest was treated in [19]. In [20], a unifying framework was proposed for finite- and fixed-time bipartite containment control of first-order CANs over arbitrary weakly connected signed digraphs.
