The literature on time-varying distributed optimization with constraints remains notably limited. Researchers such as He et al. [11] introduced a predictive correction method with the limitation of requiring identical Hessian matrices for each agent’s cost function, and Wang et al. [12] overlooked the nonlinear inequality constraints in the resource allocation problem. Meanwhile, Sun et al. [13] explored the adaptation of penalty approaches to avoid the need for uniform Hessian matrices. These studies highlight a gap in the literature regarding the complexities introduced by nonuniform local inequality constraints.

In the domain of distributed optimization, the robustness of algorithms against disturbances is a critical issue, as their absence can lead to unreliable system performance or even instability. With growing interest in frameworks that can manage both disturbances and time-varying costs, traditional methods like sliding mode and internal model control often fall short, focusing mainly on static cost functions. Recently, Zhang et al. [14] proposed a robust distributed optimization framework for multi-agent systems with time-varying costs affected by disturbances. However, this approach did not take into account time-varying constraints. This paper studies the distributed continuous-time optimization problem in the context of timevarying cost functions incorporating time-varying nonuniform local inequality constraints subject to disturbances.
