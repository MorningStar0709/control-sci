# Numerator:

$$s + K / M$$

First, we have s. The units of $s { \mathrm { ~ a r e ~ s e c } } ^ { - 1 } .$ . Checking the next term $\textstyle { \frac { K } { M } } ,$ , we convert each term into its fundamental units and multiply them:

$$
\begin{array}{c c c} K & 1 / M \\ \hline \frac {k g}{\sec^ {2}} & 1 / k g & = \frac {1}{\sec^ {2}} \neq \sec^ {- 1} \end{array}
$$

The units do not agree with $\sec ^ { - 1 }$ so this is an ERROR!

Denominator: The rst term is $s ^ { 4 } .$ Therefore, all terms in demoninator must have net units of $\sec ^ { - 4 }$

The next term is

$$\frac {M _ {1} + M _ {2}}{M _ {1} M _ {2}} B s ^ {3}$$

In the $\frac { M _ { 1 } + M _ { 2 } } { M _ { 1 } M _ { 2 } }$ term, the units reduce to 1/M by cancellation. Therefore we can break it down as

$$
\begin{array}{c c c c} 1 / M & B & s ^ {3} \\ \hline 1 / k g & k g / s e c & \sec^ {- 3} & = \sec^ {- 4} \end{array}
$$
