# Reduction by Feedforward

Measurable disturbances can also be reduced by feedforward. The generic principle is illustrated in Fig. 6.3. The disturbance is measured, and a control signal that attempts to counteract the disturbance is generated and applied to the process. Feedforward is particularly useful for disturbances generated by changes in the command or reference signals or for cascaded processes when disturbances downstream are generated by variations in processes upstream.
