# C. Lyapunov-Based Stability Condition

To guarantee convergence and safety, we define a quadratic Lyapunov candidate function:

$$V (x) = \frac {1}{2} \left(z ^ {2} + \beta v _ {r} ^ {2} + \gamma v _ {e} ^ {2}\right), \tag {5}$$

where $\beta$ and $\gamma$ are positive weighting coefficients.

The time derivative of V (x) is:

$$\dot {V} (x) = z (v _ {r} - h u) + \beta v _ {r} (a _ {l} - u) + \gamma v _ {e} u. \tag {6}$$

The stability condition is expressed as:

$$\dot {V} (x) \leq - c V (x), \tag {7}$$

where $c > 0$ is a decay constant. To softly enforce this condition during training, we define a Lyapunov penalty term:

$$\mathcal {L} _ {\text { stab }} = \lambda \max (0, \dot {V} (x) + c V (x)), \tag {8}$$

where λ controls the penalty strength. This ensures that trajectories violating the stability constraint incur additional loss.
