# B.4 Proof of Lemma 3.3

First, noting that $\| M \| _ { F } = \| v e c ( M ) \|$ , the upper bound on $\| v e c ( M ) \|$ follows directly from $M \in \mathcal { M }$ and (15).

We then prove (27). Recalling the expression of $\nabla f _ { t } ( v e c ( M ) )$ given by (23), we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\| \nabla f _ {t} (\boldsymbol {v e c} (M)) \right\| \leq 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \boldsymbol {v e c} (M) - x _ {t} \right\| \\ \leq 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| \left(\left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| \left\| \boldsymbol {v e c} (M) \right\| + \left\| x _ {t} \right\|\right). \\ \end{array}
$$

It then follows from Lemma 3.2 and (15) that

$$\| \nabla f _ {t} (\pmb {v e c} (M)) \| \leq 2 \sqrt {h} R _ {y} (\sqrt {\min \{p , n \}} \kappa_ {F} ^ {2} \frac {\sqrt {h} R _ {y}}{1 - \gamma_ {F}} + R _ {x}).$$

Next, we prove (28). Recalling the expression of $\nabla ^ { 2 } f _ { t } ( v e c ( M ) )$ given by (24), we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\| \nabla^ {2} f _ {t} (\boldsymbol {v e c} (M)) \right\| = 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} ^ {\top} Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| \\ \leq 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| ^ {2} \\ \leq 2 h R _ {y} ^ {2}, \\ \end{array}
$$

where we used the fact that $\left\| Y _ { t - 1 : t - h } ^ { \top } \right\| = \left\| Y _ { t - 1 : t - h } \right\|$ and Lemma 3.2.

Finally, we prove (29). Recalling that $f _ { t } ( M ) = \| x _ { t } - \hat { x } _ { t } ( M ) \| ^ { 2 }$ with $\begin{array} { r } { \hat { x } _ { t } ( M ) = \sum _ { s = 1 } ^ { h } M _ { s } y _ { t } } \end{array}$ −s for any $M \in \mathcal { M }$ and $Y _ { t - 1 : t - h }$ defined in (22), we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} f _ {t} (M) \leq 2 \| x _ {t} \| ^ {2} + 2 \left\| \sum_ {s = 1} ^ {h} M _ {s} y _ {t - s} \right\| ^ {2} \\ \leq 2 R _ {x} ^ {2} + 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| ^ {2} \left\| \boldsymbol {v e c} (M) \right\| ^ {2} \\ \leq 2 R _ {x} ^ {2} + 2 h R _ {y} ^ {2} \min \{p, n \} \kappa_ {F} ^ {4} \frac {1}{(1 - \gamma_ {F}) ^ {2}}, \\ \end{array}
$$

where the last relation follows from the upper bounds on $\| Y _ { t - 1 : t - h } \|$ and $\| v e c ( M ) \|$ that we have shown above. ■
