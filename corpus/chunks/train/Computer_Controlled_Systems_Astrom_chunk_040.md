DDC was a major change of direction in the development of computer-controlled systems. Interest was focused on the basic control functions instead of the supervisory functions of the earlier systems. Considerable progress was made in the years 1963–1965. Specifications for DDC systems were worked out jointly between users and vendors. Problems related to choice of sampling period and control algorithms, as well as the key problem of reliability, were discussed extensively. The DDC concept was quickly accepted although DDC systems often turned out to be more expensive than corresponding analog systems.
