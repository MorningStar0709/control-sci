The example shows that to solve the problem, the specified closed-loop characteristic polynomial and consequently the controller must be of sufficiently high orders. This can also be seen easily by simply counting equations and unknowns. Increasing the order of the controller with one gives an increase of two parameters, but the number of equations only increases by one. The problem will thus be overdetermined when the closed-loop characteristic polynomial has sufficiently high order. The increased degrees of freedom can be used to introduce auxiliary constraints.
