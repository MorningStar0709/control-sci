# Kinematic Model of the Robot

A differential-drive mobile robot is a widely used robotic platform for warehouse applications and consists of two independently actuated wheels mounted on a common axis, enabling both forward motion and rotation through differential wheel speeds [14]. This simple mechanical structure makes the model convenient for analysis, control design, and simulation. The pose of the robot in the planar workspace is defined by the state vector

$$
\mathbf {x} (t) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {r} (t) \\ y _ {r} (t) \\ \theta (t) \end{array} \right],
$$

where $x _ { r } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } , y _ { r } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ denote the position of the robot in the 2D global frame, and θ(t) denotes its orientation. Let $\omega _ { l } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ and $\omega _ { r } ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ be the angular velocities of the left and right wheels, and by denoting with r > 0 and L > 0 the wheel radius and the distance between wheels, respectively, the forward linear velocity $v ( t ) \in \mathbb { R }$ and angular velocity $w ( t ) \in  { \mathbb { R } }$ of the robot’s center are:

$$
\begin{array}{l} v (t) = \frac {r}{2} (\omega_ {r} (t) + \omega_ {l} (t)) \\ w (t) = \frac {2}{L} \left(\omega_ {r} (t) - \omega_ {l} (t)\right) \tag {1} \\ \end{array}
$$

On the other hand, the nonholonomic kinematic model of the robot can be expressed as:

$$\dot {x} _ {r} (t) = v (t) \cos \theta (t)\dot {y} _ {r} (t) = v (t) \sin \theta (t) \tag {2}\dot {\theta} (t) = w (t)$$
