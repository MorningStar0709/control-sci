$$v [ \beta (t) ] ^ {T} = [ 1, \sigma , \sigma^ {2}, \sigma^ {3}, \sigma^ {4}, \sigma^ {5}, \sigma^ {6}, \sigma^ {7}, \sigma^ {8}, \sigma^ {9} ]g ^ {T} (t) = [ 0 - \hat {b} _ {1}, 0, - \hat {a} _ {1}, 1, 0, 0, 0, 0, 1 ]$$

For this particular case, the highest power of $\sigma$ is 9 and therefore we can limit the domain D for $i _ { \mathrm { m a x } } = 1 0$ (but this is a particular case). Defining now a vector whose components are det $M [ \hat { \theta } ( t ) ]$ obtained for various $\sigma _ { i }$ , one gets:

$$p ^ {T} (t) = [ g ^ {T} (t) v (\sigma_ {1}), \dots , g ^ {T} (t) v (\sigma_ {1 0}) ] = g ^ {T} (t) N \tag {12.76}$$

where N is a $1 0 \times 1 0$ Vandermonde matrix:

$$
N = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c} 1 & 1 & \dots & \dots & 1 \\ \sigma_ {1} & \sigma_ {2} & & & \sigma_ {1 0} \\ \sigma_ {1} ^ {2} & \sigma_ {2} ^ {2} & & & \sigma_ {1 0} ^ {2} \\ \vdots & \vdots & & & \vdots \\ \vdots & \vdots & & & \vdots \\ \sigma_ {1} ^ {9} & \sigma_ {2} ^ {9} & & & \sigma_ {1 0} ^ {9} \end{array} \right] \tag {12.77}
$$

Therefore, the objective is to show that indeed $\| \boldsymbol { p } ( t ) \| > 0$ which will imply that:

$$\exists \sigma_ {i} \in D \quad \text { such that } | \det M [ \bar {\theta} (t) ] | > 0$$

From (12.76) one has:

$$\left\| p (t) \right\| ^ {2} = p ^ {T} (t) p (t) = g ^ {T} (t) N N ^ {T} g (t) \geq \left\| g (t) \right\| ^ {2} \lambda_ {\min} [ N N ^ {T} ] \tag {12.78}$$

From matrix calculus, one has (Lancaster and Tismenetsky 1985):

$$
\begin{array}{l} (\det N) ^ {2} = \det N N ^ {T} = \lambda_ {\min} [ N N ^ {T} ] \dots \lambda_ {\max} [ N N ^ {T} ] \\ \leq \lambda_ {\min} N N ^ {T} (\operatorname{tr} [ N N ^ {T} ]) ^ {l - 1} \tag {12.79} \\ \end{array}
$$

taking into account that:

$$\operatorname{tr} [ N N ^ {T} ] = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {l} \lambda_ {i} [ N N ^ {T} ]$$

Combining (12.78) and (12.79), one gets:

$$\| p (t) \| \geq \| g (t) \| \frac {| \det N |}{(\operatorname{tr} [ N N ^ {T} ]) ^ {(l - 1) / 2}} \tag {12.80}$$

A lower bound for the vector $\| g ( t ) \|$ can be obtained taking into account the hypothesis that the true plant model polynomials do not have common factors.

$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 <   \delta_ {0} \leq | \det M (\theta) | = | \det M [ \hat {\theta} (t) + F (t) ^ {1 / 2} \beta^ {*} (t) ] | \\ = \left| g ^ {T} (t) v \left(\beta^ {*} (t)\right) \right| \leq \| g (t) \| \| v \left(\beta^ {*} (t)\right) \| \tag {12.81} \\ \end{array}
$$

From the above relationship, one gets:

$$\| g (t) \| \geq \frac {\delta_ {0}}{\max \| v (\beta^ {*} (t)) \|} \tag {12.82}$$

and from (12.80), it results that:

$$\| p (t) \| \geq \frac {\delta_ {0} | \det N |}{\max \| v (\beta^ {*} (t)) \| (\operatorname{tr} N N ^ {T}) ^ {(l - 1) / 2}} \tag {12.83}$$

which is the desired result. The solution to the “singularity” problem presented above can be extended to cover the case when the system is subject to disturbances (see Sect. 12.3). The extension of this solution to the stochastic case is discussed in Guo (1996).
