# C. Wheeled Mobile Robot Kinematics

Consider a nonholonomic wheeled mobile robot that cannot slip in the lateral direction. The corresponding Pfaffian constraint is

$$\dot {x} \sin (\theta) - \dot {y} \cos (\theta) = 0.$$

The kinematic model can be obtained by expressing the entire range of possible velocities, which is shown as follows

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} \\ \dot {y} \\ \dot {\theta} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \cos \theta & 0 \\ \sin \theta & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \mu \\ \omega \end{array} \right] = C (\theta) u, \tag {4}
$$

where $u = [ \mu , \omega ] ^ { \intercal } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 }$ is the control input, including linear velocity µ and angular velocity ω.
