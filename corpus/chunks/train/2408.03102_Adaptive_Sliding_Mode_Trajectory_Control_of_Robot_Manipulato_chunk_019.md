# 5. Conclusion

In this paper, we present an adaptivesliding mode approach for position control of robot manipulators, in joint space, in the presence of uncertain torques due to vibration and payload variation. Firstly, the problem of tracking control for an n-degree-of-freedom, revolute-joints, serial robot arm is formulated. Secondly, the controller is designed based on the Lyapunov analysis and following the adaptive and sliding mode control approach. The rms values of the error are 0.119 deg and 0.03 deg for link 1 and link 2, respectively. Which means, despite the uncertainty that is caused by the uncertain dynamics, vibration, and payload variation, the controller is shown to guarantee asymptotic tracking displacement errors. Additionally, the input torque rms values are 1.81 Nm and 1.67 Nm for joint 1 and joint 2, respectively. These values are relatively law compared with other approaches from the literature despite the high oscillation. This makes the controller implementable at low cost and reasonable control effort.
