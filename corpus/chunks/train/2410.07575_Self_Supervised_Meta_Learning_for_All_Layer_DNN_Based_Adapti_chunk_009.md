# A. Self-Supervised Meta-Learning

In this section, we introduce Self-Supervised Meta-Learning (SSML). Following previous works [32], we assume that w changes continuously along the trajectory and leverage the temporal continuity of environment conditions for dataset generation and self-supervised learning. Precollected trajectories are divided into short clips, each split into adaptation and training sets. Inspired by [30], the SSML algorithm follows a Bi-level optimization structure with an adaptation stage as the inner loop and a training stage as the outer loop (Fig. 1 (b))

During the adaptation stage, gradient descent-based adaptation is applied to the adaptation set, computing the network parameter residual $\Delta \theta .$ In the training stage, the prediction loss of the network $f ( q , \dot { q } , \theta _ { 0 } + \Delta \theta )$ is evaluated on the training set to update $\theta _ { 0 }$ . The SSML training process is visualized in Fig. 2 and outlined in Algorithm 1.

1) Data Collection: The SSML task dataset is defined as

$$\mathcal {D} _ {k} = \{\mathcal {B} _ {k} ^ {a}, \mathcal {B} _ {k} ^ {t} \}\mathcal {B} _ {k} ^ {a} = \{x ^ {(j)}, y ^ {(j)} \} _ {j = k} ^ {k + H _ {a}} \tag {5}\mathcal {B} _ {k} ^ {t} = \{x ^ {(j)}, y ^ {(j)} \} _ {j = k + H _ {a} + 1} ^ {k + H _ {a} + H _ {t}}$$

where $B _ { k } ^ { a }$ and $B _ { k } ^ { t }$ denote the adaptation and training sets, respectively, with $( x ^ { ( j ) } , y ^ { ( j ) } )$ being the robot state and disturbance measurement at step $j .$ . The disturbance measurement is obtained via numerical differentiation with robot’s nominal dynamics model [41]. $H _ { a }$ and $H _ { t }$ represent the adaptation and prediction horizons for each trajectory clip. The entire meta-learning dataset is constructed as

$$\mathcal {D} = \left\{\mathcal {D} _ {k} \right\} _ {k = 1} ^ {N} \tag {6}$$

2) Adaptation stage: The regression loss $\mathcal { L } ( \theta , B )$ on dataset is defined as

$$\mathcal {L} (\theta , \mathcal {B}) = \sum_ {x ^ {(j)}, y ^ {(j)} \in \mathcal {B}} \| y ^ {(j)} - f (x ^ {(j)}, \theta) \| ^ {2} \tag {7}$$

The adaptation stage performs gradient descent on each adaptation set $B _ { k } ^ { a }$ to update $\Delta \theta _ { k }$ :

$$\Delta \theta_ {k} ^ {\prime} = \Delta \theta_ {k} - \alpha \nabla_ {\theta} \mathcal {L} (\theta_ {0}, \mathcal {B} _ {k} ^ {a}) \tag {8}$$
