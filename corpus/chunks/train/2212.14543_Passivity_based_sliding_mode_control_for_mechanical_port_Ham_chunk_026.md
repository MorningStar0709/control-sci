# 5 Robustness analysis

Sliding mode control is known as a robust control method against modeling uncertainties and disturbances. In this section, we investigate the robustness of the proposed control system. Let us consider the system (1) with disturbances

$$
\binom{\dot {q}}{\dot {p}} = \left( \begin{array}{c c} 0 _ {m} & I _ {m} \\ - I _ {m} & - D _ {0} (q, p) \end{array} \right) \binom{\nabla_ {q} H _ {0}}{\nabla_ {p} H _ {0}} + \binom{0 _ {m}}{G _ {0} (q)} u + \binom{d _ {\mathrm{um}}}{d _ {\mathrm{m}}},
H _ {0} (q, p) = \frac {1}{2} p ^ {\top} M (q) ^ {- 1} p, \tag {35}$$

where $d _ { \mathrm { u m } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ and $d _ { \mathrm { m } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ represent unmatched and matched disturbance respectively. Since we consider mechanical systems, it is unlikely that disturbances will enter the kinematics, but here we assume that sensor noise and other factors are influencing the kinematics.

For the system (35), let us apply the feedback input (15) that has free parameters $\phi$ and U . In this section, a vector function that is linear in momentum η is selected as φ, so the sliding variable σ is given by

$$\sigma = \phi (q, \eta) = \psi (q) + \eta ,$$

where $\psi : \mathbb { R } ^ { m }  \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is a diffeomorphism satisfying $\psi ( 0 ) = 0$ . This choice satisfies the condition of $\phi ( q , \eta )$ in Theorem 1. Note that the matrix Λ defined by (14) becomes a function of $q$ as

$$\Lambda (q) = \frac {\partial \psi (q)}{\partial q} T (q) + T (q) ^ {\top} \frac {\partial \psi (q)}{\partial q} ^ {\top}.$$

Then the feedback input is calculated as

$$u = - G (q) ^ {- 1} \bigg (\Lambda (q) \nabla_ {\sigma} U (\sigma) - \bigg (D (q, \eta) + \frac {\partial \psi (q)}{\partial q} T (q) \bigg) \eta \bigg),$$

and we obtain the following closed-loop port-Hamiltonian system with disturbances

$$
\binom{\dot {q}}{\dot {\eta}} = \left( \begin{array}{c c} - T (q) \frac {\partial \psi (q)}{\partial q} ^ {- \top} & T (q) \\ - T (q) ^ {\top} & - \frac {\partial \psi (q)}{\partial q} T (q) \end{array} \right) \binom{\nabla_ {q} H _ {\mathrm{smc}}}{\nabla_ {\eta} H _ {\mathrm{smc}}} + \binom{d _ {\mathrm{um}}}{T (q) ^ {\top} d _ {\mathrm{m}}},
H _ {\mathrm{smc}} (q, \eta) = \frac {1}{2} \| \eta \| ^ {2} + U (\psi (q) + \eta). \tag {36}$$
