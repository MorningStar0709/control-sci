# B. Synthesis for Prescribed Gain Robustness

We now turn from certification to synthesis. Here the objective is not merely to verify robustness for a given controller, but to design the nominal feedback gain $K$ so that a desired gain interval $\left[ \underline { { \kappa } } , \bar { \kappa } \right]$ guarantees that the origin is GES for the closed loop (7). The key point is that, for fixed $\left( \underline { { \kappa } } , \bar { \kappa } \right)$ , the endpoint LMIs in Prop. 5 can be converted into convex synthesis conditions by the standard change of variables $X = P ^ { - 1 }$ and $Y = K X$ .

Recall that

$$\tilde {A} (\kappa) = A _ {0} - \kappa U V ^ {\top}, \quad A _ {0} = A - B K, \tag {31}$$

with U fixed by $B , c ,$ and G. For synthesis, it is convenient to rewrite $\tilde { A } ( \kappa )$ in a form affine in $K$ .

The next lemma gives this decomposition. The key observation is that, once $\kappa , G ,$ , and α are fixed, the dependence of the perturbed active mode on the gain K is affine.
