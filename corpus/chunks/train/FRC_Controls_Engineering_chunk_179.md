# 6.12.7 Observability of specific states

If you want to know if a specific state is observable, first find its corresponding eigenvalue λ in A. Then, that state is observable if

$$
\operatorname{rank} \left(\left[ \begin{array}{c} \lambda \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} \\ \mathbf {C} \end{array} \right]\right) = n \tag {6.44}
$$

where n is the number of states.
