In response to these challenges, control engineers have progressively shifted towards more sophisticated modeling techniques that better account for the nonlinearities present in UAV systems. Early methods, such as Proportional-Derivative (PD) control, offered a practical and computationally simple solution for basic flight stabilization, but they were limited in their ability to handle more complex flight conditions. As UAV missions have expanded in scope and complexity, the need for more advanced control algorithms, capable of dealing with nonlinear dynamics, multivariable interactions, and external disturbances, has become evident. This has led to the exploration of optimization-based strategies, such as MPC, and the increasing integration of data-driven methods to improve model accuracy.
