Remark 5 (Attainable Sets Represent Actuator Constraints). The low-level control torques, $\dot { { \boldsymbol { \tau } } } ^ { F }$ and $\tau ^ { E }$ , as a function of nominal control torque τ¯ and dynamic input allocator ζ, must lie within the attainable sets of the FES-induced torque, $\mathbb { A } _ { F }$ , and the exoskeleton torque, $\mathbb { A } _ { E }$ , respectively. This can be expressed as follows

$$\tau^ {F} \in \mathbb {A} _ {F} (\tau^ {F _ {f}} \in \mathbb {A} _ {F _ {f}}, \tau^ {F _ {e}} \in \mathbb {A} _ {F _ {e}}), \tag {15a}\tau^ {E} \in \mathbb {A} _ {E}, \tag {15b}$$

where $\tau ^ { F _ { f } }$ and $\tau ^ { F _ { e } }$ are flexor and extensor components of $\tau ^ { F }$ . These sets account for the attainable torque of both assistive devices while incorporating their magnitude and bandwidth constraints. Note that bandwidth limits are associated with the actuator dynamics (e.g., rate and acceleration constraints) [45]–[47].
