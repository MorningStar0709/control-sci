# 1 Introduction

This paper is concerned with optimal control of the form

$$\text { Minimize } \quad \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {\infty} [ s ^ {\top} x (t) + r ^ {\top} u (t) ] \text { over } \{u (t) \} _ {t = 0} ^ {\infty} \tag {1}\text { subject to } \quad x (t + 1) = A x (t) + B u (t)(x (t), u (t)) \in \mathcal {P}, \quad x (0) = x _ {0}.$$

where $\mathcal { P }$ is a proper cone in $\mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ . Assumptions are made to make sure that the objective function is non-negative and thus finite if and only if the Bellman equation

$$J ^ {*} (x) = \min _ {(x (t), u (t)) \in \mathcal {P}} \left[ s ^ {\top} x + r ^ {\top} u + J ^ {*} (A x + B u) \right]$$

has a non-negative solution $J ^ { * }$ . The minimizing u gives an optimal control policy. See for example [1, section 3.1].

The setting above turns out be one of the rare situations when the Bellman equation has an explicit solution. Otherwise, the most well known case from the literature is probably linear dynamics and quadratic cost, introduced in the pioneering work of [2]. Another case of fundamental importance is when both x and u are restricted to finite sets. This setting leads to shortest path problems on graphs, with an extensive literature dating back to the work of [3]. Recently, a third class of problems with explicit solutions to the Bellman equation was introduced in the context of linear systems on the positive orthant in $\mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ (the so called positive systems), see [4, 5]. A closely related case with bilinear dynamics is treated in [6].

The purpose of this note is to show that several previous instances where the Bellman equation can be solved explicitly are in fact special cases of a more general class of optimal control problems stated in terms of positive cones.
