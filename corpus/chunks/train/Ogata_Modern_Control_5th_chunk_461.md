The infinitesimal semicircular detour around the origin in the s plane maps into the GH plane as a semicircle of infinite radius. Figure 7–51 shows the s-plane contour and the $G ( s ) H ( s )$ locus in the GH plane. Points A, B, and C on the s-plane contour map into the respective points $A ^ { \prime } , B ^ { \prime }$ , and $C ^ { \prime }$ on the $G ( s ) H ( s )$ locus. As seen from Figure 7–51, points D, E, and F on the semicircle of infinite radius in the s plane map into the origin of the GH plane. Since there is no pole in the righthalf s plane and the $G ( s ) H ( s )$ locus does not encircle the $- 1 + j 0$ point, there are no zeros of the function $1 + G ( s ) H ( s )$ in the right-half s plane. Therefore, the system is stable.

For an open-loop transfer function $G ( s ) H ( s )$ involving a $1 / s ^ { n }$ factor (where $n = 2 , 3 , \dots { } )$ , the plot of $G ( s ) H ( s )$ has n clockwise semicircles of infinite radius about the origin as a representative point s moves along the semicircle of radius e (where $\varepsilon \ll 1 )$ . For example, consider the following open-loop transfer function:

$$G (s) H (s) = \frac {K}{s ^ {2} (T s + 1)}$$

Then

$$\lim _ {s \rightarrow \varepsilon e ^ {j \theta}} G (s) H (s) = \frac {K}{\varepsilon^ {2} e ^ {2 j \theta}} = \frac {K}{\varepsilon^ {2}} e ^ {- 2 j \theta}$$
