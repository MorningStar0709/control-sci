The standard first step of data preparation is the rescaling of $U _ { i } ( n )$ to fit the range [−1, 1] for the neural network training algorithm to work properly. Then this data series is transformed to delay-coordinate embedding vectors36,37 $R ( n )$ of dimension $D _ { e }$ :

$$R (n) = \big (U _ {i} (n), U _ {i} (n - 1), \dots , U _ {i} (n - D _ {e} + 1) \big). \tag {3}$$

The dimension $D _ { e }$ is usually unknown in model reconstruction procedure. There are rather heuristic algorithms of their determinations1,37. We will not use them since dimension reduction procedure via VAE is not sensible to the very exact choice of $D _ { e }$ . It is enough to take it excessively large.

Vectors R are used to train the VAE whose detailed structure is described in Appendix B. The result is a two-step map. The first part, the encoder, maps $R$ to two vectors $\mu$ and $\sigma$ of the reduced dimension $D _ { u }$ . The second part, the decoder, maps a random sample u of a Gaussian distribution $N ( \mu , \sigma )$ to a vector $R ^ { \prime }$ that is approximately equals to R.
