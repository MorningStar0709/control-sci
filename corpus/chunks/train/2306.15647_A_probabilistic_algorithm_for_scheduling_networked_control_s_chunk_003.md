◦ NCSs whose shared communication networks have a limited capacity and are prone to data losses. In particular, we consider a probabilistic data loss model.

We work on the following premise: We consider an NCS consisting of multiple discrete-time linear plants whose feedback loops are closed through a shared communication network. We assume that the plants are unstable in openloop and the controllers are stabilizing. Due to a limited communication capacity of the network, only a few plants can exchange information with their controllers at any instant of time. Consequently, the remaining plants operate in open-loop at every time instant. Additionally, the plants communicating with their controllers at an instant of time may operate in open-loop as the control input may be lost in the network. Our specific contributions are the following:

◦ First, we consider the probabilistic scheduling algorithm of [13] and provide necessary and sufficient conditions on the plant and the controller dynamics, the capacity of the network and the probability of data losses, such that a scheduling logic obtained from this algorithm ensures stochastic stability of each plant in the NCS under data losses.   
◦ Second, given the plant dynamics, the capacity of the shared communication network and the probability of data losses, we discuss the design of state-feedback controllers such that our stability conditions are satisfied.

A generalized version of the stability condition presented in [13] falls as a special case of our results. Similar to [13], the key tool of our analysis is a Markovian jump linear system modelling of the plants in the NCS. Numerical examples are presented to demonstrate our results.

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows: In Section II we formulate the problem under consideration. Our results are presented in Section III. Numerical experiments are discussed in Section IV. We conclude in Section V with a brief discussion on future research direction.
