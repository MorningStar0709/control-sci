# V. EXAMPLE AND SIMULATION

In this section, two numerical examples from [11], [38] are employed to certificate the effectiveness and merits of our proposed methods. All numerical computations were performed using Matlab, together with the SeDuMi toolbox [39].

Example 1. Consider the linear system used in [38]

$$
\dot {x} (\nu) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & - 0. 1 \end{array} \right] x (\nu) + \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0. 1 \end{array} \right] u (\nu), \nu \geq 0.
$$

Discretizing the system leads to

$$x (t + 1) = A (T _ {k}) x (t) + B (T _ {k}) u (t), t \in \mathbb {N} \tag {42}$$

where $T _ { k } > 0$ is a discretization interval, and

$$
A (T _ {k}) := e ^ {\left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & - 0. 1 \end{array} \right] T _ {k}}, B (T _ {k}) := \int_ {0} ^ {T _ {k}} e ^ {A (s)} \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0. 1 \end{array} \right] d s.
$$

We assume that the linear sampled-data state-feedback controller $u ( t ) \ = \ K x ( t _ { k } )$ is used to control the system. For $t \in \mathbb { N } _ { [ t _ { k } , t _ { k + 1 } - 1 ] } ,$ , the system (42) can be written as

$$x (t + 1) = A (T _ {k}) x (t) + B (T _ {k}) K x (t _ {k}). \tag {43}$$

(Upper bounds of h for known A and B) Similar to [25], [28], the controller gain $K = - [ 3 . 7 5 ~ 1 1 . 5 ]$ is employed. By setting $\sigma _ { 1 } = 0 , \sigma _ { 2 } = 0$ , with θ → ∞ (that is, $\eta ( \tau _ { j } ^ { k } ) = 0 )$ , the proposed triggering scheme (9) reduces to a periodic transmission scheme. We first consider the model-based stability analysis. Leveraging Theorem 1 with discretization interval $T _ { k } = 0 . 0 1$ , the maximum sampling interval h¯ leading to closed-loop stability is $\bar { h } = 1 7 3$ with ${ h = 1 }$ . Compared with the modelbased results of $h = 1 1 1$ obtained by [40], h¯ = 122 by [25], $\bar { h } = 1 3 3$ by [41], h¯ = 136 by [28], and h¯ = 169 by [23], Theorem 1 in our paper provides improvements of 55.8%, 41.8%, 30.0%, 27.2%, and 2.3%, respectively.
