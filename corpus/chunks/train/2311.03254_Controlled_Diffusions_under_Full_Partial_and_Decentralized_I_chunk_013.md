To study this, we adopt Girsanov’s measure transformation, building on [5]. This enables us to transform the probability measure P to an equivalent probability measure $\mathbb { P } _ { 0 }$ , under which the SDE becomes

$$d X _ {t} ^ {\prime} = \sigma (X _ {t} ^ {\prime}) d W _ {t},$$

which in fact is policy independent.

Somewhat differently from the previous subsection, we define relaxed wide-sense admissible control policy in the following. As in the above, we first place the Young topology on the control action space, by viewing the joint distribution of $X ^ { ' }$ and the control policy m to be a probability measure on $C ( [ 0 , T ] ; \mathbb { R } ^ { d } ) \times \mathcal { P } _ { \lambda } ( [ 0 , T ] \times \mathbb { U } )$ with its fixed marginal on $C ( [ 0 , T ] ; \mathbb { R } ^ { d } )$ to be the Wiener measure, moreover we require that, under the measure-transformed model, $m _ { [ 0 , s ] }$ be independent of $W _ { t } \mathrm { ~ - ~ } W _ { s }$ for any $t > s$ . Let UWRC denotes the space of all wide-sense admissible control policies . A typical element of UWRC is denoted by m (without loss of generality) .

For any $t \geq 0$ , let

$$Z _ {t} = \exp \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {t} \sigma^ {- 1} (X _ {s}) b (X _ {s}, m _ {s}) d W _ {s} - \frac {1}{2} \int_ {0} ^ {t} | \sigma^ {- 1} (X _ {s}) b (X _ {s}, m _ {s}) | ^ {2} d s \right].$$
