# D.5 Proof of gradient dominance property in Lemma 2

Note that $X = X _ { ( L ) }$ satisfies

$$X = A _ {L} X A _ {L} ^ {\intercal} + Q + L R L ^ {\intercal}. \tag {19}$$

Then, by combining (18) and (19), and some algebraic manipulation, we recover part of the gradient information, i.e., $\left( - L R + A _ { L } X H ^ { \intercal } \right)$ , in the gap of cost matrices by arriving at the following identity

$$
\begin{array}{l} X - X ^ {*} - A _ {L ^ {*}} (X - X ^ {*}) A _ {L ^ {*}} ^ {\intercal} \\ = (L R - A _ {L} X H ^ {\intercal}) (L - L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal} + (L - L ^ {*}) \left(R L ^ {\intercal} - H X A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}\right) \\ - (L - L ^ {*}) R \left(L - L ^ {*}\right) ^ {\intercal} - (L - L ^ {*}) H X H ^ {\intercal} \left(L - L ^ {*}\right) ^ {\intercal} \tag {20} \\ \preceq \frac {1}{a} (L R - A _ {L} X H ^ {\intercal}) (R L ^ {\intercal} - H X A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}) + a (L - L ^ {*}) (L - L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal} \\ - (L - L ^ {*}) (R + H X H ^ {\intercal}) (L - L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal} \\ \end{array}
$$

where the upperbound is valid for any choice of $a > 0$ . Now, as $R \succ 0 .$ , we choose $a = \underline { { \lambda } } ( R ) / 2$ . As $X \succeq 0 .$ , it further upperbounds

$$
\begin{array}{l} X - X ^ {*} - A _ {L ^ {*}} (X - X ^ {*}) A _ {L ^ {*}} ^ {\intercal} \\ \preceq \frac {2}{\underline {{\lambda}} (R)} (- L R + A _ {L} X H ^ {\intercal}) (- R L ^ {\intercal} + H X A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}) \\ - \frac {\underline {{\lambda}} (R)}{2} (L - L ^ {*}) (L - L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Now, let $\tilde { X }$ and $\widehat { X }$ be, respectively, the unique solution of the following Lyapunov equations

$$\tilde {X} = A _ {L ^ {*}} \tilde {X} A _ {L ^ {*}} ^ {\intercal} + (- L R + A _ {L} X H ^ {\intercal}) (- R L ^ {\intercal} + H X A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}),\widehat {X} = A _ {L ^ {*}} \widehat {X} A _ {L ^ {*}} ^ {\intercal} + (L - L ^ {*}) (L - L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal}.$$

Then by comparison, we conclude that

$$X - X ^ {*} \preceq \frac {2}{\underline {{\lambda}} (R)} \tilde {X} - \frac {\underline {{\lambda}} (R)}{2} \hat {X}.$$

Recall that by the fact in (16),

$$J (L) - J (L ^ {*}) = \operatorname{tr} \left[ (X - X ^ {*}) H ^ {\intercal} H \right] \leq \frac {2}{\underline {{\lambda}} (R)} \operatorname{tr} \left[ \tilde {X} H ^ {\intercal} H \right] - \frac {\underline {{\lambda}} (R)}{2} \operatorname{tr} \left[ \widehat {X} H ^ {\intercal} H \right]. \tag {21}$$
