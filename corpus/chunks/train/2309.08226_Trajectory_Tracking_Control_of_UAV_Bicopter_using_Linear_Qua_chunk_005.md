Where $C _ { T }$ is the propeller’s thrust coefficient. $\Omega _ { R }$ and $\Omega _ { L }$ represent the rotors’ right and left rotational speeds, and $\gamma _ { R }$ and $\gamma _ { L }$ represent the rotors’ right and left tilt angles.

$$

The rotation subsystem (roll, pitch, and yaw) is the inner loop, and the translation subsystem (x, y position, and z (altitude)) is the outer loop. Based on the dynamic solution of the model using Newton-Euler [21]–[23], we get Eq. (13) for translational motion and Eq. (14) for circular motion, where s = sin and c = cos.

$$\ddot {x} = - \frac {1}{m} (s \phi s \psi + c \phi s \theta c \psi) u _ {1} - \frac {c \theta c \psi}{m} u _ {3}\ddot {y} = - \frac {1}{m} (- s \phi c \psi + c \phi s \theta s \psi) u _ {1} + \frac {c \theta s \psi}{m} u _ {3}\ddot {z} = g - \frac {1}{m} (c \phi c \theta) u _ {1} - \frac {s \theta}{m} u _ {3} \tag {13}\ddot {\phi} = \frac {L}{I _ {x x}} u _ {2}\ddot {\theta} = \frac {h}{I _ {y y}} u _ {3}\ddot {\psi} = \frac {L}{I _ {z z}} u _ {4} \tag {14}$$
