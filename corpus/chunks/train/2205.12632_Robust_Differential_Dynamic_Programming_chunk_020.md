Lemma $4 . 2 \dot { } :$ Let $_ { r }$ be a symmetric matrix and let $W _ { 1 }$ have full column rank with left inverse $\boldsymbol { W } _ { 1 } ^ { \dagger }$ . Then, the dual matrix inequalities

$$\binom{(\boldsymbol {W} _ {2} \boldsymbol {W} _ {1} ^ {\dagger}) ^ {\top}}{- \boldsymbol {I}} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} ^ {- 1} \binom{(\boldsymbol {W} _ {2} \boldsymbol {W} _ {1} ^ {\dagger}) ^ {\top}}{- \boldsymbol {I}} \succ 0, \binom{\boldsymbol {I}}{0} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} ^ {- 1} \binom{\boldsymbol {I}}{0} \prec 0$$

imply the primal matrix inequalities

$$\binom {\boldsymbol {W} _ {1}} {\boldsymbol {W} _ {2}} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} \binom {\boldsymbol {W} _ {1}} {\boldsymbol {W} _ {2}} \prec 0, \quad \binom {0} {\boldsymbol {I}} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} \binom {0} {\boldsymbol {I}} \succ 0. \tag {19}$$

Proof: First, we apply Lemma 4.1 to conclude that the dual matrix inequalities are equivalent to

$$\binom {\boldsymbol {I}} {\left(\boldsymbol {W} _ {2} \boldsymbol {W} _ {1} ^ {\dagger}\right)} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} \binom {\boldsymbol {I}} {\left(\boldsymbol {W} _ {2} \boldsymbol {W} _ {1} ^ {\dagger}\right)} \prec 0, \quad \binom {0} {\boldsymbol {I}} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {P} \binom {0} {\boldsymbol {I}} \succ 0. \tag {20}$$

Now, since $\boldsymbol { W } _ { 1 } ^ { \dagger }$ is a left inverse of $W _ { 1 } ,$ it holds that $W _ { 1 } ^ { \dagger } W _ { 1 } \ = \ I$ . Consequently, by multiplying (20) from both sides with $W _ { 1 }$ , we obtain (19).

This form of the dualization lemma can be applied to (18), since it does not require a large identity block in the projection matrix.
