# 2.5 Noise estimator stability proof

The noise estimator block goal is to achieve the closed-loop stabilization of the predictor as well as to ensure a suitable disturbance estimation capability, i.e. estimate the noise vector $\overline { { \mathbf { w } } }$ . The noise estimator block governing equations are as follows

$$
\overline {{{\mathbf {w}}}} (k) = \left[ \begin{array}{l} \overline {{{w}}} _ {1} \\ \overline {{{w}}} _ {2} \\ \overline {{{w}}} _ {3} \end{array} \right] (k) = \mathbf {L e} _ {m} (k) = \left[ \begin{array}{l} l _ {1} \\ l _ {2} \\ l _ {3} \end{array} \right] \mathbf {e} _ {m} (k), \tag {11}
\mathbf {e} _ {m} (k) = \mathbf {y} (k) - \mathbf {y} _ {m} (k).$$

In (11), $\mathbf { y } _ { m }$ is the estimated output of the internal model, y is the measurement output of the plant, while L collects the closed-loop predictor gains $l _ { k } , k = 1 , \ldots , 3 .$ , for the specific DC motor case. As a result, the closed-loop predictor model can be determined by designing and tuning the $l _ { k }$ gains tradingoff between the closed-loop stability and the estimation performance.

A static-feedback is selected for the design of noise estimator, and its stability analysis is already studied in [1], Appendix B.
