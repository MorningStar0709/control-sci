$$a _ {y m} = - N V _ {m} \left(\frac {d \lambda_ {y}}{d t}\right) \sin \theta_ {m} + N V _ {m} \lambda_ {z} \cos \theta_ {m}, \tag {4.89a}a _ {z m} = - N V _ {m} \left(\frac {d \lambda}{d t}\right) \cos \psi_ {m}, \tag {4.89b}$$

where

$$
N = \text { navigation constant },V _ {m} = \text { missile's velocity },(d \lambda_ {y} / d t) = y \text {-component of LOS rate},
\begin{array}{l} \theta_ {m}, \psi_ {m} = \text { Euler   angles   from   LOS   to   target   body   coordinate   system } \\ \qquad (\theta \text { corresponds   to   the   elevation   or   pitch   angle   and } \psi \\ \qquad \text { to   the   azimuth   angle }). \end{array}
$$

In the discussion that follows, we will briefly discuss the target maneuver model, target equations, perturbation equations, and white noise roll rate. Note that target maneuver has been modeled in many different ways. For instance, in tracking a highly maneuvering target, the target can be modeled as a jerk model∗ [5], [10], [20].
