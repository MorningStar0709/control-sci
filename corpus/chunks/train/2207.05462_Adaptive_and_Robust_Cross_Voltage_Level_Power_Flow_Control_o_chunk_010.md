The number of possible sets of DERs $n _ { \mathrm { m a x } }$ results from

$$n _ {\max} = 2 ^ {m} - 1, \tag {15}$$

where m is the total number of DERs participating in the control loop. The electrical grid and all loads are not considered in the reference control loop model. In a congestion-free grid, the electrical lines affect the control loop through the line losses and their reactive power behavior. The loads affect the control loop via the voltage dependency. As shown in [23], the electrical grid and the load have only a minor impact on the control system’s performance and stability. Therefore, the electrical grid and all loads are neglected in the reference control loop model, in order to reduce computational costs.
