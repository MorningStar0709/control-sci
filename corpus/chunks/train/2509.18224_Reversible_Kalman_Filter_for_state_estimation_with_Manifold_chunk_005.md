# A. Quaternions, Rotations, and Axis-Angle Representation

We represent elements of SO(3) (3D rotations) using quaternions. A detailed exposition of quaternion kinematics can be found in [12].

For a rotation $R \in S O ( 3 )$ and a vector $v \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ , we denote by $R \cdot v$ the action of R on v. Let (0|v) denote the quaternion associated with v by appending a fourth coordinate equal to zero. There exists a unit quaternion q such that

$$q (0 | v) q ^ {- 1} = (0 | R \cdot v),$$

establishing the correspondence between unit quaternions and rotations. We denote by $R ( q )$ the rotation matrix associated with a unit quaternion q.

Furthermore, quaternions admit a logarithmic map, providing a correspondence between unit quaternions and elements of $\mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ :

$$\log (q) = u = \alpha \mathbf {u},$$

where u is the unit vector along the axis of rotation, and α is the rotation angle.

Conversely, the exponential map from $\mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ to $S O ( 3 )$ is given by

$$\exp (u) = I _ {3} + \sin (\alpha) [ \mathbf {u} ] _ {\times} + (1 - \cos (\alpha)) [ \mathbf {u} ] _ {\times} ^ {2},$$

where $[ \mathbf { u } ] _ { \times }$ is the skew-symmetric matrix associated with u. The relation between logarithmic map of quaternion and Lie groups is described in [13].
