# 2.4.4 Gao’s reaching law approach

Gao and Hung proposed a reaching law which directly specifies the dynamics of the switching surface by the differential equation, is represented in the generalized form as:

$$\dot {s} = - \varepsilon . s g n (s) - k. h (s) \tag {2.16}$$

where

$$\varepsilon = \operatorname{diag} \left[ \varepsilon_ {1}, \dots , \varepsilon_ {m} \right], \quad \varepsilon_ {i} > 0, i = 1, 2, \dots , m \tag {2.17}k = \operatorname{diag} \left[ k _ {1}, \dots , k _ {m} \right], \quad k _ {i} > 0, i = 1, 2, \dots , m \tag {2.18}$$

and

$$s g n (s) = \left[ s g n \left(s _ {1}\right), s g n \left(s _ {2}\right), \dots , s g n \left(s _ {m}\right) \right] ^ {T} \tag {2.19}k (s) = \left[ k \left(s _ {1}\right), k \left(s _ {2}\right), \dots , k \left(s _ {m}\right) \right] ^ {T} \tag {2.20}s _ {i}. k _ {i} (s) > 0, k _ {i} (0) = 0 \tag {2.21}$$
