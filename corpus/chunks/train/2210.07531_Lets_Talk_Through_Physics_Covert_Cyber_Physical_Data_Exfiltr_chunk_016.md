Definition III.1 (Stealthiness). We define stealthiness as the attacker’s ability to deviate the CPS’s state such that any threshold levels of the system are not crossed as a result of the attack, the attacked state(s) do not strongly correlate with non-attacked state variables, and the attack is conducted on a state which does not utilize a ’colored-noise’ state estimator, i.e., if the measurement noise is correlated, then computing an ensemble average for the auto-correlation of the measurement noise (empirically) would differ from the known correlation signal.

This basic structure provides an outline for our domainspecific design of both an attack and defense strategy for cyberphysical data exfiltration.
