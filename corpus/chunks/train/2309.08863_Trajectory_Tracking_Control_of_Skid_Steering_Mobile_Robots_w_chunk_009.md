In Figure 1, ICR is the instantaneous centre of rotation and $\boldsymbol { d _ { i } } = \left[ d _ { i x } , d _ { i y } \right] ^ { T }$ and $\boldsymbol { d _ { C } } = \left[ d _ { c x } , d _ { c y } \right] ^ { T }$ are the radius vectors and therefore:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} d _ {1 x} \\ d _ {2 x} \\ d _ {1 y} \\ d _ {3 y} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{l} d _ {4 x} \\ d _ {3 x} \\ d _ {2 y} \\ d _ {4 y} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{l} d _ {c x} - a \\ d _ {c x} + b \\ d _ {c y} + c \\ d _ {c y} - c \end{array} \right] \tag {3}
$$

and

$$\omega = - \frac {v _ {i x}}{d _ {i y}} = - \frac {v _ {x}}{d _ {c y}} = \frac {v _ {i y}}{d _ {i x}} = \frac {v _ {y}}{d _ {c x}} \tag {4}| \omega | = \frac {\| v _ {i} \|}{\| d _ {i} \|} = \frac {\| v \|}{\| d _ {c} \|},$$

where $\| * \|$ denotes the Euclidian norm, and a, b and c are positive geometrical parameters of the robot. The rigidity constraint can be extracted from Eqs. (3) and (4) as follows:

$$v _ {L} = v _ {1 x} = v _ {2 x}, \quad v _ {R} = v _ {3 x} = v _ {4 x} \tag {5}v _ {F} = v _ {2 y} = v _ {3 y}, \qquad v _ {B} = v _ {1 y} = v _ {4 y}$$

In Eq. (5) $v _ { L }$ and $v _ { R }$ are the x-component of the left and right wheels’ linear velocity respectively, and $v _ { F }$ and $v _ { B }$ are the $y -$ component of the front and back wheels’ linear velocities respectively. Eq. (5) can be rewritten using Eq. (2).

$$v _ {L} = r \omega_ {1} (1 - s _ {1}) = r \omega_ {2} (1 - s _ {2}) \tag {6}v _ {R} = r \omega_ {3} (1 - s _ {3}) = r \omega_ {4} (1 - s _ {4})$$

The ICR can be defined in the robot’s local frame as follows:

$$I C R = \left[ x _ {0}, y _ {0} \right] ^ {T} = \left[ - d _ {c x}, - d _ {c y} \right] ^ {T} \tag {7}$$

Substituting Eq. (7) into (4) gives us:

$$\omega = \frac {v _ {x}}{y _ {0}} = - \frac {v _ {y}}{x _ {0}}. \tag {8}$$

The relationship between the wheels’ linear velocity and the linear and angular velocities of the robot at COM can be derived using Eqs. (3)- (8).

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} v _ {L} \\ v _ {R} \\ v _ {F} \\ v _ {B} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 1 & - c \\ 1 & c \\ 0 & - x _ {0} + b \\ 0 & - x _ {0} - a \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} v _ {x} \\ \omega \end{array} \right] \tag {9}
$$

The slip ratio $s _ { i }$ is defined for each specific wheel. Assuming all wheels slip independently and the robot moves with the linear $v _ { x }$ and angular ? velocities, then the longitudinal vehicle slip ratio $s _ { v }$ can be defined at any arbitrary point, e.g., COM, at the vehicle-level as follows:

$$s _ {v} = \frac {v _ {I x} - v _ {x}}{v _ {I x}}, \tag {10}$$
