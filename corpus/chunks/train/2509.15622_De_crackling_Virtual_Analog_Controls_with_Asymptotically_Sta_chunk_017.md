# 5.1. Model loss comparison

Table 1 shows the means and 95 % confidence intervals of the best evaluation losses over 10 different training runs for all the different device and model combinations. The means and confidence intervals were calculated using linear values and then converted to decibel scale. On the RAT and DFZ datasets, the stable models have a higher average loss, indicating lower modeling performance. However, for the CS-3, the loss values are similar across models, and for the MARSHALL, the stable models outperform the unconstrained models. Based on these results, the stabilized models do not have a significant negative effect on modeling performance.
