# B. Robust quaternion-based sliding mode attitude control

In the previous section, the backstepping approach is used to tackle the underactuated problem of the UAV. The QABC controller in (24) generates desired orientation $Q _ { d }$ for attitude control. The proposed control system becomes complete once we design attitude control. The main objective of the following section is to design sliding mode control to ensure that the orientation and angular velocity are followed accurately. The attitude control steps are as follows:

Step 5: Formulate quaternion-based attitude errors [23], [25]:

$$\tilde {q _ {0}} = q _ {0} q _ {0 d} + \boldsymbol {q} _ {d} \boldsymbol {q} ^ {\top}\tilde {q} = q _ {0 d} \boldsymbol {q} - q _ {0} \boldsymbol {q} _ {d} + [ \boldsymbol {q} ] _ {\times} \boldsymbol {q} _ {d}, \quad \tilde {q} = [ \tilde {q} _ {1}, \tilde {q} _ {2}, \tilde {q} _ {3} ] ^ {\top} \tag {25}\tilde {R} _ {Q} = R _ {Q} R _ {d} ^ {\top}$$

$R _ { Q }$ and $\tilde { \omega }$ are respectively quaternion rotation matrix and angular velocity error, defined by [1]:

$$R _ {Q} = \left(q _ {0} ^ {2} - \boldsymbol {q} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {q}\right) I _ {3} + 2 \boldsymbol {q} \boldsymbol {q} ^ {\top} + 2 q _ {0} [ \mathbf {q} ] _ {\times} \tag {26}\tilde {\omega} = \omega - \tilde {R} _ {Q} \omega_ {d} \tag {27}$$

$\omega _ { d }$ is obtained through the following expression [23]:

$$\dot {\boldsymbol {Q}} _ {d} = (q _ {0 d} \mathbf {I} _ {3} + [ \boldsymbol {q} _ {d} ] _ {\times}) \omega_ {d} \tag {28}$$

Using (25), (26), (27), one can find the attitude error dynamics as [7], [25]:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \dot {\tilde {q}} _ {0} & = \frac {1}{2} \tilde {\boldsymbol {q}} ^ {\top} \tilde {\omega} \\ \dot {\tilde {\boldsymbol {q}}} & = \frac {1}{2} \left(\tilde {q} _ {0} \tilde {\omega} + [ \tilde {\boldsymbol {q}} ] _ {\times} \tilde {\omega}\right) \\ J _ {m} \dot {\tilde {\omega}} & = \left[ J _ {m} \omega \right] _ {\times} \omega + \mathcal {T} + \\ & J _ {m} \left[ \tilde {\omega} \right] _ {\times} \tilde {R} _ {Q} \omega_ {d} - J _ {m} \tilde {R} _ {Q} \dot {\omega} _ {d} \end{array} \right. \tag {29}
$$

Step 6: Let us introduce the sliding surface for QASMC control design (29)

$$s = \gamma_ {1} \tilde {\boldsymbol {q}} + \tilde {\omega} \tag {30}$$

where $\gamma _ { 1 }$ is a positive constant. Then by computing the derivative of (30), the following equation is obtained as:

$$\dot {s} = \gamma_ {1} \dot {\tilde {\boldsymbol {q}}} + \dot {\tilde {\omega}} \tag {31}$$
