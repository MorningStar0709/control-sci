# 4.3.2 Stability Conditions

It was established in Chapter 3 that internal stability depends solely on the A matrix of the realization—more specifically, on the eigenvalues of A. The B and C matrices enter into the transfer function of the system; they may play a role in input-output stability, but not in internal stability. Internal stability is unaffected by the addition of new external inputs or the deletion of existing ones, because such changes modify only the B matrix, through the addition or deletion of columns. Similarly, outputs can be added or deleted with no effect on internal stability, because that affects only the C matrix. We shall add inputs and outputs, and view this as injecting test inputs into the system and taking extra measurements, neither of which would be expected to change the stability properties of the system.

The “test” inputs and outputs are chosen so that the resulting modified system is controllable and observable. As we saw in Chapter 3, such a system has no hidden modes, and internal stability is then guaranteed by input–output (i.e., transfer function) stability.
