# B. Unstable Batch Reactor with Process Noise

We now consider the model of an unstable batch reactor derived from [23, §2.6], which we write in the form (3) by letting $m = p = n = 2$ and:

$$
\begin{array}{l} A _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 0. 9 7 & - 4 8. 6 3 \\ 2. 6 4 3 & 5. 8 6 7 \end{array} \right], A _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 5. 2 9 7 & - 1 0. 4 7 \\ - 0. 2 7 6 4 & 6. 3 7 1 \end{array} \right] \tag {57} \\ B _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 5 9. 4 4 & - 1 2. 6 3 \\ 1 2. 5 9 & 0. 8 6 9 6 \end{array} \right], B _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & - 3. 1 4 6 \\ 5. 6 7 9 & 0 \end{array} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

We assume that the process noise $w \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 }$ affects the system via $E _ { 0 } = I _ { 2 } , E _ { 1 } = 0 _ { 2 }$ , while we let $v = 0$ .

In this example, we run Algorithm 1 several times to highlight the effect of the noise energy on the feasibility of the LMI (S). Each run considers a dataset over [0, 3] s where $x ( 0 ) = 0 , u ( \cdot )$ is a sum of sinusoids (the same in each run), and $w ( \cdot )$ is randomly generated. In particular, given five values of $\delta _ { w } ,$ , we generate 200 signals uniformly extracted from the ball of radius $\sqrt { \delta _ { w } }$ in $\mathcal { L } _ { 2 } [ 0 , 3 ]$ , approximated via a Fourier orthonormal basis comprising the constant signal and sine/cosine functions with frequencies $2 j \pi / 3 , j \in$ $\{ 1 , \ldots , 1 0 0 \}$ .
