According to (4), the decoupling control of active and reactive power for the WT-GSC is realized through the gridvoltage-oriented vector control technique. Hence, the $d -$ and q-axes currents can be referred to as the active and reactive currents, respectively.

To summarize, the control equations for the voltage outerloop and current inner-loop of the WT-GSC are expressed as

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\{ \begin{array}{l} i _ {d \text {ref}} = K _ {p D C} \left(V _ {D C \text {ref}} - V _ {D C}\right) + K _ {i D C} \int \left(V _ {D C \text {ref}} - V _ {D C}\right) \mathrm{d} t \\ i _ {q \text {ref}} = K _ {p Q} \left(Q _ {\text {gref}} - Q _ {g}\right) + K _ {i Q} \int \left(Q _ {\text {gref}} - Q _ {g}\right) \mathrm{d} t \end{array} \right. (5) \\ \left\{ \begin{array}{l} v _ {d \text {ref}} = K _ {p d} (i _ {d \text {ref}} - i _ {d}) + K _ {i d} \int (i _ {d \text {ref}} - i _ {d})   \mathrm{d} t + \omega L _ {g} i _ {q} + v _ {g d} \\ v _ {q \text {ref}} = K _ {p q} (i _ {q \text {ref}} - i _ {q}) + K _ {i q} \int (i _ {q \text {ref}} - i _ {q})   \mathrm{d} t - \omega L _ {g} i _ {d} + v _ {g q} \end{array} \right. (6) \\ \end{array}
$$

where $i _ { d \mathrm { r e f } }$ and $i _ { q \mathrm { r e f } }$ are the d- and q-axes current reference values from the voltage loop, respectively; $v _ { d \mathrm { r e f } }$ and $v _ { q \mathrm { r e f } }$ are the $d -$ and q-axes voltage reference values from the current loop, respectively; $V _ { D C }$ and $V _ { D C \mathrm { r e f } }$ are the actual and reference values of the DC-side voltage, respectively; $Q _ { g }$ and $Q _ { g \mathrm { r e f } }$ are the actual and reference values of the reactive power output, respectively; $K _ { p D C }$ and $K _ { i D C }$ are the PI parameters for fixed DC-side voltage control; $K _ { p Q }$ and $K _ { i Q }$ are the PI parameters for fixed reactive power control; $K _ { p d } , K _ { i d } , K _ { p q } ,$ and $K _ { i q }$ are the PI parameters for current inner-loop control.
