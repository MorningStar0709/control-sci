# Exercise 1.36: Normal distribution

Given a normal distribution with scalar parameters m and $\sigma$ ,

$$p _ {\xi} (x) = \sqrt {\frac {1}{2 \pi \sigma^ {2}}} \exp \left[ - \frac {1}{2} \left(\frac {x - m}{\sigma}\right) ^ {2} \right] \tag {1.62}$$

By direct calculation, show that

(a)

$$\mathcal {E} (\xi) = m\operatorname{var} (\xi) = \sigma^ {2}$$

(b) Show that the mean and the maximum likelihood are equal for the normal distribution. Draw a sketch of this result. The maximum likelihood estimate, xˆ, is defined as

$$\hat {x} := \arg \max _ {x} p _ {\xi} (x)$$

in which arg returns the solution to the optimization problem.
