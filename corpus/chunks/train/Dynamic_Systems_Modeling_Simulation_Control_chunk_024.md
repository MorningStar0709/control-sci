4. Chapter 11 presents five engineering case studies in dynamic systems and control. The five case studies, inspired by research articles, are (1) vibration isolation in a vehicle suspension system, (2) an electromechanical (solenoid) actuator, (3) a pneumatic air-brake system, (4) hydraulic servomechanism control, and (5) feedback control of a magnetic levitation system. These cases illustrate topics from the previous chapters: modeling, simulation, linearization, analytical methods, and control. Instructors may wish to introduce the various applications contained in Chapter 11 early in the semester as certain topics are covered and then revisit the cases studies as the course progresses. Again, I cannot overstate the importance of presenting analysis of real, complex engineering systems to undergraduate students in order to motivate them, engage their interest, and illustrate key concepts in dynamic systems and control.
