As in [6, 22, 23] the diffusion coefficient in (1.7) is uncontrolled. The existing literature on pathwise stochastic control does indeed mostly focus on the case where the control process appears in the drift, but not in the diffusion term. A notable exception is the work of Allan and Cohen [2]. They studied the degeneracy phenomenon induced by directly controlling the diffusion coefficient in great detail, and introduced a method to resolve the degeneracy whilst retaining dynamic programming, albeit at the cost of altering the primal problem in a significant manner.

We consider a class of rough stochastic control problems where, for any admissible control $u \in { \mathcal { A } }$ the state dynamics follows of rough stochastic differential equations (rSDEs) of the form

$$
\begin{array}{l} d X _ {s} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u} = b \left(s, X _ {s} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u}, u _ {s}\right) d s + \sigma \left(s, X _ {s} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u}, u _ {s}\right) d W _ {s} + \hat {\sigma} \left(s, X _ {s} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u}\right) d \boldsymbol {\eta} _ {s}, \quad s \in [ t, T ] \\ \mathbf {X} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u} = \mathbf {X} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u} \end{array} \tag {1.8}
X _ {t} ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}, u} = x$$
