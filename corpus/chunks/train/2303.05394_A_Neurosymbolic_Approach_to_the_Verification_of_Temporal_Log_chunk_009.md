Given the predicates $( i , k )$ the Algorithm 1 constructs the next segment of STL2NN. Line 5 returns the node corresponding to $\mu _ { i } ( \mathbf { s } _ { k } )$ , i.e. the node labeled (i, k) in the second layer of the network. Then the network structure follows the structure of the STL formula. For example, in Line 7, we obtain the nodes corresponding to $\varphi _ { 1 }$ and $\varphi _ { 2 }$ at time $k ,$ and these nodes are then input to the ReLU unit that outputs the min of these two nodes (as defined in Lemma (1)). The interesting case is for temporal operators (Lines 15,12). A temporal operator represents the min or max or combination thereof of subformulas over different time instants. Suppose the scope of the temporal operator requires performing a min over \` different time instants, then in the function MinMaxNode, we arrange these \` inputs in a balanced binary tree of depth at most O(log \`) and repeatedely use the ReLU unit defined in Lemma 1 (see Appendix A.1). Executing Algorithm 1, will lead to a directed acyclic graph, DAG network with depth at most $O ( \log K | \varphi | )$ (as there are at most |ϕ| operators in $\varphi )$ and each operator can require a network of depth at most $O ( \log K )$ .
