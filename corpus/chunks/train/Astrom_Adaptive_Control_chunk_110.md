# EXAMPLE 2.6 Sinusoid

Let $u(t) = \sin \omega t$ . It follows that

$$\left(q ^ {2} - 2 q \cos \omega + 1\right) u (t) = 0$$

A sinusoid can thus at most be PE of order 2. Since

$$
C _ {2} = \frac {1}{2} \left( \begin{array}{c c} 1 & \cos \omega \\ \cos \omega & 1 \end{array} \right)
$$

it follows that a sinusoid is actually PE of order 2.
