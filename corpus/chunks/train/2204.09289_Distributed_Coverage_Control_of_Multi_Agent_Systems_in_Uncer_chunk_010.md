According to Fourier theorem, the heat flow vector at any point in the system is directly proportional to the temperature gradient at that point, but in the opposite direction, always along the direction of temperature reduction. The motion of the agent can be compared with the flow of heat flow, but the motion direction is opposite to the heat flow vector, which makes the agent always move in the direction of heat source, that is, in the direction towards more workload.
