# II. SYSTEM CONCEPTS

By the term system, we mean a whole that serves a given purpose and has a real or virtual skin that separates the whole from its environment. In the skin of a system, there are input and output interfaces that provide the connections between a system and its environment — in the case of the CPS, the sensors and actuators establish the links between the CS and the PS. A sensor generates an observation by observing some part of the state of its environment, the PS, at a given interaction-instant. An actuator forces the result of a computation executed in the CS on its environment (the PS) at an assumed interaction-instant. There are two very different models of time in the PS and the CS.
