# A. UAV Quaternion-based Translational Dynamic

The objectives of translation motion are to track desired position $P _ { d } = [ p _ { d 1 } , p _ { d 2 } , p _ { d 3 } ] \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ and linear velocity $V _ { d } =$ $[ v _ { d 1 } , v _ { d 2 } , v _ { d 3 } ] \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ . The translation dynamics of UAV is given by: [1], [7]:

$$
\text { Translation }: \quad \left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {P} \\ \dot {V} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} V \\ g e _ {z} + m ^ {- 1} R _ {Q} ^ {\top} T \end{array} \right]. \tag {1}
$$

where $P = [ p _ { x } , p _ { y } , p _ { z } ] \ \in \ \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ and $V = [ v _ { 1 } , v _ { 2 } , v _ { 3 } ] \ \in \ \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ with $P , V \in \{ \mathcal { T } \}$ are position and linear velocity of UAV, respectively. m and g denotes mass and gravity acceleration, respectively. $T = [ 0 , \overset { \smile } { 0 } , - \Im ] ^ { \intercal } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ is the total control input, ℑ denotes the total thrust, and $e _ { z } = [ 0 , 0 , 1 ] ^ { \top }$ is a standard basis vector of inertial frame. $R _ { Q }$ is the quaternion rotation matrix as follows [1], [7]:

$$R _ {Q} = \left(q _ {0} ^ {2} - \boldsymbol {q} ^ {\top} \boldsymbol {q}\right) I _ {3} + 2 \boldsymbol {q} \boldsymbol {q} ^ {\top} + 2 q _ {0} [ \mathbf {q} ] _ {\times} \tag {2}$$

where $q _ { 0 }$ and $\pmb { q } = [ q _ { 1 } , q _ { 2 } , q _ { 3 } ] ^ { \top }$ are unit-quaternions and $[ \pmb q ] _ { \times }$ is Skew-symmetric matrix. By substituting (2) in (1), the vehicle translation expanded state space model is obtained as [1], [16]:

$$
\text { Translation: } \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \dot {p} _ {x} & = v _ {1} \\ \dot {v} _ {1} & = - 2 m ^ {- 1} \Im (q _ {0} q _ {2} + q _ {1} q _ {3}) \\ \dot {p} _ {y} & = v _ {2} \\ \dot {v} _ {2} & = - 2 m ^ {- 1} \Im (q _ {2} q _ {3} - q _ {0} q _ {1}) \\ \dot {p} _ {z} & = v _ {3} \\ \dot {v} _ {3} & = m ^ {- 1} \Im (q _ {1} ^ {2} + q _ {2} ^ {2} - q _ {0} ^ {2} - q _ {3} ^ {2}) + g \end{array} \right. \tag {3}
$$

Assumption 1. The quadrotor is a rigid symmetric body, and the desired position trajectory $P _ { d }$ is bounded and twice differentiable.

To this point, the translation state space is defined by (3) and $T = [ \bar { 0 } , 0 , - \Im ] ^ { \top } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ as a total control input should be designed to track desired position and guarantee the stability of the translation dynamics by generating total thrust ℑ and desired values for rotational dynamics.
