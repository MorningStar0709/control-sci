# D.4 Proof of existence of global minimizer in Lemma 2

The domain S is non-empty whenever $( A , H )$ is observable. Thus, by continuity of $L \to J ( L )$ , there exists some finite $\alpha > 0$ such that the sublevel set $S _ { \alpha }$ is non-empty and compact. Therefore, the minimizer is an interior point and thus must satisfy the first-order optimality condition $\nabla J ( L ^ { * } ) = 0$ . Therefore, by coercivity, it is stabilizing and unique which satisfies

$$L ^ {*} = A X ^ {*} H ^ {\intercal} \left(R + H X ^ {*} H ^ {\intercal}\right) ^ {- 1},$$

with $X ^ { * }$ being the unique solution of

$$X ^ {*} = A _ {L ^ {*}} X ^ {*} A _ {L ^ {*}} ^ {\intercal} + Q + L ^ {*} R (L ^ {*}) ^ {\intercal}. \tag {18}$$

As expected, the global minimizer $L ^ { * }$ is equal to the steady-state Kalman gain, but explicitly dependent on the noise covariances $Q$ and $R .$ .
