# Multiplication f(t) by ${ \pmb { e } } ^ { - a t }$ in the time domain

$$\mathscr {L} \{e ^ {- a t} f (t) \} = F (s + a) \tag {8.9}$$

Therefore, taking the Laplace transform of $e ^ { - a t } f ( t )$ is equal to the Laplace transform of $f ( t )$ with complex variable s replaced by $s + a$ . For example, note that the Laplace transforms of $e ^ { - a t }$ sin ??t and $e ^ { - a t }$ cos ??t in Table 8.1 (numbers 10 and 11) are simply the Laplace transforms of sin ??t and cos ??t with s replaced by $s + a$ .
