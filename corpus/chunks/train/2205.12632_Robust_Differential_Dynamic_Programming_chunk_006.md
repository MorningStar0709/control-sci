Now, we show how the maximization over the constraint set $\Delta _ { t } \in \overline { { \Delta } }$ is carried out. To this end, we make use of the celebrated Sprocedure to recast (4) as an LMI optimization problem.

To employ the S-procedure, we assume that the signals $\delta _ { t } ^ { x } , \delta _ { t } ^ { w }$ and $\delta _ { t } ^ { u }$ related through

$$w _ {t} + \delta_ {t} ^ {w} = \Delta_ {t} (g (x _ {t} + \delta_ {t} ^ {x}, u _ {t} + \delta_ {t} ^ {u}, w _ {t} + \delta_ {t} ^ {w})) \quad \Delta_ {t} (\cdot) \in \overline {{\Delta}} \tag {6}$$

satisfy the quadratic inequality

$$
\left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {x} \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {u} \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {w} \end{array} \right) ^ {\top} \left( \begin{array}{c c c c} m _ {t} ^ {1 1} & m _ {t} ^ {1 2} & m _ {t} ^ {1 3} & m _ {t} ^ {1 4} \\ m _ {t} ^ {2 1} & M _ {t} ^ {2 2} & M _ {t} ^ {2 3} & M _ {t} ^ {2 4} \\ m _ {t} ^ {3 1} & M _ {t} ^ {3 2} & M _ {t} ^ {3 3} & M _ {t} ^ {3 4} \\ m _ {t} ^ {4 1} & M _ {t} ^ {4 2} & M _ {t} ^ {4 3} & M _ {t} ^ {4 4} \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {x} \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {u} \\ \delta_ {t} ^ {w} \end{array} \right) \geq 0 \tag {7}
$$

for a family of matrices $M _ { t } \in \mathcal { M }$ . Stated otherwise, we assume $( 7 )$ is a relaxation of (6). For the remainder of this work, we assume that M is of the generic conic form

$$\mathcal {M} = \left\{\sum_ {i = 1} ^ {s} \lambda_ {i} \boldsymbol {M} ^ {(i)} \mid \lambda_ {i} \in \mathbb {R} _ {\geq 0} \right\} \tag {8}$$

with given symmetric $M ^ { ( i ) } , ~ i ~ = ~ 1 , \dots , s$ and $s \in \mathbb N$ Many of the multipliers applied in robust control admit the form (8). We call the matrices $M ^ { ( 1 ) } , \ldots , M ^ { ( s ) }$ th e generators of $\mathcal { M } .$ . Clearly, with this definition, (7) can equivalently be demanded only on the set of generators of M, such that (7) boils down to a finite number of quadratic constraints.
