# Taking into Account Measurable Disturbances

In a number of applications measurable disturbances act upon the output of the process through a certain transfer operator. The knowledge of this transfer would be useful for compensating the effect of the measurable disturbances. In this case, the plant output is described by:

$$A (q ^ {- 1}) y (t + d + 1) = B ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + C ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) v (t) \tag {7.130}$$

where $v ( t )$ is the measurable disturbance and $C ^ { * } ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ is a polynomial of order $n _ { C }$ .

$$C ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) = c _ {1} + c _ {2} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + c _ {n _ {c}} q ^ {- n _ {c} + 1} \tag {7.131}$$

Using the polynomial equation (7.118) the $d + 1$ step ahead filtered prediction of $y ( t )$ is:

$$
\begin{array}{l} P (q ^ {- 1}) y (t + d + 1) = F (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) + B ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) E (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + C ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) E (q ^ {- 1}) v (t) \\ = R (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) + S (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + W (q ^ {- 1}) v (t) \tag {7.132} \\ \end{array}
$$

where:

$$R \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) = F \left(q ^ {- 1}\right); \quad S \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) = B ^ {*} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) E \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) \tag {7.133}W (q ^ {- 1}) = C ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) E (q ^ {- 1})$$

Forcing the prediction to equal $P ( q ^ { - 1 } ) y ^ { * } ( t + d + 1 )$ yields the controller.

$$P \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) = R \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) y (t) + S \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) u (t) + W \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) v (t) = \theta_ {c} ^ {T} \phi_ {c} (t) \tag {7.134}$$

Here in this case:

$$\theta_ {c} ^ {T} = \left[ s _ {0}, \dots , s _ {n _ {S}}, r _ {0}, \dots , r _ {n _ {R}}, w _ {0}, \dots , w _ {n _ {W}} \right] \tag {7.135}\phi_ {c} ^ {T} (t) = [ u (t), \dots , u (t - n _ {S}), y (t), \dots , y (t - n _ {R}), v (t), \dots , v (t - n _ {W}) ] \tag {7.136}$$

which will assure (7.114) even in the presence of measurable disturbances.
