# Example 6.11 Ship steering

It can be shown that the relative increase in resistance due to deviations from a straight-line course can be approximately expressed as

$$\frac {\Delta R}{R} = \frac {k}{T} \int_ {0} ^ {T} \left(\phi^ {2} (t) + \rho \delta^ {2} (t)\right) d t$$

where $\phi$ is the heading deviation, $\delta$ is the rudder angle, R is the resistance, and $\rho$ is a parameter. Typical parameter values for a tanker are k = 0.014 and $\rho = 0.1$ .
