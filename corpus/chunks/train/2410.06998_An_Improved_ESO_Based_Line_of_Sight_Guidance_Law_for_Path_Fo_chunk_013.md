# 3.2. Guidance law design

The IELOS guidance law presented in this paper incorporates a nonlinear tracking differentiator and anti-saturation within the ESO-based LOS guidance law. The diagram of the IELOS guidance law and the photograph of the AUH are depicted in Figure2. Subsequently, the stability analysis will be presented. To start with, the fundamental guidance law is proposed as

$$\psi_ {d} = \alpha_ {k} (w) + \arctan (\frac {- y _ {e}}{\Delta} - \beta_ {s a t}) \tag {13}$$

Define

$$u _ {p} = U \cos (\psi_ {d} - \alpha_ {k}) + \kappa x _ {e} \tag {14}$$

Combing (4), ??̇ can be deprived as

$$\dot {w} = \frac {U \cos (\psi_ {d} - \alpha_ {k}) + \kappa x _ {e}}{\sqrt {x _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w) + y _ {k} ^ {\prime 2} (w)}} \tag {15}$$

Substituting (14) into the first equality of (7) gives

$$\dot {x} _ {e} = - \kappa x _ {e} - U \cos (\psi - \alpha_ {k}) \beta + \dot {\alpha} _ {k} y _ {e} \tag {16}$$

Known triangle inequality

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \sin \left(\tan^ {- 1} \left(- \frac {y _ {e}}{\Delta} - \beta_ {s a t}\right)\right) = - \frac {y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + \left(y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}\right) ^ {2}}} \\ \cos \left(\tan^ {- 1} \left(- \frac {y _ {e}}{\Delta} - \beta_ {s a t}\right)\right) = \frac {\Delta}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + \left(y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}\right) ^ {2}}} \end{array} \right. \tag {17}
$$

The time derivative of $y _ { e }$ is deprived to

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {y} _ {e} = - U \frac {(y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t})}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + (y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}) ^ {2}}} + U \frac {\Delta \beta_ {s a t}}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + (y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}) ^ {2}}} - \dot {\alpha} _ {k} x _ {e} \\ = - \frac {U y _ {e}}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + \left(y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}\right) ^ {2}}} - \frac {U \Delta \left(\beta_ {s a t} - \beta\right)}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + \left(y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}\right) ^ {2}}} - \dot {\alpha} _ {k} x _ {e} \tag {18} \\ \end{array}
$$

Finally, the error dynamics in terms of $x _ { e }$ and $y _ { e }$ become

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {e} = - \kappa x _ {e} - U \cos (\psi - \alpha_ {k}) \beta + \dot {\alpha} _ {k} y _ {e} \\ \dot {y} _ {e} = - \frac {U y _ {e}}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + (y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}) ^ {2}}} - \frac {U \Delta (\beta_ {s a t} - \beta)}{\sqrt {\Delta^ {2} + (y _ {e} + \Delta \beta_ {s a t}) ^ {2}}} - \dot {\alpha} _ {k} x _ {e} \end{array} \right. \tag {19}
$$
