$$u _ {k} ^ {H, r} (x _ {k} ^ {H, r}, \hat {\theta} ^ {r}, \hat {\Theta} ^ {r}) = \underset {u, c} {\arg \max} \sum_ {i, j \in \mathcal {V}} u _ {i, j} \tag {11a}s. t. \quad b _ {2} (u, c, \hat {\theta} ^ {r}, \hat {\Theta} ^ {r}) \leq 0 \tag {11b}$$

which maximizes how many nodes the agent visits while satisfying capacity constraints before returning to the depot to re-charge. The variable $c \in \mathbb { R } ^ { | V | \times | V | \times L }$ tracks the capacity states along the routes, with $c _ { i , j , l }$ l tracking the predicted value of the agent’s lth capacity state as the agent departs node $v _ { i }$ towards node $v _ { j }$ . The full constraints (11b) are listed in the Appendix. (11) primarily differs from (8) in its abstraction of the agent model (10) and by enforcing capacity constraints robustly with respect to the estimated bound set $\theta \in { \hat { \Theta } } ^ { r }$ , since the true value of θ is not known.

We propose approximating (11) with a data-driven MPC policy

$$\pi^ {H, r} (x _ {k} ^ {H, r}, \hat {\theta} ^ {r}, \hat {\Theta} ^ {r}) : \mathcal {S} ^ {H, r} \to \{0, 1 \} ^ {| V | \times | V |} \tag {12}$$

which, at iteration r, maps a high-level state xH,k $x _ { k } ^ { H , r } \in \mathcal { S } ^ { H , r }$ to a valid task assignment. The closed-loop high-level system under such a policy is

$$u _ {k} ^ {r} = \pi^ {H, r} (x _ {k} ^ {H, r}, \hat {\theta} ^ {r}, \hat {\Theta} ^ {r}) \tag {13a}n _ {k + 1} ^ {r, c l} = \sum_ {j \in \mathcal {V}} u _ {k, n _ {k} ^ {r}, j} ^ {r} j \tag {13b}c _ {l, k + 1} ^ {r, c l} = c _ {l, k} ^ {r} + \sum_ {j \in \mathcal {V}} u _ {k, n _ {k} ^ {r}, j} ^ {r} \theta_ {n _ {k} ^ {r}, j, l}, l \in [ 1, L ]. \tag {13c}$$
