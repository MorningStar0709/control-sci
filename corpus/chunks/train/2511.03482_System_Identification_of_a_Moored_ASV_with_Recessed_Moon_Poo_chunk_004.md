Data-driven machine learning techniques gained popularity due to their ability to model complex inputoutput relations in an automated manner directly from data, not requiring the effort of gaining prior knowledge on the system to develop a full model. In particular, recurrent neural networks (RNN) [10] and long short-term memory networks (LSTM) [11], along with their bidirectional LSTM (BiLSTM) [12, 13] variant, have been demonstrated to be effective for building equation-free data-driven models for ship motions, and provide multi-step ahead forecasting of the ship’s degree-of-freedom in several sailing conditions, including calm water and waves [14]. The strength of machine and deep learning methods lies in their ability to capture relevant hidden and nonlinear dynamics and input-output relations directly from available data, their compactness, and fast evaluation. However, deep learning models typically require large datasets of high-fidelity data for training (more complex architectures usually require more expensive training) and to generalize effectively.
