$$\partial \mathbf {V} / \partial b = [ (\partial V _ {r} / \partial b) - V _ {\theta} (\partial \theta / \partial b) ] \hat {\mathbf {r}} + [ (\partial V _ {\theta} / \partial b) + V _ {r} (\partial \theta / \partial b) ] \hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}. \tag {6.124b}$$

This equation describes the change in the velocity vector V arising from perturbations in any of the four burnout variables V, γ , h, and $\theta _ { o } .$ , which, in turn, take the role of b in (6.124b). Now, letting $\mathbf { V } _ { P }$ be the perturbed velocity vector at $t = t _ { 1 }$ , and $\mathbf { V } _ { I }$ the unperturbed velocity vector, the following expression results:

$$\mathbf {V} _ {P} = \mathbf {V} _ {I} + \delta \mathbf {V}, \tag {6.125}$$

where

$$\delta \mathbf {V} = (\partial \mathbf {V} / \partial b) \delta b. \tag {6.126}$$

Thus, the error in velocity at the endpoint is

$$(\partial \mathbf {V} / \partial b) \delta = \mathbf {V} _ {P} - \mathbf {V} _ {I}. \tag {6.127}$$

From (6.124b), the radial component of δV, $\delta \mathrm { V } _ { \hat { \mathbf { r } } }$ is

$$\delta V _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \left[ \left(\partial V _ {r} / \partial b\right) - V _ {\theta} (\partial \theta / \partial b) \right] \delta b. \tag {6.128a}$$

and the traverse component is

$$\delta V _ {\hat {\theta}} = \left[ \left(\partial V _ {\theta} / \partial b\right) + V _ {r} (\partial \theta / \partial b) \right] \delta b. \tag {6.128b}$$
