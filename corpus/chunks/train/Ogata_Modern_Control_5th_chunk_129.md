The polytropic exponent n is unity for isothermal expansion. For adiabatic expansion, n is equal to the ratio of specific heats $c _ { p } / c _ { v }$ , where $c _ { p }$ is the specific heat at constant pressure and $c _ { v }$ is the specific heat at constant volume. In many practical cases, the value of n is approximately constant, and thus the capacitance may be considered constant.

The value of $d \rho / d p$ is obtained from Equations (4–10) and (4–11). From Equation (4–10) we have

$$d p = K n \rho^ {n - 1} d \rho$$

or

$$\frac {d \rho}{d p} = \frac {1}{K n \rho^ {n - 1}} = \frac {\rho^ {n}}{p n \rho^ {n - 1}} = \frac {\rho}{p n}$$

Substituting Equation (4–11) into this last equation, we get

$$\frac {d \rho}{d p} = \frac {1}{n R _ {\mathrm{gas}} T}$$

The capacitance C is then obtained as

$$C = \frac {V}{n R _ {\text { gas }} T} \tag {4-12}$$

The capacitance of a given vessel is constant if the temperature stays constant. (In many practical cases, the polytropic exponent n is approximately 1.0 \~ 1.2 for gases in uninsulated metal vessels.)
