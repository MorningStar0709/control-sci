$$\tilde {H} (t, \tilde {x}, \tilde {y}, \tilde {z}, u) := \tilde {b} _ {t} (\tilde {x}, u) ^ {\top} \tilde {y} + \operatorname{Tr} \left[ \tilde {\sigma} _ {t} (\tilde {x}, u) ^ {\top} \tilde {z} \right] + \tilde {h} _ {t} (\tilde {x}, u).$$

The standard SMP states that if $( u ^ { * } , \tilde { X } ^ { * } )$ is an optimal state/control process for the transformed, then

$$\partial_ {u} \tilde {H} (t, \tilde {X} _ {t} ^ {*}, \tilde {Y} _ {t} ^ {*}, \tilde {Z} _ {t} ^ {*}, u _ {t} ^ {*}) (v - u _ {t} ^ {*}) \geq 0,$$

where $( \tilde { Y } ^ { * } , \tilde { Z } ^ { * } )$ solves the BSDE

$$d \tilde {Y} _ {t} = - \partial_ {\tilde {x}} \tilde {H} (t, \tilde {X} ^ {*}, \tilde {Y} _ {t} ^ {*}, \tilde {Z} _ {t} ^ {*}, u _ {t} ^ {*}) d t + \tilde {Z} _ {t} ^ {*} d W _ {t}, \tilde {Y} _ {T} = \partial_ {\tilde {x}} \tilde {g} (\tilde {X} _ {T} ^ {*}). \tag {4.12}$$

Furthermore, under suitable convexity conditions on the Hamiltonian and the terminal payoff function, if $u ^ { * }$ is an admissible control with corresponding state dynamics ${ \tilde { X } } ^ { * }$ , such that

$$\tilde {H} (t, \tilde {X} ^ {*}, \tilde {Y} ^ {*}, \tilde {Z} ^ {*}, u ^ {*}) = \min _ {v \in V} \tilde {H} (t, \tilde {X} ^ {*}, \tilde {Y} ^ {*}, \tilde {Z} ^ {*}, v)$$

then the control $u ^ { * }$ is optimal. To obtain the corresponding SMP for rough stochastic control problems we fix a rough path η omitting it in the upper index, denote by $A ^ { - 1 }$ the solution to the stochastic RDE (4.8) and introduce the Hamiltonian

$$H (t, x, \tilde {y}, \tilde {z}, u) := \left(A _ {t} ^ {- 1} b _ {t} (x, u)\right) ^ {\top} \tilde {y} + \operatorname{Tr} \left[ \left(A _ {t} ^ {- 1} \sigma_ {t} (x, u)\right) ^ {\top} \tilde {z} \right] + h _ {t} (x, u). \tag {4.13}$$

For any $u \in { \mathcal { A } } ,$ let $X ^ { u }$ be the unique solution to the rSDE (4.2) and let $( \tilde { Y } ^ { u } , \tilde { Z } ^ { u } )$ be the solution to the (classical) BSDE

$$d \tilde {Y} _ {t} = - \left(\partial_ {x} b _ {t} (X _ {t} ^ {u}, u _ {t}) ^ {\top} \tilde {Y} _ {t} + \partial_ {x} \sigma_ {t} (X _ {t} ^ {u}, u _ {t}) \tilde {Z} _ {t} + A _ {t} \partial_ {x} h (X _ {t} ^ {u}, u _ {t})\right) d t + \tilde {Z} _ {t} d W _ {t}, \tilde {Y} _ {T} = A _ {T} \partial_ {x} g (X _ {T} ^ {u}). \tag {4.14}$$
