$$L (F, \mu_ {i, k}) = \sum_ {i, k} \mu_ {i, k} (d _ {i, k} - \operatorname{Tr} (F \Phi_ {i, k})) +y ^ {\top} (W \partial \phi^ {\top} F ^ {\top} + F \partial \phi W - \dot {W} + 2 \lambda W - \rho G G ^ {\top}) y =\operatorname{Tr} \left\{F \left(2 \partial \phi W y y ^ {\top} - \sum_ {i, k} \mu_ {i, k} \Phi_ {i, k}\right) \right\} - \tag {20}\operatorname{Tr} \left\{\left(\dot {W} - 2 \lambda W + \rho G G ^ {\top}\right) y y ^ {\top} \right\} + \sum_ {i, k} \mu_ {i, k} d _ {i, k}.$$

Expanding $\dot { W }$ by leveraging (19c) and (1) yields:

$$\dot {W} = \sum_ {i} \frac {\partial W}{\partial x _ {i}} (\phi^ {\top} F ^ {\top}) e _ {i} = \sum_ {i} \frac {\partial W}{\partial x _ {i}} (e _ {i} ^ {\top} \otimes \phi^ {\top}) \mathrm{vec} (F ^ {\top}). \tag {21}$$

Using the former identity and the fact that $\mathrm { T r } ( A B ) ~ =$ $\operatorname { v e c } ( B ) ^ { \top } \operatorname { v e c } ( A ^ { \top } )$ , the Lagrangian becomes:

$$
\begin{array}{l} L (F, \mu_ {i, k}) = \operatorname{vec} \left(2 \partial \phi W y y ^ {\top} - \sum_ {i, k} \mu_ {i, k} \Phi_ {i, k}\right) ^ {\top} \operatorname{vec} \left(F ^ {\top}\right) \\ - \sum_ {i} \operatorname{Tr} \left(\frac {\partial W}{\partial x _ {i}} y y ^ {\top}\right) \left(e _ {i} ^ {\top} \otimes \phi^ {\top}\right) \operatorname{vec} \left(F ^ {\top}\right) \\ + \operatorname{Tr} ((2 \lambda W - \rho G G ^ {\top}) y y ^ {\top}) + \sum_ {i, k} \mu_ {i, k} d _ {i, k}. \tag {22} \\ \end{array}
$$

Notice that $L ( F , \mu _ { i , k } )$ is affine in F , therefore, the dual function:

$$g \left(\mu_ {i, k}\right) = \sup _ {F} L \left(F, \mu_ {i, k}\right) \tag {23}$$

is finite only if (19b) holds. Therefore:

$$
g \left(\mu_ {i, k}\right) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \operatorname{Tr} \left\{\left(2 \lambda W - \rho G G ^ {\top}\right) y y ^ {\top} \right\} + \sum_ {i, k} [ \mu d ] _ {i, k} \\ \text {   if   (19b)   holds   and   } \\ \infty \quad \text {   otherwise   } \end{array} \right. \tag {24}
$$
