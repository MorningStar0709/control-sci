# B. Closed-Loop Structure Interpretation

The proposed method in this work introduces a new regulation pathway where the control law is derived from the system’s dynamics rather than from the error surface. This leads to a naturally stable closed-loop structure represented as:

$$x \rightarrow \alpha (x) \rightarrow u = - \lambda \tanh (\alpha) \rightarrow \beta = \frac {u}{g (x)} \rightarrow s = \alpha - \beta \rightarrow x$$

This loop illustrates that the command is based on internal dynamics $\alpha ,$ and the control influence $\beta$ is implicitly injected through u. The sliding variable s emerges as a structural tool for analysis rather than an active component of the control law. This implicit feedback ensures convergence and robustness without the need for error-driven switching or observer design.

As a consequence, this framework avoids discontinuities, removes observer dependency, and yields a control law that is smooth and physically implementable.
