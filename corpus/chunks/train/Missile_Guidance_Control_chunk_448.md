For application in real time, (5.43a), (5.43b), and (5.43c) can be accurately approximated by the following equations:

$$\frac {d ^ {2} x}{d t ^ {2}} = - \rho (h) V _ {a} B _ {D} / 2 \left[ \left(\frac {d x}{d t}\right) - W _ {x} \right], \tag {5.44a}\frac {d ^ {2} y}{d t ^ {2}} = - \rho (h) V _ {a} B _ {D} / 2 \left[ \left(\frac {d y}{d t}\right) - W _ {y} \right], \tag {5.44b}\frac {d ^ {2} z}{d t ^ {2}} = - \rho (h) V _ {a} B _ {D} / 2 \left[ \left(\frac {d z}{d t}\right) - W _ {z} \right] - g _ {o}, \tag {5.44c}h \cong z + \{(x ^ {2} + y ^ {2}) / 2 (z + R _ {e}) \}. \tag {5.45}$$

Note that for a flat Earth approximation, $h = z$ . This is a satisfactory approximation when the origin is selected close to the target, for example, $x , y ~ \le ~ 1 0 0$ miles (160.93 km).
