$$\mathbb {E} \left[ \frac {(\theta h _ {\iota \wedge \tau_ {n}} X _ {\iota \wedge \tau_ {n}} ^ {\hat {u}} - \theta h _ {0} x - Y _ {0}) ^ {2}}{Y _ {\iota \wedge \tau_ {n}}} \right] = Y _ {0}.$$

Let $n \to \infty$ by Fatou’s lemma we have

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ \frac {(\theta h _ {\iota} X _ {\iota} ^ {\hat {u}} - \theta h _ {0} x - Y _ {0}) ^ {2}}{Y _ {\iota}} \right] \leqslant Y _ {0}.$$

Since Y, h are bounded, we get

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ (X _ {\iota} ^ {\hat {u}}) ^ {2} \right] < C \tag {3.22}$$

$$\Lambda_ {t} ^ {\hat {\eta}} = \frac {\theta h _ {0} x + Y _ {0} - \theta h _ {t} X _ {t} ^ {\hat {u}}}{Y _ {t}}.$$

Combined with (3.22) and the fact that Y, h are bounded, we immediately obtain for any $t \in [ 0 , T ]$ ,

$$\mathbb {E} [ (\Lambda_ {t} ^ {\hat {\eta}}) ^ {2} ] < \infty ,$$

which proves $\hat { \eta } \in { \mathcal A } .$
