$$E \left[ V _ {0} \right] = E _ {u} \left[ M _ {0} ^ {u ^ {*}} \right] = \inf _ {u \in \mathcal {U}} J (u).$$

Moreover, the value process $V _ { t } ( \omega )$ satisfies the following backward SDE (BSDE)

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{c} d V _ {t} = - H (t, \mathbf {X} _ {t}, \mathbf {Z} _ {t}) d t + \mathbf {Z} _ {t} d B _ {t}, 0 \leq t <   T \\ V _ {T} (\omega) = \xi (\mathbf {X} _ {T} (\omega)) \end{array} , \right. \tag {3.10}
$$

where the Hamiltanian

$$H (t, \mathbf {x}, \mathbf {z}) = \inf _ {u \in \mathcal {U}} f (t, \mathbf {x}, \mathbf {z}; u)$$

and

$$f (t, \mathbf {x}, \mathbf {z}; u) = c (\mathbf {x}, t, u) + \mathbf {z} \alpha (t, \mathbf {x}, u),\alpha (t, \mathbf {x}, u) = \sigma^ {- 1} (t, \mathbf {x}) \mu (t, \mathbf {x}, u).$$

From Pardoux-Peng theory [7] on the relation between quasi-linear parabolic equation and backward SDEs , we know that the value process as well as $Z _ { t } ( \omega )$ can be expressed in terms of a deterministic function $v ( t , x )$

$$V _ {t} (\omega) = v (t, \mathbf {X} _ {t} (\omega))Z _ {t} (\omega) = \nabla v (t, \mathbf {X} _ {t} (\omega)) \sigma (t, \mathbf {X} _ {t} (\omega))$$

where the value function $v ( t , { \bf x } )$ satisfies the following Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equation

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{c} 0 = \frac {\partial v}{\partial t} (t, \mathbf {x}) + \mathcal {L} v (t, \mathbf {x}) + H (t, \mathbf {x}, \nabla_ {x} v \sigma (t, \mathbf {x})), 0 \leq t <   T, \mathbf {x} \in R ^ {d} \\ v (T, \mathbf {x}) = \xi (\mathbf {x}) \end{array} . \right. \tag {3.11}
$$
