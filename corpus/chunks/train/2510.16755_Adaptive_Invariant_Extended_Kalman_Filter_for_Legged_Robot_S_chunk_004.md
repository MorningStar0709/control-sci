# A. $S E _ { 2 + N } ( 3 )$ Group Representation

Let G be a matrix Lie group and g its corresponding Lie algebra. One of the matrix Lie group, $S E _ { 2 + N } ( 3 )$ , represents rigid body motion in 3D space with position of N -landmarks:

$$
S E _ {2 + N} (3) = \left\{\chi = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c c} R & v & p & r _ {1} & \dots & r _ {N} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 1 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 1 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 1 & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 1 \end{array} \right), R \in S O (3), v, p, r _ {i} \in \mathbb {R} ^ {3} \right\} \tag {1}
$$

The $R , v ,$ and $p$ are a rigid body’s rotation, velocity, and position, respectively. The $r _ { 1 }$ to rN are each landmarks. The corresponding Lie algebra $\mathfrak { s e } _ { 2 + N } ( 3 )$ is defined as follow:

$$
\mathfrak {s c} _ {2 + N} (3) = \left\{\xi^ {\wedge} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c c} \omega^ {\times} & a & v & u _ {1} & \dots & u _ {N} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \end{array} \right), \xi = \left( \begin{array}{c} \xi_ {R} \\ \xi_ {v} \\ \xi_ {p} \\ \xi_ {r _ {1}} \\ \vdots \\ \xi_ {r _ {N}} \end{array} \right) \in \mathbb {R} ^ {9 + 3 N} \right\} \tag {2}
$$

The operator $( \cdot ) ^ { \wedge } : \mathbb { R } ^ { d i m { \mathfrak { g } } } \to { \mathfrak { g } }$ is used to map vector space elements to tangent space. The operator $( \cdot ) ^ { \times } : \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }  \mathfrak { s o } ( 3 )$ is one of the $( \cdot ) ^ { \wedge }$ operator in SO(3) groups. It is defined so that $[ x ] ^ { \times } y = x \times y$ . The ω and a are angular velocity and acceleration of the rigid body, respectively, and u is the velocity of landmarks. Lie groups and their corresponding Lie algebras are related through the exponential map:

$$\exp (\cdot): \xi^ {\wedge} \rightarrow \chi \tag {3}\operatorname{Exp} (\cdot): \xi \rightarrow \chi \tag {4}$$

The adjoint matrix at $\chi , A d _ { \chi } .$ describes how a Lie algebra elements transforms under the group action.

$$\left(A d _ {\chi} \xi\right) ^ {\wedge} = \chi \xi^ {\wedge} \chi^ {- 1} \tag {5}$$
