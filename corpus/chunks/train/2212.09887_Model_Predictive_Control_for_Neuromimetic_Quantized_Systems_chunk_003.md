# 2. MPC FOR QUANTIZED SYSTEMS

We know that the existence of a control Lyapunov function provides sufficient conditions for the existence of a controller that ensures asymptotical stability for a discretetime system. Therefore, establishing the MPC’s stability can be approached by finding a candidate-Lyapunov function as its cost function. In general, the cost function contains the terminal cost and stage cost with the form

$$J (x, U) = p \left(x _ {N}\right) + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {N - 1} q \left(x _ {i}, u _ {i}\right),$$

where we have a prediction horizon N and the predicted input sequence $U = \{ u _ { 0 } , \ldots , u _ { N - 1 } \}$ . The commonly used cost functions $p ( \cdot )$ and $q ( \cdot )$ are quadratic in the states and control inputs. Then, the QS-MPC approach can be formulated as solving the following optimization problem:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \min _ {u _ {0 | k}, \ldots , u _ {N - 1 | k}} J (x _ {q} (k), U (k)) = | x _ {N | k} - x _ {r e f} (N | k) | _ {P} ^ {2} \\ + \sum_ {n = 0} ^ {N - 1} | x _ {n | k} - x _ {r e f} (n | k) | _ {Q} ^ {2} + | u _ {n | k} | _ {R} ^ {2} \\ s. t. \quad u _ {n | k} \in \{- 1, 0, 1 \} ^ {m}, \\ x _ {q} (k) = x _ {0 | k}, \\ x _ {n + 1 | k} = A _ {q} x _ {n | k} + h B _ {q} u _ {n | k}, \\ x _ {r e f} (n + 1 | k) = e ^ {H h} x _ {r e f} (n | k), \\ \forall n = 0, 1,..., N - 1, \tag {4} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $P , Q , R$ are positive definite matrices, h is the sampling interval and the function $| x | _ { P } ^ { 2 } = x ^ { T } P x$ , with similar definitions for Q and R. The reference system $x _ { r e f }$ is the linear time-invariant system (2). Matrix $H = A { \stackrel { . } { + } }$ BK is the state matrix of $x _ { r e f }$ and could be thought of as specifying a target behavior of (2) to ensure the feedback law $u \ = \ K x + v$ asymptotically steers the closed loop system toward the goal point $x _ { g }$ . Following general MPC procedures, the optimal input at time k for the quantized system is $u ^ { * } ( k ) \bar { = } u _ { 0 \mid k } ^ { * }$ , which is the first element in the predicted optimal input sequence $U ^ { * } ( k )$ .
