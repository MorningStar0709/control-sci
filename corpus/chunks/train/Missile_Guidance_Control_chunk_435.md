# 5.7.3 Relationship Among REP, DEP, and CEP

When the REP and DEP are approximately equal, the bivariate normal distribution approaches circular normal, where $\sigma _ { R } = \sigma _ { D } = \sigma$ . Thus, since $C E P = 1 . 1 7 7 4 \sigma$ ,

$$C E P = 1. 1 7 7 4 \left(\sigma_ {R} + \sigma_ {D}\right) / 2, \tag {5.16}R E P = 0. 6 7 4 5 \sigma_ {R},D E P = 0. 6 7 4 5 \sigma_ {D}.$$

Therefore,

$$C E P = 0. 8 7 3 (R E P + D E P). \tag {5.17}$$

When REP and DEP are not equal, this relationship can also be used to approximate CEP even when REP and $D E P$ differ by a factor as much as two. Beyond this range, the approximation is increasingly poor, and the values given in Table 5.2 should be used.
