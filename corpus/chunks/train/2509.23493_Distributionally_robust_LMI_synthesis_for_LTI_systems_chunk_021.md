$$
\left( \begin{array}{c c c c c} - \mathcal {P} & 0 & 0 & \mathcal {A} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {C} ^ {\top} \\ 0 & - \lambda I & \lambda I & \mathcal {B} ^ {\top} & \mathcal {D} ^ {\top} \\ 0 & \lambda I & - Q - \lambda I & 0 & 0 \\ \mathcal {A} & \mathcal {B} & 0 & - \mathcal {P} ^ {- 1} & 0 \\ \mathcal {C} & \mathcal {D} & 0 & 0 & - I \end{array} \right) <   0.
$$

This matrix inequality is nonlinear in the certificate P. Thus, we multiply this matrix inequality from both sides with diag $( I , I , I , \mathcal { P } , I )$ to achieve our desired result.

Proposition 6.2 (Preliminary synthesis problem 2): Let A be stable. Then, the optimization problem from Theorem 5.6 is equivalent to

$$\underset {\lambda \geq 0, \mathcal {P} > 0, Q} {\text { minimize }} \quad \operatorname{tr} (Q \Sigma_ {\mathrm{nom}}) + \lambda \gamma^ {2} \tag {14a}
\text { s.t. } \left( \begin{array}{c c c} - \mathcal {P} & \mathcal {A} ^ {\top} \mathcal {P} & \mathcal {C} ^ {\top} \\ \mathcal {P A} & - \mathcal {P} & 0 \\ \mathcal {C} & 0 & - I \end{array} \right) \prec 0, \tag {14b}

\left( \begin{array}{c c c c} - \lambda I & \lambda I & \mathcal {B} ^ {\top} \mathcal {P} & \mathcal {D} ^ {\top} \\ \lambda I & - Q - \lambda I & 0 & 0 \\ \mathcal {P B} & 0 & - \mathcal {P} & 0 \\ \mathcal {D} & 0 & 0 & - I \end{array} \right) <   0. \tag {14c}
$$

Proof: The proof is analogous to the proof of Proposition 6.1.

Unfortunately, the usage of the Schur complement alone is not enough to convexify the synthesis problem. Proposition 6.1 and Proposition 6.2 give proof for that, as terms of the form $\mathcal { P A } , \mathcal { P B }$ appear. These terms become bilinear, when we add the controller parameters to the decision variables. Fortunately, there is yet another celebrated result from control theory that can be invoked to convexify the synthesis problem.
