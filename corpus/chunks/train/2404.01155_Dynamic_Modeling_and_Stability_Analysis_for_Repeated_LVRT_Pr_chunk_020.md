$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} (\boldsymbol {\xi}) = \dot {\boldsymbol {x}} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {\xi} + \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} \dot {\boldsymbol {x}} \\ = 2 \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma} \boldsymbol {x} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {\sigma} \boldsymbol {u} _ {\sigma}) \\ = 2 \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma} \boldsymbol {x} _ {e} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {\sigma} \boldsymbol {u} _ {\sigma}) + \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {T} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P} + \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma}) \boldsymbol {\xi} \\ = \min _ {i \in \mathbb {N} ^ {+}} 2 \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {i} \boldsymbol {x} _ {e} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {i} \boldsymbol {u} _ {i}) + \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} + \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {A} _ {\sigma}) \boldsymbol {\xi} \\ <   \min _ {\boldsymbol {\lambda} \in \boldsymbol {\Lambda}} 2 \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P} (\boldsymbol {A} _ {\boldsymbol {\lambda}} \boldsymbol {x} _ {e} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {\boldsymbol {\lambda}} \boldsymbol {u} _ {\boldsymbol {\lambda}}) - \mu \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {\xi} \\ = - \mu \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {\xi} <   0 \tag {20} \\ \end{array}
$$

which obtains $\dot { V } \left( \pmb { \xi } \right) < 0 .$ . Therefore, according to the Lyapunov stability criterion, the switched system is asymptotically stable about ${ \bf \delta x } _ { e } ,$ which completes the proof.

For a switched system with n subsystems, (19c) is equivalent to

$$\sigma = j, \quad \text { if } \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {b} _ {j} \leq \underset {i \neq j} {\forall} \boldsymbol {\xi} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \boldsymbol {P} \boldsymbol {b} _ {i} \tag {21}$$

where $1 \leq i \neq j \leq n$ and $n \geq 2 .$ .
