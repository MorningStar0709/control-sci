The sigma-point based FHDP is summarized in Algorithm 2. Although the algorithm is written in its simple form, it is also possible to use the regularization and line search methods described in Section II-C as part of it. Although in the line search, a straightforward way is to use the original cost function as the merit function, in its implementation, it is important to take into account that the cost function minimized by the FHDP is not exactly the original cost function (see Sec. IV).

Algorithm 2 Sigma-point dynamic programming (SPDP)   
Input: Initial state $\hat{x}_1$ , nominal control $\hat{u}_k$ , for $k = 1, \ldots, T - 1$ , nominal state $\hat{x}_k$ , for $k = 2, \ldots, T$ , terminal covariance $\Sigma_T$ , and joint covariance $\Sigma_k$ Output: Update $\hat{u}_k$ and $\hat{x}_k$ 1: Backward pass:
2: Given $\Sigma_T$ , compute $a_T$ , $b_T$ , and $C_T$ using (37)
3: Compute $V_T$ , $v_T$ , and $S_T$ using (32)
4: for $k = T - 1$ to 1 do
5: Given $\Sigma_k$ , compute $a_Q$ , $b_Q$ and $C_Q$ using (36).
6: Compute the coefficients of $Q_k(x_k, u_k)$ using (30).
7: Compute $d$ and $K$ using (10), and $v_k$ and $S_k$ using (12).
8: end for
9: Forward pass:
10: Start from $\hat{x}_1$ .
11: for $k = 1$ to $T - 1$ do
12: $\hat{u}_k \leftarrow u_k + \delta u_k$ , where $\delta u_k$ is given by (11).
13: $\hat{x}_{k+1} \leftarrow f_k(\hat{x}_k, \hat{u}_k)$ 14: end for
15: Repeat from Step 1 until convergence.
