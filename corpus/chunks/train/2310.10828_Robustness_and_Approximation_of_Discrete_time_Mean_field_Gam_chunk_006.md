# 1.1 Contributions

In this paper, we explore the robustness of stationary mean-field equilibria when confronted with uncertainties in the model. Our approach involves first establishing conditions for the convergence of value iteration-based algorithms within the context of mean-field games. Subsequently, we leverage these results to illustrate that the mean-field equilibrium derived from this value iteration algorithm remains robust even in scenarios where the system dynamics are not precisely known. We then apply these insights into robustness to address the finite model approximation problem in mean-field games, demonstrating that finely quantized state spaces yield mean-field equilibria for the finite model that closely approximate the nominal solution.
