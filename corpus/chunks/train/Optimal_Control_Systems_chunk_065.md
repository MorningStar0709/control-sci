# Example 2.3

If

$$f (t) = \left(t _ {1} + t _ {2}\right) ^ {2} \tag {2.1.5}$$

find the increment of the function $f(t)$ .

Solution: The increment $\Delta f$ becomes

$$
\begin{array}{l} \Delta f \triangleq f (t + \Delta t) - f (t) \\ = \left(t _ {1} + \Delta t _ {1} + t _ {2} + \Delta t _ {2}\right) ^ {2} - \left(t _ {1} + t _ {2}\right) ^ {2} \\ = \left(t _ {1} + \Delta t _ {1}\right) ^ {2} + \left(t _ {2} + \Delta t _ {2}\right) ^ {2} + 2 \left(t _ {1} + \Delta t _ {1}\right) \left(t _ {2} + \Delta t _ {2}\right) - \\ \left(t _ {1} ^ {2} + t _ {2} ^ {2} + 2 t _ {1} t _ {2}\right) \\ = 2 \left(t _ {1} + t _ {2}\right) \Delta t _ {1} + 2 \left(t _ {1} + t _ {2}\right) \Delta t _ {2} + \left(\Delta t _ {1}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\Delta t _ {2}\right) ^ {2} \\ + 2 \Delta t _ {1} \Delta t _ {2}. \tag {2.1.6} \\ \end{array}
$$

(b) Increment of a Functional: Now we are ready to define the increment of a functional.

DEFINITION 2.2 The increment of the functional J, denoted by $\Delta J$ , is defined as

$$\Delta J \triangleq J (x (t) + \delta x (t)) - J (x (t)). \tag {2.1.7}$$

Here $\delta x(t)$ is called the variation of the function $x(t)$ . Since the increment of a functional is dependent upon the function $x(t)$ and its variation $\delta x(t)$ , strictly speaking, we need to write the increment as $\Delta J(x(t), \delta x(t))$ .
