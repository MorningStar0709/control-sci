In eq. $( 3 ) , u _ { p } , u _ { q } ,$ and $u _ { r }$ represent the difference in rotor speeds that enact rolling, pitching, and yawing moments respectively. This is used as the input vector in this paper since it results in a diagonal control effectiveness matrix, B, in section III. The control moments depend on the quadrotor configuration and, for the quadrotors used in this paper1, may be constructed from the rotor speeds via

$$u _ {p} = \left(\omega_ {1} + \omega_ {4}\right) - \left(\omega_ {2} + \omega_ {3}\right)u _ {q} = \left(\omega_ {1} + \omega_ {2}\right) - \left(\omega_ {3} + \omega_ {4}\right) \tag {4}u _ {r} = S _ {r} \left[ \left(\omega_ {1} + \omega_ {3}\right) - \left(\omega_ {2} + \omega_ {4}\right) \right]$$

$S _ { r } = 1$ for clockwise rotation of $\omega _ { \mathrm { l } }$ and $S _ { r } = - 1$ if counterclockwise. The aerodynamic moment models themselves are (up to) forth order polynomial functions of the associated body rotational rates $\Omega _ { B }$ and control moments [27], [28].
