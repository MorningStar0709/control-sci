# A. Motion Estimation

We choose a sample discrete gait (parameterized by time, τ ) switching between foot extremal positions (α− and $\alpha ^ { + }$ , Fig. 4a). To compute motion over a cycle, this gait is transposed onto the system’s stratified panels (9) along the x $( d z ^ { x } )$ and $\textbf { y } \left( d z ^ { y } \right)$ coordinate axes respectively as shown in Fig. 4b. We observe that the $d z ^ { x }$ stratified panel is anti-symmetric about $\alpha = 0$ , indicating that no net motion is accrued along that direction $( z _ { \phi } ^ { x } \ = \ 0 )$ for symmetric gaits. On the other hand, the symmetric $d z ^ { y }$ stratified panel indicates equal (due to the mechanical coupling) displacement in the y-direction is obtained during each contact phase. The net displacement (Fig. 4c) accrued over the above gait-cycle, $z _ { \phi } ,$ , is simply the integration of the stratified panels along α and aligns with the robot center’s trajectory (Fig. 4d) computed using the boundary integral [3].
