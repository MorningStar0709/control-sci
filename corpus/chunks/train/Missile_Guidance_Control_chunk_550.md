$$(d r / d \theta) ^ {2} = (r ^ {4} / r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o}) \{V _ {o} ^ {2} - 2 \mu [ (1 / r _ {o}) - (1 / r) ] \} - (h ^ {2} / r ^ {2}). \tag {6.97}$$

An explicit expression for the trajectory will result from integrating (6.97), the result of which may be written as follows:

$$d \theta = \frac {- d (\frac {r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o}}{r})}{\sqrt {V _ {o} ^ {2} - 2 \mu (\frac {1}{r _ {o}} - \frac {1}{r}) - \frac {r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o}}{r ^ {2}}}}. \tag {6.98}$$

Now let

$$x = r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o} / r. \tag {6.99a}$$

Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} 2 \mu / r = \left[ \left(r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o}\right) / r \right] \left[ 2 \mu / \left(r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o}\right) \right] \\ = \left[ 2 \mu / r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o} \right] x. \tag {6.99b} \\ \end{array}
$$

Now let

$$a = - 1, \quad b = 2 \mu / r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos \phi_ {o}, \quad c = V _ {o} ^ {2} - (2 \mu / r _ {o}). \tag {6.99c}$$

Then (6.98) may be written in the form

$$d \theta = - [ d x / (a x ^ {2} + b x + c) ^ {1 / 2} ]. \tag {6.100}$$

From integral tables [8] we have

$$\theta = - (1 / \sqrt {- a}) \sin^ {- 1} \left\{(- 2 a x - b) / \sqrt {b ^ {2} - 4 a c} \right\} + \kappa , \tag {6.101}$$

where κ is the constant of integration. Substituting the values for a, b, c, and x from (6.99a) and (6.99c), we obtain r in the form

$$
\begin{array}{l} r = \left[ r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o} / \mu \right] / \left\{1 - \left[ \left(r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos_ {o}\right) / \mu \right] \left[ V _ {o} ^ {2} - \left(2 \mu / r _ {o}\right) \right. \right. \\ \left. + \left(\mu^ {2} / \left(r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o}\right) \right] ^ {1 / 2} \cos \theta \right\}. \tag {6.102} \\ \end{array}
$$

Equation (6.102), like (6.2) and (6.21), is the equation of a conic in polar coordinates (r, θ ), whose eccentricity is

$$e = [ (r _ {o} V _ {o} \cos_ {o}) / \mu ] \{V _ {o} ^ {2} - (2 \mu / r _ {o}) + (\mu^ {2} / (r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o}) \} ^ {1 / 2}. \tag {6.103a}$$

Then

$$r = [ r _ {o} ^ {2} V _ {o} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \phi_ {o} / \mu ] / (1 - e \cos \theta). \tag {6.103b}$$

As before, the value of the eccentricity determines which type of conic the trajectory is. That is,

$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 <   e <   1, \quad \text {ellipse}, \\ e = 1, \quad \text { parabola }, \\ e > 1, \quad \text { hyperbola }. \\ \end{array}
$$

If the conic is an ellipse, the trajectory either returns to the Earth or moves as a satellite about the Earth.
