# B. Van der Pol Oscillator

Figure 2 presents the response of the Van der Pol oscillator. The observer-free method achieves smooth convergence and moderate undershoot, while HOSMC produces the largest amplitude of oscillation. Adaptive SMC also ensures convergence, but with a more aggressive response that leads to a higher overshoot before settling. Both methods perform well, but the observer-free strategy achieves a more stable and regular behavior without requiring adaptive gain tuning. Classical SMC displays slower decay with no overshoot. The results show that the observer-free strategy achieves a balance between convergence speed and smoothness without generating high-amplitude oscillations.
