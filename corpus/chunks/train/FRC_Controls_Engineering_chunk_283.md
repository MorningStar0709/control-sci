Using these results, we can compute the covariance of $\mathbf { z } = \mathbf { A } \mathbf { x } + \mathbf { B } \mathbf { y }$ .

$$\Sigma_ {z} = \operatorname{cov} (\mathbf {z}, \mathbf {z})\Sigma_ {z} = E [ (\mathbf {z} - \overline {{\mathbf {z}}}) (\mathbf {z} - \overline {{\mathbf {z}}}) ^ {\mathsf {T}} ]\Sigma_ {z} = E \left[ \left(\mathbf {A x} + \mathbf {B y} - \mathbf {A} \overline {{{\mathbf {x}}}} - \mathbf {B} \overline {{{\mathbf {y}}}}\right) \left(\mathbf {A x} + \mathbf {B y} - \mathbf {A} \overline {{{\mathbf {x}}}} - \mathbf {B} \overline {{{\mathbf {y}}}}\right) ^ {\mathrm{T}} \right]\Sigma_ {z} = E \left[ \left(\mathbf {A} (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) + \mathbf {B} (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}})\right) \left(\mathbf {A} (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) + \mathbf {B} (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}})\right) ^ {\top} \right]\Sigma_ {z} = E \left[ \left(\mathbf {A} (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) + \mathbf {B} (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}})\right) \left((\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {A} ^ {\top} + (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {B} ^ {\top}\right) \right]\Sigma_ {z} = E \left[ \mathbf {A} (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {A} ^ {\top} + \mathbf {A} (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {B} ^ {\top} \right.\left. + \mathbf {B} (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}}) (\mathbf {x} - \overline {{\mathbf {x}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {A} ^ {\top} + \mathbf {B} (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}}) (\mathbf {y} - \overline {{\mathbf {y}}}) ^ {\top} \mathbf {B} ^ {\top} \right]$$

Since x and y are independent, $\Sigma _ { x y } = 0$ and the cross terms cancel out.
