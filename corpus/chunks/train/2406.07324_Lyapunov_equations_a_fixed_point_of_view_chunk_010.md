# 2.3 Uniqueness of solutions

We will now deduce that 2) holds. We proceed by contradiction, and assume that there is at least one more fixed-point $Y _ { \alpha }$ . Let

$$\gamma_ {\alpha} = \operatorname{tr} \left(A ^ {\mathsf {T}} Y _ {\alpha} A + \alpha C ^ {\mathsf {T}} C\right)$$

and consider the function

$$g (\theta) = \theta X _ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) Y _ {\alpha}.$$

Note that although $X _ { \alpha }$ and $Y _ { \alpha }$ must both be positive definite by our argument from the previous subsection, $g \left( \theta \right)$ is not positive definite for all θ. As explained in ${ \mathrm { F i g u r e } } 3 ,$ there are values of θ such that $g \left( \theta \right)$ is only positive semidefinite, and when θ is large enough, $g \left( \theta \right) \not \in { \mathcal { C } }$ .

We will now investigate what happens when we pass matrices defined by the above function through $f \left( \cdot \right)$ . First, note that

$$
\begin{array}{l} A ^ {\mathsf {T}} g (\theta) A + \alpha C ^ {\mathsf {T}} C = \theta \lambda_ {\alpha} X _ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha} Y _ {\alpha} \\ \Longrightarrow f (g (\theta)) = \frac {\theta \lambda_ {\alpha}}{\theta \lambda_ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}} X _ {\alpha} + \frac {(1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}}{\theta \lambda_ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}} Y _ {\alpha}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Observe from the right-hand-side of the above that

$$f (g (\theta)) = g \left(\frac {\theta \lambda_ {\alpha}}{\theta \lambda_ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}}\right).$$

This means that when we pass a matrix of the form of $g \left( \theta \right)$ through $f \left( \cdot \right)$ , we get a matrix on the same form back, and more specifically, the θ value of the returned matrix is given by

$$g ^ {- 1} \left(f (g (\theta))\right) = \frac {\theta \lambda_ {\alpha}}{\theta \lambda_ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}}.$$

Therefore, when studying the fixed-points of $f \left( \cdot \right)$ that can be written on the form of $g \left( \theta \right)$ , we can equivalently study the function $\theta \mapsto g ^ { - 1 } \left( f \left( g \left( \theta \right) \right) \right)$ ). More specifically, $g \left( \theta \right)$ is a fixed-point of $f \left( \cdot \right)$ if and only if

$$\theta = \frac {\theta \lambda_ {\alpha}}{\theta \lambda_ {\alpha} + (1 - \theta) \gamma_ {\alpha}}. \tag {8}$$
