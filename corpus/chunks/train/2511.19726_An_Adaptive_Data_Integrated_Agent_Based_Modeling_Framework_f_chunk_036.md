# 10.3 Environment Layer: Emissions Capacity and Sectors

The environment is defined by an emissions capacity $C _ { t }$ and optionally a sectoral structure. Let $S = \{ 1 , \dots , M \}$ denote sectors. Each sector j has a capacity $C _ { j , t }$ and receives emissions from agents $N ( j )$ . Aggregate emissions satisfy:

$$E _ {j, t} = \sum_ {i \in N (j)} e _ {i, t}, \qquad E _ {t} = \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} E _ {j, t}.$$

This parallels the load on nodes in a distribution network, but without spatial geometry.
