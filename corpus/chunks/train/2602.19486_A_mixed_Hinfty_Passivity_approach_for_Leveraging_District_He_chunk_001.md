# I. INTRODUCTION

As decarbonization accelerates, electrified heating is becoming central to low-carbon energy systems. Heat pumps (HPs) enable efficient electricity-to-heat conversion and significantly tighten the coupling between electric power systems (EPSs) and district heating systems (DHSs). Large-scale initiatives including the EU Heat Pump Accelerator [1] and China’s National Action Plan [2] highlight the rapid growth of HP deployment. At the same time, the expansion of large data centers increases the need for fast and reliable ancillary services, intensifying the demand for flexible resources. Leveraging their inherent thermal storage capacity, DHSs present strong potential to contribute to EPS frequency support.

Frequency regulation in EPSs is hierarchical: primary control acts within seconds, secondary restores nominal frequency within minutes, and tertiary optimizes long-term operation. The thermal inertia of DHSs fits the secondary-control time scale. DHSs typically use optimal setpoint scheduling and tracking, where energy-sharing optimization yields costefficient setpoints and controllers follow them. Yet uncertain thermal demand causes setpoint deviations. Our prior work [3] proposed an LQR temperature regulator achieving optimal power sharing within a heating system without having a prior knowledge of the disturbances. The integration of such involved control schemes within a power system, while ensuring good performance in the combined electrothermal network is though a non-trivial problem.
