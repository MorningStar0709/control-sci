$$\dot {\boldsymbol {x}} = \boldsymbol {A} _ {1} \boldsymbol {x} + \boldsymbol {B} _ {1} \boldsymbol {u} _ {1}, \sigma = 1
\boldsymbol {A} _ {1} = \frac {1}{\omega^ {2} L _ {g} ^ {2} + K _ {p d} K _ {p q}} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - K _ {p q} K _ {i d} & - \omega L _ {g} K _ {i q} \\ \omega L _ {g} K _ {i d} & - K _ {p d} K _ {i q} \end{array} \right] \tag {9}

\pmb {B} _ {1} = \frac {1}{\omega^ {2} L _ {g} ^ {2} + K _ {p d} K _ {p q}} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} K _ {p q} K _ {i d} & \omega L _ {g} K _ {i q} \\ - \omega L _ {g} K _ {i d} & K _ {p d} K _ {i q} \end{array} \right]
$$

where $\begin{array} { r } { { \boldsymbol { x } } = [ i _ { d } , \ i _ { q } ] ^ { \mathrm { T } } , \ u _ { 1 } = [ I _ { d 1 } , \ 0 ] ^ { \mathrm { T } } , \ I _ { d 1 } } \end{array}$ is a constant, and σ is the discrete state variable.

2) LVRT Subsystem: According to Technical specification for connecting wind farm to power system of China, the reactive power priority control strategy is adopted when the wind turbine works under the LVRT condition [15]. This strategy prioritizes the reactive current requirements, then the reference value of active current is determined by fixed DCside voltage control and the current constraints of the WT-GSC, which is denoted as

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} i _ {d \text { ref }} = \min \left\{I _ {d 2}, i _ {d \max} \right\}, i _ {d \max} = \sqrt {I _ {\max} ^ {2} - i _ {q \text { ref }} ^ {2}} \\ i _ {q \text { ref}} = K _ {1} (0. 9 - v _ {g}), 0. 2 \leq v _ {g} \leq 0. 9, 1. 5 \leq K _ {1} \leq 3 \end{array} \right. \tag {10}
$$

where $K _ { 1 }$ is the dynamic reactive current proportional coefficient, $I _ { d 2 }$ is the constant active current reference value given by fixed DC-side voltage control, $i _ { d \operatorname* { m a x } }$ is the upper limit of active current, and $I _ { \mathrm { m a x } }$ is the upper limit of WT-GSC current.

Supposing the wind turbine is connected to the grid through a transmission line with impedance $R + j \omega L$ , the grid voltage is a constant $v _ { G }$ , and the capacity of WT-GSC is large enough, then we combine (4) and power flow equations to obtain the grid-connected point voltage as

$$
\begin{array}{l} v _ {g} \approx v _ {G} + \Delta v = v _ {G} + \frac {P _ {g} R + Q _ {g} X}{v _ {g}} \tag {11} \\ = v _ {G} + 1. 5 R i _ {d} - 1. 5 \omega L i _ {q}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Substituting (11) into (10) and taking the time derivative yields

$$\frac {\mathrm{d} i _ {q \text { ref }}}{\mathrm{d} t} = K _ {1} \left(- 1. 5 R \frac {\mathrm{d} i _ {d}}{\mathrm{d} t} + 1. 5 \omega L \frac {\mathrm{d} i _ {q}}{\mathrm{d} t}\right). \tag {12}$$
