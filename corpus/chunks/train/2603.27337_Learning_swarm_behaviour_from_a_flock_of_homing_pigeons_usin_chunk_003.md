In recent years, there has been a tremendous development in understanding of swarm behaviour in a flock of birds. Despite the progress made, there are open questions about the philosophy behind how the birds generate their trajectories while flying as a flock. It has been hypothesised that natural movements and locomotion are a result of an optimal control problem [16], [17]. The Inverse Optimal Control (IOC)

problem [18] [19] from the control systems community and the Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL) problem [20], [21] from the Machine Learning community both identify the cost/ reward function as the most succinct, robust and transferable definition of a task [20]. Mombaur et al formulated the human walking problem as an optimal control problem [18] and attempted to transfer biological locomotion from humans to humanoid robots using a bilevel IOC method [18]. Westermann et al analysed the human jumping movements and formulated the problem as an inverse optimal control problem [22]. Jin et al formulated the human squatting task as an optimal control problem with multi-phase cost functions and hence, put forward a minimum-principle based IOC method to recover the cost functions for each phase of the squatting task. Pinsler et al formulates a flock of birds as a maximum entropy inverse reinforcement learning problem [23].

There are numerous algorithms for the control of multiagent systems. Robust adaptive control algorithms for a swarm of multi-UAV systems have been proposed by Su et al [25], Nguyen et al [26], Shu et al [27], Wang et al [28]. Shen and Wei proposes a leader-follower algorithm for multi-UAV flocking control [29]. Wu et al proposes a semi-definite programming based robust control algorithm for containment coordination in a swarm system [30]. Considering the importance of decision making in multi-agent systems, Rizk et al surveys existing works on control of multi-agent systems [31]. Na et al puts forward a deep reinforcement learning bioinspired algorithm for collision avoidance in a swarm system [32]. Sliding mode control for multi-agent systems have been proposed by Li et al [33], Li et al [34] and Dong et al [35]. Optimal control algorithms have also been put forward for multi agent-systems, for example, by Wang et al [36] and Ertug et al [37].
