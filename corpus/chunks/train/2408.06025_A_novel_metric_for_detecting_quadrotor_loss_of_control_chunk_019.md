The ground truth non-LOC flights are used to determine which hyper-parameter combinations promote the greatest FCMW sensitivity to LOC while limiting false positives of non-LOC. Figure 3 summarizes the performance of the FCMW across all the MVW and CF combinations. In general, the shorter the MVW , the more prone the FCMW metric is to falsely label a non-LOC flight as a LOC flight. Longer MVW s reduce the sensitivity of the FCMW (see top subplot of fig. 3). Conversely, the lower the CF, the more capable the FCMW is at correctly identifying a non-LOC flight. However, if the CF is too low, then the FCMW becomes insensitive to LOC detection as the majority of the aerodynamics are filtered out (refer to bottom subplot of fig. 3). The optimum combination is found to be $M V W =$ 1.0s and $C F = 3 0 \mathrm { H z }$ as this combination correctly identifies all non-LOC flights while being the most sensitive to LOC detection. As expected, this MVW is below the fastest observed crash of 1.3s.
