# 3.6.2 SATURATION LIMITS

Saturation limits have been put in place in the controller.

In the case of $\theta _ { d }$ and $\phi _ { d } ,$ , the saturation limit is the maximum rotation angle that the controller should command in order to maintain stable flight. Without this lim ${ \mathrm { i t } } ,$ a position error in ?? or ?? would cause the controller to command continuously increasing values of $\theta$ and $\phi ,$ placing the quadrotor into an uncontrolled spin.

The saturation limits on the outputs of the controller, i.e., $u _ { z } , u _ { \theta } , u _ { \phi } ,$ , and $u _ { \psi }$ are in place to limit the performance of the quadrotor to realistic capabilities. The limit values, shown in Table $^ { 2 , }$ are derived from the force and torque calculations discussed in Section 3.2, as suggested by Selby [20], combined with a new term $\omega _ { m a x }$ which is a user-specified maximum angular velocity for the motor-propeller system.
