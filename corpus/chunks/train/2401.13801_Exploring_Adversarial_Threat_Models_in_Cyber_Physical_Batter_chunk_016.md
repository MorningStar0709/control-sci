# C. Optimal control-based input attack generation

In this subsection, we will discuss the potential design of input attack signals, denoted by $U _ { a }$ in (5)-(6). The $U _ { a }$ is essentially an additive current signal that is superimposed with user’s intended current signal $U _ { n o m }$ . To this end, the adversary’s objective is to cause either overcharging or overdischarging. Both of these objectives can be formulated as following a reference state trajectory desired by the adversary. This reference trajectory will enable the adversarial requirements of overcharging and over-discharging. The adversarial objective has two components: (i) induce unsafe states of overcharging or over-discharging as fast as possible by applying input attack $U _ { a } .$ , and (ii) achieve such unsafe state with minimum possible efforts, that is, using limited $U _ { a }$ signal energy.
