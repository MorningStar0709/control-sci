In order to account for time-varying control bounds, adaptive CBFs (aCBFs) [31] have been proposed by introducing penalty functions in HOCBFs constraints, which provide flexible and adaptive control strategies over time. However, this approach requires extensive hyperparameter tuning, especially when the inter-sampling effect cannot be ignored. Moreover, this approach, along with the previously mentioned methods, cannot handle safety constraints with mixed relative degrees. To address feasibility issues while ensuring safety, this article proposes a novel approach to safety-critical control problems, introducing a new type of aCBFs called Auxiliary-Variable Adaptive Control Barrier Functions (AVCBFs). Compared to HOCBFs and original aCBFs, AVCBFs are designed to more effectively handle safety constraints that involve high-order or mixed relative degrees.
