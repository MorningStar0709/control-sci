# 5.3 Scenario 3: Saturated control signal

To further evaluate the performance of the proposed controller, more challenging reference trajectories are used in Scenario 3, in which control signals become saturated in a certain period. The equations of those trajectories are given by:

$$\phi_ {d} = \frac {1}{3} \sin (2 t) + \frac {1}{6} \sin (6 t)\theta_ {d} = \frac {1}{3} \cos (2 t) + \frac {1}{6} \cos (4 t) \tag {39}\psi_ {d} = \frac {\pi}{1 0} t + \frac {1}{5} \sin (3 t) + 0. 5$$

Figure 5 shows the tracking results. Similar to previous scenarios, the LNMPC introduces the fastest transient responses and smallest steady-state errors. Its control signals also do not exhibit the chattering phenomenon as with the SMC, as shown in Figure 6. Especially, the advantage of the LNMPC is clearly shown in period $6 < t < 7 \ \mathrm { s }$ , where the control signals from all controllers are saturated due to the system constraints. The tracking errors of the BSC and SMC for pitch angle θ quickly increase, whereas that error of the LNMPC remains the same, as can be seen in Figure 5a. That advantage comes from the capability of the LNMPC to predict its future states and then provide optimal control signals to better adapt to their changes.
