# 3. Adaptive observers for conductance-based models

Following the notation of [11], the conductance-based network model (6) can be written in the form

$$\dot {v} = \Phi (v, w, u) \theta + b (v, w, u) \tag {7a}\dot {w} = g (v, w) \tag {7b}\dot {\theta} = 0 \tag {7c}$$

where $v ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { v } }$ is a vector of measured output membrane voltages, $u ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { v } }$ is a vector of input currents, $w ( t ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { w } }$ is a vector of unmeasured internal states, and $\theta \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { \theta } }$ is a vector of biophysical parameters to be estimated. The vector of unknown constant parameters θ is included in the state-space model via (7c).

In the present paper, the vector θ only includes maximal conductances.

Example 5. Consider the HCO described in Example 4. Let

$$\mu^ {(i)} = \left(\mu_ {\mathrm{Na}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{H}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{T}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{A}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{K}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{L}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{KCa}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{KIR}, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{syn}, p, i}, \mu_ {\mathrm{leak}, i}\right) ^ {\mathsf {T}} \tag {8}$$

for $i , p \in \mathcal { N } = \{ 1 , 2 \}$ and $p \neq i .$ . The estimated parameters of the HCO are chosen as

$$\theta = \operatorname{col} \left(\mu^ {(1)}, \mu^ {(2)}\right) \tag {9}$$

with $\mu ^ { ( 1 ) }$ and $\mu ^ { ( 2 ) }$ given by (8). Letting $\boldsymbol { v } = ( v _ { 1 } , v _ { 2 } ) ^ { \top }$ and $w = \mathrm { c o l } ( w ^ { ( 1 ) } , w ^ { ( 2 ) } )$ , the voltage dynamics of the model can then be written as (7a), where

$$
\Phi (v, w) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \varphi (v _ {1}, w ^ {(1)}) & 0 \\ 0 & \varphi (v _ {2}, w ^ {(2)}) \end{array} \right]
b (t) = (u _ {1} (t) / c _ {1}, u _ {2} (t) / c _ {2}) ^ {\mathsf {T}}$$

with

$$
\varphi (v _ {i}, w ^ {(i)}) = - \frac {1}{c _ {i}} \left( \begin{array}{c} m _ {\mathrm{Na}, i} ^ {3} h _ {\mathrm{Na}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{Na}}) \\ m _ {\mathrm{H}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{H}}) \\ m _ {\mathrm{T}, i} ^ {2} h _ {\mathrm{T}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{Ca}}) \\ m _ {\mathrm{A}, i} ^ {4} h _ {\mathrm{A}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{K}}) \\ m _ {\mathrm{K}, i} ^ {4} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{K}}) \\ m _ {\mathrm{L}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{Ca}}) \\ (\frac {[ \mathrm{Ca} ] _ {i}}{1 5 + [ \mathrm{Ca} ] _ {i}}) ^ {4} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{K}}) \\ \sigma_ {m, \text {KIR}} (v _ {i}) (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{K}}) \\ s _ {\text {syn,p}, i} (v _ {i} - E _ {\mathrm{G}}) \\ v _ {i} - E _ {\text {leak}} \end{array} \right) ^ {\top}
$$

for $i = 1 , 2$ and $p \neq i .$ .
