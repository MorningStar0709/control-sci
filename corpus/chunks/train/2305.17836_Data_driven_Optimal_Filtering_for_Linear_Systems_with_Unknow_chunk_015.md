$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {L} \varepsilon (L, \mathcal {Y}) = - 2 H ^ {\intercal} e _ {T} (L) y (T - 1) ^ {\intercal} \\ + 2 \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {T - 1} - (A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}) ^ {t} H ^ {\intercal} e _ {T} (L) y (T - t - 1) ^ {\intercal} + \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {t} (A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}) ^ {t - k} H ^ {\intercal} e _ {T} (L) y (T - t - 1) ^ {\intercal} L ^ {\intercal} (A _ {L} ^ {\intercal}) ^ {k - 1} H ^ {\intercal}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Finally, using this approximate gradient, the so-called SGD update proceeds as,

$$L _ {k + 1} = L _ {k} - \eta_ {k} \nabla_ {L} \widehat {J} _ {T} (L),$$

for $k \in \mathbb { Z } ,$ , where $\eta _ { k } > 0$ is the step-size. Numerical results of the application of this algorithm appears in Appendix G.
