$$
\begin{array}{l} v _ {t} ^ {\top} x _ {t} - v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} x _ {t + 1} + v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} B _ {d} d _ {t} \\ = \left(v _ {t + 1} + X B _ {d} d _ {t}\right) ^ {\top} (A - B _ {u} K _ {x}) x _ {t} - v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} \left(A x _ {t} + B _ {u} u _ {t}\right) \\ = - \left(K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}\right) ^ {\top} H \left(u _ {t} + K _ {x} x _ {t}\right) + d _ {t} ^ {\top} B _ {d} ^ {\top} X \left(A x _ {t} + B _ {u} u _ {t}\right). \\ \end{array}
$$

Use these two results to simplify the last two lines of (10) thus yielding:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {t} \\ u _ {t} \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Q & S \\ S ^ {\top} & R \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {t} \\ u _ {t} \end{array} \right] = (u _ {t} - u _ {t} ^ {o}) ^ {\top} H (u _ {t} - u _ {t} ^ {o}) \\ - \left(K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}\right) ^ {\top} H \left(K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}\right) \tag {11} \\ + d _ {t} ^ {\top} B _ {d} ^ {\top} X B _ {d} d _ {t} + 2 v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} B _ {d} d _ {t} \\ + (x _ {t} ^ {\top} X x _ {t} - x _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} X x _ {t + 1}) + 2 (v _ {t} ^ {\top} x _ {t} - v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} x _ {t + 1}). \\ \end{array}
$$

Finally, we obtain the cost by summing from $t = - \infty$ to $\infty$ . The two terms on the last line of (11) form telescoping sums. These telescoping sums equal zero due to the assumption that $x _ { - \infty } = 0$ and the controller K is stabilizing so that $x _ { t } \to 0$ as $t \to \infty$ . Thus the cost is:

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (K, d) = \sum_ {t = - \infty} ^ {\infty} \left[ (u _ {t} - u _ {t} ^ {o}) ^ {\top} H (u _ {t} - u _ {t} ^ {o}) \right. \tag {12} \\ - \left(K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}\right) ^ {\top} H (K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}) \\ \left. + d _ {t} ^ {\top} B _ {d} ^ {\top} X B _ {d} d _ {t} + 2 v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} B _ {d} d _ {t} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

The terms on the second and third lines only depend on d and not the choice of u. Moreover, $H \succ 0$ and the first term is minimized by $u _ { t } = u _ { t } ^ { o }$ . Hence the non-causal controller $K ^ { o }$ minimizes the cost. □

The minimal cost achieved by the non-causal controller is obtained by setting $u _ { t } = u _ { t } ^ { o }$ in (12):

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (K ^ {o}, d) = \sum_ {t = - \infty} ^ {\infty} \left[ - (K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}) ^ {\top} H (K _ {d} d _ {t} + K _ {v} v _ {t + 1}) \right. \\ \left. + d _ {t} ^ {\top} B _ {d} ^ {\top} X B _ {d} d _ {t} + 2 v _ {t + 1} ^ {\top} B _ {d} d _ {t} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

The non-causal dynamics for $v _ { t }$ in (8) are stable when iterated backward from $v _ { \infty } = 0$ . If $d \in \ell _ { 2 }$ then $v \in \ell _ { 2 }$ and this cost is finite.
