# 4. Controllability and Graph partition

In this section, the controllability of matrix-weighted signed networks is discussed by using graph partition. Firstly, the definition of equitable partition of matrix-weighted signed networks is given. Secondly, the controllability of matrix-weighted signed networks in fixed topology, switching topology and the heterogeneous system is analyzed by using the concept of equitable partition.

$$\sum_ {t _ {1} \in V _ {j}, t _ {1} \in N _ {r +}} \mathcal {A} _ {r t _ {1}} = \sum_ {t _ {2} \in V _ {j}, t _ {2} \in N _ {s +}} \mathcal {A} _ {s t _ {2}}$$

and for any $r , s \in V _ { i } , i , j = 1 , 2 , \ldots , k$

$$\sum_ {t _ {1} \in V _ {j}, t _ {1} \in N _ {r -}} \mathcal {A} _ {r t _ {1}} = \sum_ {t _ {2} \in V _ {j}, t _ {2} \in \mathcal {N} _ {s -}} \mathcal {A} _ {s t _ {2}}$$

Remark 2. Compared with the definition for scaler weighted network in Guan and Wang (2018), The sum of scalar weights in previous definition is replaced by the sum of matrices. This shows the difference between matrix weighted network and scalar weighted network when using the concept of equitable partition to analyze controllability.
