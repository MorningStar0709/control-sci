# A. Adaptive Backstepping for Position Control

A quaternion-based ABC algorithm is designed for the UAV translation to track predefined desired translation trajectories to resolve persistent singularity issues in Euler-based controllers (e.g., [33]). Backstepping is a superior technique for underactuated systems because of its recursive approach for implementation [44]. The first step is to find the error between the UAV’s true and desired position. Based on (6), the error equations $e _ { p } = [ e _ { x p } , e _ { y p } , e _ { z p } ] ^ { \top } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ can be derived as follow:

$$e _ {x p} = x _ {p} - x _ {d}e _ {y p} = y _ {p} - y _ {d} \tag {9}e _ {z p} = z _ {p} - z _ {d}$$

such that the derivative of (9) is given by

$$\dot {e} _ {x p} = \dot {x} _ {p} - \dot {x} _ {d} = v _ {1} - \dot {x} _ {d}\dot {e} _ {y p} = \dot {y} _ {p} - \dot {y} _ {d} = v _ {2} - \dot {y} _ {d} \tag {10}\dot {e} _ {z p} = \dot {z} _ {p} - \dot {z} _ {d} = v _ {3} - \dot {z} _ {d}$$

Let us define the following Lyapunov function candidate and subsequently formulate the controller that ensures system stability. Consider the following Lyapunov function:

$$V _ {1} = \frac {1}{2} e _ {x p} ^ {2}V _ {3} = \frac {1}{2} e _ {y p} ^ {2} \tag {11}V _ {5} = \frac {1}{2} e _ {z p} ^ {2}$$

In view of (11) and with the substitution of (10), one obtains

$$\dot {V} _ {1} = e _ {x p} (\dot {e} _ {x p}) = e _ {x p} (v _ {1} - \dot {x} _ {d})\dot {V} _ {3} = e _ {y p} \left(\dot {e} _ {y p}\right) = e _ {y p} \left(v _ {2} - \dot {y} _ {d}\right) \tag {12}\dot {V} _ {5} = e _ {z p} (\dot {e} _ {z p}) = e _ {z p} (v _ {3} - \dot {z} _ {d})$$

Therefore, $v _ { 1 d } , \ v _ { 2 d } .$ , and $v _ { 3 d }$ should be selected to stabilize (12) as as follows:

$$v _ {1 d} = - m _ {x p} e _ {x p} + \dot {x} _ {d}v _ {2 d} = - m _ {y p} e _ {y p} + \dot {y} _ {d} \tag {13}v _ {3 d} = - m _ {z p} e _ {z p} + \dot {z} _ {d}$$

where $m _ { x p } , m _ { y p } ,$ and $m _ { z p }$ are non-zero and non-negative constants. Consider the translation dynamics (6) and let Assumption 1 and 2 hold true. The position errors in (9) converges to origin if the desired $v _ { 1 d } , v _ { 2 d }$ , and $v _ { 3 d }$ are selected as (13).
