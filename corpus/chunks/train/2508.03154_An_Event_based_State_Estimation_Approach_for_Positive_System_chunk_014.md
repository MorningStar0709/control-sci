# Illustrative Example

In this section, a numerical analysis is performed using simulation to validate the effectiveness of the proposed event-based positive observer design. To assess the performance of the proposed positive observer, we consider two simulation scenarios: (i) an academic example (Example 1), and (ii) a threetank water system model (Example 2).

Example 1. Consider the following autonomous positive linear system,

$$
\dot {x} (t) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 1 & 3 \\ 0 & - 1 \end{array} \right] x (t), \tag {35}

y (t) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 1 & 0 \end{array} \right] x (t).
$$

For $\alpha = 0 . 3$ and $\beta = 1 . 5 ,$ , using sufficient conditions in Theorem 1 and solving them in MATLAB using the YALMIP toolbox, the feasible solutions P , Q, and observer gain matrix, L obtained are:

$$
P = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0. 3 6 5 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 1. 1 7 3 6 \end{array} \right], \quad Q = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0. 4 0 5 6 & 0 \\ 0 & 0. 9 0 7 9 \end{array} \right]

L = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0. 9 0 3 7 & 0 \end{array} \right] ^ {\top}.
$$
