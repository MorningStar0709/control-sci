# V. SIMULATION

The performance and effectiveness of the proposed learningbased SHS contingency detection framework is evaluated using the IEEE 33-bus distribution test system, with detailed configuration parameters provided in [32]. The system under consideration consists of generators $G _ { 1 } , \ G _ { 2 } .$ , and $G _ { 4 } ,$ each with an active power capacity of 0.2 MW, and one PV–BESS unit with a power capacity of 0.2 MVA. Generator $G _ { 1 }$ at bus 1 serves as the slack bus, providing a total power capacity of 4 MW. The system states defined for the dynamic model (outlined in Section II) include the states $x _ { i } = [ \delta _ { i } , \omega _ { i } ]$ for each generator, where $\delta _ { i }$ and $\omega _ { i }$ represent the rotor angle and rotor speed deviation, respectively, and 16 additional states representing the internal dynamics and control states of the PV–BESS system. Also, two Phasor Measurement Units (PMUs) are installed at generators $G _ { 1 }$ and $G _ { 2 }$ to measure rotor angles $\delta _ { 1 }$ and $\delta _ { 2 }$ .
