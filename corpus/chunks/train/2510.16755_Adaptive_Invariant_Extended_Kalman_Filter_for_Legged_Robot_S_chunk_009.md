# A. System Dynamics & Measurement Model

The system dynamics of legged robots is generally formulated as described in [7] and [12]:

$$\dot {R} _ {B} = R _ {B} \left(\omega - w _ {\omega}\right) ^ {\times} \tag {16a}\dot {v} _ {B} = R _ {B} (a - w _ {a}) + g \tag {16b}\dot {p} _ {B} = v _ {B} \tag {16c}\dot {p} _ {F _ {i}} = R _ {B} (- w _ {f _ {i}}) \tag {16d}\dot {b} _ {\omega} = w _ {b \omega} \tag {16e}\dot {b} _ {a} = w _ {b a} \tag {16f}\binom {\omega} {a} = \binom {\tilde {\omega} - b _ {\omega}} {\tilde {a} - b _ {a}} \tag {17}$$

The ω and a are bias-corrected angular velocity and acceleration. The ω˜ and a˜ are measured values by the gyroscope and accelerometer, which are corrupted by sensor noise denoted as $w _ { \omega } , w _ { a }$ . The $b _ { \omega }$ and $b _ { a }$ are the corresponding biases with noise $w _ { b \omega } , w _ { b a } .$ . The g is the gravitational acceleration vector. $w _ { f _ { i } }$ is each i-th foot linear velocity noise. The noise terms are assumed to be white Gaussian noise with constant variance. The $w _ { f _ { i } }$ is set to be small during contact and larger during swing phases, thereby relaxing the static assumption of the foot model (16d). System dynamics can be formed as an invariant observer (6a), and it satisfies Eq. (9).

$$\dot {\boldsymbol {\chi}} _ {t} = f \left(\chi_ {t}, u _ {t}\right) - \chi_ {t} w ^ {\wedge} \tag {18a}
f = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c c} R _ {B} \omega^ {\times} & R _ {B} a + g & v _ {B} & \mathbf {0} _ {3, 1} & \dots & \mathbf {0} _ {3, 1} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \end{array} \right) \tag {18b}

w ^ {\wedge} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c c} w _ {\omega} ^ {\times} & w _ {a} & \mathbf {0} _ {3, 1} & w _ {f _ {1}} & \dots & w _ {f _ {N}} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \mathbf {0} _ {1, 3} & 0 & 0 & 0 & \dots & 0 \end{array} \right) \tag {18c}
$$

The Right-Invariant error and the bias error are given by:

$$\eta^ {r} = \hat {\chi} \chi^ {- 1} \tag {19a}\epsilon_ {b} = \binom {\epsilon_ {b \omega}} {\epsilon_ {b a}} = \binom {b _ {\omega} - \hat {b} _ {\omega}} {b _ {a} - \hat {b} _ {a}} \tag {19b}$$
