One of the most appealing characteristics of neural networks is their ability for data generalization. This is achieved through the extraction of data features and dependencies that may initially appear obscure. In the context of dynamical systems reconstruction, this aptitude could facilitate significant advancements in the development of models based on experimental data. In this paper, we investigate the extent to which neural networks can reproduce the dynamical regimes of a system, observed for various values of its control parameters, when only a single scalar time series of this system is available. Created neural network models a family of dynamical systems parameterized by a control parameter. This family exhibits behavior consistent with that of the original system, i.e. discovers its specific regimes and transitions. This is demonstrated by the example of a neuromorphic Hodgkin-Huxley system. The reconstruction is carried out in two steps. Initially, a variational autoencoder is trained to convert the time series into a sequence of reconstructed state vectors. These are then used for training a neural network map. The trained map operates as a dynamical system with discrete time, having one control parameter. We show that it is capable of reproducing the dynamical regimes of the original system, including those that were not demonstrated during training.
