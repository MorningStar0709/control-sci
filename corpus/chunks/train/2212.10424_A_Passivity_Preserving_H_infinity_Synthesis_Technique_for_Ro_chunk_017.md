# A. 2 link end-effector control

We illustrate our design on the two link manipulator in Figure 1. We take links of length 1m, each with a mass of 3Kg. The controller (2), (3) corresponds to the one represented in Figure 1, with virtual spring and dampers aligned to Cartesian axes, pulling the end effector position $z _ { c }$ towards a desired reference $r = [ 1 . 0 ; 1 . 0 ]$ .

For simplicity, we assume that the disturbing force acts on the end effector as well, that is, $z _ { d } ~ = ~ z _ { c } .$ We choose the following weighting matrices, which add a larger weight to vertical displacement/velocity errors. In a pick and place task, this choice could be motivated by the desire of reaching a tighter control of the vertical position of the robot, to compensate for uncertainties on the load.

$$
W _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 1. 0 & 0. 0 \\ 0. 0 & 1 0. 0 \end{array} \right] \qquad W _ {2} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0. 0 1 & 0. 0 \\ 0. 0 & 0. 1 \end{array} \right]
$$

We also consider three poses, each requiring the end effector to be in the upper right quadrant. $q _ { b } ^ { * }$ corresponds to r.

$$
q _ {a} ^ {*} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} - 1. 0 \\ \pi / 2 + 1. 0 \end{array} \right], \quad q _ {b} ^ {*} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0. 0 \\ \pi / 2 \end{array} \right], \quad q _ {c} ^ {*} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1. 0 \\ \pi / 2 - 1. 0 \end{array} \right],
$$

We construct LMI 2 for two different gains: $\gamma = 1 0 . 0 \mathrm { : }$ , and $\gamma ~ = ~ 0 . 1$ . The choice of $\gamma$ determines the level of performance, with smaller $\gamma$ corresponding to tighter position control (higher stiffness of the robot).
