# Vertical Motion

Example 2.1 presents a mechanical system with horizontal translational motion. Many mechanical systems involve vertical translational motion where the gravitational forces must be accounted for in the dynamical equations. One example is the suspension system for an automobile, where shock absorbers and springs support the axle–wheel assembly and dampen vibrations from the road. Whether or not gravitational forces explicitly appear in the mathematical model depends on the choice of displacement coordinates, which we demonstrate with a simple 1-DOF example.
