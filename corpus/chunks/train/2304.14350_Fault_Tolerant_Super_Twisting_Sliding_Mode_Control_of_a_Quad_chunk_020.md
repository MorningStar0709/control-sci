Fig. 12-15 show simulations indicating the reference tracking results when the fourth rotor of the quadrotor experiences 60% power loss. In case of a rotor failure in the quadrotor, the control allocation algorithm activates, ensuring that the quadrotor stably follows the given references. The control allocation algorithm can compensate for the failure of the fourth rotor with the help of the other 3 rotors.

In case of 60% rotor failure, the quadrotor can still follow the given references. However, a small overshoot is observed at the peaks of the sinusoidal references. This is due to power loss in one rotor of the quadrotor. As explained in Section II, the torque difference between the rotor pairs (1,3) and (2,4) produces the yaw motion. Due to the power loss in the fourth rotor, the torque difference between the rotor pairs increases, and a higher overshoot occurs during the yaw angle reference tracking. When evaluated in general, it is clear that the control allocation algorithm works successfully and achieves stable reference tracking by compensating for rotor failure.
