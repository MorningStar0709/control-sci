# A. Simulation With Harmonic Road

Fig. 6 presents the time response results for the body acceleration, tire deflection, suspension travel and single-link angle of the quarter-car in response to a sinusoidal road disturbance of frequency 2 Hz, for the passive, $H _ { \infty }$ and RMPC controllers. It can be seen that the $H _ { \infty }$ controller can significantly attenuate the performance objectives, with a 67% and 64% root mean square value (RMS) drop in the sprung mass acceleration and the tire deflection, respectively, as compared to the passive suspension. The RMPC controller achieves further improvements by reducing further body acceleration by 18.7% and tire deflection by 19.8%, as compared to the $H _ { \infty }$ controller. Moreover, Fig. 7 indicates that the RMPC control scheme utilizes a larger part of the available actuator output torque-speed operating range than the $H _ { \infty }$ scheme does. It does so while still satisfying all the hard (torque and speed) and soft (power) constraints of the SAVGS actuator, which also reflects on the slightly larger suspension travel and single-link angle with the RMPC than with the $H _ { \infty }$ scheme in Fig. 6.
