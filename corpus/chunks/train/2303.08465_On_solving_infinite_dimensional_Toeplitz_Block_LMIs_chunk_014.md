# B. Truncated and banded approximation of TBLMI

The aim of this part is to show that (12) can be approximated by a banded version (see (15)) whose m-truncation (see (16)) is now tractable numerically since only a finite number of unknowns must be taken into account. To this end, we define in the sequel, for any TB operator ${ \mathcal { A } } ,$ its p−banded version denoted by $\cdot$ and obtained by deleting all its phasors of order higher than p.

Theorem 4: The following results hold true:

1) Assume that A is a bounded operator on $\cdot$ . The operator $\cdot$ converges to A in $\cdot$ -operator norm i.e.

$$\lim _ {p \to + \infty} \| \mathcal {A} - \mathcal {A} _ {b (p)} \| _ {\ell^ {2}} = 0$$

2) Under Assumption 1, if P is a solution to (12) then there exists $\cdot$ such that for any $p \geq p _ { 0 }$ ,

$$\mathcal {L} (\mathcal {P}; \mathcal {A} _ {s _ {b (p)}}, s \in \mathbb {S}) < 0 \tag {15}$$

3) For given p and m, the m−truncated and p−banded TBLMI:

$$\Pi_ {m} \big (\mathcal {L} \big (\mathcal {P}; \mathcal {A} _ {s _ {b (p)}}, s \in \mathbb {S} \big) \big) < 0. \tag {16}$$

involves a finite number of unknowns.

Proof: Let us show the first assertion. As $\cdot$ where $\cdot$ (see (7)) and using the Fourier series of A:

$$A (t) = \sum_ {k \in \mathbb {Z}} A _ {k} e ^ {\mathrm{j} \omega k t} a. e.,$$

we can write:

$$\left\| \mathcal {A} - \mathcal {A} _ {b (p)} \right\| _ {\ell^ {2}} = \left\| A - A _ {b (p)} \right\| _ {L ^ {\infty}} = \left\| \sum_ {| k | > p} A _ {k} e ^ {\mathrm{j} \omega k t} \right\| _ {L ^ {\infty}} \tag {17}$$

As by assumption there exists a constant $C _ { 1 }$ such that

$$\left\| \mathcal {A} \right\| _ {\ell^ {2}} = \left\| A \right\| _ {L ^ {\infty}} = \left\| \sum_ {k \in \mathbb {Z}} A _ {k} e ^ {\mathrm{j} \omega k t} \right\| _ {L ^ {\infty}} < C _ {1}$$

the series $\textstyle \sum _ { k \in \mathbb { Z } } A _ { k } e ^ { \mathrm { j } \omega k t }$ converges almost everywhere and $\begin{array} { r } { \operatorname* { l i m } _ { p \to + \infty } \sum _ { | k | > p } A _ { k } e ^ { \mathrm { i } \omega k t } = 0 ~ a . e ^ { } } \end{array}$ . Taking the limit w.r.t. p in (17) leads to the result.
