# 2.1 Dirichlet Eigenvalue Problem

By Ito formula, we have

$$d u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) = \mathcal {L} u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) d t + \sigma^ {\top} \nabla u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) d \mathbf {B} _ {t}, \tag {2.4}$$

i.e.,

$$
\begin{array}{l} u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) = u (x _ {0}) + \int_ {0} ^ {t} \mathcal {L} u (\mathbf {X} _ {s}) d s + \int_ {0} ^ {t} \sigma^ {\top} \nabla u (\mathbf {X} _ {s}) d \mathbf {B} _ {s} \\ = u \left(x _ {0}\right) + \int_ {0} ^ {t} \left(\lambda - V \left(\mathbf {X} _ {s}\right)\right) u \left(\mathbf {X} _ {s}\right) d s + \int_ {0} ^ {t} \sigma \left(\mathbf {X} _ {s}\right) ^ {\top} \nabla u \left(\mathbf {X} _ {s}\right) d \mathbf {B} _ {s}. \tag {2.5} \\ \end{array}
$$

Due to the fact that the last Ito integral term in (2.5) is a Martingale [6] , therefore the following defines Martingale with respect to a Bt−natural filtration $\{ \mathcal { F } _ { t } \} _ { t \ge 0 }$ ,

$$M _ {t} = u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) - u (\mathbf {x} _ {0}) - \int_ {0} ^ {t} (\lambda - V (\mathbf {X} _ {s})) u (\mathbf {X} _ {s}) d s, \tag {2.6}$$

namely, for any $s < t ,$

$$E \left[ M _ {t} \mid \mathcal {F} _ {s} \right] = M _ {s}, \tag {2.7}$$

which implies for any measurable set $A \in { \mathcal { F } } _ { s } $ ,

$$\int_ {A} M _ {t} P (d \omega) = \int_ {A} E [ M _ {t} | \mathcal {F} _ {s} ] P (d \omega) = \int_ {A} M _ {s} P (d \omega) \tag {2.8}$$

or

$$\int_ {A} (M _ {t} - M _ {s}) P (d \omega) = 0, \tag {2.9}\int_ {\Omega} \left(M _ {t} - M _ {s}\right) I _ {A} (\omega) P (d \omega) = 0 \tag {2.10}$$

where $I _ { A } ( \omega )$ is the indicator function of the set A.

In particular, if we take $A = \Omega \in { \mathcal { F } } _ { s }$ in (2.10), we have

$$E \left[ M _ {t} - M _ {s} \right] = 0. \tag {2.11}$$

i.e. the Martingale Mt has a constant expectation.

In the case of finite domain D, τ∂D is a stopping time where $\tau _ { \partial D }$ is the first exit time of the process $X _ { t }$ outside D, then $M _ { t \wedge \tau _ { \partial D } }$ is still a Martingale [6], thus

$$E \left[ M _ {t \wedge \tau_ {\partial D}} - M _ {s \wedge \tau_ {\partial D}} \right] = 0. \tag {2.12}$$

Remark 2.1. We could define a different generator L by not including $\mu ^ { \intercal } \nabla$ in (2.2), then the Martingale in (2.13) will be changed to

$$M _ {t} ^ {*} = u (\mathbf {X} _ {t}) - u (\mathbf {x} _ {0}) - \int_ {0} ^ {t} \left(\lambda - \mu^ {\top} (\mathbf {X} _ {s}) \nabla - V (\mathbf {X} _ {s})\right) u (\mathbf {X} _ {s}) d s, \tag {2.13}$$
