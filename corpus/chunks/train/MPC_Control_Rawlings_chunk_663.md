Lemma 6.6 (Converse theorem for exponential stability). If the zero steady-state solution of $x ^ { + } = f ( x )$ is globally exponentially stable, then there exists Lipschitz continuous $V : \mathbb { R } ^ { n } \to \mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }$ that satisfies the following: there exist constants $a , b , c , \sigma > 0$ , such that for all $\boldsymbol { x } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$

$$
\begin{array}{l} a | x | ^ {\sigma} \leq V (x) \leq b | x | ^ {\sigma} \\ V (f (x)) - V (x) \leq - c | x | ^ {\sigma} \\ \end{array}
$$

Moreover, any $\sigma > 0$ is valid, and the constant c can be chosen as large as one wishes.

The proof of this lemma is discussed in Exercise B.3.
