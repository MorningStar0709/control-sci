# 5.2. ESO for Rotational Motion

Proposition 2 (Rotational ESO). Define $e _ { R } = s _ { K } ( E _ { R } )$ , where $s _ { K } ( \cdot )$ is as defined by Lemma 6. Define $e _ { w } ( E _ { R } , e _ { \Omega } )$ as follows:

$$e _ {w} (E _ {R}, e _ {\Omega}) = \frac {\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d} t} e _ {R} = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {3} K _ {i} \mathbf {e} _ {i} \times (e _ {\Omega} \times E _ {R} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \mathbf {e} _ {i}). \tag {61}$$

Define the positive scalar gains $k _ { a 1 }$ and $k _ { a 2 }$ , which make the matrix $\mathcal { A } _ { a } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 \times 2 }$ defined as:

$$
\mathcal {A} _ {a} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} - k _ {a 1} & 1 \\ - k _ {a 2} & 0 \end{array} \right], \tag {62}
$$

a Hurwitz matrix. The ESO designed for the rotational motion is given by:

$$\dot {\hat {R}} = \hat {R} \hat {\Omega} ^ {\times},\dot {\hat {\Omega}} = E _ {R} J ^ {- 1} \left[ J \Omega \times \Omega + \hat {\tau} _ {D} + \tau + k _ {a 1} J \phi_ {1} (\psi_ {a}) + \kappa_ {a} J (e _ {R} ^ {\mathrm{T}} e _ {R}) ^ {\frac {1 - p}{p}} H \Big (e _ {R}, \frac {p - 1}{p} \Big) e _ {w} \right] \tag {63}+ E _ {R} J ^ {- 1} (\kappa_ {a} J e _ {w}) + E _ {R} e _ {\Omega} ^ {\times} E _ {R} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \widehat {\Omega},\dot {\hat {\tau}} _ {D} = J k _ {a 2} \phi_ {2} (\psi_ {a}),$$

where $\psi _ { a }$ is defined as follows:

$$\psi_ {a} = e _ {\Omega} + \kappa_ {a} \left[ e _ {R} + \left(e _ {R} ^ {\mathrm{T}} e _ {R}\right) ^ {\frac {1 - p}{p}} e _ {R} \right], \kappa_ {a} > \frac {1}{2}. \tag {64}$$

In addition, the constant $k _ { a 3 }$ is defined and it occurs in the terms $\phi _ { 1 } ( \psi _ { a } )$ and $\phi _ { 2 } ( \psi _ { a } )$ , where it takes the place of k3 in (10).

Theorem 3. With the observer errors for the rotational ESO defined by (47), the rotational kinematics and dynamics given by (39), and the ESO for rotational motion given in Proposition 2, the error dynamics of the ESO is given by:

$$\dot {E} _ {R} = E _ {R} e _ {\Omega} ^ {\times},J \dot {e} _ {\Omega} = - k _ {a 1} J \phi_ {1} (\psi_ {a}) - \kappa_ {a} J \left[ (e _ {R} ^ {\mathrm{T}} e _ {R}) ^ {\frac {1 - p}{p}} H \Big (e _ {R}, \frac {p - 1}{p} \Big) e _ {w} + e _ {w} \right] + e _ {\tau}, \tag {65}\dot {e} _ {\tau} = - k _ {a 2} J \phi_ {2} (\psi_ {a}) + \dot {\tau} _ {D}.$$

The error dynamics (65) is almost globally FFTS at the origin $( ( E _ { R } , e _ { \Omega } , e _ { \tau } ) = ( I , 0 , 0 ) )$ , when the resultant disturbance torque is constant $( \dot { \tau } _ { D } = 0 )$ and the observer gains are constrained according to Proposition 2.
