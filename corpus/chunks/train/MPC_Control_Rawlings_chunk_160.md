# Exercise 1.38: Expectation and covariance under linear transformations

Consider the random variable $x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ with density $p _ { x }$ and mean and covariance

$$\mathcal {E} (x) = m _ {x} \quad \operatorname{cov} (x) = P _ {x}$$

Consider the random variable $\ b { y } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p }$ defined by the linear transformation

$$y = C x$$

(a) Show that the mean and covariance for y are given by

$$\mathcal {E} (y) = C m _ {x} \quad \operatorname{cov} (y) = C P _ {x} C ^ {\prime}$$

Does this result hold for all C? If yes, prove it; if no, provide a counterexample.

(b) Apply this result to solve Exercise A.35.
