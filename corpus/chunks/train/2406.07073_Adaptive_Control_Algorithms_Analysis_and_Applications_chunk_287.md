$$\operatorname{Prob} \left\{\lim _ {t \rightarrow \infty} \varepsilon (t + d + 1) = E e (t + d + 1) \right\} = 1 \tag {6.77}$$

Taking into account the form of (6.70), Theorem 4.2, together with Lemma 4.1 can also be used observing that the optimal linear prediction error $E e ( t + d + 1 )$ is an innovation process $\omega ( t + d + 1 ) = E e ( t + d + 1 )$ satisfying the martingale type properties:

$$\mathbf {E} \{\omega (t + d + 1) | \mathcal {F} _ {t} \} = 0\mathbf {E} \{\omega^ {2} (t + d + 1) | \mathcal {F} _ {t} \} = \mu^ {2} \leq \left(\sum_ {i = 0} ^ {n _ {E}} e _ {i} ^ {2}\right) \pi^ {2}\lim _ {N \to \infty} \sup \frac {1}{N} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \omega^ {2} (t) < \infty$$

yielding to the same convergence condition (6.76) of the PAA.
