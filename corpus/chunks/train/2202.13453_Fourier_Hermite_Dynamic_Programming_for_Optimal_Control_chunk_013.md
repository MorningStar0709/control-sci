$$
\begin{array}{l} g (y) \approx \mathbb {E} [ g (y) ] + \mathbb {E} [ g (y) H _ {1} (\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)) ] ^ {\top} (\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)) \\ + \frac {1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \left\{\mathbb {E} [ g (y) H _ {2} (\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)) ] \right. \\ \left. \times (\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu) (y - \mu) ^ {\top} \Lambda^ {- \top} - I) \right\}. \tag {25} \\ \end{array}
$$

Let us denote

$$a _ {G} = \mathbb {E} [ g (y) ],b _ {G} = \mathbb {E} [ g (y) H _ {1} (\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)) ], \tag {26}C _ {G} = \mathbb {E} \left[ g (y) H _ {2} \left(\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)\right) \right].$$

Then, (25) can be rewritten as

$$
\begin{array}{l} g (y) \approx a _ {G} - \frac {1}{2} \operatorname{tr} \left\{C _ {G} \right\} + b _ {G} ^ {\top} \left(\Lambda^ {- 1} (y - \mu)\right) \tag {27} \\ + \frac {1}{2} (y - \mu) ^ {\top} [ \Lambda C _ {G} ^ {- 1} \Lambda^ {\top} ] ^ {- 1} (y - \mu). \\ \end{array}
$$

Now, we are ready to apply this approximation to the action-value function.
