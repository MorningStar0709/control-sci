Figure 4: Comparison of formation error for different values of velocity consensus coupling parameter.

To highlight the importance of the velocity consensus factor, we compare the formation error for different values of $c _ { 2 } .$ The formation error is defined as

$$e _ {f} = \sum_ {i, j \in \mathcal {V}} \| p _ {i} - p _ {j} - d _ {i j} \| ^ {2},$$

which is zero only when the desired formation is achieved. Given the second-order dynamics, every agent exhibits inertia. The potential term provides acceleration to the agent until the desired displacement is not achieved. But by the time the desired displacement is achieved, the agent gains certain momentum which causes it to move beyond the desired equilibrium point. If we consider a two-agent system, then with $c _ { 2 } = 0 ,$ , we observe that they will keep oscillating back and forth without ever converging to a single point. Hence the velocity consensus terms help the agents to drive to the desired formation in a stable fashion. The parameter $c _ { 2 }$ also attempts to provide a group velocity to the complete formation. As shown in Fig. $^ { 4 , }$ for $c _ { 2 } = 0 . 0 6 , 0 . 1$ , and 0.2, we see that with lower values of $c _ { 2 } ,$ the agents tend to oscillate. A lower value of $c _ { 2 }$ also increases the chances of internal collision among the agents.
