$$\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}) \in \left[ \inf _ {y \in \Theta} \alpha_ {i} (y), \sup _ {y \in \Theta} \alpha_ {i} (y) \right] =: \mathcal {K}, \quad \text { for any } n, \tag {4.1}$$

with K being compact. Thanks to the fourth display equation in (3.10) we have that $v ( \alpha _ { i } ( \theta ) , i ; \theta ) =$ $v ( \alpha _ { i } ( \theta ^ { n } ) , i ; \theta ^ { n } ) = k _ { 1 }$ for any $n \in \mathbb { N }$ . Hence, using Proposition 3.3 and Lemma 4.2 we can write

$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = v \left(\alpha_ {i} \left(\theta^ {n}\right), i; \theta^ {n}\right) - v \left(\alpha_ {i} (\theta), i; \theta\right) \\ = \left(v (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; \theta^ {n}) - v (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; \theta)\right) + \left(v (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; \theta) - v (\alpha_ {i} (\theta), i; \theta)\right) \\ = - \int_ {\theta^ {n}} ^ {\theta} v _ {\theta} (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; y) d y + \int_ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta)} ^ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n})} v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) d y \\ \end{array}
$$

or, equivalently,

$$\int_ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta)} ^ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n})} \big (- v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) \big) d y = \int_ {\theta^ {n}} ^ {\theta} \big (- v _ {\theta} (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; y) \big) d y. \tag {4.2}$$

By Lemma 4.2 we have $v _ { x } ( x , i ; \theta ) < 0$ for fixed $\theta \in \Theta$ and for any $( x , i ) \in { \mathcal { C } } ^ { \theta }$ , then for $\epsilon \in ( 0 , \delta )$ small enough we have that

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta)} ^ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n})} \big (- v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) \big) d y \geq \int_ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \epsilon} ^ {\alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \delta} \big (- v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) \big) d y \tag {4.3} \\ \geq \min _ {y \in [ \alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \epsilon , \alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \delta ]} | v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) | (\delta - \epsilon) > 0, \\ \end{array}
$$

and

$$\int_ {\theta^ {n}} ^ {\theta} \big (- v _ {\theta} (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; y) \big) d y \leq \max _ {y \in \Theta} | v _ {\theta} (\alpha_ {i} (\theta^ {n}), i; y) | (\theta - \theta^ {n}). \tag {4.4}$$

Combining (4.3) and (4.4) with (4.2) we arrive at the inequality,

$$0 < \min _ {y \in [ \alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \epsilon , \alpha_ {i} (\theta) + \delta ]} | v _ {x} (y, i; \theta) | (\delta - \epsilon) \leq \max _ {(x, y) \in \mathcal {K} \times \Theta} | v _ {\theta} (x, i; y) | (\theta - \theta^ {n}) \leq C (\theta - \theta^ {n}),$$

for a constant C not depending on n (thanks to Lemma 4.2). Thus, letting $n \to \infty$ leads to a contradiction.
