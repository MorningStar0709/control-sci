$$
\begin{array}{l} J ^ {*} (k + 1) - J ^ {*} (k) \leq \tilde {J} (k + 1) - J ^ {*} (k) \\ = - x ^ {* T} (n | k) Q x ^ {*} (n | k) \leq 0, \\ \end{array}
$$

indicating that $x ^ { * } ( n | k )$ converges to the origin as $k  \infty .$ . Consider

$$x ^ {*} (n | k) = A ^ {n} x (k) + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {n - 1} A ^ {i} b u ^ {*} (n - 1 - i | k) \tag {18}= A ^ {n} x (k) + S U _ {[ 0: n - 1 ]} ^ {*} (k), \tag {19}$$

where $S \triangleq [ A ^ { n - 1 } b , \cdots , b ]$ is non-singular, and

$$U _ {[ 0: n - 1 ]} ^ {*} (k) \triangleq [ u ^ {*} (0 | k), \dots , u ^ {*} (n - 1 | k) ] ^ {T}.$$

From (19), it follows that

$$U _ {[ 0: n - 1 ]} ^ {*} (k) = S ^ {- 1} \left(x ^ {*} (n | k) - A ^ {n} x (k)\right),$$

thus

$$u (k) = u ^ {*} (0 | k) = [ 1, 0, \dots , 0 ] _ {1 \times n} U _ {[ 0: n - 1 ]} ^ {*} (k) \tag {20}= [ 1, 0, \dots , 0 ] _ {1 \times n} S ^ {- 1} (x ^ {*} (n | k) - A ^ {n} x (k)). \tag {21}$$

Substituting (21) into (3) yields

$$x (k + 1) = (A - b K _ {d b}) x (k) + B S _ {n} ^ {T} x ^ {*} (n | k), \tag {22}$$

where $K _ { d b } = [ 1 , 0 , \cdots , 0 ] S ^ { - 1 } A ^ { n }$ is the deadbeat gain [23], i.e., all eigenvalues of $\left( A - b K _ { d b } \right)$ are zero; and $S _ { n } ^ { T } =$ $[ 1 , 0 , \cdots , 0 ] S ^ { - 1 }$ . Therefore, (22) implies that $x ( k ) = 0$ is asymptotically stable.

Note that x(k) approaches the origin asymptotically. Hence, after some finite time $T _ { 1 } > 0$ , the state enters a small neighborhood of the origin where constraints (4) are inherently fulfilled, reducing the constrained problem to its unconstrained counterpart, i.e.,

$$u ^ {*} (i | k) = - K _ {d b} x ^ {*} (i | k),$$

such that

$$x ^ {*} (i | k) = 0, \forall i \geq n,$$

and $J ( k ) = 0$ in (15) reaches its minimum. Consequently,

$$u (k) = - K _ {d b} x ^ {*} (0 | k) = - K _ {d b} x (k),$$

such that

$$x (k + 1) = (A - b K _ {d b}) x (k),$$

and its state converges to the origin in finite time $T = T _ { 1 } +$ $n < + \infty$ . 
