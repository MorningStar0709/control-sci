# First step

We set $I : \mathcal { M } \times \mathcal { B }  \mathbb { R }$ as

$$
\begin{array}{l} I (\mu , A) = \int_ {A} \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \mu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} - \int_ {A} \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (x, y, \mu) \mathrm{dy}\right) \mu (\mathrm{dx}) \quad \text {for any} \quad \mu \in \mathcal {M} \quad \text {and} \quad A \in \mathcal {B}. \tag {65} \\ := I _ {1} (\mu , A) - I _ {2} (\mu , A) \\ \end{array}
$$

We show that there is a constant $L _ { \scriptscriptstyle I } > 0$ such that

$$\left\| I (\mu , \cdot) - I (\nu , \cdot) \right\| \leq L _ {I} \| \mu - \nu \| \text { for any } \mu \in \mathcal {M}. \tag {66}$$

Fix $\mu , \nu \in \mathcal { M } _ { 2 }$ . We have ( formally represents a Lebesgue measure)

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\| I _ {1} (\mu , \cdot) - I _ {1} (\nu , \cdot) \right\| = \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \mu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} - \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \nu) \nu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} \right\| \\ \leq \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \mu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} - \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \nu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} \right\| \\ + \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \nu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} - \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \nu) \nu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{dx} \right\| \\ = \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} a (z, x, \mu) \left\{\mu (\mathrm{dz}) - \nu (\mathrm{dz}) \right\}\right) \mathrm{dx} \right\| \tag {67} \\ + \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} \left\{a (z, x, \mu) - a (z, x, \nu) \right\} \nu (\mathrm{dz})\right) \mathrm{d} x \right\| \\ \leq \left\| \mu - \nu \right\| + \left\| \left(\int_ {\Omega} \left\{a (z, x, \mu) - a (z, x, \nu) \right\} \nu (\mathrm{d} z)\right) \mathrm{d} x \right\| \\ \leq \left\| \mu - \nu \right\| + \left\| \nu \right\| \sup _ {x, z \in \Omega} \left| a (z, x, \mu) - a (z, x, \nu) \right| \\ \leq \left\| \mu - \nu \right\| + 2 L _ {a} \left\| \mu - \nu \right\| \\ \end{array}
$$

where we used $\left| a \right| \leq 1$ $\left\| \nu \right\| \leq 2$ , and $\int _ { \Omega } \mathrm { d } x = 1$ . Hence, we obtain

$$\left\| I _ {1} (\mu , \cdot) - I _ {1} (\nu , \cdot) \right\| \leq (2 L _ {a} + 1) \| \mu - \nu \|. \tag {68}$$
