$$\dot {m} (t) = \frac {\frac {\mathrm{d} \| \epsilon (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t} \| y (t) \| - \| \epsilon (t) \| \frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \| ^ {2}}\dot {m} (t) \leq \frac {\frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \|} + \frac {\| \epsilon (t) \| \frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \| ^ {2}}\dot {m} (t) \leq \frac {\frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \|} + \frac {(\alpha \beta + \beta - 1) \| y (t) \| \frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \| ^ {2}}\dot {m} (t) \leq \beta (\alpha + 1) \frac {\frac {\mathrm{d} \| y (t) \|}{\mathrm{d} t}}{\| y (t) \|}. \tag {24}$$

We have $\epsilon ( t ) = \beta y ( t _ { k } ) - y ( t ) \geq 0$ , thus

$$\epsilon \left(t _ {k}\right) = \beta y \left(t _ {k}\right) - y \left(t _ {k}\right),\epsilon \left(t _ {k}\right) = (\beta - 1) y \left(t _ {k}\right). \tag {27}$$

$$m \left(t _ {k}\right) = \frac {\left\| \epsilon \left(t _ {k}\right) \right\|}{\left\| y \left(t _ {k}\right) \right\|} = \beta - 1. \tag {28}$$

$$\int_ {t _ {k}} ^ {t} \dot {m} (t) \mathrm{d} \tau \leq \int_ {t _ {k}} ^ {t} \beta (\alpha + 1) \| A \| \mathrm{d} \tau .$$

From the event rule (3), it can be assured that at the next sampling instance, we have

$$t _ {k + 1} - t _ {k} \geq \frac {\alpha}{(\alpha + 1) \| A \|}. \tag {32}$$

$$\frac {\alpha}{(\alpha + 1) \| A \|} > 0, \tag {33}$$
