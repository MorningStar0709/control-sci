# 2.3. Model Predictive Control for Navigation

This section introduces the MPC algorithm for geomagnetic navigation by utilizing the mapless geomagnetic navigation method theory.

The model input is denoted as $U ( k ) = ( v _ { x k } , v _ { y k } ) ^ { T }$ , where $v _ { x k }$ and $v _ { y k }$ represent the velocity components at time k along the x and y directions, respectively. Wherein the magnetic declination D and the magnetic inclination I are designated as state variables $S ( k ) = ( D _ { k } , I _ { k } ) ^ { T }$ . Specifically, $D _ { k }$ denotes the magnetic declination at time k, while $I _ { k }$ represents the magnetic inclination at time k. The uncertainty model is established on the zero-order holder with a specified sampling period of T and transformed into the following discrete form as follows (Choi et al., 2022):

$$S (k + 1) = A S (k) + B (k) U (k), \tag {13}A = I _ {2}, \tag {14}B (k) = G (k) T, \tag {15}$$

Prediction is performed over a prediction interval of a given length of N. For the convenience of subsequent calculations, the predicted vectors at each time step are calculated as follows:

$$
\bar {S} (k + 1) = \left( \begin{array}{c} S (k + 1 | k) \\ S (k + 2 | k) \\ \vdots \\ S (k + N | k) \end{array} \right), \tag {16}

\bar {U} (k + 1) = \left( \begin{array}{c} U (k | k) \\ U (k + 1 | k) \\ \vdots \\ U (k + N - 1 | k) \end{array} \right), \tag {17}
$$

where $U ( k + i | k )$ and $S ( k + i | k )$ represent the system input and state vectors predicted at time $k ,$ where $i = 1 , 2 , \cdots , N$ .

The geomagnetic values of magnetic declination and magnetic inclination do not change significantly within $1 ^ { \circ }$ (approximately 111.32 km) (Gavoret et al., 1986). Therefore, $G ( k )$ and $B ( k )$ are considered constant within the prediction interval. The initial conditions at time $k$ is as follows:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} S (k | k) = S (k) \\ U (k | k) = U (k). \end{array} \right. \tag {18}
$$

The state $S ( k + i | k )$ predicted at time k is as follows:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} S (k + 1 | k) = A S (k | k) + B U (k | k) \\ S (k + 2 | k) = A S (k + 1 | k) + B U (k + 1 | k) \\ \vdots \\ S (k + N | k) = A S (k + N - 1 | k) + B U (k + N - 1 | k), \end{array} \right. \tag {19}
$$

where, the expression for $\bar { S } ( k )$ and ${ \bar { U } } ( k )$ are as follows:

$$\bar {S} (k + 1) = M S (k) + C (k) \bar {U} (k), \tag {20}
M = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c} A & A ^ {2} & \dots & A ^ {N} \end{array} \right) ^ {T}, \tag {21}
