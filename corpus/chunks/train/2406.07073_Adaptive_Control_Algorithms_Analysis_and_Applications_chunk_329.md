# 7.6 Minimum Variance Tracking and Regulation

This strategy concerns the design of a controller ensuring a minimum variance of the controlled variable (plant output) around the reference, in the presence of random disturbances. In a stochastic environment it is assumed that the plant and disturbance are described by the following ARMAX model:

$$A (q ^ {- 1}) y (t + d + 1) = B ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + C (q ^ {- 1}) e (t + d + 1) \tag {7.149}$$

where $e ( t )$ is a sequence of normally distributed independent random variables of zero mean and finite variance $\sigma ^ { 2 }$ (white noise) and the zeros of $C ( z ^ { - 1 } )$ ) are in $| z | < 1$ . The hypotheses made upon the plant model are those made for tracking and regulation with independent objectives. The main restrictive assumption is the requirement that $B ^ { \star } ( z ^ { - 1 } )$ has all its zeros inside the unit circle (hypothesis (H3)). The objective is to compute for the plant and disturbance model (7.149) the control $u ( t )$ :

$$u (t) = f _ {u} (y (t), y (t - 1), \dots , u (t - 1), u (t - 2), \dots)$$

which minimizes the following criterion

$$
\begin{array}{l} \min _ {u (t)} J (t + d + 1) = \mathbf {E} \{[ y (t + d + 1) - y ^ {\star} (t + d + 1) ] ^ {2} \} \\ \approx \lim _ {N \rightarrow \infty} \frac {1}{N} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {N} \left\{\left[ y (t + d + 1) - y ^ {\star} (t + d + 1) \right] ^ {2} \right\} \tag {7.150} \\ \end{array}
$$

For the case $y ^ { \star } ( t + d + 1 ) = 0$ , the criterion (7.150) becomes:

$$\min _ {u (t)} J (t + d + 1) = \mathbf {E} \{y ^ {2} (t + d + 1) \}$$

and corresponds to the original formulation of the minimum variance control (Åström and Wittenmark 1973). To solve this problem, in addition to the plant model, the disturbance model should be known.
