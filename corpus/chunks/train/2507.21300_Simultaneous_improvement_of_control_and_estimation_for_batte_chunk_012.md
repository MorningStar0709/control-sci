# C. Algorithm

The objective (16) is quadratic in $\ v { x } _ { k } ^ { p }$ and (linear in) $\Sigma _ { k | k } ^ { p } ,$ but the dynamic constraint (15) is generally nonconvex. This makes the problem belong to the realm of nonlinear optimization, in particular, a nonlinear model predictive control (MPC) problem. We first list the certainty equivalence linear MPC problem that omits the OCV models, then use its solution to compare against that of the nonlinear MPC.

Problem 1. Linear MPC in the SOC dynamics (certainty

equivalence, no OCV model)

$$\min _ {U _ {k _ {0}} ^ {k _ {0} + N}, \{x _ {k} \} _ {k = k _ {0}} ^ {k _ {0} + N}} \mathbb {E} \sum_ {k = k _ {0}} ^ {k _ {0} + N} \left(c \left(q ^ {\top} x _ {k} - r _ {k}\right) ^ {2} + I _ {k} ^ {\top} R I _ {k}\right)$$

subject to:

$$x _ {k + 1} = x _ {k} + \operatorname{diag} (\left\{\frac {\eta^ {i} \Delta t}{Q _ {n o m} ^ {i}}: i = 1, 2, \dots , n \right\}) I _ {k},I _ {k} ^ {i} \in [ I _ {\text { min }} ^ {i}, I _ {\text { max }} ^ {i} ], \quad x _ {k} ^ {i} \in [ 0, 1 ], \tag {17}i = 1, 2, \dots , n, \quad k = k _ {0}, k _ {0} + 1, \dots , k _ {0} + N,$$

where $I _ { m i n } ^ { i }$ and $I _ { m a x } ^ { i }$ are the minimum and maximum allowable currents.

Problem 2. Nonlinear MPC in the information state $( x _ { k } ^ { p } , \Sigma _ { k | k } ^ { p } )$

$$\min _ {U _ {k _ {0}} ^ {k _ {0} + N}, \{(x _ {k} ^ {p}, \Sigma_ {k | k} ^ {p}) \} _ {k = k _ {0}} ^ {k _ {0} + N}} \hat {J} _ {k _ {0}}$$

subject to (15) and (17).

Problem 2 is nonlinear and nonconvex and can be approached by some nonlinear solver, say a Newton method. For simplicity and computational efficiency, we rely on Algorithm 1, which constructs a linear approximation of the information state dynamics instead of solving the full nonlinear MPC problem. The detailed implementation can be found in our Julia code at https://github.com/msramada/batterydual-control.
