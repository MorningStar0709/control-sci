# Behavioral Cloning of a Geometric Controller

For simplicity, we have trained a NN controller by behavioural cloning the geometric pose-stabilization controller introduced by Kanayama et al. [17], which regulates the robot’s motion via longitudinal and lateral error components. Such a controller will provide the baseline neural network implementation to investigate the proposed Trojan attack. The geometric controller can be summarized as follows:

Let $( x _ { d } , y _ { d } )$ be the desired position and $( x _ { r } ( t ) , y _ { r } ( t ) , \theta ( t ) )$ be the robot’s current pose. The Cartesian tracking errors are first computed in the world frame:

$$\Delta x (t) = x _ {d} - x _ {r} (t), \quad \Delta y (t) = y _ {d} - y _ {r} (t). \tag {5}$$

These errors are then transformed into the robot’s bodyfixed coordinate frame:

$$e _ {x} (t) = \cos \theta (t) \Delta x (t) + \sin \theta (t) \Delta y (t), \tag {6}e _ {y} (t) = - \sin \theta (t) \Delta x (t) + \cos \theta (t) \Delta y (t). \tag {7}$$

The geometric controller defines the forward linear velocity v and angular velocity w as:

$$v (t) = k _ {x} e _ {x} (t), \quad w (t) = k _ {y} e _ {y} (t), \tag {8}$$

where $k _ { x }$ and $k _ { y }$ are positive control gains. From $v ( t )$ and $\omega ( t )$ , the right and left wheel angular velocities are computed by inverting the relation (1).
