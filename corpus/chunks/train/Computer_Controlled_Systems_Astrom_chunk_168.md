# Example 3.2 Stability of a second-order system

Let the characteristic equation be

$$A (z) = z ^ {2} + a _ {1} z + a _ {2} = 0 \tag {3.5}$$

Jury's scheme is

$$
\begin{array}{c c c} 1 & a _ {1} & a _ {2} \\ a _ {2} & a _ {1} & 1 \\ \hline 1 - a _ {2} ^ {2} & a _ {1} (1 - a _ {2}) \\ a _ {1} (1 - a _ {2}) & 1 - a _ {2} ^ {2} \\ \hline 1 - a _ {2} ^ {2} - \frac {a _ {1} ^ {2} (1 - a _ {2})}{1 + a _ {2}} \end{array} \quad \begin{array}{c c c} \alpha_ {2} = a _ {2} \\ \alpha_ {1} = \frac {a _ {1}}{1 + a _ {2}} \end{array}
$$

All the roots of Eq. (3.5) are inside the unit circle if

$$
\begin{array}{r l} 1 - a _ {2} ^ {2} & > 0 \\ \frac {1 - a _ {2}}{1 + a _ {2}} \left((1 + a _ {2}) ^ {2} - a _ {1} ^ {2}\right) & > 0 \end{array}
$$

This gives the conditions

$$
\begin{array}{l} a _ {2} <   1 \\ a _ {2} > - 1 + a _ {1} \\ a _ {2} > - 1 - a _ {1} \\ \end{array}
$$
