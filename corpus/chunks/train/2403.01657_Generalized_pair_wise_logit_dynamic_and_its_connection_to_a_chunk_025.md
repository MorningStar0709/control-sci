# 3.2 Myopic version

Following the scaling approach (Barker, 2019; Barker et al., 2022), we characterize the GPL dynamic as a version of the MFG. To this aim, for each small increment $h > 0$ of time, we set the scaled objective

$$J _ {h} (t, x, a) = \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} \int_ {\Omega} F (a _ {s} (y)) \mathrm{d} y \mathrm{d} s + \frac {1}{h} \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} G (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right]. \tag {39}$$

The scaling by $h ^ { - 1 }$ in the second term, which is motivated from the model predictive control wherein the current control applies, is designed using the prediction for the near future (Barker, 2019). To proceed further, we assume $G = U$ with (15) and that the law of is stationary and, again, that the control is Markovian. We infer an approximation of the right-hand side of (39):

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} \int_ {\Omega} F (a (X _ {s}, y)) \mathrm{d} y \mathrm{d} s + \frac {1}{h} \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} G (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right] \\ = \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} \int_ {\Omega} F (a (X _ {s}, y)) \mathrm{d} y \mathrm{d} s + \frac {1}{h} \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} U (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right]. \tag {40} \\ \approx h \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {\Omega} F (a (X _ {t}, y)) \mathrm{d} y + \frac {1}{h} U (X _ {t + h}, \mu (t, \cdot)) \mid X _ {t} = x \right] \\ \end{array}
$$

For bounded and sufficiently smooth $\phi = \phi \mathopen { } \mathclose \bgroup ( t , x \aftergroup \egroup ) : \mathopen { } \mathclose \bgroup [ 0 , + \infty \aftergroup \egroup ) \times \Omega  \mathbb { R }$ , Itô’s lemma (e.g., Theorem 1.14 of Øksendal and Sulem (2019)) implies

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ \phi (X _ {t + h}) | X _ {t} = x \right] \approx \phi (x) + h \int_ {\Omega} a (x, y) (\phi (y) - \phi (x)) d y. \tag {41}$$

By (15), if $U ( \cdot , \mu )$ is sufficiently smooth, then we infer

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {1}{h} \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} \int_ {\Omega} F (a (X _ {s}, y)) \mathrm{d} y \mathrm{d} s + \frac {1}{h} \int_ {t} ^ {t + h} U (X _ {s}, \mu) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right] \\ \approx \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {\Omega} F (a (x, y)) \mathrm{d} y + \frac {1}{h} (U (x, \mu) + h \int_ {\Omega} a (x, y) (U (y, \mu) - U (x, \mu)) \mathrm{d} y) \mid X _ {t} = x \right]. \tag {42} \\ = \frac {1}{h} U (x, \mu) + \int_ {\Omega} \left\{- F (a (x, y)) + a (x, y) (U (y, \mu) - U (x, \mu)) \right\} d y \\ \end{array}
$$
