1. Asymptotic optimality. We prove the convergence of our learning-based policy to the optimal policy. We first focus on the Erlang-B queueing system, i.e., an $M / M / k / k + N$ system with $N = 0$ ,—see Section 3.2.1—, where using an intricate argument based on martingale sequences, we establish asymptotic optimality for our learning rule. In Section 4.2.1, we show that the argument and the result generalize to the $M / M / k / k + N$ system with $N > 0$ .

2. Finite-time performance analysis. Next we characterize the finite-time regret of our learning in the two distinct service rate regimes relevant to our system. Once again, we start by focusing on the Erlang-B queueing system—see Section 3.2.2. In the high service-rate regime, we show finite regret, and in the low service-rate regime, the exploration done by our policy leads to a regret upper bound that scales as log(n), where n is the number of arrivals. The analysis for the multi-server setting is based on Doob’s decomposition and concentration inequalities for martingale sequences. Further, on Section 4.2.2, using the same proof methodology, we show that the results generalize to the $M / M / k / k + N$ system with $N > 0$ .
