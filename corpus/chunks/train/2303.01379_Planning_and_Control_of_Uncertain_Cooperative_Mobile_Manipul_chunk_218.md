In this chapter we introduce and formalize the concept of distance and bearing rigidity (abbreviated as D&B Rigidity in the following). This is motivated by the fusion of both distance and pose constraints in the cooperative grasping problem. D&B Rigidity in SE(3) aims at studying the problem of under what conditions the geometric pattern of a network can be uniquely determined if both the bearing and the distance of each edge in the network is fixed. In this direction, we focus on the notion of infinitesimal rigidity for D&B frameworks. We first formally define a D&B framework in SE(3), similarly to Section E.2 of Appendix E:
