Remark 6: It can be observed that, as long as condition (30) is satisfied, the proposed feedback optimization scheme (29) mitigates the timescale separation requirement imposed in the results of [12]. An important consequence of this is that, contrary to the results in [12], the variations of θ in our setting no longer need to be measured relative to a highly conservative timescale separation parameter. Another important relaxation made in Theorem 2 relative to [12] is that Theorem 2 does not make any assumptions on the degree of homogeneity in either subsystem. Indeed, the fixed-time feedback optimization scheme presented in [12] requires that the homogeneity degree of the plant in the 0-limit be smaller than that of the controller, while the homogeneity degree of the plant in the ∞-limit be larger than the corresponding homogeneity degree of the controller (see [3]). Meanwhile, Theorem 2 circumvents this requirement by instead only requiring that (30) holds. □
