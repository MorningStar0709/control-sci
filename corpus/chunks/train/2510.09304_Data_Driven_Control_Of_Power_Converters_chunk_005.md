In the present case, this model can be derived without too much difficulties as it remains a simple architecture, but this converter remains a good example of the complexity of the control task as the system is bilinear and subject to input saturation. This is usually handled by linearizing the model around a desired equilibrium point, the control design is then performed on the subsequent linear model. However, this does not allow to ensure that the performance and stability requirements are going to be met when the equilibrium point changes due to variations in the load or the input voltage. Lyapunov-based approach have shown promising results, see [13] for instance, but they control directly the commutations of the switching devices, which make them not suited for PWM-based control, and their scalability is limited as the architecture becomes more complex and the state dimension increases. The next paragraph investigates the use of a datadriven control technique to overcome these challenges.
