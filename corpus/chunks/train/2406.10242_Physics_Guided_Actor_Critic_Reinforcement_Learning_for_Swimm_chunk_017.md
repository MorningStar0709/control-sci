# D. Optimal Stationary Control of BK flow

The control strategy discussed so far was not optimal, but prescribed. In the case of the BK flow, and as discussed in more details in [1], we can also find the optimal stationary control — that is one which solves Eq. (1) at $T \to \infty$ in the case of no penalty for the special case of the (negative) reward function

$$r \left(\boldsymbol {s} (t ^ {\prime}), \boldsymbol {a} (t ^ {\prime})\right) := - \left\| \boldsymbol {a} (t ^ {\prime}) \right\| _ {2} ^ {2} - \beta \left\| \boldsymbol {s} (t ^ {\prime}) \right\| _ {2} ^ {2}, \tag {15}$$

most suitable for the particle problem – where we balance of the quadratic cost of action with the quadratic cost penalizing quadratically for increase in the inter-particle distance.

Considering the steady state (infinite horizon) and no discount and utilizing Eq. (15) while also assuming that $\phi > \phi ^ { ( s ) } = \tilde { D } / 2$ , where $\tilde { D } = D ( d + 2 ) ( d - 1 )$ , we arrive at

$$
\begin{array}{l} \phi^ {*} = \arg \min _ {\phi} \left[ (\phi^ {2} + \beta) \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} s ^ {2} P (s | \phi) \mathrm{d} s \right] \\ = \arg \min _ {\phi} \frac {\phi^ {2} + \beta}{2 \phi - \tilde {D}} = \frac {\tilde {D} + \sqrt {4 \beta + \tilde {D} ^ {2}}}{2}. \tag {16} \\ \end{array}
$$
