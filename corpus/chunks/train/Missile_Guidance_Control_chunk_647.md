$$\frac {d \mathbf {v}}{d t} = (c m / M) \mathbf {u} - (\mu / r ^ {3}) \mathbf {r},\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t} = \mathbf {v},\frac {d M}{d t} = - m,$$

where

$$\mathbf {r} = (r, 0, 0) \text { is the radius vector },\mathbf {v} = (v _ {1}, v _ {2}, v _ {3}) \text { is the velocity vector, }\boldsymbol {u} = (u _ {1}, u _ {2}, u _ {3}) \text { is a unit thrust vector, }M = \text { mass of the rocket },m = \text { mass - flow rate } (0 \leq m \leq d ^ {2} m / d t ^ {2}),c = \text { exhaust velocity }.$$

The components of all vectors are given in a spherical coordinate system $( r , \theta , \varphi )$ with the origin at the attracting center. The above equations can be used as a basis for further study, depending on the needs and/or application of the user.
