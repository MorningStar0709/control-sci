$$f (\mathbf {y} \mid \mathbf {x}) = \mathbb {E} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left[ \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} \delta (X _ {t + h} ^ {(i)} - y _ {i}) \right], \tag {2.4}$$

where $\delta ( \cdot )$ is the Dirac Delta function.

Proof From the sifting property of the Dirac Delta function, we have the following calculations:

$$\mathbb {E} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left[ \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} \delta (X _ {t + h} ^ {(i)} - y _ {i}) \right] = \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {n}} \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} \delta (u _ {i} - y _ {i}) \cdot f (\mathbf {u} | \mathbf {x}) d \mathbf {u} = f (\mathbf {y} | \mathbf {x}),$$

where in the second equality is obtained from the sifting property of the Dirac Delta function. This completes the proof. 

Remark 2.1. Note that in (2.4), we just need the one-dimensional Dirac Delta function. The intuitive reason can be seen from the above proof: as we gradually integrate out the dummy variables one by one while freezing the other dummy variables. This turns out to be convenient for us to provide an explicit representation of the multi-variate transition density function of the multi-dimensional Markov process.

Now we move on to extend the representation (2.3) to the multi-dimensional case. Substituting the representation (2.2) for each Dirac Delta function into equation (2.4), we obtain the following explicit representation of the transition density function of the multidimensional Markov process X:

$$
\begin{array}{l} f (\mathbf {y} \mid \mathbf {x}) = \mathbb {E} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left[ \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} \delta (X _ {t + h} ^ {(i)} - y _ {i}) \right] = \mathbb {E} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left[ \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} \left(\sum_ {k _ {i} = 0} ^ {\infty} g _ {k _ {i}} ^ {(i)} (X _ {t + h} ^ {(i)}) g _ {k _ {i}} ^ {(i)} (y _ {i})\right) \right] \\ = \sum_ {k _ {1}, \dots , k _ {n} = 0} ^ {\infty} \left(\prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} g _ {k _ {i}} ^ {(i)} (y _ {i})\right) \mathbb {E} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left[ \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {d} g _ {k _ {i}} ^ {(i)} \left(X _ {t + h} ^ {(i)}\right) \right]. \tag {2.5} \\ \end{array}
$$
