# B. Parameter Optimization Based on the PSO Algorithm

The PSO algorithm is a type of heuristic algorithms characterized by stochastic optimization, memory retention, parallel computing, and global searching capabilities [45], [46]. Its advantages of fast convergence, few parameters, and simple steps render it suitable for the high-dimensional nonlinear optimization problem investigated in this section.

In this parameter optimization problem, the particle position represents the parameter group corresponding to the fitness value $\mu ,$ and the particle velocity represents parameter updates causing the change of $\mu .$ After stochastic initialization, all particles iteratively update their velocities v and positions z by tracking personal and global best positions, expressed as

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {v} _ {i} ^ {m + 1} = w ^ {m} \boldsymbol {v} _ {i} ^ {m} + c _ {1} \boldsymbol {r} _ {1, i} ^ {m} \left(\boldsymbol {z} _ {p b, i} ^ {m} - \boldsymbol {z} _ {i} ^ {m}\right) + c _ {2} \boldsymbol {r} _ {2, i} ^ {m} \left(\boldsymbol {z} _ {g b, i} ^ {m} - \boldsymbol {z} _ {i} ^ {m}\right) \\ \boldsymbol {z} _ {i} ^ {m + 1} = \boldsymbol {z} _ {i} ^ {m} + \boldsymbol {v} _ {i} ^ {m + 1} \end{array} \right. \tag {33}
$$

where the superscript m denotes the mth iteration, the subscript i denotes the ith particle, w is the inertia weight, $c _ { 1 }$ and $c _ { 2 }$ are acceleration coefficients, $\mathbf { \Delta } _ { \mathbf { r } _ { 1 } }$ and $\mathbf { \boldsymbol { r } } _ { 2 }$ are vectors comprising random numbers in the interval (0, 1), and $z _ { p b }$ and $z _ { g b }$ represent position vectors corresponding to the particles’ personal and global best values of $\mu ,$ respectively.

The PSO algorithm terminates when m reaches the maximum number of iterations or the fitness value $\mu$ converges, obtaining the global optimal solution for the parameter group and corresponding fitness value.
