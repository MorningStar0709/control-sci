Section II introduces the framework of energy shaping and damping injection as the interconnection of the robot with virtual spring and dampers. Working in operation space, this allows for intuitive considerations on the shaping of the robot potential energy. It also allows to draw a simple comparison with impedance control. Section III discusses operation space impedance control, building the connection between closedloop impedance shaping and robust control synthesis. The main contribution of the paper is in Section IV, which provides the design method based on linear matrix inequalities to synthesize automatically stiffness and damping parameters of our impedance controller. The synthesis is driven by $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ gain criteria. Our approach is illustrated on a 2-link and on a 7-link manipulator in Section V. Conclusions follow.
