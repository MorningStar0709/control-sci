It remains to comment on the value q by which the finite set and the mapping (11) are parameterized. Roughly speaking, the supported level (i.e., the number of consecutive encrypted multiplications) but also the computational effort (of encryptions, homomorphic operations, and decryptions) increase with $q$ while the risk of overflow decreases with $q .$ Hence, q should be chosen large enough to support sufficiently many consecutive operations and to avoid overflows but otherwise as small as possible for computational efficiency.
