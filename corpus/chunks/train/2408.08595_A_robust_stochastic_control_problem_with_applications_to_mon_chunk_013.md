$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \mathcal {E} \left(\int_ {t} ^ {T} - h _ {s} ^ {- 1} L _ {s} ^ {\prime} d W _ {s}\right) ^ {p} \right] = \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \left(\frac {h _ {t}}{h _ {T}}\right) ^ {p} \exp \left(p \int_ {t} ^ {T} [ - A _ {s} + (D _ {s} ^ {- 1} B _ {s}) ^ {\prime} C _ {s} + (D _ {s} ^ {- 1} B _ {s}) ^ {\prime} h _ {s} ^ {- 1} L _ {s} ] d s\right) \right] \\ \leqslant K \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \exp \left(K \int_ {t} ^ {T} (| A _ {s} | + | L _ {s} |) d s\right) \right] \\ \leqslant K \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \exp \left(\int_ {t} ^ {T} \left(\frac {\varepsilon^ {2} A _ {s} ^ {2}}{2} + \frac {\varepsilon^ {2} | L _ {s} | ^ {2}}{2} + \frac {K}{\varepsilon^ {2}}\right) d s\right) \right] \\ \leqslant K \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \exp \left(\int_ {t} ^ {T} \varepsilon^ {2} A _ {s} ^ {2} d s\right) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbb {E} _ {t} \left[ \exp \left(\int_ {t} ^ {T} \varepsilon^ {2} | L _ {s} | ^ {2} d s\right) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \leq K. \tag {3.12} \\ \end{array}
$$

where K denotes a constant that may vary from line to line; we used the boundedness of $h , B , C , D$ in the first inequality and John-Nirenberg Inequality to BMO-martingales $\int _ { 0 } ^ { \cdot } \varepsilon A _ { s } d W _ { s }$ and $\int _ { 0 } ^ { \cdot } \varepsilon L _ { s } d W _ { s }$ in the last inequality.

Next, we introduce the following backward stochastic differential equation:

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} d Y _ {t} = \left[ - | D _ {t} ^ {- 1} B _ {t} + h _ {t} ^ {- 1} L _ {t} | ^ {2} Y _ {t} + 2 (D _ {t} ^ {- 1} B _ {t} + h _ {t} ^ {- 1} L _ {t}) ^ {\prime} Z _ {t} \right] d t + Z _ {t} ^ {\prime} d W _ {t}, \\ Y _ {T} = 1, \end{array} \right. \tag {3.13}
