# A. Mean and covariance dynamics

We shifted from a cost in the partially observed state $x _ { k }$ in (5) into a cost in the fully observed ’information state’, represented by the pair $\left( \boldsymbol { x } _ { k | k } , \boldsymbol { \Sigma } _ { k | k } \right)$ . The latter description is fully observed as it can be tracked and calculated from a Bayesian filter. A natural choice here is the eKF, as it is one of the minimalist algorithms to approximate the evolution of the first two central moments.

In addition to (7), we define

$$x _ {k + 1 | k} = \mathbb {E} \left\{x _ {k + 1} \mid Z _ {k _ {0}} ^ {k}, u _ {k} \right\},\Sigma_ {k + 1 \mid k} = \mathbb {E} \left\{\left[ x _ {k + 1} - x _ {k + 1 \mid k} \right] \left[ x _ {k + 1} - x _ {k + 1 \mid k} \right] ^ {\top} \mid Z _ {k _ {0}} ^ {k}, u _ {k} \right\}. \tag {11}$$

The eKF recursion is defined by

$$x _ {k + 1 \mid k} = f (x _ {k \mid k}, I _ {k}), \quad \Sigma_ {k + 1 \mid k} = F _ {k} \Sigma_ {k \mid k} F _ {k} ^ {\top} + \Sigma_ {w}, \tag {12}$$

where

$$x _ {k \mid k} = x _ {k \mid k - 1} + \Omega_ {k} \left[ y _ {k} - h (x _ {k \mid k - 1}) \right],\Sigma_ {k \mid k} = \left[ I - \Omega_ {k} H _ {k} \right] \Sigma_ {k \mid k - 1},\Omega_ {k} = \Sigma_ {k | k - 1} H _ {k} ^ {\top} \left[ H _ {k} \Sigma_ {k | k - 1} H _ {k} ^ {\top} + \Sigma_ {v} \right] ^ {- 1},F _ {k} = \left. \frac {\partial f (x , I _ {k})}{\partial x} \right| _ {x _ {k | k}} = \mathbb {I} _ {n \times n}, \tag {13}H _ {k} = \left. \frac {\partial h (x)}{\partial x} \right| _ {x _ {k | k - 1}},= \operatorname{diag} (\left\{\frac {\partial h ^ {i} (x)}{\partial x} | _ {x _ {k | k - 1} ^ {i}}: i = 1, \dots , n \right\}),$$

and the recursion is initialized by $x _ { k _ { 0 } | k _ { 0 } } , \Sigma _ { k _ { 0 } | k _ { 0 } }$ , given at the beginning of each time-step.

It is important to note that the eKF results from applying first-order approximations (the Jacobians $F _ { k }$ and $H _ { k } )$ on nonlinear systems to reach a similar form to the Kalman filter, with $\Omega _ { k }$ mimicking the Kalman gain [24]. Hence, although the cost description in (10) is exact, the central moments provided by the eKF are only approximates and not exact.

The variables $x _ { k | k } , \Sigma _ { k | k }$ are random since they are functions of the random observation sequence $Y _ { k _ { 0 } } ^ { k }$ (not yet available at the current time $k _ { 0 } )$ . The expectation in (10) averages over all possible future observations, given by the random variable $Y _ { k _ { 0 } } ^ { k }$ . Next, we employ an important approximation to omit this expectation, and work with a deterministic surrogate instead.
