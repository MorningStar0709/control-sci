# 3.2 PAA—Examples

The a priori adjustable predictor is given in the general case by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + 1) = - \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {A}} \hat {a} _ {i} (t) y (t + 1 - i) + \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {B}} \hat {b} _ {1} (t) u (t - d - i + 1) \\ = \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) \phi (t) \tag {3.63} \\ \end{array}
$$

in which:

$$\hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) = [ \hat {a} _ {1} (t), \dots , \hat {a} _ {n _ {A}} (t), \hat {b} _ {1} (t), \dots , \hat {b} _ {n _ {B}} (t) ] \tag {3.64}$$

and for the estimation of ${ \hat { \theta } } ( t )$ , the algorithm given in (3.51) through (3.54) (or in (3.46) through (3.48)) is used, with the appropriate dimension for $\hat { \theta } ( t ) , \phi ( t )$ and F (t).
