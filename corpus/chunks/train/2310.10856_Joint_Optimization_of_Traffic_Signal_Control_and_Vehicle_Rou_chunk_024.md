# 3.3.2. Action definition

At control step ??, RA ?? selects a route for vehicles positioned upstream of the route divergence points corresponding to the OD pair. RA ?? 's action space consists of the available route options for these vehicles. In the specific example network, RA 2 has two route options: Route1 and Route2. This means RA 2 can choose between Route1 and Route2 for vehicles positioned upstream of divergence points at control step ??.

Due to the edge capacity, the number of these vehicles during one control step is usually not large, and some of them may still be on the edge after this control step, which will follow the next action at the subsequent control step. Therefore, it is reasonable to choose the same route for vehicles during one

control step.

$$a _ {i, t} = a _ {i, t} ^ {r}, a _ {i, t} ^ {r} \in \text { Route } _ {i}, \forall i \in C _ {R} \tag {5.}$$
