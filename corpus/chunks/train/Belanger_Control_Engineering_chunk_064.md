iv. Energy balance. The heat energy contained by the tank is $H(t) = \rho Vc_{v}T(t)$ . During the interval $\Delta t$ , the inflow conveys $F\rho c_{v}T_{0}\Delta t$ joules while $F\rho c_{v}T\Delta t$ joules are taken out. The reaction produces $r\Delta H\Delta t$ , and $Q\Delta t$ is added. Write $H(t + \Delta t)$ as $H(t) + \text{net energy into the tank during } \Delta t$ . Let $\Delta t \to 0$ , and generate a differential equation for $H(t)$ , then for $T(t)$ .

The state variables are $c_{A}, c_{B}, c_{C}$ , and T, and the inputs are $F, T_{0}, c_{A0}$ , and Q.
