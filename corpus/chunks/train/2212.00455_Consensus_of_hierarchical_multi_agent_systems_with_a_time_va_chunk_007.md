with $M \in \mathbb { N } _ { + }$ and for notation simplicity, $L = N + 1$ labels the leader. The leader dynamics is interpreted as a piecewise constant function determined by the higher control level, since $x _ { L } ( 0 ) = u _ { L } ( 0 )$ .

The time-varying set of active followers is expressed as an equality constraint, i.e.

$$\sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} q _ {i} (k) = \sigma (m), \quad \forall k = m M. \tag {7}$$

Hence, until the next update, it holds that:

$$\sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} q _ {i} (k + 1) = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} q _ {i} (k) \tag {8}$$

for all $k \in \{ ( m - 1 ) M + 1 , m M \}$ , while keeping

$$1 \leq \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} q _ {i} (k) \leq N, \quad \forall k \in \mathbb {N}.$$

The described TH-MAS system can be characterized as a multi-rate system with a higher and lower layer working at different sampling times as in Figure 2, such that the time $( t \in \mathbb { N } _ { + } )$ is quantized as

$$t = k T _ {s} = m T _ {s} ^ {\prime},$$

where $T _ { s } \in \mathbb { R }$ and $T _ { s } ^ { ' } \in \mathbb { R }$ are their respective sampling times. The sampling time difference is determined by

$$T _ {s} ^ {\prime} = M \cdot T _ {s} \text { with } M \in \mathbb {N} _ {+}. \tag {9}$$

Finally, an overall system is defined as

$$
\Sigma_ {d} := \boldsymbol {x} (k + 1) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \mathbf {I} _ {N} & \mathbf {0} _ {N} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {N} ^ {\top} & (1 - q _ {L} (k)) \end{array} \right] \boldsymbol {x} (k) + B (k) \boldsymbol {u} (k), \tag {10}
$$

with

$$
B (k) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} w q _ {1} (k) & 0 & \dots & 0 \\ 0 & w q _ {2} (k) & 0 & \vdots \\ \vdots & 0 & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & \dots & \dots & q _ {L} (k) \end{array} \right] \text {and} \boldsymbol {x _ {0}} = \boldsymbol {x} (0),
$$

where the state and control input vectors are

$$\boldsymbol {x} (k) := \left[ x _ {1} (k), \dots , x _ {N} (k), x _ {L} (k) \right] ^ {\top} \in \mathbb {R} ^ {N + 1}$$

and

$$\boldsymbol {u} (k) := \left[ u _ {1} (k), \dots , u _ {N} (k), u _ {L} (k) \right] ^ {\top} \in \mathbb {R} ^ {N + 1}.$$
