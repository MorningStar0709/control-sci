# B. Observer-Based Control Design

The special structure of (15), where F is Hurwitz by construction (see Algorithm 1) and Hz can be replaced by y in the differential equation, allows us to design an observer of the plant (3) in the coordinates of the non-minimal realization, without any knowledge of the parameters in A and B. In particular, define the following dynamical system:

$$\dot {\hat {z}} = F \hat {z} + G u + L y, \tag {16}$$

having the same structure of (15), with user-defined matrices $F , G ,$ and L as per Algorithm 1.

Then, define the estimation error

$$\tilde {x} := x - \Pi \hat {z}. \tag {17}$$

Using (3), (13), (16), and (17), the observer dynamics become

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\hat {z}} = F \hat {z} + G u + L (C x + v) \\ = F \hat {z} + G u + L C (\tilde {x} + \Pi \hat {z}) + L v \tag {18} \\ = (F + L H) \hat {z} + G u + L C \tilde {x} + L v, \\ \end{array}
$$

and then we obtain the following estimation error dynamics:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\tilde {x}} = A (\tilde {x} + \Pi \hat {z}) + B u + E w \\ - \Pi ((F + L H) \hat {z} + G u + L C \tilde {x} + L v) \tag {19} \\ = (A - \Pi L C) \tilde {x} + E w - \Pi L v \\ = \tilde {\Lambda} \tilde {x} + E w - \Pi L v. \\ \end{array}
$$

The interconnection of (3) and (16) can be rewritten in the coordinates (˜x, zˆ) as follows:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {\tilde {x}} \\ \dot {\hat {z}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \tilde {\Lambda} & 0 _ {n p \times \mu} \\ L C & F + L H \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \tilde {x} \\ \hat {z} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 _ {n p \times m} \\ G \end{array} \right] u + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} E & - \Pi L \\ 0 _ {\mu \times q} & L \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} w \\ v \end{array} \right]. \tag {20}
$$

Since $\tilde { \Lambda }$ in (14) is Hurwitz and the pair $( F + L H , G )$ is controllable, the desired controller (5) is obtained by combining the observer (16) and the feedback law

$$u = K \hat {z}, \tag {21}$$

where the gain $K \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m \times \mu }$ must be chosen so that $F +$ $L H + G K$ is Hurwitz. Since H depends on the unknown plant parameters (see (13)), we cannot design K using solely the prior knowledge of the pair $( F + L H , G )$ . Therefore, in the following, we compute K with data-driven techniques using the input–output trajectory (4).
