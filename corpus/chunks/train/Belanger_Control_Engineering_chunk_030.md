# 2.3 MODELING FROM CONSERVATION PRINCIPLES

Conservation principles lead to balance equations, in which the rate of change of a quantity (e.g., momentum, matter, or heat) is equated to other quantities, such as force and flow. This leads quite naturally to equations in state form.

This section gives several examples of modeling. They will serve throughout the book to demonstrate design principles.
