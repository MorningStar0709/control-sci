$$\frac {d u}{d t} = - \dot {\phi} A \sin \phi + \dot {\phi} B \cos \phi . \tag {6.80}$$

In order to have the trajectory pass through the general point $( r , \phi )$ , we must have

$$\phi = \phi \text {when} u = 1 / r,\frac {d u}{d t} = - \left(\frac {d r}{d t}\right) / r ^ {2} \text {when} \frac {d \phi}{d t} = h / r ^ {2}.$$

Substituting these equations into (6.79) and (6.80) gives the simultaneous equations

$$A \cos \phi + B \sin \phi = (1 / r) (\mu / r ^ {2}), \tag {6.81a}A \sin \phi - B \cos \phi = r / h ^ {2}. \tag {6.81b}$$

The solution of (6.81a) and (6.81b) is

$$A = \left[ (1 / r) - \left(\mu / h ^ {2}\right) \right] \cos \phi + (\dot {r} / h) \sin \phi , \tag {6.82a}B = \left[ (1 / r) - \left(\mu / h ^ {2}\right) \right] \sin \phi - (\dot {r} / h) \cos \phi . \tag {6.82b}$$

Furthermore, we need to have the trajectory pass through the target, that is, $r = r _ { t }$ when $\phi = 0$ . Hence (6.79) becomes

$$1 / r _ {t} = \left(\mu / h ^ {2}\right) + A. \tag {6.83}$$

Substituting (6.82a) into (6.83) results in

$$1 / r _ {t} = \left(\mu / h ^ {2}\right) + \left[ (1 / r) - \left(\mu / h ^ {2}\right) \right] \cos \phi + (\dot {r} / h) \sin \phi . \tag {6.84}$$

From (6.84) we will now develop an expression for the velocity required at any point $( r , \phi )$ to have the missile impact at the target in the free-fall. In order to do this, we note from Figure 6.11 that

$$U _ {H} = V _ {R} \sin \gamma ,U _ {V} = V _ {R} \cos \gamma ,$$

where $V _ { R }$ is the required velocity and γ is the flight path (or pitch) angle from the local vertical. Since the angular momentum h is constant, the product of r and $U _ { H }$ at any point must equal h, because the horizontal component of velocity is the only one that contributes to the angular momentum. Furthermore, it must equal negative h, since positive h tends to open up the range angle, whereas we want to close the range angle. The vertical component is simply the radial velocity $d r / d t$ . Thus,

$$r U _ {H} = - h, \quad \text { implying } \quad h = - r V _ {R} \sin \gamma ; \tag {6.85a}U _ {V} = \frac {d r}{d t}, \quad \text { implying } \quad \left(\frac {d r}{d t}\right) = V _ {R} \cos \gamma . \tag {6.85b}$$

Substituting (6.85) into (6.84), we obtain

$$1 / r _ {t} = \left[ \mu (1 - \cos \phi) / r ^ {2} V _ {R} ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \gamma \right] + (\cos \phi / r) - \left(V _ {R} \cos \gamma / r V _ {R} \sin \gamma\right) \sin \phi . \tag {6.86}$$
