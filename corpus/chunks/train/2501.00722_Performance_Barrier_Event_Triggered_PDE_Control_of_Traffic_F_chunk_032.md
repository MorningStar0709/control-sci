This adjustment introduces a safeguard mechanism: if a disturbance causes the Lyapunov function to exceed the performance barrier $( \mathrm { i . e . , } W ^ { p } ( t ) < 0 )$ , control transitions from P-CETC to R-CETC. Rather than being seen as a limitation, this transition is an essential safety feature of the design. During disturbances, infrequent control updates can be detrimental, and a higher frequency of updates is preferred to reduce the duration for which the plant operates in open-loop conditions. The proposed modification ensures this by transferring control to R-CETC whenever disturbances are substantial enough to breach the performance barrier. Since R-CETC enforces a strict decrease in the Lyapunov function, it may even restore the Lyapunov function below the performance barrier, thereby allowing the system to revert to P-CETC operation.
