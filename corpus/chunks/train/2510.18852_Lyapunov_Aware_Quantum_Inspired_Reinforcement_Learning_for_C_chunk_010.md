# D. Reward and Objective Function

The reinforcement learning reward balances spacing control, comfort, and smoothness:

$$r _ {t} = - w _ {z} z _ {t} ^ {2} - w _ {a} u _ {t} ^ {2} - w _ {j} (u _ {t} - u _ {t - 1}) ^ {2}, \tag {9}$$

where $w _ { z } , \ w _ { a }$ , and $w _ { j }$ denote weights for spacing error, acceleration magnitude, and jerk, respectively.

The training objective is:

$$J (\theta) = \mathbb {E} \left[ \sum_ {t} r _ {t} \right] - \mathbb {E} \left[ \sum_ {t} \mathcal {L} _ {\text { stab }, t} \right], \tag {10}$$

ensuring maximization of performance while maintaining Lyapunov-stable trajectories.
