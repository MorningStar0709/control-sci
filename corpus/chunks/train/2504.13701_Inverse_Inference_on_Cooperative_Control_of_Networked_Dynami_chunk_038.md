$$q - \operatorname{rank} (W) \geq q - N ^ {2} n ^ {2} \geq (N - 2) N n ^ {2} > 3, \tag {88}$$

which is even looser than the necessary condition $q \mathrm { ~ - ~ }$ $\operatorname { r a n k } ( W ) = 1$ to make θ a unique solution up to a scalar. Hence, ker(H) contains infinite solutions, and the proof is completed. □
