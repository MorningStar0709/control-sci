The expression of $q ^ { - d - 1 } \bar { B } ^ { \ast } R ^ { \prime }$ can be further developed taking into account (7.223) and (7.202):

$$
\begin{array}{l} q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} \left(\sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} F _ {j}\right) \\ = q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} \left(\sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} (q ^ {- j} F _ {j} + \bar {A} E _ {j})\right) - q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} \bar {A} E _ {j} \\ = - q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} P _ {D} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} - q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {A} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} \bar {B} ^ {*} E _ {j} \tag {7.231} \\ \end{array}
$$

Considering in addition (7.204), one gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} R ^ {\prime} = q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} \left(\sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} F _ {j}\right) \\ = q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {B} ^ {*} P _ {D} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} - q ^ {- d - 1} \bar {A} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} (P _ {D} G _ {j - d} + q ^ {- j + d} H _ {j - d}) \\ = q ^ {- d - 1} P _ {D} \left(\sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} q ^ {j} (\bar {B} ^ {*} - \bar {A} G _ {j - d})\right) - q ^ {- 1} \bar {A} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} H _ {j - d} \tag {7.232} \\ \end{array}
$$

On the other hand, using (7.222), one has:

$$\bar {A} S ^ {\prime} = \bar {A} P _ {D} + q ^ {- 1} \bar {A} \sum_ {j = h _ {i}} ^ {h _ {p}} \gamma_ {j} H _ {j - d} \tag {7.233}$$

Introducing (7.232) and (7.233) in (7.230) yields:

$$P = P _ {D}. P _ {G P C} \tag {7.234}$$

where $P _ { G P C }$ is given by (7.228).

The stability of the generalized predictive control will require that the polynomial $P _ { G P C }$ given by (7.229) be asymptotically stable. This, however, will depend upon the choice of the design parameters $\lambda , h _ { i } , h _ { p } , h _ { c }$ for a given plant model. A number of results are available (Clarke and Mohtadi 1989; M’Saad et al. 1993a) which allows to give rules for choosing the various design parameters. These rules are summarized below:

1. $\begin{array} { r } { h _ { i } = d + 1 , h _ { c } < h _ { p } = h _ { i } + h _ { c } - 1 , \lambda = 0 , P _ { G P C } = \beta _ { 0 } B ^ { * } ( q ^ { - 1 } ) , \beta _ { 0 } = \frac { 1 } { b _ { 1 } } . } \end{array}$

2. $h _ { i } = n _ { B } + d , h _ { c } = n _ { A } + n _ { D } , h _ { p } \geq n _ { A } + n _ { B } + d - 1 , \lambda = 0 ,$ A and $B ^ { * }$ do not have common factors $P _ { G P C } = 1$ and $P = P _ { D }$ (one gets the pole placement).
