$$\widehat {\mathcal {L}} (y _ {\theta}, p _ {\sigma}) = \frac {| \Omega |}{n _ {d}} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {d}} \Bigl (\Delta y _ {\theta} (X _ {i}) + f (X _ {i}) + P _ {U} (- \lambda^ {- 1}) p _ {\sigma} (X _ {i}) \Bigr) ^ {2} + \alpha_ {b} ^ {y} \frac {| \partial \Omega |}{n _ {b}} \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {n _ {b}} \bigl (y _ {\theta} (Y _ {j}) \bigr) ^ {2}+ \alpha_ {i} \frac {| \Omega |}{n _ {d}} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {d}} \left(\Delta p _ {\sigma} (X _ {i}) + y _ {\theta} (X _ {i}) - y _ {d} (X _ {i})\right) ^ {2} + \alpha_ {b} ^ {p} \frac {| \partial \Omega |}{n _ {b}} \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {n _ {b}} \left(p _ {\sigma} (Y _ {j})\right) ^ {2}.$$

The empirical loss $\widehat { \mathcal { L } } ( y _ { \theta } , p _ { \sigma } )$ is then minimized with respect to the NN parameters $( \theta , \sigma )$ , with a minimizer $( \theta ^ { * } , \sigma ^ { * } )$ . The approximation to the optimal control ¯u is given by $P _ { U } \left( - \lambda ^ { - 1 } p _ { \sigma ^ { * } } \right)$ . Note that the change from the unconstrained case is rather minimal in terms of implementation.
