# Example 1.10 Magnetic flow meters

A magnetic flow meter is based on the principle that current that moves in a magnetic field generates a voltage. In a typical meter a magnetic field is generated across the pipe and the voltage is measured in a direction orthogonal to the field. To compensate for electrolytic voltages that often are present, it is common to use a pulsed operation in which the field is switched on and off periodically. This switching causes an inherent sampling.
