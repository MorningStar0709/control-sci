# 5 Leveraging social infrastructure for community resilience

To effectively counter misinformation within critical infrastructure sectors, social infrastructure – including the extended network of professionals involved in maintaining the cyber, physical, and social facets of these systems – can be leveraged. This broad community encompasses various stakeholders like utility workers, IT professionals, emergency responders, and local government officials, all pivotal in driving resilience efforts across different sectors. It is imperative that mechanisms are put in place to combat misinformation effectively to ensure that during times of crisis, truthful and helpful information prevails, aiding in efficient and safe disaster responses. There are many social infrastructure and community engagements that can be employed in this regard.
