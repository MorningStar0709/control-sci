Since every Brownian path $B ( \omega )$ can almost surely be lifted to a Brownian rough path $B ( \omega )$ (either in the Itˆo sense or the Stratonovich sense), there are at least two ways to link pathwise stochastic and rough stochastic control problems. The first is to evaluate the value function of the rough problem at $\eta = B ( \omega )$ i.e. to consider the function

$$\left(\inf _ {u \in \mathcal {A}} J ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}} (u; t, x)\right) \Big | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \boldsymbol {B} (\omega)}.$$

Our results on the rough stochastic control problem guarantee that this approach is well-defined, and our maximum principle for rough stochastic control problems provides necessary and sufficient conditions for controls to be optimal in the pathwise, non-anticipative setting.

The second approach is to substitute the Brownian rough path into the dynamics (1.8) and solve the corresponding control problem, which results in the value function

$$\operatorname{essinf} _ {u \in \mathcal {A}} J ^ {\boldsymbol {B} (\omega)} (u; t, x) \qquad \mathrm{respectively}, \qquad \operatorname{essinf} _ {u \in \bar {\mathcal {A}}} J ^ {\boldsymbol {B} (\omega)} (u; t, x)$$

depending on whether only non-anticipative or anticipative controls are admissible. When only non-anticipative controls are allowed it has been shown in the companion paper [16] that both approaches are equivalent under certain conditions, that is, a.s.

$$\left(\inf _ {u \in \mathcal {A}} J ^ {\boldsymbol {\eta}} (u; t, x)\right) \Big | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \boldsymbol {B} (\omega)} = \operatorname{essinf} _ {u \in \mathcal {A}} J ^ {\boldsymbol {B} (\omega)} (u; t, x).$$

In that work, the authors also proved a dynamical programming principle and related rough HJB equations for controlled rough SDEs with bounded coefficients. Our focus will be on unbounded coefficients.

Our existence of solution results for rough SDEs allows us to give meaning to the state dynamics (1.8) and hence to justify the second approach and to define the corresponding control problem if anticipative controls are allowed.
