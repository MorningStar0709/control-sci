# Stability Margins of CBF-QP Safety Filters: Analysis and Synthesis

Shima Sadat Mousavi, Pol Mestres, and Aaron D. Ames

Abstract— Control barrier function (CBF)-QP safety filters enforce safety by minimally modifying a nominal controller. While prior work has mainly addressed robustness of safety under uncertainty, robustness of the resulting closed-loop stability is much less understood. This issue is important because once the safety filter becomes active, it modifies the nominal dynamics and can reduce stability margins or even destabilize the system, despite preserving safety. For linear systems with a single affine safety constraint, we show that the active-mode dynamics admit an exact scalar loop representation, leading to a classical robust-control interpretation in terms of gain, phase, and delay margins. This viewpoint yields exact stability-margin characterizations and tractable linear matrix inequality (LMI)- based certificates and synthesis conditions for controllers with certified robustness guarantees. Numerical examples illustrate the proposed analysis and the enlargement of certified stability margins for safety-filtered systems.
