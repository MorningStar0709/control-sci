# 9.4 Frequency Distribution of the Asymptotic Bias in Closed-Loop Identification

The method for the analysis of the frequency distribution of the asymptotic bias presented in Sect. 4.4, is very useful for assessing and comparing the behavior of the various algorithms in the presence of noise, or when the plant model and the estimated model do not have the same structure.

For a plant model of the form:

$$y (t) = G (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + W (q ^ {- 1}) e (t) \tag {9.61}$$

one gets in open-loop operation a prediction error equation:

$$\varepsilon (t) = \hat {W} ^ {- 1} (q ^ {- 1}) \{[ G (q ^ {- 1}) - \hat {G} (q ^ {- 1}) ] u (t) + [ W (q ^ {- 1}) - \hat {W} (q ^ {- 1}) ] e (t) \} + e (t) \tag {9.62}$$

Minimizing ${ \bf E } \{ \varepsilon ^ { 2 } ( t ) \}$ leads to the basic formula of asymptotic bias distribution for the open-loop identification methods:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {\theta} ^ {*} = \arg \min _ {\hat {\theta} \in \mathscr {D}} \int_ {- \pi} ^ {\pi} | \hat {W} ^ {- 1} (e ^ {- j \omega}) | ^ {2} [ | G (e ^ {- j \omega}) - \hat {G} (e ^ {- j \omega}) | ^ {2} \phi_ {u} (\omega) \\ + \left| W \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) - \hat {W} \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) \right| ^ {2} \phi_ {e} (\omega) ] d \omega \tag {9.63} \\ \end{array}
$$

In closed-loop operation, the input u(t) is given by:

$$u (t) = S _ {y p} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) r _ {u} (t) + S _ {u p} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) W \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) e (t) \tag {9.64}$$

where $r _ { u } ( t )$ is the external excitation added to the output of the controller $( r _ { u }$ is equal to $r \frac { T } { S }$ if the external excitation is applied on the reference) and $S _ { y p }$ and $S _ { u p }$ are the output and input sensitivity functions respectively. Substituting u(t) in (9.62) and (9.63), one gets:

$$\varepsilon (t) = \hat {W} ^ {- 1} \{(G - \hat {G}) S _ {y p} r _ {u} (t) + [ (G - \hat {G}) S _ {u p} W + (W - \hat {W}) ] e (t) \} + e (t) \tag {9.65}$$

where the argument $q ^ { - 1 }$ has been dropped out to simplify the notation and (9.63) will take the form:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {\theta} ^ {*} = \arg \min _ {\hat {\theta} \in \mathscr {D}} \int_ {- \pi} ^ {\pi} | \hat {W} ^ {- 1} | ^ {2} [ | G - \hat {G} | ^ {2} | S _ {y p} | ^ {2} \phi_ {r _ {u}} (\omega) \\ + | (G - \hat {G}) S _ {u p} W + (W - \hat {W}) | ^ {2} \phi_ {e} (\omega) ] d \omega \tag {9.66} \\ \end{array}
$$
