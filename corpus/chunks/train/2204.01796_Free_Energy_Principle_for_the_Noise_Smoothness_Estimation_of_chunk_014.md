# A. Simulation settings

A random system with $A = \left\lceil \begin{array} { c c } { 0 . 0 4 8 4 } & { 0 . 7 5 3 5 } \\ { - 0 . 7 6 1 7 } & { - 0 . 2 1 8 7 } \end{array} \right\rceil , B =$

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} 0. 3 6 0 4 \\ 0. 0 7 7 6 \end{array} \right], \text {and} C = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0. 2 2 6 5 & - 0. 4 7 8 6 \\ 0. 4 0 6 6 & - 0. 2 6 4 1 \\ 0. 3 8 7 1 & 0. 3 8 1 7 \\ - 0. 1 6 3 0 & - 0. 9 2 9 0 \end{array} \right] \text {was used to}
$$

generate the synthetic data for a total time of $T = 3 2 s$ with increments $d t = 0 . 1 s ,$ and a Gaussian bump input $\nu = e ^ { - 0 . 2 5 ( t - 1 2 ) ^ { 2 } }$ . The colored noise was generated using $\Pi ^ { w } = e ^ { 6 } I _ { 2 }$ and $\Pi ^ { z } = e ^ { 6 } I _ { 4 }$ . This simulation setting will be used throughout the paper, unless mentioned otherwise. We generate eight such time series data using different levels of noise smoothness $s ,$ ranging from 0.1 to 0.8 and use it for the analysis in this section.
