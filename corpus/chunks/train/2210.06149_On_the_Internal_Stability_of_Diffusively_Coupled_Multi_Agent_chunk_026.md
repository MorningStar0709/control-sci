# 4.2. Proof of Proposition 4.3

Bring in minimal realizations

$$
P _ {i} (s) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} A _ {i} & B _ {i} \\ \hline C _ {i} & D _ {i} \end{array} \right] \quad \text {and} \quad K (s) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} A _ {K} & B _ {K} \\ \hline C _ {K} & D _ {K} \end{array} \right]
$$

so the realization

$$
P (s) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} A _ {P} & B _ {P} \\ \hline C _ {P} & D _ {P} \end{array} \right] := \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \operatorname{diag} \{A _ {i} \} & \operatorname{diag} \{B _ {i} \} \\ \hline \operatorname{diag} \{C _ {i} \} & \operatorname{diag} \{D _ {i} \} \end{array} \right]
$$

is also minimal. To prove the first item of the Proposition it is then sufficient to show that 𝜆 is an uncontrollable mode of

$$
P (s) K (s) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} A _ {K} & 0 & B _ {K} \\ B _ {P} C _ {K} & A _ {P} & B _ {P} D _ {K} \\ \hline D _ {P} C _ {K} & C _ {P} & D _ {P} D _ {K} \end{array} \right].
$$

To this end, note that (4) implies $( \mathbb { 1 } \otimes I ) ^ { \top } \left[ \begin{array} { l l } { C _ { K } } & { D _ { K } } \end{array} \right] = 0$ and condition (14a) is equivalent to the existence of $0 \neq v \in \mathbb { C } ^ { m }$ such that $v = B _ { i } ^ { \top } \eta _ { i }$ for some $\eta _ { i }$ such that $\eta _ { i } ^ { \top } ( \lambda I - A _ { i } ) = 0$ . The latter is equivalent to the existence of $\eta \neq 0$ such that

$$\eta^ {\top} (\lambda I - A _ {P}) = 0 \quad \text { and } \quad \eta^ {\top} B _ {P} = (1 \otimes v) ^ {\top}$$

for some $v \neq 0 .$ . Therefore,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & \eta^ {\top} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} A _ {K} - \lambda I & 0 & B _ {K} \\ B _ {P} C _ {K} & A _ {P} - \lambda I & B _ {P} D _ {K} \end{array} \right] \\ = v ^ {\top} (\mathbb {1} \otimes I) ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} C _ {K} & 0 & D _ {K} \end{array} \right] = 0 \\ \end{array}
$$

and the PBH test for the realization of 𝑃𝐾 fails for the mode at 𝜆, proving the first item. The second item follows by similar arguments. 
