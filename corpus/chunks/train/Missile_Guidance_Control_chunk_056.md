Also, from Figure 2.4(a) we can write the velocity of the point p in the form

$$\mathbf {V} _ {p} = \frac {d (\boldsymbol {r} _ {c m})}{d t} + \boldsymbol {\omega} \times \boldsymbol {r} _ {p / c m},$$

or

$$\mathbf {V} _ {p} = \mathbf {V} _ {c m} + \mathbf {V} _ {p / c m}.$$

Then, from Newton’s second law we have

$$\delta \mathbf {F} = d m \left(\frac {d \mathbf {V}}{d t}\right). \tag {2.28}$$

The total external force acting on the missile is found by summing all the elements of the missile. Therefore,

$$\sum \delta \mathbf {F} = \mathbf {F}. \tag {2.29}$$

The velocity of the differential mass dm is

$$\mathbf {V} = \mathbf {V} _ {c m} + \left(\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t}\right), \tag {2.30}$$

where $\mathbf { V } _ { c m }$ is the velocity of the center of mass (cm) of the missile, and $d \mathbf { r } / d t$ is the velocity of the element relative to the center of mass. Substituting (2.30) for the velocity into (2.29) results in

$$\sum \delta \mathbf {F} = \mathbf {F} = \left(\frac {d}{d t}\right) \sum \left[ \mathbf {V} _ {c m} + \left(\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t}\right) \right] d m. \tag {2.31}$$

Assuming that the mass of the missile is constant, (2.31) can be written in the form

$$\mathbf {F} = m \left(\frac {d \mathbf {V} _ {c m}}{d t}\right) + \left(\frac {d}{d t}\right) \sum \left(\frac {d \mathbf {r}}{d t}\right) d m, \tag {2.32a}$$

or

$$\mathbf {F} = m \left(\frac {d \mathbf {V} _ {c m}}{d t}\right) + \left(\frac {d ^ {2}}{d t ^ {2}}\right) \sum \mathbf {r} d m. \tag {2.32b}$$

Since r is measured from the center of the mass, the summation $\scriptstyle \sum$ rdm is equal to 0. Thus, the force equation becomes simply

$$\mathbf {F} = m \left(\frac {d \mathbf {V} _ {c m}}{d t}\right), \tag {2.33}$$

which relates the external force on the missile to the motion of the vehicle’s center of mass. Similarly, we can develop the moment equation referred to a moving center of mass. For the differential element of mass, dm, the moment equation can then be written as

$$\delta \mathbf {M} = d \left(\frac {\delta \mathbf {H}}{d t}\right) = \left(\frac {d}{d t}\right) (\mathbf {r} \times \mathbf {V}) d m. \tag {2.34}$$

The velocity of the mass element can be expressed in terms of the velocity of the center of mass and the relative velocity of the mass element to the center of mass. Therefore,

$$\mathbf {V} _ {p} = \mathbf {V} _ {c m} + \left(\frac {d \mathbf {r} _ {p / c m}}{d t}\right) = \mathbf {V} _ {c m} + \boldsymbol {\omega} \times \mathbf {r}, \tag {2.35}$$
