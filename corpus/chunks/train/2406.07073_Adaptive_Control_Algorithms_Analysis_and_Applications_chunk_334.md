# Properties of the Tracking (Regulation) Error

Introducing the control law of (7.158) in (7.149), one obtains:

$$y (t + d + 1) = y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) + E (q ^ {- 1}) e (t + d + 1) \tag {7.169}$$

and the tracking error becomes:

$$\varepsilon^ {0} (t + d + 1) = y (t + d + 1) - y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) = E (q ^ {- 1}) e (t + d + 1) \tag {7.170}$$

One observes that the tracking (regulation error) in the case of minimum variance tracking and regulation is a moving average of order d. It satisfies the property:

$$\mathbf {E} \{\varepsilon^ {0} (t + i) \varepsilon^ {0} (t) \} = 0; \quad \text { for } i > d \tag {7.171}$$

In particular for $d = 0$ , the tracking error is a white noise. Note that property of (7.171) suggests a practical test for the optimal tuning of a digital controller if the delay d is known.
