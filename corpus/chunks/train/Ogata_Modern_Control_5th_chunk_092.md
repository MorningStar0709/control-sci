$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} _ {1} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {3} \\ \dot {x} _ {4} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \frac {M + m}{M l} g & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\ - \frac {m}{M} g & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {1} \\ x _ {2} \\ x _ {3} \\ x _ {4} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ - \frac {1}{M l} \\ 0 \\ \frac {1}{M} \end{array} \right] u \tag {3-22}

\left[ \begin{array}{l} y _ {1} \\ y _ {2} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{l} x _ {1} \\ x _ {2} \\ x _ {3} \\ x _ {4} \end{array} \right] \tag {3-23}
$$

Equations (3–22) and (3–23) give a state-space representation of the inverted-pendulum system. (Note that state-space representation of the system is not unique. There are infinitely many such representations for this system.)
