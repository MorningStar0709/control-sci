# 5.1 High-fidelity simulation environments

The performance of the proposed planning and control scheme is evaluated in the high-fidelity simulation environment developed by AGX Dynamics [21] and Matlab/Simulink. As shown in Fig. 4, AGX Dynamics is able to model the wheel loader’s tire/soil interaction considering soil deformation, and elasticity, slip, and an-isotropic friction in forward and transverse directions [22]. The wheel loader’s drive train is simulated by the AGX Drivetrain module with 1D dynamics of engine, clutch, gearbox, and differential. The nonlinear MPC planner and LPV-MPC controller are calculated in Matlab/Simulink and communicate control inputs and states with AGX Dynamics. YALMIP [23] is used to operate and solve the optimization problem from LPV-MPC, adaptive LTI-MPC, and nonlinear MPC. The solver Sedumi [24] is used to solve the semi-definite programming of LTI-MPC and LPV-MPC. The solver fmincon is used to solve the nonlinear programming. We perform the simulation on a Dell Precision 7510 with Intel Core i7-6820HQ CPU @2.70GHz x8, and the simulation is conducted in high-fidelity environment with real-time feasibility. The MPC controllers are computed at 5 Hz with a prediction horizon of 10 steps.
