From (6), it is easy to verify that $\| e ( t ) \|$ is bounded. It is assumed that $\| e ( t ) \| \leq \bar { e } ,$ , where the constant $\bar { e } > 0$ . From (5), one obtains

$$\dot {f} (t) \geq - \beta \bar {e} ^ {2} - \bar {f}, f (t _ {k} ^ {+}) = \bar {f}. \tag {41}$$

By the comparison lemma, $f ( t ) \geq g ( t )$ for $t \in [ t _ { k } , t _ { k + 1 } )$ , with $g ( t )$ being the solution of

$$\dot {g} (t) = - \beta \bar {e} ^ {2} - \bar {f}, g (t _ {k} ^ {+}) = \bar {f}. \tag {42}$$

Solving (42) obtains that

$$t - t _ {k} = \frac {\bar {f}}{\beta \bar {e} ^ {2} + \bar {f}} - \frac {g (t)}{\beta \bar {e} ^ {2} + \bar {f}}. \tag {43}$$

Since $f ( t ) \geq g ( t )$ , the inter-event time $t _ { k + 1 } \mathrm { ~ - ~ } t _ { k }$ is lower bounded by the time that it takes for $g ( t )$ to decrease from $\bar { f }$ to 0. It thus follows from (43) that

$$t _ {k + 1} - t _ {k} \geq \frac {\bar {f}}{\beta \bar {e} ^ {2} + \bar {f}}. \tag {44}$$

It is concluded from (44) that the lower bound of the interevent intervals $t _ { k + 1 } - t _ { k } , k \in \mathbb { N }$ is strictly positive for any time, which validates Theorem 1 (ii).
