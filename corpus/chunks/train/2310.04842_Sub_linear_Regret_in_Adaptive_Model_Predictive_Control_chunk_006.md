To this aim, we use MPC, with a receding horizon $N$ . Specifically, at time t, given the current system state $x _ { t }$ and the past observations used to derive an estimator $\theta _ { t }$ of $\theta ^ { \star }$ , we will identify a control policy $( u _ { k | t } ) _ { k = 0 , \dots , N - 1 }$ minimizing the cost along a predicted system trajectory $( x _ { k | t } ) _ { k = 0 , \dots , N }$ . We use the well-known dual mode prediction paradigm (Kouvaritakis & Cannon, 2016) with the following predicted control sequence,

$$
u _ {k | t} = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} K x _ {k | t} + v _ {k | t} & \forall k \in \{0, \ldots , N - 1 \}, \\ K x _ {k | t} & \forall k \geq N, \end{array} \right.
$$

where $\{ v _ { 0 \mid t } , \ldots { v _ { N - 1 \mid t } } \}$ are the optimization variables to be determined by the MPC. The resulting prediction dynamics will be for $k \in \{ 0 , \ldots , N - 1 \}$ ,

$$x _ {0 | t} = x _ {t},x _ {k + 1 | t} = \Phi (\theta_ {t}) x _ {k | t} + B (\theta_ {t}) v _ {k | t},$$
