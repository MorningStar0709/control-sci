There are at least two reasons why it is more efficient to train the residual policy instead of starting from scratch. First of all, if the prior policy is good, then the RL-method just has to make minor improvements. However, the main point here is that a prior controller that nudges the system in the right direction can improve the performance of the RL-methods substantially by ensuring that more exciting data is collected from the start. Also note that the structure in (13) allows the RL-method to eventually cancel out the prior controller if the data indicate that it is not useful beyond the initial data collecting phase. This reduces the risk of locking the RL-method into an inefficient control structure.
