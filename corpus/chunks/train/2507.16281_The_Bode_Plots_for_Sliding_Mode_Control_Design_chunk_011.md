The structure of the quasi-linear approximator comprises a parallel set of linear operators, each corresponding to a distinct stationary component of the input signal [28]. These operators—often referred to as weighting functions—are designed to minimize the mean squared error between the output of the approximator and the actual response of the nonlinearity [42]. A fundamental limitation of this method is its reliance on the input signal closely resembling the assumed waveform (5). This constraint introduces a trade-off between the lowpass filtering capability of the linear subsystem and the highfrequency harmonic content generated by the nonlinear SM controller.
