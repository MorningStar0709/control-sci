This inequality, which does not impose $\dot { V } ( x )$ negative for all $( \zeta , x ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { r } \times \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 n }$ is on the core of the stability conditions of the whole system composed by the feedback connection of the linear subsystem $S _ { L }$ and the nonlinear subsystem $\mathcal { S } _ { N }$ . The result stated in the next lemma is a consequence of the inequality (29).

Lemma 2 Let the control gains $K _ { 0 }$ and K be given. If the matrix inequalities

$$
\begin{array}{l} A _ {K} ^ {T} P + P A _ {K} + P \left[ \begin{array}{l} E _ {0} ^ {T} \\ F _ {0} ^ {T} \\ G _ {0} \\ G _ {0} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} Z _ {d} \left[ \begin{array}{l} E _ {0} ^ {T} \\ F _ {0} ^ {T} \\ G _ {0} \\ G _ {0} \end{array} \right] P + \tag {30} \\ + \left[ \begin{array}{c} B K \\ \gamma^ {- 1} G _ {0} \\ \alpha^ {- 1} D _ {g} G _ {0} \\ 0 \end{array} \right] ^ {T} Z _ {d} ^ {- 1} \left[ \begin{array}{c} B K \\ \gamma^ {- 1} G _ {0} \\ \alpha^ {- 1} D _ {g} G _ {0} \\ 0 \end{array} \right] + \rho P <   0 \\ \end{array}
$$

and

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} \rho S & \bullet \\ C _ {g 0} & \kappa I _ {n} \end{array} \right] > 0 \tag {31}
$$

are satisfied for scalars α $\cdot > 0 , \gamma > 0 , \rho > 0$ , a four-block matrix $Z _ { d } = \mathrm { d i a g } ( \mu I _ { n } , \beta I _ { n } , \pi I _ { n } , \kappa I _ { n } ) > 0 $ , and matrices $S > 0 , P > \overrightarrow { 0 }$ of appropriate dimensions then the inequality

$$\dot {V} < \rho c (\sigma) (v - V) \tag {32}$$

holds, for all 0 $\neq \zeta \in \mathbb { R } ^ { r }$ and 0 $\neq x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 2 n }$ .

Proof: With (30) and (31), the inequality (29) becomes

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} <   - c (\sigma) \rho V (x) + c (\sigma) \kappa^ {- 1} \zeta^ {T} C _ {g 0} ^ {T} C _ {g 0} \zeta \\ <   - c (\sigma) \rho V (x) + c (\sigma) \rho v (\zeta) \tag {33} \\ \end{array}
$$

which reproduces (32), proving thus the claim.

The last two lemmas are very important to establish the global stability of the whole system under consideration.
