# Proof of Lemma 2

Fix $M > 0 , \ z \in \left[ - M , M \right]$ , and $\kappa \in \left( 0 , 1 \right]$ . We have

$$\frac {\partial}{\partial \kappa} \ln e _ {\kappa} (z) = \frac {1}{e _ {\kappa} (z)} \frac {\partial e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\partial \kappa} = - \frac {1}{\kappa} \ln e _ {\kappa} (z) + \frac {z}{\kappa \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}}, \tag {56}$$

and hence

$$\frac {\partial e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\partial \kappa} = e _ {\kappa} (z) \left(- \frac {1}{\kappa} \ln e _ {\kappa} (z) + \frac {z}{\kappa \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}}\right). \tag {57}$$

We have

$$
\begin{array}{l} - \frac {1}{\kappa} \ln e _ {\kappa} (z) + \frac {z}{\kappa \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}} = - \frac {1}{\kappa^ {2}} \ln \left(\kappa z + \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}\right) + \frac {\kappa z}{\kappa^ {2} \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}}. \tag {58} \\ = \frac {1}{\kappa^ {2}} \left(- \ln \left(\kappa z + \sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}\right) + \frac {\kappa z}{\sqrt {\kappa^ {2} z ^ {2} + 1}}\right). \\ \end{array}
$$

Consequently, it follows that

$$\frac {\partial e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\partial \kappa} = e _ {\kappa} (z) \frac {H (\kappa z)}{\kappa^ {2}} \tag {59}$$

with  given by

$$H (u) = - \ln \left(u + \sqrt {u ^ {2} + 1}\right) + \frac {u}{\sqrt {u ^ {2} + 1}} \quad \text { for any } \quad u \in \mathbb {R}. \tag {60}$$

An elementary calculation yields

$$\frac {\mathrm{d} H (u)}{\mathrm{d} u} = - \frac {u ^ {2}}{(u ^ {2} + 1) ^ {3 / 2}} \leq 0 \quad \text { for any } \quad u \in \mathbb {R}. \tag {61}$$

This shows that $H \left( u \right) = - \frac { u ^ { 3 } } { 3 } + O \left( u ^ { 4 } \right) \ \mathrm { i f } \ \left| u \right|$ u is small. Therefore, for fixed $M , z$ , it follows that

$$\lim _ {\kappa \rightarrow + 0} \frac {\partial e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\partial \kappa} = \lim _ {\kappa \rightarrow + 0} \frac {e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\kappa^ {2}} H (\kappa z) = - \lim _ {\kappa \rightarrow + 0} \frac {e _ {\kappa} (z)}{\kappa^ {2}} \frac {\kappa^ {3} z ^ {3}}{3} = 0 \tag {62}$$

Furthermore, we have $\operatorname* { l i m } _ { \kappa \to + 0 } e _ { \kappa } \left( z \right) = \exp \left( z \right) < + \infty$ . Therefore, for each bounded $z \in \mathbb { R }$ , the partial derivative $\frac { \partial e _ { \kappa } \left( z \right) } { \partial \kappa }$ exists and is continuous for $\kappa \in [ 0 , 1 ]$ . This proves the first statement.
