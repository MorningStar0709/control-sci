# 4.1. An equivalent LQ stochastic control problem

For $u \in \mathcal { L } ^ { 2 }$ we define the cost functional J by

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (u) = \frac {1}{2} E \left[ \left(\mathcal {H} ^ {u} (T) + \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} (T) \xi\right) ^ {\top} \left(\mathcal {H} ^ {u} (T) + \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} (T) \xi\right) \right] + E \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} (u (s)) ^ {\top} \mathscr {R} (s) u (s) d s \right] \\ + E \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} \left(\mathcal {H} ^ {u} (s) + \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} (s) \zeta (s)\right) ^ {\top} \mathcal {Q} (s) \left(\mathcal {H} ^ {u} (s) + \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} (s) \zeta (s)\right) d s \right] \\ - E \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {T} 2 (u (s)) ^ {\top} \mathcal {Q} (s) \left(\mathcal {H} ^ {u} (s) + \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} (s) \zeta (s)\right) d s \right], \tag {31} \\ \end{array}
$$

where the state process $\mathcal { H } ^ { u } = ( \mathcal { H } ^ { u } ( s ) ) _ { s \in [ 0 , T ] }$ is the solution to the SDE (25) and $\mathcal { Q }$ and R are defined in (10) and (14).

It is a direct consequence of Proposition 3.6 and Proposition 3.5 that the control problems pertaining to $J ^ { p m }$ and J are equivalent in the following sense.

Corollary 4.1. (i) It holds a.s. that

$$\inf _ {X \in \mathcal {A} ^ {p m}} J ^ {p m} (X) = \inf _ {u \in \mathcal {L} ^ {2}} J (u) - \frac {1}{2} d ^ {\top} \gamma^ {- 1} (0) d.$$
