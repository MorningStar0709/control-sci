Assumption 2: At steady state, the sliding variable can be approximated by the waveform

$$\sigma (t) = \sigma_ {0} (t) + A \sin (\omega t), \tag {5}$$

under the assumption that the disturbance signal (3) evolves slowly compared to the chattering frequency, i.e., Ω ≪ ω.

Remark 2: Although the steady-state sliding variable (5) is commonly decomposed into slow-motions (a distorted version of the perturbation (3), attenuated and phase-shifted) and fast-motions (chattering effects) [37], [38]. This interpretation should not be regarded as evidence of a strict superposition principle. Such quasi-linear behavior cannot be expected to hold uniformly across the entire frequency spectrum, nor for arbitrary magnitudes of the external disturbance [28].
