# Mechanical Systems with Nonlinearities

Many mechanical systems involve nonlinear effects such as Coulomb (or dry) friction, gear backlash, and stiffness elements that do not exhibit linear force–displacement characteristics. Another nonlinear effect is the presence of discontinuous forces, such as contact or damper forces that occur only when a mass remains in contact with a mechanical element that has stiffness and/or damping properties.
