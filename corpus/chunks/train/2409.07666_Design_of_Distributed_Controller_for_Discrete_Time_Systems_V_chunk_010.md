(14) shows the nonsingularity of $\tilde { G }$ and $\tilde { H }$ in (11); otherwise, for a non-zero vector $x \in \ker { \tilde { G } }$ , pre- and post-multiplying (14) by $x ^ { \top }$ and $x ,$ respectively, would yield zero.

Then, by pre- and post-multiplying the matrix inequality in (11) with $\tilde { H } ^ { - \top }$ and $\tilde { H } ^ { - 1 }$ , we get

$$
\begin{array}{l} O \prec \tilde {H} ^ {- \top} \Psi (\tilde {G}, \tilde {Q}, \tilde {Z}) \tilde {H} ^ {- 1} + \rho W \\ = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} + \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} - \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} \tilde {Q} \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} & * \\ \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} (\tilde {A} + \tilde {B} \tilde {K}) & \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} \tilde {Q} \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} \end{array} \right] + \rho W, \tag {15} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\tilde { K } = \tilde { Z } \tilde { G } ^ { - 1 }$ . We can transform (15) by using Finsler’s lemma and setting $X = E ^ { \top } \tilde { G } ^ { - 1 } E , P = \check { E } ^ { \top } \tilde { G } ^ { - \top } \tilde { Q } \tilde { G } ^ { - 1 } E$ . Here, X is nonsingular, because $\tilde { G }$ is nonsingular and E has full column rank.

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} E ^ {\top} & O \\ O & E ^ {\top} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} + \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} - \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} \tilde {Q} \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} & * \\ \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} (\tilde {A} + \tilde {B} \tilde {K}) & \tilde {G} ^ {- \top} \tilde {Q} \tilde {G} ^ {- 1} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} E & O \\ O & E \end{array} \right] \\ = \left[ \begin{array}{l l} X ^ {\top} + X - P & * \\ X ^ {\top} (A + B K) & P \end{array} \right] \succ O. \tag {16} \\ \end{array}
$$

Note that $W ^ { \perp } = \mathrm { b l k d i a g } ( E ^ { \top } , E ^ { \top } )$ is used for the Finsler’s lemma. Here, (16) shares a similar structure with the extended LMI [13] and can likewise be transformed by utilizing the following inequality,

$$
\begin{array}{l} (P - X ^ {\top}) ^ {\top} P ^ {- 1} (P - X ^ {\top}) \succeq O \\ \Leftrightarrow X P ^ {- 1} X ^ {\top} \succeq X + X ^ {\top} - P, \tag {17} \\ \end{array}
$$

allowing a transformation of (16) as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X P ^ {- 1} X ^ {\top} & * \\ X ^ {\top} (A + B K) & P \end{array} \right] \succ O \\ \Leftrightarrow \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X P ^ {- 1} X ^ {\top} & * \\ A + B K & X ^ {- \top} P X ^ {- 1} \end{array} \right] \succ O \\ \Leftrightarrow (A + B K) ^ {\top} X P ^ {- 1} X ^ {\top} (A + B K) - X P ^ {- 1} X ^ {\top} \prec O. \\ \end{array}
$$
