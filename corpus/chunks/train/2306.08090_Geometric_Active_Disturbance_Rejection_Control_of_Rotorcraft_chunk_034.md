\begin{array}{l} \leq - \left(k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\min} \{L _ {T} \} - \frac {1}{2}\right) \psi_ {T} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \psi_ {T} \\ - k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\mathrm{min}} \{L _ {T} \} (\psi_ {T} ^ {\mathbf {T}} \psi_ {T}) ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \\ - \kappa_ {T} k _ {T P} (\tilde {b} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \tilde {b}) - \kappa_ {T} k _ {T P} (\tilde {b} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \tilde {b}) ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \\ - \left(\Gamma_ {t 1} - \frac {m ^ {2}}{2 \lambda_ {\min} \{\mathcal {P} _ {t} \}}\right) V _ {t} - \Gamma_ {t 2} V _ {t} ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \\ \leq - \left(2 k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\min} \{L _ {T} \} - 1\right) m ^ {- 1} \left(\frac {1}{2} m \psi_ {T} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \psi_ {T}\right) \\ - 2 ^ {\frac {1}{p}} k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\min} \left\{L _ {T} \right\} m ^ {- \frac {1}{p}} \left(\frac {1}{2} m \psi_ {T} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \psi_ {T}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \\ - 2 \kappa_ {T} \left(\frac {1}{2} k _ {T P} \tilde {b} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \tilde {b}\right) - 2 ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \kappa_ {T} k _ {T P} ^ {\frac {p - 1}{p}} \left(\frac {1}{2} k _ {T P} \tilde {b} ^ {\mathrm{T}} \tilde {b}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \\ - \left(\Gamma_ {t 1} - \frac {m ^ {2}}{2 \lambda_ {\min} \{\mathcal {P} _ {t} \}}\right) V _ {t} - \Gamma_ {t 2} V _ {t} ^ {\frac {1}{p}}, \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\Gamma _ { t 1 } , \Gamma _ { t 2 }$ and $\mathcal { P } _ { t }$ are as defined in the proof of Theorem 2. Thus, we obtain the following inequality:

$$\dot {V} _ {T} \leq - \Gamma_ {T 1} V _ {T} - \Gamma_ {T 2} V _ {T} ^ {\frac {1}{p}}, \tag {89}$$

where $\Gamma _ { T 1 }$ and $\Gamma _ { T 2 }$ are defined by:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \Gamma_ {T 1} = \\ \min \left\{\left(2 k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\min} \{L _ {T} \} - 1\right) m ^ {- 1}, 2 \kappa_ {T}, \Gamma_ {t 1} - \frac {m ^ {2}}{2 \lambda_ {\min} \{\mathcal {P} _ {t} \}} \right\}, \\ \Gamma_ {T 2} = \\ \min \left\{2 ^ {\frac {1}{p}} k _ {T D} \lambda_ {\min} \{L _ {T} \} m ^ {- \frac {1}{p}}, 2 ^ {\frac {1}{p}} \kappa_ {T} k _ {T P} ^ {\frac {p - 1}{p}}, \Gamma_ {t 2} \right\}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Based on (89), we conclude that when the resultant disturbance force is constant $( \dot { \varphi } _ { D } = 0 )$ , and the ESO and ADRC gains satisfy the constraints in Proposition 1 and Proposition 3, the tracking error dynamics (85) for the translational motion is FFTS. This concludes the proof of Theorem 4.
