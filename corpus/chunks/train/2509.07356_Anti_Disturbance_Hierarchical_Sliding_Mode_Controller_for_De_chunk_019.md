# 5.2.2 Low-Frequency Disturbance Condition

Low-frequency disturbances simulate slowly varying periodic external forces, such as swells or ocean currents. The mathematical expression is:

$$F _ {\text { low - frequency }} = - 5 0 \sin (2 \pi \cdot 0. 3 \cdot t) \tag {24}$$

where t represents time, and 0.3 Hz denotes the frequency of the low-frequency disturbance. As shown in Fig. 4, the trolley and load trajectories exhibit stable dynamic response characteristics under low-frequency disturbances. The system converges to the target position quickly and maintains low-amplitude oscillations in the steady-state phase, with minimal impact from the disturbance frequency. This indicates that the controller successfully suppresses the effect of slowly varying disturbances through the adaptive gain adjustment mechanism, demonstrating excellent steady-state performance and adaptability.
