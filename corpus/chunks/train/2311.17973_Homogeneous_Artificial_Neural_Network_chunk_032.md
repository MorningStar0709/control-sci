$$\mathcal {J} _ {\epsilon} (x _ {i} + z _ {j}, x _ {i}, G) \approx \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {n} e _ {i} ^ {\top} z _ {j} \sum_ {\lambda \in \{0, 1 \} ^ {n}} f (x _ {k} + z _ {j}, x _ {k}, x _ {k} + \mathrm{diag} (\lambda) z _ {j}, G). \tag {36}$$

The presented discretizations of the functionals $J _ { \epsilon }$ and $\mathcal { T } _ { \epsilon }$ can be utilized for identification of the generator $G _ { \epsilon }$ of the practical dilation, however, in this case, an anti-Hurwitz solution of the optimization problem (30) may exist even if the ANN $g _ { \epsilon }$ approximates a non-homogeneous function. So, the practical necessary condition of homogeneity (21) has be utilized in order to check if the ANN indeed approximates a dϵ-homogeneous (at least on Ω) function. In the view of Proposition 4, a correctness of this conclusion depends on approximation precision of ANN and increases as $\epsilon  0$ .
