Therefore, with less controller intervention q, the hybrid agent will achieve better performance. Unsurprisingly, the PID controller performs better than the $H _ { \infty }$ controller, which trades both the performance and energy efficiency for more robustness against noise and disturbance. Base controller with more robustness allows training with lower mixing factor q, but since TurtleSim is relatively simple and allows both base controllers to train and test with arbitrary q, the advantage of $H _ { \infty }$ is not well reflected in this experiment.
