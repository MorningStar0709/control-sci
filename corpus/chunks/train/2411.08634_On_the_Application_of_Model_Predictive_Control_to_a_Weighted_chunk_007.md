These constraints force the agent to move by ensuring that each state $x _ { i }$ along the trajectory is at least distance V away from each previous state $x _ { j }$ for $j < i .$ The parameter V is related to the visibility of the agent, i.e., it determines how far the agent needs to travel in order to collect a new reward. Note that the constraint is implemented as a soft constraint with the slack variables $\epsilon _ { i } \in \mathbb { R }$ in order to allow the agent to slightly violate a CC if this leads to a better trajectory. The slack variables appear as an additional term in the modified objective function

$$J (\mathbf {x} _ {0: N}, \mathbf {u} _ {0: N - 1}) =c _ {1} \sum_ {k = 0} ^ {N - 1} u _ {k} ^ {\top} R u _ {k} - c _ {2} \sum_ {k = 0} ^ {N} r (x _ {k}) + c _ {3} \sum_ {k = 0} ^ {N} \epsilon_ {k}. \tag {5}$$

Note that the dynamics (2b) in the MPC formulation are generally arbitrary. For the implementation, simple linear dynamics $f ( x _ { k } , u _ { k } ) = A x _ { k } + B u _ { k }$ are assumed, in the form of a double integrator:

$$
A = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & 0 & \Delta t & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & \Delta t \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right], B = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ \Delta t & 0 \\ 0 & \Delta t \end{array} \right]
$$

. Here, the states are the positions and velocities, and the inputs are the accelerations. The function $\rho ( x _ { i } , y _ { i } )$ is defined as the distance between the positions of two states, i.e.,

$$\rho (x _ {i}, x _ {j}) = \sqrt {(x _ {i , 1} - x _ {j , 1}) ^ {2} + (x _ {i , 2} - x _ {j , 2}) ^ {2}}. \tag {6}$$

This leads to the quadratic CCs

$$(x _ {i, 1} - x _ {j, 1}) ^ {2} + (x _ {i, 2} - x _ {j, 2}) ^ {2} \geq V ^ {2} - \epsilon_ {i}\forall i = 1, \dots , N \text { and } j = 0, \dots , i - 1. \tag {7}$$
