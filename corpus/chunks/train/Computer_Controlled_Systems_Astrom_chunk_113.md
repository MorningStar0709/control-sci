# 2.4 Discrete-Time Systems

The previous section showed how to transform a continuous-time system into discrete-time form. In most of the remaining part of this chapter we will disregard how the difference equation representing the discrete-time system has been obtained. Instead we will concentrate on the properties of difference equations. Time-invariant discrete-time systems can be described by the difference equation

$$
\begin{array}{l} x (k + 1) = \Phi x (k) + \Gamma u (k) \\ (1) = C _ {1} (1) - D _ {1} (1) \end{array} \tag {2.16}
y (k) = C x (k) + D u (k)
$$

For simplicity the sampling time is used as the time unit, h = 1.
