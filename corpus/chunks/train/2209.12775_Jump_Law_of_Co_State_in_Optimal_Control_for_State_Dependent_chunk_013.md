# B. Second-order switching system with time-varying switching interface

Consider the following second-order system with the timevarying switching interface $g ( x , t ) = x _ { 1 } + x _ { 2 } + t - 1 = 0 \colon$

$$
\mathrm{s} 1: \quad \left\{ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {1} = 2 x _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} = u \end{array} , \quad x _ {1} + x _ {2} + t \leq 1 \right. \tag {22}
$$

switches to

$$
\mathrm{s} 2: \quad \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \dot {x} _ {1} = x _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} = u \end{array} , \quad x _ {1} + x _ {2} + t \geq 1 \right. \tag {23}
$$

The discontinuous stage cost is

$$
L = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} u ^ {2}, & x _ {1} + x _ {2} + t \leq 1 \\ \frac {1}{2} u ^ {2}, & x _ {1} + x _ {2} + t \geq 1 \end{array} . \right. \tag {24}
$$

We consider the boundary conditions: $x _ { 0 } = [ 0 , 0 ] ^ { \top } , x _ { f } =$ $[ 2 , 2 ] ^ { \top }$ and $t _ { f } = 2$ . The numerical solution gives the optimal switching time instant as $\tau ~ = ~ 0 . 4 7 9 0$ with $J ~ = ~ 1$ .1539 and $\Delta \lambda = [ 0 . 1 3 1 7 , 0 . 1 3 1 8 ] ^ { \top }$ . ∆λ using jump law of (20) is $[ 0 . 1 3 1 8 , \dot { 0 . } 1 3 1 8 ] ^ { \top }$ .
