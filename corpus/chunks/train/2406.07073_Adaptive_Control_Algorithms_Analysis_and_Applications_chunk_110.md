$$\tilde {\theta} (t) = \hat {\theta} (t) - \theta \tag {3.16}$$

From (3.1) and (3.4) it results:

$$\varepsilon^ {0} (t + 1) = y (t + 1) - \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + 1) = \theta^ {T} \phi (t) - \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) \phi (t) = - \phi^ {T} (t) \tilde {\theta} (t) \tag {3.17}$$

Subtracting θ in the two terms of (3.15) and using (3.17) one gets:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\theta} (t + 1) = \tilde {\theta} (t) - F (t) \phi (t) \phi^ {T} (t) \tilde {\theta} (t) = [ I - F \phi (t) \phi^ {T} (t) ] \tilde {\theta} (t) \\ = A (t) \tilde {\theta} (t) \tag {3.18} \\ \end{array}
$$

Equation (3.18) corresponds to a time-varying dynamical system. A necessary stability condition (but not sufficient) is that the eigen values of A(t) be inside the unit circle at each instant t . This leads to the following condition for the choice of the adaptation gain as F = αI :

$$\alpha < \frac {1}{\phi^ {T} (t) \phi (t)} \tag {3.19}$$
