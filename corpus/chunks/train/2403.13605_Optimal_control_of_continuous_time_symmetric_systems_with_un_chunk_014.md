As the following proposition shows, finite-horizon gains (10) are monotonic with respect to the horizon length $t _ { f }$ . Therefore, the finite-horizon system gains (10) lowerbound the conventional (infinite-horizon) system norms.

$$\| \mathcal {G} \| _ {p, t _ {1}} \leq \| \mathcal {G} \| _ {p, t _ {2}}$$

Proof. Define

$$\bar {\mathcal {L}} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {1}) = \left\{u \in \mathcal {L} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {2}) \mid u (t) = 0, t \in (t _ {1}, t _ {2} ] \right\}$$

$$\| \mathcal {G} \| _ {p, t _ {1}} = \sup _ {u \in \mathcal {L} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {1}) \setminus 0} \frac {\| y \| _ {p , t _ {1}}}{\| u \| _ {p , t _ {1}}} = \sup _ {u \in \bar {\mathcal {L}} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {1}) \setminus 0} \frac {\| y \| _ {p , t _ {1}}}{\| u \| _ {p , t _ {2}}} \leq\sup _ {u \in \bar {\mathcal {L}} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {1}) \setminus 0} \frac {\| y \| _ {p , t _ {2}}}{\| u \| _ {p , t _ {2}}} \leq \sup _ {u \in \mathcal {L} _ {p} ^ {m} (0, t _ {2}) \setminus 0} \frac {\| y \| _ {p , t _ {2}}}{\| u \| _ {p , t _ {2}}} = \| \mathcal {G} \| _ {p, t _ {2}}.$$
