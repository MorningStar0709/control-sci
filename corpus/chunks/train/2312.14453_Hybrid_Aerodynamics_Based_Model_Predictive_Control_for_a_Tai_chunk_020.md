Training was performed with the MATLAB Deep Learning Toolbox [28] using the Levenberg–Marquardt backpropagation algorithm to minimize the root-mean-square error (RMSE) of the predicted and target acceleration. Table II summarizes the accelerations predicted by the nonlinear model and the hybrid models with various sizes of hidden layers, where the maximum solving times of the OCP are also listed. The results show that the NN compensator attenuated the error of the nonlinear model by more than 50%. The acceleration RMSE was largest in the X direction, as this direction contained the largest aerodynamic area. An FFNN with 10 neurons was applied in the experiments, due to its favorable trade-off between predictive accuracy and implementation time.
