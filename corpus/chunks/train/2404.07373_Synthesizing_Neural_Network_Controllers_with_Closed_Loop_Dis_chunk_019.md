# 4.1 Bilinear Matrix Inequality (BMI) Condition for Dissipativity

To convert the condition given by Lemma 1 into a BMI, we make two assumptions: that $X _ { e e } \preceq 0$ and that $M _ { \Delta _ { p } v v } \succeq 0$ . This allows definition of $L _ { X }$ and $L _ { \Delta _ { p } }$ such that

$$X _ {e e} = - L _ {X} ^ {\top} L _ {X}, \quad M _ {\Delta_ {p} v v} = L _ {\Delta_ {p}} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta_ {p}}. \tag {19}$$

Then, we define $L _ { \Delta } = \mathrm { d i a g } ( L _ { \Delta _ { p } } , 0 )$ , such that $M _ { v v } =$ $L _ { \Delta } ^ { \top } L _ { \Delta }$ . Common quadratic supply rates that satisfy this negative semidefiniteness condition on $X _ { e e }$ include those for $L _ { 2 }$ gain and passivity. Common IQC multipliers that satisfy this positive semidefiniteness condition on $M _ { \Delta _ { p } v v }$ include those that characterize LTI dynamics, multiplication by a (possibly time-varying) real scalar, and memoryless nonlinearities sector-bounded in [0, 1]; see [11].

Going forward, we let $\lambda = 1$ . This is without loss of generality since λ can be absorbed into the ${ M } _ { \Delta _ { \bar { F } } }$ and Λ variables.

With the semidefiniteness assumptions in (19), we can apply the Schur complement to (11) to arrive at the equivalent condition

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} F & \left[ \begin{array}{c c} C _ {v} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & C _ {e} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \\ D _ {v w} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & D _ {e w} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \\ D _ {v d} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & D _ {e d} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \end{array} \right] \\ \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} L _ {\Delta} C _ {v} & L _ {\Delta} D _ {v w} & L _ {\Delta} D _ {v d} \\ L _ {X} C _ {e} & L _ {X} D _ {e w} & L _ {X} D _ {e d} \end{array} \right] & - I \end{array} \right] \preceq 0 \quad (2 0)
$$

where

$$
\begin{array}{l} F = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} A ^ {\top} P + P A & P B _ {w} & P B _ {d} \\ B _ {w} ^ {\top} P & 0 & 0 \\ B _ {d} ^ {\top} P & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right] \\ + (\star) ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & M _ {v w} \\ M _ {v w} ^ {\top} & M _ {w w} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} C _ {v} & D _ {v w} & D _ {v d} \\ 0 & I & 0 \end{array} \right] \\ - \left(\star\right) ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X _ {d d} & X _ {d e} \\ X _ {d e} ^ {\top} & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 0 & 0 & I \\ C _ {e} & D _ {e w} & D _ {e d} \end{array} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

Since the matrices of the closed-loop system are affine in the controller parameters θ, this condition is a bilinear matrix inequality in $P , \theta , \Lambda , L _ { \Delta _ { p } } , M _ { \Delta _ { p } v w } , M _ { \Delta _ { p } w w } , X _ { d d } .$ , $X _ { d e }$ , and $L _ { X }$ .
