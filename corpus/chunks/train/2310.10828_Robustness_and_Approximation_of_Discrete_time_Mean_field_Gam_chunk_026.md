Assume that we have $( Q ^ { n } , \mu ^ { n } ) \to ( Q , \mu ) ;$ ; that is, $\mu ^ { n }$ converges to $\mu$ weakly in ${ \mathcal { P } } ( X )$ and $Q _ { \mathrm { m i n } } ^ { n }$ converges to $Q _ { \mathrm { m i n } }$ continuously. We further suppose that $\| Q _ { \operatorname* { m i n } } ^ { n } \| _ { w _ { \operatorname* { m a x } } } \leq C _ { n }$ and $\| Q _ { \operatorname* { m i n } } \| _ { w _ { \operatorname* { m a x } } } \leq C _ { \infty }$ . Our aim is to show that

$$F (\cdot , Q ^ {n}, \mu^ {n}, \cdot , p _ {n}) \rightarrow F (\cdot , Q, \mu , \cdot , p)$$

continuously which implies that

$$\min _ {a} F (x ^ {n}, Q ^ {n}, \mu^ {n}, a, p _ {n}) \rightarrow \min _ {a} F (x, Q, \mu , a, p),$$

whenever $x _ { n } \to x$ in X. Let $K \subset X$ be compact, then triangle inequality gives us the following

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} | F (x, Q ^ {n}, \mu^ {n}, a, p _ {n}) - F (x, Q, \mu , a, p) | \\ = \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| c (x, a, \mu^ {n}) + \beta \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} ^ {n} (y) p _ {n} \left(d y \mid x, a ^ {n}, \mu^ {n}\right) - c (x, a, \mu) \right. \\ - \beta \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) p (d y | x, a, \mu) \Big | \\ \leq \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| c (x, a, \mu^ {n}) - c (x, a, \mu) \right| \\ + \beta \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} ^ {n} (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, a, \mu^ {n}) - \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) p (d y | x, a, \mu) \right|. \\ \end{array}
$$

To see the vanishing of the first term on the last line, observe that the cost function $c ( \cdot , \cdot , \mu ^ { n } )$ converges to $c ( \cdot , \cdot , \mu )$ continuously as $n  \infty$ by Assumption 1-(a). Therefore, it is sufficient to analyse the vanishing of the second term on the last line. We will use the following triangle inequality on the second term as follows

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} ^ {n} (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, a, \mu^ {n}) - \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) p (d y | x, a, \mu) \right| \\ = \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} ^ {n} (y) - Q _ {\min} (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, a, \mu^ {n}) \right. \\ + \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) \left(p _ {n} \left(d y \mid x, a, \mu^ {n}\right) - p \left(d y \mid x, a, \mu\right)\right) \\ \leq \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| \int_ {X} (Q _ {\min} ^ {n} (y) - Q _ {\min} (y)) p _ {n} (d y | x, a, \mu^ {n}) \right| \\ + \sup _ {x, a \in K \times A} \left| \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) \left(p _ {n} \left(d y | x, a, \mu^ {n}\right) - p \left(d y | x, a, \mu\right)\right) \right|. \\ \end{array}
$$
