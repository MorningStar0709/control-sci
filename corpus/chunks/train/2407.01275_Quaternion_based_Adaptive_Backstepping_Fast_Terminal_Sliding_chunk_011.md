# A. Math Notation

In this paper, R describes the set of real numbers. $\mathbb { R } ^ { c \times d }$ represents the set of real-numbers with dimensional space $c -$ by-d. $\pmb { I } _ { c } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { c \times c }$ and $\mathbf { 0 } _ { c } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { c \times c }$ represent identity and zero matrices, respectively. $\| y \| = \sqrt { y ^ { \top } y }$ refers to an Euclidean norm of the column vector $y \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ . Let $[ v ] _ { \times }$ denote a skewsymmetric matrix such that

$$
[ v ] _ {\times} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 0 & - v _ {3} & v _ {2} \\ v _ {3} & 0 & - v _ {1} \\ - v _ {2} & v _ {1} & 0 \end{array} \right], \quad v = \left[ \begin{array}{c} v _ {1} \\ v _ {2} \\ v _ {3} \end{array} \right]
$$

For $M \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 \times 3 }$ and $w , v \in \mathbb { R } ^ { 3 }$ , the following characteristics hold true [11], [12]:

$$- [ v ] _ {\times} v = 0 _ {3 \times 1}[ w ] _ {\times} ^ {\top} = - [ w ] _ {\times}[ w ] _ {\times} v = - v [ w ] _ {\times} \tag {1}[ M v ] _ {\times} = M [ v ] _ {\times} M ^ {\top}$$

In this work, we consider a UAV travelling in 3D space where the vehicle’s moving-frame (body-frame) is denoted by $\{ B \} =$ $\{ x _ { B } , y _ { B } , z _ { B } \}$ while the inertial-frame is described by $\{ \mathcal { T } \} =$ $\{ x _ { p } , y _ { p } , z _ { p } \}$ .
