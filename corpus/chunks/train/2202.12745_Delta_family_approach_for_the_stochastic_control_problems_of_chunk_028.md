# 3.3 Optimal control stopping problem under SLV models

In this section, we solve the optimal control stopping problems under SLV models using the delta family methods combined with operator splitting technique. Consider a fixed time horizon [0, T ]. Let the stochastic processes and random variables be defined on a filtered probability space $( \Omega , F , \{ \mathcal { F } _ { t } \} _ { 0 \leq t \leq T } , P )$ , where P is the probability measure, and $F = \{ \mathcal { F } _ { t } \} _ { 0 < t < T }$ is the filtration generated by two standard Brownian motions $W _ { t } ^ { 1 }$ and $W _ { t } ^ { 2 }$ with $d \langle W _ { t } ^ { 1 } , W _ { t } ^ { 2 } \rangle = \rho d t$ and $- 1 \leq \rho \leq 1$ . In addition, we assume that the markets do not have transaction costs or taxes, and the trading happens continuously.

We assume that the financial market consists of one risk-free asset with interest rate zero and one risky asset. The price of the risky asset $S _ { t }$ is subject to the SLV model as follows

$$\frac {d S _ {t}}{S _ {t}} = \omega (S _ {t}, \mathcal {V} _ {t}) d t + \eta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) \Gamma (S _ {t}) d W _ {t} ^ {1},$$

and the variance process $\nu _ { t }$ is given by a fractional square-root process

$$d \mathcal {V} _ {t} = \beta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) d t + \zeta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) d W _ {s} ^ {2}.$$

Remark 3.1. Some typical SLV models are listed as follows:

(i) Heston model

$$\omega (S _ {t}, \mathcal {V} _ {t}) = (r + \lambda \mathcal {V} _ {t}) S _ {t}, \eta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \sqrt {\overline {{\mathcal {V} _ {t}}}}, \Gamma (S _ {t}) = S _ {t},\beta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \kappa (\theta - \mathcal {V} _ {t}), \zeta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \sigma \sqrt {\mathcal {V} _ {t}}. \tag {3.21}$$

(ii) $4 / 2$ model

$$\omega (S _ {t}, \mathcal {V} _ {t}) = (r + \lambda \mathcal {V} _ {t}) S _ {t}, \eta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = a \sqrt {\mathcal {V} _ {t}} + b / \sqrt {\mathcal {V} _ {t}}, \Gamma (S _ {t}) = S _ {t},\beta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \kappa (\theta - \mathcal {V} _ {t}), \quad \zeta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \sigma \sqrt {\mathcal {V} _ {t}}. \tag {3.22}$$

(iii) α-Hyper model

$$\omega (S _ {t}, \mathcal {V} _ {t}) = (r + \lambda \mathcal {V} _ {t}) S _ {t}, \eta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \exp (\mathcal {V} _ {t}), \Gamma (S _ {t}) = S _ {t},\beta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \kappa (\theta - \exp (a \mathcal {V} _ {t})), \zeta (\mathcal {V} _ {t}) = \sigma \sqrt {\mathcal {V} _ {t}}. \tag {3.23}$$
