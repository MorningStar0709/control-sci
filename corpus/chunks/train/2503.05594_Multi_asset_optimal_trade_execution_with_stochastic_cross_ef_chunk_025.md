We next introduce a running position target. To this end let $\zeta = ( \zeta ( s ) ) _ { s \in [ 0 , T ] }$ be an $\mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ -valued progressively measurable process such that

$$\left\| \gamma^ {\frac {1}{2}} \zeta \right\| _ {\mathcal {L} ^ {2}} < \infty . \tag {18}$$

We remark that

$$E \Bigg [ \Big | \int_ {0} ^ {T} (X (s) - \zeta (s)) ^ {\top} \Xi (s) (X (s) - \zeta (s)) d s \Big | \Bigg ]$$

is finite for all $X \in \mathcal { A } ^ { f v }$ (cf. Lemma 5.5).

Finally, we introduce for all $X \in \mathcal { A } ^ { f v }$ the cost functional $J ^ { f v }$ given by (3), that is,

$$J ^ {f v} (X) = E \left[ \int_ {[ 0, T ]} \left(D ^ {X} (s -)\right) ^ {\top} d X (s) + \frac {1}{2} \int_ {[ 0, T ]} (\Delta X (s)) ^ {\top} \gamma (s) d X (s) \right] \tag {19}+ E \bigg [ \int_ {0} ^ {T} (X (s) - \zeta (s)) ^ {\top} \Xi (s) (X (s) - \zeta (s)) d s \bigg ].$$

The finite-variation stochastic control problem is to minimize $J ^ { f v }$ over $\mathcal { A } ^ { f v }$ .

Remark 2.4. Similar to [4], we can consider general initial times $t \in [ 0 , T ]$ and replace the expected values by conditional expectations $E [ \cdot | \mathcal { F } _ { t } ]$ . All results in Section 2.4 and Section 3 as well as Corollary 4.1 remain valid.
