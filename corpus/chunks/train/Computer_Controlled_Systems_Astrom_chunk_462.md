It can be computed numerically by a finite-dimensional approximation of the state. This can, for example, be obtained through the discrete-time system obtained by dividing the sampling interval $h$ into $N$ equal parts. A complete treatment requires functional analysis, which is outside the scope of this book. Details are given in the References.

Another way to deal with frequency response of sampled systems is to realize that the output generated by a sinusoid with frequency $\omega_{0}$ contains the frequencies $\omega_{n}=n\omega_{n}\pm\omega_{0}$ . The system can then be properly characterized by the transfer functions for all those frequencies.
