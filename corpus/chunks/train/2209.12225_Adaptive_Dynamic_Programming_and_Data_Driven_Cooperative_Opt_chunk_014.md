Using Kronecker product properties, (24) can be written in a compact form as follows, wherein the approximated values of $P _ { i , k }$ and $K _ { i , k + 1 }$ can be solved in the sense of least square errors

$$
\Psi_ {i j k} \left[ \begin{array}{c} \operatorname{vecs} (P _ {i, k}) \\ \operatorname{vec} (K _ {i, k + 1}) \\ \operatorname{vec} ((D _ {i} - S _ {i} (X _ {i j}) ^ {\mathrm{T}} P _ {i, k}) \end{array} \right] = \Phi_ {i j k}, \tag {25}
$$

where

$$
\begin{array}{c} \Psi_ {i j k} = [ \delta_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} \bar {x} _ {i j}}, - 2 \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} \bar {x} _ {i j}} (I _ {n i} \otimes K _ {i, k} ^ {\mathrm{T}} R _ {i}) - 2 \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} u _ {i}} (I _ {n i} \otimes R _ {i}), \\ - 2 \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} v} ], \end{array}
\Phi_ {i j k} = - \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} \bar {x} _ {i j}} \operatorname{vec} \left(Q _ {i} + K _ {i, k} ^ {\mathrm{T}} R _ {i} K _ {i, k}\right),\delta_ {a} = \left[ \operatorname{vecv} \left(a \left(t _ {1}\right)\right) - \operatorname{vecv} \left(a \left(t _ {0}\right)\right), \dots , \right.\operatorname{vecv} \left(a \left(t _ {s}\right)\right) - \operatorname{vecv} \left(a \left(t _ {s - 1}\right)\right) ] ^ {\mathrm{T}},\Gamma_ {a, b} = \left[ \int_ {t _ {0}} ^ {t _ {1}} a \otimes b \mathrm{d} \tau , \int_ {t _ {1}} ^ {t _ {2}} a \otimes b \mathrm{d} \tau , \dots , \int_ {t _ {s - 1}} ^ {t _ {s}} a \otimes b \mathrm{d} \tau \right] ^ {\mathrm{T}}.$$

The time sequence $\{ t _ { l } \} _ { l = 0 } ^ { s }$ is a strictly increasing sequence. The uniqueness of the solution to equation (25) is guaranteed when the following rank condition is met:

$$\operatorname{rank} \left(\left[ \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} \bar {x} _ {i j}}, \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} u _ {i}}, \Gamma_ {\bar {x} _ {i j} v} \right]\right) = \frac {n _ {i} (n _ {i} + 1)}{2} + (m _ {i} + q _ {i}) n _ {i}. \tag {26}$$

Remark 3: In order to satisfy the condition in (26), exploration noise is added to the applied input during the learning phase. The exploration noise is usually random noise, random sinusoidal signals, or summation of sinusoidal signals with different frequencies.
