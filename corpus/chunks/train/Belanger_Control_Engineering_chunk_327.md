# 6.3.1 Introduction

The simplest controller design is a pure gain adjusted (as in the foregoing examples) to satisfy stability objectives. In many cases this does not satisfy the performance specifications, and it is therefore necessary to alter the shape of the loop gain frequency response by using a compensator with dynamics in cascade with the plant. This is known as dynamic compensation.

Bode's gain-phase relationship will give some useful insight about the shape of $L(j\omega)$ at crossover. We will then examine compensation schemes aimed at meeting steady-state specifications, followed by compensation to increase system bandwidth.
