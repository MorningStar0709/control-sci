According to Fig.10, the training curves for signal control and joint control show similar performance, with both converging within a few episodes. However, joint control surpasses signal control by establishing an enhanced control policy for both route and signal timings. This improvement leads to notable enhancements in network performance, including a higher number of arrived vehicles and a reduced average vehicle delay. On the other hand, the initial performance of the vehicle routing control is relatively high, owing to the initial random exploration and optimistic parameter settings that prompt the agents to take actions with higher rewards. However, as the training progresses, the agents fail to improve their strategies.
