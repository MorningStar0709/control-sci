# (b) Aerodynamic Pitching Moment

The aerodynamic pitching moment M is a function of the pitch rate $Q$ as well as $\alpha _ { p }$ and $\delta _ { p }$ . Define now the components of the pitching moment as

$$M = M _ {o} + M _ {q}, \tag {3.29}$$

where

Mo = moment contribution from the AOA (angle of attack) in pitch $( \alpha _ { p } )$ and pitch control surface deflection $( \delta _ { p } )$ ,

$M _ { q }$ = pitching moment rate.

These two components are commonly described in the following manner:

$$M _ {o} = C _ {m} q S l _ {r e f}, \tag {3.30a}M _ {q} = (C _ {M} Q q S (l _ {r e f}) ^ {2}) / 2 V _ {M}, \tag {3.30b}$$

where

$C _ { m }$ = pitching moment coefficient, which is a function of Mach number, $\alpha _ { p }$ , and $\delta _ { p }$ ,

$C _ { M }$ = coefficient of moment due to pitch rate, or rate of change of pitching moment,

$V _ { M }$ = total velocity of the missile.
