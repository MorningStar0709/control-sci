# A. Infinite dimensional Toeplitz block (TB) matrices

The Toeplitz transformation of a T −periodic function a ∈ $L ^ { 2 } ( [ 0 \ T ] , \mathbb { R } )$ , denoted $\cdot$ , defines a constant Toeplitz and infinite dimensional matrix as follows:

$$
\mathcal {T} (a) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c} \ddots & & \vdots & & \ddots \\ & a _ {0} & a _ {- 1} & a _ {- 2} \\ \dots & a _ {1} & a _ {0} & a _ {- 1} & \dots \\ & a _ {2} & a _ {1} & a _ {0} \\ \ddots & & \vdots & & \ddots \end{array} \right],
$$

$$\mathcal {H} (a ^ {+}) = \left(a _ {i + j - 1}\right) _ {i, j > 0}, \quad \mathcal {H} (a ^ {-}) = \left(a _ {- i - j + 1}\right) _ {i, j > 0}.$$
