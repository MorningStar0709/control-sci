# Remarks

• The nonsingularity of the matrix M implies the controllability and the observability of the associated state space representation.   
• The condition number of M allows to evaluate the ill conditioning of the matrix M, i.e., the closeness of some poles and zeros.   
• The matrix M is also used for solving the diophantine equation (Bezout identity):

$$A (z ^ {- 1}) S (z ^ {- 1}) + z ^ {- d} B (z ^ {- 1}) R (z ^ {- 1}) = P (z ^ {- 1})$$

for S and R, given P (see Sect. 7.3 Pole Placement).
