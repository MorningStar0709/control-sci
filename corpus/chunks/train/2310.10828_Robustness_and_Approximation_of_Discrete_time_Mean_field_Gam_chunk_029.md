Since $p _ { n }$ converges to p weakly and continuously, we obtain that $\mu _ { 1 } ^ { n }$ converges to $\mu _ { 1 }$ weakly [44, Theorem 3.3]. One can now proceed via induction by repeating the same argument mutatis mutandis since the convergence results we use will be sufficient. Suppose that up until k-th iteration, $( Q _ { k } ^ { n } , \mu _ { k } ^ { n } )$ converges to $\left( Q _ { k } , \mu _ { k } \right)$ as in the statement. From Step 1 and Lemma 4, we have $f ^ { n } ( x ^ { n } , Q _ { k } ^ { n } , \mu _ { k } ^ { n } ) \to f ( x , Q _ { k } , \mu _ { k } )$ whenever $x ^ { n }$ converges to x in X. Sufficient to show that $\mu _ { k + 1 } ^ { n } \to \mu _ { k + 1 }$ weakly as Step 1 takes care of convergence of the iteration of $Q _ { k + 1 } ^ { n }$ and $Q _ { k + 1 }$ . For an arbitrary real valued bounded continuous function g, we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left| \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, f ^ {n} (x, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) \mu_ {k} ^ {n} (d x) - \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f (x, Q _ {k}, \mu_ {k}), \mu_ {k}) \mu_ {k} (d x) \right| \\ \leq \left| \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, f ^ {n} (x, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) [ \mu_ {k} ^ {n} (d x) - \mu_ {k} (d x) ] \right| \\ + \int_ {X} \left| \int_ {X} g (y) \left[ p _ {n} (d y | x, f ^ {n} (x, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) - p (d y | x, f (x, Q _ {k}, \mu_ {k}), \mu_ {k}) \right] \right| \mu_ {k} (d x). \\ \end{array}
$$

Note that as in the base case of the induction, the following convergence holds

$$\int_ {X} g (y) p _ {n} (d y | x ^ {n}, f ^ {n} (x ^ {n}, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) \rightarrow \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f (x, Q _ {k}, \mu_ {k}), \mu_ {k}).$$

This implies that

$$
\begin{array}{l} \lim _ {n} \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, f ^ {n} (x, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) \mu_ {k} ^ {n} (d x) = \lim _ {n} \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, f ^ {n} (x, Q _ {k} ^ {n}, \mu_ {k} ^ {n}), \mu_ {k} ^ {n}) \mu_ {k} (d x) \\ = \int_ {X} \int_ {X} g (y) p (d y | x, f (x, Q _ {k}, \mu_ {k}), \mu_ {k}) \mu_ {k} (d x) \\ \end{array}
$$

once again by [44, Theorem 3.3]. Hence, we can conclude that the terms in the above inequality vanish. Thus, $\mu _ { k + 1 } ^ { n }$ converges to $\mu _ { k + 1 }$ weakly and we are done with the induction.

Step 3: Convergence of the mean-field equilibria to the limiting case

Based on the assumptions we have made, the value iteration algorithm yields a mean-field equilibrium under any transition probability $p _ { n }$ and $p$ as a consequence of assumption (v) in the theorem statement.
