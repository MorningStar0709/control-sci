# Regressor Form

Taking into account (2.64), the j step ahead predictor can be expressed as:

$$C \hat {y} (t + j) = F _ {j} y (t) + G _ {j} u (t + j - d - 1) \tag {2.84}$$

where:

$$G _ {j} = E _ {j} B ^ {*} = g _ {0} + g _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + g _ {j + n _ {B} - 2} q ^ {- j - n _ {B} + 2} \tag {2.85}$$

from which one obtains:

$$C \hat {y} (t + j) = \theta^ {T} \phi (t) \tag {2.86}$$

where:

$$\theta^ {T} = \left[ f _ {0} ^ {j}, \dots , f _ {n _ {F}} ^ {j}, g _ {0}, \dots , g _ {j + n _ {B} - 2} \right] \tag {2.87}\phi^ {T} (t) = [ y (t), \dots , y (t - n _ {F}), u (t + j - d - 1), \dots , u (t - n _ {B} - d + 1) ] \tag {2.88}$$

An alternative regressor form is:

$$\hat {y} (t + j) = \theta_ {e} ^ {T} \phi_ {e} (t) \tag {2.89}$$

where:

$$\theta_ {e} ^ {T} = [ \theta^ {T}, c _ {1}, \dots , c _ {n _ {C}} ] \tag {2.90}\phi_ {e} (t) = [ \phi^ {T} (t), - \hat {y} (t + j - 1), \dots , - \hat {y} (t + j - n _ {C}) ] \tag {2.91}$$

For the case j = 1, the predictor equation takes the form:

$$\hat {y} (t + 1) = \left(C ^ {*} - A ^ {*}\right) y (t) + q ^ {- d} B ^ {*} u (t) - C ^ {*} \hat {y} (t) \tag {2.92}$$

allowing the representation shown above. However, this predictor can also be rewritten as:

$$\hat {y} (t + 1) = - A ^ {*} y (t) + q ^ {- d} B ^ {*} u (t) + C ^ {*} [ y (t) - \hat {y} (t) ] \tag {2.93}$$

leading to the regressor form representation:

$$\hat {y} (t + 1) = \theta_ {e} ^ {T} \phi_ {e} (t) \tag {2.94}$$

where:

$$\theta_ {e} ^ {T} = \left[ a _ {1}, \dots , a _ {n _ {A}}, b _ {1}, \dots , b _ {n _ {B}}, c _ {1}, \dots , c _ {n _ {C}} \right] \tag {2.95}\phi_ {e} ^ {T} (t) = [ - y (t), \dots , - y (t - n _ {A} + 1), u (t - d), \dots , u (t - d - n _ {B} + 1),\varepsilon (t), \dots , \varepsilon (t - n _ {C} + 1) ] \tag {2.96}\varepsilon (t) = y (t) - \hat {y} (t) \tag {2.97}$$

Equation (2.93) can also be equivalently rewritten as (by adding $\pm A ^ { * } \hat { y } ( t ) \rangle$ ):

$$\hat {y} (t + 1) = - A ^ {*} \hat {y} (t) + q ^ {- d} B ^ {*} u (t) + [ C ^ {*} - A ^ {*} ] [ y (t) - \hat {y} (t) ] \tag {2.98}$$

leading to the regression form representation (2.94) where now:

$$\theta_ {e} ^ {T} = \left[ a _ {1}, \dots , a _ {n _ {A}}, b _ {1}, \dots , b _ {n _ {B}}, h _ {1}, \dots , h _ {n _ {C}} \right] \tag {2.99}\phi_ {e} ^ {T} (t) = [ - \hat {y} (t), \ldots , - \hat {y} (t - n _ {A} + 1), u (t - d), \ldots , u (t - d - n _ {B} + 1),\varepsilon (t), \dots , \varepsilon (t - n _ {C} + 1) ] \tag {2.100}h _ {i} = c _ {i} - a _ {i}; \quad i = 1, \dots , n _ {C} \tag {2.101}$$

These different forms of one step ahead predictor will be used for the estimation of the parameters of a plant model in a stochastic environment.

An important characteristic of the predictors for ARMAX models is that the predicted values depend upon (i) current and previous values of the input and output and (ii) current and previous values of the prediction.
