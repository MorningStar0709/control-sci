# 3.1 Mean field formulation

We show that the GPL dynamic can be obtained as a version of an MFG to better understand the relationship between them. The discussion in this section is heuristic but gives new insights into the two formulations. For controls as a random field, see the literature (Cartea et al., 2019; Kroell et al., 2023). See Section 3.3 for the applicability and limitation of the proposed methodology, particularly its theoretical and technical aspects.

Consider a continuous-time jump-driven stochastic process $X = \left( X _ { t } \right) _ { t \geq 0 }$ with a random field $a _ { t } \left( \cdot \right) : \Omega \to \left[ 0 , + \infty \right) \ \left( t \geq 0 \right)$ . We assume and expect that the solution to (25) is found in a strong (pathwise, right-continuous and has left limit) sense for each initial condition:

$$\mathrm{d} X _ {t} = \mathrm{d} P _ {t}, t > 0 \tag {25}$$

with an initial condition $X _ { 0 } = x \in \Omega$ , where $P = \left( P _ { t } \right) _ { t \geq 0 }$ is a compound jump process with a Lévy density $a _ { t } \left( \cdot \right)$ (a product of a PDF of jump size and jump intensity) and jump size $z _ { t } - X _ { t - }$ at time $t > 0$ , where $z _ { t }$ is a jump size valued in Ω. Then, it follows that $X _ { t }$ is almost surely contained in objective function to be maximized by  :

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (t, x, a) = \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {T} \frac {1}{a _ {s}} F (a _ {s}) \mathrm{d} P _ {s} + \int_ {t} ^ {T} G (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right] \\ = \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {T} \int_ {\Omega} \frac {a _ {s} (y)}{a _ {s} (y)} F (a _ {s} (y)) \mathrm{d} y \mathrm{d} s + \int_ {t} ^ {T} G (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) \mathrm{d} s \mid X _ {t} = x \right], 0 \leq t \leq T \tag {26} \\ = \mathbb {E} \left[ - \int_ {t} ^ {T} \int_ {\Omega} F (a _ {s} (y)) d y d s + \int_ {t} ^ {T} G (X _ {s}, \mu (s, \cdot)) d s \mid X _ {t} = x \right] \\ \end{array}
$$
