$$
w ^ {\top} X w - x ^ {\top} X x - \left[ \begin{array}{l} y \\ u \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Q & S \\ S ^ {\top} & R \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{l} y \\ u \end{array} \right] \preceq 0, \quad \forall w: \left[ \begin{array}{l} q \\ w \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \Delta \left[ \begin{array}{l} q \\ w \end{array} \right] \preceq 0. \tag {81}
$$

The infinitely many constraints (81) are implied by

$$
w ^ {\top} X w - x ^ {\top} X x - \left[ \begin{array}{c} y \\ u \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Q & S \\ S ^ {\top} & R \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} y \\ u \end{array} \right] - \alpha \left[ \begin{array}{c} q \\ w \end{array} \right] ^ {\top} \Delta \left[ \begin{array}{c} q \\ w \end{array} \right] \preceq 0,
$$

for any $\alpha \geq 0$ . This technique is well-know in robust control as the S-procedure (Boyd et al., 1994). With $q = { \binom { x } { u } }$ and $y =$ $C x + D u$ , we can write equivalently

$$
\underbrace {\left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ u \\ w \end{array} \right] ^ {\top}} _ {= \Omega} \phi^ {\top} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c} - X & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & X & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \hline 0 & 0 & - Q & - S & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & - S ^ {T} & - R & 0 \\ \hline 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & - \alpha \Delta \end{array} \right] \phi \left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ u \\ w \end{array} \right] \leq 0, \tag {82}
$$

with

$$
\phi = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} I & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & I \\ \hline C & D & 0 \\ 0 & I & 0 \\ \hline I & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & I & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & I \end{array} \right].
$$
