PROOF. Let $\xi \in \mathsf { C p } ( \mathsf { e } [ \cdot | \mathbf { x } ] , \mathsf { e } [ \cdot | \mathbf { z } ] )$ . Hence

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {\mathsf {X} \times \mathsf {X}} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x, y)   \xi (d x, d y) = \int_ {\mathsf {X} \times \mathsf {X}} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x, y)   \xi (d x | y)   \xi (d y) \\ = \frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \int_ {\mathsf {X}} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x, z _ {i}) \xi (d x | z _ {i}) \\ = \frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \int_ {\mathsf {X}} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x, z _ {i}) \xi (d x | z _ {i}) \\ \end{array}
$$

Note that for all $z _ { i }$ , the support of $\xi ( \cdot | z _ { i } )$ must be a subset of $\{ x _ { 1 } , \ldots , x _ { M } \}$ . This implies that the last term is equal to the following:

$$\frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x _ {j}, z _ {i}) \xi (x _ {j} | z _ {i})$$

Define the following matrix $A ^ { \xi } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { M \times M }$ as follows: $A _ { j , i } ^ { \xi } : = \xi ( x _ { j } | z _ { i } )$ for all $j , i = 1 , \dots , M$ Then, for any $i = 1 , \ldots , M$ , we have

$$\sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} A _ {j, i} ^ {\xi} = \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} \xi (x _ {j} | z _ {i}) = 1$$

Moreover, for any $j = 1 , \dots , M$ , we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} A _ {j, i} ^ {\xi} = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \xi (x _ {j} | z _ {i}) \xi (z _ {i}) \frac {1}{\xi (z _ {i})} \\ = M \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \xi (x _ {j}, z _ {i}) = M \xi (x _ {j}) = 1 \\ \end{array}
$$

Hence, $A ^ { \xi }$ is a doubly stochastic matrix. By Birkhoff–von Neumann theorem [4, Theorem 2.1.6], for some $k \geq 1$ and $\begin{array} { r } { \lambda _ { 1 } , \dots , \lambda _ { k } \in ( 0 , 1 ) , \sum _ { l = 1 } ^ { k } \lambda _ { l } = 1 } \end{array}$ , we can write

$$A ^ {\xi} = \sum_ {l = 1} ^ {k} \lambda_ {k} A ^ {l}$$

where $A ^ { l }$ is a permutation matrix which realizes some permutation $\sigma _ { l } \in S _ { \{ 1 : M \} }$ , for each $l = 1 , \ldots , k$ . Hence, we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {\mathsf {X} \times \mathsf {X}} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x, y) \xi (d x, d y) = \frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x _ {j}, z _ {i}) \xi (x _ {j} | z _ {i}) \\ = \frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} \sum_ {j = 1} ^ {M} \sum_ {l = 1} ^ {k} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x _ {j}, z _ {i}) \lambda_ {k} A _ {j, i} ^ {l} \\ = \sum_ {l = 1} ^ {k} \lambda_ {k} \left\{\frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x _ {j}, z _ {\sigma_ {l} (j)}) \right\} \\ \geq \inf _ {\sigma \in \mathcal {S} _ {\{1: M \}}} \frac {1}{M} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {M} d _ {\mathsf {X}} (x _ {i}, z _ {\sigma (i)}) \\ \end{array}
$$
