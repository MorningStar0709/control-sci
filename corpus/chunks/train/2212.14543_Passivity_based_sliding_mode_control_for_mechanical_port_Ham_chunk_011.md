# 3.1 Generalized kinetic potential energy shaping

In this subsection, we show one of the main results of this paper that the generalized version of KPES is proposed. The resulting closed-loop port-Hamiltonian systems have special potential functions depending nonlinearly on both configuration q and momentum η. The following lemma gives such closed-loop systems.

Lemma 1. Consider the system (3) with any function $U : \mathbb { R } ^ { m }  \mathbb { R } ,$ , any smooth vector function $\phi : \mathbb { R } ^ { m } \times \mathbb { R } ^ { m } \to \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ satisfying $\phi ( 0 , 0 ) = 0 ,$ , and any matrix $D _ { \mathrm { d } } ( q , \eta ) \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m \times m }$ . Suppose that the partial derivatives of $\cdot \phi ( q , \eta )$ with respect to q and η are nonsingular, that is, there exists

$$\frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial q} ^ {- 1}, \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial \eta} ^ {- 1}$$

for all q and η. Then, the feedback

$$u = - G (q) ^ {- 1} \left\{\left(D _ {\mathrm{d}} (q, \eta) - D (q, \eta)\right) \eta + \left(T (q) ^ {\top} \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial q} ^ {\top} + D _ {\mathrm{d}} (q, \eta) \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial \eta} ^ {\top}\right) \nabla_ {\sigma} U (\sigma) \right\} \tag {5}$$

converts (3) into the closed-loop Hamiltonian system

$$
\binom{\dot {q}}{\dot {\eta}} = \underbrace {\left( \begin{array}{c c} - T (q) \frac {\partial \sigma (q , \eta)}{\partial \eta} ^ {\top} \frac {\partial \sigma (q , \eta)}{\partial q} ^ {- \top} & T (q) \\ - T (q) ^ {\top} & - D _ {\mathrm{d}} (q , \eta) \end{array} \right)} _ {J _ {\mathrm{d}} (x)} \binom{\nabla_ {q} H _ {\mathrm{d}}}{\nabla_ {\eta} H _ {\mathrm{d}}},
H _ {\mathrm{d}} (q, \eta) = \frac {1}{2} \| \eta \| ^ {2} + U (\phi (q, \eta)), \tag {6}$$

where $\sigma \equiv \phi ( q , \eta )$ . Furthermore, if the function U is positive definite and smooth, and if

$$\frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial q} T (q) \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial \eta} ^ {\top} + \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial \eta} T (q) ^ {\top} \frac {\partial \phi (q , \eta)}{\partial q} ^ {\top} \succ 0, \tag {7}D _ {\mathrm{d}} (q, \eta) + D _ {\mathrm{d}} (q, \eta) ^ {\top} \succ 0 \tag {8}$$

hold, then the origin of the transformed system (6) is asymptotically stable with the Lyapunov function $H _ { \mathrm { d } } ( q , \eta )$ .
