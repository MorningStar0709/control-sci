$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} \leq - \sum_ {\substack {o = 1 \\ n}} ^ {n} k _ {1, o} z _ {o} ^ {2 q} - \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} k _ {2, o} z _ {o} ^ {2 p} + \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} \left(\frac {u _ {o} ^ {2}}{2} + \frac {\lambda_ {o} ^ {2}}{2}\right) \tag{21} \\ - \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} \frac {\tau_ {o}}{\varepsilon_ {o}} \tilde {\varphi} _ {o} \hat {\varphi} _ {o} + 0. 8 3 5 5 R \Phi \\ \end{array}
$$

Case 4: Self-triggered strategy

This self-triggered approach calculates the subsequent trigger time $t _ { k + 1 }$ by considering the present control signal $g ( t )$ , its rate of variation, and dynamic parameters $\theta , \vartheta _ { 1 } , \operatorname* { m a x } \{ | d ( t ) | , \pi \}$ . By removing the need for constant threshold monitoring typical of traditional event-triggered control, this framework maintains adaptive accuracy. The adaptive controller and triggering condition are designed as

$$d (t) = - (1 + \theta) (\alpha_ {n} \tanh \frac {z _ {n} \alpha_ {n}}{\Phi} + \bar {\vartheta} _ {1} \tanh \frac {z _ {n} \bar {\vartheta} _ {1}}{\Phi}) \tag {22}t _ {j + 1} = t _ {j} + \frac {\theta | g (t) | + \vartheta_ {1}}{\max \{| d (t) | , \pi \}} \tag {23}$$

where Φ, $\vartheta _ { 1 } , 0 < \theta < 1 , \tau$ π and $\bar { \vartheta } _ { 1 } > \vartheta _ { 1 } / ( 1 - \theta )$ are positive designed parameters. Similar to the (21), one gets

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} \leq - \sum_ {\substack {o = 1 \\ n}} ^ {n} k _ {1, o} z _ {o} ^ {2 q} - \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} k _ {2, o} z _ {o} ^ {2 p} + \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} \left(\frac {u _ {o} ^ {2}}{2} + \frac {\lambda_ {o} ^ {2}}{2}\right) \tag{24} \\ - \sum_ {o = 1} ^ {n} \frac {\tau_ {o}}{\varepsilon_ {o}} \tilde {\varphi} _ {o} \hat {\varphi} _ {o} + 0. 5 5 7 R \Phi \\ \end{array}
$$
