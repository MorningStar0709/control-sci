# I. INTRODUCTION

A UTONOMOUS underwater vehicles (AUVs) enableocean observation, inspection, and intervention [1]–[4]. Among AUV propulsion solutions, propeller thrusters dominate practical platforms because of its advantages in efficiency and robustness [5], [6]. Reliable trajectory tracking in missions is a core capability for thrusters [7], [8]. In field operations, the control of thruster is subject to faults that arise from blockage or entanglement, blade breakage, mechanical looseness, and motor or gear failures [9], [10].
