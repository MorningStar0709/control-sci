# 5.12 Matrix calculus

Matrix calculus uses partial derivatives. See subsection 4.1.4 for how they work.

We’ll need a vector-valued function to demonstrate some common operations in matrix calculus. Let f(x, u) be a vector-valued function defined as

$$
\mathbf {f} (\mathbf {x}, \mathbf {u}) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} f _ {1} (\mathbf {x}, \mathbf {u}) \\ \vdots \\ f _ {m} (\mathbf {x}, \mathbf {u}) \end{array} \right] \text {where} \mathbf {x} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ x _ {m} \end{array} \right] \text {and} \mathbf {u} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} u _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ u _ {n} \end{array} \right]
$$
