Figure 10.32 Characteristic root locations for Eq. (10.37).

A plot of the paths or loci followed by the n closed-loop poles (or roots) as a single parameter is varied is called the root locus. Figure 10.32 shows the root locus (the thick solid lines) for the two roots as the gain $K _ { P }$ is varied from zero to infinity. The root locus shown in Fig. 10.32 demonstrates that the two closed-loop roots start at the open-loop poles $( s = 0$ and $s = - 3 )$ ) when $K _ { P } = 0$ , move toward each other along the negative real axis as $K _ { P }$ increases until they meet at $s = - 1 . 5$ , and then move along vertical asymptotes with a real part equal to –1.5 as gain $K _ { P }$ is increased to infinity.
