$$
p \left(\left[ \begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {X} \\ Y \end{array} \right]\right) = \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {K} \omega_ {k} \mathcal {N} _ {k} \left(\left[ \begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {X} \\ Y \end{array} \right] \mid \left[ \begin{array}{l} \boldsymbol {\mu} _ {\boldsymbol {X}} \\ \mu_ {Y} \end{array} \right] _ {k}, \left[ \begin{array}{l l} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X X}} & \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X Y}} \\ \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {Y X}} & \Sigma_ {\boldsymbol {Y Y}} \end{array} \right] _ {k}\right). \tag {15}
$$

By applying the expectation maximization algorithm to the dataset, the parameters of the GMM (15) are iteratively optimized. Subsequently, the marginal probability density function of the input vector X is derived as

$$p (\boldsymbol {X}) = \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {K} \omega_ {k} \mathcal {N} _ {k} \left(\boldsymbol {X} \mid \boldsymbol {\mu} _ {\boldsymbol {X}, k}, \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X X}, k}\right). \tag {16}$$

Based on (16), the posterior responsibility of the k-th component given the input vector X is derived as

$$\gamma_ {k} (\boldsymbol {X}) = p (k \mid \boldsymbol {X}) = \frac {\omega_ {k} \mathcal {N} _ {k} \left(\boldsymbol {X} \mid \boldsymbol {\mu} _ {\boldsymbol {X} , k} , \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X X} , k}\right)}{\sum_ {j = 1} ^ {K} \omega_ {j} \mathcal {N} _ {j} \left(\boldsymbol {X} \mid \boldsymbol {\mu} _ {\boldsymbol {X} , j} , \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X X} , j}\right)}. \tag {17}$$

Moreover, the conditional expectation of Y given X within the k-th component is expressed as

$$m _ {k} (\boldsymbol {X}) = \mathbb {E} [ Y \mid \boldsymbol {X}, k ] = \mu_ {Y, k} + \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {Y \boldsymbol {X}, k} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\boldsymbol {X} \boldsymbol {X}, k} ^ {- 1} \left(\boldsymbol {X} - \boldsymbol {\mu} _ {\boldsymbol {X}, k}\right). \tag {18}$$

Combining (17) and (18), the total conditional expectation of the stability margin Y is obtained as

$$Y = f (\boldsymbol {X}) = \mathbb {E} [ Y \mid \boldsymbol {X} ] = \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {K} \gamma_ {k} (\boldsymbol {X}) m _ {k} (\boldsymbol {X}). \tag {19}$$

Formula (19) establishes a smooth (d + 1)-dimensional regression hypersurface over the sampled data points, effectively approximating the high-dimensional nonlinear mapping from the system parameters $( x _ { 1 } , \cdots , x _ { d } )$ to the stability margin y.
