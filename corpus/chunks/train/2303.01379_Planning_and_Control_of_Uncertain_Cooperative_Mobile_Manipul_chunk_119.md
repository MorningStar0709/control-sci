The main problem at hand is the design of a feedback control law $u _ { 1 } \in U _ { D _ { 1 } }$ for agent 1 which guarantees that the error signal $e _ { D _ { 1 } }$ with dynamics given in (2.65), satisfies lim $\mathsf { l } _ { t \to \infty } \| e _ { D _ { 1 } } ( x _ { q _ { 1 } } ( t ) ) \| \to 0$ , while ensuring singularity avoidance, collision avoidance between the leader, the object and the obstacles as well as collision avoidance between the leader and the followers in their current position. The role of the followers $\{ 2 , \ldots , N \}$ is, through the load-sharing coefficients $c _ { 2 } , \ldots , c _ { N }$ in (2.60), to contribute to the object trajectory execution, as derived by the leader agent 1, while also avoiding collisions. In order to solve the aforementioned problem, we propose a NMPC scheme, that is presented hereafter.

Consider a sequence of sampling times $\{ t _ { j } \} , j \in \mathbb { N }$ as defined in the centralized scheme, with $t _ { j + 1 } = t _ { j } + h _ { s } , h _ { s } \in ( 0 , T _ { p } )$ , and $T _ { p }$ the respective horizon. For agent 1, the open-loop input signal applied in between the sampling instants is given by the solution of the following FHOCP:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \min _ {\hat {u} _ {1} (\cdot)} J _ {D _ {1}} (e _ {D _ {1}} (x _ {q _ {1}} (t _ {j})), \hat {u} _ {1} (\cdot)) := \min _ {\hat {u} _ {1} (\cdot)} \left\{V _ {D _ {1}} (e _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (t _ {j} + T _ {p}))) \right. \\ \left. + \int_ {t _ {j}} ^ {t _ {j} + T _ {p}} \left[ F _ {D _ {1}} (e _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (s)), \hat {u} _ {1} (s)) \right] d s \right\} \tag {2.66a} \\ \end{array}
$$

subject to:

$$\dot {e} _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (s)) = f _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (s), \hat {u} _ {1} (s)), e _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (t _ {j})) = f _ {D _ {1}} (x _ {q _ {1}} (t _ {j})), \tag {2.66b}e _ {D _ {1}} (\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} (s)) \in E _ {D _ {1}} (q _ {- 1} (t _ {j})), s \in [ t _ {j}, t _ {j} + T _ {p} ], \tag {2.66c}\hat {u} _ {1} (s) \in U _ {D _ {1}}, s \in [ t _ {j}, t _ {j} + T _ {p} ], \tag {2.66d}e _ {D _ {1}} \left(\hat {x} _ {q _ {1}} \left(t _ {j} + T _ {p}\right)\right) \in \mathcal {E} _ {D _ {1}}. \tag {2.66e}$$
