# IV. RESULTS: SIMULATION AND FLIGHT DATA

We first apply the FCMW metric on a simulated quadrotor to demonstrate its use for cases with full model knowledge and actuator faults. Such faults are considered true LOC scenarios. As a more practical demonstration, the FCMW is also applied to real quadrotor flight data where LOC is suspected, but not guaranteed, to occur. For this case, a full model of the quadrotor is assumed to be unavailable, and thus the FCMW is derived only from estimates of B made on the flight data. Note, however, that the implicit assumption made here is that the quadrotor remains (theoretically) controllable by $r a n k ( \mathbf { C } ) = n$ . This assumption is reasonable since no (partial) actuator faults occur in the flight data.
