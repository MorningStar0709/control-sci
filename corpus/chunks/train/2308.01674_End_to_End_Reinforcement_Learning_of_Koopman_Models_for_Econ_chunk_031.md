$$c _ {\mathrm{ss}} = F _ {\mathrm{ss}} \cdot p _ {t} \cdot \Delta t _ {\mathrm{ctrl}}, \tag {11}$$

and the costs induced by the current control action

$$c _ {t} = F _ {t} \cdot p _ {t} \cdot \Delta t _ {\mathrm{ctrl}}, \tag {12}$$

using the electricity price $p _ { t }$ . Then, we calculate the reward

$$
r _ {t} = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} - 1, & \text { if   constraint   violation } \\ (c _ {\mathrm{ss}} - c _ {t}) \beta , & \text { otherwise }, \end{array} \right. \tag {13}
$$

wherein $\beta$ is a hyperparameter used to balance the influence of cost savings and constraint violations.
