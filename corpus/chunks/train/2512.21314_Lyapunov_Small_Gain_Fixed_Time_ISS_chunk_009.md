# A. Notation

We use $\mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }$ to denote the set of nonnegative real numbers. Given $n \in \mathbb { N } ,$ , we denote $[ n ] : = \{ 1 , 2 , . . . , n \}$ . We use ${ \mathcal { C } } ( X , Y )$ to denote the set of all continuous mappings $f : X \to Y$ between metric spaces $X$ and $Y$ . We use $f ^ { - 1 }$ to denote the inverse of a function $f .$ Moreover, if $X \subset \mathbb { R }$ and $Y \subset \mathbb { R } .$ , we use $f ^ { \prime }$ to denote the derivative of $f .$ We introduce the following classes of comparison functions, which will play an important role in this paper:

$$\mathcal {K} := \{\alpha \in \mathcal {C} (\mathbb {R} _ {\geq 0}, \mathbb {R} _ {\geq 0}): \alpha (0) = 0, \alpha \text { strictly increasing } \}\mathcal {K} _ {\infty} := \{\alpha \in \mathcal {K}: \lim _ {s \to \infty} \alpha (s) = \infty \}\mathcal {K} _ {\infty} ^ {P} := \{\alpha \in \mathcal {K} _ {\infty}: \alpha (s) = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n} c _ {i} s ^ {p _ {i}} \text {for some} c _ {1},..., c _ {n} \neq 0,\left. p _ {1}, \dots , p _ {n} > 0, \text { and } \alpha^ {\prime} (s) > 0 \forall s > 0 \right\}\mathcal {K} _ {\infty} ^ {\mathrm{FxT}} := \left\{\alpha \in \mathcal {K} _ {\infty} ^ {P}: \alpha (s) = c _ {1} s ^ {p _ {1}} + c _ {2} s ^ {p _ {2}} \text { for some } c _ {1}, c _ {2} > 0, \right.\left. p _ {1} \in (0, 1), p _ {2} > 1 \right\}\mathcal {K} _ {\infty} ^ {- P} := \{\alpha \in \mathcal {K} _ {\infty}: \alpha^ {- 1} \in \mathcal {K} _ {\infty} ^ {P} \}$$
