DEFINITION 2.1 z-TRANSFORM Consider the discrete-time signal $\{f(kh): k = 0,1,\ldots\}$ . The $z$ -transform of $f(kh)$ is defined as

$$\mathcal {Z} \{f (k h) \} = F (z) = \sum_ {k = 0} ^ {\infty} f (k h) z ^ {- k} \tag {2.27}$$

where $z$ is a complex variable. The $z$ -transform of $f$ is denoted by $\mathbb{Z}f$ or $F$ . The inverse transform is given by

$$f (k h) = \frac {1}{2 \pi i} \oint F (z) z ^ {k - 1} d z \tag {2.28}$$

where the contour of integration encloses all singularities of $F(z)$ .
