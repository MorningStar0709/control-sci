# Partial-fraction expansion with complex poles

When the poles of the Laplace transform Y(s) are complex, the partial-fraction expansion method presented by Eq. (8.15) can be used. In this case, however, the residues $a _ { i }$ associated with Eq. (8.15) will themselves be complex coefficients, which can lead to cumbersome algebra with complex numbers. An alternative approach is to “complete the square” of the denominator of the Laplace transform so that it fits the format of entries 10 and 11 in Table 8.1. This method is best illustrated with an example.
