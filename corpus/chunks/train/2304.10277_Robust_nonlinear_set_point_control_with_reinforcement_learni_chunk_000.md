# Robust nonlinear set-point control with reinforcement learning

Ruoqi Zhang1 , Per Mattsson1 and Torbjorn Wigren ¨ 1

Abstract— There has recently been an increased interest in reinforcement learning for nonlinear control problems. However standard reinforcement learning algorithms can often struggle even on seemingly simple set-point control problems. This paper argues that three ideas can improve reinforcement learning methods even for highly nonlinear set-point control problems: 1) Make use of a prior feedback controller to aid amplitude exploration. 2) Use integrated errors. 3) Train on model ensembles. Together these ideas lead to more efficient training, and a trained set-point controller that is more robust to modelling errors and thus can be directly deployed to realworld nonlinear systems. The claim is supported by experiments with a real-world nonlinear cascaded tank process and a simulated strongly nonlinear pH-control system.
