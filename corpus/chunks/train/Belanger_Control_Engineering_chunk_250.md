Other rules exist concerning the points where the locus leaves the real axis (breakaway points) or enters the real axis (breaking points) and the angles at which the branches leave poles or arrive at zeros. With the development of computer packages, those rules have lost some of their former importance. (The reader interested in the details is referred to any of the classical textbooks on control, such as Dorf [4] and Shinners.)
