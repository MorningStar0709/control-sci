# The Servo Problem

In the servo problem, the task is to make the process variables respond to changes in a command signal in a given way. Servo performance is typically specified in terms of requirements on the step response or the frequency response. Typical specifications for step responses include settling time and overshoot. Specifications can also be given in the frequency domain, for example, in terms of bandwidth. An alternative is to use a model that gives the desired response to command signals.

It is often very advantageous to use a two-degree-of-freedom configuration, because this admits a complete decoupling of the responses to disturbances and command signals. For such systems the feedback is first designed to solve the regulation problem and the feedforward is then designed to solve the servo problem. Examples of this were given in Secs. 4.6 and 5.4.
