In missiles, the center of gravity (cg) normally shifts due to the burning off of fuel. Consequently, a new moment is produced. Therefore, $M _ { o }$ and $N _ { o }$ are evaluated at a reference center of gravity at launch. Also, the actual center of gravity moves when the missile begins to move. However, the referenced center of gravity remains in the original position. Thus, applying this concept of a referenced center of gravity, the moment contributions due to the movement of the actual center of gravity can be evaluated.
