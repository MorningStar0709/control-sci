# 6.1. Implementations

Noise and Brightness Calibration We calibrate brightness and noise using a Basler Dart DaA1280-54uc camera [3], with an exposure time of 5 ms, a gain of 10 dB, and an aperture of f/16. These settings ensure unsaturated images under daylight with a large DoF. Noise calibration follows [51, 53], and brightness is calibrated by matching intensity histograms of physically captured and images captured by the simulator or from the dataset.

Perception Task The perception task is object detection with Faster R-CNN [37]. We use a ResNet-50 [17] backbone and pretrain the model on ImageNet [11]. Training uses the AdamW optimiser [31] with batch size 1, learning rate 1 · 10−4, and weight decay 1 · 10−6.

Camera Hardware Optimisation Camera hardware parameters are optimised using a GA with 10 candidates per generation over 35 generations, totalling 350 designs. Fitness is defined as the mean Average Precision (mAP) averaged over intersection over union (IoU) thresholds from 0.5 to 0.95. When optimising the camera’s FoV, we add the ratio of true positives at 0.5 IoU to all visible objects in a 180◦ horizontal FoV as a secondary metric. This metric avoids overfitting to narrow FoVs that may inflate mAP. We assign a weight of 1 to the mAP term and 1.1 to the true positive (TP) ratio term, based on empirical tuning.

Adaptive Control Network Optimisation The ACC model is trained using Adam [24] with a learning rate of 1 · 10−5 and a batch size of 1. The fitness function for optimising perturbations is the AP at an IoU threshold of 0.5, using a single threshold to reduce training time.

The supplementary material provides further details on GA implementation for perturbations and camera hardware.
