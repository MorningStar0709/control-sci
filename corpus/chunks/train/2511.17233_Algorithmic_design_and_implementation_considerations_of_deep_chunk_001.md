# I. INTRODUCTION

Deep learning-based MPC utilizes the constraint satisfaction capability of MPC and the function approximation capability of Deep Neural Network (DNN). It is evident that machine learning and deep learning techniques can enhance the performance of MPC. However, there is still a lack of successful results demonstrating the use of deep learning to augment MPC policies in uncertain environments, employing deep neural networks (DNNs) to learn uncertainties online [1]. One of the results to address such problem was reported in [2].

Deep MPC [2] is based on Tube-MPC [3] and inherits mathematical analysis from tube-MPC itself, which guarantees input-to-state stability of the controlled system. In addition, the idea of [2] is based on collecting the training data for DNN online while the system is in operation and the training data is collected from a DNN itself, similar to [4]. This mechanism is summarized in Fig. 1 and will be discussed in more detail later.

To integrate a DNN within the tube-MPC framework, it is essential that the DNN produces bounded outputs. This allows MPC to treat the DNN output as an additional bounded disturbance [5]. This requirement can be addressed by using bounded activation functions in the output layer and constraining the weights (trainable parameters of the outermost layer) through projection onto a bounded set.
