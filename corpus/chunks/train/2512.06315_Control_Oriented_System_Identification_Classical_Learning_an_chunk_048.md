# 4.2.1. Implicit Representations

Implicit representations of nonlinear systems that allow for a convex parameterization of all stable models have been introduced to facilitate robust nonlinear system identification minimizing simulation or prediction errors (Megretski, 2008; Tobenkin et al., 2010; Manchester et al., 2012; Umenberger and Manchester, 2018). For example, considering an implicit representation of the form

$$e (x _ {k + 1}) = f (x _ {k}, u _ {k}), \quad y _ {k} = g (x _ {k}, u _ {k}), \tag {37}$$

$$

Such implicit representations have also been extended to obtain convex parametrizations guaranteeing monotonicity, positivity, and contraction properties (Revay et al., 2021).
