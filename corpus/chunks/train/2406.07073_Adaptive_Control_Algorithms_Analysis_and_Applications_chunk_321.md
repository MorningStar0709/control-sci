# Controller Equation

The controller equation is given by:

$$S (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) = - R (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) + P (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {\star} (t + d + 1) \tag {7.107}$$

Taking into account the form of $S ( q ^ { - 1 } )$

$$
\begin{array}{l} S (q ^ {- 1}) = s _ {0} + s _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + s _ {n _ {S}} q ^ {- n _ {S}} = s _ {0} + q ^ {- 1} S ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) \\ = B ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) S ^ {\prime} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.108} \\ \end{array}
$$

with

$$s _ {0} = b _ {1} \tag {7.109}$$

Equation (7.107) takes the form:

$$u (t) = \frac {1}{b _ {1}} [ P (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {\star} (t + d + 1) - S ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) u (t - 1) - R (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) ] \tag {7.110}$$

or in a regressor form:

$$\theta_ {C} ^ {T} \phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) = P (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {\star} (t + d + 1) \tag {7.111}$$

where:

$$\phi_ {C} ^ {T} (t) = [ u (t), \dots , u (t - n _ {S}), y (t), \dots , y (t - n _ {R}) ] \tag {7.112}\theta_ {C} ^ {T} = [ s _ {0}, \dots , s _ {n _ {S}}, r _ {0}, \dots , r _ {n _ {R}} ] \tag {7.113}$$

Observe in (7.110) that low values of $| b _ { 1 } |$ may lead to very large plant inputs. But this is exactly what happens when one has a significant fractional delay or an unstable zero.
