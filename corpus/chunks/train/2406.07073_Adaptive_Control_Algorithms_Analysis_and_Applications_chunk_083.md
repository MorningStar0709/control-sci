To see that r effectively corresponds to the dimension of the minimal state space representation let us consider the following observable canonical form:

$$x (t + 1) = A _ {0} x (t) + B _ {0} u (t) \tag {2.21}y (t) = C _ {0} x (t) \tag {2.22}$$

where $x ( t )$ is the state vector and the matrices (or vectors) $A _ { 0 }$ , $B _ { 0 }$ , $C _ { 0 }$ are given by:

(a) $n _ { B } + d > n _ { A }$

$$
A _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c c c} - a _ {1} & 1 & & & & & & \\ \vdots & & \ddots & & & & 0 & \\ \vdots & & & \ddots & & & \\ - a _ {n _ {A}} & \dots & \dots & \dots & 1 & \dots & \dots & \\ 0 & & & & & \ddots & \\ \vdots & & 0 & & & & \ddots & \\ 0 & & & & & & & 1 \end{array} \right] \Bigg \} n _ {A}   \Bigg \} n _ {B} + d - n _ {A}

B _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \\ b _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ \vdots \\ b _ {n _ {B}} \end{array} \right] \Bigg \} d
C _ {0} = [ 1, 0, \dots , 0 ]$$

(b) $n _ { A } \geq n _ { B } + d$

$$
A _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} - a _ {1} & 1 & \dots & \dots & \dots & 0 \\ \vdots & & \ddots & & & \vdots \\ \vdots & & & \ddots & & \vdots \\ \vdots & & & & \ddots & 0 \\ - a _ {n _ {A}} & & & & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right], \qquad B _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \\ b _ {1} \\ \vdots \\ b _ {n _ {B}} \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{array} \right] \left\{ \begin{array}{l} d \\ n _ {B} \\ n _ {A} - (n _ {B} + d) \end{array} \right.
C _ {0} = [ 1, 0, \dots , 0 ]$$

The input-output transfer function is given by:

$$G (z) = C _ {0} (z I - A _ {0}) ^ {- 1} B _ {0} = \frac {\bar {B} (z)}{\bar {A} (z)} = \frac {z ^ {r - d} B (z ^ {- 1})}{z ^ {r} A (z ^ {- 1})} \tag {2.23}$$
