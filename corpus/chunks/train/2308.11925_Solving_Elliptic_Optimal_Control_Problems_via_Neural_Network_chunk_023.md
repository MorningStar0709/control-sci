$$
\begin{array}{l} - \Delta \tilde {e} _ {y} ^ {R} = f + u _ {\sigma} + \Delta y _ {\theta} \text {in} \Omega \quad \text {with} \quad \tilde {e} _ {y} ^ {R} = 0 \text {on} \partial \Omega , \\ - \Delta \tilde {e} _ {p} ^ {R} = y _ {\theta} - y _ {d} + \Delta p _ {\sigma} \mathrm{in} \Omega \quad \mathrm{with} \quad \tilde {e} _ {p} ^ {R} = 0 \mathrm{on} \partial \Omega . \\ \end{array}
$$

By the standard elliptic regularity estimate, we have

$$\left\| \tilde {e} _ {y} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \leq c \left\| f + u _ {\sigma} + \Delta y _ {\theta} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \quad \mathrm{and} \quad \left\| \tilde {e} _ {p} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \leq c \left\| y _ {\theta} - y _ {d} + \Delta p _ {\sigma} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)}.$$

Hence, we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\| R \bar {y} - y _ {\theta} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} = \left\| e _ {y} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} = \left\| \tilde {e} _ {y} ^ {R} - \zeta^ {y} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \leq \left\| \tilde {e} _ {y} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} + \left\| \zeta^ {y} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \\ \leq c \left\| f + u _ {\sigma} + \Delta y _ {\theta} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} + c \left\| y _ {\theta} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\partial \Omega)}, \\ \| R \bar {p} - p _ {\sigma} \| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} = \left\| e _ {p} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} = \left\| \tilde {e} _ {p} ^ {R} - \zeta^ {p} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \leq \left\| \tilde {e} _ {p} ^ {R} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} + \| \zeta^ {p} \| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} \\ \leq c \left\| y _ {\theta} - y _ {d} + \Delta p _ {\sigma} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\Omega)} + c \left\| p _ {\sigma} \right\| _ {L ^ {2} (\partial \Omega)}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Therefore, we obtain the desired estimates and complete the proof of the lemma.
