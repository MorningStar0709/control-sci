As the system outputs for different contingency scenarios are compared for contingency detection, the min-max criteria is chosen for $\delta _ { m i n }$ as

$$
\delta_ {m i n} = \min _ {\substack {i, j \in S \\ i \neq j}} \max _ {t \in [ 0, \tau_ {0} ]} | y _ {i} ^ {0} (t) - y _ {j} ^ {0} (t) |. \tag{13}
$$

This formulation ensures that $\delta _ { m i n }$ represents the smallest maximum deviation between output trajectories of any two distinct contingency scenarios, which helps in distinguishing them effectively by maximizing the separability of the closest scenarios in terms of their system responses.
