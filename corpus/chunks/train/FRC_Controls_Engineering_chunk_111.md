# 5.9.1 Special constant matrices

I is the identity matrix, a typically square matrix with ones along its diagonal and zeroes elsewhere. 0 is a matrix filled with zeroes and 1 is a matrix filled with ones. An optional subscript m × n denotes the matrix having m rows and n columns.

$$
\mathbf {I} _ {3 \times 3} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \quad \mathbf {0} _ {3 \times 2} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right] \quad \mathbf {1} _ {3 \times 2} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{array} \right]
$$
