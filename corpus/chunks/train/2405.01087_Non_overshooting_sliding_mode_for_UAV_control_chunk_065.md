In the following, we discuss the case $| e _ { 1 } \left( t \right) | \le e _ { 1 c }$ .

Finite-time stability conditions

For the sliding mode (18) when $| e _ { 1 } \left( t \right) | \le e _ { 1 c }$ a Lyapunov function candidate is selected as

$$V _ {1} = \frac {1}{2} \sigma (t) ^ {2} \tag {82}$$

where, the sliding function $\sigma \left( t \right) = e _ { 2 } \left( t \right) + k _ { 1 } e _ { 1 } \left( t \right)$ . Then, we get

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} _ {1} = \left(e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t)\right) \left\{- k _ {2} \mathrm{sign} [ e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t) ] + d (t) + k _ {1} e _ {2} (t) \right\} \\ = - k _ {2} \left| e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t) \right| + \left(k _ {1} e _ {2} (t) + d (t)\right) \left(e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t)\right) \\ \leq - k _ {2} \left| e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t) \right| + \left(k _ {1} \left| e _ {2} (t) \right| + L _ {d}\right) \left| e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t) \right| \\ = - \left(k _ {2} - k _ {1} \left| e _ {2} (t) \right| - L _ {d}\right) \left| e _ {2} (t) + k _ {1} e _ {1} (t) \right| \\ = - \sqrt {2} \left(k _ {2} - k _ {1} \left| e _ {2} (t) \right| - L _ {d}\right) V ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \tag {83} \\ \end{array}
$$

If $k _ { 2 } > k _ { 1 } | e _ { 2 } ( t ) | + L _ { d } .$ , then the sliding mode is finite-time stable. That means there exists a finite time $t _ { s } > 0$ , for $t \geq t _ { c } + t _ { s }$ , the sliding function $\sigma \left( t \right) = e _ { 2 } \left( t \right) + k _ { 1 } e _ { 1 } \left( t \right) = 0$ .
