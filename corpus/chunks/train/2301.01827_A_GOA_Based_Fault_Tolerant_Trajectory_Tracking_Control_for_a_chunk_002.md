HE study of vehicle controls has been extended to various conditions where fault tolerance is widely involved in the corresponding control design, which is denoted as faulttolerant control (FTC) [1, 2]. Scientists have worked on the FTC study for decades in various fields such as crafts in the air or space, land vehicles and industrial manufacturing [3–10]. In previous studies, the FTC is usually applied to alleviate abrupt errors and provides the most feasible solution when inevitable damages happen for equipment in different fields [11]. However, the research regarding the FTC on underwater vehicles (UV) has not been thoroughly investigated, due to the complexity brought by the underwater environment and the UV system [12–15].
