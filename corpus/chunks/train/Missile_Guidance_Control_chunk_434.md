Because most users are interested only in the weapon delivery accuracy about the target, an estimation of sigma may be made by root-sum-square addition of the alongtrack and crosstrack standard deviations with their respective mean points of impact. Then, the CEP may be computed as indicated in paragraph (5), above. This estimation is fairly accurate if the mean point of impact is a small distance from the target center (i.e., less than 25% of the standard deviation). If the MPI is a large distance from the target center, the system has a bias that should be corrected. Thus,

$$\sigma_ {X _ {T}} \approx (\sigma_ {X} ^ {2} + \bar {X} _ {M} ^ {2}) ^ {1 / 2}, \tag {5.15a}\sigma_ {Y _ {T}} \approx (\sigma_ {Y} ^ {2} + \bar {Y} _ {M} ^ {2}) ^ {1 / 2}. \tag {5.15b}$$
