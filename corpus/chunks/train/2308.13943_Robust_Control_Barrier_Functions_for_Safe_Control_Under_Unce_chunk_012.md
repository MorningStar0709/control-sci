$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \begin{array}{c} \hat {x} (k + 1) \\ \hat {f} _ {i} (k + 1) \end{array} \right] = A \left[ \begin{array}{c} \hat {x} (k) \\ \hat {f} _ {i} (k) \end{array} \right] + B (b (\hat {x} (k)) + a (\hat {x} (k)) u _ {i} (k)) \\ + L \left(y _ {i} (k) - C \left[ \begin{array}{c} \hat {x} (k) \\ \hat {f} _ {i} (k) \end{array} \right]\right) \tag {13} \\ \end{array}
$$

where ${ \hat { x } } ( k )$ and ${ \hat { f } } _ { i } ( k )$ are the estimations of $x ( k )$ and $f _ { i } ( k )$ , and L is the observer gain. Since the system (13) is discrete, the eigenvalues of $A - L C$ need to be placed at $\omega _ { o }$ inside the unit circle to make the observer converge.

Assumption 2. There exist positive known constants $l _ { f }$ and $b _ { f }$ such that for any $x \in X , u \in U$ , and $t \geq 0$ , the following inequality holds:

$$\left| \frac {\partial f _ {i} (t , x , u)}{\partial t} \right| \leq l _ {f}, \quad | f _ {i} (t, x, u) | \leq b _ {f}. \tag {14}$$

Remark 1. This assumption essentially states that the change and the magnitude of the disturbance $f _ { i } ,$ , rather than $d _ { i } ,$ , for each output measurement are bounded.

Based on the results of our previous work [15], the estimation error of ${ \hat { f } } _ { i }$ is as follows:

$$f _ {i} (k) - \hat {f} _ {i} (k) = p (k) * \Delta f _ {i} (k) \tag {15}
p (k) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} 1 & 1 \leq k \leq r + 1 \\ \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {r + 1} \frac {1}{(i - 1) !} (1 - \omega_ {o}) ^ {i - 1} \left(\prod_ {j = - i + 1} ^ {- 1} (k + j)\right) \omega_ {o} ^ {k - i} & k \geq r + 2. \end{array} \right. \tag {16}
$$

For simplicity, let $\textstyle \prod _ { j = 0 } ^ { - 1 } ( k + j ) = 1$ . From the definition of derivative and Assumption 2, we have $f _ { i } ( t + T ) - f _ { i } ( t ) \leq$ $l _ { f } T$ , where $T$ is the estimation sample time, $l _ { f }$ is the upper bound defined in (14). For simplicity, let us define

$$\gamma (\omega_ {o}, T) = \left(\sum_ {k = 1} ^ {\infty} p (k)\right) l _ {f} T \tag {17}$$

Lemma 1. Given the system (11) and the discrete ESO in (13), subject to Assumption 2, the estimation error bound of disturbance can be obtained as

$$\left| f _ {i} (k) - \hat {f} _ {i} (k) \right| \leq \gamma \left(\omega_ {o}, T\right). \tag {18}$$
