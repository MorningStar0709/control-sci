# 1.3.3 Constraints

The control $\mathbf{u}(t)$ and state $\mathbf{x}(t)$ vectors are either unconstrained or constrained depending upon the physical situation. The unconstrained problem is less involved and gives rise to some elegant results. From the physical considerations, often we have the controls and states, such as currents and voltages in an electrical circuit, speed of a motor, thrust of a rocket, constrained as

$$\mathbf {U} _ {+} \leq \mathbf {u} (t) \leq \mathbf {U} _ {-}, \quad \text { and } \quad \mathbf {X} _ {-} \leq \mathbf {x} (t) \leq \mathbf {X} _ {+} \tag {1.3.10}$$

where, +, and - indicate the maximum and minimum values the variables can attain.
