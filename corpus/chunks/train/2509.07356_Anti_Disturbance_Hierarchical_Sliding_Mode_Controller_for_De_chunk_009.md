# 3.2.1 Sliding Mode Control Term

Sliding mode control limits the control input using a saturation function, defined as:

$$u _ {s m 0} (t) = - K _ {s 0} \operatorname{sat} \left(\frac {s _ {0} (t)}{\phi_ {0}}\right) \tag {10}u _ {s m 1} (t) = - K _ {s 1} \operatorname{sat} \left(\frac {s _ {1} (t)}{\phi_ {1}}\right) - K _ {r 1} \dot {s} _ {1} (t) \tag {11}u _ {s m 2} (t) = - K _ {s 2} \operatorname{sat} \left(\frac {s _ {2} (t)}{\phi_ {2}}\right) - K _ {r 2} \dot {s} _ {2} (t) \tag {12}$$

The function $\operatorname { s a t } ( \cdot )$ is a smooth saturation function, defined as:

$$\operatorname{sat} (\cdot) = \frac {\cdot}{| \cdot | + \phi} \tag {13}$$

where $\phi$ is the smoothing factor used to adjust the saturation range. When | · | is small, sat(·) behaves approximately as a linear function; when | · | is large, its output gradually approaches a constant value of −1 or 1.
