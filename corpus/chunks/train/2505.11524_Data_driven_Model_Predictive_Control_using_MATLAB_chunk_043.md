# 3.5 PEM based D-LMPC

Prediction error methods (PEMs) are optimization-based SysID approaches in which the model parameters are estimated by minimizing a function of the prediction error [15]. The training data is considered to be available in the form of input-output samples as in Eq. (33). Let the unknown parameters to be identified: A, B, C and $\mathbf { x } _ { \mathrm { 0 } }$ be stored in a parameter matrix as:

$$
\boldsymbol {\theta} _ {\mathbf {x}} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l l} \mathbf {A} & \mathbf {B} & \mathbf {C} ^ {\top} & \mathbf {x} _ {0} \end{array} \right]. \tag {61}
$$

The predicted state and output sequence for the training data can be computed as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {X} _ {\mathrm{D}} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l l} \mathbf {A x} _ {0} + \mathbf {B u} _ {0} & \mathbf {A} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} _ {0} + \mathbf {A B u} _ {0} + \mathbf {B u} _ {1} & \dots & \mathbf {A} ^ {\mathrm{D}} \mathbf {x} _ {0} + \mathbf {A} ^ {\mathrm{D} - 1} \mathbf {B u} _ {0} + \dots + \mathbf {B u} _ {\mathrm{D} - 1} \end{array} \right] \tag {62} \\ \hat {\mathbf {Y}} _ {\mathrm{D}} = \mathbf {C} \mathbf {X} _ {\mathrm{D}} = \mathbf {f} _ {\mathrm{yp}} (\pmb {\theta} _ {\mathbf {x}}). \\ \end{array}
$$

The loss function for PEM is defined as

$$L _ {y} = \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {\mathrm{D}} \| \mathbf {y} _ {k} - \hat {\mathbf {y}} _ {k} \| ^ {2} = \| \mathbf {Y} _ {\mathrm{D}} - \hat {\mathbf {Y}} _ {\mathrm{D}} \| _ {F} ^ {2} = \operatorname{trace} \left(\left[ \mathbf {Y} _ {\mathrm{D}} - \mathbf {f} _ {\mathrm{yp}} \left(\boldsymbol {\theta} _ {\mathbf {x}}\right) \right] ^ {\top} \left[ \mathbf {Y} _ {\mathrm{D}} - \mathbf {f} _ {\mathrm{yp}} \left(\boldsymbol {\theta} _ {\mathbf {x}}\right) \right]\right). \tag {63}$$

The optimization problem for PEM-based linear state-space model identification becomes:

$$\min _ {\boldsymbol {\theta} _ {x}} L _ {y}. \tag {64}$$
