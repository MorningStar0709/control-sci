# 3. Continuous extension to progressively measurable strategies

The aim in this section is to extend the problem formulation of Section 2.3 to a broader class of admissible strategies. The alternative representations of the execution costs and of the deviation process that are derived in Section 2.4 lead to our problem formulation for progressively measurable strategies in Section 3.1. We show in the main result Theorem 3.9 in Section 3.2 that the control problem in Section 3.1 can be interpreted as the continuous extension to progressively measurable strategies of the control problem for finite-variation strategies stated in Section 2.3. As helpful tools we introduce the stochastic differential equation (SDE) (25) and the bijection (27) (see moreover Proposition 3.6) between our set of progressively measurable execution strategies and the set of square-integrable progressively measurable processes.
