Proof: To prove this theorem, we use complete (strong) induction, starting with the base cases for $l = 1$ and $l = 2 ,$ assuming the validity of the statement for all values up to l − 1, and then proving the result for l.

Base step (l = 1 and l = 2): For l = 1, the Riccati equation in (23) can be rewritten as

$$
\begin{array}{l} P _ {1} = A _ {1} ^ {\top} \left(B _ {1, 1} P _ {1} ^ {- 1} B _ {1, 1} + B _ {2, 1} R _ {1} ^ {- 1} B _ {2, 1} ^ {\top}\right) ^ {- 1} A _ {1} + Q \tag {24} \\ = A ^ {\mathsf {T}} \big (P _ {1} ^ {- 1} + B R ^ {- 1} B ^ {\mathsf {T}} \big) ^ {- 1} A + Q, \\ \end{array}
$$

which is a reformulation of the standard Riccati equation

$$P _ {1} = A ^ {\mathsf {T}} P _ {1} A - A ^ {\mathsf {T}} P _ {1} B \big (R + B ^ {\mathsf {T}} P _ {1} B \big) ^ {- 1} B ^ {\mathsf {T}} P _ {1} A + Q, \tag {25}$$

obtained by applying the Woodbury matrix identity. Therefore, under Assumptions 2 and $^ { 3 , }$ and given the positive definiteness of the weighting matrices, the Riccati equation for the Re-MPC at this step has a unique PD solution $P _ { 1 } \succ 0$ . For $l = 2 .$ , the Riccati equation in (23) can be written as

$$

where $\mathcal { L } \ = \ Q ^ { - 1 } + B R ^ { - 1 } B ^ { \mathsf { T } }$ and $\mathcal { P } \ = \ P _ { 2 } ^ { - 1 } + B R ^ { - 1 } B ^ { \mathsf { T } }$ .
