The state-space abstraction, described in Definition 3, partitions the state-space into a finite number of regions that are mutually disjoint, together span the entire state-space, and the transitions between the regions under the subsystems dynamics are well-defined. Notice that the individual sets in the partition, $\mathcal { X } _ { j } , j = 1 , 2 , \dotsc , n$ are allowed to contain infinite elements. Clearly, whether such a partition exists, depends on the subsystems dynamics, $( A _ { i } , b _ { i } ) , i \in \mathcal { N }$ and the set of admissible continuous control, U. We shall employ this state-space abstraction towards designing maximum hands-off hybrid control sequences for the switched system (2.1). Our results will, therefore, be applicable to discrete-time switched linear systems that admit the state-space abstractions under consideration. In Section 4 we will provide sufficient conditions on the subsystems dynamics, $( A _ { i } , b _ { i } ) , i \in \mathcal { N }$ and the set of admissible continuous control, U, under which a switched system (2.1) will admit a state-space abstraction.
