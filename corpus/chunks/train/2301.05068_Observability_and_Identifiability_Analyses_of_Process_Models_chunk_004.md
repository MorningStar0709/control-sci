# 2 Methods

In this work, we consider systems of ordinary differential equations of the form:

$$
M: \left\{ \begin{array}{r l} \dot {x} & = f (x (t), u (t), \theta) \\ y & = h (x (t), \theta) \\ x \left(t _ {0}\right) & = x _ {0} \end{array} \right. \tag {1}
$$

with state variables $x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ , initial state $x _ { 0 }$ , manipulated variables $u \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p }$ , measurement variables $\boldsymbol { y } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { q }$ , and model parameters $\theta \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ . Generally, model parameters (such as microbial growth or decay rates) can be time-variant. However, they vary at a slow rate of change and their dynamics do not follow a defined differential equation. Therefore, their implicit time dependence is suppressed in the notation.
