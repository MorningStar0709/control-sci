# B. Avoidance of Zeno-Like Behavior

We now investigate the existence of a minimum interval between two consecutive triggering instants in the considered discrete-time framework. Due to the discrete-time nature of the closed-loop system, inter-execution times are inherently lower bounded by one sampling step, and thus Zeno behavior is excluded by construction.

Accordingly, the objective here is not only to establish Zeno avoidance, but rather to ensure that the triggering mechanism yields a well-posed and nontrivial execution pattern. In particular, it is required that the event-triggering condition does not lead to pathological behaviors such as persistent triggering at every iteration, which would eliminate any computational or communication advantages.
