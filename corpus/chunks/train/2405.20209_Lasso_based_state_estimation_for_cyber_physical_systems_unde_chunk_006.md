# 3 Lasso approach

The proposed Lasso formulation for problem (3) is

$$\left(x ^ {\star}, a ^ {\star}\right) = \underset {x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {n}, a \in \mathbb {R} ^ {p \tau}} {\operatorname{argmin}} \frac {1}{2} \| y - \mathcal {O} x - a \| _ {2} ^ {2} + \lambda \| a \| _ {1} \tag {4}$$

where $\lambda > 0$ . Problem (4) is a partial Lasso because only a part of the vector to estimate is sparse, $\mathrm { i . e . , }$ we apply the $\ell _ { 1 }$ regularization only on a.

We notice that constrained counterparts of Lasso are the bases for the $\ell _ { 1 } { \mathrm { - b a s e d } }$ , block-sparsity decoders proposed by [5], for the noise-free case, and by [16], in the presence of bounded measurement noise. The Lasso formulation in (4) may envisage the presence of measurement noise as well; moreover, we can resort to several effective algorithms for unconstrained optimization to solve it.

In this work, we consider the iterative soft thresholding algorithm (ISTA) proposed by [4], which is a proximal gradient algorithm. ISTA iteration consists of a gradient step and a componentwise soft thresholding operation, defined by $\mathrm { S } _ { \nu \lambda } [ w ] = w - \nu \lambda \mathrm { s i g n } ( w ) \mathrm { i f } \ | w | \ge \nu \lambda $ , and 0 otherwise; $\nu > 0$ is the gradient step size. Accelerated versions of ISTA are available, see, e.g., [1] and [2]. Its simple structure allows us to build a state observer upon it, as illustrated in Sec. 4.
