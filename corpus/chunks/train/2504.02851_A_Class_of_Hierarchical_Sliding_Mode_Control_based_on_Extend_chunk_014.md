# A. PD-SMC attitude controller for UAVs

In this section, the PD-SMC attitude controller (PD-SMC-AC) is proposed to create the desired roll and pitch angles for the UAVs. For the attitude controller, we consider ϕ, θ, ψ (the roll, pitch, yaw angles) of the quadrotor UAVs as the class of actuated states, which can be controlled by $u _ { 2 } , u _ { 3 } , u _ { 4 } .$ , respectively.

Considering the sliding surface as:

$$s _ {\phi} = c _ {\phi} e _ {7} + e _ {8} \tag {37}$$

where $c _ { \phi }$ is a positive constant, and $e _ { 7 } = \hat { \phi } - \phi _ { r } , e _ { 8 } = \dot { \hat { \phi } } - \dot { \phi } _ { r }$ , and $\phi _ { r }$ is the desired roll angle.

The Lyapunov function is chosen as:

$$V _ {\phi} = \frac {1}{2} s _ {\phi} ^ {2} \tag {38}$$

and its derivative:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} _ {\phi} = s _ {\phi} \dot {s} _ {\phi} \\ = s _ {\phi} \left(c _ {\phi} (\dot {\hat {\phi}} - \dot {\phi} _ {r}) + \ddot {\phi} - \ddot {\phi} _ {r} - \ddot {\epsilon} _ {\phi}\right) \\ \approx s _ {\phi} \left(c _ {\phi} (\dot {\hat {\phi}} - \dot {\phi} _ {r}) + \ddot {\phi} - \ddot {\phi} _ {r}\right) \tag {39} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\epsilon _ { \phi }$ is the observed error of roll angle. Therefore, the control law $u _ { 2 }$ is proposed as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} u _ {2} = \frac {1}{b _ {\phi}} [ - c _ {\phi} \hat {\phi} - (c _ {\phi} + 1) \dot {\hat {\phi}} + c _ {\phi} \phi_ {r} + (c _ {\phi} + 1) \dot {\phi} _ {r} \\ \left. - f _ {\phi} - K _ {\phi} \operatorname{sat} \left(s _ {\phi}\right) + \ddot {\phi} _ {r} \right] \tag {40} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $K _ { \phi }$ is a positive number, sa $\displaystyle ( s _ { \phi } )$ is the saturation sign function, which is defined by:

$$
\operatorname{sat} (s _ {\phi}) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l} - 1 \text {   if   } s _ {\phi} \leq - 1 \\ s _ {\phi} \text {   if   } - 1 <   s _ {\phi} <   1 \\ 1 \text {   if   } s _ {\phi} \geq 1. \end{array} \right. \tag {41}
$$

Substituting the control law (40), and the dynamic equation (36) into (39) with the note that the estimation error will converge to 0, the derivative of the Lyapunov candidate becomes:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {V} _ {\phi} = s _ {\phi} \left[ - c _ {\phi} (\hat {\phi} - \phi_ {r}) - (\dot {\hat {\phi}} - \dot {\phi} _ {r}) - K _ {\phi} \operatorname{sat} (s _ {\phi}) \right] \\ = - s _ {\phi} ^ {2} - s _ {\phi} K _ {\phi} \mathrm{sat} (s _ {\phi}). \tag {42} \\ \end{array}
$$
