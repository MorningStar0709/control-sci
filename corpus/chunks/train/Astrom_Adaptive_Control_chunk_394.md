# Floquet Theory

To investigate the stability properties of (6.17), we consider the homogeneous part, when $A(t)$ is periodic with period $\tau$ and continuous for all $t$ . The solution is given by

$$x (t) = \Phi (t, t _ {0}) x (t _ {0})$$

where $\Phi(t, t_0)$ satisfies the linear matrix differential equation

$$\frac {d \Phi}{d t} = A (t) \Phi \tag {6.18}$$

Since $A(t)$ is periodic with period $\tau$ , it follows that $A(t + \tau) = A(t)$ . This implies that if $\Phi(t)$ is a solution, then $\Phi(t + \tau)$ is also a solution. Since the two solutions to Eq. (6.18) differ only in their initial conditions it follows that

$$\Phi (t + \tau) = \Phi (t) W \tag {6.19}$$

where W is a nonsingular constant matrix. Since the matrix $\Phi(t)$ is nonsingular for all t, it follows that W is also nonsingular. By repeated use of this equation we find that
