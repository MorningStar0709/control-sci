# 3 Optimal pair for the robust control problem

In this section, we drive the optimal control and optimal value of the robust control problem (2.4), which involve the unique solutions to two BSDEs with unbounded coefficients. The maim technique is to find the saddle point. Following [4], we introduce a family of stochastic process process $R ^ { ( \eta , u ) }$ defined by

$$R _ {t} ^ {(\eta , u)} := h _ {t} X _ {t} ^ {u} + \frac {1}{2 \theta} \left(\Lambda_ {t} ^ {\eta} Y _ {t} - 1\right), \quad t \in [ 0, T ], (\eta , u) \in \mathcal {A} \times \Pi ,$$

where h and Y are determined by two BSDEs studied in subsection 3.1. To verify that $( \hat { \eta } , \hat { u } )$ is the optimal pair of the MMV problem, we are aiming toprove the following five statements:

$$R _ {T} ^ {(\eta , u)} = X _ {T} ^ {u} + \frac {1}{2 \theta} \left(\Lambda_ {T} ^ {\eta} - 1\right) \text { for all } (\eta , u) \in \mathcal {A} \times \Pi ; \tag {3.1}R _ {0} ^ {(\eta , u)} = R _ {0} \text { is a constant for all } (\eta , u) \in \mathcal {A} \times \Pi ; \tag {3.2}\mathbb {E} ^ {\mathbb {P} ^ {\hat {\eta}}} \left[ R _ {T} ^ {(\hat {\eta}, u)} \right] \leqslant R _ {0} \text { for all } u \in \Pi ; \tag {3.3}\mathbb {E} ^ {\mathbb {P} ^ {\eta}} \left[ R _ {T} ^ {(\eta , \hat {u})} \right] \geqslant R _ {0} \text { for all } \eta \in \mathcal {A}; \tag {3.4}\mathbb {E} ^ {\mathbb {P} ^ {\hat {\eta}}} \left[ R _ {T} ^ {(\hat {\eta}, \hat {u})} \right] = R _ {0}. \tag {3.5}$$
