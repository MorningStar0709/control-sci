The flatness-based control concept is illustrated for a Timoshenko beam [17], which is governed by two coupled wave equations. In the studied setup, the beam is clamped at one end and actuated at the opposite end by a boundary force and torque serving as the control inputs (see Figure 2). This is a prototypical hyperbolic MI system, for which numerous control strategies exist in the literature, including direct boundary assignment [8], flatness-based feedforward tracking control [1], backstepping approaches [10, 12, 2], and control in a distributed port-Hamilton framework [11].

The paper is structured as follows. Section II introduces the Timoshenko beam model and specifies the control objective. In Section III a flatness-based parametrization is derived, from which the HCF is explicitly constructed in Section IV. A tracking controller is derived in Section V, ensuring the control objectives are met. Finally, the results are verified through simulation in Section VI.
