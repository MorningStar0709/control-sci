# 2. $\scriptstyle { \mathcal { H } } _ { \infty }$ Controller Synthesis

The setup of the classical $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ controller design problem consisting of a plant P and a controller K is illustrated in Figure 1. The plant is defined as

$$
\mathbf {P}: \left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} (t) \\ z (t) \\ y (t) \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} A & B _ {1} & B _ {2} \\ C _ {1} & D _ {1 1} & D _ {1 2} \\ C _ {2} & D _ {2 1} & D _ {2 2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (t) \\ w (t) \\ u (t) \end{array} \right]
$$

with $A \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n \times n }$ , Bj ∈ Rn×mj , $C _ { i } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { p _ { i } \times n }$ , and $D _ { i j } \in$ $\mathbb { R } ^ { p _ { i } \times m _ { j } }$ for $i , j = 1 , 2$ . For $t \in [ 0 , \infty )$ , x denotes the state, u and w denote the control and disturbance input, respectively, whereas y and z denote the measured and performance output, respectively. A controller of order k is given by

$$
\mathbf {K}: \left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \\ y _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} A _ {\mathbf {K}} & B _ {\mathbf {K}} \\ C _ {\mathbf {K}} & D _ {\mathbf {K}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \\ u _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \end{array} \right]
$$

with $A _ { \mathbf { K } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { k \times k } , B _ { \mathbf { K } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { k \times p _ { 2 } } , C _ { \mathbf { K } } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m _ { 2 } \times k } .$ , and $D _ { \mathbf { K } } \in$ $\mathbb { R } ^ { m _ { 2 } \times p _ { 2 } }$ , and is coupled with the plant P by the coupling conditions $u _ { \bf K } ( \cdot ) = y ( \cdot )$ and $u ( \cdot ) = y _ { \bf K } ( \cdot )$ .

The goal in $\mathcal { H } _ { \infty }$ synthesis is to tune the controller, i. e., to determine matrices $A _ { \bf K } , B _ { \bf K } , C _ { \bf K }$ , and $D _ { \mathbf { K } }$ such that the performance output is minimized in an appropriate norm for all admissible disturbance inputs. This can be quantified using the transfer functions of plant and controller, that are defind as follows

$$
\begin{array}{l} P (s) := \left[ \begin{array}{c c} P _ {1 1} (s) & P _ {1 2} (s) \\ P _ {2 1} (s) & P _ {2 2} (s) \end{array} \right] \\ := \left[ \begin{array}{c} C _ {1} \\ C _ {2} \end{array} \right] (s I _ {n} - A) ^ {- 1} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} B _ {1} & B _ {2} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} D _ {1 1} & D _ {1 2} \\ D _ {2 1} & D _ {2 2} \end{array} \right], \\ \end{array}
K (s) := C _ {\mathbf {K}} \left(s I _ {k} - A _ {\mathbf {K}}\right) ^ {- 1} B _ {\mathbf {K}} + D _ {\mathbf {K}}. \tag {1}$$
