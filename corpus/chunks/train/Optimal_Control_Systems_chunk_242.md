$$\boxed {\mathbf {B} ^ {\prime} \boldsymbol {\Phi} ^ {\prime} (- s) \mathbf {Q} \boldsymbol {\Phi} (s) \mathbf {B} + \mathbf {R} = [ \mathbf {I} + \bar {\mathbf {K}} \boldsymbol {\Phi} (- s) \mathbf {B} ] ^ {\prime} \mathbf {R} [ \mathbf {I} + \bar {\mathbf {K}} \boldsymbol {\Phi} (s) \mathbf {B} ]} \tag {4.5.12}$$

or equivalently,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {B} ^ {\prime} \left[ - s \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} ^ {\prime} \right] ^ {- 1} \mathbf {Q} [ s \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} ] ^ {- 1} \mathbf {B} + \mathbf {R} \\ = \left[ \mathbf {I} + \bar {\mathbf {K}} [ - s \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} ] ^ {- 1} \mathbf {B} \right] ^ {\prime} \mathbf {R} \left[ \mathbf {I} + \bar {\mathbf {K}} [ s \mathbf {I} - \mathbf {A} ] ^ {- 1} \mathbf {B} \right]. \tag {4.5.13} \\ \end{array}
$$

The previous relation is also called the Kalman equation in frequency domain.
