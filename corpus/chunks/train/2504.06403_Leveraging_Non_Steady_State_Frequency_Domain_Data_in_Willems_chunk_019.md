# B. Noisy data

Next, we consider the case with noisy data, for which we modify the method based on Theorem 2 to include preprocessing of the data with the goal of approximating the noise-free data. To this end, we adopt the popular heuristic for observable systems, discussed in, e.g., [24], that noisefree data, for $L _ { 0 } \geqslant \ell _ { \Sigma }$ and under the appropriate PE conditions, satisfies

$$
\operatorname{rank} \left[ \begin{array}{l} \Psi_ {L _ {0} + 1} (\hat {U} _ {[ 0, M - 1 ]}) \\ \Psi_ {L _ {0} + 1} (\hat {\Omega} _ {[ 0, M - 1 ]}) \\ \hline \Psi_ {L _ {0} + 1} (\hat {Y} _ {[ 0, M - 1 ]}) \end{array} \right] = (n _ {u} + 1) (L _ {0} + 1) + n _ {x}. \tag {14}
$$
