Finding the orientation from the vector observation (3a) is tantamount to the Wahba’s problem [35] that minimizes the cost function

$$J _ {\omega} (t _ {k}) = \frac {1}{2} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {k} a _ {i} \| \boldsymbol {\epsilon} _ {\omega_ {i}} \| ^ {2}, \tag {7}$$

There are many algorithm to solve the above optimization problem [36–39], of which the parametrization of the orientation by a unit quaternion is the most elegant and useful one. It becomes immediately clear from expanding the expression on RHS of (7) in terms of the angular velocities,

$$J _ {\omega} = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {k} a _ {i} \left(\| \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1 _ {i}} \| ^ {2} + \| \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2 _ {i}} \| ^ {2}\right) - \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {k} a _ {i} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2 _ {i}} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {A} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1 _ {i}}, \tag {8}$$

that the cost function is minimized when the last term in RHS of (8) is maximized. Moreover, using identity (2), one can show that

$$\boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2 _ {i}} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {A} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1 _ {i}} = \boldsymbol {\eta} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {\Omega} (\boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1 _ {i}}, \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2 _ {i}}) \boldsymbol {\eta}, \tag {9}$$

where

$$
\boldsymbol {\Omega} (\boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1}, \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2}) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1} ^ {T} + \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} ^ {T} - \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} \boldsymbol {I} & \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} \times \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1} \\ (\boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} \times \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1}) ^ {T} & \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {1} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {\omega} _ {2} \end{array} \right].
$$
