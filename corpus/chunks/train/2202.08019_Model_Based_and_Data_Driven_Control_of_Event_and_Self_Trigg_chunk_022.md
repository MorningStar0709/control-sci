Remark 6 (Novelty). Up to date, there are few research efforts devoted to data-driven control of discrete-time systems under aperiodic sampling, in particular data-driven control under event-triggering schemes. Theorem 2, which is based on Theorem 1, provides a stability condition and a co-design method of the controller and the ETS for unknown sampleddata control systems. A possibly large sampling interval h and a triggering matrix Ω for the triggering condition (9) can be searched for by using Theorem 2. The application of Theorem 2 is simple, requiring only a solution of LMIs which can be constructed based on noisy data. Besides, when θ approaches infinity, $\sigma _ { 1 } = 0$ , and $\sigma _ { 2 } = 0$ , the triggering condition (9) degenerates to the periodic sampling scheme studied in [25], [28]. In comparison to the results in [25], [28], Theorem 2 provides larger values of h, which shows the role of the DLF in reducing conservatism of stability conditions.
