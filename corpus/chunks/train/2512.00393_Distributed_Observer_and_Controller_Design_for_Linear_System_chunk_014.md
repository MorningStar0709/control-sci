In the case where $\nu ( t ) \neq 0 ,$ consider the following state feedback sliding mode control law that can stabilize system (6):

$$u = K x - \beta h (B ^ {\top} P x), \tag {11}$$

where $\beta$ is a scalar, ?? is a matrix gain, and function $h ( \cdot )$ is defined as

$$
h (\omega) = \left\{ \begin{array}{c} \| \omega \| ^ {- 1} \omega , \omega \neq 0 \\ 0, \omega = 0. \end{array} \right. \tag {12}
$$

On this basis, we aim to prove that the following control strategy also achieves stabilization:

$$u ^ {\iota} = K ^ {\iota} \hat {x} _ {i ^ {\prime}} - \beta^ {\iota} h \left(\left(B ^ {\iota}\right) ^ {\top} P \hat {x} _ {i ^ {\prime}}\right), \tag {13}$$

where $\beta ^ { \iota }$ is a scalar gain, and $\hat { x } _ { i ^ { \prime } }$ is the state estimate from any one of the aforementioned observer nodes.

The separation principle established in this paper can be interpreted as follows: The design of distributed observers is independent of $K ^ { \iota } , \beta ^ { \iota }$ , and ?? in (10) and (13). Conversely, the distributed controllers can be designed without accounting for the observer dynamics.
