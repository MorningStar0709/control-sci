$$\boldsymbol {\xi} _ {k + 1} = \hat {\boldsymbol {A}} (\boldsymbol {\alpha} _ {k}) \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {k} + \hat {\boldsymbol {B}} \boldsymbol {u} _ {k}. \tag {6}$$

The indicator function

$$
\boldsymbol {\alpha} _ {k} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l l} \alpha_ {1, k} & \alpha_ {2, k} & \dots & \alpha_ {\bar {\delta}, k} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} \tag {7}
$$

is thereby defined as $\alpha _ { i , k } = 1 \mathrm { i f } \ u _ { k - i }$ is active for $k T _ { d } \leq$ $t < ( k + 1 ) T _ { d }$ and $\alpha _ { i , k } = 0$ otherwise, with $\begin{array} { r } { \sum _ { i = 1 } ^ { \delta } \alpha _ { i , k } = 1 } \end{array}$ The matrices $\hat { A } ( \alpha _ { k } )$ and Bˆ are given as

$$
\hat {\boldsymbol {A}} \left(\boldsymbol {\alpha} _ {k}\right) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \boldsymbol {A} _ {\boldsymbol {d}} & \alpha_ {1, k} \boldsymbol {B} _ {d} \dots \alpha_ {\bar {\delta} - 1, k} \boldsymbol {B} _ {d} & \alpha_ {\bar {\delta}, k} \boldsymbol {B} _ {d} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {m \times n} & \mathbf {0} _ {m} \dots \mathbf {0} _ {m} & \mathbf {0} _ {m} \\ \mathbf {0} _ {(\bar {\delta} - 1) m \times n} & \boldsymbol {I} _ {(\bar {\delta} - 1) m} & \mathbf {0} _ {(\bar {\delta} - 1) m \times m} \end{array} \right], \tag {8}

\hat {\boldsymbol {B}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \mathbf {0} _ {m \times n} & \boldsymbol {I} _ {m \times m} & \mathbf {0} _ {m \times (\bar {\delta} - 1) m} \end{array} \right] ^ {T} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \hat {\boldsymbol {b}} _ {1} & \hat {\boldsymbol {b}} _ {2} & \ldots \hat {\boldsymbol {b}} _ {m} \end{array} \right].
$$

Due to the fact that there is only one signal acting between two sampling instants, the lifted model (6) can easily be written as a switched system

$$\boldsymbol {\xi} _ {k + 1} = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {\bar {\delta}} \alpha_ {i, k} \hat {\boldsymbol {A}} _ {i} \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {k} + \hat {\boldsymbol {B}} \boldsymbol {u} _ {k} \tag {9}$$
