# Adaptive Control

When digital computers are used to implement a controller, it is possible to implement more complicated control algorithms. A natural step is to include both parameter estimation methods and control design algorithms. In this way it is possible to obtain adaptive control algorithms that determine the mathematical models and perform control system design on-line. Research on adaptive control began in the mid-1950s. Significant progress was made in the 1970s when feasibility was demonstrated in industrial applications. The advent of the microprocessor made the algorithms cost-effective, and commercial adaptive regulators appeared in the early 1980s. This has stimulated vigorous research on theoretical issues and significant product development. See, for instance, Åström and Wittenmark (1973, 1980, 1995), Åström (1983b, 1987), and Goodwin and Sin (1984).
