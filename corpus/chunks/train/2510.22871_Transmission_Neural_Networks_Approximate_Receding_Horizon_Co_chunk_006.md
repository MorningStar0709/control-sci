Proposition 1 (Conditional Probability of Infection [22]) Assume (A1) and (A2) hold. Given the state configuration at time k denoted by $X ( k ) = x \in \{ 0 , 1 \} ^ { n }$ , the transition probability to a state configuration $q \in \{ 0 , 1 \} ^ { n }$ is given by

$$
\begin{array}{l} \operatorname * {P r} (X (k + 1) = q | X (k) = x) \\ = \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {n} \operatorname * {P r} (X _ {i} (k + 1) = q _ {i} | X (k) = x) \tag {4} \\ = \prod_ {i = 1} ^ {n} \left(q _ {i} \rho_ {i} (k + 1) + (1 - q _ {i}) (1 - \rho_ {i} (k + 1))\right) \\ \end{array}
$$

where

$$\rho_ {i} (k + 1) = 1 - \prod_ {j \in N _ {i} ^ {\circ k}} \left(1 - w _ {i j} ^ {k} x _ {j}\right), \quad i \in [ n ]. \tag {5}$$

This explicit representation of the transition probability to the next state configuration given the current state configuration will be used later in the Markov Decision Process (MDP) formulation for controlling virus spread over networks (see Section V).
