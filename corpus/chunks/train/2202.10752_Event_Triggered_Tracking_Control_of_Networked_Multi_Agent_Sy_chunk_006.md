# A. Hybrid System

The basic concepts of hybrid systems are introduced below; see [24] for the details. Consider the following hybrid system

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \dot {x} = F (x, w), & (x, w) \in C, \\ x ^ {+} = G (x, w), & (x, w) \in D, \end{array} \right. \tag {1}
$$

where $x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ is the system state, $w \in \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is the external input, $F : C \to \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ is the flow map, $G : D  \mathbb { R } ^ { m }$ is the jump map, $C$ is the flow set and D is the jump set.
