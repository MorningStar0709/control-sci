# B. INERTIAL DISTURBANCE MODELING

By attaching a payload to the UAV Bicopter, it is possible to model inertial disturbances. Previously, it was presumed that the Bicopter operated under nominal conditions $\left( J _ { 0 } \right)$ , so the inertial matrix was diagonal according to Eq. (18). However, when provided payload, the Bicopter’s inertial matrix will undoubtedly change. In order to facilitate the analysis, the inertial matrix is separated into two types: the inertial matrix at nominal conditions and the inertial matrix that results from the presence of payload $( \Delta J )$ . The value of $\Delta J$ is affected by the products’ mass, shape, and position of payload. The addition of the inertial matrix that appears due to the presence of payload referred to as the perturbed inertia matrix, allowing the inertial matrix to be written as Eq. (19).

$$
J _ {0} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} I _ {x x} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & I _ {y y} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & I _ {z z} \end{array} \right] \tag {18}
J _ {\delta} = J _ {0} + \Delta J \tag {19}$$

It is assumed that the UAV Bicopter’s payload will carry a solid cubic-shaped block filled with liquid as shown in Fig. 3 with the specifications in Table 1. Because the payload is in the form of blocks, the inertial disturbance matrix is a symmetric matrix with zero off-diagonal elements as in Eq. (20), with the matrix elements consisting in Eq. (21).
