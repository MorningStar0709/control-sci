# 4.2 Partial Convexification of BMI

We now use a change of variables to arrive at a matrix inequality that is linear in the new decision variables, such that $P , \Lambda$ , and θ can be recovered from the new decision variables. This change of variables is based on the one introduced in [16] for $\bar { \mathcal { H } } _ { \infty }$ control. For simplicity, we restrict the dimension of the controller state to match the dimension of the plant state, so $n _ { k } = n _ { p }$ . See [16] for notes on when the controller dimension differs from the plant dimension.

To apply this change of variables, we first require positive definiteness of the storage function parameter: $P \succ 0$ . Then, we introduce the following partition of P and $P ^ { - 1 }$ :

$$
P = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} S & U \\ U ^ {\top} & \star \end{array} \right] \quad P ^ {- 1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} R & V \\ V ^ {\top} & \star \end{array} \right],
$$

where $S , R , U , V \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n _ { p } \times n _ { p } } , S \succ 0$ , and $R \succ 0$ . Further, define

$$
Y \triangleq \left[ \begin{array}{c c} R & I \\ V ^ {\top} & 0 \end{array} \right]. \tag {21}
$$

We now left- and right-multiply the matrix in (20) by

diag $; ( Y ^ { \top } , I )$ and its transpose to get the condition

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Y ^ {\top} & \\ & I \end{array} \right] F \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Y & \\ & I \end{array} \right] & \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Y ^ {\top} C _ {v} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & Y ^ {\top} C _ {e} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \\ D _ {v w} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & D _ {e w} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \\ D _ {v d} ^ {\top} L _ {\Delta} ^ {\top} & D _ {e d} ^ {\top} L _ {X} ^ {\top} \end{array} \right] \\ \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} L _ {\Delta} C _ {v} Y & L _ {\Delta} D _ {v w} & L _ {\Delta} D _ {v d} \\ L _ {X} C _ {e} Y & L _ {X} D _ {e w} & L _ {X} D _ {e d} \end{array} \right] & - I \end{array} \right] \preceq 0 \tag {22}
$$

where

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} Y ^ {\top} & \\ & I \end{array} \right] F \left[ \begin{array}{c c} Y & \\ & I \end{array} \right] =

\left[ \begin{array}{c c c} Y ^ {\top} A ^ {\top} P Y + Y ^ {\top} P A Y & Y ^ {\top} P B _ {w} & Y ^ {\top} P B _ {d} \\ B _ {w} ^ {\top} P Y & 0 & 0 \\ B _ {d} ^ {\top} P Y & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right]
