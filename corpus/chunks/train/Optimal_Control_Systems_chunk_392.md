\- Step 7: Switch Curve: From Figure 7.8, we see that there are two curves labeled $\gamma_{+}$ and $\gamma_{-}$ which transfer any initial state $(x_{1}, x_{2})$ to the origin $(0, 0)$ .

1. The $\gamma_{+}$ curve is the locus of all (initial) points $(x_{1}, x_{2})$ which can be transferred to the final point $(0, 0)$ by the control $u = +1$ . That is

$$\gamma_ {+} = \left\{\left(x _ {1}, x _ {2}\right): \quad x _ {1} = \frac {1}{2} x _ {2} ^ {2}, \quad x _ {2} \leq 0 \right\}. \tag {7.2.21}$$

2. The $\gamma_{-}$ curve is the locus of all (initial) points $(x_{1}, x_{2})$ which can be transferred to the final point $(0, 0)$ by the control $u = -1$ . That is

$$\gamma_ {-} = \left\{\left(x _ {1}, x _ {2}\right): x _ {1} = - \frac {1}{2} x _ {2} ^ {2}, x _ {2} \geq 0 \right\}. \tag {7.2.22}$$

3. The complete switch curve, i.e., the $\gamma$ curve, is defined as the union (either or) of the partial switch curves $\gamma_{+}$ and $\gamma_{-}$ . That is

$$
\begin{array}{l} \gamma = \left\{\left(x _ {1}, x _ {2}\right): \quad x _ {1} = - \frac {1}{2} x _ {2} | x _ {2} | \right\}, \\ = \gamma_ {+} \cup \gamma_ {-} \tag {7.2.23} \\ \end{array}
$$

where, $\cup$ means the union operation.
