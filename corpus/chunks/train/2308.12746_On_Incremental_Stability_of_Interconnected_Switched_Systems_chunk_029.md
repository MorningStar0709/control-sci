$$
\| \delta \xi \| = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \| \delta \xi_ {1} \| \\ \cdot \\ \cdot \\ \| \delta \xi_ {N} \| \end{array} \right], \quad \gamma (\| \delta u _ {e x t} \| _ {t}) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \gamma_ {1} (\| \delta u \| _ {t}) \\ \cdot \\ \cdot \\ \gamma_ {N} (\| \delta u \| _ {t}) \end{array} \right], \text {   and   } \beta (s, t) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} \beta_ {1} (s _ {1}, t) \\ \cdot \\ \cdot \\ \beta_ {N} (s _ {N}, t) \end{array} \right] \tag {43}
$$

The gain matrix $\Lambda ^ { I S S } : \mathbb { R } _ { + } ^ { N } \mapsto \mathbb { R } _ { + } ^ { N }$ is a map defined by

$$\Lambda^ {I S S} ((s _ {1}, s _ {2},.., s _ {N}) ^ {T}) := \big (\sum_ {l = 1} ^ {N} \gamma_ {1 l} (s _ {l}),..., \sum_ {l = 1} ^ {N} \gamma_ {N l} (s _ {l}) \big) ^ {T}. \tag {44}$$

Then, the ISS relationship in (42) can be rewritten in vectorised form as,

$$\left| \left| \delta \xi (t) \right| \right| \leq \beta \left(\left| \left| \delta \xi (0) \right| \right|, t\right) + \Lambda^ {I S S} \left(\left| \left| \delta \xi \right| \right| _ {t}\right) + \gamma \left(\left| \left| \delta u _ {e x t} \right| \right| _ {t}\right). \tag {45}$$

We introduce the following diagonal operator $D : \mathbb { R } _ { + } ^ { N } \mapsto \mathbb { R } _ { + } ^ { N }$ such that for $\rho _ { j } \in \mathcal { K } _ { \infty } , j = \{ 1 , 2 , . . N \}$

$$
D ((s _ {1}, s _ {2},.., s _ {N}) ^ {T}) = \left[ \begin{array}{c} (i d + \rho_ {1}) (s _ {1}) \\ \cdot \\ \cdot \\ (i d + \rho_ {N}) (s _ {N}) \end{array} \right]. \tag {46}
$$

We say that the gain matrix $\Lambda ^ { I S S }$ defined in (44) satisfies the small gain condition [36, 37], if there exists an operator ?? as in (46), such that for all $s , s \neq 0$ we have

$$\Lambda^ {I S S} \circ D (s) := \Lambda^ {I S S} (D (s)) \neq s. \tag {47}$$
