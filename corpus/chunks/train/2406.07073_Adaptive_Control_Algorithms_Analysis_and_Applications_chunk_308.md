# Alternative Expression of S and R in the Case of Auxiliary Poles

Assume that $S _ { D }$ and $R _ { D }$ are solutions of the polynomial equation:

$$P _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) = A (q ^ {- 1}) S _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) + q ^ {- d - 1} B ^ {\star} (q ^ {- 1}) R _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.49}$$

Then the solutions for the case

$$P (q ^ {- 1}) = P _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) P _ {F} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.50}$$

can be expressed as:

$$S (q ^ {- 1}) = P _ {F} (q ^ {- 1}) S _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}); \quad R (q ^ {- 1}) = P _ {F} (q ^ {- 1}) R _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {7.51}$$

(multiply both sides of (7.49) by $P _ { F } ( q ^ { - 1 } ) )$ ).
