Remark 2. Theorem 3 and Corollary 1 establish that the kernel-weighted overlap is a valid quantity for limiting the mass of the population inside dangerous regions. In practice, as shown in Sections IV-A and IV-B, the bound ε from Corollary 1 is conservative, and looser bounds suffice.

In practice, the threshold ε need not satisfy the conservative bound $\varepsilon = B ( \mu )$ from Corollary 1. Instead, it can be tuned as a design parameter: smaller values of ε enforce a tighter safety margin between the population and the dangerous regions, at the cost of a more restrictive feasible set $\mathcal { H } _ { C B F } ( t , \rho )$ , which may lead to larger deviations from the nominal control. The choice of ε thus reflects a trade-off between safety stringency and control performance.
