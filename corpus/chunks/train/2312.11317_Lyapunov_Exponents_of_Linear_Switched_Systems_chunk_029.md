In order to be an admissible control, it must hold $u _ { * } \in [ 0 , 1 ]$ . The inequality $u _ { * } \geq 0$ is equivalent to the condition

$$\left\{\mathrm{tr} (A B) > \frac {\mathrm{tr} (A ^ {2}) + \mathrm{tr} (B ^ {2})}{2} \right\} \cup \left\{\mathrm{tr} (A B) \leq \mathrm{tr} (B ^ {2}) \right\},$$

while the other inequality $u _ { * } \leq 1$ is equivalent to

$$\left\{\mathrm{tr} (A B) \geq \mathrm{tr} (A ^ {2}) \right\} \cup \left\{\mathrm{tr} (A B) < \frac {\mathrm{tr} (A ^ {2}) + \mathrm{tr} (B ^ {2})}{2} \right\},$$

Proposition 5.5. $u _ { * }$ is an admissible control if and only if

$$\operatorname{tr} (A B) \geq \operatorname{tr} (A ^ {2}) \quad o r \quad \operatorname{tr} (A B) \leq \operatorname{tr} (B ^ {2}).$$

Then, we obtain

$$u _ {*} A + (1 - u _ {*}) B = \frac {\operatorname{tr} (A B) - \operatorname{tr} (B ^ {2})}{2 \operatorname{tr} (A B) - \operatorname{tr} (A ^ {2}) - \operatorname{tr} (B ^ {2})} A + \frac {\operatorname{tr} (A B) - \operatorname{tr} (A ^ {2})}{2 \operatorname{tr} (A B) - \operatorname{tr} (A ^ {2}) - \operatorname{tr} (B ^ {2})} B, \tag {5.6}$$
