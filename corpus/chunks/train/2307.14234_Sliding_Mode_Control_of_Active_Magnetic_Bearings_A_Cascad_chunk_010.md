# 3.4 Current control law

The task of the current controller is to ensure that the appropriate current deviation $i _ { r e f }$ is generated by the AMB coils such that the demanded total magnetic force is applied to the rotor mass for its axial positioning. Let the current tracking error be denoted by $e = i - i _ { r e f }$ . Its dynamics reads:

$$\dot {e} = \frac {d i}{d t} - \frac {d i _ {r e f}}{d t} = \frac {2 \dot {z}}{s _ {0} + z} i + \frac {2 (s _ {0} + z)}{\mu_ {0} n ^ {2} A} (u - R i) + q _ {i} - \frac {d i _ {r e f}}{d t}. \tag {10}$$

The control law

$$u = \frac {\mu_ {0} n ^ {2} A}{2 (s _ {0} + z)} \left[ - \frac {2 \dot {z}}{s _ {0} + z} i + \frac {d i _ {r e f}}{d t} - k _ {i} \operatorname{sgn} (e) \right] + R i, k _ {i} > Q _ {i} \tag {11}$$
