# A. Contraction theory

Unless otherwise stated, all vector fields $F ( t , x ) \ \in \ \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ , where $t \in \mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }$ and $x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ , are supposed to be continuous in t and locally Lipschitz in x. We say that F is strongly infinitesimally contracting with respect to $\left\| \cdot \right\| _ { P }$ with rate $c > 0$ if for all $x , \tilde { x } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ and $t \geq 0$ the inequality holds

$$\left(F (t, x) - F (t, \tilde {x})\right) ^ {\top} P (x - \tilde {x}) \leq - c \| x - \tilde {x} \| _ {P} ^ {2}.$$

A strongly contracting time-invariant system with $F ( t , x ) ~ = ~ F ( x )$ always admits a unique and globally exponentially stable equilibrium.
