As presented below, the leader agent will converge to its destination from almost all initial conditions that satisfy Assumption 4.3, whereas the followers might get stuck in local minima. Once the leader reaches ε-close to its destination (where ε was introduced in Assumption 4.3), it switches off its control and the next robotic agent in the priority list becomes the leader. We assume that once an agent reaches its goal, it can broadcast this information for the next agent in priority. This occurs iteratively until all the robotic agents reach their destinations.
