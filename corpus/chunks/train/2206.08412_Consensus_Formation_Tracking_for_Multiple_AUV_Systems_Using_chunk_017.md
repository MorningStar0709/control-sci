$$\dot {V} _ {s} = s ^ {\mathrm{T}} \left[ - \beta_ {0} \text {sign} (s) + \bar {J} (\eta) \bar {B} d \right]\leq - \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} \left(\beta_ {0} - \left\| J _ {i} \left(\eta_ {i}\right) B _ {i} d _ {i} \right\|\right) \left| s _ {i} \right| \tag {19}\leq - \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} \varepsilon | s _ {i} | \leq - \sqrt {\frac {2}{\lambda_ {\max} (L + B) ^ {- 1}}} V ^ {1 / 2}.$$

By taking integral on both sides, we can conclude

$$\sqrt {V _ {s} (t)} \leq - \sqrt {\frac {1}{2 \lambda_ {\max} (L + B) ^ {- 1}}} t + V _ {s} (0). \tag {20}$$

Above inequality shows that under proposed sliding mode control law (16) and (17) the sliding variable s will reach to zero in finite time and stay there for all future time, i.e., $s \equiv 0 , t \geq T$ , where

$$T = \sqrt {2 \lambda_ {\max} (L + B) ^ {- 1}} V _ {s} (0). \tag {21}$$

Once system is sliding on the surface s ≡ 0, the overall behavior of the system is then determined by the linear differential equation

$$\dot {\bar {e}} _ {1} + k _ {1} (L + B) \bar {e} _ {1} = 0.$$

Moreover, as the Lemma 1 shows $L + B$ is a positive definite matrix, it follows that $\bar { e } _ { 1 }  0$ as t → ∞. When $\bar { e } _ { 1 }  0 , \dot { \bar { e } } _ { 1 }$ will also tend to 0; then, according to (8), $\bar { e } _ { 2 }  0$ as well. That is, the error dynamics (8) is asymptotically stable and its converging rate is dependent on the $k _ { 1 }$ .
