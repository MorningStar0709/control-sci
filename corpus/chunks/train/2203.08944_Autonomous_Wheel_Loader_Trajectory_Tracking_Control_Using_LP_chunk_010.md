Since the problem is formulated to calculate the trajectory from the initial state to the target state, $x ( i | k )$ is not considered in the cost function. The initial and target states and desired control actions $( x _ { 0 } , x _ { f } , u _ { 0 } , u _ { f } )$ are considered as equality constraints. $\gamma _ { m a x }$ is the maximum allowed articulated angle. $D ^ { 2 } ( x ( i | k ) )$ is the distance to the two obstacles: the pile and the dump truck (see Fig. 1), and $D _ { s f } ^ { 2 }$ is the safety distance. The nonlinear MPC problem finds the optimal control sequence (U) during the given time horizon defined by N and $T _ { s }$ . The generated trajectory contains the desired vehicle state at every time step along with the associated control command.

In this paper, the trajectory planner is not executed at every time instant due to limited computational capacity. Since the application scenario does not consider moving obstacles, the trajectories are calculated offline with initial and target states corresponding to the loading and unloading points. The location of the turning point (see Fig. 1) is determined directly by the nonlinear MPC planner.
