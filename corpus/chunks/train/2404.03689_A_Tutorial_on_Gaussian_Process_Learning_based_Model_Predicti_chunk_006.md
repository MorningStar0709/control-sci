# 2.1 Mathematical Basics

This part introduces the essential mathematical concepts that GPR is built on, including the multivariate normal (MVN) distribution, kernels, and the non-parametric nature of GPR [5].

2.1.1 Gaussian and Multivariate Normal Distributions. Central to GPR are the Gaussian/normal distribution and its generalization to multiple dimensions, the MVN distribution. These distributions form the probabilistic cornerstone for modeling and making predictions in systems with inherent uncertainties. A Gaussian distribution, or univariate normal distribution, for a variable ?? is defined by its probability density function (PDF):

$$P _ {X} (x) = \frac {1}{\sqrt {2 \pi \sigma^ {2}}} \exp \left(- \frac {(x - \mu) ^ {2}}{2 \sigma^ {2}}\right), \tag {1}$$
