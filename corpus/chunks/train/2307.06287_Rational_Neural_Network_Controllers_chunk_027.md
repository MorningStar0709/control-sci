$$V (z) - \rho (z) \in \Sigma [ z ],\rho (z) > 0,- \frac {\partial V}{\partial z} (z) (f (z) + g (z) u) - \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {n _ {d}} s _ {k} (X) d _ {k} (z) \dots- \sum_ {k = 1} ^ {\ell} \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {k}} \left(p _ {i} ^ {k} (x ^ {k}) - q _ {i} ^ {k} (x ^ {k}) x _ {i} ^ {k + 1}\right) \dots- \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {n _ {u}} \left(p _ {i} ^ {\ell} (x ^ {\ell}) - q _ {i} ^ {\ell} (x ^ {\ell}) u _ {i}\right) \in \Sigma [ X ],s _ {k} (X) \in \Sigma [ X ], \forall k = 1, \dots , n _ {d},q _ {i} ^ {k} (x ^ {k}) \neq 0, \forall i = 1, \dots , n _ {k}, k = 0, \dots , \ell ,$$

where X is a vector of all the system and NN states, i.e. $X = ( x , u , z )$ . Then the equilibrium of the system is stable.

The above proposition can generate a feasible SOS program, however the rational NN controller may be difficult to recover. This is because the coefficients in the $q _ { i } ^ { k } ( x ^ { k } )$ terms will be set to very small values by the SOS program, due to each term cancelling with the adjacent layers. We therefore propose an alternative rational NN controller structure in the following section to mitigate this issue.
