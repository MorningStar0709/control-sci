To make W and B input-dependent while maintaining the contractivity of the FRNN, we parameterize W according to Theorem 35, replacing each free variable and B with an inputdependent feedforward neural network. Because these feedforward networks typically consist of compositions of linear transformations and MONE nonlinearities, they are globally Lipschitz. This allows us to design a highly expressive, locally Lipschitz implicit neural network that remains mathematically guaranteed to be well-posed and strictly contracting. We validate this approach on the MNIST and CIFAR-10 image classification benchmarks. As shown in Table II, our model achieves competitive accuracy while remaining parameter efficient, due to a higher expressivity. Full implementation details and code are provided in our repository.2
