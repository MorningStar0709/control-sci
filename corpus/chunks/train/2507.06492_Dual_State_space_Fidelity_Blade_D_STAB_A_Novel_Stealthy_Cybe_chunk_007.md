# E. Main Contributions

The main contributions of this work are two-fold. First, we propose the D-STAB, a novel and stealthy cyber-physical attack paradigm. This paradigm leverages a coupled framework, consisting of a high-fidelity adversarial constrained optimal control problem (OCP) and a low-fidelity inverse constrained OCP. This approach allows for precise manipulation of system behavior while remaining undetected by the defender relying on low-fidelity models. Second, we demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed attack paradigm through its application in cyber-physical battery systems. The case study showcases how D-STAB can exploit the fidelity gap in real-world systems, manipulating battery charging processes while evading detection.
