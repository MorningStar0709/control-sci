# 3.1 The Linear Quadratic Regulator

In this example we will connect the standard linear-quadratic-regulator (LQR) problem to Theorem 1. This will give an interpretation of our more abstract notation and results in a hopefully more familiar setting. In particular we will show that when the optimization in (i) corresponds to the LQR problem, conditions (ii) and (iii) reduce to the algebraic Riccati equations and semi-definite programs that are standard in the solution to this problem.

We will in fact study a slightly more general problem. The connections to the standard LQR will be established at the end of the section. The optimization problem that we consider first is:

$$
\text { Minimize } \quad \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {\infty} \operatorname{tr} \left(\left[ \begin{array}{c c} S & R _ {1} \\ R _ {1} ^ {\top} & R _ {2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X (t) & U _ {1} (t) \\ U _ {1} (t) ^ {\top} & U _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right]\right)
\text { over } \quad \{U _ {1} (t), U _ {2} (t) \} _ {t = 0} ^ {\infty},
\text { subject   to } \quad X (t + 1) = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} F & G \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} X (t) & U _ {1} (t) \\ U _ {1} (t) ^ {\top} & U _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} F ^ {\top} \\ G ^ {\top} \end{array} \right],

\left[ \begin{array}{c c} X (t) & U _ {1} (t) \\ U _ {1} (t) ^ {\top} & U _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right] \succeq 0,   X (0) = X _ {0} \succeq 0.
$$

In the above, the variables are the matrices $X ( t ) , U _ { 1 } ( t )$ and $U _ { 2 } ( t )$ . The problem data is the matrices $S , R _ { 1 } , R _ { 2 }$ , F and G, where we additionally require that

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} S & R _ {1} \\ R _ {1} ^ {\top} & R _ {2} \end{array} \right] \succ 0. \tag {9}
$$

Although it may not be immediately clear at first sight, this is an optimization of precisely the type considered in Theorem 1(i) where $\mathcal { P }$ is the positive semidefinite cone (which is positive, proper and self-dual).

We will now explain the correspondence between the variables and data in the above, and those in Theorem 11. The variables are related as follows:

$$x (t) \longleftrightarrow X (t)
u (t) \longleftrightarrow \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & U _ {1} (t) \\ U _ {1} (t) ^ {\top} & U _ {2} (t) \end{array} \right].
$$
