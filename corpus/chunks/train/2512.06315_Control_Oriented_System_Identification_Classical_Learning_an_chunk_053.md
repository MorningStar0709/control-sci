$$
\mathcal {R} _ {k} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} Z _ {k + 1} \\ Y _ {k} \end{array} \right] - \left[ \begin{array}{c} A   Z _ {k} + B   U _ {k} \\ C   Z _ {k} + D   U _ {k} \end{array} \right]. \tag {49}
$$

To enforce stability of the learned model, we desire the spectral radius of A to be smaller than 1. However, directly penalizing the spectral radius (A) yields a non-smooth, non-convex ρobjective. As a practical convex surrogate for the upper bounds of (A), we instead impose a penalty on the operator 2-norm ρ∥A∥2. We enforce this penalty in the form of a soft constraint by solving the unconstrained optimization problem

$$\min _ {A, B, C, D} \quad c (\theta , \mathcal {W}) := \| \mathcal {R} \| _ {F} ^ {2} + \lambda \| A \| _ {2} ^ {2}, \tag {50}$$

where $\lambda > 0$ is a hyper-parameter that balances model accuλ >racy and stability. To make the problem more computationally tractable, we introduce slack variables and to transform the problem into a convex problem

$$(A _ {1} ^ {*}, B _ {1} ^ {*}, C _ {1} ^ {*}, D _ {1} ^ {*}, \gamma^ {*}, t ^ {*}) = \operatorname{argmin} _ {A, B, C, D, \gamma , t} \| \mathcal {R} \| _ {F} ^ {2} + \lambda \tau^ {2},
\text { s.t. } \quad \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \gamma I & A \\ A ^ {\top} & \gamma I \end{array} \right] \succeq 0, \quad \tau \geq \gamma , \quad \tau \geq 0, \tag {51}
$$

$$\left(A _ {2} ^ {*}, B _ {2} ^ {*}, C _ {2} ^ {*}, D _ {2} ^ {*}\right) = \operatorname{argmin} _ {A, B, C, D} \| \mathcal {R} \| _ {F} ^ {2} \tag {52}$$
