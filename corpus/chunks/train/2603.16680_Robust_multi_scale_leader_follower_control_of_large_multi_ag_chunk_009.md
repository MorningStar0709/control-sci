# A. Followers control design

As mentioned above, to study the stability properties of the follower population, we assume it is possible to arbitrarily choose their drift.

Under this assumption, we choose $\nu ^ { F L }$ as the convex combination

$$v ^ {F L} (x, t) = [ 1 - \alpha (t) ] v ^ {F F} (x) + \alpha (t) v ^ {F B} (x, t), \tag {8}$$

where $\alpha : \mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }  [ 0 , 1 ]$ is a design function, $\nu ^ { F F }$ is a feedforward action and $\dot { \nu } ^ { F B }$ is a feedback correction. More precisely, $\nu ^ { F F }$ is chosen as in [13], that is

$$v ^ {F F} (x) = D \frac {\bar {\rho} _ {x} ^ {F} (x)}{\bar {\rho} ^ {F} (x)}. \tag {9}$$

This choice ensures that, in absence of any disturbance and unmodeled dynamics, when $ { \boldsymbol \nu } ^ { F B } = 0$ , the followers displace according to the desired density profile if $\| g _ { 1 } \| _ { \infty } < 2$ , with

$$g _ {1} (x) = \left[ \frac {\bar {\rho} _ {x} ^ {F} (x)}{\bar {\rho} ^ {F} (x)} \right] _ {x}. \tag {10}$$

For more details on this result, see [13].

Theorem 1: We choose $\nu ^ { F L }$ in (8), with $\nu ^ { F B }$ such that

$$\left[ \rho^ {F} (x, t) v ^ {F B} (x, t) \right] _ {x} = q ^ {F} (x, t). \tag {11}$$

Here

$$q ^ {F} (x, t) = - k _ {p} ^ {F} e ^ {F} (x, t) - k _ {s} ^ {F} (t) \mathrm{sign} \left[ e ^ {F} (x, t) \right] + \beta (t), \tag {12}$$

where $\beta$ is a bounded function of time. $\mathrm { I f } \ \| g _ { 1 } \| _ { \infty } < 2 , k _ { p } ^ { F } > 0$ ,

$$k _ {s} ^ {F} (t) > \frac {\alpha (t) D \| \bar {\rho} _ {x x} ^ {F} (\cdot) \| _ {\infty} + k \| \bar {\rho} _ {x} ^ {F} (\cdot) \| _ {\infty}}{\alpha (t)}, \tag {13}$$

then $e ^ { F }$ globally asymptotically converges to 0 in $\mathcal { L } ^ { 2 } ( \Omega )$ , $\forall \alpha \in ( 0 , 1 ]$ .

Proof: We substitute (8) with (11) into (4b), yielding (dropping dependence from space and time for compactness)

$$\rho_ {t} ^ {F} + (1 - \alpha) D \left(\rho^ {F} \frac {\bar {\rho} _ {x x} ^ {F}}{\bar {\rho} ^ {F}}\right) _ {x} + \alpha q ^ {F} \pm k \rho_ {x} ^ {F} = D \rho_ {x x} ^ {F}. \tag {14}$$

Recalling that $e ^ { F } = \bar { \rho } ^ { F } - \rho ^ { F }$ , we can rephrase (14) in terms of the error function, resulting in

$$
\begin{array}{l} e _ {t} ^ {F} = - \alpha D \bar {\rho} _ {x x} ^ {F} - (1 - \alpha) D \left(e ^ {F} \frac {\nabla \bar {\rho} ^ {F}}{\bar {\rho} ^ {F}}\right) _ {x} + \alpha q ^ {F} \\ \pm k \bar {\rho} _ {x} ^ {F} \mp k e _ {x} ^ {F} + D e _ {x x} ^ {F}. \tag {15} \\ \end{array}
$$
