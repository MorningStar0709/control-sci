$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {1} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} \\ y _ {k} \\ y _ {c} \\ y _ {m} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ - \frac {\bar {k}}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {\bar {c}}{\bar {m}} & \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {0 . 1}{\bar {m}} \\ 0. 3 \bar {k} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0. 2 \bar {c} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ - \bar {k} & - \bar {c} & 1 & - 1 & - 1 & - 0. 1 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{l} x _ {1} \\ x _ {2} \\ F \\ u _ {k} \\ u _ {c} \\ u _ {m} \end{array} \right], \quad \left[ \begin{array}{l} u _ {k} \\ u _ {c} \\ u _ {m} \end{array} \right] = \Delta \left[ \begin{array}{l} y _ {k} \\ y _ {c} \\ y _ {m} \end{array} \right].
$$

That is,

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{l} \dot {x} _ {1} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} \end{array} \right] = \mathcal {F} _ {\ell} (M, \Delta) \left[ \begin{array}{l} x _ {1} \\ x _ {2} \\ F \end{array} \right]
$$

where

$$
M = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c c c} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ - \frac {\bar {k}}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {\bar {c}}{\bar {m}} & \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {1}{\bar {m}} & - \frac {0 . 1}{\bar {m}} \\ \hline 0. 3 \bar {k} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0. 2 \bar {c} & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ - \bar {k} & - \bar {c} & 1 & - 1 & - 1 & - 0. 1 \end{array} \right], \Delta = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} \delta_ {k} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \delta_ {c} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \delta_ {m} \end{array} \right].
$$
