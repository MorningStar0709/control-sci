(4.1.2) Formulation (I I ) The required velocity can also be expressed as follows:

(4.1.3) Formulation (III) Here we will take the required velocity to be

$$\mathbf {V} _ {R} = \frac {\sqrt {\mu}}{r _ {v} r _ {t} \sin \theta} \left\{\mathbf {r} _ {t} - \left[ 1 - \left(r _ {t} / p\right) (1 - \cos \theta) \right] \mathbf {r} _ {v} \right\}, \tag {27}$$

where

$$r _ {v} r _ {t} \sin \theta = | \mathbf {r} _ {v} \times \mathbf {r} _ {t} |. \tag {28}$$

The semilatus rectum $p$ will be determined by the constraint on a required time-offlight $( T - t _ { o } )$ as determined by the methods presented in Section 5.

(5) Solution for Trajectory Parameter: Spherical Earth Case We see from the previous section that the required velocity is expressible in terms of the geometry defined by $\mathbf { r } _ { v }$ and $\mathbf { r } _ { t }$ and in terms of one or more trajectory parameters, as yet undetermined. These parameters are uniquely determined for a spherical Earth model by the requirement of a fixed total time of flight, such that the time of flight remaining, $t _ { f f } = T - t _ { o }$ , is determined.
