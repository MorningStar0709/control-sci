Proposition 2.2. Let $\varepsilon , \tau _ { 1 } , \tau _ { 2 } > 0$ be the positive parameters, X and Y be complete separable metric spaces, $\mu \in \mathcal { M } _ { + } ( X ) , \ \nu \in \mathcal { M } _ { + } ( Y )$ be positive measures, and let $c \in L ^ { \infty } ( X \times Y )$ be a cost function. Then for fixed $M \geq 0$ there exist constants $\alpha , \beta \in \mathbb { R }$ such that for any $u \in L ^ { \infty } ( X ) , v \in L ^ { \infty } ( Y )$ satisfying

$$D _ {\varepsilon} ^ {\tau_ {1}, \tau_ {2}} (u, v) \geq - M, \tag {2.14}$$

holds that $\big | \big | u ^ { ( c , \tau _ { 2 } , \varepsilon ) } \big | \big | _ { \infty } \leq \alpha _ { ; }$ , and $\big | \big | v ^ { ( c , \tau _ { 1 } , \varepsilon ) } \big | \big | _ { \infty } \leq \beta$

Proof. Suppose u and v satisfy condition (2.14), then we obtain the following relation

$$0 \leq \tau_ {1} \int_ {X} e ^ {- \frac {u}{\tau_ {1}}} \mathrm{d} \mu + \tau_ {2} \int_ {Y} e ^ {- \frac {v}{\tau_ {2}}} \mathrm{d} \nu + \varepsilon \int_ {X \times Y} e ^ {\frac {u + v - c}{\varepsilon}} \mathrm{d} \mu \otimes \nu \leq M + \tau_ {1} \mu (X) + \tau_ {2} \nu (Y) \equiv A.$$

See that each of the integral terms is nonnegative, and thus bounded by A, as their sum is bounded from above by A. Consequently, we can consider these three inequalities individually.

In the following, we will exploit the convexity of the exponential function and apply Jensen’s inequality to the normalized probability measures

$$\mathrm{d} \rho \equiv \frac {1}{\mu (X)} \mathrm{d} \mu \qquad \mathrm{and} \qquad \mathrm{d} \sigma \equiv \frac {1}{\nu (Y)} \mathrm{d} \nu .$$

For the first term, by the direct application of Jensen’s inequality

$$A \geq \tau_ {1} \int_ {X} e ^ {- \frac {u}{\tau_ {1}}} \mathrm{d} \mu = \tau_ {1} \mu (X) \int_ {X} e ^ {- \frac {u}{\tau_ {1}}} \mathrm{d} \rho \geq \tau_ {1} \mu (X) \exp \left(- \frac {\int_ {X} u \mathrm{d} \rho}{\tau_ {1}}\right) > 0,$$

and, in particular, we see that

$$\int_ {X} u \mathrm{d} \rho \geq \tau_ {1} \log \left(\frac {\tau_ {1} \mu (X)}{A}\right) \equiv \omega_ {1}. \tag {2.15}$$

By an analogous computation, we also obtain

$$\int_ {Y} v \mathrm{d} \sigma \geq \tau_ {2} \log \left(\frac {\tau_ {2} \nu (Y)}{A}\right) \equiv \omega_ {2}. \tag {2.16}$$
