# 9.1.1 The Basic Equations

The objective is to estimate the parameters of the plant model defined by the transfer operator:

$$G (q ^ {- 1}) = \frac {q ^ {- d} B (q ^ {- 1})}{A (q ^ {- 1})} \tag {9.1}$$

where:

$$B (q ^ {- 1}) = b _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + b _ {n _ {B}} q ^ {- n _ {B}} = q ^ {- 1} B ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {9.2}A (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + a _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + a _ {n _ {A}} q ^ {- n _ {A}} = 1 + q ^ {- 1} A ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {9.3}$$

The plant is operated in closed loop with an RST digital controller (without lack of generality). The output of the plant operating in closed loop is given by (see Fig. 9.1b):

$$y (t + 1) = - A ^ {*} y (t) + B ^ {*} u (t - d) + A v (t + 1) = \theta^ {T} \varphi (t) + A v (t + 1) \tag {9.4}$$

where $u ( t )$ is the plant input, $y ( t )$ is the plant output, $v ( t )$ is the output disturbance noise and:

$$\theta^ {T} = [ a _ {1}, \dots , a _ {n _ {A}}, b _ {1}, \dots , b _ {n _ {B}} ] \tag {9.5}\varphi^ {T} (t) = [ - y (t), \dots , - y (t - n _ {A} + 1), u (t - d), \dots , u (t - n _ {B} + 1 - d) ] \tag {9.6}u (t) = - \frac {R}{S} y (t) + r _ {u} \tag {9.7}$$

where $r _ { u }$ is the external excitation added to the output of the controller $( r _ { u }$ is equal to $r \frac { T } { S }$ if the external excitation is applied on the reference).

For a fixed value of the estimated parameters, the predictor of the closed loop (the design system) can be expressed as:

$$\hat {y} (t + 1) = - \hat {A} ^ {*} \hat {y} (t) + \hat {B} ^ {*} \hat {u} (t - d) = \hat {\theta} ^ {T} \phi (t) \tag {9.8}$$

where

$$\hat {\theta} ^ {T} = \left[ \hat {a} _ {1}, \dots , \hat {a} _ {n _ {A}}, \hat {b} _ {1} \dots , \hat {b} _ {n _ {B}} \right] \tag {9.9}\phi^ {T} (t) = [ - \hat {y} (t), \dots , - \hat {y} (t - n _ {A} + 1), \hat {u} (t - d), \dots , \hat {u} (t - n _ {B} + 1 - d) ] \tag {9.10}\hat {u} (t) = - \frac {R}{S} \hat {y} (t) + r _ {u} \tag {9.11}$$

The closed-loop prediction (output) error is defined as:

$$\varepsilon_ {C L} (t + 1) = y (t + 1) - \hat {y} (t + 1) \tag {9.12}$$
