# 4.1 Robustness of Value Iteration Algorithm

In this section, we show that if we have MFGs with converging transition probabilities under the assumption that value iteration algorithm works for the space of Borel measurable Q functions with $w _ { m a x } \mathrm { - b o u n d e d }$ minima and the nominal model has an unique equilibrium, then mean-field equilibria obtained for the MFGs converge to that of the limiting case.

We will define the space of Borel measurable funtions on $X \times A$ such that their infimum on A are bounded by $w _ { \mathrm { m a x } }$ as follows:

$$B _ {b, w _ {\max}} (X \times A) = \{f \in B (X \times A): \| f _ {\min} \| _ {w _ {\max}} \leq C \text {for some constant} C \}.$$

We will equip $B _ { b , w _ { \mathrm { m a x } } } ( X \times A )$ with the w-norm.

To simplify the notation, we will assume that our mean-field games differ only in their transition probabilities, which we will denote as $p _ { n }$ . A similar analysis can be carried out for the case of varying cost functions $c _ { n }$ under the condition that $c _ { n }$ continuously converges to c.

We will denote each game by $\mathrm { M F G } _ { n }$ and corresponding mean-field equilibrium operator as

$$H _ {1, p _ {n}}: B _ {b, w _ {\max}} (X \times A) \times \mathcal {P} (X) \to B _ {b, w _ {\max}} (X \times A) \times \mathcal {P} (X): (Q, \mu) \to (H _ {1, p _ {n}} ^ {1} (Q, \mu), H _ {2, p _ {n}} ^ {1} (Q, \mu)),$$

where the iterations of the Q-functions is handled by the operator

$$H _ {1, p _ {n}} ^ {1} (Q, \mu) (x, a) = c (x, a, \mu) + \beta \int_ {X} Q _ {\min} (y) p _ {n} (d y | x, a, \mu)$$

and assuming that a measurable minimizer

$$f _ {1, p _ {n}} (x, Q, \mu) \in \underset {a \in A} {\operatorname{argmin}} H _ {1, p _ {n}} ^ {1} (Q, \mu) (x, a), \tag {8}$$

exists (which is the case under our assumptions), we define the ergodicity operator to handle the iterations on the state-measures

$$H _ {2, p _ {n}} ^ {1} (Q, \mu) (\cdot) = \int_ {X} p _ {n} (\cdot | x, f _ {1, p _ {n}} (x, Q, \mu), \mu) \mu (d x).$$

We remark that as in Lemma 2, any Q-function in $B _ { b , w _ { \mathrm { m a x } } } ( X \times A )$ is mapped to a Q-function in $B _ { b , w _ { \mathrm { m a x } } } ( X \times A )$ by the operator $H _ { 1 , p _ { n } } ^ { 1 }$ .
