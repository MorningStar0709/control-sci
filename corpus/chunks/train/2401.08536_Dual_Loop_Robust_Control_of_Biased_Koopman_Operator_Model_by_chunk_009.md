The control input matrix U and output Y are not lifted to keep the input-output mapping. The approximate system matrices can be obtained by solving the least square regression problem [24].

$$\min _ {A _ {p}, B _ {p}} \left| \left| \mathbf {X} _ {\text { lift }} ^ {2} - A _ {p} \mathbf {X} _ {\text { lift }} ^ {1} - B _ {p} \mathbf {U} \right| \right| _ {F} \tag {9}\min _ {C _ {p}} \left| \left| \mathbf {Y} - C _ {p} \mathbf {X} _ {\text {lift}} ^ {1} \right| \right| _ {F} \tag {10}$$

It is equivalently interpreted as linear system identification using input-state-output data. The optimal solution of (9) will give the state-space matrices $A _ { p }$ and $B _ { p } .$ Similarly, (10) will provide matrix $C _ { p }$ for the output. The optimal solutions of least regression can be analytically expressed as:

$$\left[ A _ {p}, B _ {p} \right] = \mathbf {X} _ {\text { lift }} ^ {2} \left[ \mathbf {X} _ {\text { lift }} ^ {1}; \mathbf {U} \right] ^ {\dagger} \tag {11}C _ {p} = \mathbf {Y} \left[ \mathbf {X} _ {\text {lift}} ^ {1} \right] ^ {\dagger} \tag {12}$$

Note that, in the absence of measurement noises, the model matrices $( A _ { p } , B _ { p } , C _ { p } )$ can render accurate approximations of input-output mapping by the high-dimensional linear system in observables space with small approximation error and regression error. However, when measurement noises are present, the noise will perturb data snapshots, lifted/filtered by basis function ??(⋅), and eventually render model mismatch with biased modes and magnitudes. The next section will investigate the model mismatch induced by noises, and quantify the bound of the model mismatch.
