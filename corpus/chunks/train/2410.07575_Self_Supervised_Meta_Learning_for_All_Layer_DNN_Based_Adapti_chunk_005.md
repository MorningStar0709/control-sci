# B. Adaptive Control with Neural Networks

Neural network-based adaptive control has also been widely studied. Early works focused on shallow or singlelayer neural networks [20]–[22], while recent approaches shift towards using deep neural networks to model complex dynamics [23], [24] or constrain network Lipschitz gain to ensure stability in DNN-based adaptive control [25]. Since neural network controllers require appropriate initialization for convergence [26], some studies pretrain DNNs before deployment to enhance control performance [27]– [29]. However, typical pertaining methods simply aggregate data and learn average disturbance, so the resulting neural networks lack rapid adaptability, which is critical in dynamic environments.
