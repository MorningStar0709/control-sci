# 3. Multi-stage MPC

Unlike standard MPC, multi-stage MPC explicitly takes into account the model uncertainty. Multi-stage MPC models uncertainty as a tree of discrete scenarios (called a scenario tree) used to depict the evolution of uncertainty along the prediction horizon [3, 6]. To reduce the problem size in the scenario tree that grows exponentially with the prediction horizon, it is often proposed to limit the branching of the tree only up to a certain stage, called the robust horizon [5, 6]. Fig. 1 shows a scenario tree with a robust horizon $N _ { R } = 2$ and nine scenarios, where branching stops at $t _ { k + N _ { R } }$ and the parameter realizations are kept constant until $t _ { k + N }$ . The number of scenarios is given by $\mathrm { c a r d ( \mathbb { C } ) } =$ $( N _ { D } ) ^ { N _ { R } }$ , where $\mathbb { C }$ is the scenario index set and $N _ { D }$ is the number of discrete parameter realizations. The problem size is significantly reduced when $N _ { R } < < N$ , and the fully branched scenario tree is recovered by setting $N _ { R } = N$ .

Given $x _ { k }$ at $t _ { k }$ <<multi-stage MPC requires solving the following problem:

$$V _ {N} ^ {\mathrm{ms}} \left(x _ {k}, \mathbf {d} ^ {c}\right) = \min _ {z _ {i} ^ {c}, v _ {i} ^ {c}} \sum_ {c \in \mathbb {C}} \omega_ {c} \left(\psi \left(z _ {N} ^ {c}, d _ {N - 1} ^ {c}\right) + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {N - 1} \ell \left(z _ {i} ^ {c}, v _ {i} ^ {c}, d _ {i} ^ {c}\right)\right) \tag {3a}\text { s.t. } z _ {i + 1} ^ {c} = f (z _ {i} ^ {c}, \nu_ {i} ^ {c}, d _ {i} ^ {c}), i = 0, \dots , N - 1 \tag {3b}z _ {0} ^ {c} = x _ {k}, \tag {3c}\nu_ {i} ^ {c} = \nu_ {i} ^ {c ^ {\prime}}, \left\{\left(c, c ^ {\prime}\right) \mid z _ {i} ^ {c} = z _ {i} ^ {c ^ {\prime}} \right\} \tag {3d}d _ {i - 1} ^ {c} = d _ {i} ^ {c}, i \geq N _ {R} \tag {3e}z _ {i} ^ {c} \in \mathbb {X}, \nu_ {i} ^ {c} \in \mathbb {U}, d _ {i} ^ {c} \in \mathbb {D}, \tag {3f}z _ {N} ^ {c} \in \mathbb {X} _ {f} ^ {c}, \tag {3g}\forall c, c ^ {\prime} \in \mathbb {C}$$
