$$
\begin{array}{l} D _ {\varepsilon} ^ {\tau_ {1}, \tau_ {2}} (u, v) = - \tau_ {1} \int_ {X} (e ^ {- \frac {u}{\tau_ {1}}} - 1) \mathrm{d} \mu - \tau_ {2} \int_ {Y} (e ^ {- \frac {v}{\tau_ {2}}} - 1) \mathrm{d} \nu - \varepsilon \int_ {Y} e ^ {\frac {v}{\varepsilon}} e ^ {- \frac {(\tau_ {2} + \varepsilon) u ^ {(c , \tau_ {2} , \varepsilon)}}{\tau_ {2}   \varepsilon}} \mathrm{d} \nu \\ = - \tau_ {1} \int_ {X} (e ^ {- \frac {u}{\tau_ {1}}} - 1) \mathrm{d} \mu - \int_ {Y} \left[ \tau_ {2} (e ^ {- \frac {v}{\tau_ {2}}} - 1) + \varepsilon   e ^ {\frac {v}{\varepsilon} - \frac {(\tau_ {2} + \varepsilon) u ^ {(c , \tau_ {2} , \varepsilon)}}{\tau_ {2}   \varepsilon}} \right] \mathrm{d} \nu . \\ \end{array}
$$

Invoking the variational principle one can show that, in fact,

$$u ^ {(c, \tau_ {2}, \varepsilon)} \in \underset {v \in L ^ {\infty} (Y)} {\arg \min} \int_ {Y} \left[ \tau_ {2} (e ^ {- \frac {v}{\tau_ {2}}} - 1) + \varepsilon e ^ {\frac {v}{\varepsilon} - \frac {(\tau_ {2} + \varepsilon) u ^ {(c , \tau_ {2} , \varepsilon)}}{\tau_ {2} \varepsilon}} \right] \mathrm{d} \nu ,$$

Therefore, (2.9) and (2.10) hold, and by an identical computation (2.11), (2.12) hold as well. □
