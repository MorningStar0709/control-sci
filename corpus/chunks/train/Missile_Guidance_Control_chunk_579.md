$$\delta V _ {L} = - V _ {1} \times (\text { angle between } V _ {1} \text { and } \Delta V _ {1} (\text { i.e., } V _ {1} \text {-perturbed})), \tag {6.159}$$

which assumes a small angle (a valid assumption) between $V _ { 1 }$ and $\Delta V _ { 1 }$ .

Next, the angle between $V _ { 1 }$ and $\Delta V _ { 1 }$ is $\beta \cos \rho ,$ , where $\beta$ is the plane separation angle and $\rho$ is the angle measured from $V _ { 1 }$ to the line normal to the axis of separation. Note that if $V _ { 1 }$ were along the normal to the axis of separation, the angle between $V _ { 1 }$ and $\Delta V _ { 1 }$ would simply be $\beta$ . The farther that $V _ { 1 }$ is from the normal (i.e., $\rho$ grows toward $9 0 ^ { \circ } )$ , the smaller the angle between $V _ { 1 }$ and $\Delta V _ { 1 }$ becomes.

Now it is necessary to express $\rho$ in terms of known angles $\rho = 9 0 ^ { \circ } + \gamma - \gamma _ { 1 } - \theta ^ { \prime }$ . Substituting this angle in

$$\delta V _ {L} = - V _ {1} \beta \cos \rho , \tag {6.160a}$$

we obtain

$$\delta V _ {L} = - V _ {1} \left(\delta n / r _ {o} \sin \gamma\right) \cos \left[ 9 0 ^ {\circ} + \left(\gamma - \gamma_ {1} - \theta^ {\prime}\right) \right], \tag {6.160b}$$

where

$$\theta^ {\prime} = \theta - \theta_ {o}, \tag {6.161}$$

which reduces to

$$\delta V _ {L} = V _ {1} (\delta n / r _ {o} \sin \gamma) \sin (\gamma - \gamma_ {1} - \theta^ {\prime}), \tag {6.162}$$

and thus the desired error coefficient is

$$\delta V _ {L} = V _ {1} \sin (\gamma - \gamma_ {1} - \theta^ {\prime}) / r _ {o} \sin \gamma .$$

Upon substitution from (6.139a), and (6.139b), we have

$$V _ {r} = V [ - (\sin \theta^ {\prime} / \lambda \sin \gamma) + \cos (\gamma - \theta^ {\prime}) ]$$

and

$$V _ {\theta} = V [ ((1 - \cos \theta^ {\prime}) / (\lambda \sin \gamma)) + \sin (\gamma - \theta^ {\prime}) ],$$

and we obtain

$$\partial V _ {L} / \partial n = (- V / r _ {o}) [ (\cos (\gamma - \theta^ {\prime}) - \cos \gamma) / \lambda \sin^ {2} \gamma ]. \tag {6.163}$$

The last error coefficient to be derived is $( \partial V _ { L } / V _ { n } )$ , which relates the out-of-plane or lateral velocity $( \delta V _ { L } )$ at the end-of-coast point $( r _ { 1 } , \theta _ { 1 } )$ to the out-of-plane velocity perturbation $( \delta V _ { n } )$ at cutoff. The geometry for this case is illustrated in Figure 6.20. The development is similar to that for $\partial V _ { L } / \partial n$ . The plane separation angle $\beta$ is

$$\beta = (1 / \cos \alpha) (\delta V _ {n} / V) = (1 / \sin \gamma) (\delta V _ {n} / V). \tag {6.164}$$
