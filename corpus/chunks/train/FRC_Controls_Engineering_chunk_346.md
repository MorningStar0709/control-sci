# 11.2 Angular motion

$$\sum \tau = I \alpha$$

where $\sum \tau$ is the sum of all torques applied to an object in Newton-meters, I is the moment of inertia of the object in $k g { - } m ^ { 2 }$ (also called the rotational mass), and α is the net angular acceleration of the object in $\textstyle { \frac { r a d } { s ^ { 2 } } }$ s 2 .

$$\theta (t) = \theta_ {0} + \omega_ {0} t + \frac {1}{2} \alpha t ^ {2}$$

where $\theta ( t )$ is an object’s angle at time t, $\theta _ { 0 }$ is the initial angle, $\omega _ { 0 }$ is the initial angular velocity, and α is the angular acceleration.
