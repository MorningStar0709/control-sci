As an example, one can ask what is the identification method which has to be used in conjunction with the pole placement (combined eventually with the shaping of the sensitivity functions). In this case, the closed-loop error (see Fig. 9.2) is a measure in the time domain of the discrepancy between the desired and achieved performances in tracking and has the expression:

$$\varepsilon_ {C L} (t + 1) = S _ {y p} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) \left[ G \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) - \hat {G} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) \right] \hat {T} _ {u r} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) r (t) + S _ {y p} \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) v (t + 1) \tag {9.95}$$

where $S _ { y p }$ is the achieved sensitivity function and $\hat { T } _ { u r }$ is the transfer function between r and uˆ. Over an infinite time horizon and when $r ( t )$ has a Fourrier transform, the discrepancy in performance takes the following expression in the frequency domain:

$$J = \int_ {- \pi} ^ {\pi} \left| S _ {y p} \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) \right| ^ {2} \left[ G \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) - \hat {G} \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) \right| ^ {2} \left| \hat {T} _ {u r} \left(e ^ {- j \omega}\right) \right| ^ {2} \phi_ {v} (\omega) + \phi_ {v} (\omega) ] d \omega \tag {9.96}$$

This is exactly the identification criterion for AF-CLOE and F-CLOE identification methods given in Sect. 9.4 (for $r _ { u } = 0 )$ .

For other examples of combining control objectives and identification in closed loop see Van den Hof and Schrama (1995), Zang et al. (1995).
