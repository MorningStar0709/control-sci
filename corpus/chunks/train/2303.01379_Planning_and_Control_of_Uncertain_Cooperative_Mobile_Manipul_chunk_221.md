$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial x _ {i}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial p _ {i}} & \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial R _ {i}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} (p _ {i} - p _ {j}) ^ {\top} & 0 _ {1 \times 3} \end{array} \right], \\ \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial x _ {j}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial p _ {j}} & \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , d}}{\partial R _ {j}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} (p _ {j} - p _ {i}) ^ {\top} & 0 _ {1 \times 3} \end{array} \right], \\ \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial x _ {i}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial p _ {i}} & \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial R _ {i}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} - \frac {P _ {r} (\gamma_ {e , b})}{\| p _ {j} - p _ {i} \|} R _ {i} ^ {\top} & S (\gamma_ {e, b}) R _ {i} ^ {\top} \end{array} \right], \\ \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial x _ {j}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial p _ {j}} & \frac {\partial \gamma_ {e , b}}{\partial R _ {j}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} P _ {r} (\gamma_ {e, b}) \\ \| p _ {j} - p _ {i} \| \end{array} R _ {i} ^ {\top} 0 _ {3 \times 3} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

Here, $P _ { r } ( \gamma _ { e , b } )$ is defined as

$$P _ {r} (\gamma_ {e, b}) := I _ {3} - \frac {(p _ {j} - p _ {i}) (p _ {j} - p _ {i}) ^ {T}}{\| p _ {j} - p _ {i} \| ^ {2}},$$

and projects vectors onto the orthogonal complement of $( p _ { j } - p _ { i } )$ . See [157] for more discussion on this projection matrix and its use in the bearing rigidity theory. Infinitesimal motions, therefore, are motions $x ( t )$ produced by velocities $v ( t )$ that lie in the nullspace of $\mathcal { R } _ { \mathcal { G } }$ , for which it holds that $\begin{array} { r } { \dot { \gamma } _ { \mathcal { G } } = \mathcal { R } _ { \mathcal { G } } ( x ( t ) ) v ( t ) = 0 } \end{array}$ , where $v : = [ \dot { p } _ { 1 } ^ { \top } , \omega _ { 1 } ^ { \top } , \ldots , \dot { p } _ { N } ^ { \top } , \omega _ { N } ^ { \top } ] ^ { \top }$ , as defined before. The infinitesimal motions therefore depend on the number of motion degrees of freedom the entire framework possesses. This directly relates to the structure of the underlying graph. Motions that preserve the distances and bearings of the framework for any underlying graph are called D&B trivial motions. This leads to the definition of infinitesimal rigidity, stated below.
