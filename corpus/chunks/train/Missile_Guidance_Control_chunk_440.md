$$A _ {c x} = 2 (\Omega_ {z} V _ {y} - \Omega_ {y} V _ {z}), \tag {5.26a}A _ {c y} = 2 (\Omega_ {x} V _ {z} - \Omega_ {z} V _ {x}), \tag {5.26b}A _ {c z} = 2 (\Omega_ {y} V _ {x} - \Omega_ {x} V _ {y}). \tag {5.26c}$$

If the x-axis points east, the y-axis north, and the z-axis up, the Earth rate components become

$$\Omega_ {x} = 0, \tag {5.27a}\Omega_ {y} = | \Omega | \cos \phi , \tag {5.27b}\Omega_ {z} = | \Omega | \sin \phi , \tag {5.27c}$$

where $\phi$ is the latitude of the target. The velocity components are

$$V _ {x} = V _ {x r}, \tag {5.28a}V _ {y} = V _ {y r}, \tag {5.28b}V _ {z} = V _ {z r} - g t. \tag {5.28c}$$

Substituting the above components into (5.26), we have

$$A _ {c x} = 2 \Omega_ {z} V _ {y r} - 2 \Omega_ {y} (V _ {z r} - g t), \tag {5.29a}A _ {c y} = - 2 \Omega_ {z} V _ {x r}, \tag {5.29b}A _ {c z} = 2 \Omega_ {y} V _ {x r}. \tag {5.29c}$$

When (5.29) is substituted into (5.24), one obtains the Coriolis correction components. We conclude this section by developing the impact point prediction, represented by $( X _ { i p } , Y _ { i p } )$ coordinates in the target plane. In order to obtain the impact point prediction equations, we need the time-of-fall and trail relationships. These equations are given in terms of a Taylor series expansion in the form

$$t _ {f i} = t _ {f} + \frac {\partial t _ {f}}{\partial Z} (Z _ {i} - Z _ {r}) + \frac {\partial t _ {f}}{\partial V _ {z}} (V _ {z _ {i}} - V _ {z _ {r}}) + \frac {\partial t _ {f}}{\partial V _ {h}} (V _ {h _ {i}} - V _ {h _ {r}}) \tag {5.30a}T _ {r _ {i}} = T _ {r} + \frac {\partial T _ {r}}{\partial Z} (Z _ {i} - Z _ {r}) + \frac {\partial T _ {r}}{\partial V _ {z}} (V _ {z _ {i}} - V _ {z _ {r}}) + \frac {\partial T _ {r}}{\partial V _ {h}} (V _ {h _ {i}} - V _ {h _ {r}}) \tag {5.30b}$$

where $t _ { f }$ and $T _ { r }$ are the time-of-fall and trail output from integrating the trajectory for the reference and release conditions $Z _ { r } , \ V _ { h r } , \ V _ { z r }$ . Once $t _ { f i }$ and $T _ { r i }$ have been obtained, the bomb impact point prediction equations can be expressed by the following equations:

$$X _ {i p} = X _ {i} + V _ {x i} t _ {f i} - T _ {r i} \sin \psi + \omega_ {x c}, \tag {5.31a}Y _ {i p} = Y _ {i} + V _ {y i} t _ {f i} - T _ {r i} \cos \psi + \omega_ {y c}, \tag {5.31b}$$
