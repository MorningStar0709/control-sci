# 6.3.1 Direct Adaptive Prediction

Consider the ARMAX plant + disturbance model:

$$y (t + 1) = - A ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) + q ^ {- d} B ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) + C (q ^ {- 1}) e (t + 1) \tag {6.40}$$

where $e ( t )$ is a discrete-time Gaussian white noise $( 0 , \sigma )$ . For the model (6.40), the expression of $y ( t + d + 1 )$ takes the form (see Sect. 2.2):

$$C y (t + d + 1) = F y (t) + G u (t) + C E e (t + d + 1) \tag {6.41}$$

where:

$$G = B ^ {*} E \tag {6.42}$$

and $E ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ and $F ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ ) are solutions of:

$$C = A E + q ^ {- d - 1} F \tag {6.43}$$

The linear optimal predictor has the form:

$$\hat {y} (t + d + 1) = - C ^ {*} \hat {y} (t + d) + F y (t) + G u (t) \tag {6.44}$$

While in the linear case with known parameters, the predictors for deterministic case and stochastic case look the same (except that P is replaced by C in the stochastic case), in the adaptive case there will be a significative difference since the polynomial C (characterizing the disturbance model) is unknown. To accommodate this situation some modifications of the predictor (6.44) will be considered.

The natural extension for the adaptive prediction in stochastic case will be to define an adjustable predictor as:

$$\hat {y} ^ {0} (t + d + 1) = - \hat {C} ^ {*} (t) \hat {y} (t + d) + \hat {F} (t) y (t) + \hat {G} (t) u (t) = \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) \phi (t)\hat {y} (t + d + 1) = \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t + d + 1) \phi (t)$$

where $\hat { y } ^ { 0 } ( t + j )$ and $\hat { y } ( t + j )$ will define the a priori and the a posteriori outputs of the adjustable predictor. Unfortunately, difficulties arise in the generation of the a posteriori predicted outputs for $j > 1 \left( \mathrm { i . e . , } d > 0 \right)$ . An approximate algorithm can indeed be used by defining the adjustable predictor as:

$$\hat {y} ^ {0} (t + d + 1) = - \hat {C} ^ {*} (t) \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + d) + \hat {F} (t) y (t) + \hat {G} (t) u (t) = \hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) \phi (t) \tag {6.45}$$

where:

$$\hat {\theta} ^ {T} (t) = [ \hat {c _ {1}} (t), \dots , \hat {c} _ {n _ {C}} (t), \hat {f _ {0}} (t), \dots , \hat {f} _ {n _ {F}} (t), \hat {g} _ {0} (t), \dots , \hat {g} _ {n _ {G}} (t) ] \tag {6.46}\phi^ {T} (t) = \left[ - \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + d), \dots , - \hat {y} ^ {0} (t + d - n _ {C}), y (t), \dots , y (t - n _ {F}), \right.\left. u (t), \dots , u \left(t - n _ {G}\right) \right] \tag {6.47}$$
