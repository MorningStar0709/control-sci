# I. INTRODUCTION

Over recent decades, cooperative control of multi-agent systems (MASs) has received significant research interest, owing to its vast range of applications, including power systems, multirobot systems, sensor networks [1–5]. As one of its typical topics, cooperative output regulation (COR) has been well investigated. Various distributed control methods have been developed to address the COR problem for linear MASs and nonlinear MASs [6–11].

In real-world applications, communication networks of MASs are frequently susceptible to cyber-attacks. Denial-ofservice (DoS) attacks, among the most prevalent types of cyber-attacks, disrupt the timely transfer of information by blocking or severing network connectivity [12]. There has been significant research on resilient cooperative control of MASs under DoS attacks recently, see, for example, [13–23]. Particularly, the resilient consensus problem of homogeneous linear MASs is studied over both undirected graphs [13, 14] and directed graphs [15, 16]. Then, the resilient consensus problem of heterogeneous linear MASs over directed graphs is studied in [17, 18]. The problem of resilient COR is addressed for heterogeneous MASs over undirected graphs with linear agent dynamics [19, 20] and nonlinear agent dynamics [21].
