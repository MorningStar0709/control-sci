The error constants $K _ { p } , K _ { v }$ , and $K _ { a }$ describe the ability of a unity-feedback system to reduce or eliminate steady-state error.Therefore, they are indicative of the steady-state performance. It is generally desirable to increase the error constants, while maintaining the transient response within an acceptable range. It is noted that to improve the steadystate performance we can increase the type of the system by adding an integrator or integrators to the feedforward path. This, however, introduces an additional stability problem. The design of a satisfactory system with more than two integrators in series in the feedforward path is generally not easy.
