The functional form and the derivation procedure, namely (43), suggest that if the current utility is high such that $U \left( x , \mu \right) > > U \left( y , \mu \right)$ , then the logit function is close to 0. Then, the player should not change the current action. We thus found an explicit connection to the logit function and a controlled pure-jump stochastic process; the latter can be understood as a trajectory of actions chosen by a representative player. This kind of explicit connection between a logit dynamic and an MFG has not been reported so far.
