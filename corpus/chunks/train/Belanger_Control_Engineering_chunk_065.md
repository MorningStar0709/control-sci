2.12 High-wire artist A high-wire artist uses a balancing rod for stability purposes. Figure 2.27 is a simplified representation of the physical situation. The person is represented by a bar of mass $M_M$ pivoting about one of its end points. The moment of inertia of the bar about the pivot is $J_M$ . The rod, of mass $M_R$ , rotates about a point situated at a distance $\ell$ from the feet of the artist. The person exerts a torque $\tau$ on the rod, which has a moment of inertia $J_R$ about its pivoting axis, which goes through the rod's center of gravity.
