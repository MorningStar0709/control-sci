It is a well-known fact that the negative feedback interconnection of two passive systems is again passive (see Khalil (2002, Theorem 6.1)) and thus Lyapunov stable, i. e., all eigenvalues of the corresponding closed-loop system matrix have nonpositive real part and the ones on the imaginary axis are semi-simple. In our setting, a negative feedback interconnection is a coupling of the plant and controller obtained from the modified coupling conditions $u _ { \bf K } ( \cdot ) = y ( \cdot )$ and $u ( \cdot ) = - y _ { \bf K } ( \cdot )$ . We use this coupling in the remainder of this article.

We restate the passive interconnection result for pH systems and provide a proof because our setup differs slightly from the literature.

Proposition 1. Consider a pH plant model (without the channels w and z)

$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathsf {p H}}: \left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} (t) \\ y (t) \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} (J - R) Q & G - F \\ (G + F) ^ {\top} Q & S - N \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (t) \\ u (t) \end{array} \right],
$$

that satisfies the conditions in Definition 1, and a pH controller

$$
\mathbf {K} _ {\mathrm{pH}}: \left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \\ y _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} (J _ {\mathbf {K}} - R _ {\mathbf {K}}) Q _ {\mathbf {K}} & G _ {\mathbf {K}} - F _ {\mathbf {K}} \\ (G _ {\mathbf {K}} + F _ {\mathbf {K}}) ^ {\top} Q _ {\mathbf {K}} & S _ {\mathbf {K}} - N _ {\mathbf {K}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \\ u _ {\mathbf {K}} (t) \end{array} \right],
$$

that also satisfies the conditions in Definition 1. Then the negative feedback interconnection of $\mathbf { P _ { p H } }$ with $\mathbf { K } _ { \mathsf { p H } }$ resulting from the coupling conditions $u _ { \bf K } ( \cdot ) \ = \ y ( \cdot )$ and $u ( \cdot ) = - y _ { \bf K } ( \cdot )$ results in a Lyapunov stable closed-loop system.

Proof. The closed-loop matrix of the negative feedback interconnection of $\mathbf { P _ { p H } }$ and $\mathbf { K } _ { \mathsf { p H } }$ is given by

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} (J - R) Q & 0 \\ 0 & (J _ {\mathbf {K}} - R _ {\mathbf {K}}) Q _ {\mathbf {K}} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} G - F & 0 \\ 0 & - G _ {\mathbf {K}} + F _ {\mathbf {K}} \end{array} \right]
