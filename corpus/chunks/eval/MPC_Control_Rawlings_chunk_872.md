# Example A.42: Does uncorrelated imply independent?

Let $\xi$ and $\eta$ be jointly distributed random variables with probability density function

$$
p _ {\xi , \eta} (x, y) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \frac {1}{4} [ 1 + x y (x ^ {2} - y ^ {2}) ], & | x | <   1, \quad | y | <   1 \\ 0, & \text { otherwise } \end{array} \right.
$$

(a) Compute the marginals $p _ { \xi } ( x )$ and $p _ { \eta } ( y )$ . Are $\xi$ and η independent?   
(b) Compute $\operatorname { c o v } ( \xi , \eta )$ . Are $\xi$ and η uncorrelated?   
(c) What is the relationship between independent and uncorrelated? Are your results on this example consistent with this relationship? Why or why not?

$$p _ {\xi , \eta} (x, y) = \frac {1}{4} \left[ 1 + x y (x ^ {2} - y ^ {2}) \right]$$
