# IV. CONCLUDING REMARKS

In this paper, we proposed the Dual State-Space Fidelity Blade (D-STAB) paradigm, a stealthy cyber-physical attack strategy exploiting fidelity gaps between high- and low-fidelity models in cyber-physical systems. By precisely manipulating firmware parameters using high-fidelity statespace information, D-STAB can stealthily induce severe operational disruptions. Through our case study on optimal battery charging within Battery Management Systems, we demonstrated D-STAB’s capability to stealthily manipulate critical infrastructure operations. Future research should prioritize the development of advanced detection and mitigation techniques aimed at reducing fidelity gaps.
