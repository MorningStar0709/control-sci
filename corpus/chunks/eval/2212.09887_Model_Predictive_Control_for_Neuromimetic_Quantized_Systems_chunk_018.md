Assume the optimal input sequence $U ^ { * } ( k ) ~ = ~ \{ u _ { 0 \vert k } ^ { * } , . . . ,$ $u _ { N - 1 | k } ^ { * } \}$ solves the optimization problem (4) at time k and apply $u _ { 0 | k } ^ { * }$ to the quantized system (3). At time $k + 1$ , we define a shifted input sequence $\hat { U }$ to be

$$\hat {U} (k + 1) = \left\{\hat {u} _ {0 | k + 1}, \dots , \hat {u} _ {N - 2 | k + 1}, \hat {u} _ {N - 1 | k + 1} \right\} \tag {A.1}= \{u _ {1 | k} ^ {*}, \dots , u _ {N - 1 | k} ^ {*}, \hat {u} _ {N - 1 | k + 1} \},$$

where $\hat { u } _ { N - 1 | k + 1 } \in \{ - 1 , 0 , 1 \} ^ { m }$ is the predicted next $N - 1$ input at time $k + \mathrm { \bar { 1 } }$ , which has not been defined up to this point. Figure(A.1) exhibits the relationship between $\hat { U } ( k + 1 )$ and $U ^ { * } ( k )$ . Then, $J ( x _ { q } ( k + 1 ) , \hat { U } ( k + 1 ) )$ can be expressed as

$$J (x _ {q} (k + 1), \hat {U} (k + 1)) = J (x _ {q} (k), U ^ {*} (k)) - | u _ {0 | k} ^ {*} | _ {R} ^ {2}- | x _ {0 | k} - x _ {r e f} (0, k) | _ {Q} ^ {2} - | x _ {N | k} - x _ {r e f} (N, k) | _ {P} ^ {2}+ | x _ {N - 1 | k + 1} - x _ {r e f} (N - 1, k + 1) | _ {Q} ^ {2}+ | x _ {N | k + 1} - x _ {r e f} (N, k + 1) | _ {P} ^ {2} + | \hat {u} _ {N - 1 | k + 1} | _ {R} ^ {2}. \tag {A.2}$$

Since the second term $( - | u _ { 0 | k } ^ { * } | _ { R } ^ { 2 } )$ and third term $( - | x _ { 0 | k } -$ $x _ { r e f } ( k , 0 ) | _ { Q } ^ { 2 } )$ in the above polynomial are always smaller or equal than zero, the sum of the last four terms in (A.2) is non–positive providing a sufficient condition to guarantee the optimal cost function J is non-increasing at time $k + 1$ . Meanwhile, it should be noted that the optimal input sequence $U ^ { * } ( k + 1 ) ~ = ~ \{ u _ { 0 | k + 1 } ^ { * } , . . . , u _ { N - 2 | k + 1 } ^ { * } , u _ { N - 1 | k + 1 } ^ { * } \}$ obtained by solving the optimization problem (4) at time $k + 1$ should have an equal or lower cost than any other quantized inputs including $\hat { U } ( k + 1 )$ , i.e.,

$$J (x _ {q} (k + 1), U ^ {*} (k + 1)) \leq J (x _ {q} (k + 1), \hat {U} (k + 1)). \tag {A.3}$$

Therefore, from (A.2) and (A.3), we can obtain

$$J (x _ {q} (k + 1), U ^ {*} (k + 1)) - J (x _ {q} (k), U ^ {*} (k))\leq J (x _ {q} (k + 1), \hat {U} (k + 1)) - J (x _ {q} (k), U ^ {*} (k)) \tag {A.4}\leq - | x _ {0 | k} - x _ {r e f} (k, 0) | _ {Q} ^ {2} - | u _ {0 | k} ^ {*} | _ {R} ^ {2} \leq 0,$$

as long as the sum of the last four terms in (A.2) is non–positive.
