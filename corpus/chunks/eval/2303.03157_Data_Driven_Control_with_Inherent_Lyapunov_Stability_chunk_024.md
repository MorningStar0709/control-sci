# C. Proof of Theorem IV.2

Proof. Suppose (16) holds and represent the difference as

$$\Delta := \frac {\alpha \epsilon_ {\mathrm{pd}} r ^ {2}}{M _ {r}} - (L _ {f} + L _ {f *}) \delta - e > 0. \tag {30}$$

We first prove that V is strictly decreasing along any trajectories outside $N _ { r }$ for the true closed-loop system $f _ { u ^ { * } }$ . Then, we show that they arrive at $N _ { r }$ by contradiction.

For all $x \in \mathcal { X } \setminus N _ { r } , V$ is strictly decreasing for the closedloop system $f _ { u } ^ { * }$ ∗ since

$$\nabla_ {f _ {u ^ {*}} ^ {*}} V (x) \leq - \alpha V (x) \leq - \alpha \epsilon_ {\mathrm{pd}} \| x \| ^ {2} \leq - \alpha \epsilon_ {\mathrm{pd}} r ^ {2} < 0. \tag {31}$$

Then, we show V is also strictly decreasing for the true system $f _ { u ^ { * } }$ by bounding the difference

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {f _ {u ^ {*}}} V (x) - \nabla_ {f _ {u ^ {*}} ^ {*}} V (x) \\ \leq \| \nabla V (x) \| _ {2} \| f (x, u ^ {*} (x)) - f ^ {*} (x, u ^ {*} (x)) \| _ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$

The generalization error of the learned closed-loop system can be bounded as

$$\left\| f (x, u ^ {*} (x)) - f ^ {*} (x, u ^ {*} (x)) \right\| _ {2}\leq \left\| f (x, u ^ {*} (x)) - f (y, v) \right\| _ {2} + \left\| f (y, v) - f ^ {*} (y, v) \right\| _ {2} \tag {33}+ \left\| f ^ {*} (y, v) - f ^ {*} (x, u ^ {*} (x)) \right\| _ {2}\leq L _ {f} \delta + e + L _ {f *} \delta = (L _ {f} + L _ {f *}) \delta + e$$

where $( y , v )$ is the nearest data sample in D from $( x , u ^ { * } ( x ) )$ ). The first inequality is from the triangle inequality and the second inequality is from the definitions of $L _ { f } , L _ { f ^ { * } } , \delta , e \mathrm { ~ }$ . Putting the above inequalities together with (30), we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {f _ {u ^ {*}}} V (x) \leq \nabla_ {f _ {u ^ {*}} ^ {*}} V (x) \\ + \| \nabla V (x) \| _ {2} \| f (x, u ^ {*} (x)) - f ^ {*} (x, u ^ {*} (x)) \| _ {2} \\ \leq - \alpha \epsilon_ {\mathrm{pd}} r ^ {2} + M _ {r} ((L _ {f} + L _ {f *}) \delta + e) = - M _ {r} \Delta . \tag {34} \\ \end{array}
$$

Now, we prove that all trajectories arrive at $N _ { r }$ by contradiction. Suppose there exists $x ( 0 ) \in \mathcal { X } \setminus N _ { r }$ such that the closed-loop system $f _ { u ^ { * } }$ generates a trajectory $x ( t ) \notin N _ { r }$ for all $t \geq 0$ . Then, $\| x ( t ) \| _ { 2 } \geq r$ implies

$$V (x (t)) \geq \alpha \epsilon_ {\mathrm{pd}} r ^ {2}. \tag {35}$$

However, since $x ( t ) \notin N _ { r } , \dot { V } ( x ( t ) ) \leq - M _ { r } \Delta < 0$ for all $t \geq 0$ . Through the integration from $V ( x ( 0 ) )$ ,

$$V (x (t)) = V (x (0)) + \int_ {0} ^ {t} \dot {V} (x (\tau)) d \tau \tag {36}\leq V (x (0)) - M _ {r} \Delta t.$$

This implies that there exists large T such that $V ( x ( T ) ) <$ $\alpha \epsilon _ { \mathrm { p d } } r ^ { 2 }$ which is a contradiction to (35). Thus, $x ( t )$ arrives at $N _ { r }$ . □
