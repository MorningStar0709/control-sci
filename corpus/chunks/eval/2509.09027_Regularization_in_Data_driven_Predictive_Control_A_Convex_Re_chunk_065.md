# B.4 Proof of Corollaries 1 and 2

We prove Corollaries 1 and 2 via showing both (22) and (28) can be represented in the form of (17). Thus, Corollaries 1 and 2 are actually special cases of Theorem 1.

For changing (22) to the same form as (17), we can let

$$x _ {1} := \operatorname{col} (\sigma_ {y}, u, y), \quad x _ {2} := g, \quad \mathcal {X} := \Gamma \times \mathcal {U} \times \mathcal {Y},A _ {1} := \operatorname{col} (\mathbb {0}, I), \quad A _ {2} := - H _ {\mathrm{d}}, \quad b := - \operatorname{col} (u _ {\text {ini}}, y _ {\text {ini}}, \mathbb {0}),M := \operatorname{diag} (\lambda_ {y} I, R, Q), \quad D := (I - \Pi_ {1}).$$

Similarly, we transform (28) to the form of (17) by letting

$$x _ {1} := \operatorname{col} (\sigma_ {y}, u, y), \quad x _ {2} := g, \quad \mathcal {X} := \Gamma \times \mathcal {U} \times \mathcal {Y},A _ {1} := \operatorname{col} (\mathbb {0}, I), \quad A _ {2} := - \hat {H}, \quad b := - \operatorname{col} (u _ {\text {ini}}, y _ {\text {ini}}, \mathbb {0}),M := \operatorname{diag} (\lambda_ {y} I, R, Q), \quad D := Q _ {\mathrm{c}}.$$

We note that the only differences between transformations of (22) and (28) are the choices of D and A2.

$\operatorname { A s } \Gamma , \mathcal { U } , \mathcal { V }$ are convex sets, X defined above for (22) and (28) are convex. Thus, all conditions in Theorem 1 are satisfied and that completes the proof.
