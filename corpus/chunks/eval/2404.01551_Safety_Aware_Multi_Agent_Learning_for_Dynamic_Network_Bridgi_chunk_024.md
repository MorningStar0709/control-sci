# B. Impact of the safety-informed edges

Figure 4a shows the mean return (cumulative reward) curves for Approach A and Approach A-Node, over the course of training. Approach A (with safety edge features) converges to higher returns compared to Approach A-Node, indicating that safety-informed edges facilitate more effective coordination strategies. Additionally, Figure 4b presents the average frequency of safety filter activations across training episodes. The results show that Approach A triggers fewer safety interventions than Approach A-Node, particularly in the later stages of training.

These findings suggest that edge-level safety features, rather than node-level, help achieve better performance while incurring in less safety activations.
