# Example A.43: Independent and uncorrelated are equivalent for normals

If two random variables are jointly normally distributed,

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \xi \\ \eta \end{array} \right] \sim N \left(\left[ \begin{array}{c} m _ {x} \\ m _ {y} \end{array} \right], \left[ \begin{array}{c c} P _ {x} & P _ {x y} \\ P _ {y x} & P _ {y} \end{array} \right]\right)
$$

Prove $\xi$ and $\eta$ are statistically independent if and only if $\xi$ and η are uncorrelated, or, equivalently, P is block diagonal.
