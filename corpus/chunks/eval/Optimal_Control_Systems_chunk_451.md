# Addition/Subtraction of Matrices

The addition (or subtraction) of two matrices A and B is simply the addition of the corresponding elements in a particular row and column, and hence, obviously these two matrices should be of the same size or order. Thus we get a new matrix C as

$$\mathbf {C} = \mathbf {A} + \mathbf {B} \tag {A.2.3}$$

where, $c_{ij} = a_{ij} + b_{ij}$ . The addition of two matrices is commutative as

$$\mathbf {A} + \mathbf {B} = \mathbf {B} + \mathbf {A}. \tag {A.2.4}$$
