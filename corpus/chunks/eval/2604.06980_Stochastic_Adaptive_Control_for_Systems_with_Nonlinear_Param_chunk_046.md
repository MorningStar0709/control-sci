$$
\begin{array}{l} \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \left\| \frac {\phi_ {\tau} \phi_ {\tau} ^ {\top}}{\tau^ {\eta}} I (1 + \| \phi_ {\tau} \| ^ {2} > \tau^ {\eta}) \right\| \\ \leq \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \left\| \frac {\phi_ {\tau} \phi_ {\tau} ^ {\top}}{\tau^ {\eta}} \right\| \left(\frac {1 + \| \phi_ {\tau} \| ^ {2}}{\tau^ {\eta}}\right) ^ {\frac {\gamma - 2}{4}} \tag {C.13} \\ = O \left(\sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \frac {\| \phi_ {\tau} \| ^ {2 + \frac {\gamma - 2}{2}}}{\tau^ {\eta (\frac {1}{2} + \frac {\gamma}{4})}}\right), \text { a.s. } \\ \end{array}
$$

By the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality and (B.13), we get

$$
\begin{array}{l} \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \frac {\left\| \phi_ {\tau} \right\| ^ {2 + \frac {\gamma - 2}{2}}}{\tau^ {\eta (\frac {1}{2} + \frac {\gamma}{4})}} = \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \frac {\left\| \phi_ {\tau} \right\| ^ {1 + \frac {\gamma}{2}}}{\tau^ {\eta (\frac {1}{2} + \frac {\gamma}{4})}} \\ \leq \left[ \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \| \phi_ {\tau} \| ^ {\gamma} \right] ^ {\frac {\gamma + 2}{2 \gamma}} \left[ \sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \frac {1}{\tau^ {\eta \gamma (\gamma + 2) / 2 (\gamma - 2)}} \right] ^ {\frac {\gamma - 2}{2 \gamma}} \tag {C.14} \\ \leq t ^ {\frac {\gamma + 2}{2 \gamma}} t ^ {\frac {\gamma - 2}{2 \gamma} - \eta (2 + \gamma) / 4} = t ^ {1 - \eta (2 + \gamma) / 4}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Since $\begin{array} { r } { \eta > \frac { 8 b } { \gamma - 2 } } \end{array}$ , we have $1 - \eta ( 2 + \gamma ) / 4 < 1 - 2 b - \eta$ Combining this with (C.13) and (C.14) gives

$$\sum_ {\tau = 0} ^ {t} \frac {\phi_ {\tau} \phi_ {\tau} ^ {\top}}{\tau^ {\eta}} I (1 + \| \phi_ {\tau} \| ^ {2} > \tau^ {\eta}) = o (t ^ {1 - 2 b - \eta}), \text { a.s. } \tag {C.15}$$

From (C.12), (C.15), and (C.9), we get $t ^ { 1 - 2 b - \eta } = O ( \lambda _ { t } )$ $\mathrm { a . s . , }$ where $\lambda _ { t }$ is given in (20). With this, Theorem 3 follows from property (iii) of Theorem 1. ✷
