D is positive exactly when the equation has distinct real roots, and is zero exactly when it has equal real roots. More precisely, the discriminant of a polynomial p of degree n over a given field is the quantity.

$$D (p) = (- 1) ^ {n (n - 1) / 2} R (p, p ^ {\prime}),$$

where R is the resolvent of p and $p ^ { \prime }$ .
