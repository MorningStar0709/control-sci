Even though the travel discomfort somewhat increases with larger c for P-CETC and P-PETC compared with their regular counterparts, they still achieve a considerable discomfort reduction compared with the open-loop control. Therefore, when selecting the parameter $c$ for P-ETC, a balance should be sought between system performance metrics. The increasing c has no adverse effect on the traffic metrics when contrasting R-STC with P-STC. In the cases of P-CETC and P-PETC, they may accomplish fewer $N _ { t }$ and better safety at the expense of somewhat increased but still satisfactory travel discomfort.

In conclusion, the proposed P-ETCs reduce travel discomfort by 45%-92% relative to driver’s natural behavior (openloop) and increase driver safety, measured by the average dwell time, by as much as 2.9× relative to their regular counterparts with the frequent-switching VSL schedule.
