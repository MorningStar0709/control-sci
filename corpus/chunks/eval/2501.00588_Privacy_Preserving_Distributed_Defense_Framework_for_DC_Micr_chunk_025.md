# C. Robustness to Load Changes

The second case study demonstrates the robustness of the proposed control strategy in response to variations in load conditions. The attacks are characterized by: to $\delta _ { i } \ =$ [3 exp (0.1t) , 4 exp (0.2t) , 2 exp (0.2t) , exp (0.1t)]T, ∀i = 1, 2, 3, 4, and have been initiated at $t = 5 . 2 s$ . The experiment involves altering the load resistance for converter 1 and 4 by introducing additional resistance equal to 90Ω in parallel, at $t = 9 s$ , followed by a halving of the resistance at t = 14s. Fig. 6 illustrates the trajectories of voltage and current. Notably, as depicted in this figure, the objectives of proportional load sharing and voltage regulation are successfully achieved, underscoring the resilience of the control strategy in accommodating uncertainties in load parameters.
