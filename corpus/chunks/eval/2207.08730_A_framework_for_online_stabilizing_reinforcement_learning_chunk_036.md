$$\alpha_ {\text { low }} (\| X _ {k \delta} \|) \leq \hat {J} ^ {w ^ {\#}} (X _ {k \delta}) \leq \alpha_ {\text { up }} (\| X _ {k \delta} \|) \implies\hat {\alpha} _ {\text {low}} \left(\| X _ {k \delta} \|\right) \leq \hat {J} ^ {w ^ {\#}} \left(X _ {k \delta}\right) \leq \hat {\alpha} _ {\mathrm{up}} \left(\| X _ {k \delta} \|\right). \tag {37}$$

The latter indicates that (C4) is satisfied.

Observe that

$$
\begin{array}{l} J ^ {w ^ {k - 1}} (X _ {k \delta}) \geq J ^ {w ^ {k - 1}} (\Lambda_ {U _ {(k - 1) \delta}} (X _ {(k - 1) \delta})) - \\ \operatorname{Lip} _ {J} \left\| X _ {k \delta} - \Lambda_ {U _ {(k - 1) \delta}} (X _ {(k - 1) \delta}) \right\| \geq \\ L (\Lambda_ {U _ {(k - 1) \delta}} (X _ {(k - 1) \delta})) - \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {J}} \mathrm{Lip} _ {f} (\bar {f} + \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z}) \delta^ {2} - \\ \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {j}} \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z} \delta , \tag {38} \\ \end{array}
$$

while at the same time

$$
\begin{array}{l} \hat {J} ^ {w ^ {\#}} (X _ {k \delta}) - L (\Lambda_ {U _ {(k - 1) \delta}} (X _ {(k - 1) \delta})) + \\ \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {J}} \mathrm{Lip} _ {f} (\bar {f} + \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z}) \delta^ {2} + \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {J}} \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z} \delta \leq \\ 2 \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {J}} \mathrm{Lip} _ {f} (\bar {f} + \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z}) \delta^ {2} + 2 \mathrm{Lip} _ {\hat {J}} \sigma_ {\max} \mathrm{Lip} _ {Z} \delta \leq \\ \frac {\bar {\nu}}{2} \delta + \frac {\bar {\nu}}{6} \delta = \varepsilon \delta . \tag {39} \\ \end{array}
$$

Substituting (39) into (38) yields

$$J ^ {w ^ {\#}} (X _ {k \delta}) - J ^ {w ^ {k - 1}} (X _ {k \delta}) \leq \varepsilon \delta , \tag {40}$$

which is equivalent to (C1).

Hence, feasibility of CALF is shown.
