$$\mathcal {A} _ {L + E} - \mathcal {A} _ {L} = \mathcal {M} _ {L} [ E ] + o (\| E \|).$$

This implies that

$$\mathrm{d} (\mathcal {A} _ {L} ^ {\intercal} H ^ {\intercal} H \mathcal {A} _ {L}) \big | _ {L} [ E ] = \mathcal {M} _ {L} [ E ] ^ {\intercal} H ^ {\intercal} H \mathcal {A} _ {L} + \mathcal {A} _ {L} ^ {\intercal} H ^ {\intercal} H \mathcal {M} _ {L} [ E ]$$

On the other hand,

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {X} _ {L + E} - \mathcal {X} _ {L} = (I \otimes E) \vec {\omega} \vec {\omega} ^ {\intercal} (I \otimes L ^ {\intercal}) \\ + (I \otimes L) \vec {\omega} \vec {\omega} ^ {\intercal} (I \otimes E ^ {\intercal}) \\ - \vec {\xi} \vec {\omega} ^ {\intercal} (I \otimes E ^ {\intercal}) + (I \otimes E) \vec {\omega} \vec {\xi} ^ {\intercal} + o (\| E \|). \\ \end{array}
$$

Therefore, the second claim follows by the chain rule.
