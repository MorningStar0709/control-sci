# 7.4. Policy Gradient for Extended States

The basic policy gradient algorithm is Sutton and Barto (2018)

$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {\pmb {\theta}} \mathbb {E} _ {\pmb {\tau} \sim p _ {\pmb {\pi} _ {\pmb {\theta}}}} [ c (\pmb {\tau}) ] = \nabla_ {\pmb {\theta}} \int \pi_ {\pmb {\theta}} (\pmb {\tau}) c (\pmb {\tau}) d \tau \\ = \int \nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) c (\boldsymbol {\tau}) d \tau \tag {26} \\ = \int \pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) \nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \log \pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) c (\boldsymbol {\tau}) d \tau \\ = \mathbb {E} _ {\pmb {\tau} \sim p _ {\pmb {\pi} _ {\pmb {\theta}}}} [ c (\pmb {\tau}) \nabla_ {\pmb {\theta}} \log \pi_ {\pmb {\theta}} (\pmb {\tau}) ]. \\ \end{array}
$$

The probability of a trajectory with length T is

$$\pi_ {\theta} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) = p \left(\mathcal {X} (T _ {0})\right) \prod_ {t = T _ {0}} ^ {T - 1} p _ {\pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} (\boldsymbol {u} (t) | \mathcal {X} (t)) p \left(\mathcal {X} (t + 1) | \mathcal {X} (t), \boldsymbol {u} (t)\right). \tag {27}$$

Expanding the terms in (27) and canceling the transition probability independent of θ, we have

$$\nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \log \pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) = \sum_ {t = 0} ^ {T - 1} \nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \log p _ {\boldsymbol {\pi} _ {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} \left(\boldsymbol {u} (k) \mid \boldsymbol {y} (k)\right), \tag {28}$$

and therefore,

$$\nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \mathbb {E} _ {\pi_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} [ c (\boldsymbol {\tau}) ] = \mathbb {E} _ {\boldsymbol {\tau} \sim p _ {\boldsymbol {\pi} _ {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} (\boldsymbol {\tau})} \left[ c (\boldsymbol {\tau}) \sum_ {k = 0} ^ {K - 1} \nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\theta}} \log p _ {\boldsymbol {\pi} _ {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} (\boldsymbol {u} (k) \mid \boldsymbol {y} (k)) \right] \tag {29}$$

Hence, the policy gradient algorithm still holds for the output-feedback case.
