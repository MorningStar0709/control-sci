# 11.1 INTRODUCTION

This final chapter encapsulates the fundamental topics associated with the modeling, simulation, and control of dynamic systems by presenting case studies in engineering. Most of the case studies presented here are inspired by research that has been presented at professional conferences or published in the archival journals. All case studies involve physical engineering systems and many of the complexities associated with real-world systems. Each case study begins with the development of the mathematical modeling equations, followed by analysis of the system response using numerical simulations and/or analytical methods. In most cases, each study concludes with a design section, where the performance impact of varying system parameters is investigated.
