# Solution

The region $\mathbb { X } ( c )$ generated by the inequality $y = \operatorname* { m a x } ( x _ { 1 } , x _ { 2 } ) \leq c$ is sketched in Figure A.12. Applying (A.31) then gives

$$
\begin{array}{l} P _ {\eta} (y) = \int_ {- \infty} ^ {y} \int_ {- \infty} ^ {y} p _ {\xi} (x _ {1}, x _ {2}) d x _ {1} d x _ {2} \\ = P _ {\xi} (y, y) \\ = P _ {\xi_ {1}} (y) P _ {\xi_ {2}} (y) \\ \end{array}
$$

which has a clear physical interpretation. It says the probability that the maximum of two independent random variables is less than some value is equal to the probability that both random variables are less than that value. To obtain the density, we differentiate

$$
\begin{array}{l} p _ {\eta} (y) = p _ {\xi_ {1}} (y) P _ {\xi_ {2}} (y) + P _ {\xi_ {1}} (y) p _ {\xi_ {2}} (y) \\ = p _ {\xi_ {1}} (y) \int_ {- \infty} ^ {y} p _ {\xi_ {2}} (x) d x + p _ {\xi_ {2}} (y) \int_ {- \infty} ^ {y} p _ {\xi_ {1}} (x) d x \\ \end{array}
$$
