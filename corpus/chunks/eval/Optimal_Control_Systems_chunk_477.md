# Derivative of Quadratic Forms

Some useful results in obtaining the derivatives of quadratic forms and related expressions are given below.

$$\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x}} (\mathbf {A x}) = \mathbf {A}\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {y}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {y}\right) = \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {y}} \left(\mathbf {y} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {x}\right) = \mathbf {x}\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {y}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {A} \mathbf {y}\right) = \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {y}} \left(\mathbf {y} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {A} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {x}\right) = \mathbf {A} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {x}\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {A x}\right) = \mathbf {A x} + \mathbf {A} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {x}\frac {\partial^ {2}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {2}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {A x}\right) = \mathbf {A} + \mathbf {A} ^ {\prime}. \tag {A.3.4}$$

If there is a symmetric matrix $\mathbf{P}$ , then

$$\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {P x}\right) = 2 \mathbf {P x}\frac {\partial^ {2}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {2}} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} \mathbf {P} \mathbf {x}\right) = 2 \mathbf {P}. \tag {A.3.5}$$
