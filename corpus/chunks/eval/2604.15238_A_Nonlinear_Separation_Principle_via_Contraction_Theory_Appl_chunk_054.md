# EXTENSION TO DIAGONALLY SYMMETRIZABLE GRAPHS

Theorem 41 (Contraction of Graph FRNN): Consider the dynamics (48) with a MONE nonlinearity Ψ. If

(A1) The adjacency matrix A can be decomposed as $A =$ $H D ^ { - 1 }$ , where $D \succ 0$ is diagonal and $H = H ^ { \top }$ .   
(A2) The eigenvalues of A lie in [0, 1].   
(A3) W satisfies matrix inequality (25) for $P \succ 0$ , diagonal $Q \succ 0 ,$ , rate $c > 0 ,$ , which satisfy $P Q ^ { - 1 } P \prec 4 ( 1 - c ) P$ .

Then, the dynamics (48) are strongly infinitesimally contracting with rate c in the norm $\| \cdot \| _ { D \otimes P }$ .

Proof: We show that the weight matrix $A ^ { \top } \otimes W$ satisfies (25) for weights $D \otimes P$ and $D \otimes Q$ . Substituting these weights into (25), and using the mixed product property of Kronecker products Lemma 40 yields

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 (1 - c) D \otimes P & D \otimes P + H \otimes W ^ {\top} Q \\ D \otimes P + H ^ {\top} \otimes Q W & - 2 D \otimes Q \end{array} \right] \preceq 0 \tag {93}
$$

Next, we apply a congruence transformation. Left and right multiplying (93) by the non-singular block diagonal matrix $T _ { Q } = \bar { \mathrm { d i a g } ( D ^ { - 1 / 2 } \otimes I _ { n } , D ^ { - 1 / 2 } \otimes I _ { n } ) }$ yields,

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 (1 - c) I _ {n} \otimes P & * \\ I _ {n} \otimes P + \bar {H} ^ {\top} \otimes Q W & - 2 I _ {n} \otimes Q \end{array} \right] \preceq 0, \tag {94}
$$

where $\bar { H } = D ^ { - 1 / 2 } H D ^ { - 1 / 2 }$ . Since H is symmetric and D is diagonal, H¯ is also symmetric. Further, the eigenvalues of A are identical to that of H¯ , as $D ^ { 1 / 2 } A D ^ { - 1 / 2 } = \overline { { { \cal H } } }$ .

Performing an SVD for H¯ yields ${ \bar { H } } = U \Lambda U ^ { \top }$ , due to its symmetric nature. Further, each entry of Λ is in [0, 1]. We now apply the unitary transform, $T = \mathrm { d i a g } ( U \otimes I _ { n } , U \otimes I _ { n } )$ to (94). Utilizing the fact that $U ^ { \top } U = U U ^ { \top } = I _ { m } , ( 9 4 )$ is equivalent to

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 (1 - c) I _ {n} \otimes P & * \\ I _ {n} \otimes P + \Lambda \otimes Q W & - 2 I _ {n} \otimes Q \end{array} \right] \preceq 0. \tag {95}
$$

Due to the diagonal nature of $I _ { n }$ and Λ, this matrix may be permuted into a block diagonal form through the perfect shuffle permutation [32, Proposition 1]. Therefore, the condition (95) is decomposable into n conditions, and the ith condition has the form,

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c} - 2 (1 - c) P & P + \lambda_ {i} W ^ {\top} Q \\ P + \lambda_ {i} Q W & - 2 Q \end{array} \right] \preceq 0, \tag {96}
$$
