In particular, $\gamma$ at the initial time is equal to the diagonal matrix $I _ { 2 }$ , but at later times contains entries on the off-diagonal. We thus have cross-impact. This means that selling in the first asset leads to lower prices not only in the first asset, but also in the second asset. The lower prices in the second asset are exploited by implementing a buy program in the second asset. Moreover, observe that buying in the second asset increases the prices also in the first asset, which is beneficial for the sell task in the first asset.
