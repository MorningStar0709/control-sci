Using these two signals permits corrections to be made for ionospheric delays in signal propagation time. Furthermore, these signals are modulated with two codes: (1) the C/A or clear/access (also known as coarse/acquisition) that provides for easy lock-on to the desired signal, and (2) the P or precise code, which provides for precision measurement of time. As indicated above, the $L _ { 1 }$ signal is modulated with both P and C/A pseudorandom noise (PRN) codes in phase quadrature, while the $L _ { 2 }$ signal is modulated only with the P -code. Moreover, the C/A code is a short code operating at 1.023 Mbps, while the P -code is a long-precision code operating at 10.23 Mbps. Navigation accuracy of the GPS on the order of meter level for military users and decameter level for civilian users has been achieved. Superimposed on the $L _ { 1 }$ and $L _ { 2 }$ signals are navigation and system data including satellite ephemeris, atmospheric propagation correction data, and satellite clock bias information.
