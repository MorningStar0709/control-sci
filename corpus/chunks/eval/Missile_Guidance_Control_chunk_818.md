# At Sea Level

To = temperature (288.1667) [kelvin]

Po = static pressure (101314.628) $[ N / m ^ { 2 } ]$

At altitude z, an approximation to the standard atmosphere is used. The atmosphere is divided into three zones as follows:

(1) $z \leq 1 1 , 0 0 0 m .$   
(2) 1, 000 m < z ≤ 25, 000 m,   
(3) $z > 2 5 , 0 0 0 m .$

Different formulas are used to find the ambient atmospheric temperature and pressure, $T _ { a }$ and $P _ { a }$ , in each of the zones.

$$T _ {a} = 2 1 6. 6 6 6 6 6 6 6 7 + (3. 0 0 0 1 4 5 8 1 6 \times 1 0 ^ {- 3}) (z - 2 5 0 0 0) [ K ], \tag {D.5}P _ {a} = (2 4 8 9. 7 7 3 4 6 7) \left\{\exp \left[ - 1. 5 7 6 8 8 3 2 0 2 \times 1 0 ^ {- 4} (z - 2 5 0 0 0) \right] \right\} [ N / m ^ {2} ]. \tag {D.6}$$

In all three zones, the ambient atmospheric density and the speed of sound are given by

$$\rho_ {a} = P _ {a} / R T _ {a} \left[ k g / m ^ {3} \right], \tag {D.7}V _ {a} = 2 0. 0 3 7 6 7 3 \sqrt {T _ {a}} [ m / s e c ], \tag {D.8a}$$

where R is the gas constant $( 2 8 6 . 9 9 2 3 6 [ ( \mathrm { N - m } ) / ( \mathrm { K } _ { p } - \mathrm { K } ) ]$ . Note that the speed of sound $V _ { a }$ , can also be calculated from the relation

$$V _ {a} = k R T, \tag {D.8b}$$

where

k = the ratio of specific heat of the gas (= 1.4 for air),

$$R = \text { gas constant } (= 2 8 6. 9 9 2 3 6 [ (\mathrm{N} - \mathrm{m}) / (\mathrm{Kp} - ^ {\circ} \mathrm{K}) ]),$$
