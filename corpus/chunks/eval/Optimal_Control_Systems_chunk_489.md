# Detectability

A system is detectable if its unobservable states, if any, are stable. Its observable states may be stable or unstable. Thus, the pair $(\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{C})$ is detectable if there is a matrix L such that $(\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{L}\mathbf{C})$ can be made asymptotically stable. This is equivalent to the observability of the unstable modes of A.
