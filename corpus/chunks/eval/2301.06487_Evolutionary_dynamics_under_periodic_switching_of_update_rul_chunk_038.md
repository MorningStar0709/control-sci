# A.2 IM Updating

For IM updating [21], [27], at each time step a player of the population is randomly selected as the focal player and update its strategy who always has l cooperators and k − l defectors among its k neighbors. If the focal player adopts strategy D, then the fitness of the focal player is

$$\bar {g} _ {F} ^ {D} = g _ {F} ^ {D}, \tag {A23}$$

the fitness of a C-neighbor is

$$\bar {g} _ {C} ^ {D} = g _ {C} ^ {D}, \tag {A24}$$

and the fitness of a D-neighbor is

$$\bar {g} _ {D} ^ {D} = 1 - \omega + \omega [ (k - 1) x _ {C | D} b ]. \tag {A25}$$

Since the focal player can either stay with its own strategy or imitate one of its neighbors’ strategies with probability proportional to their fitness, the probability that the focal player adopts the strategy C is given by

$$\Theta = \frac {l \bar {g} _ {C} ^ {D}}{l \bar {g} _ {C} ^ {D} + (k - l) \bar {g} _ {D} ^ {D} + \bar {g} _ {F} ^ {D}}. \tag {A26}$$

Therefore, $x _ { C }$ increases by $\textstyle { \frac { 1 } { n } }$ with probability

$$P \left(\Delta x _ {C} = \frac {1}{n}\right) = x _ {D} \sum_ {l = 0} ^ {k} \binom {k} {l} \left(x _ {C | D}\right) ^ {l} \left(x _ {D | D}\right) ^ {k - l} \Theta . \tag {A27}$$

Consequently, the number of CC-pairs increases by l and hence $x _ { C C }$ increases by $\frac { 2 l } { k n }$ with probability

$$P \left(\Delta x _ {C C} = \frac {2 l}{k n}\right) = x _ {D} \binom {k} {l} (x _ {C | D}) ^ {l} (x _ {D | D}) ^ {k - l} \Theta . \tag {A28}$$

In the alternative case, the randomly selected focal player adopts strategy C. The fitness of the focal player is

$$\bar {g} _ {F} ^ {C} = g _ {F} ^ {C}, \tag {A29}$$

the fitness of a D-neighbor is

$$\bar {g} _ {D} ^ {C} = g _ {D} ^ {C}, \tag {A30}$$

and the fitness of a C-neighbor is

$$\bar {g} _ {C} ^ {C} = 1 - \omega + \omega \Bigl \{\bigl [ (k - 1) x _ {C | C} + 1 \bigr ] (b - c) - (k - 1) x _ {D | C} c \Bigr \}. \tag {A31}$$

The probability that the focal player adopts the strategy $D$ is

$$\Upsilon = \frac {(k - l) \bar {g} _ {D} ^ {C}}{l \bar {g} _ {C} ^ {C} + (k - l) \bar {g} _ {D} ^ {C} + \bar {g} _ {F} ^ {C}}. \tag {A32}$$

Thus, $x _ { C }$ decreases by $\textstyle { \frac { 1 } { n } }$ with probability

$$P \left(\Delta x _ {C} = - \frac {1}{n}\right) = x _ {C} \sum_ {l = 0} ^ {k} \binom {k} {l} \left(x _ {C | C}\right) ^ {l} \left(x _ {D | C}\right) ^ {k - l} \Upsilon . \tag {A33}$$

Therefore, the number of CC-pairs decreases by l and hence $x _ { C C }$ decreases by $\frac { 2 l } { k n }$ with probability

$$P \left(\Delta x _ {C C} = - \frac {2 l}{k n}\right) = x _ {C} \binom {k} {l} (x _ {C | C}) ^ {l} (x _ {D | C}) ^ {k - l} \Upsilon . \tag {A34}$$
