Since the above inequality holds for any realization of the random variables $b _ { 0 } , \dots , b _ { \lfloor T / \tau \rfloor }$ , taking the expectation $\mathbb { E } _ { b } [ \cdot ]$ with respect to $b _ { 0 } , \dots , b _ { \lfloor T / \tau \rfloor }$ on both sides of the above inequality completes the proof of part (iii).

Proof of part $( i v )$ . We show that $\{ - \langle \tilde { \varepsilon } _ { t } ^ { s } , M _ { t } - M ^ { \star } \rangle \} _ { t \geq \tau }$ form a martingale difference sequence. For any $t \in \{ \tau , . . . , T - 1 \}$ , we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} _ {\tilde {v}} \Big [ \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ - \langle \tilde {\varepsilon} _ {t} ^ {s}, M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \rangle \Big ] \Big ] = \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ - \mathbb {E} _ {\tilde {v}} \langle \nabla \tilde {f} _ {t} (M _ {t}) - \nabla f _ {t} (M _ {t}), M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \rangle \Big ] \\ = \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ \mathbb {E} _ {\tilde {v}} \Big [ \langle 2 Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \tilde {v} _ {t}, M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \rangle \Big ] \Big ] \\ \stackrel {(a)} {=} \mathbb {E} _ {b} \left[ \langle 2 Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \mathbb {E} _ {\tilde {v}} [ \tilde {v} _ {t} ], \mathbb {E} _ {\tilde {v}} [ M _ {t} ] - M ^ {\star} \rangle \right] \stackrel {(b)} {=} 0, \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\mathbb { E } _ { \widetilde { v } } [ \cdot ]$ denotes the expectation with respect to $\tilde { v } _ { 0 } , \ldots , \tilde { v } _ { T - 1 }$ . To obtain (a), we use the fact that $\{ \tilde { v } _ { t } \} _ { t \geq 0 }$ is independent of $M _ { t }$ and $\{ y _ { t } \} _ { t \ge 0 }$ (and thus $Y _ { t - 1 : t - h } )$ ; and (b) follows from the fact that $\tilde { v } _ { t } \stackrel { i . i . d . } { \sim } \mathcal { N } ( 0 , \tilde { V } )$ . In addition, we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ \big | - \langle \tilde {\varepsilon} _ {t} ^ {s}, M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \rangle \big | \Big ] \leq \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ \| \tilde {\varepsilon} _ {t} ^ {s} \| \| M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \| \Big ] \\ \leq \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ 2 \left\| Y _ {t - 1: t - h} \right\| \left\| \tilde {v} _ {t} \right\| \left\| M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \right\| \Big ] \\ \leq 4 \sqrt {h} R _ {y} R _ {\tilde {v}} R _ {M}, \\ \end{array}
$$

where the last inequality again uses Lemmas 3.2-3.3 and the fact $M _ { t } , M ^ { \star } \in { \mathcal { K } } _ { M }$ . Hence, applying Lemma E.3, we conclude that further under the event $\mathcal { E } _ { \tilde { v } }$ , it holds with probability at least $1 - \delta / 1 2$

that

$$\sum_ {t = \tau} ^ {T - 1} \mathbb {E} _ {b} \Big [ - \langle \tilde {\varepsilon} _ {t} ^ {s}, M _ {t} - M ^ {\star} \rangle \Big ] \leq 4 \sqrt {h} R _ {y} R _ {\tilde {v}} R _ {M} \sqrt {T \log \frac {2 4}{\delta}}.$$
