$$\hat {u} _ {f} (t) = \frac {B _ {G}}{A _ {G}} d (t) + \frac {B _ {M}}{A _ {M}} \hat {y} _ {f} (t) \tag {15.69}$$

Since $A _ { G }$ and $A _ { M }$ are Hurwitz polynomials and that $d ( t )$ is bounded, it results that:

$$| \hat {u} _ {f} (t) | \leq C _ {5} + C _ {6} \max _ {0 \leq k \leq t + 1} | v ^ {0} (k) |; \quad 0 < C _ {5}, C _ {6} < \infty \tag {15.70}$$

It is interesting to remark that for Algorithm III taking into account (15.53), the stability condition is that:

$$\frac {A _ {M}}{\hat {A} _ {M}} \frac {\hat {P}}{P} \frac {G}{\hat {G}} - \frac {\lambda_ {2}}{2} \tag {15.71}$$

should be a strictly positive real transfer function. However, this condition can be rewritten for $\lambda _ { 2 } = 1$ as (Ljung and Söderström 1983):

$$\left| \left(\frac {A _ {M}}{\hat {A} _ {M}} \cdot \frac {\hat {P}}{P} \cdot \frac {G}{\hat {G}}\right) ^ {- 1} - 1 \right| < 1 \tag {15.72}$$

for all $\omega .$ . This roughly means that it always holds provided that the estimates of $A _ { M } , P$ , and $G$ are close to the true values (i.e., $H ( e ^ { - j \omega } )$ in this case is close to a unit transfer function).
