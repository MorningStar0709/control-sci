Proof. Represent x as a linear combination of $\mu$ and ν:

$$x = c _ {1} \mu + c _ {2} \nu , \tag {98}$$

where ν is a vector perpendicular to $\mu ,$ such that $\mu ^ { \mathrm { T } } \nu = 0 .$ Next, define two non-zero scalars, $c _ { 1 } , c _ { 2 }$ . Using (98), express Y in Lemma 5 in coordinates $( c _ { 1 } , c _ { 2 } ) { \mathrm { : } }$

$$Y = \frac {c _ {1} \mu + c _ {2} \nu}{(c _ {1} ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2}) ^ {\alpha}} - \frac {(1 + c _ {1}) \mu + c _ {2} \nu}{[ (1 + c _ {1}) ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} ] ^ {\alpha}}.$$

Thereafter, we obtain its partial derivatives with respect to these coordinates:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {1}} = \frac {\mu}{(c _ {1} ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2}) ^ {\alpha}} \\ - \frac {2 \alpha c _ {1} \| \mu \| ^ {2} \left(c _ {1} \mu + c _ {2} \nu\right)}{\left(c _ {1} ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2}\right) ^ {\alpha + 1}} \tag {99} \\ - \frac {\mu}{[ (1 + c _ {1}) ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} ] ^ {\alpha}} \\ + \frac {2 \alpha (1 + c _ {1}) \| \mu \| ^ {2} [ (1 + c _ {1}) \mu + c _ {2} \nu ]}{[ (1 + c _ {1}) ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} ] ^ {\alpha + 1}}, \\ \end{array}

\begin{array}{l} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {2}} = \frac {\nu}{(c _ {1} ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2}) ^ {\alpha}} \\ - \frac {2 \alpha c _ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} \left(c _ {1} \mu + c _ {2} \nu\right)}{\left(c _ {1} ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2}\right) ^ {\alpha + 1}} \tag {100} \\ - \overline {{[ (1 + c _ {1}) ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} ] ^ {\alpha}}} \\ + \frac {2 \alpha c _ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} [ (1 + c _ {1}) \mu + c _ {2} \nu ]}{[ (1 + c _ {1}) ^ {2} \| \mu \| ^ {2} + c _ {2} ^ {2} \| \nu \| ^ {2} ] ^ {\alpha + 1}}. \\ \end{array}
$$

Thereafter, we employ the fact that the local maxima of $Y ^ { \mathrm { { T } } } Y$ satisfy:

$$\frac {\partial}{\partial c _ {1}} (Y ^ {\mathrm{T}} Y) = \frac {\partial}{\partial c _ {2}} (Y ^ {\mathrm{T}} Y) = 0,$$

we obtain the following equivalent conditions for the maxima:

$$\nu^ {\mathrm{T}} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {1}} = \mu^ {\mathrm{T}} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {2}} = 0, \tag {101}\mu^ {\mathrm{T}} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {1}} = 0, \tag {102}\nu^ {\mathrm{T}} \frac {\partial Y}{\partial c _ {2}} = 0. \tag {103}$$
