Remark This theorem is useful when one cannot conclude that, for a given scheme, that (D.13) is satisfied. One can then choose eventually a sequence $r ( t )$ to satisfy the conditions of (D.35) and (D.34). The results of Theorem D.3 are weaker than those of Theorem D.2, but from the specific properties of the sequence $r ( t )$ is then possible in certain cases to conclude upon the convergence of certain schemes (Goodwin and Sin 1984; Goodwin et al. 1980c). Note that if in addition of (D.34) one assumes $\textstyle \operatorname* { l i m } _ { t \to \infty } r ( t ) = \infty$ , using Kronecker’s lemma one obtains from (D.36), (D.37), (D.38) and (D.40) results of the form of Theorem D.2.

Proof One considers the following near supermartingale:

$$
\begin{array}{l} T (t) = \frac {1}{r (t)} [ x ^ {T} (t) P x (t) + \bar {x} ^ {T} (t) \bar {P} (t) \bar {x} (t) ] \\ + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {t - 1} \frac {\left[ x ^ {T} (i) , u ^ {T} (i + 1) \right] M \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (i) \\ u (i + 1) \end{array} \right]}{r (i + 1)} + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {t - 1} \frac {x ^ {T} (i) M _ {0} x (i)}{r (i + 1)} \\ + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {t - 1} \frac {[ \bar {x} (i) , \bar {u} (i + 1) ] \bar {M} (t) \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bar {x} (i) \\ \bar {u} (i + 1) \end{array} \right]}{r (i + 1)} \\ + \sum_ {i = 0} ^ {t - 1} \frac {u ^ {T} (i + 1) K u (i + 1)}{r (i + 1)} \tag {D.40} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $P , \bar { P } ( t ) , M , M _ { 0 } , \bar { M } ( t )$ , K result from Lemmas C.5 and C.7 respectively. Denote:

$$V (t) = x ^ {T} (t) P x (t) + \bar {x} ^ {T} (t) \bar {P} (t) \bar {x} (t) \tag {D.41}$$

Using Lemmas C.5 and C.7 and taking also into account (D.6) and (D.8), one has:

$$
\begin{array}{l} V (t + 1) = V (t) - [ x ^ {T} (t), u ^ {T} (t + 1) ] M \left[ \begin{array}{c} x (t) \\ u (t + 1) \end{array} \right] - x ^ {T} (t) M _ {0} x (t) \\ - [ \bar {x} ^ {T} (t), \bar {u} ^ {T} (t + 1) ] \bar {M} (t) \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bar {x} (t) \\ \bar {u} (t + 1) \end{array} \right] - \bar {y} (t + 1) [ \varLambda - \varGamma (t) ] \bar {y} (t + 1) \\ - u ^ {T} (t + 1) K u (t + 1) + 2 \bar {y} ^ {T} (t + 1) \omega (t + 1) \tag {D.42} \\ \end{array}
$$
