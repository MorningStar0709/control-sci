If the off-diagonal elements in $\Xi$ were 0, then the optimal strategy would be composed of the optimal strategies in the respective single-asset problems. Observe that in the singleasset problem with initial position 0, initial deviation 0, price impact 1, resilience 3, and risk coefficient 1, the optimal strategy is to stay in the position 0 (cf., for example, Lemma 4.9). Therefore, with 0.5 replaced by 0 in (C.27), the optimal strategy would entail no trading in the second asset. However, with Ξ chosen as in (C.27), we see from Figure 4 that it is optimal to take on a negative position in the second asset with an initial sell block trade and to buy back the missing amount of shares over (0, T ].
