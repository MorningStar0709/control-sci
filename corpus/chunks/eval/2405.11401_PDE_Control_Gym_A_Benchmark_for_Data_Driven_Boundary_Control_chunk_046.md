# C.1. Second-Order Finite Difference Scheme

We consider 2D benchmark Navier Stokes PDE in the form:

$$\nabla \cdot \boldsymbol {u} = 0, \quad (x, y, t) \in ([ 0, 1 ] \times [ 0, 1 ] \times \mathbb {R} _ {+}), \tag {25a}\frac {\partial \boldsymbol {u}}{\partial t} + \boldsymbol {u} \cdot \nabla \boldsymbol {u} = - \frac {1}{\rho} \nabla p + \nu \nabla^ {2} \boldsymbol {u}, \quad (x, y, t) \in ((0, 1) \times (0, 1) \times \mathbb {R} _ {+}), \tag {25b}\boldsymbol {u} (x, 0, t) = U (x, t), \quad (x, t) \in ([ 0, 1 ] \times \mathbb {R} _ {+}), \tag {25c}\boldsymbol {u} (x, 1, t) = \boldsymbol {u} (1, y, t) = \boldsymbol {u} (0, y, t) = 0, \quad (x, y, t) \in ([ 0, 1 ] \times [ 0, 1 ] \times \mathbb {R} _ {+}), \tag {25d}$$

with boundary control input in the top boundary and the velocity at all other boundaries is set to be zero. We incorporate a predictor-corrector Le and Moin (1991) scheme for the imcompressive Navier Stokes equations. In the scheme, we denote $\pmb { u } = ( u , v )$ is the velocity vector, and spatial position is $\pmb { x } = ( x , y )$ .

We first present the predictor step that does not consider the pressure,

$$
\begin{array}{l} u _ {i, j} ^ {*} = u _ {i, j} ^ {n} + \Delta t (\nu (\frac {u _ {i - 1 , j} ^ {n} - 2 u _ {i , j} ^ {n} + u _ {i + 1 , j} ^ {n}}{(\Delta x) ^ {2}} + \frac {u _ {i , j - 1} ^ {n} - 2 u _ {i , j} ^ {n} + u _ {i , j + 1} ^ {n}}{(\Delta y) ^ {2}})) \\ - \Delta t (u _ {i, j} ^ {n} \frac {u _ {i + 1 , j} ^ {n} - u _ {i - 1 , j} ^ {n}}{2 \Delta x} + v _ {i, j} ^ {n} \frac {u _ {i , j + 1} ^ {n} - u _ {i , j - 1} ^ {n}}{2 \Delta y})), \tag {26} \\ \end{array}

\begin{array}{l} v _ {i, j} ^ {*} = v _ {i, j} ^ {n} + \Delta t (\nu (\frac {v _ {i - 1 , j} ^ {n} - 2 v _ {i , j} ^ {n} + v _ {i + 1 , j} ^ {n}}{(\Delta x) ^ {2}} + \frac {v _ {i , j - 1} ^ {n} - 2 v _ {i , j} ^ {n} + v _ {i , j + 1} ^ {n}}{(\Delta y) ^ {2}})) \\ - \Delta t (u _ {i, j} ^ {n} \frac {v _ {i + 1 , j} ^ {n} - v _ {i - 1 , j} ^ {n}}{2 \Delta x} + v _ {i, j} ^ {n} \frac {v _ {i , j + 1} ^ {n} - v _ {i , j - 1} ^ {n}}{2 \Delta y}), \tag {27} \\ \end{array}
$$

where $\Delta t$ denotes the temporal timestep, $\Delta x , \Delta y$ denotes the spatial time step, $n = 0 , \ldots N t$ , $i = 0 , \ldots , N x , j = 0 , \ldots , N y$ where $N t , N x , N y$ are the total number of timesteps, respectively. $u _ { i , j } ^ { * } , v _ { i , j } ^ { * }$ are the resulted velocity field that does consider pressure.
