# A.8 Norms in Rn

A norm in $\mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ is a function $| \cdot | : \mathbb { R } ^ { n } \to \mathbb { R } _ { \geq 0 }$ such that

(a) $| x | = 0$ if and only if $x = 0 ;$   
(b) $| \lambda x | = | \lambda | \ | x |$ , for all $\lambda \in \mathbb { R } , x \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ ;   
(c) $| x + y | \leq | x | + | y |$ , for all $x , y \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ .

Let ${ \mathcal { B } } : = \{ x \mid | x | \leq 1 \}$ denote the closed ball of radius 1 centered at the origin. For any $\boldsymbol { x } \in \mathbb { R } ^ { n }$ and $\rho > 0$ , we denote by x ⊕ $\rho \mathcal { B }$ or $B ( x , \rho )$ the closed ball $\{ z \mid | z - x | \leq \rho \}$ of radius $\rho$ centered at x. Similarly $\{ x \mid \left| x \right| < 1 \}$ denotes the open ball of radius 1 centered at the origin and $\{ z \mid | z - x | < \rho \}$ the open ball of radius $\rho$ centered at $x ;$ closed and open sets are defined below.
