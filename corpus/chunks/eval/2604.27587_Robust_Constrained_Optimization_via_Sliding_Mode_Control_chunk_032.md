# 5 Conclusion

This paper proposed a sliding-mode control framework for continuous-time constrained optimization that guarantees finite-time satisfaction of equality constraints and robustness to matched disturbances and measurement noise. By embedding discontinuous SMC laws into Lagrangian-based dynamics, the optimality conditions were reformulated as a nonsmooth control-affine system, and Lyapunov analysis established finite-time reachability of the constraint manifold, independent of convexity, outperforming PDGD and PI-CMO on nonconvex problems. Finite-time convergence to optimal solutions was further achieved via a normalized gradient flow with NTSMC, demonstrated through distributed parameter estimation. In robot obstacle avoidance, the method outperformed APF (failure in narrow passages) and PGF (only asymptotic constraint satisfaction), while Shidoku feasibility results showed finite-time reachability without full-rank assumptions and recovery of KKT equivalence when the rank condition holds.

Future work will extend the framework to a discrete-time setting, investigating whether key properties such as finite-time convergence and constraint enforcement are preserved under discretization, and developing suitable modifications when necessary. Additionally, co-authors are actively working on extending the framework to handle inequality constraints using augmented Lagrangian or penalty-based approaches. We will also explore distributed formulations to enable scalable implementations for large-scale systems.
