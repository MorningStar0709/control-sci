# 6 Numerical Examples

In this section, we present some numerical examples to validate the efficacy of the proposed observers.

Example 1 Consider the discrete-time descriptor system in the form of (1) with

$$
E = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right], \quad A = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 0. 1 5 3 & 0. 0 4 5 & 0. 0 6 9 \\ 0. 1 5 6 & 0. 2 5 2 & 0. 1 5 6 \\ 0. 1 3 5 & - 0. 1 7 1 & - 0. 6 3 6 \end{array} \right],

B = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0. 2 \end{array} \right], \quad C = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right].
$$

A data-driven observer is constructed according to Theorem 1. The state data length is chosen as $T = 2 0$ . The historical input sequence has length $T + s - 1 = 2 0$ and is generated from the uniform distribution on $( - 5 , 5 )$ . The initial slow-subsystem state is selected randomly, and the free parameter in Remark 5 is chosen as $K _ { 1 } = 0$ . The resulting observer matrix $\Sigma _ { X _ { p } }$ is

$$
\Sigma_ {X _ {p}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c} 0. 0 0 4 6 & - 0. 2 0 0 1 & - 0. 0 2 5 7 \\ 0. 0 3 9 7 & 0. 0 0 3 7 & 0. 1 4 2 2 \\ - 0. 0 0 4 6 & 0. 2 0 0 1 & 0. 0 2 5 7 \end{array} \right],
$$

whose eigenvalues have maximum modulus 0.2083. Hence, $\Sigma _ { X _ { p } }$ is Schur stable. For validation, another trajectory is generated with testing input $u ( k ) = 4 \sin ( k )$ . The initial slow-subsystem state and the initial observer state xˆ(0) are both selected randomly from $( 0 , 2 ) ^ { 2 }$ and $( 0 , 2 ) ^ { 3 }$ , respectively. Figure 1 shows that the estimated states closely track the true states.
