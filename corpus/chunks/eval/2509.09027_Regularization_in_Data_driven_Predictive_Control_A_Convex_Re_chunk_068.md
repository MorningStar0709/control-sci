# C Additional example: Two-wheeled robot

We here present the closed-loop control performance of DDPC variants and Equality-form SPC for a two-wheeled robot to illustrate the effectiveness of the framework in a more realistic application. The kinematic model of the robot is nonlinear [58]

$$z _ {x} (k + 1) = z _ {x} (k) + \Delta t \cdot \cos (z _ {\delta} (k)) \cdot v (k),z _ {y} (k + 1) = z _ {y} (k) + \Delta t \cdot \sin (z _ {\delta} (k)) \cdot v (k), \tag {C.1}z _ {\delta} (k + 1) = z _ {\delta} (k) + \Delta t \cdot w _ {t},$$

where $\left( z _ { x } , z _ { y } , z _ { \delta } \right)$ denotes the position and heading angle of the robot (i.e., state), v, w represent tangential and angular velocities $( i . e .$ , input) and $\Delta t = 0 . 0 2 5 \mathrm { s }$ is the sampling time. We aim to track the following heartshaped reference trajectory $( r _ { x } , r _ { y } , r _ { \delta } )$ ) while minimizing the control effort

$$
\begin{array}{l} r _ {x} (t) = 1 6 \sin^ {3} (t - 6), \\ r _ {y} (t) = 1 3 \cos (t) - 5 \cos (2 t - 1 2) \\ - 2 \cos (3 t - 1 8) - \cos (4 t - 2 4), \\ \end{array}
r _ {\delta} (t) = \arctan \left(\frac {r _ {y} (t + 1) - r _ {y} (t)}{r _ {x} (t + 1) - r _ {x} (t)}\right).$$

We choose the cost matrices $Q = \mathrm { d i a g } ( 2 , 2 , 8 )$ , and R = $6 . 2 5 \times 1 0 ^ { - 4 }$ , and set the weighting parameter $\lambda _ { y } = 3 { \times } 1 0 ^ { 6 }$ for all approaches. The remaining parameters are tuned separately for each approach.
