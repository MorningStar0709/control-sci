# Matrix

An $n \times m$ matrix $\mathbf{A}$ is an arrangement of $nm$ elements $a_{ij}$ ( $i = 1, 2, ..., n; j = 1, 2, ..., m$ ) into $n$ rows and $m$ columns as

$$
\mathbf {A} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l l} a _ {1 1} & a _ {1 2} & \dots & a _ {1 m} \\ a _ {2 1} & a _ {2 2} & \dots & a _ {2 m} \\ \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots \\ a _ {n 1} & a _ {n 2} & \dots & a _ {n m} \end{array} \right]. \tag {A.2.1}
$$

The $nxn$ of the matrix $\mathbf{A}$ is also referred to as order, size or dimension of the matrix.
