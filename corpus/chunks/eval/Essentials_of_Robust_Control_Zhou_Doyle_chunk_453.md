$$
\Phi = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} M _ {2} ^ {\sim} & N _ {2} ^ {\sim} \\ - \tilde {N} _ {2} & \tilde {M} _ {2} \end{array} \right].
$$

Then $\Phi ^ { \sim } \Phi = \Phi \Phi ^ { \sim } = I$ and

$$
\begin{array}{l} \vec {\delta} _ {g} (P _ {1}, P _ {2}) = \inf _ {Q \in \mathcal {H} _ {\infty}} \left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c c} M _ {2} ^ {\sim} & N _ {2} ^ {\sim} \\ - \tilde {N} _ {2} & \tilde {M} _ {2} \end{array} \right] \left\{\left[ \begin{array}{c} M _ {1} \\ N _ {1} \end{array} \right] - \left[ \begin{array}{c} M _ {2} \\ N _ {2} \end{array} \right] Q \right\} \right\| _ {\infty} \\ = \inf _ {Q \in \mathcal {H} _ {\infty}} \left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c} M _ {2} ^ {\sim} M _ {1} + N _ {2} ^ {\sim} N _ {1} - Q \\ - \tilde {N} _ {2} M _ {1} + \tilde {M} _ {2} N _ {1} \end{array} \right] \right\| _ {\infty} \geq \| \Psi (P _ {1}, P _ {2}) \| _ {\infty} \\ \end{array}
$$

where

$$
\Psi (P _ {1}, P _ {2}) := - \tilde {N} _ {2} M _ {1} + \tilde {M} _ {2} N _ {1} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \tilde {M} _ {2} & \tilde {N} _ {2} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & I \\ - I & 0 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} M _ {1} \\ N _ {1} \end{array} \right]. \tag {17.1}
$$

It will be seen in the next section that $\| \Psi ( P _ { 1 } , P _ { 2 } ) \| _ { \infty }$ is related to the ν-gap metric. The above lower bound may actually be achieved. Consider, for example,

$$P _ {1} = \frac {k _ {1}}{s + 1}, P _ {2} = \frac {k _ {2}}{s + 1}.$$

Then it is easy to verify that $P _ { i } = N _ { i } / M _ { i } , i = 1 , 2$ , with

$$N _ {i} = \frac {k _ {i}}{s + \sqrt {1 + k _ {i} ^ {2}}}, M _ {i} = \frac {s + 1}{s + \sqrt {1 + k _ {i} ^ {2}}},$$

are normalized coprime factorizations and it can be further show $^ { \mathrm { n , } }$ as in Georgiou and Smith [1990], that

$$
\delta_ {g} (P _ {1}, P _ {2}) = \| \Psi (P _ {1}, P _ {2}) \| _ {\infty} = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \frac {| k _ {1} - k _ {2} |}{| k _ {1} | + | k _ {2} |}, & \text {if} | k _ {1} k _ {2} | > 1; \\ \frac {| k _ {1} - k _ {2} |}{\sqrt {(1 + k _ {1} ^ {2}) (1 + k _ {2} ^ {2})}}, & \text {if} | k _ {1} k _ {2} | \leq 1. \end{array} \right.
$$

An immediate consequence of Theorem 17.1 is the connection between the uncertainties in the gap metric and the uncertainties characterized by the normalized coprime factors. The following corollary states that a ball of uncertainty in the directed gap is equivalent to a ball of uncertainty in the normalized coprime factors.
