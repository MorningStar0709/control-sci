# 4.4.1 LANDING CONTROL

A ship landing mission was designed and simulated using the parameters shown in Table 5.

Figure 16 shows the UAV performing the mission for which it was designed: landing on a moving ship. The UAV starts at a point in space and translates to align itself with the moving ship. Once its position error is within tolerance, it triggers the descent controller.

The performance of the descent controller can be seen in Figure 17. The UAV waits at its holding altitude of 0 ?? whilst it aligns itself in ?? and ??. The controller plans then executes a descent so that it meets the ship at the peak of a wave to minimise the relative velocity.

The final position error was 0.110 ?? and the relative velocity at touchdown was $0 . 9 6 3 m s ^ { - 1 }$ , compared to a target relative velocity of $1 m s ^ { - 1 }$ .
