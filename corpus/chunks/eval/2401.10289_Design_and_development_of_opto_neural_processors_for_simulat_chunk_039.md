To explore the potential of live neural networks in their computational capabilities and energy efficiency, a biological plausible multi-layer neural network training algorithm is proposed in this work. The neuron model, synapse model and methods of training have been kept as biologically accurate as possible in simulations. Optogenetic methods are included for precise spatio-temporal activation. Its obtained accuracy of 92% on the MNIST dataset is comparable to accuracy produced by ANNs. Possible applications of this algorithm can be extended to hybrid robots, live computational chips and can potentially be extended to neuroprosthetics and treatment of neurodegenerative diseases.
