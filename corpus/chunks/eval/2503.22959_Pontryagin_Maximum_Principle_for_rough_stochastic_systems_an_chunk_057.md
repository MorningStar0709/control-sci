$$\mathbb {E} \big [ g (X _ {T} ^ {\eta , u ^ {\eta}}) \big ] \big | _ {\eta = \mathbf {B} (\omega^ {\prime})} = \mathbb {E} ^ {\prime \prime} \big [ g (X _ {T} ^ {\eta , u ^ {\eta}}) | _ {\eta = \mathbf {B} (\omega^ {\prime})} \big ] = \mathbb {E} ^ {\prime \prime} \big [ g (\bar {X} _ {T} ^ {\bar {u}}) \big ].$$

As a result,

$$\mathbb {E} \left[ \xi \mathbb {E} [ g (X _ {T} ^ {\eta , u ^ {\eta}}) ] | _ {\eta = \mathbf {B} (\omega^ {\prime})} \right] = \mathbb {E} [ \xi g (\bar {X} _ {T} ^ {\bar {u}}) ]$$

and the equality of the cost function follows. Regarding the value function, the a.s. inequality

$$\bar {\mathcal {V}} (\omega) \geq \mathcal {V} (\boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} \left(\omega^ {\prime}\right)}$$

follows from the definition of admissible controls and (5.12). To establish the opposite inequality, we fix $\varepsilon > 0$ and recall from Lemma 4.8 that there exists an ε-optimal control $u ^ { \varepsilon } \in \mathcal A ^ { o }$ . Letting $\bar { u } ^ { \varepsilon }$ be the corresponding anticipative control given by (5.6) the desired inequality follows from

$$\bar {\mathcal {V}} (\omega) - \varepsilon \leq \bar {J} (\bar {u} ^ {\varepsilon}; \omega) - \varepsilon = J (u ^ {\varepsilon} (\cdot , \boldsymbol {\eta}); \boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} (\omega^ {\prime})} - \varepsilon \leq \mathcal {V} (\boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} (\omega^ {\prime})} \quad \mathbb {P} \text {-a.s.}$$

(2) Let $( \varepsilon , u ^ { \varepsilon } , \bar { u } ^ { \varepsilon } )$ be as in (1) and $\bar { u } ^ { * }$ be an optimal anticipative control. Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} J (u ^ {*, \eta}; \boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} (\omega)} = \bar {J} (\bar {u} ^ {*}; \omega) = \operatorname{essinf} _ {\bar {u} \in \bar {\mathcal {A}}} \bar {J} (\bar {u}; \omega) \\ \leq \bar {J} (\bar {u} ^ {\varepsilon}; \omega) = J (u ^ {\varepsilon} (\cdot , \boldsymbol {\eta}); \boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} (\omega)} \leq \mathcal {V} (\boldsymbol {\eta}) | _ {\boldsymbol {\eta} = \mathbf {B} \left(\omega^ {\prime}\right)} - \varepsilon \quad \mathbb {P} \text {-a.s.} \\ \end{array}
$$
