The proof for the normalized right coprime factorization is similar. All other inequalities follow from noting that

$$
\left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c} I \\ K _ {\infty} \end{array} \right] (I + P _ {s} K _ {\infty}) ^ {- 1} \tilde {M} _ {s} ^ {- 1} \right\| _ {\infty} \leq \gamma
$$

and

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c} I \\ K _ {\infty} \end{array} \right] (I + P _ {s} K _ {\infty}) ^ {- 1} \tilde {M} _ {s} ^ {- 1} \right\| _ {\infty} = \left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c} W _ {2} \\ W _ {1} ^ {- 1} K \end{array} \right] (I + P K) ^ {- 1} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} W _ {2} ^ {- 1} & P W _ {1} \end{array} \right] \right\| _ {\infty} \\ = \left\| \left[ \begin{array}{c} W _ {1} ^ {- 1} \\ W _ {2} P \end{array} \right] (I + K P) ^ {- 1} \left[ \begin{array}{c c} W _ {1} & P W _ {2} ^ {- 1} \end{array} \right] \right\| _ {\infty} \\ \end{array}
$$

This theorem shows that all closed-loop objectives are guaranteed to have bounded magnitude and the bounds depend only on $\gamma , W _ { 1 } , W _ { 2 }$ , and P .
