# A. Proof of Lemma 1

Since the observability indices of the pair (C, A) are uniform (see [20, As. 3]), the existence of matrices Π and H is a direct consequence of [20, Thm. 3]. Then, item 1 follows from [20, Thm. 4]. To prove item 2, we note from the proof of [20, Thm. 3] that

$$\Phi (A - \Pi L C) \Phi^ {\top} = A _ {0} - \Phi \Pi L C _ {0} = I _ {p} \otimes (\bar {A} + \lambda_ {\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{e} _ {n} ^ {\top}), \tag {66}$$

where we used the notation of Appendix I-B and let $\lambda _ { \mathrm { { m } } } : =$ $- [ \lambda _ { 0 } \ . . . \ \lambda _ { n - 1 } ] ^ { \top }$ . We conclude the proof by noticing that

$$
\begin{array}{l} A - \Pi L C = \Phi^ {\top} (I _ {p} \otimes (\bar {A} + \lambda_ {\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{e} _ {n} ^ {\top})) \Phi \\ = \bar {A} \otimes I _ {p} + \Phi^ {\top} (I _ {p} \otimes \lambda_ {\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{e} _ {n} ^ {\top}) \Phi \tag {67} \\ = \bar {A} \otimes I _ {p} + \lambda_ {\mathrm{m}} \mathrm{e} _ {n} ^ {\top} \otimes I _ {p} = \tilde {\Lambda}. \\ \end{array}
$$
