# C. High-Level Dynamics as LTV System

Note that the high-level dynamics (10) may be compactly written as a linear time-varying system

$$
\begin{array}{l} n _ {k + 1} ^ {r} = B _ {n} u _ {k} ^ {r} \\ c _ {k + 1} ^ {r} = c _ {k} ^ {r} + B _ {c} (\hat {\theta} ^ {r}) u _ {k} ^ {r} \\ \end{array}
$$

where

$$
\begin{array}{l} u _ {k} ^ {r} \in \mathbb {B} ^ {| V | ^ {2}}, | | u _ {k} ^ {r} | | _ {1} = 1 \\ B _ {n} = \mathbf {1} _ {1, | V |} \otimes [ 1, 2, \dots , | V | ] \\ B _ {c} (\hat {\theta} ^ {r}) \in \mathbb {R} ^ {L \times V ^ {2}}, B _ {c} (\hat {\theta} ^ {r}) _ {i, j} = \hat {\theta} _ {a, b, i} ^ {r} \\ a = [ \mathbf {1} _ {1, | V |}, \mathbf {2} _ {1, | V |}, \dots , \mathbf {V} _ {1, | V |} ] _ {j}, b = B _ {n, j} \\ \end{array}
$$
