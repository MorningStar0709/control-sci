# D. Proof of Theorem 3

The proof of the theorem technically follows that of Theorem 1. The difference is in invocations of the stabilizing policy.

Consider Algorithm 3. Let us denote, for brevity:

$$
\begin{array}{l} x ^ {\circ} := x _ {\text {prev}}, \\ \hat {\hat {x}} ^ {\circ} = \hat {\hat {x}} w _ {\text {prev}} (- 0) \end{array} \tag {41}
\hat {J} ^ {\circ} := \hat {J} ^ {w _ {\text { prev }}} (x ^ {\circ}).$$

Also, let us denote:

$$\hat {\mathbb {K}} := \left\{k \in \mathbb {N} _ {0}: \text {critic finds a solution} w ^ {*} \right\}, \tag {42}\mathbb {K} _ {L} := \{k \in \mathbb {N} _ {0}: \eta \text { is invoked } \},$$

the sets of time step indices where the critic succeeds to find a solution $w ^ { * }$ and, respectively, where it does not and so the stabilizing policy η is invoked.

Now, define:

$$
x _ {k} ^ {\circ} := \left\{ \begin{array}{l} x _ {k}, k \in \hat {\mathbb {K}}, \\ x _ {k - 1} ^ {\circ}, k \in \mathbb {K} _ {L}, \end{array} \right.
$$

and, by the same token, $\hat { J } _ { k } ^ { \circ }$

Notice that, although the user of Algorithm 3 might not be aware of a Lyapunov function associated with a known stabilizing policy, such a Lyapunov function always exists [49]. By Assumption 2, L is a Lyapunov function associated with η and ν is the corresponding sample-wise decay rate. Notice realizations of CALF as per Algorithms 2, 1 require knowledge of such an L, whereas Algorithm 3 only assumes its existence which is justified by converse theorems [49].

Let $L _ { k }$ denote $L ( X _ { k \delta } )$ . When running Algorithm 3, we expect the behavior of $L _ { k }$ and $\hat { J } _ { k } ^ { \circ }$ to look schematically like Figure 4 depicts.

It holds that

$$\forall k \in \mathbb {N} _ {0} \| x _ {k} ^ {\circ} \| \leq \hat {\alpha} _ {\text { low }} ^ {- 1} (\hat {J} _ {k} ^ {\circ}) \leq \hat {\alpha} _ {\text { low }} ^ {- 1} (\hat {J} _ {0} ^ {\circ}).$$

Observe that

$$\forall k \in \mathbb {N} _ {0}L (x _ {k} ^ {\circ}) \leq \alpha_ {\mathrm{up}} (\| x _ {k} ^ {\circ} \|) \leq \alpha_ {\mathrm{up}} (\hat {\alpha} _ {\mathrm{low}} ^ {- 1} (\hat {J} _ {k} ^ {\circ})) \leq \alpha_ {\mathrm{up}} (\hat {\alpha} _ {\mathrm{low}} ^ {- 1} (\hat {J} _ {0} ^ {\circ})).$$

Since for all $k \in \mathbb { K } _ { L } , L _ { k }$ is non-increasing, we conclude that
