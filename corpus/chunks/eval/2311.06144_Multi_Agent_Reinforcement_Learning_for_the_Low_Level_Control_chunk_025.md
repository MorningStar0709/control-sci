# VI. CONCLUSION

In this paper, we present multi-agent reinforcement learning frameworks for quadrotor low-level control by decomposing the quadrotor dynamics into translational and yawing parts, each exhibiting distinct characteristics. Additionally, we introduce regularization terms to mitigate steady-state errors and prevent aggressive control inputs, promoting efficient control. We demonstrate that our MARL frameworks outperform traditional single-agent counterparts through simto-sim verification, significantly enhancing training efficiency while maintaining flight-worthy control performance.

While simulation verification provides a valuable proving ground, our ongoing efforts aim to advance its applicability in real-world scenarios. Future work involves validating our frameworks through actual flight experiments, known as simto-real transfer, using domain randomization or adaptation techniques to bridge the reality gap in multi-agent settings.
