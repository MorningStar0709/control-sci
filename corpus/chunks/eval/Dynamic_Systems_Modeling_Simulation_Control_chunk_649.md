# Frequency Response

The objective of the vibration isolation system is to suppress the motion of the vehicle cabin floor $z _ { 0 } ( t )$ that is transmitted to the driver. The previous case showed that the seat-suspension system damps out an impulsive input in about 1.1 s, and the impulse response for driver displacement $z _ { 2 } ( t )$ exhibits two peaks during the transient response with a period of about 0.6 s. The impulse response for acceleration of the driver exhibited an initial spike when the impulse is applied, followed by a quickly damped response to zero.

Travel over a rough road will result in a repeating (periodic) input. For example, a vehicle moving with constant speed over equally spaced road bumps will experience a periodic road displacement with fixed frequency, which may be modeled as a sinusoidal input function. Hence, we can use frequency-response methods to analyze the performance of the seat-suspension system.
