We alter the system referenced in [10] such that it is no longer open-loop contractive and it has a control input:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} _ {1} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} - x _ {2} - \frac {3}{2} x _ {1} ^ {2} - \frac {1}{2} x _ {1} ^ {3} \\ 3 x _ {1} + x _ {2} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] u. \tag {28}
$$

Running the optimization program (19) with respect to (28) results in:

To demonstrate the need for a nonlinear contraction metric, let’s draw from the example in [12]:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {x} _ {1} \\ \dot {x} _ {2} \\ \dot {x} _ {3} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} - x _ {1} + x _ {3} \\ x _ {1} ^ {2} - x _ {2} - 2 x _ {1} x _ {3} + x _ {3} \\ - x _ {2} \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array} \right] u. \tag {30}
$$

First it should be noted that trying to find a constant matrix W and polynomial $\rho$ that makes the system closedloop contractive resulted in infeasible solutions, meaning W was rank deficient or the solver could not find a solution. As mentioned before, enforcing contraction in the closed loop with respect to the Riemannian metric requires the geodesic in (27), which amounts to finding:

$$\gamma^ {*} = \underset {\gamma \in \Gamma (\mathbf {0}, x (t))} {\arg \min} \int_ {0} ^ {1} \frac {\partial \gamma (s)}{\partial s} ^ {\top} M (\gamma (s)) \frac {\partial \gamma (s)}{\partial s} d s \tag {33}$$
