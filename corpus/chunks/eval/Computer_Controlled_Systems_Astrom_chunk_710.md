# Admissible Controls

It is assumed that the control law is such that $u(k)$ , that is, the value of the control signal at time k, is a function of $y(k)$ , $y(k-1)$ , ... and $u(k-1)$ , $u(k-2)$ , ... Thus the computational delay is negligible in comparison with the sampling period. It is very easy to modify the results to take delays in the computations into account.

There are two versions of the theory. A linear control law may be postulated. It is then sufficient to assume that the disturbances $e(i)$ and $e(j)$ are uncorrelated for $i \neq j$ . If $e(i)$ and $e(j)$ are assumed to be independent, it can be shown that the optimal-control law is linear. The formula for the optimal-control law is the same in both cases.
