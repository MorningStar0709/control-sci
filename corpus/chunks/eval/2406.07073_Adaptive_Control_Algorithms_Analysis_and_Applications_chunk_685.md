# 15.2 Basic Equations and Notations

The objective is to estimate (and to adapt) the parameters of the feedforward filter $N ( q ^ { - 1 } )$ such that the measured residual error (acceleration or force in AVC, noise in ANC) be minimized in the sense of a certain criterion. The description of the various blocks will be made with respect to Fig. 15.4.

The primary path is characterized by the asymptotically stable transfer operator:2

$$D (q ^ {- 1}) = \frac {B _ {D} (q ^ {- 1})}{A _ {D} (q ^ {- 1})} \tag {15.1}$$

where

$$B _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) = b _ {1} ^ {D} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + b _ {n _ {B _ {D}}} ^ {D} q ^ {- n _ {B _ {D}}} \tag {15.2}A _ {D} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + a _ {1} ^ {D} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + a _ {n _ {A _ {D}}} ^ {D} q ^ {- n _ {A _ {D}}} \tag {15.3}$$

The unmeasurable value of the output of the primary path (when the compensation is active) is denoted z(t). The secondary path is characterized by the asymptotically stable transfer operator:

$$G (q ^ {- 1}) = \frac {B _ {G} (q ^ {- 1})}{A _ {G} (q ^ {- 1})} \tag {15.4}$$

where:

$$B _ {G} (q ^ {- 1}) = b _ {1} ^ {G} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + b _ {n _ {B _ {G}}} ^ {G} q ^ {- n _ {B _ {G}}} = q ^ {- 1} B _ {G} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {15.5}A _ {G} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + a _ {1} ^ {G} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + a _ {n _ {A _ {G}}} ^ {G} q ^ {- n _ {A _ {G}}} \tag {15.6}$$

The positive feedback coupling is characterized by the asymptotically stable transfer operator:

$$M (q ^ {- 1}) = \frac {B _ {M} (q ^ {- 1})}{A _ {M} (q ^ {- 1})} \tag {15.7}$$

where:

$$B _ {M} (q ^ {- 1}) = b _ {1} ^ {M} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + b _ {n _ {B _ {M}}} ^ {M} q ^ {- n _ {B _ {M}}} = q ^ {- 1} B _ {M} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {15.8}A _ {M} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + a _ {1} ^ {M} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + a _ {n _ {A _ {M}}} ^ {M} q ^ {- n _ {A _ {M}}} \tag {15.9}$$

Both $B _ { G }$ and $B _ { M }$ have a one step discretization delay. The identified models of the secondary path and of the positive feedback coupling will be denoted $\hat { G }$ and $\hat { M }$ , respectively.

The optimal feedforward filter (unknown) is defined by:

$$N (q ^ {- 1}) = \frac {R (q ^ {- 1})}{S (q ^ {- 1})} \tag {15.10}$$

where:

$$R \left(q ^ {- 1}\right) = r _ {0} + r _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + r _ {n _ {R}} q ^ {- n _ {R}} \tag {15.11}S (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + S _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + S _ {n _ {S}} q ^ {- n _ {S}} = 1 + q ^ {- 1} S ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {15.12}$$

The estimated filter is denoted by $\hat { N } ( q ^ { - 1 } ) \mathrm { o r } \hat { N } ( \hat { \theta } , q ^ { - 1 } )$ when it is a linear filter with constant coefficients or $\hat { N } ( t , q ^ { - 1 } )$ during estimation (adaptation) of its parameters.
