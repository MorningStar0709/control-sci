$$
\begin{array}{l} M ^ {*} D M + j (G M - M ^ {*} G) - \beta^ {2} D \leq 0 \\ \Longleftrightarrow M ^ {*} D _ {1} ^ {2} M + j \left(\beta D _ {1} G _ {1} D _ {1} M - \beta M ^ {*} D _ {1} G _ {1} D _ {1}\right) - \beta^ {2} D _ {1} ^ {2} \leq 0 \\ \Longleftrightarrow (D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}) ^ {*} (D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}) + j (\beta G _ {1} D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1} - \beta (D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}) ^ {*} G _ {1}) - \beta^ {2} I \leq 0 \\ \Longleftrightarrow \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{\beta} - j G _ {1}\right) ^ {*} \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{\beta} - j G _ {1}\right) - (I + G _ {1} ^ {2}) \leq 0 \\ \Longleftrightarrow \overline {{\sigma}} \left[ \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{\beta} - j G _ {1}\right) (I + G _ {1} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \right] \leq 1. \\ \end{array}
$$

Similarly, the following corollary can be shown.

Corollary 18.5 $\mu _ { \Delta } ( M ) \leq r \beta$ if there are $D _ { 1 } \in \mathcal { D }$ and $G _ { 1 } \in \mathcal G$ such that

$$\overline {{\sigma}} \left(\left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{\beta} - j G _ {1}\right) (I + G _ {1} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}\right) \leq r \leq 1.$$

Proof. This follows by noting that

$$\overline {{\sigma}} \left(\left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{\beta} - j G _ {1}\right) (I + G _ {1} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}\right) \leq r \leq 1\Longrightarrow \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{r \beta} - j \frac {G _ {1}}{r}\right) ^ {*} \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{r \beta} - j \frac {G _ {1}}{r}\right) \leq I + G _ {1} ^ {2} \leq I + \left(\frac {G _ {1}}{r}\right) ^ {2}.$$

Let $G _ { 2 } = \frac { G _ { 1 } } { r } \in \mathcal { G } .$ . Then

$$
\begin{array}{l} \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{r \beta} - j G _ {2}\right) ^ {*} \left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{r \beta} - j G _ {2}\right) \leq I + G _ {2} ^ {2} \\ \Longrightarrow \overline {{\sigma}} \left(\left(\frac {D _ {1} M D _ {1} ^ {- 1}}{r \beta} - j G _ {2}\right) (I + G _ {2} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}\right) \leq 1 \\ \Longrightarrow \mu_ {\Delta} (M) \leq r \beta . \\ \end{array}
$$

Note that this corollary is not necessarily true if $r > 1$ . It is fairly easy to check that the well-posedness condition, main loop theorem, robust stability, and robust performance theorems for the mixed $\mu$ setup are exactly the same as the ones for complex µ problems.
