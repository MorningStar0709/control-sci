# A.2 Matrices

This product $< \mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}>$ which is a scalar, is often called the inner product of these two vectors.

On the other hand, the outer product $\mathbf{x} > < \mathbf{y}$ of two vectors is defined as

$$
\mathbf {x} > <   \mathbf {y} = \mathbf {x y} ^ {\prime} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} x _ {1} y _ {1} & x _ {1} y _ {2} & \dots & x _ {1} y _ {n} \\ x _ {2} y _ {1} & x _ {2} y _ {2} & \dots & x _ {2} y _ {n} \\ \dots & \dots & \dots & \dots \\ x _ {n} y _ {1} & x _ {n} y _ {2} & \dots & x _ {n} y _ {n} \end{array} \right], \tag {A.1.8}
$$

which is a matrix defined next.
