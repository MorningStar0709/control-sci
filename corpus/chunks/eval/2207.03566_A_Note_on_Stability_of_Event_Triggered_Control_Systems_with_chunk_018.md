# VI. Conclusions

We have revisited the event-triggered control problem for time-delay systems considered in [1]. It has been shown that under the sufficient conditions proposed in [1] the event-triggered control system is globally asymptotically stable rather than just uniformly bounded and globally attractive. Moreover, our analysis has allowed us to arbitrarily choose a parameter in the eventtriggering condition to both ensure global asymptotic stability and non-existence of Zeno behavior in the event-triggered time-delay control systems. A numerical example has been presented to verify the theoretical results.
