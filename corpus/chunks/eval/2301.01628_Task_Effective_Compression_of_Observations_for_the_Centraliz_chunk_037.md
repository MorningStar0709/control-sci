As demonstrated by Fig. 9, TRI is an indirect metric of the effectiveness of communications that can explain the behaviour of different communication designs. It is also observed that the TRI metric magnifies the performance gap between different schemes as they get closer to the optimal performance. Nevertheless, TRI can be utilized as a standalone measure to quantify the effectiveness of a communication design since it almost perfectly predicts the average return performance of the a communication policy - without the need for the communication to be tested when solving the real task.
