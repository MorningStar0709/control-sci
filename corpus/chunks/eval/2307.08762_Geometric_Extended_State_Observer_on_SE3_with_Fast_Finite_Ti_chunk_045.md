A set of numerical simulations are conducted. The numerical simulation results present the stable performance of the FFTS-ESO scheme in estimating external force and torque disturbances acting on the UAV in different scenarios. The behavior of the FFTS-ESO is compared with two stateof-the-art observers for disturbance estimation. Using a realistic set of data for several simulated flight scenarios of a rotorcraft UAV, numerical simulations show that the FFTS-ESO, unlike the LESO and FxTSDO, is always stable and its convergence is robust to measurement noise and pose singularities. The proposed FFTS-ESO is implemented on the FCU of a multi-rotor UAV, with disturbance rejection control using feedback of disturbance estimates from the FFTS-ESO. The results validate the proposed FFTS-ESO experimentally and show the supremacy of the disturbance rejection control scheme over the original control scheme.
