The poor performance of the solenoid designs with stronger electromagnets (i.e., large N) can be explained by the corresponding increase in coil inductance $L _ { 0 }$ . Equation (11.12) illustrates that the initial coil inductance $L _ { 0 }$ is proportional to $N ^ { 2 }$ . While increasing inductance $L _ { 0 }$ increases the strength of the magnetic field and the electromagnetic force, it also slows down the response of the solenoid circuit. To show this effect, consider the mathematical model of the solenoid coil with nominal inductance $L _ { 0 }$ and without the back-emf term

$$L _ {0} \dot {I} + R I = e _ {\text { in }} (t) \tag {11.22}$$
