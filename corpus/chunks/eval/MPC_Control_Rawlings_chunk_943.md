Theorem B.11 merely provides a sufficient condition for global asymptotic stability that might be thought to be conservative. The next result, a converse stability theorem by Jiang and Wang (2002), establishes necessity under a stronger hypothesis, namely that $f ( \cdot )$ is continuous rather than locally bounded and uses the fact, established in Jiang and Wang (2002), Lemma 2.7, that the existence of a continuous Lyapunov function for the system $x ^ { + } = f ( x )$ and set A implies the existence of a smooth Lyapunov function.3
