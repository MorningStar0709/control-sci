The log determinant of generalized precision can be calculated using Equations 4 and 5 as:

$$
\begin{array}{l} \ln | \tilde {\Pi} | = \ln | S \otimes \Pi^ {z} | + \ln | S \otimes \Pi^ {w} | \\ = \ln (| S | ^ {m} | \Pi^ {z} | ^ {(p + 1)}) + \ln (| S | ^ {n} | \Pi^ {w} | ^ {(p + 1)}) \tag {19} \\ = (p + 1) \left(\ln | \Pi^ {z} | + \ln | \Pi^ {w} |\right) + (n + m) \ln | S |. \\ \end{array}

$$\frac {\partial \ln | \tilde {\Pi} |}{\partial s} = (n + m) \frac {\partial \ln | S |}{\partial s}, \frac {\partial^ {2} \ln | \tilde {\Pi} |}{\partial s ^ {2}} = (n + m) \frac {\partial^ {2} \ln | S |}{\partial s ^ {2}}. \tag {20}$$

$$\frac {\partial \ln | \tilde {\Pi} |}{\partial s} = 4 2 (n + m) \frac {1}{s}, \frac {\partial^ {2} \ln | \tilde {\Pi} |}{\partial s ^ {2}} = - 4 2 (n + m) \frac {1}{s ^ {2}}. \tag {21}$$
