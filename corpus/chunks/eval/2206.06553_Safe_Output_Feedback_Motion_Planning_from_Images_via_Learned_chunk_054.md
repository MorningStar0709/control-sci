# 4D nonholonomic car:

$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \dot {p} _ {x} \\ \dot {p} _ {y} \\ \dot {\phi} \\ \dot {v} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} v \cos (\phi) \\ v \sin (\phi) \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \omega \\ a \end{array} \right] + \left[ \begin{array}{c c} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] w _ {x} \tag {E.15}
$$

where $p _ { x }$ and $p _ { y }$ are the x and y translations of the car, φ is its orientation, and v is its linear velocity. where $\boldsymbol { u } = [ \omega , a ] ^ { \top }$ . To make (E.15) compatible with SoS programming, when searching for the CCM and OCM, we polynomialize the dynamics by fitting a degree 5 polynomial to sin(·) and cos(·) over $[ - \pi / 2 , \pi / 2 ]$ .
