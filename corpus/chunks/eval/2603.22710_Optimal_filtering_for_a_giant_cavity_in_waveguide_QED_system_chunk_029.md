$$W _ {\mathrm{coh}} ^ {\pm} (q, p) = \exp \left(- \frac {(q - q _ {0} \mp \beta) ^ {2}}{\sigma^ {2}} - \sigma^ {2} (p - p _ {0}) ^ {2}\right), \tag {46}$$

where $( q _ { 0 } , p _ { 0 } )$ denotes the phase-space center of the cat state, β represents the separation between the two coherent components, and $\sigma > 0$ characterizes the effective width of each Gaussian packet. The specific values of σ and $\beta$ are provided in the caption of Fig. 4.

The quantum interference between the two coherent components is described by

$$
\begin{array}{l} W _ {\text { int }} (q, p) = \exp \left(- \frac {(q - q _ {0}) ^ {2}}{\sigma^ {2}} - \sigma^ {2} (p - p _ {0}) ^ {2}\right) \\ \times \cos \left(\frac {2 \beta}{\sigma^ {2}} (q - q _ {0}) - 2 \beta \sigma^ {2} (p - p _ {0})\right), \tag {47} \\ \end{array}
$$

which generates the characteristic oscillatory fringes in phase space.

Combining these terms, the Wigner function used in the numerical simulations is approximated by

$$\bar {W} (q, p) = \frac {1}{2} \left(W _ {\mathrm{coh}} ^ {+} (q, p) + W _ {\mathrm{coh}} ^ {-} (q, p) + W _ {\mathrm{int}} (q, p)\right), \tag {48}$$

followed by a normalization

$$W (q, p) = \frac {\bar {W} (q , p)}{\max _ {q , p} | \bar {W} (q , p) |}, \tag {49}$$

which is introduced solely for visualization purposes and does not affect the underlying phase-space structure.

The resulting Wigner function for cat states typically shows an interference pattern between the two coherent state components, with characteristic negative regions that reveal the quantum nature of the superposition, as shown in Fig. 4. This negativity distinguishes cat states from classical mixtures and demonstrates their non-classical behavior. In Fig. 4, subfigures (a) and (c) present the initial cat states of the original system and the filter, respectively. As indicated by their Wigner functions, there is a significant difference between the two initial cat states. However, by the final time $T _ { f }$ , the cat states shown in subfigures (b) and (d) demonstrate that the filter provides a highly accurate estimate of the original system’s state.
