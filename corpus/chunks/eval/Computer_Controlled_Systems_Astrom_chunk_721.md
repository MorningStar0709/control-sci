# The Case When $C$ Has Zeros on the Unit Circle

The predictor of (12.16) is a dynamic system with the characteristic polynomial $C(z)$ . The assumption that C has all its zeros inside the unit disc thus guarantees that the predictor is stable in steady state. The initial conditions are irrelevant because their influence will decay exponentially.

It follows from the spectral factorization that C may be chosen to have its zeros inside the unit disc or on the unit circle. The zeros outside the unit disc is mirrored in the unit circle. Compare with Example 12.1. Thus it remains to discuss the case when C has zeros on the unit circle.
