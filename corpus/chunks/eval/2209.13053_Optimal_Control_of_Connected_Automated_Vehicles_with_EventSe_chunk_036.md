Constraint violation. It is worth noting that an “infeasible” QP does not necessarily imply a constraint violation, since violating a CBF constraint does not always imply the violation of an original constraint in (3), (4), and (5). This is due to the conservative nature of a CBF whose intent is to guarantee the satisfaction of our original constraints.
