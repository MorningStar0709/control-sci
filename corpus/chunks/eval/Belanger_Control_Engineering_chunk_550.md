# ◆ ◆ ◆ REMARK

A strictly proper $P(z)$ under pure-gain feedback will always become unstable for sufficiently large $|k|$ , because there are always as many branches going to infinity as the pole-zero difference. This is different from the continuous-time case, in which for a pole excess of 1 or 2, it is possible for all branches to remain in the left-half plane as k tends to infinity, thus retaining stability.
