From these calculations, the time derivative of $x _ { C }$ is given by

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C} (t)}{\mathrm{d} t} = \frac {E (\Delta x _ {C})}{\Delta t} \\ = \frac {\frac {1}{n} P (\Delta x _ {C} = \frac {1}{n}) - \frac {1}{n} P (\Delta x _ {C} = - \frac {1}{n})}{\frac {1}{n}} \\ = \omega \bar {\Psi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega) \\ = \omega \left\{\frac {k x _ {C} (1 - x _ {C | C})}{(k + 1) ^ {2}} \right\} - 2 (k c + b) \\ + \frac {(k - 1) b (x _ {C | C} - x _ {C})}{1 - x _ {C}} \left[ 2 + \frac {(k - 1) (1 - 2 x _ {C} + x _ {C | C})}{1 - x _ {C}} \right. \\ \left. \left. - \frac {k (k - 1) c \left(1 - 2 x _ {C} + x _ {C | C}\right)}{1 - x _ {C}} \right] \right\} + o (\omega) \Bigg \}. \tag {A35} \\ \end{array}
$$

Accordingly, the time derivative of xCC is given by

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C C} (t)}{\mathrm{d} t} = \frac {E (\Delta x _ {C C})}{\Delta t} \\ = \frac {\sum_ {l = 0} ^ {k} \frac {2 l}{k n} \left[ P (\Delta x _ {C C} = \frac {2 l}{k n}) - P (\Delta x _ {C C} = - \frac {2 l}{k n}) \right]}{\frac {1}{n}} \\ = \frac {2 x _ {C} (1 - x _ {C \mid C})}{k + 1} [ 1 - (k - 1) \frac {x _ {C \mid C} - x _ {C}}{1 - x _ {C}} ] + o (\omega). \tag {A36} \\ \end{array}
$$

Furthermore, we have

$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C | C} (t)}{\mathrm{d} t} = \bar {\Phi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega) \\ = \frac {2 (1 - x _ {C \mid C})}{k + 1} [ 1 - (k - 1) \frac {x _ {C \mid C} - x _ {C}}{1 - x _ {C}} ] + o (\omega). \tag {A37} \\ \end{array}
$$

Combining (A35) and (A37), we have the following dynamical system

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C}}{\mathrm{d} t} = \omega \bar {\Psi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega), \\ \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C | C}}{\mathrm{d} t} = \bar {\Phi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega). \end{array} \right. \tag {A38}
$$

Similar to the PC rule, system (A38) can be rewritten with a change in time scale as

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C} (\tau)}{\mathrm{d} \tau} = \bar {\Psi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega), \\ \omega \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C | C} (\tau)}{\mathrm{d} \tau} = \bar {\Phi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, \omega), \end{array} \right. \tag {A39}
$$

where τ = ωt. Letting ω → 0 in (A39), we obtain the reduced model

$$
\left\{ \begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm{d} x _ {C} (\tau)}{\mathrm{d} \tau} = \bar {\Psi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, 0), \\ 0 = \bar {\Phi} (x _ {C}, x _ {C | C}, 0). \end{array} \right. \tag {A40}
$$
