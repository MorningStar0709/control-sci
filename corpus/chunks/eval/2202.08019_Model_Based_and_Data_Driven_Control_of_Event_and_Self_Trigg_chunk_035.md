Applying the same controller gain and the triggering matrix as in the ETS control (which also guarantees the stability under the STS when using the same parameters in the ETS scheme, since as discussed in Remark 7 the STS is a special case of the ETS), the system state trajectory under the data-based STS (41) with $s = j h$ is depicted in Fig. 7 for $t \in [ 0 ,$ 400]. All states converge to the origin, thereby validating the practicality of our proposed co-design method and STS. Note that, in Fig. 7, only 22 out of 200 samples were transmitted to the controller. This illustrates the usefulness of the STS in reducing transmissions while ensuring stability. Moreover, note that in Figs 6 and 7, more data were generated by the STS compared with the ETS. The main reason is that the STS law (41) is more conservative than that of ETS (9).
