# 16.2.3 Digital Controller

The basic equation for the two-degree of freedom RST digital controller is:

$$S (q ^ {- 1}) u (t) = T (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) - R (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) \tag {16.4}$$

where:

$$S (q ^ {- 1}) = s _ {0} + s _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + s _ {n _ {S}} q ^ {- n _ {S}} = s _ {0} + q ^ {- 1} S ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {16.5}R (q ^ {- 1}) = r _ {0} + r _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + r _ {n _ {R}} q ^ {- n _ {R}} \tag {16.6}T (q ^ {- 1}) = t _ {0} + t _ {1} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + t _ {n _ {T}} q ^ {- n _ {T}} \tag {16.7}$$

$u ( t )$ is the plant input, $y ( t )$ is the plant output and $y ^ { * } ( t + d + 1 )$ is the desired tracking trajectory either stored in the computer or generated by a tracking reference model:

$$y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) = - A _ {m} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {*} (t + d) + B _ {m} (q ^ {- 1}) r (t) \tag {16.8}$$

where:

$$A _ {m} (q ^ {- 1}) = 1 + a _ {1} ^ {m} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + a _ {n _ {A}} ^ {m} q ^ {- n _ {A}} = 1 + q ^ {- 1} A _ {m} ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) \tag {16.9}B _ {m} (q ^ {- 1}) = b _ {0} ^ {m} + b _ {1} ^ {m} q ^ {- 1} + \dots + b _ {n _ {B}} ^ {m} q ^ {- n _ {B}} \tag {16.10}$$

and $r ( t )$ is the reference signal. Equation (16.4) can also be written as:

$$u (t) = \frac {1}{s _ {0}} [ T (q ^ {- 1}) y ^ {*} (t + d + 1) - S ^ {*} (q ^ {- 1}) u (t - 1) - R (q ^ {- 1}) y (t) ] \tag {16.11}$$

Note that for a number of control algorithms (like pole placement) $s _ { 0 } = 1$ in (16.11).
