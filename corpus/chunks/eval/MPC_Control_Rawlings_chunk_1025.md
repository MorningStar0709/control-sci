(b) The difference between the dual and the primal solutions is known as the duality gap. Strong duality is defined as the property that equality is achieved in (C.32) and the duality gap is zero (Boyd and Vandenberghe, 2004, p. 225).

$$\max _ {\lambda \in \mathbb {R} ^ {m}} q (\lambda) = \min _ {x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {n}} V (x) \quad \text { subject to } g (x) = 0 \tag {C.33}$$

Show that strong duality is equivalent to the existence of $\lambda _ { 0 }$ such that

$$\min _ {x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {n}} V (x) - \lambda_ {0} ^ {\prime} g (x) = \min _ {x \in \mathbb {R} ^ {n}} V (x) \quad \text { subject to } g (x) = 0 \tag {C.34}$$

Characterize the set of all $\lambda _ { 0 }$ that satisfy this equation.
