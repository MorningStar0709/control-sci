$$- \dot {\boldsymbol {\lambda}} (t) = \mathcal {H} _ {\mathcal {Z}}, \quad \boldsymbol {\lambda} \left(t _ {f}\right) = \left[ F _ {x}, 0, \dots 0 \right] ^ {\prime}, \tag {7.6.46}$$

and for the control

$$\frac {\partial \mathcal {H}}{\partial \alpha_ {p}} = \mathcal {H} _ {\alpha_ {p}} = 0, \tag {7.6.47}$$

where the subscripts in $\mathcal{H}_{\lambda}$ , $\mathcal{H}_{\mathcal{Z}}$ , and $F_{x}$ denote the partial derivative with respect to the subscripted variable.

The previous set of equations for the states (7.6.45) and costates (7.6.46) and their initial and final conditions constitute a two point boundary value problem (TPBVP). Such problems can be solved, depending on the difficulty, with a closed solution, or highly nonlinear problems must be solved with specialized software [68, 129, 130, 63].
