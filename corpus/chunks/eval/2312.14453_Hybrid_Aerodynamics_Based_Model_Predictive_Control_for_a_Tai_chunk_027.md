# V. CONCLUSION

This study developed an NN-based hybrid aerodynamic model of a quadrotor tail-sitter UAV that exhibits good accuracy and a low computational cost. HMPC for position tracking in hovering flight was implemented based on this model and was validated in real-world flight experiments. HMPC was strongly generalizable to unseen trajectories and achieved a 57% reduction in the tracking error relative to NMPC. The robustness of HMPC was confirmed in the presence of a wind disturbance of up to 3m/s. This approach simplifies the modeling and identification process of a quadrotor tailsitter UAV without requiring wind tunnel experiments or CFD calculations. Future work could extend this approach to encompass transition and cruise stages of UAV operation. Additionally, there is potential for exploring the feasibility of training the model online, allowing it to adapt dynamically to changing conditions, such as a mass change or variable wind disturbances.
