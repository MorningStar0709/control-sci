$$
\begin{array}{l} \left(\frac {\sqrt {n} - \delta}{\sqrt {n}}\right) ^ {1 - p} - \frac {\sqrt {n - 1}}{n ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p}} (1 - \delta^ {2}) ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p} \\ = \frac {1}{n ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p}} \left(\sqrt {n} (\sqrt {n} - \delta) ^ {1 - p} - \sqrt {n - 1} (1 - \delta^ {2}) ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p}\right). \\ \end{array}
$$

To show that this quantity is positive whenever $n \geq 2$ and $p < 1$ , it suffices to show that $\hat { ( } \sqrt { n } - \delta ) ^ { 1 - p } \geq ( 1 - \delta ^ { 2 } ) ^ { 1 - \frac { 1 } { 2 } p }$ . Since $1 - \delta ^ { 2 } \in [ 0 , 1 ]$ and $p < 1$ , it suffices to show

$$
\begin{array}{l} (n + \delta^ {2} - 2 \delta \sqrt {n}) ^ {1 - p} \geq (1 - \delta^ {2}) ^ {1 - p} \\ \Longleftrightarrow 2 \delta^ {2} - 2 \delta \sqrt {n} + n - 1 \geq 0 \tag {65} \\ \end{array}
$$

for $n \geq 2 .$ . The left hand side of (65) is quadratic in $\delta ,$ so we can compute the critical point and find that the left hand side has a minimum value of $\frac { n } { 2 } - 1 \geq 0$ . Hence, it follows that

$$c := \frac {(1 - \delta M \sqrt {n}) ^ {1 - p} - R _ {n - 1} (1 - \delta^ {2}) ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p} (M \sqrt {n}) ^ {2 - p}}{n ^ {1 - \frac {1}{2} p}} > 0.$$

This establishes the result.
