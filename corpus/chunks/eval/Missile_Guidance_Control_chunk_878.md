Let us now return to the discussion of conics. To recapitulate, a conic is the locus of points whose distance from a fixed point F and a fixed line $D D ^ { \prime }$ have a constant ratio e. The fixed point F is called the focus, the fixed line $D D ^ { \prime }$ the directrix, and the ratio e the eccentricity. Letting m be the distance from the focus to the directrix $D D ^ { \prime }$ , the polar equation for the conic is

$$r = e (m - r \cos \theta),$$

or

$$r = e m / (1 + e \cos \theta). \tag {G.2}$$

By letting $\theta = 0 ^ { \circ } , 9 0 ^ { \circ } , 1 8 0 ^ { \circ }$ , and $\tan ^ { - 1 } ( b / a )$ , important distances are found; see Figure G.1.

Other expressions describing the geometry of the ellipse are as follows:

$$
\begin{array}{l} r = l / (1 + e \cos \theta) \\ = r _ {p} (1 + e) / (1 + e \cos \theta) \\ = a (1 - e ^ {2}) / (1 + e \cos \theta), \tag {G.3} \\ \end{array}
$$
