The preceding theorem shows that the norm of each submatrix transfer function is bounded above by $\|T_{wz}\|$ , so that making $\|T_{wz}\|$ small forces all components to be at least as small. The lower bound applies only to the submatrix of maximum norm; it is therefore possible for some submatrices to be appreciably smaller in norm than $(1/\sqrt{mn})\|T_{wz}\|$ . The design can proceed by trial and error. For example, in Example 8.7, if it is required that $\|W_{2}U\|\leq1$ and the algorithm actually returns the value $\|W_{2}U\|=0.2$ , another try can be made with a scaled-down $W_{2}$ (or, equivalently, an increased upper bound on $\|W_{2}U\|$ ).
