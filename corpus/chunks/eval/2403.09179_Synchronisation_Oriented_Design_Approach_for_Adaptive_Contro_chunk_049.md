# 2. Design Considerations

Besides the separation between the bandwidth $\omega _ { e }$ of $\Sigma _ { e } ^ { C L }$ and the bandwidth $\omega _ { f }$ of $\Sigma _ { f }$ (discussed in the end of Sec. II.D.2 and in the beginning of Sec. V), the choice of $\omega _ { f }$ is also limited by the fact that the uncertainty lying beyond the actuator bandwidth $\omega _ { a }$ cannot be cancelled by matching a plant input. The input component for uncertainty cancellation $u _ { a d } \left( t \right) = - \hat { \Delta } \left( t \right)$ acts only through the physical actuator of the plant with a finite bandwidth. Hence, $\omega _ { a }$ is in practice the maximum admissible value of $\omega _ { f }$ for meaningful uncertainty cancellation. The same reasoning is the underlying design principle of the $\mathcal { L } _ { 1 }$ adaptive control methods. In summary, a reasonable design guideline is to choose parameters to satisfy max $\left| \lambda \left( A _ { m } \right) \right|$ ≪ $\omega _ { e } < \omega _ { f } \leq \omega _ { a }$ .
