$$
\sigma = \phi (\tilde {q}, \tilde {\eta}) = \left( \begin{array}{c c} 2 & 0 \\ 2 & 2 \end{array} \right) \binom{\tilde {q} _ {1}}{\tilde {q} _ {2}} + \tilde {\eta}.

In this case, the matrix $\tilde { \Lambda }$ defined by (29) is calculated as

$$
\tilde {\Lambda} (q) = \frac {\sqrt {3}}{\sqrt {1 6 - 9 \cos^ {2} q _ {2}}} \left( \begin{array}{c c} 4 & - 3 \cos q _ {2} \\ - 3 \cos q _ {2} & 1 2 \end{array} \right) \succ 0,
$$

which satisfies Assumption 3. As mentioned in Section 3, general p-norm of the sliding variable $U ( \sigma ) = k \| \sigma \| _ { s } ^ { r }$ satisfying (23) can be selected as a potential function satisfying Assumption 2. This class of functions has two parameters r and s. The free parameter r define how the input is continuous and the parameter s changes the behavior in the reaching mode. In this simulation, two potential functions are selected as

$$U (p + \phi (q)) = 2 \| \sigma \| _ {2} ^ {1. 3} = 2 \| p + \phi (q) \| _ {2} ^ {1. 3},U (p + \phi (q)) = 2 \| \sigma \| _ {1} = 2 \| p + \phi (q) \| _ {1}.$$

The desired position of the end of the manipulator arm is selected by

$$\binom{x ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)}{y ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)} = \binom{1 + 0. 5 \cos t}{0. 5 \sin t}.$$

By calculating the inverse kinematics, the desired trajectory of the angle is derived as

$$q ^ {\mathrm{d}} (x ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t), y ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)) = \binom{\tan^ {- 1} \Big (\frac {y ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)}{x ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)} \Big) - \cos^ {- 1} \Big (\frac {l _ {1} ^ {2} - l _ {2} ^ {2} + r ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t) ^ {2}}{2 l _ {1} r ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t)} \Big)}{\cos^ {- 1} \Big (\frac {r ^ {\mathrm{d}} (t) ^ {2} - l _ {1} ^ {2} - l _ {2} ^ {2}}{2 l _ {1} l _ {2}} \Big)},$$

with $r ^ { \mathrm { d } } ( t ) ^ { 2 } \equiv x ^ { \mathrm { d } } ( t ) ^ { 2 } + y ^ { \mathrm { d } } ( t ) ^ { 2 }$ . The initial condition of the state is given by

$$(q (0) ^ {\top}, p (0) ^ {\top}) ^ {\top} = (0, 0, 0, 0) ^ {\top}.$$
